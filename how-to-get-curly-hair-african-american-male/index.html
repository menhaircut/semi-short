How to Get Curly Hair African American Male

Getting curly hair as an African American male is possible, but it may require some work. You can achieve curly hair in two ways: by using chemicals or by using natural methods. Chemical methods involve relaxing the hair and then curling it, while natural methods involve using styling products and techniques to enhance your natural curl pattern.

Chemical Methods

The most common chemical method for achieving curly hair is a perm or a texturizer. A perm involves using chemicals to break down the hair's protein structure and then reforming it into a new shape. A texturizer is a milder form of a perm that loosens the curl pattern rather than completely changing it.

It's important to note that chemical methods can be damaging to the hair if not done correctly, so we recommend going to a professional stylist who has experience working with African American hair.

Natural Methods

If you don't want to use chemicals on your hair, there are several natural methods you can try to enhance your natural curl pattern. These methods include:

  • Twist-outs: Twist small sections of damp hair and allow them to dry. Once dry, unravel the twists for defined curls.
  • Braid-outs: Braid damp hair and allow it to dry. Once dry, unravel the braids for a wavy or curly look.
  • Finger coils: Twirl small sections of hair around your finger to create coils. Allow them to dry, then separate them for a curly look.

Step-by-Step Instructions

If you've decided to try natural methods to achieve curly hair, here are some step-by-step instructions to follow:

  1. Shampoo and condition your hair, then towel-dry it.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to your hair.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections and twist, braid, or finger coil each section.
  4. Allow your hair to air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting.
  5. Once your hair is dry, unravel the twists, braids, or coils.
  6. Use your fingers or a pick to fluff out your hair and create volume.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Now that you've achieved curly hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair after washing to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or using a satin or silk pillowcase.
  • Trim your ends regularly to keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Can I achieve curly hair without using chemicals?

Yes, you can achieve curly hair without using chemicals. Natural methods like twist-outs, braid-outs, and finger coils can help enhance your natural curl pattern.

Can I get a perm or texturizer if I have damaged hair?

No, you should not get a perm or texturizer if you have damaged hair. Chemical treatments can further damage your hair and lead to breakage.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

African American hair is naturally dry, so you should avoid washing it too often. Aim to wash your hair once a week or every other week, depending on your hair type and how oily your scalp gets.

Hair Care Advice

Lastly, here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or treatments on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and health.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Brush or comb your hair gently to avoid breakage.
  • If you're experiencing hair loss or other hair issues, consult a dermatologist or trichologist for advice.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve curly hair as an African American male and keep your hair healthy and looking great. Good luck!
