How To Keep A Wig From Falling Off Your Head Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Oct 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Wearing a wig is a great way to change up your look without having to commit to a new hairstyle. However, one of the biggest problems wig wearers face is keeping their wig in place. Nothing is more frustrating than feeling like your wig is about to fall off your head in public. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to keep a wig from falling off your head.

How to Keep a Wig from Falling Off Your Head

There are several ways to keep a wig from falling off your head. Here are some of the most effective methods:

1. Wig Clips

Wig clips are small combs that can be sewn onto the inside of your wig. They attach to your natural hair and help keep the wig in place. Wig clips are a great option if you have some hair of your own to clip them onto. However, if you are completely bald, wig clips may not be the best option for you.

2. Wig Tape

Wig tape is a double-sided tape that can be used to secure your wig to your head. It is a popular choice among wig wearers because it is easy to use and provides a strong hold. Wig tape can be applied to the inside of your wig or directly onto your scalp.

3. Wig Band

A wig band is a stretchy band that can be worn underneath your wig. It helps keep your wig in place by providing a non-slip surface for the wig to rest on. Wig bands are a great option if you have sensitive skin or if you don't want to use adhesives.

4. Wig Grip

A wig grip is a silicone band that can be worn underneath your wig. It provides a non-slip surface for the wig to rest on and helps keep the wig in place. Wig grips are a great option if you have sensitive skin or if you don't want to use adhesives.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to keep a wig from falling off your head:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

If you have hair of your own, make sure it is flat against your head. You can do this by braiding your hair or using a wig cap. If you are completely bald, skip to step 2.

Step 2: Apply Adhesive

If you are using wig tape or glue, apply it to the inside of your wig or directly onto your scalp. If you are using wig clips, sew them onto the inside of your wig.

Step 3: Position Your Wig

Place your wig on your head and adjust it until it is in the desired position. Make sure it is secure and not too tight or too loose.

Step 4: Secure Your Wig

If you are using wig tape or glue, press down firmly to secure your wig. If you are using wig clips, clip them onto your natural hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help keep your wig in place:

1. Use a Wig Cap

A wig cap is a great way to keep your hair flat and provide a barrier between your scalp and the wig. It can also help prevent the wig from slipping.

2. Adjust the Straps

Most wigs come with adjustable straps that can be tightened or loosened to fit your head. Make sure to adjust the straps to fit your head securely.

3. Avoid Windy Conditions

Windy conditions can make it difficult to keep your wig in place. Try to avoid windy areas or wear a scarf or hat to help keep your wig in place.

FAQ

Q: How often should I wash my wig?

A: It depends on how often you wear your wig. If you wear your wig every day, you should wash it every 2-3 weeks. If you wear it less frequently, you can wash it every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I wear my wig while swimming?

A: It is not recommended to wear your wig while swimming. Chlorine and salt water can damage the wig fibers and cause tangling.

Hair Care Tips for Wigs

Here are some hair care tips to help keep your wig looking its best:

1. Use a Wig Brush

Use a wig brush to gently detangle your wig. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

2. Store Your Wig Properly

When you're not wearing your wig, store it on a wig stand or in a wig box to help maintain its shape.

3. Avoid Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools on your wig, as this can damage the fibers.

With these tips and tricks, you can keep your wig in place and feel confident wearing it out in public. Remember to take good care of your wig to keep it looking beautiful for years to come.
