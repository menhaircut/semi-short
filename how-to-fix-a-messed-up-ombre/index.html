How To Fix A Messed Up Ombre

Written by Daniel Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you frustrated with a botched ombre job? Don't worry, it's not the end of the world. In this article, we will guide you through how to fix a messed up ombre in simple, easy-to-follow steps. Whether it's a DIY job gone wrong or a professional mishap, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

Description

Ombre is a hair coloring technique where the hair gradually fades from one color to another. It's a popular trend and looks great when done right. However, if not done properly, it can result in a disaster. A messed up ombre can be due to various reasons such as uneven color distribution, incorrect technique, or using the wrong products. The good news is that it's fixable, and you don't have to live with a bad hair day forever.

How to Fix a Messed Up Ombre

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step is to assess the damage. Take a good look at your hair and identify the areas that need fixing. Is the color too light or too dark? Are there any harsh lines or patches? Once you have a clear idea of what needs to be fixed, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Decide on the Corrective Color

The next step is to decide on the corrective color. The corrective color should be based on the current state of your hair and the desired end result. If the ombre is too light, you may need to add some darker shades to even it out. On the other hand, if it's too dark, you may need to lighten it up. Consult with a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about which corrective color to use.

Step 3: Prepare the Corrective Color

Once you have decided on the corrective color, it's time to prepare it. Follow the instructions on the box carefully if you're using a boxed dye. If you're using a professional hair color, mix it according to the instructions provided by the manufacturer. Make sure you mix the color well to avoid any uneven patches.

Step 4: Apply the Corrective Color

Now it's time to apply the corrective color. Start from the areas that need the most attention and work your way down. Use a small brush to apply the color evenly and make sure you don't miss any spots. Be careful not to overlap the color onto the already colored parts of your hair. Leave the color on for the recommended time and rinse it off with lukewarm water.

Tips

  • Use a clarifying shampoo before applying the corrective color to remove any product buildup.
  • Do a strand test before applying the corrective color to avoid any allergic reactions or unwanted results.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days after applying the corrective color to prevent damage to your hair.

FAQ

How long does it take to fix a messed up ombre?

The time it takes to fix a messed up ombre depends on the extent of the damage and the corrective color needed. It can take anywhere from a few hours to a few days to fix a messed up ombre.

Can I fix a messed up ombre at home?

Yes, you can fix a messed up ombre at home, but it's recommended to consult with a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about the process.

Hair Care

After fixing your messed up ombre, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and color. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the color. Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage. Avoid using heat styling tools frequently and always use a heat protectant spray before styling. Trim your hair every few months to get rid of split ends and maintain a healthy length.

That's it! We hope this article has helped you fix your messed up ombre. Remember, with a little patience and effort, you can achieve the perfect ombre look that you've always wanted.
