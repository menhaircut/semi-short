How To Sleep With Wet Curly Hair The Best Way To Preserve Your Curls Overnight

Written by Christine Sep 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that sleeping with wet hair can be a disaster. Not only can it cause frizz and tangles, but it can also lead to breakage and damage. However, sometimes you just don't have the time or energy to fully dry your hair before bed. So, what's the best way to sleep with wet curly hair? In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks to help you preserve your curls overnight without causing damage.

Why Sleeping with Wet Curly Hair is a Bad Idea

Before we dive into the best way to sleep with wet curly hair, let's talk about why it's not ideal. When your hair is wet, it's in its most fragile state. The water weakens the hair shaft, making it more susceptible to damage. Additionally, wet hair is more prone to tangling and frizz, which can be difficult to tame once it dries. Finally, sleeping on wet hair can cause it to flatten and lose its shape, which is the last thing you want if you're trying to preserve your curls.

How to Sleep with Wet Curly Hair

Step 1: Use a Microfiber Towel

Before you even think about going to bed, you need to dry your hair as much as possible. However, you don't want to use a regular towel, as it can cause friction and breakage. Instead, opt for a microfiber towel, which is gentler on your hair and absorbs water more efficiently.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

To help keep your curls hydrated and defined overnight, you should apply a leave-in conditioner before going to bed. This will help seal in moisture and prevent your hair from drying out while you sleep. Just make sure to use a lightweight formula that won't weigh your hair down or make it greasy.

Step 3: Plop Your Hair

If you're not familiar with the term "plopping," it's a technique that involves wrapping your hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel to help enhance your curls while they dry. To plop your hair before bed, simply lay a t-shirt or microfiber towel flat on your bed, flip your wet hair over onto it, and wrap the fabric around your head. This will help keep your curls intact and prevent them from getting flattened overnight.

Step 4: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

If you want to further minimize damage and frizz while you sleep, you should invest in a silk pillowcase. Unlike cotton or polyester, silk is gentle on your hair and won't cause as much friction. It also helps prevent your hair from getting tangled, which can be a nightmare for curly hair.

Tips for Sleeping with Wet Curly Hair

Now that you know the basic steps for sleeping with wet curly hair, here are some additional tips to help you wake up with beautiful, bouncy curls:

  • Apply a curl cream or gel before plopping to help enhance your curls even more.
  • Avoid using hair ties or clips, as they can leave marks and cause breakage.
  • If you don't have a silk pillowcase, try sleeping with your hair in a loose pineapple on top of your head.
  • Avoid sleeping on your back or stomach, as this can cause your hair to get flattened or tangled.
  • In the morning, gently shake out your curls and fluff them with your fingers.

FAQs

Can I sleep with wet curly hair if I don't have time to fully dry it?

Yes, you can. Just make sure to follow the steps outlined above to help minimize damage and frizz.

Will sleeping with wet curly hair ruin my curls?

It can potentially lead to damage and frizz, but as long as you take steps to minimize the risk, you should be able to preserve your curls overnight.

What's the best way to dry curly hair?

The best way to dry curly hair is to air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting. Avoid using a regular towel, as it can cause friction and damage.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

While sleeping with wet curly hair can be a challenge, it's just one of many obstacles that curly-haired individuals face. To keep your curls looking their best, make sure to follow a few basic hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools as much as possible, as they can cause damage and frizz.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for your hair type and texture.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help keep your curly hair looking beautiful and healthy, even while you sleep.
