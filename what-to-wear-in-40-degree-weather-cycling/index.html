What To Wear In 40 Degree Weather Cycling Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jul 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're an avid cyclist, you know that the weather can make or break your ride. When it comes to cycling in 40 degree weather, proper clothing is key to staying comfortable and safe. In this article, we'll discuss what to wear in 40 degree weather cycling, including how to dress in layers, what fabrics to choose, and other helpful tips.

Description

When cycling in 40 degree weather, it's important to dress in layers to stay comfortable and safe. Layering allows you to adjust your clothing as your body temperature changes during your ride. You should also choose fabrics that wick away sweat and moisture, keeping you dry and warm.

How to Dress in Layers

When it comes to layering for cycling in 40 degree weather, start with a base layer made of a moisture-wicking fabric, such as polyester or merino wool. This will keep sweat away from your skin, preventing chills and discomfort. Next, add a mid-layer, such as a fleece or wool sweater, to insulate your body. Finally, add an outer layer, such as a windbreaker or waterproof jacket, to protect you from the elements.

It's important to choose layers that are easy to remove or add, depending on your body temperature. For example, if you start to feel too warm, remove your outer layer or mid-layer. If you start to feel cold, add another layer.

What Fabrics to Choose

When choosing fabrics for cycling in 40 degree weather, look for materials that are breathable, moisture-wicking, and quick-drying. These fabrics will help regulate your body temperature and keep you dry and comfortable during your ride. Some good options include polyester, merino wool, and synthetic blends.

Avoid cotton, as it absorbs moisture and takes a long time to dry, leaving you feeling cold and uncomfortable. Instead, opt for fabrics that are specifically designed for athletic activities, such as cycling.

Tips for Staying Comfortable

Here are some additional tips for staying comfortable when cycling in 40 degree weather:

  • Wear gloves and a hat to keep your extremities warm
  • Use toe covers or shoe covers to protect your feet
  • Wear sunglasses to protect your eyes from wind and sun glare
  • Bring a scarf or neck gaiter to protect your face and neck from the wind
  • Choose cycling shorts or tights made of a moisture-wicking fabric

FAQ

Q: Can I wear cotton when cycling in 40 degree weather?

A: It's best to avoid cotton when cycling in 40 degree weather, as it absorbs moisture and takes a long time to dry. Instead, opt for moisture-wicking fabrics that will keep you dry and comfortable.

Q: Do I need a waterproof jacket when cycling in 40 degree weather?

A: It's a good idea to bring a waterproof jacket when cycling in 40 degree weather, as the weather can be unpredictable. A waterproof jacket will protect you from rain and wind, keeping you dry and warm.

Hair Care

Cycling in 40 degree weather can be tough on your hair, as the wind and moisture can cause damage and dryness. To protect your hair, wear a helmet that fits properly and covers your head completely. You can also wear a headband or scarf to protect your hairline and keep your hair in place. After your ride, wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to restore moisture and prevent damage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can stay comfortable and safe when cycling in 40 degree weather. Remember to dress in layers, choose moisture-wicking fabrics, and protect your extremities and hair from the elements. Happy cycling!
