How To Cut Thick Long Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon for a simple trim? Or maybe you just want to try your hand at giving yourself a new haircut? Whatever your reasons, cutting your own hair at home can be both fun and cost-effective. However, if you have thick long hair, it can be a bit intimidating to attempt a DIY haircut. But fear not, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. Read on for our step-by-step guide on how to cut thick long hair at home.

Description

Thick long hair can be challenging to manage and style, but it also offers versatility when it comes to haircuts. From layered looks to blunt cuts, thick long hair can be styled in many ways. However, getting a professional haircut can be expensive, and sometimes it's hard to know exactly what you want. Cutting your own hair at home can be an affordable and fun option, especially if you have a busy schedule. This guide will help you achieve a perfect haircut without having to leave your home.

How to Cut Thick Long Hair at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step to any DIY haircut is to make sure you have the right tools. For cutting thick long hair, you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • A handheld mirror (or a friend to help you check the back of your head)

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's best to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair tends to stretch and can result in uneven cuts. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles, and section it off using hair clips.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting small sections of hair at a time. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on, so start with less. Use your comb to hold the hair taut before cutting, and make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid split ends. If you want to add layers, cut at an angle, but be careful not to cut too much off at once.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

After you've finished cutting a section, release it from the clip and check your progress in the mirror. Make sure the length and layers are even on both sides. If you need to make adjustments, go back and trim as needed.

Step 5: Finish with Detailing

Once you're happy with the length and layers, it's time to do some detailing. Use your scissors to trim any flyaways or uneven ends. You can also use texturizing shears to add texture and movement to your hair.

Tips for Cutting Thick Long Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve a perfect haircut:

  • Start with less hair and work your way up to more. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take breaks between sections to avoid fatigue or making mistakes.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member, especially when it comes to the back of your head.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it can also be frustrating if you rush through it.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes! Just make sure to cut it when it's dry and use a diffuser when blow-drying to enhance your curls.

How often should I trim my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and damage.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Mistakes can happen, but they can also be fixed. If you accidentally cut too much off, try to blend it in with the rest of your hair or visit a professional stylist to help you fix it.

Hair Care

Now that you've achieved a perfect haircut, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid heat styling tools or use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially if you have thick long hair. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and be patient, and you'll achieve a perfect haircut from the comfort of your own home!
