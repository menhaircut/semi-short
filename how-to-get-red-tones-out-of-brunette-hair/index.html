How To Get Red Tones Out Of Brunette Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a brunette who's struggling to get rid of those stubborn red tones in your hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Whether you're dealing with natural red undertones or you've recently dyed your hair and ended up with an unwanted red hue, there are several methods you can try to get back to your desired shade. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps on how to get red tones out of brunette hair.

Description

Red tones in brunette hair can be frustrating to deal with, but there are many ways to remove them. The key is finding the right method that works for you. It's important to note that the process may take some time and experimentation, so be patient and don't give up!

How to Get Red Tones Out of Brunette Hair: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Determine Your Hair's Undertones

The first step in removing red tones from your hair is to figure out what type of undertones you're dealing with. If you're unsure, take a closer look at your hair in natural light. If you see orange or copper tones, you likely have warm undertones. If your hair has a purple or blue cast, you have cool undertones. Knowing your undertones will help you choose the right method for removing red tones.

Step 2: Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Using a clarifying shampoo once a week can help remove buildup and excess color from your hair. Choose a clarifying shampoo that's sulfate-free and gentle on your hair. Massage it into your scalp and hair, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 3: Try a Color-Depositing Shampoo

If you have warm undertones, try using a blue shampoo to neutralize the orange and copper tones. For cool undertones, use a purple shampoo to cancel out any red or brassy tones. Use the color-depositing shampoo once a week to maintain your hair's color.

Step 4: Use a Toner

If your hair is still showing red tones after using a clarifying and color-depositing shampoo, consider using a toner. A toner is a semi-permanent hair color that helps neutralize unwanted hues. Choose a toner that's specifically designed for your hair's undertones.

Step 5: Visit a Professional

If you've tried all of the above methods and still can't get rid of the red tones, it may be time to visit a professional stylist. They can help you find the right solution for your hair and ensure that the process is done correctly.

Tips for Getting Red Tones Out of Brunette Hair

  • Don't try to remove red tones from your hair all at once. It's better to take a gradual approach to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Be patient. Removing red tones from your hair may take some time and experimentation.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQs

Can I dye my hair to get rid of red tones?

Yes, you can dye your hair to get rid of red tones, but it's important to choose the right dye for your hair's undertones. If you're unsure, it's best to visit a professional stylist.

Will removing red tones damage my hair?

It depends on the method you use and the condition of your hair. To avoid damage, take a gradual approach and use products that are gentle on your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Brunettes

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.

By following these steps and tips, you can get rid of those unwanted red tones in your brunette hair and achieve the shade you desire. Remember to be patient and take a gradual approach to avoid damaging your hair. Good luck!
