How To Cut A Guys Hair Into A Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to give a fresh new look to your male clients? One of the most popular styles for men is the fade. It's a versatile look that can go from a low skin fade to a high bald fade. In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut a guys hair into a fade.

Description

Fade is a hairstyle that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom of the head. The hair on the top is left longer, and the hair on the sides and back are tapered shorter. The fade can be low, medium, or high, depending on how short you want the hair to be. It's a trendy and modern look that works well for all hair types.

How to Cut a Guys Hair into a Fade

Step 1: Choose the Right Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a clipper, scissors, a comb, and hair clippers. The clipper should have guards of various sizes, and the scissors should be sharp enough to cut through hair easily. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to wet the hair down if needed.

Step 2: Determine the Length of the Fade

Decide how short you want the hair to be. This will determine the length of the fade. If the client wants a low fade, start with a longer guard and gradually work your way down to a shorter guard. For a high fade, start with a shorter guard and work your way up to a longer guard.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Start by cutting the hair on the sides. Use a clipper with a guard that matches the desired length of the fade. Start at the bottom and work your way up, gradually decreasing the length of the guard. Use a comb to blend the hair in between each guard size. Be sure to make clean lines around the ears and neckline.

Step 4: Move to the Back

Once you have finished the sides, move to the back. Use the same technique as the sides, gradually decreasing the guard size. Be sure to blend the hair between each guard size to create a smooth transition.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Use scissors to cut the hair on the top. Start with a longer length and gradually cut the hair shorter until it's the desired length. Use a comb to blend the hair in between each cut.

Step 6: Blend the Top and Sides

Use scissors to blend the hair on the top and sides. Use a comb to hold the hair up and cut it at an angle to create a seamless blend.

Step 7: Clean Up

Use clippers and a razor to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neckline. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that may have been missed.

Step 8: Style the Hair

Once the haircut is complete, style the hair as desired. Use a product such as pomade, gel, or wax to create the desired look.

Tips

  • Make sure your tools are clean and well-maintained.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a comb to blend the hair between each guard size.
  • Be patient and take your time. The fade requires precision and attention to detail.

FAQ

Q: Can the fade be done on any hair type?

A: Yes, the fade can be done on any hair type. However, the length of the fade may vary depending on the texture of the hair.

Q: How often should the fade be maintained?

A: The fade should be maintained every 2-3 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep the fade looking sharp, it's important to maintain healthy hair. Encourage your clients to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and to avoid over-styling the hair with heat tools. Regular trims will also help to keep the hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting a guy's hair into a fade can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it can be a simple and easy process. Follow these steps and tips to create a fresh and modern look for your male clients.
