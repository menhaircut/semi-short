Why Do Dogs Shed Too Much Hair

Written by Aprilia Jun 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Dogs are furry and adorable pets that bring joy and companionship to their owners. However, they also come with a downside: shedding. Shedding is a natural process that all dogs go through, but some breeds shed more than others. In this article, we will explain why dogs shed too much hair and how you can manage it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To Manage Shedding
  • Step By Step Guide

Description

Dogs shed for a variety of reasons, including seasonal changes, stress, illness, and aging. Shedding is a natural process that allows dogs to get rid of dead hair and grow new hair. Some breeds shed more than others, and dogs with thick coats tend to shed more than those with short coats.

Shedding is a normal and healthy process, but excessive shedding can be a sign of an underlying health issue. If your dog is shedding excessively, it is important to take them to the vet for a check-up to rule out any health problems.

How To Manage Shedding

If your dog sheds too much hair, there are several things you can do to manage it. Here are some tips:

1. Brush Your Dog Regularly

Brushing your dog regularly can help to remove loose hair and prevent it from ending up all over your house. Use a brush that is suitable for your dog's coat type, and be gentle when brushing to avoid hurting your dog's skin.

2. Bathe Your Dog

Bathing your dog can help to remove loose hair and dirt from their coat. Use a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner, and be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid leaving any residue on your dog's skin.

3. Feed Your Dog a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help to reduce shedding by promoting healthy skin and coat. Feed your dog a high-quality dog food that is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.

4. Keep Your Home Clean

Clean your home regularly to prevent dog hair from accumulating. Use a vacuum cleaner with a pet hair attachment to remove hair from carpets, furniture, and floors.

5. Control Your Dog's Stress

Stress can cause excessive shedding in dogs. Try to minimize your dog's stress by providing them with a comfortable and safe environment, and by spending quality time with them.

Step By Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to managing your dog's shedding:

Step 1: Brush your dog regularly

Use a brush that is suitable for your dog's coat type, and brush gently to avoid hurting your dog's skin.

Step 2: Bathe your dog

Bathe your dog using a dog-specific shampoo and conditioner, and rinse thoroughly.

Step 3: Feed your dog a healthy diet

Feed your dog a high-quality dog food that is rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Step 4: Keep your home clean

Clean your home regularly to prevent dog hair from accumulating.

Step 5: Control your dog's stress

Minimize your dog's stress by providing a comfortable and safe environment and spending quality time with them.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for managing your dog's shedding:

  • Use a de-shedding tool to remove loose hair from your dog's coat.
  • Consider using a dog-specific air purifier to reduce pet dander and hair in your home.
  • Use a dog-specific conditioner to help prevent tangles and matting in your dog's coat.
  • Consult with your vet if your dog is shedding excessively or if you have any concerns about their health.

FAQ

Q: How often should I brush my dog?

A: You should brush your dog at least once a week, but some breeds may require more frequent brushing.

Q: Why do dogs shed more in the spring?

A: Dogs shed more in the spring because they are getting rid of their winter coat to make way for a lighter summer coat.

Q: Can I use human shampoo on my dog?

A: No, you should never use human shampoo on your dog as it can be too harsh and cause skin irritation.
