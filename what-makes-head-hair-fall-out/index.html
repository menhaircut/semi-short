What Makes Head Hair Fall Out

Written by Petter Nov 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss and wondering what could be causing it? Hair loss can be a frustrating and distressing experience, but understanding the root causes can help you find the best solution to prevent further hair loss. In this article, we will explore what makes head hair fall out.

Table of Content

  1. Genetics
  2. Hormonal Changes
  3. Stress
  4. Poor Hair Care
  5. Medical Conditions

1. Genetics

One of the most common causes of hair loss is genetics. If hair loss runs in your family, it's likely that you will experience it too. Male-pattern baldness and female-pattern baldness are both genetic conditions that cause hair loss. Male-pattern baldness typically starts with a receding hairline and thinning at the crown, while female-pattern baldness often results in a widening part and thinning all over the scalp.

2. Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also cause hair loss. Pregnancy, menopause, and thyroid disorders can all affect hormone levels and lead to hair loss. Androgenetic alopecia, also known as female-pattern hair loss, is a hormonal condition that can cause hair loss in women.

3. Stress

Stress can also play a role in hair loss. When you're under stress, your body produces more cortisol, which can lead to hair loss. Telogen effluvium is a type of hair loss that's caused by stress. It's typically temporary and hair will grow back once the stressor is removed.

4. Poor Hair Care

Poor hair care can also cause hair loss. Over-styling, using heat tools, and using harsh chemicals can all damage hair and lead to breakage and hair loss. Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can also cause hair loss over time.

5. Medical Conditions

There are several medical conditions that can cause hair loss. Alopecia areata is an autoimmune disorder that causes hair loss in patches. Scalp infections, such as ringworm, can also cause hair loss. Trichotillomania is a hair-pulling disorder that can lead to hair loss.

6. Medications

Certain medications can also cause hair loss. Chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and birth control pills can all cause hair loss. If you're experiencing hair loss and are taking medication, talk to your doctor to see if the medication could be causing the hair loss.

7. Nutrition

Your diet can also affect hair loss. A lack of protein, iron, and other essential nutrients can lead to hair loss. Eating a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins can help prevent hair loss.

8. Age

As we age, our hair naturally thins and can lead to hair loss. This is because hair follicles shrink and produce thinner, shorter hair. Age-related hair loss is also more common in men than women.

9. Hairstyling

Hairstyling can also play a role in hair loss. Using heat tools, like flat irons and curling irons, can damage hair and lead to breakage and hair loss. Chemical treatments, like hair dye and bleach, can also damage hair and cause hair loss.

10. Smoking

Smoking can also contribute to hair loss. Nicotine restricts blood flow to hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss. Quitting smoking can help improve hair health.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss

There are several steps you can take to prevent hair loss:

  • Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, iron, and vitamins
  • Avoid over-styling and using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Quit smoking
  • Manage stress levels
  • Talk to your doctor about medication side effects

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hair loss be reversed?

It depends on the cause of the hair loss. If the hair loss is caused by a medical condition or medication, treating the underlying condition or stopping the medication may help reverse the hair loss. If the hair loss is caused by genetics, it may not be reversible, but there are treatments available to slow down or stop the hair loss.

Is hair loss normal?

It's normal to lose some hair every day, but if you're noticing excessive hair loss or thinning, it's important to talk to your doctor to determine the cause.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss. Telogen effluvium is a type of hair loss that's caused by stress. It's typically temporary and hair will grow back once the stressor is removed.

Hair Care Tips

Proper hair care can help prevent hair loss. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid over-styling and using heat tools
  • Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles and hair accessories
  • Protect hair from the sun and chlorine

In conclusion, hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, poor hair care, medical conditions, medications, nutrition, age, hairstyling, and smoking. Understanding the cause of your hair loss can help you find the best solution to prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth.
