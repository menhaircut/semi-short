What To Do For Chlorine Damaged Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Swimming in a pool is a fun and refreshing activity during summer. But, the chlorine in the water can wreak havoc on your hair. Chlorine can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and damaged. Fortunately, there are ways to protect your hair from chlorine damage and restore its health. In this blog post, we'll share some tips on what to do for chlorine damaged hair.

Description

Chlorine is a chemical commonly used in swimming pools to kill bacteria and prevent the growth of algae. However, it can also damage your hair by stripping it of its natural oils and leaving it dry and brittle. Chlorine can also cause your hair to turn green, especially if you have light-colored hair. This is because the copper in the water reacts with the chlorine, creating a green tint. If you have chlorine damaged hair, don't worry. There are ways to repair and restore your hair's health.

How to Protect Your Hair from Chlorine Damage

The best way to protect your hair from chlorine damage is to wear a swimming cap. A swimming cap can create a barrier between your hair and the water, preventing the chlorine from coming into contact with your hair. If you don't like wearing a swimming cap, you can also wet your hair with fresh water before getting into the pool. This will help your hair absorb less chlorine.

Step-by-Step Guide: What to Do for Chlorine Damaged Hair

Step 1: Rinse Your Hair

After swimming in a pool, rinse your hair with fresh water. This will help remove any chlorine residue from your hair. Use a gentle shampoo to wash your hair, and be sure to massage your scalp to stimulate blood flow and promote hair growth.

Step 2: Deep Conditioning

Deep conditioning is essential for repairing chlorine damaged hair. Use a hair mask or deep conditioner that contains moisturizing ingredients such as coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil. Apply the conditioner to your hair and leave it on for at least 30 minutes. Rinse it off with cool water.

Step 3: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to help protect your hair from further damage. A leave-in conditioner will also help to detangle your hair, making it easier to comb through.

Tips for Treating Chlorine Damaged Hair

Here are some additional tips for treating chlorine damaged hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers and flat irons, as they can further damage your hair.
  • Limit your use of hair products such as hairspray and gel, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

FAQ

Q: Can chlorine damage hair permanently?

A: Chlorine can damage hair, but it's not usually permanent. With the right care and treatments, you can restore your hair's health and vitality.

Q: Can I swim with chlorine damaged hair?

A: Yes, you can swim with chlorine damaged hair. Just be sure to protect your hair with a swimming cap or wet it with fresh water before getting into the pool.

Q: How long does it take to repair chlorine damaged hair?

A: The length of time it takes to repair chlorine damaged hair depends on the severity of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to see results.

Hair Care: Prevention is Key

The best way to prevent chlorine damaged hair is to take steps to protect your hair before getting into the pool. Wear a swimming cap, wet your hair with fresh water, or apply a leave-in conditioner. With the right care and treatments, you can restore your hair's health and enjoy swimming all summer long.
