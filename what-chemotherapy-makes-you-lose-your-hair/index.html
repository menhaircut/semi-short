What Chemotherapy Makes You Lose Your Hair Understanding The Process

Written by Oliver Sep 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Chemotherapy is one of the most common treatments for cancer. It involves the use of powerful drugs to destroy cancer cells in the body. However, chemotherapy can also cause a range of side effects, including hair loss. In this article, we will be discussing what chemotherapy makes you lose your hair, and how you can take care of your hair during and after treatment.

What is chemotherapy?

Chemotherapy is a treatment that uses drugs to kill cancer cells. The drugs are usually given through an IV, but they can also be given in pill form. Chemotherapy can be used to cure cancer, control cancer growth, or to relieve symptoms caused by cancer.

How does chemotherapy affect hair loss?

Chemotherapy drugs target rapidly dividing cells in the body, which includes cancer cells as well as hair follicles. This is why chemotherapy can cause hair loss. Hair loss due to chemotherapy is usually temporary, and hair will often start to grow back once treatment is over.

When does hair loss occur during chemotherapy?

Hair loss due to chemotherapy can occur at different times depending on the drugs used and the individual's response to treatment. Hair may start to fall out within a few weeks of starting chemotherapy, or it may not happen until several months into treatment. Hair loss may also occur gradually or all at once.

How much hair loss can you expect during chemotherapy?

How much hair you lose during chemotherapy depends on the drugs used and the dose of the drugs. Some people may only experience thinning of the hair, while others may lose all of their hair, including eyebrows and eyelashes. Hair loss can also vary from person to person, even if they are receiving the same treatment.

How to take care of your hair during chemotherapy?

It is important to take care of your hair during chemotherapy to minimize damage and promote healthy regrowth. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a soft brush
  • Protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather

Remember to be gentle with your hair and scalp, as they can be sensitive during chemotherapy.

Tips for caring for your hair after chemotherapy

After chemotherapy, your hair may take some time to grow back. Here are some tips for caring for your hair as it regrows:

  • Continue to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools until your hair is fully grown back
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair until your hair is fully grown back
  • Be patient â it may take several months for your hair to fully grow back

Frequently Asked Questions

Does everyone lose their hair during chemotherapy?

No, not everyone loses their hair during chemotherapy. Hair loss is a common side effect, but it can vary from person to person.

Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, in most cases, hair will grow back after chemotherapy. However, it may take several months for your hair to fully grow back.

Can I color or perm my hair during chemotherapy?

It is not recommended to color or perm your hair during chemotherapy, as this can cause further damage to the hair and scalp.

Conclusion

Chemotherapy can cause hair loss, but it is usually temporary. Taking care of your hair during and after treatment can help minimize damage and promote healthy regrowth. Remember to be patient with your hair, as it may take some time to fully grow back.
