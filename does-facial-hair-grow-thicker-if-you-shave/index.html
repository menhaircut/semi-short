Does Facial Hair Grow Thicker If You Shave

Written by Oliver Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Facial hair is a common topic among men, and it's no surprise that many want to know the answer to one question: does facial hair grow thicker if you shave? This is a common myth that has been around for years, but is there any truth to it? In this article, we will explore the science behind facial hair growth and shaving to determine whether facial hair grows thicker if you shave.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

The idea that facial hair grows thicker if you shave is a myth. This is because shaving does not affect the hair follicle, which is responsible for the thickness of the hair. Shaving only removes the hair that has grown out of the follicle. However, there are some reasons why people may believe that shaving makes hair grow thicker.

One reason is that when you shave, you are cutting the hair at its thickest point, which is where it emerges from the skin. When the hair grows back, it may feel thicker because it is shorter and has a blunt edge. However, this is just an illusion and the hair is not actually thicker.

How to

If you are looking to grow a beard or mustache, it is important to understand that shaving will not make your facial hair grow thicker. Instead, you should focus on maintaining a healthy lifestyle, eating a balanced diet, and taking care of your skin to promote healthy hair growth.

Step by Step

Here are some steps you can take to promote healthy facial hair growth:

  1. Eat a balanced diet that includes protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  2. Exercise regularly to improve blood flow and circulation.
  3. Take care of your skin by washing your face and using moisturizer.
  4. Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.
  5. Consider using a beard oil or balm to moisturize and nourish your facial hair.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Be patient. It takes time for facial hair to grow, so don't expect overnight results.
  • Trim your facial hair regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy.
  • If you experience any skin irritation or ingrown hairs, consider using a different shaving technique or seeking advice from a dermatologist.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about facial hair growth:

Does shaving make facial hair grow faster?

No, shaving does not affect the rate of facial hair growth. Hair grows at a fixed rate determined by genetics and other factors.

Does shaving make facial hair grow thicker?

No, shaving does not affect the thickness of facial hair. The thickness of hair is determined by the hair follicle.

What can I do to promote healthy facial hair growth?

You can promote healthy facial hair growth by maintaining a healthy lifestyle, eating a balanced diet, and taking care of your skin. You can also consider using a beard oil or balm to moisturize and nourish your facial hair.

Hair Care

Taking care of your facial hair is important to keep it looking healthy and neat. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your facial hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Apply a beard oil or balm to moisturize and nourish your facial hair.
  • Use a comb or brush to style your facial hair.
  • Trim your facial hair regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy.

In conclusion, the myth that facial hair grows thicker if you shave is just that - a myth. Shaving does not affect the thickness of facial hair, which is determined by the hair follicle. Instead, focus on maintaining a healthy lifestyle and taking care of your skin to promote healthy facial hair growth.
