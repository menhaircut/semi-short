How To Cut Volume Into Short Hair

Written by Bardi Aug 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Short hair can be a great way to show off your features and create a bold, confident look. However, if you have thick or unruly hair, cutting it short can sometimes feel like a daunting task. Luckily, there are several ways to cut volume into short hair that can help you achieve a chic and manageable style. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to cut volume into short hair, including tips and tricks for maintenance and care.

Description

Cutting volume into short hair can help create a more manageable style, particularly for those with thick, unruly hair. By incorporating layers and thinning techniques, you can reduce the bulk of your hair and create a more textured, voluminous look. Whether you prefer a pixie cut, a bob, or something in between, these techniques can help you achieve a style that is both practical and fashionable.

How to

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to prepare properly. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly, and then comb it out to remove any tangles or knots. Once your hair is ready, you can begin the cutting process.

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by dividing your hair into sections using hair clips or rubber bands. This will help you work on one section at a time and ensure that you don't miss any spots. Begin with the bottom section, and work your way up to the top.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Using a pair of sharp scissors, start cutting the bottom layer of your hair. Begin at the back of your head and work your way towards the front. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. This will create layers and help to reduce the volume of your hair.

Step 3: Thin Out Your Hair

If your hair is particularly thick, you may want to thin it out using thinning scissors. These scissors have teeth that help to remove bulk from your hair without actually cutting it. Use them to thin out the ends of your hair and create a more textured look.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Once you have cut the bottom layer and thinned out your hair, you can move on to the top layer. Use the same technique as before, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at an angle. This will create a layered, voluminous look that is both stylish and practical.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting volume into short hair:

  • Invest in a good pair of scissors. Sharp, high-quality scissors will make the cutting process easier and more precise.
  • Take your time. Cutting hair can be a time-consuming process, particularly if you are doing it yourself. Be patient and work slowly to ensure that you get the best results.
  • Consider your face shape. Different hairstyles suit different face shapes, so think about what will flatter your features when choosing a style.
  • Experiment with different products. Styling products can help to enhance the volume and texture of your hair, so don't be afraid to try different sprays, pomades, and mousses.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut volume into my hair without going to a salon?

A: Yes, you can cut volume into your hair at home. However, it's important to be careful and take your time to ensure that you get the best results.

Q: How often should I cut my hair to maintain volume?

A: It depends on your hair type and the style you choose. Generally, you should aim to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and volume.

Q: What products can I use to enhance volume in my hair?

A: There are several products that can help to enhance volume in your hair, including volumizing sprays, mousses, and pomades. Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type.

Hair Care

To keep your short hair looking its best, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips to help you maintain your new style:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.
  • Visit a salon regularly to have your hair trimmed and shaped.

Cutting volume into short hair can be a great way to create a stylish and manageable look. By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a look that flatters your features and enhances your natural beauty.
