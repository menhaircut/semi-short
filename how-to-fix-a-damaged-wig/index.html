How To Fix A Damaged Wig Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 17, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Wigs are a great way to change up your look without too much commitment. However, over time, wigs can become damaged due to regular wear and tear, improper storage, or styling techniques. Fortunately, there are ways to fix a damaged wig and restore it to its former glory. In this article, we will go over some tips and tricks for fixing a damaged wig.

How to

Step 1: Identify the Damage

Before you can fix a damaged wig, you need to identify what is wrong with it. Look for any split ends, tangles, or bald spots. If the wig is synthetic, you may also notice frizzing or melting. Once you have identified the damage, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Detangle

If your wig is tangled, try to gently detangle it with a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. Be careful not to tug too hard, as this can cause further damage.

Step 3: Trim

If your wig has split ends, you may need to trim them off. Use a pair of sharp scissors and cut the damaged ends off. Be careful not to cut too much hair, as this can make the wig look uneven.

Step 4: Repair Bald Spots

If your wig has bald spots, you can use a wig cap or hair extensions to cover them up. Alternatively, you can use a fabric marker or sharpie to color in the bald spots. Be careful not to use too much ink, as this can make the wig look unnatural.

Step 5: Fix Frizzing or Melting

If your wig is synthetic and has frizzing or melting, you may be able to fix it with a steamer or hot water. Hold the wig over steam or dip it in hot water for a few seconds, then comb it out gently. Be careful not to burn yourself or damage the wig further.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for fixing a damaged wig:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to prevent tangling and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on synthetic wigs, as they can melt.
  • Use a wig conditioner or detangler to keep your wig soft and tangle-free.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your wig, as excessive force can cause damage.

FAQ

How often should I wash my wig?

It depends on how often you wear your wig and how much product you use on it. Generally, you should wash your wig every 6-8 wears.

Can I use regular shampoo on my wig?

No, you should use a wig shampoo or a mild shampoo specifically designed for wigs. Regular shampoo can damage the hair fibers.

Hair Care

Taking care of your wig is important to prevent damage and prolong its lifespan. Here are some tips for wig care:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to prevent tangling and damage.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to heat or direct sunlight, as this can cause fading and damage.
  • Use a wig conditioner or detangler to keep your wig soft and tangle-free.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your wig, as excessive force can cause damage.

With these tips and tricks, you can fix a damaged wig and keep it looking great for longer. Remember to take care of your wig to prevent damage and prolong its lifespan.
