How To Cut Your Own Long Side Swept Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of spending money and time at the salon for a simple side swept bang trim? Cutting your own long side swept bangs may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily at home. In this article, we will guide you through the process and provide you with tips to achieve the perfect cut every time.

How to Cut Your Own Long Side Swept Bangs

Before we get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips

Now, let's get started with the step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It's important to cut your bangs when they are dry, so make sure your hair is completely dry before proceeding.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, section off the hair you want to cut into a triangle shape. The top of the triangle should be at the crown of your head, and the bottom should be at the tip of your nose. Use sectioning clips to hold the rest of your hair back.

Step 3: Comb Your Bangs

Using a comb, comb your bangs straight down. Make sure they are evenly distributed and there are no bumps or tangles.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Using sharp scissors, start cutting your bangs at the outer corner. Cut at a slight angle, following the shape of your face. Take small snips, and make sure you are cutting slowly and carefully. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Step 5: Check for Evenness

Once you've cut one side, check for evenness by comparing it to the other side. Make any necessary adjustments until both sides are the same length and shape.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Use a blow dryer and a round brush to style your bangs. Brush them to the side and use the blow dryer to create volume and shape.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Long Side Swept Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own long side swept bangs:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length.
  • Cut your bangs longer than you think you want them, as they will shorten up once they bounce back into place.
  • Don't cut your bangs straight across, as this can create a harsh look.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from different angles.

FAQs

Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you may want to cut your bangs a little longer to compensate for this.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have them fix it. It's better to have a professional fix a mistake than to try to correct it yourself and make it worse.

Hair Care

Now that you've cut your own long side swept bangs, it's important to take care of them. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. Finally, get regular trims to keep your bangs looking fresh and healthy.

Cutting your own long side swept bangs can be a fun and empowering experience. With the right tools, technique, and tips, you can achieve the perfect cut every time. So go ahead, give it a try!
