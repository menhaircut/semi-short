How To Tell If A Haircut Suits You A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Oct 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, it's important to choose a cut that flatters your face shape, skin tone, and personal style. But how can you tell if a haircut suits you? In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know to make an informed decision about your next hairstyle.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in finding a haircut that suits you is to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a style that accentuates your best features and balances out any asymmetry. The following are the most common face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond
  • Pear

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and look in the mirror. Note the widest part of your face, the shape of your jawline, and the length of your forehead. You may also want to use a measuring tape to get more precise measurements.

Once you've determined your face shape, do some research to find hairstyles that flatter your specific shape. For example, round faces benefit from hairstyles that add height and angles, while oval faces can pull off almost any style.

Considering Your Hair Type

In addition to your face shape, your hair type should also play a role in your haircut decision. If you have fine or thin hair, for example, you may want to avoid heavy layers that can make your hair look even thinner. If you have curly hair, you may want to choose a style that works with your natural texture instead of fighting against it.

Consider your hair's thickness, texture, and natural movement when choosing a haircut. You may also want to talk to your stylist about the best products and tools to use for your specific hair type.

Matching Your Skin Tone

Your skin tone can also play a role in how well a haircut suits you. Some hair colors and styles can wash out certain skin tones, while others can accentuate them. Generally speaking, warm skin tones look best with warm hair colors like golden blonde or auburn, while cool skin tones look best with cool hair colors like platinum blonde or ash brown.

If you're not sure what your skin tone is or which hair colors would work best for you, talk to your stylist or try on different wigs or hair extensions to see how different shades look against your skin.

Reflecting Your Personal Style

Your personal style should also come into play when choosing a haircut. Are you someone who likes to stand out with bold, edgy styles, or do you prefer classic, timeless looks? Do you want a low-maintenance style that you can quickly style and go, or are you willing to put in more effort for a high-maintenance look?

Consider your lifestyle, wardrobe, and personal preferences when choosing a haircut. You want a style that not only looks good on you but also makes you feel confident and comfortable in your own skin.

Trying on the Look

If you're still unsure whether a haircut will suit you, try on the look before committing to it. You can do this by using a virtual hairstyle app or trying on a wig or hair extensions in the same style. This will give you a good idea of what the style will look like on you without having to make a permanent change.

Keep in mind that the virtual app or wig may not be an exact match for your face shape, skin tone, or hair type, so use this method as a general guide rather than a guarantee.

Asking a Pro

If you're still unsure about a haircut after doing your research and trying on the look, don't hesitate to ask a professional. Your stylist can provide valuable insight into what styles would work best for your face shape, hair type, and personal style. They can also help you make adjustments to a style to make it more flattering for you.

Bring in pictures of hairstyles you like, ask for their professional opinion, and be open to their suggestions. Remember, they're the experts, and they want you to look and feel your best.

Considering Maintenance

When choosing a haircut, it's important to consider the amount of maintenance it will require. Some styles may require frequent trims, daily styling, or special products to maintain their shape and texture. Others may be more low-maintenance and allow you to simply wash and go.

Choose a style that fits your lifestyle and schedule. If you're someone who's always on the go, a low-maintenance style may be best for you. If you enjoy taking the time to style your hair and experiment with different looks, a higher-maintenance style may be more your speed.

Feeling Confident

Ultimately, the most important factor in determining whether a haircut suits you is how it makes you feel. If you feel confident, comfortable, and happy with your new style, then it's a success. If you're constantly worrying about how you look or feeling self-conscious, then it may not be the right fit.

Choose a haircut that reflects your personality, flatters your features, and makes you feel like the best version of yourself. Confidence is key.

Avoiding Common Mistakes

When choosing a haircut, there are a few common mistakes to avoid. These include:

  • Choosing a style that doesn't flatter your face shape
  • Going for a style that requires more maintenance than you're willing to do
  • Ignoring your hair type and texture
  • Choosing a style that's too trendy or doesn't reflect your personal style
  • Not communicating clearly with your stylist about what you want

Avoid these mistakes by doing your research, being honest with your stylist, and prioritizing your personal preferences over trends or outside opinions.

FAQs

Q: How do I know if a short haircut will suit me?

A: Short haircuts can be tricky, but the same principles apply. Determine your face shape and hair type, consider your personal style, and try on the look before committing to it. A professional stylist can also provide valuable advice on whether a short haircut would suit you.

Q: What if my hair is damaged or unhealthy?

A: If your hair is damaged or unhealthy, it may be best to focus on improving its condition before making any drastic changes. A stylist can recommend products and treatments to help repair and nourish your hair.

Q: How often should I get my hair cut?

A: The frequency of haircuts depends on your hair type, style, and personal preference. Generally speaking, a trim every 6-8 weeks can help maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I change my mind about a haircut?

A: Of course! Hair grows back, and if you're not happy with your new style, you can always change it. Just be honest with your stylist and communicate clearly about what you want.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to confidently choose a haircut that suits you and makes you feel amazing. Remember to prioritize your personal preferences and don't be afraid to ask for professional advice. Happy styling!
