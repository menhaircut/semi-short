How To Draw Facial Hair With Makeup A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add some facial hair to your look, but aren't quite ready to commit to growing your own, you can achieve the desired effect with makeup. Whether you're going for a full beard, a mustache, or just some scruff, this guide will walk you through the steps to create the look you want.

Description

Drawing facial hair with makeup can be a fun and creative way to change up your look. With a few basic tools and some practice, you can create a variety of styles and looks. From a full beard to a soul patch, there are many options to choose from. The key is to experiment with different techniques and find what works best for you.

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few supplies. You'll need a makeup sponge, a small brush, and some makeup that matches your hair color. You may also want to use a stencil or template to help guide your application.

Step 2: Prep Your Skin

Start by cleaning and moisturizing your skin. This will help the makeup go on smoothly and stay in place. If you have any facial hair already, make sure it is trimmed and neat.

Step 3: Apply the Base

Using your makeup sponge or brush, apply a thin layer of makeup to the area where you want the facial hair to be. Start with a light touch and build up the color gradually.

Step 4: Define the Shape

Using your brush, start to define the shape of the facial hair. You can use a stencil or template to help guide your application, or you can freehand it for a more natural look. Be sure to pay attention to the direction of the hair growth and use light, feathery strokes to create a realistic texture.

Step 5: Add Details

Once you have the basic shape in place, you can start to add details like highlights and shadows to create depth and dimension. Use a darker shade of makeup to create shadows where the hair would naturally be thicker, and a lighter shade to add highlights where the hair would catch the light.

Step 6: Blend

Use a clean brush or sponge to blend the makeup for a more natural look. This will help soften any harsh lines and create a seamless transition between the makeup and your skin.

Step 7: Set the Makeup

Once you're happy with the look, use a setting spray or powder to help the makeup stay in place. This will also help prevent smudging or rubbing off throughout the day.

Tips

  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be afraid to experiment with different techniques and styles until you find what works best for you.
  • Use a light touch when applying the makeup, and build up the color gradually for a more natural look.
  • Pay attention to the direction of the hair growth and use light, feathery strokes to create a realistic texture.
  • If you're using a stencil or template, be sure to hold it firmly in place to prevent smudging or shifting during application.
  • Use a setting spray or powder to help the makeup stay in place throughout the day.

FAQ

Q: Will the makeup rub off or smudge?

A: If you use a setting spray or powder to set the makeup, it should stay in place throughout the day. However, it's always a good idea to avoid touching your face or rubbing the area where the makeup is applied.

Q: Can I use regular makeup for this?

A: Yes, you can use regular makeup as long as it matches your hair color and is safe for use on your skin.

Q: How do I remove the makeup?

A: You can remove the makeup with a gentle cleanser or makeup remover. Be sure to follow your usual skincare routine to keep your skin healthy and hydrated.

Hair Care

If you're using makeup to create facial hair, it's important to take care of your skin and hair to keep them healthy and looking their best. Be sure to moisturize regularly and keep your facial hair trimmed and neat. You can also use a beard oil or balm to keep your hair soft and conditioned.

With these tips and techniques, you can create a variety of facial hair styles using makeup. Whether you're going for a rugged look or just want to switch things up, this is a fun and creative way to experiment with your appearance.
