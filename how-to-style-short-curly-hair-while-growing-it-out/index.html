How To Style Short Curly Hair While Growing It Out

Written by Christine Oct 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have short curly hair, you might be wondering how to style it while growing it out. It can be a challenging process, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a beautiful look that will help you feel confident and stylish. In this blog post, we'll cover the best ways to style short curly hair while growing it out, as well as hair care tips to keep your locks healthy and strong.

Description

Short curly hair is a popular choice for many women, but it can be difficult to maintain as it grows out. As your hair gets longer, it may become harder to manage, and you may find yourself struggling to find a style that works for you. However, with the right styling techniques and products, you can keep your hair looking great while it grows out.

How To

1. Use the Right Products

The first step to styling short curly hair while growing it out is to use the right products. Look for products that are designed specifically for curly hair, as these will help to define your curls and keep them looking great. You should also use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy, and a styling gel or mousse to help hold your curls in place.

2. Experiment with Different Styles

As your hair grows out, you may need to experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. Try parting your hair in different ways, or adding bangs to create a new look. You can also try braiding your hair or using a headband or hair clips to add some variety to your style.

3. Use Heat Styling Tools Sparingly

While heat styling tools can be useful for creating a variety of styles, they can also damage your hair if used too often. Try to use heat styling tools sparingly, and always use a heat protectant spray before using them. You should also avoid using them on wet hair, as this can cause even more damage.

Step by Step

1. Start with Clean Hair

The first step to styling short curly hair while growing it out is to start with clean hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to wash your hair, and then gently pat it dry with a towel.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Next, apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it hydrated and healthy. Be sure to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a comb.

3. Use a Styling Gel or Mousse

To help define your curls and keep them in place, use a styling gel or mousse. Be sure to apply it evenly throughout your hair, using your fingers or a comb to distribute it.

4. Experiment with Different Styles

As your hair grows out, try experimenting with different styles to find what works best for you. You can try parting your hair in different ways, adding bangs, or using hair clips or a headband to create a new look.

Tips

1. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Curly hair is more fragile than straight hair, so it's important to be gentle when styling it. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle it.

2. Avoid Using Harsh Chemicals

Harsh chemicals can damage your hair and make it harder to manage. Try to avoid using products that contain sulfates or other harsh chemicals, and opt for natural or organic products instead.

3. Get Regular Trims

Even though you're growing your hair out, it's still important to get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

FAQ

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair while growing it out?

A: While you can straighten your curly hair, it's important to be careful not to damage it. Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools, and try to limit the amount of heat you use on your hair.

Q: How can I keep my curly hair looking healthy?

A: To keep your curly hair looking healthy, be sure to use a leave-in conditioner, avoid harsh chemicals, and get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Q: Can I dye my curly hair while growing it out?

A: Yes, you can dye your curly hair while growing it out, but be sure to use a gentle dye that won't damage your hair. You should also use a deep conditioner to keep your hair hydrated after dyeing it.

Hair Care

To keep your short curly hair looking great while growing it out, it's important to take good care of it. Be sure to wash your hair regularly, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner. You should also use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your hair. Finally, be sure to get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.
