How To Evenly Cut Afro Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of uneven haircuts that leave your afro hair looking less than perfect? Look no further, as we have the ultimate guide on how to evenly cut afro hair. From the tools you need to the techniques you should use, we have got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools Needed
  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning the Hair
  4. Cutting the Hair
  5. Blending the Hair

Tools Needed

Before you start cutting your afro hair, you will need to gather some essential tools. These include:

  • Clippers with guards of different sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Cape or towel to protect your clothes

Preparation

Before cutting your hair, it is essential to prepare it properly. Here is how to do it:

  1. Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner and let it air dry.
  2. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  3. Trim any split ends or knots with scissors.

Sectioning the Hair

Now that your hair is prepared, it is time to section it. This will make it easier to cut it evenly. Here is how you can section your hair:

  1. Divide your hair into four sections: front, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to secure each section.
  2. Starting with the back section, divide it into smaller subsections, depending on the thickness of your hair.
  3. Clip each subsection so that it is out of the way while you work on the other sections.

Cutting the Hair

Now it is time to cut your hair. Follow these steps:

  1. Start with the longest guard on your clippers and work your way down to the shortest.
  2. Use your clippers to cut the back section first, moving from the nape of your neck to the crown of your head.
  3. Repeat this process with the side sections, cutting from the bottom up.
  4. Finally, move to the front section and cut it according to your desired length.

Blending the Hair

Now that your hair is cut, it is time to blend the different sections together. Here is how you can do it:

  1. Use scissors to trim any uneven ends or strays.
  2. Use your clippers to blend the sections together by using a higher guard and moving up from the bottom of the hair to the top.
  3. Blend the sections together until you achieve a seamless look.

Clean Up

After you have finished cutting your hair, it is time to clean up. Here is what you should do:

  1. Use a mirror to check for any missed spots or uneven areas.
  2. Brush off any loose hair from your clothes and skin.
  3. Wash your hair with water to remove any loose hair.

Tips for Evenly Cutting Afro Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve an even haircut:

  • Use clippers with guards of different sizes to achieve varying lengths.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Take your time and be patient. Rushing through the process may result in an uneven cut.

FAQs

How often should I cut my afro hair?

You should cut your afro hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my afro hair at home?

Yes, you can cut your afro hair at home if you have the right tools and follow the proper techniques.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it is essential to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools that can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet while sleeping to prevent breakage.

Now that you know how to evenly cut afro hair, you can achieve a perfect haircut at home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the proper techniques to achieve the best results.
