How To Cut Front Layers In Long Hair Yourself

Written by Oliver Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a new way to freshen up your long hair, adding front layers is a perfect way to do it. However, going to a salon to get them done can be quite expensive. So why not learn how to cut front layers in long hair yourself? Not only will it save you money, but it's also a fun way to experiment with your hair.

Description

Front layers are a type of haircut where the hair is cut shorter in the front, framing the face. This style is perfect for those with long hair who want to add some movement and dimension to their locks. It's an easy way to update your look without sacrificing the length of your hair.

How to Cut Front Layers in Long Hair Yourself

If you're ready to take on the challenge of cutting front layers in your long hair, follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools at hand. You'll need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut the layers.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the hair at the crown of your head and clip it up. Then, create a part down the middle of your head, dividing your hair into two sections. Clip each section to the side of your head.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with one section, take a small piece of hair in the front and hold it between your fingers. Angle your scissors slightly and make a small cut at the ends of the hair. Repeat this process, gradually cutting more hair as you move back. Make sure you're cutting at an angle to create a tapered look.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you're finished with one side, repeat the same process on the other side. Make sure both sides are even.

Step 6: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting, take a look at your hair in the mirror. If you notice any unevenness, make small adjustments until you're happy with the result.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting front layers in long hair yourself:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Make sure you're cutting at an angle to create a tapered look.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid making a mistake.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting front layers in long hair yourself:

Can I cut front layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut front layers in curly hair. Just make sure to cut them while your hair is dry so you can see the natural curl pattern.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always make small adjustments and try again. If you're really unhappy with the result, you can always go to a salon to get it fixed.

How often should I cut my front layers?

It's recommended to cut your front layers every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh and maintained.

Hair Care

After you've cut your front layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Use a deep conditioner once a week and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Also, try to avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair and opt for natural products instead.
