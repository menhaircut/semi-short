How To Make Your Hair Puffy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of flat, lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and bounce to your locks? If so, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll show you how to make your hair puffy using a few simple techniques and styling products. Read on to learn more!

Table of Contents

  • Section 2: How to Blow Dry Your Hair
  • Section 3: How to Use Rollers
  • Section 4: How to Tease Your Hair

  • Section 7: Frequently Asked Questions

Section 1: How to Prep Your Hair

The first step in making your hair puffy is to prep it properly. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products will help to add body and fullness to your hair. After washing, gently towel dry your hair and comb it through to remove any tangles.

Next, apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your hair. These products will help to give your hair added lift and hold. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the roots.

Finally, flip your head upside down and blow dry your hair until it's about 80% dry. This will help to create volume and lift at the roots.

Section 2: How to Blow Dry Your Hair

Once your hair is prepped, it's time to blow dry! Start by sectioning your hair into manageable sections. Use a round brush to lift and smooth your hair as you blow dry. Focus on the roots, lifting them up with the brush as you blow dry to create volume.

Once your hair is completely dry, use a cool shot of air to set the style. This will help to keep your hair puffy and voluminous all day long.

Section 3: How to Use Rollers

If you want to take your hair to the next level, try using rollers! Start by sectioning your hair and rolling it up in large, velcro rollers. Leave the rollers in for at least 20 minutes, or until your hair is completely dry.

Remove the rollers and gently brush through your hair with your fingers. This will help to create soft, bouncy curls with plenty of volume.

Section 4: How to Tease Your Hair

If you want to add even more volume to your hair, try teasing it! Take a small section of hair at the crown of your head and backcomb it using a teasing brush. Repeat this process with several sections of hair, focusing on the crown and the sides of your head.

Once you're finished teasing, gently smooth the top layer of your hair with a brush or your fingers. This will help to create a smooth, polished look while still maintaining plenty of volume.

Section 5: How to Use Products

In addition to mousse and spray, there are a few other products you can use to make your hair puffy. Dry shampoo is great for adding texture and volume to your hair, while hairspray can help to set your style and keep it in place all day long.

When using products, be sure to apply them evenly and avoid using too much. Too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Section 6: Tips for Maintaining Puffy Hair

To keep your hair looking puffy and voluminous, there are a few things you can do. First, avoid using heavy conditioners or styling products that can weigh down your hair. Instead, opt for lightweight, volumizing products that will add lift and body.

You should also avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can flatten your style and make it look limp. Instead, use your fingers to gently tousle your hair and create volume.

Section 7: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How can I make my hair puffy without heat?

A: If you prefer not to use heat styling tools, try using foam rollers or a volumizing mousse to create volume and lift.

Q: How often should I wash my hair to maintain puffy hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people can go 2-3 days between washes. Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dry, flat hair.

Q: Can I make my hair puffy if I have short hair?

A: Yes! Short hair can be puffy and voluminous too. Try using a volumizing mousse or spray, and use a round brush to lift and smooth your hair as you blow dry.

Section 8: Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and strong, there are a few things you can do. First, avoid using hot tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair and make it brittle. Instead, try air drying your hair or using low heat settings.

You should also avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle. Instead, opt for gentle, natural products that will nourish your hair and keep it looking healthy and shiny.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily make your hair puffy and voluminous. Whether you have long or short hair, these styling tricks will help you achieve the look you want. So go ahead and give it a try - your hair will thank you!
