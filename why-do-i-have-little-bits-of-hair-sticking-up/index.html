Why Do I Have Little Bits Of Hair Sticking Up

Written by Aprilia Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever looked in the mirror and noticed little bits of hair sticking up? It can be frustrating, especially when you're trying to style your hair. In this article, we'll discuss why you have little bits of hair sticking up and what you can do about it.

Description

Little bits of hair sticking up, also known as flyaways, are small strands of hair that don't lay flat. They can be caused by a variety of factors, such as humidity, static electricity, and damaged hair. Flyaways can be more noticeable if you have thin or fine hair.

How to

If you're wondering how to get rid of flyaways, there are a few things you can try:

Use a hairspray

Hairspray can help keep your hair in place and prevent flyaways. Spray a small amount onto your hairbrush and then run it through your hair.

Apply a styling product

A styling product, such as a gel or pomade, can help keep your hair in place. Apply a small amount to your hands and then run it through your hair, focusing on the areas where you have flyaways.

Use a toothbrush

A toothbrush can be a handy tool for taming flyaways. Spray a small amount of hairspray onto the toothbrush and then gently brush your hair in the areas where you have flyaways.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting rid of flyaways:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help remove any buildup that could be causing your flyaways.

Step 2: Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product, such as a gel or pomade, to your hair. Start by applying a small amount to your hands and then work it through your hair, focusing on the areas where you have flyaways.

Step 3: Blow dry your hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush. This will help smooth your hair and prevent flyaways.

Step 4: Use a hairspray

Once your hair is dry, spray a small amount of hairspray onto your hairbrush and then run it through your hair. This will help keep your hair in place and prevent flyaways.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help prevent flyaways:

Use a silk pillowcase

A silk pillowcase can help prevent flyaways by reducing friction between your hair and the pillowcase.

Use a humidifier

A humidifier can help add moisture to the air, which can help prevent flyaways caused by dry air.

Trim your hair regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends, which can cause flyaways.

FAQ

Q: Can flyaways be a sign of damaged hair?

A: Yes, flyaways can be a sign of damaged hair. If you have a lot of flyaways, it's a good idea to assess the health of your hair.

Q: Can I prevent flyaways?

A: While you can't completely prevent flyaways, there are things you can do to minimize them. Using a styling product, blow drying your hair, and using a hairspray can all help keep flyaways under control.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important if you want to prevent flyaways. Here are a few tips:

Don't wash your hair too often

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and damaged.

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Using a gentle shampoo and conditioner can help prevent damage to your hair.

Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can cause damage to your hair, which can lead to flyaways. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.

In conclusion, flyaways can be frustrating, but there are things you can do to keep them under control. Using a hairspray, applying a styling product, and using a toothbrush can all help tame flyaways. Taking care of your hair by washing it less often, using a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding heat styling can also help prevent flyaways.
