How To Cut Layers Into Long Hair Yourself

Written by Luffy Oct 07, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to add some volume and texture to your long hair, cutting layers is the perfect solution. Not only does it add movement and body, but it can also help to frame your face in a flattering way. While it may seem daunting to cut your own hair, with the right tools and techniques, you can easily achieve the desired look. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut layers into long hair yourself.

How to Cut Layers into Long Hair Yourself

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a sharp pair of scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It’s best to use professional hair cutting scissors, as they will provide a clean cut and prevent damage to your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly before cutting. This will help you to see the natural fall of your hair and ensure that you’re not cutting too much off. Make sure your hair is completely dry, as wet hair tends to stretch and you may end up cutting more than you intended.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts – two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin by cutting the bottom layer. Take a small section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut into the hair at a slight angle, starting from the middle of the hair and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Move on to the Middle Layer

Once you have cut the bottom layer, release the middle layer and repeat the cutting process. Make sure you are cutting at a slight angle, as this will create a natural-looking layered effect. Remember to cut each side evenly.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, release the top layer and repeat the cutting process. This layer should be the shortest, as it will add volume and texture to your hair. Cut the top layer at a slightly steeper angle than the other layers to create a graduated effect.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you have finished cutting all the layers, use the scissors to blend them together. This will create a seamless look and prevent any harsh lines.

Tips for Cutting Layers into Long Hair Yourself

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut small amounts at a time, as it’s easier to cut more if needed than to try and fix a mistake.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure that the layers are even.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so don’t rush the process.
  • Be patient. Cutting your own hair takes practice, so don’t expect perfection on your first try.

FAQs

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can cut layers into curly hair. However, it’s important to remember that curly hair tends to bounce up once it’s dry, so it’s best to cut less hair than you think you need.

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers into Long Hair Yourself

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. You can also use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.

Now that you know how to cut layers into long hair yourself, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, and don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With practice, you’ll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time!
