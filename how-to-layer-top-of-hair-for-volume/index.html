How To Layer Top Of Hair For Volume

Written by William Jul 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to add volume to your hair, layering is one of the most effective techniques. Hair layering creates an illusion of fuller hair by adding texture and movement. In this article, we'll show you how to layer top of hair for volume in a few simple steps.

Description

Layering top of hair for volume can be done in several ways, depending on your hair length and texture. This technique is especially useful for those with fine or thin hair as it helps to add body and fullness to the hair. Layering can also help to create a more flattering shape for your face, making it look slimmer and more defined.

How to Layer Top of Hair for Volume

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Once your hair is clean, towel dry it and apply a volumizing mousse to the roots. Blow dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to create volume at the roots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into three sections - top, middle, and bottom. Clip the bottom section away and focus on the top section.

Step 3: Create a Guide

Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold it between your fingers, about an inch away from your scalp. This will be your guide for the rest of the layers.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Using sharp scissors, cut the hair above your fingers in a straight line. This will be the first layer of your hair.

Step 5: Repeat

Take another section of hair next to the first layer and use the previous layer as a guide to cut the next layer. Continue this process until you've cut all the layers on the top section of your hair.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, blend them together by combing through your hair with a wide-tooth comb. This will help to create a natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your hair for volume:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, start with small sections
  • Blend the layers together to create a natural look
  • Use volumizing products to enhance the effect of layers
  • Consult a hairstylist if you're unsure about how to layer your hair

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering top of hair for volume:

Can layering damage my hair?

Layering done by a professional hairstylist using the right technique and tools should not damage your hair. However, if you try to layer your hair at home without proper knowledge, you may end up with uneven or over-layered hair.

How often should I get my hair layered?

It depends on your hair length and texture, but generally, it's recommended to get your hair layered every six to eight weeks to maintain the shape and fullness.

Can layering make my hair look thinner?

If not done properly, layering can make your hair look thinner. It's important to consult a hairstylist who understands your hair type and face shape to create a flattering layered look.

Hair Care

After layering your hair for volume, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain its health and fullness. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools too often as they can damage your hair
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers

Now that you know how to layer top of hair for volume, it's time to try it out and enjoy your fuller, bouncier hair!
