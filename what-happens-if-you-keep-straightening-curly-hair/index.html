What Happens If You Keep Straightening Curly Hair

Written by Robby Nov 02, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. It can look absolutely gorgeous when styled properly, but it can also be difficult to manage. Many people with curly hair choose to straighten it to make it more manageable, but what happens if you keep straightening curly hair?

  2. How to Straighten Curly Hair

Description

Straightening curly hair can be a great way to change up your look, but it can also cause damage to your hair if done too frequently. When you straighten curly hair, you are essentially changing the natural structure of your hair. Curly hair has a natural shape and texture that is different from straight hair. When you straighten curly hair, you are applying heat to the hair shaft, which can cause damage over time.

Repeatedly straightening curly hair can cause the hair to become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. It can also lead to split ends, flyaways, and frizz. In extreme cases, it can even cause permanent damage to the hair follicles, which can result in hair loss.

How to Straighten Curly Hair

Straightening curly hair can be done using a variety of methods, including using a flat iron, blow dryer, or chemical relaxer. Regardless of the method used, it is important to take steps to prevent damage to the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to straighten curly hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to prevent your hair from becoming too dry.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help to protect your hair from the heat of the straightening tool.
  3. Section your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to straighten your hair and ensure that all of your hair is straightened evenly.
  4. Using a flat iron or blow dryer, work your way through each section of your hair, taking care not to apply too much heat to any one section.
  5. Once you have straightened all of your hair, apply a light serum or oil to your hair to help keep it smooth and shiny.

Tips for Straightening Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you straighten curly hair without causing damage:

  • Avoid straightening your hair every day. Give your hair a break and only straighten it once or twice a week.
  • Use a low heat setting on your flat iron or blow dryer. High heat can cause damage to your hair.
  • Don't leave the straightening tool in one spot for too long. Keep the tool moving to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about straightening curly hair:

Is it safe to straighten curly hair?

Straightening curly hair can be safe if done properly. It is important to use a heat protectant spray or serum and to avoid applying too much heat to your hair.

Can straightening curly hair cause damage?

Yes, repeatedly straightening curly hair can cause damage to the hair, including dryness, brittleness, split ends, and hair loss.

What is the best way to straighten curly hair?

The best way to straighten curly hair is to use a low heat setting on a flat iron or blow dryer and to use a heat protectant spray or serum. It is also important to give your hair a break and only straighten it once or twice a week.

Hair Care for Straightened Curly Hair

After straightening your curly hair, it is important to take steps to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a light serum or oil to keep your hair smooth and shiny.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

Conclusion

Straightening curly hair can be a great way to change up your look, but it is important to take steps to prevent damage to your hair. By using a low heat setting on your straightening tool, using a heat protectant spray or serum, and giving your hair a break from heat styling, you can keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
