How To Curl Your Hair 1950S Style

Written by Robby Oct 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to achieve a glamorous, vintage look, then curling your hair 1950s style is the perfect choice. The 1950s era was all about big, voluminous curls that exuded femininity and elegance. In this blog post, we'll take you through a step-by-step guide on how to curl your hair 1950s style, along with some helpful tips and FAQs.

How to Curl Your Hair 1950s Style

Before we dive into the curling process, let's go over a few things you'll need:

  • Heat protectant spray
  • Curling iron or hot rollers
  • Sectioning clips
  • Hairspray

Now that you have your tools, let's get started:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual. Once it's towel-dried, apply a heat protectant spray throughout your hair to protect it from damage.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your sectioning clips, divide your hair into small sections. The smaller the section, the tighter the curl. For a more natural look, use larger sections.

Step 3: Curl Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around your curling iron or hot roller. Hold it for about 10 seconds, then release. Continue curling each section until your entire head is curled.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Once your hair is cooled down, gently brush through your curls using a wide-tooth comb. Finish off your look by spraying your hair with a light hold hairspray.

Tips for Curling Your Hair 1950s Style

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect 1950s curls:

  • For tighter curls, use a smaller curling iron or hot roller.
  • For looser curls, use a larger curling iron or hot roller.
  • For added volume, tease the roots of your hair before curling.
  • For a more natural look, curl your hair away from your face.

FAQs About Curling Your Hair 1950s Style

Q: Can I use a curling wand instead of a curling iron?

A: Yes, you can use a curling wand instead of a curling iron. However, keep in mind that a curling wand will create a different type of curl than a curling iron.

Q: How long will my curls last?

A: Your curls should last for at least a few hours, if not all day. To help them last longer, use a stronger hold hairspray and avoid touching your hair too much.

Q: Can I curl my hair 1950s style if I have short hair?

A: Yes, you can still achieve 1950s curls with short hair. Use a smaller curling iron or hot roller and focus on curling the ends of your hair.

Hair Care Tips for 1950s Curls

To keep your curls looking their best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and steps, you'll be able to achieve beautiful, 1950s-style curls that will have you feeling like a vintage queen in no time!
