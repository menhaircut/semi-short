How To Make Curly Hair Less Curly And More Wavy

Written by Luffy Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that sometimes it can be tough to manage. While some people love their curls, others prefer a looser, more relaxed wave. If you're looking to make your curls less curly and more wavy, you're in luck. There are a few easy steps you can take to achieve the look you want.

Description

Curly hair is beautiful, but it can also be challenging to style. If you're looking for a change, making your curls less curly and more wavy is a great option. This look is more relaxed and less structured than curly hair, but still has plenty of movement and bounce.

How to

Here's how to make curly hair less curly and more wavy:

Step 1: Use a Shampoo and Conditioner for Curly Hair

Curly hair needs extra moisture to stay healthy and prevent frizz. Look for a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products will help to define your curls and keep them looking soft and shiny.

Step 2: Dry Your Hair with a Microfiber Towel

Traditional towels can rough up the cuticle of your hair, causing frizz and making your curls tighter. Instead, use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze out excess water. This will help to keep your curls defined and prevent them from becoming too curly.

Step 3: Apply a Curl-Defining Cream

After towel-drying your hair, apply a curl-defining cream to help tame your curls. Use a dime-sized amount and work it through your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help to loosen your curls and create a more relaxed wave.

Step 4: Use a Diffuser Attachment on Your Blow Dryer

If you're looking to speed up your drying time, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. This will help to distribute heat evenly and prevent your curls from becoming too tight. Use a low heat setting and gently scrunch your hair with the diffuser as you dry it.

Step 5: Finish with a Texturizing Spray

To create a more wavy look, finish off with a texturizing spray. This will help to add volume and create a tousled, beachy vibe. Spray it lightly all over your hair and scrunch it with your fingers to create waves.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help make your hair less curly and more wavy:

  • Don't brush your hair when it's dry. This can cause frizz and make your curls tighter.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like straighteners and curling irons. These can damage your hair and make your curls harder to manage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Try sleeping on a silk pillowcase to help prevent frizz and tangles.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making curly hair less curly and more wavy:

Can I make my hair less curly without using heat?

Yes, you can. Try using a curl-defining cream and air-drying your hair. This will help to loosen your curls and create a more relaxed wave.

Will my hair stay wavy all day?

It depends on your hair type and the products you use. If you have naturally curly hair, your hair may start to curl up again over time. To keep your waves looking their best, try using a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to add hold and volume.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently. If you have dry hair, you can wash it less often. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it healthy and looking its best.

Hair Care

Remember, taking care of your hair is key to achieving the look you want. Be gentle when styling, avoid harsh chemicals and heat, and nourish your hair with regular trims and deep conditioning treatments.

By following these steps, you can make your curly hair less curly and more wavy. Whether you're looking for a change or just want to switch up your style, this look is easy to achieve and looks great on everyone.
