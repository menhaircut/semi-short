How To Manage Natural Hair After Big Chop

Written by Oliver Jul 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have recently done the Big Chop and are now sporting a beautiful head of natural hair, congratulations! The Big Chop can be a liberating experience, however, it can also be overwhelming. Managing your natural hair can be a daunting task, especially if you are not used to it. In this article, we will provide you with helpful tips and advice on how to manage your natural hair after the Big Chop.

How to Manage Natural Hair After Big Chop

Managing natural hair after the Big Chop requires patience, care, and attention. Here are some steps to follow:

1. Learn About Your Hair Type

It is important to know your hair type as it will help you determine the best products and techniques to use. Natural hair can be categorized into four types: Type 1 (straight), Type 2 (wavy), Type 3 (curly), and Type 4 (coily). Each hair type requires different care and attention.

2. Develop a Hair Care Routine

Creating a hair care routine is important to ensure your hair stays healthy and strong. Your hair care routine should include washing, conditioning, and moisturizing your hair, as well as protective styling. You should also deep condition your hair at least once a month.

3. Use the Right Products

Using the right products is crucial for managing natural hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type. Avoid using products that contain harmful chemicals such as sulfates, parabens, and silicones.

4. Be Gentle with Your Hair

Natural hair is fragile and can easily break. Be gentle when handling your hair, especially when detangling. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle, and start from the ends and work your way up to the roots.

5. Protect Your Hair at Night

Protecting your hair at night is important to prevent breakage and tangling. Use a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to cover your hair while you sleep. You can also use a satin or silk pillowcase.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to manage your natural hair after the Big Chop:

Step 1: Shampoo Your Hair

Start by shampooing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. Massage your scalp gently with your fingertips to remove any dirt or buildup. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Condition Your Hair

Apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while the conditioner is still in. Rinse with cool water.

Step 3: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to add extra moisture. Use your fingers to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

You can style your hair in a variety of ways, including twist outs, braid outs, and wash and go's. Find a style that works for you and your hair type.

Tips for Managing Natural Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing natural hair:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage and breakage to your hair. Instead, opt for heatless styles such as twist outs and braid outs.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly will help to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to trim your hair every three to four months.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can be damaging to your hair, so it is important to protect it. Wear a hat or scarf when you are out in the sun, or use a leave-in conditioner that contains SPF.

4. Drink Plenty of Water

Drinking plenty of water is important for healthy hair growth. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water a day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I wash my natural hair?

A: It is recommended to wash your natural hair once a week, or every two weeks if your hair is particularly dry.

Q: How do I prevent shrinkage?

A: To prevent shrinkage, you can stretch your hair by doing a twist out or braid out, or by blow drying your hair on a low heat setting.

Q: How do I detangle my natural hair?

A: To detangle your natural hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. Start from the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips:

1. Use a Deep Conditioner

Use a deep conditioner at least once a month to add extra moisture to your hair. Leave the conditioner on for 30 minutes to an hour before rinsing out.

2. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

Use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove any buildup from your hair. This will help to prevent product buildup and keep your hair healthy.

3. Protect Your Hair When Swimming

Wear a swim cap or braid your hair when swimming to protect it from chlorine and salt water.

Managing your natural hair after the Big Chop can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, you can achieve healthy, strong, and beautiful hair. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products.
