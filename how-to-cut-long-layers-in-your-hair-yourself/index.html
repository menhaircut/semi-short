How To Cut Long Layers In Your Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 31, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle but don't want to spend a lot of money at the salon, cutting long layers in your hair yourself can be a great option. With a few key tools and some patience, you can achieve a layered look that adds texture and dimension to your hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut long layers in your hair yourself, including tips and tricks for a successful DIY haircut.

Tools You Will Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and damage to your hair. You can purchase hair cutting scissors online or at a beauty supply store.

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you have a clean canvas to work with. If you have curly or wavy hair, you may want to straighten it before cutting to make it easier to see the layers.

Next, brush or comb your hair so that it's free of tangles. This will make it easier to section and cut your hair evenly.

Parting Your Hair

Using your comb, part your hair down the middle. Then, take a section of hair from the crown of your head and clip it up. This will be the top layer of your haircut.

Next, take a section of hair from the bottom of your head and clip the rest of your hair up. This will be the first layer you cut. Repeat this process until you've clipped up all of the top layers of your hair.

Cutting Your Hair

Starting with the bottom layer, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Using your hair cutting scissors, make small snips to cut the hair at the desired length. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid cutting at an angle, which can create uneven layers.

Continue cutting the rest of your layers, working your way up from the bottom to the top. As you cut each layer, be sure to blend it with the previous layer to create a seamless transition between the two.

Checking Your Layers

Once you've finished cutting all of your layers, release your clipped-up hair and check to make sure that the layers are even and blended. If you notice any uneven areas, you can make small adjustments by trimming a little bit more off those sections.

Finishing Your Haircut

Once you're happy with your layers, it's time to finish your haircut. You can do this by adding additional texture with thinning shears or by using your hair cutting scissors to add face-framing layers. Be sure to take your time and make small adjustments, as it's easier to cut more hair than it is to add it back.

Tips for Cutting Long Layers in Your Hair Yourself

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to cutting more hair if needed.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut.
  • If you're nervous about cutting too much hair, start with a small section and work your way up.
  • Take breaks as needed to rest your arms and avoid cutting your hair too quickly.

FAQs

Can I cut long layers in my curly hair?

Yes, you can cut long layers in curly hair. However, it's important to remember that curly hair can be more difficult to cut evenly, so it's important to take your time and cut small sections at a time.

Can I cut long layers in my short hair?

Long layers are generally best suited for medium to long hair. If you have short hair, you may want to consider a different haircut style or consult with a professional stylist.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting your hair, be sure to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. This includes regularly washing and conditioning your hair, using a heat protectant when styling with heat tools, and getting regular trims to prevent split ends.

Conclusion

By following these steps and tips, you can easily cut long layers in your hair yourself. Whether you're looking to save money on salon appointments or just want to try something new, cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a beautiful new haircut in no time!
