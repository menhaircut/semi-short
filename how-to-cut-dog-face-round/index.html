How To Cut Dog Face Round A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to give your furry friend a new and adorable look, consider cutting their face round. This style is perfect for dogs with long, narrow faces and can make them appear more youthful and playful. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a round face cut for your dog, as well as provide some tips and tricks for maintaining their new hairstyle. Let's get started!

  • How to Cut Dog Face Round
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Dog Face Round

To cut your dog's face round, you'll need a few tools. These include:

  • Scissors
  • Clippers
  • Comb
  • Brush
  • Styptic powder (in case of bleeding)

It's important to note that if you're not comfortable cutting your dog's hair, it's always best to seek the help of a professional groomer.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's how to cut your dog's face round:

  1. Start by brushing your dog's hair to remove any tangles or mats.
  2. Use your clippers to trim the hair around your dog's ears and the sides of their face. You want to create a rounded look, so make sure to use a comb to guide your clippers in a circular motion.
  3. Next, use your scissors to trim the hair around your dog's eyes. Be careful not to get too close to their eyes, as this can cause irritation or injury.
  4. Trim the hair on the top of your dog's head in a rounded shape, leaving it slightly longer than the hair on the sides of their face.
  5. Finally, trim the hair on your dog's chin and jawline to complete the round look.

Remember to take breaks and give your dog plenty of treats and praise throughout the process to keep them calm and happy.

Tips for Maintaining Your Dog's Haircut

Once you've achieved the perfect round face cut for your dog, you'll want to maintain it to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining your dog's new hairstyle:

  • Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Trim their hair every few weeks to maintain the round shape.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner when bathing your dog to keep their coat healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid getting water or shampoo in your dog's eyes or ears.
  • Consider using a detangling spray to make brushing easier.

FAQs

Here are some commonly asked questions about cutting a dog's face round:

Is it safe to cut my dog's hair?

Yes, as long as you use the proper tools and techniques. If you're unsure, it's best to seek the help of a professional groomer.

What if I accidentally cut my dog's skin?

Apply styptic powder to the area to stop the bleeding. If the cut is deep or doesn't stop bleeding, seek the help of a veterinarian.

What if my dog doesn't like getting their hair cut?

Take breaks and give your dog plenty of treats and praise throughout the process. You can also try using a calming spray or playing music to help them relax.

Hair Care for Your Dog

Maintaining your dog's hair is an important part of their overall health and well-being. Here are some tips for keeping your dog's coat healthy:

  • Brush your dog's hair regularly to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Trim their hair every few weeks to prevent it from getting too long.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner when bathing your dog.
  • Avoid using human hair products on your dog.
  • Consider using a detangling spray or conditioner to make brushing easier.

Cutting your dog's face round can be a fun and rewarding experience for both you and your furry friend. By following these simple steps and maintaining their new hairstyle, you'll have a happy and stylish pup in no time!
