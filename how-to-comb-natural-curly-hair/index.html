How To Comb Natural Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Aug 25, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you know that styling and maintaining your curls can be a challenge. One of the most important aspects of curly hair care is learning how to properly comb your hair. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to comb natural curly hair, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you keep your curls looking their best.

Table of Contents

  • How to Comb Natural Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Combing Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Comb Natural Curly Hair

Combing natural curly hair requires a different approach than combing straight hair. Curly hair is more delicate and prone to tangling, so it’s important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your curls.

Tools You’ll Need

Before you start combing your curly hair, you’ll need to gather a few tools:

  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Spray bottle filled with water or a leave-in conditioner
  • Curl-defining product (optional)

When to Comb

It’s best to comb your curly hair when it’s wet or damp. This will make it easier to detangle your curls without causing damage. You can also comb your hair in the shower while you’re conditioning it.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to comb your natural curly hair:

  1. Start by wetting your hair with water or a leave-in conditioner. This will help to soften your curls and make them easier to detangle.
  2. Section your hair into 2-4 sections using hair clips or hair ties.
  3. Take one section of hair and apply a curl-defining product if desired.
  4. Begin combing your hair from the bottom up using a wide-tooth comb. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.
  5. If you encounter a tangle, use your fingers to gently separate the hair before continuing to comb.
  6. Once you’ve combed the first section of hair, repeat the process on the remaining sections.
  7. When you’re finished, gently shake your curls to restore their shape.

Tips for Combing Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when combing your natural curly hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. Brushes can cause breakage and frizz in curly hair.
  • Detangle your hair before you start combing. Use your fingers to separate any knots or tangles before you begin combing.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-defining product to make combing easier.
  • Be gentle. Curly hair is more delicate than straight hair, so it’s important to be gentle when combing to avoid damaging your curls.

FAQs

Can I comb my hair when it’s dry?

It’s best to comb your curly hair when it’s wet or damp. Combing dry hair can cause breakage and frizz.

How often should I comb my hair?

You should comb your hair as often as necessary to detangle it. This will depend on your hair type and how prone it is to tangling.

What type of comb should I use?

Use a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush. Brushes can cause breakage and frizz in curly hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your natural curly hair looking healthy and beautiful. Here are a few tips to help you care for your curls:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that’s specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your curls.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help preserve your curls’ natural shape.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to reduce frizz and breakage.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the right tools and techniques, you can keep your natural curly hair looking its best.
