How To Know If Bangs Look Good On You A Comprehensive Guide

Are you considering getting bangs but not sure if they'll suit you? Bangs can be a great way to switch up your look, but they're not for everyone. In this article, we'll walk you through how to know if bangs look good on you, step by step. We'll also provide tips on how to care for your new bangs and answer some common questions about this popular hairstyle. So, let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Know if Bangs Look Good on You
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Bangs

How to Know if Bangs Look Good on You

Bangs can be a flattering addition to many face shapes, but there are a few factors to consider before taking the plunge. Here are some things to keep in mind when deciding if bangs will suit you:

  • Your face shape
  • Your hair texture
  • Your lifestyle
  • Your personal style

Let's break these factors down further.

Face Shape

One of the most important things to consider when getting bangs is your face shape. Bangs can help balance out certain features and enhance others, depending on your face shape. Here are some general guidelines:

  • Oval: Oval faces can pull off most bang styles, so lucky you!
  • Round: Side-swept bangs that hit just above the cheekbones can help elongate round faces.
  • Square: Soft, wispy bangs that hit mid-forehead can help soften the strong angles of square faces.
  • Heart: Side-swept bangs that hit below the cheekbones can balance out heart-shaped faces.
  • Long: Blunt or choppy bangs that hit at or above the eyebrows can help shorten long faces.

Remember, these are just guidelines, and ultimately the best way to know if bangs will suit you is to try them out!

Hair Texture

Bangs can work well for most hair textures, but some may require more maintenance than others. Here are some things to consider:

  • Straight: Straight hair can make blunt bangs look sharp and sleek.
  • Wavy: Wavy hair can add texture and volume to bangs, but may require more styling.
  • Curly: Curly hair can make bangs look fuller and more voluminous, but may require more maintenance.

Lifestyle

Consider your lifestyle when deciding if bangs are right for you. Bangs can require more styling and upkeep than other hairstyles, so make sure you have the time and resources to maintain them. Additionally, if you're an active person or live in a humid climate, you may find bangs to be more trouble than they're worth.

Personal Style

Finally, consider your personal style when deciding if bangs will suit you. Bangs can give off different vibes depending on the style. For example:

  • Straight, blunt bangs can give off a more edgy, fashion-forward look.
  • Side-swept bangs can give off a more natural, effortless vibe.
  • Wispy, choppy bangs can give off a more bohemian, carefree feel.

Think about what style you're going for and choose bangs that fit that aesthetic.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you decide if bangs are right for you:

  1. Consider your face shape and hair texture to determine the best bang style for you.
  2. Look up pictures of people with similar face shapes and hair textures to see how they look with bangs.
  3. Try on different bang styles using virtual try-on tools or by using hair clips to mimic the look.
  4. Consult with a hairstylist to get their opinion on whether bangs will suit you.
  5. If you decide to get bangs, start with a longer length and gradually go shorter to make sure you like the look.

Tips for Maintaining Bangs

Once you've decided to get bangs, here are some tips for keeping them looking their best:

  • Wash your bangs frequently to prevent oil buildup.
  • Blow dry your bangs with a round brush to help them keep their shape.
  • Use dry shampoo to absorb oil and add volume between washes.
  • Trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them at the desired length.
  • Use a small amount of styling product, such as hair wax or pomade, to help control flyaways.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about bangs:

Can I cut my own bangs?

We recommend going to a professional hairstylist to get your bangs cut. However, if you do decide to cut your own bangs, make sure you have sharp scissors and take your time.

Will bangs make my forehead look bigger?

It depends on the style of bangs you choose. Side-swept or wispy bangs can actually make your forehead look smaller, while blunt bangs can make it appear larger.

What if I don't like my bangs?

If you don't like your bangs, don't worry! They'll grow out eventually. In the meantime, try styling them in different ways or using hair accessories to change up the look.

Hair Care for Bangs

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to keep your bangs looking their best:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to help nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to know if bangs look good on you, it's time to decide if you want to take the plunge! Remember, bangs can be a fun way to switch up your look, but they're not for everyone. Consider your face shape, hair texture, lifestyle, and personal style before making a decision. And if you do decide to get bangs, follow our tips for maintaining them and taking care of your hair. Good luck!
