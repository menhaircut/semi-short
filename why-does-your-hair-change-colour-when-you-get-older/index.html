Why Does Your Hair Change Colour When You Get Older

Written by William Jul 25, 2023 · 3 min read

As we age, many changes occur in our body. One of the most noticeable changes is the graying of our hair. This process is natural and happens to everyone, but have you ever wondered why your hair changes color when you get older? In this article, we’ll explore the science behind gray hair and what causes it.

What Causes Gray Hair?

Gray hair occurs when the pigment cells in our hair follicles stop producing melanin. Melanin is responsible for giving our hair its color, and when it decreases, our hair turns gray or white. There are a few reasons why this process happens:

Aging

As we age, our bodies produce less melanin. This decrease in melanin production is a natural part of the aging process and is the main reason why our hair turns gray.

Genetics

Genetics also play a role in when and how our hair turns gray. If your parents or grandparents went gray early in life, you might too.

Stress

Stress is known to cause premature graying of hair. When we’re under stress, our bodies produce more cortisone, which can interfere with melanin production.

How to Care for Gray Hair?

Gray hair can be beautiful, but it requires some special care to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Gray hair tends to be drier and more fragile than other hair types. To keep it looking healthy, use a gentle shampoo that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your hair, making it brittle and dry. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with UV protection.

Use a Moisturizing Conditioner

Gray hair needs extra moisture to stay healthy and soft. Use a moisturizing conditioner after shampooing to keep your hair hydrated.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners can damage your hair and make it look dull and lifeless. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

FAQs

Does plucking a gray hair make more grow back?

No, plucking a gray hair does not make more grow back. However, it’s not a good idea to pluck your hair as it can damage the follicle and lead to hair loss.

Can stress cause gray hair?

Yes, stress can cause premature graying of hair. When we’re under stress, our bodies produce more cortisone, which can interfere with melanin production.

Can you dye gray hair?

Yes, you can dye gray hair. However, it’s important to use a hair dye that’s specifically formulated for gray hair to avoid damage.

Conclusion

Gray hair is a natural part of the aging process, but it doesn’t have to be a source of stress. With the right care and attention, gray hair can be beautiful and healthy.

Remember to use gentle products, protect your hair from the sun, and avoid heat styling. And if you choose to dye your hair, make sure to use a product that’s safe for gray hair.
