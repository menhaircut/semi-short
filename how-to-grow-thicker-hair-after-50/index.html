How To Grow Thicker Hair After 50 Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Jul 18, 2023 Â· 3 min read

In our younger years, having thick and lustrous hair comes naturally. But as we age, our hair becomes more fragile, and we start to experience hair thinning and hair loss. However, growing thicker hair after 50 is possible with proper care and a few lifestyle changes. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how you can grow thicker hair after 50.

Why Does Hair Thinning Happen?

Before we dive into the tips on growing thicker hair, let's first understand why hair thinning happens. Hair thinning can occur due to various reasons, including:

  • Hormonal changes
  • Genetics
  • Nutritional deficiencies
  • Stress
  • Medical conditions

How to Grow Thicker Hair After 50

1. Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a nutritious diet is crucial for healthy hair growth. Make sure to include foods that are rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals, such as eggs, fish, nuts, and green leafy vegetables. Also, drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, can damage your hair and lead to hair thinning. Try to avoid using them as much as possible or use a heat protectant spray before styling your hair.

3. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Using a gentle shampoo and conditioner can help prevent hair breakage and promote hair growth. Look for products that are free of sulfates and parabens, as these chemicals can strip your hair of its natural oils.

4. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp regularly can increase blood flow to your hair follicles, promoting hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp in circular motions for a few minutes each day.

5. Try Hair Growth Supplements

There are various supplements available in the market that claim to promote hair growth. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin D, and iron, as these nutrients are essential for healthy hair growth.

FAQ

1. Can hair thinning be reversed?

While hair thinning cannot be completely reversed, it can be slowed down or managed through proper care and lifestyle changes.

2. How long does it take to see results?

It may take a few months to see visible results. Consistency is key when it comes to growing thicker hair after 50.

3. Can stress cause hair thinning?

Yes, stress can cause hair thinning. When you're stressed, your body produces a hormone called cortisol, which can lead to hair loss.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you grow thicker hair:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as tight braids and ponytails.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Growing thicker hair after 50 may seem challenging, but with the right care and lifestyle changes, it's possible. Remember to be patient and consistent, and you'll soon have lustrous and healthy hair.
