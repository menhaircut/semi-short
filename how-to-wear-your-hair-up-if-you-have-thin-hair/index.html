How To Wear Your Hair Up If You Have Thin Hair

Written by Bardi Jun 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of constantly wearing your hair down because you're worried that wearing it up will reveal your thinning hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many women struggle with thin hair, but that doesn't mean you have to give up on wearing your hair up altogether. In fact, there are several styles you can try that will not only keep your hair off your face but also make it look thicker and fuller. Read on to learn how to wear your hair up if you have thin hair.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-By-Step Guide
  3. Tips to Make Your Hair Look Fuller

Step-By-Step Guide

Here are some step-by-step instructions on how to wear your hair up if you have thin hair:

1. High Ponytail

A high ponytail is a classic style that looks great on everyone. To make it work for thin hair, start by teasing the crown of your head to create volume. Then, gather your hair into a high ponytail, securing it tightly with an elastic. Wrap a small section of hair around the elastic to hide it, and secure it with a bobby pin.

2. Messy Bun

A messy bun is a great option if you're short on time. Start by pulling your hair into a high ponytail, and then twist it into a bun. Don't worry if it's not perfect – the messier, the better! Secure the bun with bobby pins, and pull out a few pieces around your face to frame it.

3. Braids

Braids are a great way to add texture and volume to thin hair. Try a simple French braid, a fishtail braid, or a Dutch braid. Start at the top of your head and work your way down, pulling small sections of hair into the braid as you go. Secure the end with an elastic, and gently pull the braid apart to make it look fuller.

4. Half-Up, Half-Down

A half-up, half-down style is perfect if you want to wear your hair up but still show off your length. Start by teasing the crown of your head to create volume, and then pull the top half of your hair back into a ponytail. Secure it with an elastic, and then wrap a small section of hair around the elastic to hide it. Leave the bottom half of your hair down, and curl it to add texture.

Tips to Make Your Hair Look Fuller

Here are some tips to make your thin hair look fuller:

  • Use volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Blow-dry your hair upside down to create volume at the roots.
  • Use a teasing comb to add volume to your hair at the crown.
  • Try a hair topper or extensions to add volume and length to your hair.

FAQs

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have thin hair?

Avoid hairstyles that are too tight, as they can pull on your hair and make it look thinner. Also, avoid styles that are too sleek, as they can flatten your hair and make it look limp.

Can I wear my hair up if I have a receding hairline?

Absolutely! Just be sure to avoid styles that pull on your hairline, as they can make it look more prominent. Instead, try styles that add volume at the crown, like a messy bun or a half-up, half-down style.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

To keep your thin hair looking its best, be sure to:

  • Wash your hair every other day to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Invest in a good dry shampoo to extend the life of your style.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and avoid split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently wear your hair up, no matter how thin it may be. Experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you, and don't be afraid to ask your stylist for advice. With a little effort, you can have beautiful, voluminous hair that looks great in any style.
