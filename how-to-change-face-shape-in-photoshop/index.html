How To Change Face Shape In Photoshop A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Want to change the shape of your face in photos but don't know where to start? Look no further! In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to change your face shape in Photoshop. Whether you want to slim down your cheeks or define your jawline, we've got you covered.

Description

Changing your face shape in Photoshop can be a fun and creative way to enhance your photos. With just a few simple tools, you can transform your face to look slimmer, wider, or more defined. This technique is commonly used by photographers and models to achieve a specific look, but anyone can use it to enhance their photos.

How to Change Face Shape in Photoshop

Step 1: Open the Photo in Photoshop

The first step is to open the photo you want to edit in Photoshop. You can do this by going to File > Open and selecting the photo from your computer. Once the photo is open, create a duplicate layer by right-clicking on the layer and selecting Duplicate Layer.

Step 2: Select the Liquify Tool

Next, select the Liquify tool by going to Filter > Liquify. This will open up the Liquify window, which will allow you to manipulate the pixels in your photo. You can use this tool to change the shape of your face, as well as adjust other features like your eyes or nose.

Step 3: Use the Forward Warp Tool

Once you have the Liquify window open, select the Forward Warp tool. This tool will allow you to push and pull pixels to change the shape of your face. Use the tool to push in areas of your face that you want to slim down, or pull out areas that you want to make wider.

Step 4: Use the Pucker Tool

If you want to make specific areas of your face smaller, use the Pucker tool. This tool will allow you to shrink down specific areas, like your cheeks or nose. Simply select the Pucker tool and click and drag over the area you want to shrink.

Step 5: Use the Bloat Tool

On the other hand, if you want to make specific areas of your face larger, use the Bloat tool. This tool will allow you to inflate specific areas, like your lips or cheeks. Simply select the Bloat tool and click and drag over the area you want to enlarge.

Step 6: Use the Freeze Mask Tool

If you accidentally push or pull too much, don't worry! You can use the Freeze Mask tool to protect specific areas from being affected by the Liquify tool. Simply select the Freeze Mask tool and paint over the areas you want to protect.

Step 7: Save Your Changes

Once you're happy with the changes you've made, click OK to close the Liquify window. Then, save your changes by going to File > Save or File > Save As.

Tips for Changing Face Shape in Photoshop

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when changing your face shape in Photoshop:

  • Use a light touch with the Liquify tool to avoid distorting your photo too much
  • Zoom in to your photo to get a more detailed view of the changes you're making
  • Experiment with different tools to find the best way to achieve the look you want

FAQ

Can I change my face shape in Photoshop if I have no experience?

Yes! Changing your face shape in Photoshop is relatively easy, even if you have no experience. Just follow the steps above and remember to use a light touch with the Liquify tool.

Can I change my hair color in Photoshop?

Yes! Changing your hair color in Photoshop is another fun and creative way to enhance your photos. You can use the Hue/Saturation tool to adjust the color of your hair.

Hair Care

Remember, changing your face shape in Photoshop is just for fun. It's important to embrace your natural beauty and take care of yourself both inside and out. This includes taking care of your hair! Make sure to use a good shampoo and conditioner that's right for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat or chemicals that can damage your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to change your face shape in Photoshop like a pro. Just remember to have fun and embrace your natural beauty!
