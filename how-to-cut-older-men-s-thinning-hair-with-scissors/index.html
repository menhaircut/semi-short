How To Cut Older Men s Thinning Hair With Scissors

Written by Eveline Nov 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're an older man with thinning hair, you may be wondering how to best style your hair to make it look fuller and more youthful. While there are many different haircuts and styles that can work for thinning hair, cutting your hair with scissors is a great option for those who want a natural, low-maintenance look. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps of how to cut older men's thinning hair with scissors, including tips for getting the best results and caring for your hair afterward.

Description

Thinning hair can be a challenge to style, but with the right techniques, you can make your hair look fuller and more voluminous. One of the best ways to do this is by cutting your hair with scissors instead of clippers. Scissors allow you to create a more natural, blended look that can help to disguise thinning areas and add texture to your hair.

How to Cut Older Men's Thinning Hair with Scissors

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and thickness of your hair, which will help you to create a more even, blended look. If your hair is wet or oily, it can be more difficult to see where to make the cuts, and you may end up with an uneven or choppy haircut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and dry, use a comb to section it into manageable sections. This will help you to get a more even cut and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair at once. Start with the sides of your head, and work your way up to the top and back.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair at an Angle

When you begin cutting your hair, hold the scissors at an angle to create a more natural, textured look. Start by cutting off small sections of hair at a time, and work your way around your head. Be sure to keep the length of your hair consistent, and avoid cutting too much hair at once.

Step 4: Blend Your Hair

Once you've cut all of the sections of your hair, use your scissors to blend the hair together. This will help to create a more natural, seamless look and prevent any choppy or uneven areas. Take small sections of hair and use a cutting motion to blend them together, working from the bottom of your hair up to the top.

Step 5: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror to make sure everything looks even and blended. If there are any areas that look uneven, go back and make small adjustments until you're happy with the overall look.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when cutting older men's thinning hair with scissors:

  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors to prevent any pulling or tugging on your hair.
  • Take your time and work slowly, especially if you're new to cutting hair.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member if you're having trouble reaching certain areas.
  • Consider using styling products like mousse or gel to add volume and texture to your hair after cutting.

FAQ

Here are some common questions about cutting older men's thinning hair with scissors:

Can I cut my own hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with scissors, but it may be easier to get a more even, professional-looking cut if you have help from a friend or family member.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's generally recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the length and shape of your haircut.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you accidentally cut too much hair or create an uneven area, don't panic. You can usually fix the mistake by making small adjustments and blending the hair together using your scissors.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and maintain its health. Here are a few tips for caring for older men's thinning hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools like blow dryers and straighteners, which can damage your hair.
  • Consider using a hair growth shampoo or supplement to help promote healthy hair growth.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support healthy hair growth and prevent thinning.

Cutting older men's thinning hair with scissors is a great way to create a natural, low-maintenance look that can help you feel more confident and youthful. By following these steps and using the right techniques, you can achieve a great haircut that looks great and is easy to maintain.
