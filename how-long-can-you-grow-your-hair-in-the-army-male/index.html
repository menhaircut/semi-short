How Long Can You Grow Your Hair In The Army Male A Complete Guide

The military is known for its strict grooming standards, which includes hair length. If you are a male soldier wondering how long you can grow your hair in the army, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will discuss everything you need to know about hair length regulations in the army.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

As per the Army Regulation 670-1, male soldiers are not allowed to have hair that touches the collar, extends below the eyebrows when headgear is removed, or extends beyond the ears when headgear is worn. The hair must also be tapered from the lower natural hairline upwards at least 3/4 inch and outward not greater than 3/4 inch to blend with the hairstyle.

This regulation applies to all male soldiers regardless of their rank or position in the military. The regulation is in place to maintain a professional and uniform appearance among soldiers.

How to

If you are a male soldier in the army, you need to keep your hair within the regulations mentioned above. This means that you need to maintain a haircut that is short and tapered. You can visit the base barber shop or a civilian barber to get a haircut that meets the regulations.

It is important to note that even if you have a medical condition that requires you to grow your hair longer, you will still need to get a waiver from the army to do so. The waiver will be granted on a case-by-case basis and will depend on the nature of your medical condition.

Step by step

Here are the step-by-step instructions on how to maintain the regulation haircut:

  1. Visit the base barber shop or a civilian barber.
  2. Ask for a short and tapered haircut.
  3. Ensure that the hair does not touch the collar, extends below the eyebrows, or extends beyond the ears.
  4. Maintain the haircut regularly to ensure that it stays within regulations.

Tips

Here are some tips that can help you maintain a regulation haircut:

  • Visit the barber regularly to maintain the haircut.
  • Use hair products that are authorized by the army.
  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using hair products that make your hair look shiny or oily.

FAQ

1. Can I grow my hair if I have a medical condition?

Yes, you can get a waiver from the army to grow your hair if you have a medical condition that requires you to do so. However, the waiver will be granted on a case-by-case basis.

2. Can I wear a wig or hairpiece to meet the regulations?

No, wearing a wig or hairpiece is not allowed to meet the regulations. The hair must be your own and must be within the prescribed length and style.

3. Can I have a beard or mustache in the army?

Yes, you can have a beard or mustache in the army as long as it meets the regulations. The beard cannot be longer than 2 inches and must be neatly trimmed. The mustache cannot extend past the corners of the mouth.

Hair Care

It is important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and within regulations. Here are some tips on how to take care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and nourished.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals.
  • Use a comb or brush to style your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair.

By following these tips, you can maintain a regulation haircut while keeping your hair healthy and well-groomed.

In conclusion, male soldiers in the army are required to maintain a short and tapered haircut that meets the regulations mentioned in Army Regulation 670-1. If you have a medical condition that requires you to grow your hair longer, you will need to get a waiver from the army. By following the tips and instructions mentioned in this article, you can maintain a regulation haircut while keeping your hair healthy and well-groomed.
