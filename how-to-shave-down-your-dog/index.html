How To Shave Down Your Dog A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your furry friend shedding all over your house? Or maybe your dog's coat is getting too long and tangled? Shaving down your dog can help solve these problems, but it's important to do it properly to avoid injuring your dog's skin. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to shave down your dog safely and effectively.

How to Shave Down Your Dog

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary supplies. This includes:

  • Clippers with a #10 blade or higher
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Brush
  • Towels
  • Clipper oil
  • Treats (to reward and distract your dog)

Make sure your clippers are in good working condition and the blades are sharp. Dull blades can pull hair and cause discomfort for your dog.

Step 2: Brush and Comb Your Dog

Before you start shaving, make sure your dog's coat is free of mats and tangles. Use a brush and comb to remove any knots or snarls. This will make the shaving process easier and more comfortable for your dog.

Step 3: Choose the Right Blade Length

The length of the blade you choose will depend on how short you want to shave your dog. A #10 blade will leave about 1/16 of an inch of hair, while a #40 blade will leave only 1/100 of an inch. If you're not sure which blade to use, start with a longer length and work your way shorter if necessary.

Step 4: Shave Your Dog

Start by shaving with the grain of your dog's hair. Use slow, steady strokes and avoid applying too much pressure. Keep the clippers flat against your dog's skin to prevent cutting or scraping. If your dog has sensitive areas, such as the belly or legs, be extra gentle.

Don't try to shave too much at once. Take breaks to let your dog rest and stretch. Offer treats to keep your dog distracted and relaxed.

Step 5: Trim any Remaining Hair

After you've finished shaving, use scissors to trim any remaining hair in hard-to-reach areas, such as around the ears and paws. Be careful not to cut your dog's skin. Use a comb to blend any uneven areas.

Tips for Shaving Your Dog

  • Start with a clean, dry dog to prevent clogged clippers and uneven cuts.
  • Use a clipper coolant spray to prevent the blades from getting too hot.
  • Trim your dog's nails before shaving to prevent scratches.
  • Be patient and take breaks as needed.
  • Offer treats and praise to keep your dog relaxed and happy.

FAQ

Can I shave my dog with human clippers?

No, human clippers are not designed for pet hair and can cause discomfort or injury to your dog.

How often should I shave my dog?

It depends on your dog's breed and how quickly their hair grows. Some breeds may only need to be shaved once or twice a year, while others may need more frequent grooming.

Should I shave my dog myself or take them to a groomer?

If you're comfortable with the process and have the necessary supplies, you can shave your dog yourself. However, if you're unsure or your dog has a particularly thick or curly coat, it may be best to take them to a professional groomer.

Hair Care

After you've shaved your dog, it's important to take care of their skin and coat. Here are a few tips:

  • Apply a soothing balm or oil to your dog's skin to prevent irritation.
  • Brush your dog regularly to prevent matting and tangles.
  • Use a gentle shampoo when bathing your dog to avoid stripping their skin of natural oils.

With these tips and techniques, you can safely and effectively shave down your dog's coat to keep them cool and comfortable. Remember to take it slow, be patient, and reward your furry friend for their cooperation. Happy grooming!
