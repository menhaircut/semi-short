Does A Full Fringe Suit A Round Face

Description

A full fringe, also known as bangs, is a popular hairstyle that can transform your look in an instant. However, not all fringes are created equal, and it's important to consider whether it suits your face shape before taking the plunge. In this article, we'll be discussing whether a full fringe suits a round face, and providing tips on how to style and maintain this look.

How to Determine If a Full Fringe Suits a Round Face

Round faces are characterized by their width and lack of angles. They typically have a rounded jawline, cheekbones, and forehead, and are often shorter in length compared to other face shapes. When it comes to fringes, a full fringe can actually work well for round faces, as it can help to create the illusion of length and angles.

However, you'll want to avoid fringes that are too short or too blunt, as they can make your face appear wider. Instead, opt for fringes that are longer and can be swept to the side, as they can help to elongate your face and create a more flattering shape.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Style a Full Fringe for a Round Face

Now that you've determined that a full fringe is suitable for your round face, it's time to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and dry your hair as usual.
  2. Section off the front part of your hair that you want to cut into a fringe.
  3. Using sharp scissors, cut your fringe to the desired length. Remember to start longer and gradually go shorter, as you can always cut more hair off later.
  4. Style your fringe by blow-drying it with a round brush. You can also add some texturizing spray or serum to give it some volume and texture.
  5. Once your fringe is dry, use a straightening iron to smooth out any kinks or flyaways.
  6. Finally, use some hairspray to hold your fringe in place.

Tips on Maintaining a Full Fringe for a Round Face

Now that you've styled your full fringe, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to prevent it from becoming too long or unruly.
  • Avoid touching your fringe too much, as this can cause it to become greasy and lose its shape.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your fringe between washes.
  • When blow-drying your fringe, use a low heat and be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can a full fringe work for all round face types?

A: Yes, a full fringe can work for all round face types. However, it's important to choose a style that suits your individual face shape and features.

Q: Will a full fringe make my face appear wider?

A: A full fringe can actually help to create the illusion of length and angles, making your face appear less wide. However, it's important to choose a style that is longer and can be swept to the side, rather than a blunt or short fringe that can make your face appear wider.

Q: Can I style my full fringe with a side part?

A: Yes, you can style your full fringe with a side part. This can help to create a more flattering shape for your round face.

Hair Care Tips for a Full Fringe

Here are some general hair care tips for maintaining your full fringe:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Now that you know whether a full fringe suits a round face, and how to style and maintain this look, it's time to try it out for yourself. Remember to choose a style that suits your individual face shape and features, and to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny.
