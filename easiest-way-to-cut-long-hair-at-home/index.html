The Easiest Way To Cut Long Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to long hair. However, with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily and effectively. This guide will walk you through the process of cutting long hair at home, from preparation to finishing touches. Whether you're looking to save money or just want to try something new, this is the perfect guide for you.

Table of Contents:

  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Trimming Your Hair
  • Texturizing Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches

Preparation:

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. This includes a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. You'll also want to make sure you're cutting your hair in a well-lit area with a clean floor.

Next, determine what type of haircut you want. Look up pictures or videos online to get an idea of what you're looking for. It's important to have a clear vision of what you want before you start cutting your hair.

Finally, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and may lead to uneven results.

Sectioning Your Hair:

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Begin with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then use the scissors to trim the ends. Be sure to cut straight across and avoid cutting too much at once.

Repeat this process with the remaining sections, working from the back to the front.

Trimming Your Hair:

Once you've trimmed the ends of each section, it's time to blend them together. Start with the back section and use the comb to comb the hair straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then use the scissors to cut vertically into the hair, creating small snips.

Repeat this process with the remaining sections, blending them together as you go.

Texturizing Your Hair:

If you want to add texture to your hair, you can use the point-cutting technique. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up. Use the scissors to cut into the hair at a diagonal angle, creating small notches.

Repeat this process throughout your hair, focusing on the ends. This will create a more textured look.

Finishing Touches:

Once you've finished cutting and texturizing your hair, it's time to check for any uneven spots or stray hairs. Use the mirror to check the back of your head and make any necessary adjustments.

Finally, style your hair as desired. You can use a straightener, curling iron, or other styling tools to achieve your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home:

  • Start small: It's better to cut too little than too much. You can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can lead to uneven cuts and split ends.
  • Take breaks: Cutting your hair can be tiring, so take breaks as needed to rest your arms and hands.
  • Ask for help: If you're nervous about cutting your hair, ask a friend or family member to assist you.

FAQs:

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut your hair if it's curly. However, keep in mind that curly hair may appear shorter when it dries, so be cautious when cutting.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair at Home:

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a wide-tooth comb to detangle, and avoiding heat styling tools when possible.

Cutting your hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home.
