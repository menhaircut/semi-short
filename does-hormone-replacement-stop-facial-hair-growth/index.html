Does Hormone Replacement Stop Facial Hair Growth

Written by Christine Oct 04, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re someone who’s struggling with excessive facial hair growth, you may be wondering if hormone replacement therapy can help. Hormone replacement therapy (HRT) is a treatment used to balance the levels of hormones in the body, and it’s often used to treat symptoms of menopause. In this article, we’ll explore whether hormone replacement therapy can stop facial hair growth, how it works, and what you can expect if you decide to try it.

How Hormone Replacement Therapy Works

Hormone replacement therapy works by supplementing the body with hormones that it may not be producing enough of on its own. For women, this typically means replacing estrogen and progesterone that are lost during menopause. For transgender individuals, hormone replacement therapy can be used to help transition to their desired gender.

Hormone replacement therapy is usually administered through pills, patches, gels, or injections. The treatment can help alleviate symptoms of menopause like hot flashes, mood swings, and vaginal dryness. It can also help with gender dysphoria and aid in the physical transition process.

Can Hormone Replacement Therapy Stop Facial Hair Growth?

Hormone replacement therapy can help reduce facial hair growth in some cases, but it’s not a guaranteed solution. The effectiveness of hormone replacement therapy in reducing facial hair growth depends on the individual’s hormone levels and the cause of the excessive hair growth.

If the excessive hair growth is caused by high levels of androgens, hormone replacement therapy can help reduce these levels and slow down facial hair growth. However, if the excessive hair growth is caused by genetics, hormone replacement therapy may not be as effective.

Types of Hormone Replacement Therapy

There are several types of hormone replacement therapy available, including:

  • Estrogen-only therapy
  • Combined estrogen and progesterone therapy
  • Testosterone therapy
  • Anti-androgen therapy

The type of hormone replacement therapy that’s right for you will depend on your individual needs and medical history. Your doctor can help you decide which type of hormone replacement therapy is best for you.

Side Effects of Hormone Replacement Therapy

Like any medication, hormone replacement therapy can cause side effects. Common side effects of hormone replacement therapy include:

  • Bloating
  • Headaches
  • Breast tenderness
  • Nausea
  • Mood changes

More serious side effects of hormone replacement therapy can include an increased risk of heart disease, stroke, and blood clots. Your doctor can help you weigh the risks and benefits of hormone replacement therapy.

How to Prepare for Hormone Replacement Therapy

If you’re considering hormone replacement therapy, it’s important to talk to your doctor about your medical history and any medications you’re currently taking. Your doctor may recommend certain lifestyle changes to help prepare your body for hormone replacement therapy.

Before starting hormone replacement therapy, your doctor will likely perform a physical exam and take a blood sample to check your hormone levels. Your doctor may also recommend a mammogram or other tests depending on your medical history.

Tips for Success with Hormone Replacement Therapy

If you decide to try hormone replacement therapy to reduce facial hair growth, here are some tips for success:

  • Be patient – it may take several months to see results
  • Follow your doctor’s instructions carefully
  • Keep track of any changes in your body and report them to your doctor
  • Take care of your skin – hormone replacement therapy can cause dryness and acne

Frequently Asked Questions

Can hormone replacement therapy stop beard growth?

Hormone replacement therapy can help reduce beard growth in some cases, but it’s not a guaranteed solution. The effectiveness of hormone replacement therapy in reducing beard growth depends on the individual’s hormone levels and the cause of the excessive hair growth.

Can hormone replacement therapy cause hair loss?

Yes, hormone replacement therapy can cause hair loss in some cases. However, this is usually a temporary side effect and hair will typically grow back once the treatment is stopped.

Is hormone replacement therapy safe?

Hormone replacement therapy can be safe for some individuals, but it’s not without risks. The risks of hormone replacement therapy depend on the individual’s medical history and the type of hormone therapy being used.

Hair Care Tips for Facial Hair

If you’re struggling with excessive facial hair growth, there are some hair care tips you can follow to help manage it:

  • Shave regularly
  • Use hair removal creams or waxing
  • Try laser hair removal
  • Consider electrolysis
  • Avoid plucking or tweezing – this can cause ingrown hairs and scarring

Overall, hormone replacement therapy can be a helpful tool in reducing facial hair growth for some individuals. However, it’s important to talk to your doctor about your individual needs and medical history before starting hormone replacement therapy.
