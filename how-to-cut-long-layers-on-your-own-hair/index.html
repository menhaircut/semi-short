How To Cut Long Layers On Your Own Hair

Written by Bardi Jul 25, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and add some dimension to your long locks, cutting long layers on your own hair is a great place to start. Not only will it give your hair some movement and texture, but it can also help with volume and weight distribution.

Description

Long layers are a versatile haircut that can work for a variety of hair types and face shapes. The layers allow for movement and texture, while still maintaining length. Cutting long layers on your own hair can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully.

How to

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair ties or clips to section off your hair.

Step 1: Start by washing and drying your hair. It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Step 2: Section off your hair. Divide your hair into three sections: top, middle, and bottom. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Start with the bottom section. Take a small subsection of hair, about one inch thick, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and use the scissors to cut at an angle. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you've trimmed all the sections in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Move on to the middle layer. Repeat the same process as the bottom layer, taking small sections of hair and cutting at an angle. Make sure to blend the bottom layer into the middle layer by cutting at a slightly steeper angle near the bottom.

Step 5: Finish with the top layer. Take small sections of hair and cut at a slightly steeper angle than the middle layer. This will help create more movement and texture at the top of your hair.

Tips

  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time. It's better to make multiple small cuts rather than one big one.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can create uneven cuts and damage your hair.
  • Start with a little bit of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. They'll make the process easier and give you better results.

FAQ

Can I cut long layers on curly hair?

Absolutely! Long layers can work well on curly hair, but it's important to cut them when your hair is dry and in its natural state. This will help you see how the curls fall and make sure you're cutting them evenly.

How often should I cut my long layers?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your layers and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your long layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims will also help keep your hair looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Cutting long layers on your own hair can be a bit daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's definitely doable. Take your time, follow the steps, and you'll be rocking a new, layered look in no time!
