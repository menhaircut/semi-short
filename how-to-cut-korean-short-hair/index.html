How To Cut Korean Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read
Are you looking to cut your hair short and want to try out the Korean short hair trend? Look no further, because we have a step-by-step guide on how to cut Korean short hair. From the tools you need to the techniques to use, we have you covered.

Description

Korean short hair is a popular trend that has been around for years. It is a versatile hairstyle that can be worn by anyone, regardless of their hair type or texture. This hairstyle is characterized by its layered and textured look, with the hair cut short at the back and longer on the top and sides. The style is perfect for those who want a low maintenance hairstyle that still looks chic and trendy.

How to Cut Korean Short Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, it is important to gather the necessary tools. You will need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Once you have everything ready, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel drying it. It is important to cut your hair when it is clean and dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts - the top, sides, back, and nape. Clip each section of hair to keep them separated.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Begin cutting the back of your hair first. Use your comb to hold the hair straight and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Continue this process until you have cut all of the hair at the back.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

Next, move to the sides of your hair. Use your comb to hold the hair straight and cut it at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Make sure to blend the hair from the back into the sides.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, move to the top of your hair. Use your comb to hold the hair straight and cut it at an angle, starting from the back and working your way to the front. Make sure to leave some length on the top of your hair for added texture and volume.

Step 6: Texturize

Once you have cut your hair, use your scissors to texturize the ends. This will help create a layered look and add more dimension to your hair.

Tips

- Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed. It's easier to cut more hair than to grow it back. - Use sharp hair cutting scissors to make clean cuts. - Take your time and be patient. Cutting your hair can take time and practice.

FAQ

Can I Cut My Own Hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair. However, it is important to have the right tools and techniques to ensure that you get the best results.

How Often Should I Cut My Hair?

It is recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the style and keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain the style. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools. Trim your hair regularly to maintain the style and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Cutting Korean short hair is easy with the right tools and techniques. Follow our step-by-step guide for a chic and trendy look that is perfect for any occasion. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to keep it looking healthy and fresh.
