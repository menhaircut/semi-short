What Unnatural Hair Color Is Best For My Warm Skin Tone

Written by Frank Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hair color, you might be wondering what unnatural hair color is best for your warm skin tone. Whether you have a golden or olive complexion, there are plenty of options to choose from. In this article, we'll explore the different hair colors that will complement your warm skin tone and provide tips on how to care for your newly colored locks.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Warm skin tones have yellow or golden undertones, which means that hair colors with warm undertones will complement them best. Some examples of warm hair colors include copper, golden blonde, honey, and caramel. These hues will bring out the warmth in your skin tone and make your hair look vibrant.

How to

When choosing an unnatural hair color for warm skin tones, consider the undertones of the color. Warm colors such as red, orange, and yellow will complement your skin tone, while cool colors such as blue and green will clash. Additionally, consider the intensity of the color. If you have a deeper skin tone, you can pull off darker, more intense shades. If you have a lighter skin tone, opt for lighter, more pastel shades.

Step by Step

Here are some steps to follow when choosing an unnatural hair color for your warm skin tone:

  1. Determine your skin tone. If you have a warm skin tone, it will have yellow or golden undertones.
  2. Consider warm hair colors such as copper, golden blonde, honey, and caramel.
  3. Avoid cool hair colors such as blue and green.
  4. Consider the intensity of the color. If you have a deeper skin tone, you can pull off darker, more intense shades. If you have a lighter skin tone, opt for lighter, more pastel shades.
  5. Consult with a professional stylist to ensure the color will work with your skin tone and hair type.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing an unnatural hair color for your warm skin tone:

  • Consider your natural hair color. If you have darker hair, you may need to bleach it before applying a bright, unnatural color.
  • Try a temporary hair color before committing to a permanent one.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your color.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair and fade your color.

FAQ

What unnatural hair colors should I avoid if I have warm skin?

Avoid cool hair colors such as blue and green, which will clash with your warm skin tone.

What hair colors complement warm skin tones?

Hair colors with warm undertones such as copper, golden blonde, honey, and caramel complement warm skin tones.

Can I dye my hair a bright, unnatural color if I have warm skin?

Yes, as long as the color has warm undertones and complements your skin tone.

Hair Care

Once you've chosen an unnatural hair color for your warm skin tone, it's important to take care of your hair to keep the color looking vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to protect your color.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair and fade your color.
  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as this can strip the color.
  • Consider using a color-depositing conditioner to refresh your color in between salon visits.

Overall, there are plenty of unnatural hair colors that will complement warm skin tones. From copper to honey to caramel, choose a color that makes you feel confident and beautiful. And with the right hair care routine, your color will stay vibrant for weeks to come.
