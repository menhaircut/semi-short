How To Stop Thick Frizzy Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Sep 07, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thick, frizzy hair, you know the struggle of trying to tame it. It can be frustrating to spend hours styling your hair, only to have it turn into a frizzy mess as soon as you step outside. But don't worry, there are ways to stop thick frizzy hair in its tracks. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about taming your unruly locks.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Thick Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Managing Thick Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Thick Frizzy Hair

How to Stop Thick Frizzy Hair

The first step to stopping thick frizzy hair is to understand what causes it. Frizz happens when your hair is dry and lacks moisture. This can be caused by a variety of factors including genetics, weather, and heat styling. Here are some ways to stop thick frizzy hair:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

One of the easiest ways to stop thick frizzy hair is to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain natural oils like argan oil, coconut oil, or jojoba oil. These oils help to hydrate your hair and keep it soft and smooth.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and frizz. Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days, depending on your hair type. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more often, but try to avoid washing it every day.

3. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair hydrated throughout the day. Apply a small amount to your hair after washing it, and focus on the ends. This will help to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking smooth and shiny.

4. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb can help to prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently detangling as you go. This will help to prevent knots and tangles, which can lead to frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to taming your thick frizzy hair:

1. Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Be gentle when washing your hair, and avoid scrubbing too hard. Rinse thoroughly, and use a towel to dry your hair gently.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly throughout your hair.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

If you want to blow dry your hair, use a heat protectant spray first. Then, use a round brush to dry your hair in sections. Be sure to keep the dryer moving, and don't concentrate the heat in one area for too long. This can cause damage to your hair and lead to frizz.

4. Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, you can style it as desired. You can use a straightener or curling iron to create waves or curls. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray, and don't use too much heat.

Tips for Managing Thick Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing thick frizzy hair:

1. Use a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help to prevent frizz and breakage. Satin is a smooth material that won't snag your hair like cotton or other materials.

2. Don't Brush Your Hair When It's Wet

Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage, so avoid brushing it when it's wet. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair after washing it.

3. Use a Hair Mask Once a Week

A hair mask can help to hydrate your hair and keep it looking healthy. Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair soft and smooth.

4. Avoid Heat Styling Too Often

Heat styling can damage your hair and lead to frizz. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools, and opt for heat-free styles like braids or buns.

FAQs

Q: Can I use oils to tame my frizzy hair?

A: Yes, you can use natural oils like coconut oil, argan oil, or jojoba oil to tame your frizzy hair. These oils help to hydrate your hair and keep it looking smooth and shiny.

Q: Is it better to air dry or blow dry my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Air drying your hair can be better for your hair, as it reduces the amount of heat damage. However, blow drying your hair can give you more control over your style and help to prevent frizz.

Hair Care Products for Thick Frizzy Hair

Here are some hair care products that are great for managing thick frizzy hair:

  • Moisturizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Heat protectant spray
  • Hair mask

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can stop thick frizzy hair in its tracks. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and don't use too much heat when styling it. With a little patience and care, you can have healthy, beautiful hair that is easy to manage.
