How To Cut Long Layers In Your Own Hair

Written by Bardi Aug 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank? Cutting long layers in your own hair can give you a fresh, new look without having to spend a fortune at the salon. In this article, we’ll go over how to cut long layers in your own hair, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • How To Cut Long Layers in Your Own Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How To Cut Long Layers in Your Own Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, it’s important to gather the necessary tools. You’ll need a pair of hair cutting scissors, a fine-tooth comb, hair clips, and a spray bottle filled with water.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It’s important to start with clean, dry hair so you can see where the natural layers fall.

Step 2: Brush Out Tangles

Using a fine-tooth comb, brush out any tangles in your hair. This will make it easier to see where the layers naturally fall.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, section your hair into three parts: the top, middle, and bottom. Clip the top section of your hair on the top of your head, and the bottom section of your hair at the nape of your neck.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer of your hair, use your comb to lift a small section of hair up to where you want to start cutting. Using your hair cutting scissors, cut the hair straight across. Repeat this process with small sections of hair until you have cut the entire bottom layer.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Next, unclip the middle layer of your hair and repeat the same process as the bottom layer. Cut small sections of hair until you have cut the entire middle layer.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, unclip the top layer of your hair and repeat the same process as the previous layers. Cut small sections of hair until you have cut the entire top layer.

Step-by-Step Guide

Let’s go over the step-by-step guide one more time to make sure you have all the details.

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Brush out tangles
  3. Section your hair into three parts: top, middle, and bottom
  4. Cut the bottom layer
  5. Cut the middle layer
  6. Cut the top layer

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Now that you know how to cut long layers in your own hair, here are a few tips to make the process easier:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting off too much at once
  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors for a clean cut
  • Don’t cut too much off at once. Remember, you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back on
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so it’s okay to take a break and come back to it later

FAQs

Can I cut long layers in curly hair?

Yes, you can cut long layers in curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to spring up more than straight hair, so you may want to cut less off than you think you need to.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It’s recommended that you cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

Once you’ve cut your own hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are a few tips:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Use a heat protectant when styling with hot tools
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and shiny
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils

Now that you know how to cut long layers in your own hair, go ahead and give it a try! With a little practice and patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home.
