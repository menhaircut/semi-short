How to Style Your Hair When Growing Out an Undercut

The key to styling your hair when growing out an undercut is to work with the length and texture you have. You may need to experiment with different styles and products to find what works for you, but here are some ideas to get started:

1. Embrace the Messy Look

When your hair is in the awkward growing-out phase, embrace the messy look. Try using a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to create a tousled, beachy vibe. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and create volume, and add a little hairspray to hold the style in place.

2. Use Hair Accessories

Headbands, hair clips, and bobby pins can be your best friends during the growing-out phase. Experiment with different styles and colors to find what works for you. For example, you could use a headband to sweep your hair back from your face, or use bobby pins to create a half-up, half-down style.

3. Try a Low Ponytail

If your hair is long enough to pull back, consider a low ponytail. This can be a sleek and sophisticated look, or you can embrace the messy look and leave some strands loose around your face.

4. Experiment with Braids

Braids are a great way to keep your hair off your face and add some texture and interest to your style. Try a simple side braid or a more complex fishtail braid. You could also experiment with braiding just a small section of hair, or braiding a crown around your head.

5. Get a Trim

As your hair grows out, it's important to get regular trims to keep the shape and prevent split ends. Talk to your stylist about the best length and style for your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are some step-by-step instructions for styling your hair when growing out an undercut:

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Wash and condition your hair as usual, and let it air-dry or blow-dry it with a diffuser.

2. Choose your styling product.

Depending on the look you're going for, choose a texturizing spray, sea salt spray, mousse, or other product that will help create volume and texture.

3. Apply the product to your hair.

Follow the instructions on the product packaging, but in general, you'll want to apply the product to the roots and mid-lengths of your hair, avoiding the ends. Use your fingers to work the product through your hair.

4. Style your hair.

Use your fingers or a comb to create the style you want. Experiment with different looks, such as a messy side part, a half-up, half-down style, or a low ponytail.

5. Finish with hairspray.

Spray a light mist of hairspray over your hair to hold the style in place.

Tips for Styling Your Hair

Here are some additional tips for styling your hair when growing out an undercut:

1. Be patient.

The growing-out phase can be frustrating, but remember that it's temporary. Be patient and try to enjoy experimenting with different styles along the way.

2. Don't be afraid to try new things.

This is a great time to experiment with new hairstyles and products. Don't be afraid to try something new â you might just discover a new favorite look.

3. Consider a new haircut.

If you're really struggling with the growing-out phase, consider getting a new haircut that will blend in with your longer hair. Talk to your stylist about your options.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing out an undercut:

1. How long does it take to grow out an undercut?

It depends on how short your hair was to begin with and how fast it grows. On average, hair grows about half an inch per month, so if you had a very short undercut, it could take several months to grow out completely.

2. Can I dye my hair while growing out an undercut?

Yes, but keep in mind that the color may not look the same on the undercut as it does on the rest of your hair. Talk to your stylist about the best options for coloring your hair during the growing-out phase.

3. Can I still get regular haircuts while growing out an undercut?

Yes, in fact, regular trims are important to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Talk to your stylist about the best length and style for your hair during the growing-out phase.

Hair Care Advice

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair when growing out an undercut:

1. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.

When your hair is growing out, it can be more prone to dryness and breakage. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

2. Use a heat protectant.

If you use heat styling tools, such as a blow dryer or flat iron, use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Avoid over-washing your hair.

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every few days, depending on your hair type.

With these tips and tricks, you can style your hair with confidence during the growing-out phase. Remember to be patient and have fun experimenting with new looks along the way.
