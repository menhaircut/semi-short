What Does Point Cutting Hair Do

Written by Aprilia Dec 03, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you are looking for a way to add texture and volume to your hair, point cutting might be the answer. This technique involves cutting the ends of the hair at an angle to create a more natural, textured look. In this article, we will discuss what point cutting is, how to do it, and some tips for maintaining your hair after the cut.

Description

Point cutting is a hair cutting technique that involves cutting the ends of the hair at an angle instead of straight across. This creates a textured look that adds volume and movement to the hair. The technique is called "point" cutting because the stylist uses the tips of the scissors to make small cuts at different angles, creating a more natural look.

How to Point Cut Hair

Point cutting is a technique that requires some skill and practice. If you are not confident in your ability to do it yourself, it is best to visit a professional stylist. However, if you want to try it at home, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. Make sure your hair is detangled and easy to comb through.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section your hair into manageable parts. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 3: Hold the Scissors at an Angle

Hold the scissors at an angle and cut into the ends of the hair, making small snips. The angle should be about 45 degrees, but you can adjust it depending on the look you want to achieve.

Step 4: Work Your Way Through the Hair

Work your way through each section of hair, cutting the ends at an angle. Take your time and be careful not to cut too much at once.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair After Point Cutting

After you have point cut your hair, there are some things you can do to maintain the look:

  • Use a texturizing spray to enhance the texture and movement of your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and texture of your cut.

FAQ

What types of hair are best suited for point cutting?

Point cutting works well on all hair types, but it is particularly effective on thick, heavy hair that needs to be thinned out.

Is point cutting the same as razor cutting?

No, point cutting and razor cutting are two different techniques. Point cutting involves using scissors to cut at an angle, while razor cutting involves using a razor to create a softer, more feathered look.

Hair Care

After point cutting your hair, it is important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. This means using a good quality shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, and protecting your hair from the sun and other environmental factors. Regular trims are also important to maintain the shape and texture of your cut.

Overall, point cutting is a great way to add texture and volume to your hair. Whether you do it at home or visit a professional stylist, this technique can help you achieve a natural, textured look that is both stylish and easy to maintain.
