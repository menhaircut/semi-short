Why Is My 17 Year Old Son Losing Hair

Written by Petter Aug 19, 2023 Â· 3 min read

As a parent, it can be concerning when you notice that your teenage son is losing hair. Hair loss is often associated with aging, but it can occur at any age, including during adolescence. In this article, we will discuss the possible reasons why your 17-year-old son may be experiencing hair loss and what you can do to help him.

Table of Contents

  • Hormones
  • Genetics
  • Stress
  • Nutrition
  • Hairstyling

Hormones

Hormonal changes can be a common cause of hair loss in teenagers. During adolescence, puberty causes a surge in hormones, particularly androgens like testosterone. These hormones can affect the hair follicles, causing them to shrink and produce thinner, weaker hair. This type of hair loss is called androgenetic alopecia and is more commonly known as male-pattern baldness.

Genetics

Genetics can also play a role in your son's hair loss. If there is a family history of hair loss, particularly on the mother's side, then your son may be more likely to experience it. Male-pattern baldness is an inherited condition that can be passed down from either parent.

Stress

Stress can also be a contributing factor to hair loss in teenagers. High levels of stress can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle, causing more hair to fall out than usual. This type of hair loss is called telogen effluvium and is usually temporary. Once the stress is alleviated, the hair should grow back.

Nutrition

A healthy diet is important for overall health, including hair health. If your son is not getting enough nutrients in his diet, it can lead to hair loss. Protein, iron, and vitamins A, B, and C are particularly important for healthy hair growth.

Hairstyling

The way your son styles his hair can also contribute to hair loss. Tight hairstyles, such as braids, cornrows, or a tight ponytail, can cause tension on the hair follicles, leading to hair loss. Chemical treatments, such as relaxers or perms, can also damage the hair and cause it to break or fall out.

Medical Conditions

There are several medical conditions that can cause hair loss in teenagers, including thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, and scalp infections. If you are concerned that your son's hair loss may be due to a medical condition, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional.

Hair Care Tips

There are several things your son can do to promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss:

  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet
  • Avoid tight hairstyles and chemical treatments
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid over-brushing or pulling on the hair
  • Consider taking a hair supplement, such as biotin or folic acid

FAQs

Q: Is it normal for a 17-year-old to experience hair loss?

A: While hair loss is more commonly associated with aging, it can occur at any age, including during adolescence.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss in teenagers?

A: Yes, high levels of stress can disrupt the normal hair growth cycle, leading to hair loss.

Q: What can I do to help my son with his hair loss?

A: Encourage him to eat a healthy diet, avoid tight hairstyles and chemical treatments, and consult with a healthcare professional if necessary.

In conclusion, hair loss in teenagers can be caused by a variety of factors, including hormonal changes, genetics, stress, nutrition, hairstyling, and medical conditions. Encouraging your son to take care of his hair and consult with a healthcare professional if necessary can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
