Big Chop Natural Hair Cuts A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 23, 2023 · 3 min read

If you’re considering a big chop natural hair cut, you’re not alone. Many women are embracing their natural hair texture and opting for shorter cuts. The big chop is a bold move that involves cutting off all of your relaxed or chemically-treated hair to start fresh with your natural curls. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process and share some tips for taking care of your new ‘do.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

The big chop is a drastic hair cut that involves cutting off all of your relaxed or chemically-treated hair. This can be a scary decision, but it’s also empowering. Many women have found that cutting off their hair has helped them embrace their natural texture and feel more confident in their skin.

The big chop can be done on any hair type, but it’s most common among women with curly or kinky hair. It’s important to note that the big chop is not the same as a “regular” haircut. It’s a permanent decision that requires some preparation and aftercare.

How to

The big chop is a simple process, but it’s important to do it right to avoid damaging your hair. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Prepare your hair

Before you chop off your hair, it’s important to prepare it for the big day. Start by deep conditioning your hair to improve its elasticity and prevent breakage. You may also want to trim your ends to make sure they’re as healthy as possible.

Step 2: Choose a length

Decide how short you want to go. Some women prefer a TWA (teeny weeny afro), while others opt for a slightly longer pixie cut. It’s up to you!

Step 3: Get the chop

Once you’ve decided on a length, it’s time to cut your hair. You can do this yourself or go to a professional stylist. Make sure you use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

Step by step

Here’s a step-by-step guide to the big chop:

  1. Wash and deep condition your hair.
  2. Trim your ends.
  3. Choose a length.
  4. Section your hair into small sections.
  5. Cut each section with sharp scissors.
  6. Style your new ‘do!

Tips

Here are some tips for taking care of your new natural hair:

  • Moisturize your hair daily.
  • Invest in a good leave-in conditioner.
  • Protect your hair at night with a satin bonnet or pillowcase.
  • Avoid using heat tools.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about the big chop:

Q: Will my hair grow back?

A: Yes! Your hair will grow back, but it may take some time. Be patient and take good care of your hair.

Q: Will my hair be curly?

A: It depends on your natural hair texture. Some women have curly hair, while others have kinky or coily hair.

Q: Will the big chop damage my hair?

A: No, as long as you do it correctly. Make sure you use sharp scissors and avoid cutting your hair too short.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your natural hair:

  • Wash your hair once a week.
  • Deep condition your hair once a month.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Trim your ends every 6-8 weeks.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind.

Congratulations on your big chop! Embracing your natural hair is a beautiful journey, and we’re here to support you every step of the way.
