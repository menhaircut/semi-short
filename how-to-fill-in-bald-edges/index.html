How To Fill In Bald Edges Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have bald edges that you wish would just disappear? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with thinning or bald edges, whether due to genetics, tight hairstyles, or chemical damage. But the good news is, there are ways to fill in bald edges and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we'll show you how to fill in bald edges with our step-by-step guide, tips, and FAQs. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Fill in Bald Edges
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Fill in Bald Edges

There are several ways to fill in bald edges, including:

  • Using hair fibers or powders
  • Applying hair growth serums or oils
  • Wearing protective hairstyles
  • Avoiding tight hairstyles and chemical treatments

Let's explore each of these methods in more detail.

Hair Fibers or Powders

Hair fibers or powders are a quick and easy way to fill in bald edges. These products work by attaching to your existing hair and creating the illusion of fuller, thicker edges. Simply apply the fibers or powders to your edges using a small brush or applicator, and blend with your fingers for a natural look. Some popular hair fiber brands include Toppik, Caboki, and Nanogen.

Hair Growth Serums or Oils

If you want to promote actual hair growth in your bald edges, try using a hair growth serum or oil. These products contain ingredients that stimulate hair follicles and increase blood flow to the scalp, which can encourage new hair growth. Some popular hair growth serums and oils include Castor oil, Peppermint oil, and Rosemary oil.

Protective Hairstyles

Wearing protective hairstyles like braids, twists, or wigs can also help fill in bald edges. These styles give your hair a break from daily manipulation and protect your edges from further damage. Just be sure to avoid tight styles that can pull on your hairline and cause more damage.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles and Chemical Treatments

To prevent further damage to your edges, avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails, buns, or weaves. These styles can put stress on your hairline and cause hair to break or fall out. Also, avoid chemical treatments like relaxers or perms, which can weaken your hair and cause it to break off.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fill in bald edges:

  1. Cleanse your hair and scalp with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a hair growth serum or oil to your bald edges, massaging gently with your fingertips.
  3. Style your hair in a protective hairstyle like braids or twists.
  4. If using hair fibers or powders, apply them to your edges using a small brush or applicator.
  5. Blend the fibers or powders with your fingers for a natural look.

Tips for Filling in Bald Edges

Here are some additional tips for filling in bald edges:

  • Be patient. It can take time to see results from hair growth serums or oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, which can further damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, like B vitamins, biotin, and zinc.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • If wearing a wig, be sure to protect your edges by using a wig cap or applying a protective gel.

FAQs

Q: Can bald edges grow back?

A: Yes, bald edges can grow back with proper care and treatment. However, it may take some time and patience to see results.

Q: Are hair fibers safe to use?

A: Yes, hair fibers are safe to use and won't damage your hair. However, be sure to choose a high-quality brand and follow the instructions carefully.

Q: Can wearing wigs cause bald edges?

A: Wearing wigs can cause bald edges if they are too tight or not properly secured. Be sure to choose a wig that fits snugly but not too tightly, and use a wig cap or protective gel to protect your edges.

Hair Care for Healthy Edges

To keep your edges healthy and prevent further hair loss, follow these hair care tips:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles and chemical treatments
  • Wear protective styles like braids, twists, or wigs
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Apply a hair growth serum or oil to your edges regularly
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth

By following these tips and using the methods we've outlined, you can fill in bald edges and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and don't be afraid to seek professional help if you need it. With time and care, you can achieve the full, healthy edges you've always wanted.
