How Do I Get Rid Of The Brassy Tones In My Hair In 2023

Written by Frank Aug 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of seeing those unwanted yellow or orange tones in your hair? You're not alone! Many people struggle with brassy hair, especially those who have dyed their hair blonde or have lightened it. In this blog post, we'll discuss how to get rid of the brassy tones in your hair and keep your hair looking beautiful.

Table of Contents

  1. Description of Brassy Hair
  2. How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

Description of Brassy Hair

Brassy hair refers to the unwanted yellow, orange, or red tones that can develop in blonde, light brown, or gray hair. These tones can be caused by a variety of factors, including sun exposure, hard water, chlorine, and hair products. Brassy hair can be frustrating, especially if you've spent time and money to achieve a certain color.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair

Getting rid of brassy hair requires a few steps, but it's not impossible. Here are some ways to get rid of those unwanted tones:

1. Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a great option for toning down brassy hair. The purple pigments in the shampoo neutralize the yellow and orange tones, leaving your hair looking cooler and more ashy. Use a purple shampoo once or twice a week, depending on your hair type.

2. Try a Toning Treatment

If your hair is very brassy, you may want to try a toning treatment. These treatments are more intense than purple shampoo and can help eliminate stubborn tones. Look for a toning treatment that contains blue or violet pigments.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A hair mask can help restore moisture to your hair and prevent it from becoming brassy. Look for a hair mask that contains ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter. These ingredients can help nourish your hair and protect it from damage.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any buildup from hair products or hard water. Rinse well.

Step 2: Apply Purple Shampoo

Apply a small amount of purple shampoo to your hair, focusing on the brassy areas. Massage the shampoo into your hair and scalp, then let it sit for a few minutes. Rinse well.

Step 3: Apply Toning Treatment (Optional)

If your hair is still brassy, apply a toning treatment to your hair. Follow the instructions on the packaging and rinse well.

Step 4: Apply Hair Mask

Apply a hair mask to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave the mask on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse well.

Tips for Preventing Brassy Hair

Preventing brassy hair is easier than getting rid of it. Here are some tips for keeping your hair looking beautiful:

1. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can cause your hair to become dry and brittle, which can lead to brassy tones. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray to protect your hair from the sun.

2. Use a Water Filter

Hard water can cause mineral buildup in your hair, leading to brassy tones. Use a water filter in your shower to remove these minerals.

3. Avoid Chlorine

Chlorine can also cause brassy hair. If you swim frequently, wear a swim cap or rinse your hair with fresh water before and after swimming.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I use purple shampoo?

A: It depends on your hair type and how brassy your hair is. If your hair is very brassy, you may want to use purple shampoo once a week. If your hair is only slightly brassy, you can use it once every two weeks.

Q: Can I use lemon juice to get rid of brassy hair?

A: While lemon juice can lighten hair, it can also cause damage and make your hair more brassy. It's better to use a purple shampoo or toning treatment.

Q: Can I use a toning treatment and purple shampoo at the same time?

A: It's not necessary to use both at the same time. If your hair is very brassy, you may want to use a toning treatment first, then follow up with purple shampoo. If your hair is only slightly brassy, you can just use purple shampoo.

Hair Care for Brassy Hair

After you've gotten rid of the brassy tones in your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent it from becoming brassy again. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Color-Safe Shampoo

Switch to a color-safe shampoo to prevent your hair from fading or becoming brassy. Look for a shampoo that is sulfate-free and contains moisturizing ingredients.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

Heat styling can damage your hair and cause it to become brassy. Use a heat protectant before blow-drying or using hot tools to prevent damage.

3. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can cause your hair to become brassy.

With these tips, you can keep your hair looking beautiful and brassy-free. Remember to use a purple shampoo or toning treatment to get rid of those unwanted tones, and take care of your hair to prevent it from becoming brassy again.
