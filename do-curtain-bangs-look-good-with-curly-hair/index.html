Do Curtain Bangs Look Good With Curly Hair

Written by Frank Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair and you're thinking about getting curtain bangs, you might be wondering if they'll look good on you. The short answer is yes, they can look amazing! In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about curtain bangs and curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Get Curtain Bangs with Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Styling Curtain Bangs with Curly Hair
  • FAQ

Description

Curtain bangs are a type of fringe that are longer at the sides and shorter in the middle. They're called "curtain" bangs because they frame your face like a set of curtains. They're great for adding texture and movement to your hair.

Curly hair can be tricky to work with, but it can also be incredibly beautiful. When paired with curtain bangs, curly hair can create a soft, romantic look that's perfect for any occasion.

How to Get Curtain Bangs with Curly Hair

The first step in getting curtain bangs with curly hair is to find a stylist who understands curly hair. Look for someone who has experience working with curly hair and can help you choose the right length and style of bangs for your face shape.

It's also important to consider the texture of your hair when getting curtain bangs. If you have tight curls, you may want to opt for shorter bangs that won't shrink up too much. If you have looser curls or waves, longer bangs may work better for you.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get curtain bangs with curly hair:

  1. Talk to your stylist about your goals and what you're looking for in a hairstyle.
  2. Choose the right length of bangs for your face shape and hair texture.
  3. Ask your stylist to cut your bangs while your hair is dry and curly.
  4. Once your bangs are cut, your stylist may use a flat iron to straighten them out slightly. This will help them fall into place better.
  5. Style your bangs using a curling iron or wand to add some texture and movement.
  6. Use a styling product like a curl cream or gel to define your curls and keep them in place.

Tips for Styling Curtain Bangs with Curly Hair

Here are some tips for styling curtain bangs with curly hair:

  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without disturbing them too much.
  • Avoid using a brush on your bangs, as this can cause them to frizz.
  • Apply a small amount of oil or serum to your bangs to add shine and prevent frizz.
  • Experiment with different styles, like pinning your bangs back or wearing them side-swept.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curtain bangs and curly hair:

Can I wear my curtain bangs straight?

Yes, you can definitely wear your curtain bangs straight. Just be sure to use a heat protectant and a flat iron with adjustable heat settings to avoid damaging your hair.

How often do I need to trim my curtain bangs?

It's best to trim your curtain bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and prevent them from getting too long.

What if I don't like my curtain bangs?

If you're not happy with your curtain bangs, talk to your stylist about making changes. They may be able to adjust the length or style to better suit your preferences.

Hair Care for Curly Hair with Curtain Bangs

Proper hair care is essential for keeping your curly hair and curtain bangs looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can cause your curls to become dry and frizzy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Overall, curtain bangs can look amazing with curly hair. Just be sure to choose the right length and style for your face shape and hair texture, and take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
