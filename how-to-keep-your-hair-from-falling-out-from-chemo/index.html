How To Keep Your Hair From Falling Out From Chemo

Written by William Nov 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about how to keep your hair from falling out from chemo. If you or someone you know is undergoing chemotherapy, you may be worried about hair loss. Chemotherapy is known to cause hair loss, but there are some steps you can take to minimize this side effect. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks for keeping your hair healthy during chemotherapy.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep Your Hair from Falling Out from Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy

Step-by-Step Guide

Here are the steps you can take to keep your hair from falling out from chemo:

Step 1: Consult with your doctor

The first step is to talk to your doctor about your concerns. They can provide you with information about the specific chemotherapy drugs you will be receiving and how they may affect your hair. They may also recommend medications or treatments to help prevent hair loss.

Step 2: Use a gentle shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that is free from harsh chemicals. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for people undergoing chemotherapy. These shampoos are formulated to be gentle on your scalp and hair.

Step 3: Avoid harsh hair treatments

Avoid harsh hair treatments such as coloring, perming, or straightening. These treatments can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Stick to gentle styling methods such as air-drying your hair or using a soft brush.

Step 4: Protect your hair from the sun

Chemotherapy can make your scalp more sensitive to the sun. Wear a hat or use a scarf to protect your scalp from the sun's rays.

Step 5: Be gentle with your hair

Be gentle with your hair when washing and brushing it. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause it to break. Use a soft brush or comb and avoid using heat styling tools such as hair dryers or curling irons.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your hair during chemotherapy:

Tip 1: Keep your hair short

Consider getting a short haircut before starting chemotherapy. Short hair is easier to manage and can help minimize the appearance of hair loss.

Tip 2: Use a silk pillowcase

Use a silk pillowcase to reduce friction on your hair while you sleep. This can help prevent hair breakage.

Tip 3: Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet can help keep your hair healthy. Make sure to eat plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables.

Tip 4: Be patient

Remember that hair loss from chemotherapy is usually temporary. Be patient and focus on taking care of yourself during this time.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair loss and chemotherapy:

Q: Will all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss?

A: No, not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss. Your doctor can provide you with information about the specific drugs you will be receiving and how they may affect your hair.

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemotherapy?

A: Yes, in most cases, hair loss from chemotherapy is temporary. Your hair will likely start to grow back a few months after your treatment ends.

Q: Can I color my hair during chemotherapy?

A: It is not recommended to color your hair during chemotherapy. The chemicals in hair dye can be harsh on your scalp and hair, and can increase the risk of hair breakage.

Q: Can I use hair extensions during chemotherapy?

A: It is not recommended to use hair extensions during chemotherapy. The weight of the extensions can pull on your hair and cause it to break.

By following these steps and tips, you can help keep your hair healthy during chemotherapy. Remember to talk to your doctor about your concerns and to be patient during this time.
