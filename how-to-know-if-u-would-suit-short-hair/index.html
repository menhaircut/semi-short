How To Know If You Would Suit Short Hair

Written by Luffy Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you thinking of chopping off your long locks and going for a shorter style? Short hair can be a bold and stylish choice, but it's important to consider whether it will suit your face shape and features. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of determining whether short hair is right for you.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in deciding whether short hair will suit you is to determine your face shape. There are four main face shapes: round, square, oval, and heart. Each shape has different features that can be accentuated or softened with the right hairstyle.

If you're not sure what your face shape is, try standing in front of a mirror and tracing the outline of your face with a bar of soap or lipstick. Then, compare your shape to the images below:

If you have a round face, a short hairstyle with volume on top can help elongate your face. If you have a square face, a soft, layered cut can soften your jawline. For oval faces, almost any short hairstyle will work well. Heart-shaped faces can benefit from a pixie cut with side-swept bangs.

Considering Your Hair Texture

In addition to your face shape, your hair texture can also play a role in whether short hair will suit you. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, a short cut can help define your curls and give you a more polished look. If you have straight hair, a pixie cut or blunt bob can look chic and sophisticated.

However, if you have fine hair, a short cut can sometimes make your hair look thinner and limp. In this case, you may want to consider a longer bob or layered cut to add volume and movement.

Matching Your Personality

Your personality can also play a role in whether short hair will suit you. If you're someone who loves to experiment with different styles and isn't afraid to stand out, a bold and edgy short cut could be perfect for you. On the other hand, if you prefer a more classic and timeless look, a simple bob or pixie cut may be more your style.

Evaluating Your Face Features

Another factor to consider when deciding whether short hair will suit you is your face features. If you have a strong nose or chin, a short cut can draw attention to these features. However, if you have striking eyes or cheekbones, a short cut can help showcase these assets.

Exploring Short Hairstyle Options

Once you've determined that short hair is right for you, it's time to start exploring different styles. Some popular short hairstyles include:

  • Pixie cut
  • Bob
  • LOB (long bob)
  • Shag
  • Cropped cut

When choosing a style, consider what will flatter your face shape and hair texture. It's also a good idea to bring pictures of styles you like to your stylist to ensure you're both on the same page.

The Haircut Process

When it comes time to get your new short cut, it's important to choose a stylist who has experience with short hair. Short haircuts require precision and attention to detail, so it's important to find someone you trust.

During your appointment, your stylist will likely start by washing and drying your hair. Then, they'll use clippers or scissors to create the desired shape and length. They may also add layers or texture to give your hair more movement and volume.

Styling Tips for Short Hair

Once you have your new short cut, you'll need to learn how to style it. Some tips for styling short hair include:

  • Invest in quality styling products, such as pomade or texture spray.
  • Experiment with different parting styles to find what flatters your face shape.
  • Use a flat iron or curling wand to add texture and volume.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Short hair requires different hair care than long hair. Some tips for keeping your short hair healthy and looking great include:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent dryness.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your shape.

FAQs

Can I still wear my hair up with a short cut?

Yes! While you may not be able to do a traditional ponytail, there are plenty of ways to style short hair up. Try a half-up, half-down style or a top knot.

Will a short cut make my face look wider?

If you have a round face, a short cut could potentially make your face look wider. However, if you get the right cut for your face shape, it can actually elongate your face.

What if I don't like my short cut?

If you're unhappy with your new short cut, don't panic! Talk to your stylist about what you don't like and see if there's anything they can do to adjust it. If you still don't like it, remember that hair grows back and you can always try a different style next time.

Is short hair harder to maintain than long hair?

Short hair can actually be easier to maintain than long hair, as it requires less washing and styling time. However, you will need to get more frequent trims to maintain your shape.



In conclusion, there are several factors to consider when deciding whether short hair will suit you. By evaluating your face shape, hair texture, personality, and face features, you can determine whether a short cut is right for you. If you do decide to go for it, make sure to choose a style that flatters your features and invest in quality styling products to keep it looking great.
