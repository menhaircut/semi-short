How To Thin A Wig Without Thinning Shears

Written by Eveline Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Wigs are an excellent way to change your hairstyle without committing to a permanent change. However, wigs can be bulky and heavy, especially if you have a lot of hair. One way to make your wig more comfortable is to thin it out. Thinning shears are the most popular tool to thin out wigs, but not everyone has access to them. In this article, we will show you how to thin a wig without thinning shears.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors that have one serrated blade and one straight blade. They are used to cut hair at an angle, which thins out the hair. While thinning shears are the most effective tool to thin out a wig, they can be costly and not widely available. In this article, we will show you how to thin out a wig using items that you already have at home.

How to Thin a Wig Without Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash Your Wig

The first step in thinning out your wig is to wash it. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean the wig, then let it air dry. This will help to remove any tangles and allow you to see the wig's natural shape.

Step 2: Comb Your Wig

Once your wig is dry, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle it. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up to the roots. Be gentle when combing to avoid damaging the wig.

Step 3: Divide Your Wig

Divide your wig into sections. Sectioning your wig will make it easier to thin out evenly. Use hair ties or clips to keep the sections separate.

Step 4: Cut the Wig

Using regular scissors, cut into the wig at a 45-degree angle. Cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid unevenness. Cut the hair closer to the roots for thicker sections and further away for thinner sections.

Step 5: Comb Your Wig Again

After you have finished cutting, comb your wig again to see if there are any uneven areas. If there are any areas that still need to be thinned out, repeat steps 3 and 4 until you have achieved your desired thickness.

Tips

- Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
- Be patient and take your time to achieve the desired thickness.
- Cut at a 45-degree angle to achieve a natural look.
- Use regular scissors instead of kitchen or paper scissors, as they may damage the hair.
- Use a comb with wide teeth to avoid pulling or damaging the hair.

FAQ

Can I thin my wig with kitchen scissors?

We do not recommend using kitchen or paper scissors to thin out your wig. These types of scissors can damage the hair and create unevenness.

Can I thin my wig with a razor?

We do not recommend using a razor to thin out your wig. Razors can damage the hair and create unevenness.

How often should I thin out my wig?

The frequency of thinning out your wig depends on how often you wear it and how often you wash it. We recommend thinning out your wig every few months to maintain its shape.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to keep your wig looking natural and healthy. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to clean your wig.
- Let your wig air dry instead of using a hair dryer.
- Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape.
- Avoid exposing your wig to excessive heat, such as direct sunlight or hot styling tools.

Thinning out a wig without thinning shears can be a daunting task, but it is possible with the right tools and technique. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a natural and comfortable wig that you can wear with confidence.
