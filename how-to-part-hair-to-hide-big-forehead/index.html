How to Part Your Hair to Hide a Big Forehead

There are a few different ways to part your hair to hide a big forehead, depending on your hair type and personal preferences. Here are three of the most popular methods:

1. Side Part

The side part is a classic hairstyle that never goes out of style. To create a side part, simply use a comb to part your hair on one side of your head, then let your hair fall naturally over your forehead. You can adjust the width of the part to cover as much or as little of your forehead as you like.

2. Curtain Bangs

Curtain bangs are a trendy hairstyle that can also help to hide a big forehead. To create curtain bangs, part your hair down the middle and then use a comb to create a small, triangular part in the front. Then, comb the hair on either side of the part forward, so that it falls over your forehead like a curtain. You can trim the bangs to your desired length, and use a texturizing spray to add volume and texture.

3. Full Bangs

Full bangs can be a bold and stylish choice for covering up a big forehead. To create full bangs, use a comb to part your hair straight down the middle, then gather a section of hair from the front of your head. Cut the hair to your desired length (or have a professional do it) and then use a round brush and a blow dryer to style the bangs forward. You can also use a flat iron to smooth the hair and add shine.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Part Your Hair to Hide a Big Forehead

Now that you know the three most popular methods for parting your hair to hide a big forehead, let's walk through the steps in more detail:

Step 1: Choose Your Method

Decide which method you want to use to part your hair â side part, curtain bangs, or full bangs. Consider your hair type, face shape, and personal style when making your decision.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before you start parting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. You can also apply some styling product to add texture and hold.

Step 3: Create Your Part

Use a comb to create your part. For a side part, comb your hair to one side of your head. For curtain bangs, part your hair down the middle and create a small triangle in the front. For full bangs, part your hair straight down the middle.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is parted, style it as desired. You can use a brush, blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron to create volume, texture, or waves. Be sure to use heat protectant spray if you're using hot tools.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Once your hairstyle is complete, use some hairspray or styling product to hold it in place. You can also add some shine serum or hair oil for extra luster.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hairstyle

Here are a few tips for keeping your new hairstyle looking fresh and stylish:

  • Wash your hair regularly to prevent oil buildup
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools
  • Trim your bangs regularly to prevent them from getting too long
  • Style your hair in different ways to avoid monotony
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated

FAQ: Hair Care

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair care:

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day or every other day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it less frequently â once or twice a week. If you exercise frequently or live in a humid climate, you may need to wash your hair more often.

Q: What's the best way to prevent split ends?

A: The best way to prevent split ends is to get regular trims â every 6-8 weeks is recommended. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage.

Q: Can I color my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can color your hair at home, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully and choose a shade that complements your skin tone. If you're not sure how to color your hair at home, it's best to consult a professional stylist.

The Bottom Line

Parting your hair is a simple and effective way to hide a big forehead. Whether you choose a side part, curtain bangs, or full bangs, there are plenty of options to suit your hair type and personal style. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips for maintaining your new hairstyle, and you'll be sure to turn heads wherever you go!
