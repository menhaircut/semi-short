How To Use Thinning Shears On Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are a great tool for people with thick hair. They can help remove some of the bulk and create a more manageable hairstyle. However, using thinning shears on thick hair can be tricky, and it's important to know what you're doing to avoid damaging your hair. In this article, we'll take you through the steps of how to use thinning shears on thick hair.

Description: What are Thinning Shears?

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade. These teeth can help remove some of the hair's bulk and create a more textured look. Thinning shears are commonly used in hair salons, but they can also be used at home.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Thinning Shears

It's important to choose the right type of thinning shears for your hair. If you have thick hair, you'll need a pair of thinning shears with more teeth. Look for thinning shears with between 30 and 40 teeth for best results.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start using your thinning shears, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to see where you need to thin your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections, starting from the bottom. Use hair clips to hold the other sections out of the way.

Step 4: Start Thinning Your Hair

Start thinning your hair by taking a small section and combing it straight. Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and make small cuts along the section. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once.

Step 5: Blend the Sections

Once you've thinned a section, comb through it to blend it with the rest of your hair. Continue thinning and blending your hair section by section.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

As you go along, check your progress in a mirror. Make sure you're thinning your hair evenly and creating the desired look.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you use thinning shears on thick hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a comb to help guide your thinning shears through your hair.
  • Don't thin your hair too much in one area, as this can create bald patches.
  • Take breaks if your hands get tired, as using thinning shears can be taxing on your hands.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about using thinning shears on thick hair:

Can I use thinning shears on wet hair?

No, it's not recommended to use thinning shears on wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to damage and breakage, and using thinning shears on wet hair can cause uneven results.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

It's best to use thinning shears on your hair every 3-4 months, as doing so more frequently can lead to over-thinning and damage.

Hair Care Tips

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair after using thinning shears. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can dry out your hair and cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Using thinning shears on thick hair can be a great way to create a more manageable hairstyle. However, it's important to choose the right thinning shears, section your hair properly, and take your time to avoid damaging your hair. With these tips, you can achieve a beautiful, textured look that you'll love.
