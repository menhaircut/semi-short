What Haircuts Look Good With Round Faces A Comprehensive Guide

If you have a round face, you might be wondering what haircuts would flatter your features. The good news is that there are many hairstyles that can enhance your natural beauty and make your face appear slimmer. In this article, we'll share tips, step-by-step guides, and FAQs about haircuts that look good with round faces. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Face Shape
  • Haircuts that Look Good with Round Faces

How to Determine Your Face Shape

Before we dive into haircuts that look good with round faces, let's first determine if you actually have a round face shape. Here are the steps:

  1. Pull your hair back away from your face.
  2. Stand in front of a mirror with good lighting.
  3. Use a washable marker to trace the outline of your face on the mirror.
  4. Step back and look at the shape you traced.

If your traced shape is roughly the same width and length, you have a round face shape. Other face shapes include oval, square, heart, and diamond.

Haircuts that Look Good with Round Faces

Now that you know your face shape, let's explore haircuts that would complement your features. Here are some options:

1. Long Layers

Long layers can add dimension and movement to a round face. The layers should start at the jawline or below to elongate the face. Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin as they can make the face look wider.

2. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can create an illusion of length and balance a round face. The bangs should be longer on the sides and gradually get shorter towards the center. Avoid blunt bangs that end at the eyebrows as they can make the face look wider.

3. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut can draw attention to the eyes and cheekbones, making a round face look more angular. The cut should have layers and be slightly longer on top. Avoid cuts that are too short or too long as they can make the face look wider.

4. Bob Cut

A bob cut can give the illusion of length and balance a round face. The cut should be longer in the front and gradually get shorter towards the back. Avoid blunt cuts that end at the chin as they can make the face look wider.

5. Shag Cut

A shag cut can add texture and volume to a round face. The cut should have layers and be slightly longer on top. Avoid cuts that are too short or too long as they can make the face look wider.

Tips for Choosing a Haircut for a Round Face

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a haircut for a round face:

  • Avoid cuts that end at the chin as they can make the face look wider.
  • Choose cuts that add height and volume to the crown of the head.
  • Avoid blunt cuts that have no layers.
  • Choose side-swept bangs instead of blunt bangs.
  • Choose cuts that have layers that start at the jawline or below.
  • Opt for styles that are longer in the front and shorter in the back.

FAQs About Haircuts for Round Faces

1. Can I wear a middle part with a round face?

A middle part can make a round face look wider. It's better to opt for a side part or a deep side part.

2. Can I wear a bob cut with a round face?

Yes, you can wear a bob cut with a round face. However, make sure the cut is longer in the front and gradually gets shorter towards the back.

3. Can I wear my hair straight with a round face?

Straight hair can make a round face look wider. It's better to opt for hairstyles that add volume and texture to the hair.

Hair Care Tips for Round Faces

Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind if you have a round face:

  • Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down the hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume to the crown of the head.
  • Avoid hairstyles that pull the hair tightly back as they can make the face look wider.
  • Use a round brush when blow drying your hair to add volume and texture.

By following these tips and choosing the right haircut, you can enhance your natural beauty and make your round face look slimmer. Experiment with different styles and see what works best for you!
