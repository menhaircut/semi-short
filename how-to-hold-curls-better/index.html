How To Hold Curls Better Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Sep 14, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you tired of spending hours curling your hair, only to have the curls fall out after an hour or two? Holding curls can be a challenge, especially if you have fine or straight hair. But don't worry, we've got you covered with some tips and tricks to help you hold curls better.

How to Hold Curls Better: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is wet or damp, the curls won't hold as well.

Step 2: Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the curling iron.

Step 3: Divide your hair into small sections. The smaller the sections, the tighter the curls will be.

Step 4: Wrap each section around the curling iron and hold for 10-15 seconds. Be sure to curl the hair away from your face to create a more natural look.

Step 5: Once all sections are curled, let your hair cool completely before touching it. This will help set the curls in place.

Tips for Holding Curls Better

Tip 1: Use the right curling iron. A smaller barrel will create tighter curls, while a larger barrel will create looser waves.

Tip 2: Don't use too much product. Too much product can weigh down your hair, making it harder for the curls to hold.

Tip 3: Use a hairspray specifically designed for holding curls. These sprays are formulated to help your curls stay in place for longer periods of time.

Tip 4: Sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid to help preserve your curls overnight.

FAQs

Q: How do I prevent my curls from falling out?

A: Be sure to let your hair cool completely before touching it, use a hairspray specifically designed for holding curls, and sleep with your hair in a loose bun or braid.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.

Hair Care for Holding Curls Better

Proper hair care is essential for holding curls better. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools, and avoid using too much product. Overusing product can weigh down your hair, making it harder for curls to hold.

Additionally, be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Dry, damaged hair is more prone to breakage and won't hold curls as well.

By following these tips and tricks, you'll be able to hold curls better and achieve the perfect curly hairstyle you've been dreaming of.
