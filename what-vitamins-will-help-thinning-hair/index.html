What Vitamins Will Help Thinning Hair

Written by Daniel Sep 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're experiencing hair loss or thinning hair, you may be wondering what vitamins can help. While there are several factors that contribute to hair loss, including genetics, stress, and hormonal changes, getting the right nutrients can help support healthy hair growth. In this article, we'll look at some of the best vitamins for thinning hair and how they can help.

Vitamin A

Vitamin A is essential for healthy hair growth because it helps produce sebum, which is the oil that keeps your scalp moisturized. Without enough sebum, your scalp can become dry and itchy, which can lead to hair loss. However, it's important not to overdo it with vitamin A, as too much can actually cause hair loss. The recommended daily intake of vitamin A is 700-900 mcg for adults.

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is crucial for healthy hair growth because it helps produce collagen, a protein that's essential for the structure of your hair. It also helps your body absorb iron, which is important for healthy hair growth. The recommended daily intake of vitamin C is 75-90 mg for adults.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is important for healthy hair growth because it helps your body absorb calcium, which is essential for hair follicle cycling. It also helps reduce inflammation, which can contribute to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of vitamin D is 600-800 IU for adults.

Vitamin E

Vitamin E is an antioxidant that helps protect your hair from damage, which can lead to hair loss. It also helps improve blood circulation, which is important for healthy hair growth. The recommended daily intake of vitamin E is 15 mg for adults.

Biotin

Biotin, also known as vitamin H, is important for healthy hair growth because it helps produce keratin, a protein that's essential for the structure of your hair. It also helps improve scalp circulation, which can promote healthy hair growth. The recommended daily intake of biotin is 30-100 mcg for adults.

Iron

Iron is important for healthy hair growth because it helps your hair cells carry oxygen. Without enough iron, your hair can become thin and brittle, which can lead to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of iron is 18 mg for women and 8 mg for men.

Zinc

Zinc is important for healthy hair growth because it helps your body produce and repair hair tissue. It also helps regulate oil glands on your scalp, which can prevent hair loss. The recommended daily intake of zinc is 8-11 mg for adults.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids

Omega-3 fatty acids are important for healthy hair growth because they help reduce inflammation and improve circulation, which can promote healthy hair follicles. They also help keep your scalp moisturized and prevent dryness, which can lead to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of omega-3 fatty acids is 250-500 mg for adults.

Tips for Healthy Hair

In addition to getting enough vitamins and nutrients, there are several things you can do to promote healthy hair growth:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Reduce stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes hair loss?

Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medications or medical conditions.

Can vitamins really help with hair loss?

While vitamins alone may not be enough to completely reverse hair loss, they can help support healthy hair growth and prevent further damage.

What other nutrients are important for healthy hair?

In addition to the vitamins and nutrients mentioned in this article, other important nutrients for healthy hair include protein, iron, and magnesium.

How long does it take to see results from vitamins for hair loss?

It can take several weeks or even months to see results from taking vitamins for hair loss, as hair growth is a slow process. However, with consistent use and a healthy lifestyle, you can help support healthy hair growth over time.

Can stress cause hair loss?

Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss by disrupting the hair growth cycle and causing hair to enter the resting phase prematurely.
