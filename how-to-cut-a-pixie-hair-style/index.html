How To Cut A Pixie Hair Style In Relaxed English Language

Written by Aprilia Sep 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read
Pixie haircuts have been in style for years, and it's not hard to see why. They're a low-maintenance and versatile haircut that can work on any hair texture and face shape. If you're thinking of cutting your hair into a pixie style, but don't know where to start, this guide is for you. In this article, we will show you how to cut a pixie hair style in relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

A pixie haircut is a short hairstyle that is typically cut close to the scalp on the sides and back and longer on top. It's a great choice for anyone who wants to experiment with a shorter style, as it can be customized to suit a variety of face shapes and hair textures.

How to

Cutting a pixie haircut is a process that requires some skill and knowledge of hair cutting techniques. If you're cutting your hair at home, it's important to have the right tools, including sharp scissors, a comb, and clips to hold sections of hair in place.

Step 1: Choose the Right Pixie Style

Before you start cutting your hair, it's important to decide on the right pixie style for you. There are many different variations, from classic pixies to textured and asymmetrical styles. Consider your face shape and hair texture, as well as your personal style preferences.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair, and then comb it into the style you want. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section off your hair into manageable sections. Start at the back of your head, and work your way forward.

Step 4: Cut the Back and Sides

Using sharp scissors, start cutting the hair at the back of your head. Work your way up to the sides, cutting the hair in small sections. Use a comb to hold the hair in place as you cut, and make sure to cut in a straight line.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Once you've cut the sides and back, it's time to move on to the top. Use the comb to lift sections of hair, and then cut in a straight line. You can choose to cut the hair shorter or longer, depending on the style you're going for.

Step 6: Blend and Texture

Once you've cut all of your hair, it's time to blend and texture the hair to create a more natural look. Use thinning shears or scissors to create texture, and blend the hair using your fingers.

Tips

- If you're not confident in cutting your own hair, it's always best to go to a professional stylist. - Take your time and work in small sections to ensure an even cut. - Use clips to keep the hair in place as you cut. - Use thinning shears or scissors to create texture and blend the hair.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my pixie haircut?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of your pixie haircut.

Q: Can I style my pixie haircut?

A: Yes, there are many ways to style a pixie haircut, including adding texture, using hair accessories, and using styling products.

Hair Care

To maintain your pixie haircut, it's important to use the right hair care products. Look for products that are designed for short hair, and avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair. Use a lightweight conditioner to keep your hair soft and hydrated, and consider using a dry shampoo to extend the time between washes. In conclusion, cutting a pixie haircut is a great way to experiment with a shorter style. By following these simple steps and using the right tools and products, you can create a stylish and low-maintenance look that works for you.
