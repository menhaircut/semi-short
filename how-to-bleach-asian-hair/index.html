How To Bleach Asian Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Bleaching Asian hair can be a daunting task, especially if you are not familiar with the process. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the perfect blonde look that you desire. In this guide, we will take you through the steps on how to bleach Asian hair, as well as some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Bleach Asian Hair

Bleaching Asian hair is a process that involves removing the pigment from your hair strands. This process requires the use of bleach, which can be harsh on your hair. Therefore, it is important to take the necessary precautions to protect your hair and scalp during the process.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to bleach Asian hair:

Step 1: Choose the Right Bleach

The first step in bleaching Asian hair is choosing the right bleach. There are different types of bleach available in the market, such as powder bleach and cream bleach. Powder bleach is more potent and is best used for heavy-duty bleaching, while cream bleach is best used for lighter bleaching.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before bleaching your hair, it is important to prepare it. This involves washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or oils that may interfere with the bleaching process. It is also important to protect your skin by applying a barrier cream around your hairline and ears.

Step 3: Mix the Bleach

Once you have chosen the right bleach, it is time to mix it. Follow the instructions on the bleach package to mix it properly. Make sure to use gloves and a non-metallic bowl and brush to mix the bleach.

Step 4: Apply the Bleach

Apply the bleach to your hair using a brush, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Make sure to apply the bleach evenly and saturate your hair thoroughly.

Step 5: Check the Bleach

Check on the bleach every 10 minutes to see how it is working. The processing time may vary depending on the level of lightness you want to achieve. Once you have achieved the desired level of lightness, rinse the bleach out of your hair with cool water.

Step 6: Tone Your Hair

After bleaching your hair, it is important to tone it to remove any brassy or yellow tones. Use a toner that is specifically designed for bleached hair to achieve the perfect blonde shade.

Tips for Bleaching Asian Hair

Here are some tips to make the process of bleaching Asian hair easier:

Tip 1: Do a Strand Test

Before bleaching your entire head, it is important to do a strand test to see how your hair will react to the bleach. This can help you avoid any potential damage to your hair.

Tip 2: Use a Deep Conditioner

Bleaching can be harsh on your hair, so it is important to use a deep conditioner to restore moisture and prevent damage.

Tip 3: Avoid Overlapping

Overlapping the bleach onto previously bleached hair can cause damage and breakage. Make sure to apply the bleach only to the unbleached hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about bleaching Asian hair:

Q: Can I bleach my hair if it is already dyed?

A: Yes, you can bleach your hair if it is already dyed. However, it is important to take extra precautions to avoid damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I bleach my hair?

A: It is best to avoid bleaching your hair too often, as it can cause damage and breakage. Only bleach your hair when necessary, and make sure to use a deep conditioner to restore moisture.

Q: Can I bleach my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can bleach your hair at home. However, it is important to follow the instructions carefully and take the necessary precautions to avoid damage to your hair and scalp.

Hair Care After Bleaching

Bleaching can be harsh on your hair, so it is important to take extra care of your hair after the process. Here are some tips to help you care for your hair after bleaching:

Tip 1: Use a Deep Conditioner

Use a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair. Leave the conditioner on for at least 10 minutes before rinsing it out.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can cause further damage to your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling wands as much as possible.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors by wearing a hat or using a hair serum with SPF.

Bleaching Asian hair can be a challenging process, but with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the perfect blonde shade. Make sure to take the necessary precautions to protect your hair and scalp during the process, and follow the tips for caring for your hair after bleaching.
