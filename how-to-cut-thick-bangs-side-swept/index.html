How To Cut Thick Bangs Side Swept A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting thick bangs side swept can be a great option. Not only does it frame your face beautifully, but it also adds a touch of sophistication to your overall look. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut thick bangs side swept in a relaxed manner, without having to go to the salon. We'll cover everything from the tools you'll need to the step-by-step process, tips, and tricks, and even some haircare advice to keep your new cut looking fresh. So, let's get started!

Tools You'll Need

Before we get into the actual cutting process, here are the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle
  • Haircutting cape or towel

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and blow-drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you start cutting. Comb your hair forward and separate the section you want to cut into a triangle shape. The point of the triangle should be at the center of your forehead.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way. Make sure you have a clear view of the section you're cutting.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Using your comb, hold the hair section straight down and determine the length you want to cut. For side-swept bangs, it's best to start with a longer length and gradually cut shorter if needed.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Using your scissors, cut your hair in a straight line. Start from the center and work your way towards the sides. Make sure you're cutting the hair in a downward motion and not straight across. This will create a softer, blended look.

Step 5: Check the Length

Once you've cut the center section, check the length and adjust if necessary. Remember, it's better to start longer and gradually cut shorter.

Step 6: Cut the Sides

Now it's time to cut the sides. Hold the hair section outwards and cut in a diagonal line towards the length you just cut in the center. Make sure both sides are even.

Step 7: Check the Symmetry

Check the symmetry of your bangs by comparing them side by side. If one side is longer, trim it until both sides are even.

Step 8: Blend the Edges

Using your scissors, blend the edges of your bangs to create a natural look. This will also help soften any harsh lines.

Step 9: Style Your Bangs

Use a water spray bottle to wet your bangs and style them as desired. You can use a round brush and a blow dryer to create volume, or a straightener for a sleeker look.

Step 10: Maintain Your Bangs

To keep your new cut looking fresh, trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks. You can also use a dry shampoo to absorb any excess oil and keep your bangs from getting greasy.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut shorter if needed
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Don't try to cut your bangs when you're in a rush or feeling stressed

FAQ

Can I cut my own thick bangs side swept?

Yes, you can cut your own thick bangs side swept as long as you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

What if I mess up?

If you mess up, don't panic. You can always go to a salon and have a professional fix your bangs.

Hair Care

To keep your new cut looking fresh, make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly. Use a heat protectant when styling with hot tools, and avoid using too much product on your bangs as it can weigh them down.

Cutting thick bangs side swept can be a great way to switch up your look. With the right tools and following the steps carefully, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and maintain your bangs regularly for the best results.
