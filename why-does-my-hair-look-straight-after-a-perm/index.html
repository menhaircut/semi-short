Why Does My Hair Look Straight After A Perm

Written by Christine Sep 25, 2023 · 5 min read
If you've recently gotten a perm and noticed that your hair looks straight, you're not alone. This can be a frustrating experience, especially if you were hoping for bouncy, curly locks. But don't worry – there are a few reasons why this might be happening, and some things you can do to help your curls bounce back.

Description

A perm is a chemical treatment that changes the structure of your hair, allowing it to hold a new shape. Typically, this means creating curls or waves in hair that is naturally straight. However, sometimes the results of a perm can be unpredictable, and your hair may end up looking straight instead of curly.

How to

To understand why this happens, it's important to know a bit about how perms work. During the perm process, a solution is applied to your hair that breaks down the bonds that hold its natural shape. Once these bonds are broken, your hair is wrapped around perm rods and a neutralizer is applied to set the new shape. If your hair looks straight after a perm, it could be because the solution wasn't left on long enough to fully break down the bonds in your hair. Alternatively, it could be because the perm rods used were too large, or were wrapped too loosely around your hair. In some cases, it's also possible that the neutralizer wasn't applied correctly, which can lead to the perm not taking as well.

Step by Step

If you're unhappy with the results of your perm, there are a few things you can do to help your curls bounce back. Here's a step-by-step guide: Step 1: Wait it out. Sometimes, it can take a few days for your curls to fully develop after a perm. Give your hair some time to settle before you decide whether or not you're happy with the results. Step 2: Talk to your stylist. If you're still unhappy with your hair after a few days, talk to your stylist. They may be able to suggest ways to help your curls develop, or they may offer to redo the perm for you. Step 3: Use a curl-enhancing product. There are plenty of products on the market that are designed to help enhance curls and waves. Look for a product that is specifically designed for permed hair, and apply it according to the instructions. Step 4: Experiment with styling. Sometimes, the way you style your hair can make a big difference in how your curls look. Try using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair, or experiment with different curling techniques to help enhance your curls.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to help you get the most out of your permed hair: - Don't wash your hair for at least 48 hours after getting a perm. This will help your curls set properly. - Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. Heat can damage permed hair and cause it to lose its shape. - Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and hydrated. - Sleep on a silk pillowcase to help reduce frizz and maintain your curls.

FAQ

Q: How long does a perm last? A: Perms typically last between 4 and 6 months, although this can vary depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of it. Q: Can I color my hair after getting a perm? A: It's generally recommended that you wait at least 2 weeks after getting a perm before coloring your hair. This will give your hair time to recover from the chemical treatment. Q: Can I still straighten my hair after getting a perm? A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair after getting a perm. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using high heat settings on your styling tools.

Hair Care

Taking care of permed hair requires a bit of extra effort, but it's worth it to maintain your new curly locks. Here are a few tips to help you care for your permed hair: - Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help keep your hair healthy. - Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and frizz. - Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair when it's damp. - Avoid using hair ties that are too tight, as this can cause damage and breakage. - Get regular trims to help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. By following these tips and taking good care of your permed hair, you can help ensure that your curls stay bouncy and beautiful. Remember, it may take a bit of time and experimentation to find the right styling techniques and products that work best for you, but with a bit of patience and persistence, you'll be able to achieve the curly hair of your dreams.
