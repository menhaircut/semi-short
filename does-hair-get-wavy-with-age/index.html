Does Hair Get Wavy With Age

Written by Petter Oct 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Welcome to this blog post where we will be discussing whether or not hair gets wavy with age. As we age, our hair goes through a lot of changes and some of these changes can be quite surprising. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon, how to embrace your wavy hair, and some tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Embrace Your Wavy Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQ

Description

As we age, the shape of our hair follicles changes, which can result in a change in texture. This can cause hair to become wavy or even curly, even if it was straight for most of our lives. The reason behind this is that the hair follicles start to produce less sebum, which is the natural oil that keeps our hair straight. As a result, the hair starts to lose its straightness and becomes more prone to waviness or curliness.

Genetics can also play a role in whether or not your hair becomes wavy with age. If your parents or grandparents have wavy or curly hair, it's more likely that you will experience this change in texture as well.

How to Embrace Your Wavy Hair

If you've noticed that your hair has become wavy or curly with age, it's important to embrace this change and learn how to work with your new texture. Here are some tips:

1. Invest in the Right Products

Choosing the right products is essential when it comes to managing wavy or curly hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type, such as sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, leave-in conditioners, and styling creams.

2. Use the Right Tools

Using the right tools can make a big difference when it comes to managing wavy hair. Invest in a good quality hair dryer with a diffuser attachment, a wide-tooth comb, and a brush with natural bristles.

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Instead of fighting your new texture, embrace it! Experiment with different hairstyles that showcase your waves or curls, such as a messy bun, a braided crown, or a half-up, half-down style.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you manage your wavy or curly hair:

1. Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by shampooing and conditioning your hair with products that are specifically designed for wavy or curly hair. Sulfate-free products are a great option, as they won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair while it's still damp. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will help to prevent breakage and damage.

4. Use a Diffuser Attachment

Attach a diffuser to your hair dryer and use it to dry your hair. This will help to enhance your natural waves or curls.

5. Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it using a brush with natural bristles or your fingers. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some additional tips for managing wavy or curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and frizzy. Aim to wash your hair no more than two or three times a week.

2. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to prevent breakage and keep your hair hydrated.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.

FAQ

Q: Will everyone's hair become wavy with age?

A: No, not everyone's hair will become wavy or curly with age. It depends on a variety of factors, including genetics and changes in the hair follicles.

Q: Can I still straighten my hair if it's become wavy with age?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your hair if it's become wavy with age. Just be sure to use heat protectant products and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

Q: Can I dye my hair if it's become wavy with age?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair if it's become wavy with age. Just be sure to use products that are designed for color-treated hair and avoid using harsh chemicals.

In conclusion, hair can become wavy or curly with age due to changes in the hair follicles and genetics. Instead of fighting this change, it's important to embrace it and learn how to work with your new texture. By investing in the right products and tools, and by following the tips for hair care, you can manage your wavy or curly hair with ease.
