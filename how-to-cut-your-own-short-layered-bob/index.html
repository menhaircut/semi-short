How To Cut Your Own Short Layered Bob

Written by William Jun 06, 2023 · 5 min read

If you’re looking for a chic and stylish haircut that’s easy to maintain, a short layered bob might be the perfect choice for you. This versatile haircut can be customized to suit your face shape and hair type, and it’s easy to maintain at home. In this post, we’ll show you how to cut your own short layered bob in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Description

A short layered bob is a haircut that features layers of different lengths, usually ranging from chin-length to just below the ears. This hairstyle is perfect for those who want a low-maintenance yet stylish look. Short layered bobs can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types, making it a versatile choice for women of all ages.

How to Cut Your Own Short Layered Bob

If you’re ready to cut your own short layered bob, follow these steps:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the tools you need. You’ll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the top, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back section of your hair. Comb it straight down and use your scissors to cut it to the desired length. Make sure to cut in a straight line.

Step 4: Layer the Back

After you’ve cut the back section, it’s time to layer it. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, starting from the top and working your way down. This will create a layered effect.

Step 5: Cut the Sides

Move on to the sides of your hair. Comb each section straight down and cut it to the desired length. Make sure to cut in a straight line.

Step 6: Layer the Sides

After you’ve cut the sides, it’s time to layer them. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, starting from the top and working your way down. This will create a layered effect that blends in with the back section.

Step 7: Cut the Top

Finally, it’s time to cut the top section of your hair. Use your comb to lift the hair straight up and cut it to the desired length. Make sure to cut in a straight line.

Step 8: Layer the Top

After you’ve cut the top section, it’s time to layer it. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, starting from the top and working your way down. This will create a layered effect that blends in with the back and side sections.

Step 9: Check for Evenness

Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, check for evenness. Use your comb to comb your hair straight down and make sure that all the layers are even. If you notice any unevenness, use your scissors to trim the hair until it’s all even.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Now that you’ve cut your own short layered bob, it’s time to style it. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to create volume and shape. You can also use a straightener or curling iron to create different looks.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own short layered bob:

  • Start with small cuts – you can always cut more, but you can’t glue the hair back on!
  • Use sharp scissors – dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Take your time – rushing can lead to mistakes.
  • Don’t cut too much – it’s better to cut a little at a time.
  • Have a backup plan – if you’re not happy with your haircut, have a hairstylist on standby to fix it.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own short layered bob:

Can I cut my own short layered bob?

Yes, you can cut your own short layered bob, but it’s important to have the right tools and take your time.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always go to a hairstylist to fix it. Alternatively, you can try to style your hair differently to hide the mistake.

How often should I cut my short layered bob?

You should cut your short layered bob every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking fresh.

Hair Care

To keep your short layered bob looking its best, make sure to take care of your hair. This includes washing it regularly, using a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. If you do use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can enjoy a chic and stylish short layered bob that’s easy to maintain and looks great.
