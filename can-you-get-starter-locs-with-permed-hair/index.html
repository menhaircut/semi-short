Can You Get Starter Locs With Permed Hair

Written by Eveline Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have permed hair and are considering getting starter locs, you might be wondering if it's possible. The short answer is yes, you can get starter locs with permed hair. However, there are some things you need to consider before making the decision to start your loc journey.

Description

Permed hair refers to hair that has been chemically treated to create curls or waves. This is also known as a relaxer or a perm. Starter locs, on the other hand, are the beginning stages of locked hair. This is achieved by allowing the hair to mat and tangle over time, eventually forming into solid, rope-like structures.

How To

The process of getting starter locs with permed hair is similar to getting them with natural hair. The main difference is that you will need to be extra careful during the locking process to prevent breakage and damage. Here's how to get starter locs with permed hair:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional

Before starting your loc journey, it's important to consult with a professional stylist who has experience working with permed hair. They can assess the condition of your hair and determine if it's healthy enough to start the locking process.

Step 2: Stop Relaxing Your Hair

Once you've decided to start your loc journey, you will need to stop relaxing your hair. This is because the chemicals in relaxers can weaken your hair and make it more prone to breakage during the locking process.

Step 3: Start the Locking Process

The locking process involves allowing your hair to mat and tangle over time. This can take several months to a year or more, depending on your hair type and the method you choose. Some popular methods for starting locs include braids, twists, and palm rolling.

Step 4: Maintain Your New Locs

Once your hair has started to lock, it's important to maintain your new locs to keep them healthy and looking their best. This includes regular washing, conditioning, and retwisting as needed.

Tips

Here are some tips to help ensure a successful transition to starter locs with permed hair:

  • Be patient - the locking process takes time
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin bonnet or scarf
  • Stay hydrated and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting starter locs with permed hair:

Can I start my locs with permed hair?

Yes, you can start your locs with permed hair. However, you will need to be extra careful during the locking process to prevent breakage and damage.

How long does it take for starter locs to form?

The amount of time it takes for starter locs to form varies depending on your hair type and the method you choose. It can take several months to a year or more.

Can I wash my hair while it's locking?

Yes, you can and should wash your hair while it's locking. However, you will need to be gentle to avoid causing damage or unraveling your new locs.

Hair Care

Once your hair has fully locked, it's important to continue taking care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining your locs:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Deep condition your hair every 2-4 weeks
  • Avoid using heavy products that can build up in your hair
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by avoiding hot tools

With proper care and maintenance, your starter locs can grow into beautiful, healthy locs that will last for years to come.
