How To Cut A Mullet With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking for a new hairstyle that's both edgy and retro, a mullet might just be the perfect choice for you. And the best part is, you can easily cut it yourself at home with just a pair of clippers. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting a mullet with clippers, from start to finish.

How to Cut a Mullet with Clippers

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, let's talk about the tools you'll need. Here's what you'll need to cut a mullet with clippers:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A mirror
  • Hair clips (optional)

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink and lead to uneven cuts. Once your hair is dry, comb it out to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into three parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate if necessary.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Start with the sides of your hair. Use the clippers with a guard that's one or two sizes shorter than what you plan to use on the top. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the comb to lift sections of hair and trim them to the desired length.

Step 4: Cut the Top

Once you've finished the sides, move on to the top of your hair. Use a longer guard than what you used on the sides, as you want the top to be longer than the sides. Start at the front and work your way back, using the comb to lift sections of hair and trim them to the desired length.

Step 5: Cut the Back

The back of your hair is where the mullet really comes to life. Use a shorter guard than what you used on the sides, but longer than what you used on the top. Start at the nape of your neck and work your way up, using the comb to lift sections of hair and trim them to the desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Sections

Now that you've cut all three sections of your hair, it's time to blend them together. Use the clippers without a guard to blend the sides and the back, creating a seamless transition. Use scissors to blend the top and the sides, creating a natural-looking flow from one section to the other.

Step 7: Finishing Touches

Once you're happy with the length and shape of your mullet, use scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. Use the clippers to clean up the hairline around your ears and neck.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet with Clippers

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut your mullet:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if necessary. It's easier to take more off than to put it back on.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from different angles.
  • Take breaks if you need them. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, and it's important to take your time to avoid mistakes.

FAQ

How long should a mullet be?

A traditional mullet has short hair on the sides and longer hair in the back. The length of the back can vary, but it should be at least a few inches longer than the sides.

Can you cut a mullet with scissors?

While you can technically cut a mullet with scissors, clippers are much easier to use and will give you a more uniform cut.

How do I style my mullet?

The beauty of a mullet is that it can be styled in many different ways. You can use product to give it texture and volume, or you can keep it sleek and smooth. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Your Mullet

Now that you've cut your mullet, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use product to style your hair, but don't overdo it. Too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your mullet looking neat and tidy.

Cutting a mullet with clippers is easier than you might think, and it's a great way to give yourself a bold new look. With a little practice and patience, you'll be rocking a stylish mullet in no time.
