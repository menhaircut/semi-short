Does A Fade Have To Be A Skin Fade

Written by Luffy Oct 17, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're a fan of short haircuts, you've probably heard of the fade. It's a popular haircut that involves cutting the hair short at the bottom and gradually tapering it upwards. However, there's a common misconception that a fade has to be a skin fade, meaning the hair is cut down to the scalp. But is that really the case? Let's dive into it!

Description

A fade is a type of haircut that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom of the head. This creates a smooth transition from longer hair at the top to shorter hair at the bottom. The fade can be done with clippers or scissors and can be customized to different lengths and styles.

How to

If you're interested in getting a fade, here's how it's typically done:

  1. Choose the length you want your hair to be at the top
  2. Decide on the length you want your hair to gradually taper towards the bottom
  3. Use clippers or scissors to create the desired length at the bottom and gradually blend it upwards
  4. Finish off with a clean line-up around the edges

Step by Step

1. Start by sectioning the hair at the top and bottom.

2. Choose the guard size for the clippers or scissors.

3. Start at the bottom of the head and cut in an upward motion to the desired length.

4. Gradually increase the guard size as you move upwards towards the top.

5. Blend the hair using scissors or clippers until you achieve a smooth transition.

6. Finish off with a clean line-up around the edges of the hairline.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting a fade:

  • Communicate clearly with your barber or stylist about the length and style you want
  • Bring reference photos to show the exact look you're going for
  • Ask for their advice on which length and style would suit your face shape and hair texture
  • Maintain the fade with regular touch-ups every few weeks

FAQ

What's the difference between a fade and taper?

A fade involves cutting the hair shorter towards the bottom, while a taper involves gradually trimming the hair shorter towards the neckline. The difference is in the length and the area of the hair that's being cut.

Can I get a fade with longer hair?

Yes, you can get a fade with longer hair. The length of the hair at the top and the length of the fade can be customized to your preference.

Do I have to get a skin fade?

No, you don't have to get a skin fade. A fade can be customized to different lengths, including a longer taper that doesn't involve cutting the hair down to the scalp.

Hair Care

To maintain a fade, it's important to keep it clean and moisturized. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type and texture. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep the hair hydrated and prevent dryness. Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.

Overall, a fade doesn't have to be a skin fade. It's a versatile haircut that can be customized to different lengths and styles. Communicate with your barber or stylist to achieve the desired look, and maintain it with regular touch-ups and proper hair care.
