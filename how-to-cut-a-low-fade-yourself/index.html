How To Cut A Low Fade Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a lot of money at the barbershop for a low fade? With a little bit of practice and patience, you can learn how to cut a low fade yourself at home. In this article, we will provide you with an easy-to-follow guide on how to cut a low fade yourself.

How to Cut a Low Fade Yourself – Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting a low fade is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Clippers
  • Clipper guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Barber brush

Make sure all your tools are clean and in good working condition.

Step 2: Decide on the Length of the Fade

The next step is to decide on the length of the fade. The length of the fade depends on personal preference, but generally, a low fade starts at the hairline and gradually fades into the skin at the nape of the neck.

Step 3: Start with the Top

Before starting the fade, you need to cut the top of the hair to the desired length. Use scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Use a comb to make sure the hair is even.

Step 4: Start the Fade

Attach the clipper guard that corresponds to the desired length of the fade. Start at the hairline and work your way down the back of the head. Use the clipper without a guard to create a bald line at the nape of the neck. This will create a seamless transition from the fade to the skin.

Step 5: Blend the Fade

Remove the clipper guard and use a lower number guard to blend the fade. Use the clipper to create a smooth transition between the hair and the bald line. Move the clipper in an upward motion to create a gradient effect.

Step 6: Trim the Sides

Use the clipper with the desired guard to trim the sides of the hair. Use the clipper to create a straight line around the ear.

Step 7: Clean Up the Neckline

Use the clipper without a guard to clean up the neckline. Begin at the center of the nape and move outwards. Use a comb to make sure the line is straight.

Step 8: Clean Up the Edges

Use the scissors to clean up any stray hairs around the edges. Use the barber brush to remove any loose hairs.

Step 9: Wash and Style the Hair

After you have finished cutting the hair, wash it with shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry the hair and style it as desired.

Tips for Cutting a Low Fade Yourself

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a low fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Practice makes perfect

FAQs

What is a low fade?

A low fade is a haircut that starts at the hairline and gradually fades into the skin at the nape of the neck.

What tools do I need to cut a low fade?

You will need clippers, clipper guards, scissors, comb, haircutting cape or towel, and a barber brush.

Can I cut a low fade myself?

Yes, with a little bit of practice and patience, you can learn how to cut a low fade yourself.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it is important to take care of it. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy
  • Use a hair mask to keep your hair moisturized

Cutting a low fade yourself may seem intimidating at first, but with practice and patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these easy steps to get the perfect low fade.
