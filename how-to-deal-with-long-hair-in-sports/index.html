How To Deal With Long Hair In Sports

Written by Robby Jul 28, 2023 Â· 3 min read

The struggle of having long hair in sports can be real. Not only can it be distracting, but it can also get in the way of your performance. In this guide, we will provide tips and tricks for dealing with long hair in sports.

Table of Contents

  • How to Deal with Long Hair in Sports
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Active Individuals

How to Deal with Long Hair in Sports

The first step to dealing with long hair in sports is to find a hairstyle that works for you. Opt for hairstyles that keep your hair away from your face and neck to minimize distractions and discomfort. Some popular hairstyles for sports include:

  • Ponytail
  • Braids
  • Bun
  • Headband

Once you find a hairstyle that works for you, it's important to secure it properly. Use hair ties, bobby pins, and headbands to ensure your hair stays in place throughout your activity.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to ensure your long hair stays secured during sports:

  1. Brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots.
  2. Choose a hairstyle that works for you.
  3. Secure your hair with hair ties, bobby pins, and/or headbands.
  4. Use hair spray or gel to keep any flyaways in place.
  5. Double-check that your hair is secure before starting your activity.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for dealing with long hair in sports:

  • Invest in sweat-wicking headbands to prevent sweat from getting into your eyes.
  • Use hair elastics without metal to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Bring extra hair ties and bobby pins to ensure you always have backups.
  • Consider wearing a helmet or hat for added protection and to keep your hair contained.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear my hair down during sports?

A: It's not recommended to wear your hair down during sports, as it can get in the way and cause distractions. Opt for a secure hairstyle instead.

Q: What if my hair gets sweaty during sports?

A: Use dry shampoo or baby powder to absorb oil and sweat from your scalp. You can also use a hair dryer on a cool setting to dry your hair after your activity.

Hair Care for Active Individuals

It's important to take care of your hair, especially if you're physically active. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as straighteners and curling irons.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product with SPF.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can easily deal with long hair in sports and focus on your performance. Remember to find a hairstyle that works for you and always secure it properly before starting your activity.
