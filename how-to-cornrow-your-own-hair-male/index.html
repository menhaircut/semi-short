How To Cornrow Your Own Hair Male A Step By Step Guide

If you're a male looking to cornrow your own hair, you've come to the right place. Cornrows are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries and can be worn by people of all genders. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cornrowing your own hair, from how to section the hair to how to braid it. We'll also provide tips for maintaining your cornrows and answer some frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Braiding Your Hair
  • Maintaining Your Cornrows
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

Sectioning Your Hair

The first step to cornrowing your own hair is to section it. This will make it easier to braid and ensure that your cornrows are neat and even. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will make it easier to manage and prevent breakage.
  2. Use a comb to part your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your cornrows to be. Use small sections for tighter cornrows and larger sections for looser cornrows.
  3. Clip or tie off each section of hair to keep it separate from the others.
  4. Repeat this process until all of your hair is sectioned.

Once your hair is sectioned, you're ready to start braiding.

Braiding Your Hair

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start braiding. Here's how to cornrow your own hair male:

  1. Choose a section of hair to start with. Make sure it's clean and free from tangles.
  2. Divide the section into three equal parts.
  3. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section.
  4. Add a small section of hair to the right section, then cross it over the middle section.
  5. Add a small section of hair to the left section, then cross it over the middle section.
  6. Repeat steps 4-5, adding small sections of hair to each side as you go.
  7. Continue braiding until you reach the end of the section.
  8. Tie off the braid with a small elastic band to keep it in place.
  9. Repeat this process on each section of hair until you've cornrowed your entire head.

Once you've finished braiding, you're ready to maintain your cornrows.

Maintaining Your Cornrows

To keep your cornrows looking fresh, here are a few tips:

  • Wrap your hair with a silk scarf or bonnet at night to prevent frizz and maintain the shape of your cornrows.
  • Wash your hair every 1-2 weeks, being careful not to disturb the cornrows.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your cornrows, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cornrow my hair if it's short?

Yes, you can cornrow short hair. However, you may need to use extensions to achieve the desired length and thickness.

How long do cornrows last?

Cornrows can last anywhere from 2-6 weeks, depending on how well you maintain them.

Can I wash my hair with cornrows?

Yes, you can wash your hair with cornrows. Just be sure to be gentle and avoid disturbing the braids.

Do I need to use product on my hair before braiding?

It's not necessary to use product on your hair before braiding, but it can help to make the braids smoother and easier to manage.

Hair Care Tips

Here are a few general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and clean.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote hair growth and overall health.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

With this guide, you should be able to cornrow your own hair male. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. With practice, you'll be able to create a variety of cornrow styles that are both stylish and functional.
