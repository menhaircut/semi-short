Are you looking for a small dog that doesn't shed hair? Look no further! In this article, we will discuss the best small dog breeds that are hypoallergenic and don't shed hair. We will also provide tips on how to care for their coats and answer some frequently asked questions about these breeds.

Top Small Dog Breeds That Don't Shed Hair

1. Bichon Frise - This breed is known for its curly, hypoallergenic coat that doesn't shed much. They are also friendly, affectionate, and great with kids.

2. Poodle - Poodles come in various sizes, including toy and miniature, making them a great choice for small dog lovers. They are highly intelligent, loyal, and don't shed much.

3. Shih Tzu - This breed has a long, flowing coat that doesn't shed much, but it does require regular grooming. They are friendly, outgoing, and make great lap dogs.

4. Maltese - These small dogs have a silky, hypoallergenic coat that doesn't shed much. They are playful, affectionate, and love to be with their owners.

5. Yorkshire Terrier - Yorkies have a long, silky coat that doesn't shed much, but it does require regular grooming. They are energetic, loyal, and make great companions.

How to Care for a Small Dog That Doesn't Shed Hair

While these breeds don't shed much, they still require regular grooming to keep their coats healthy and tangle-free. Here are some tips on how to care for your small dog that doesn't shed hair:

1. Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting and tangles.

2. Bathe your dog as needed, but not too often, as this can dry out their skin.

3. Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from getting too long and causing discomfort.

4. Clean your dog's ears regularly to prevent infections.

5. Take your dog to the vet for regular check-ups and vaccinations.

Tips for Living with a Small Dog That Doesn't Shed Hair

1. Invest in a good vacuum cleaner to keep your home clean and free of dog hair.

2. Use a lint roller or pet hair remover to remove any stray hairs from your clothes and furniture.

3. Allow your dog to have their own designated space, such as a crate or bed.

4. Provide your dog with plenty of toys and exercise to keep them happy and healthy.

5. Consider hiring a professional groomer to help maintain your dog's coat.

Frequently Asked Questions about Small Dogs That Don't Shed Hair

Q: Are these breeds good with kids?

A: Yes, these breeds are generally good with kids and make great family pets.

Q: Do these breeds require a lot of exercise?

A: It depends on the breed, but most small dogs don't require a lot of exercise and are happy with short walks or playtime indoors.

Q: Do these breeds bark a lot?

A: It depends on the individual dog, but most small dogs are known for their tendency to bark.

Hair Care Tips for Small Dogs That Don't Shed Hair

1. Use a slicker brush to remove any loose hairs and prevent matting.

2. Use a detangling spray to help loosen any stubborn tangles.

3. Trim your dog's coat regularly to maintain its shape and prevent it from becoming too long.

4. Use a conditioning spray to keep your dog's coat soft and shiny.

5. Consider getting your dog a professional haircut every few months to keep their coat looking its best.


In conclusion, there are many small dog breeds that don't shed hair and are perfect for people with allergies or those who don't want to deal with the mess of shedding. With proper grooming and care, these breeds can make great companions and family pets.
