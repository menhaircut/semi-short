What Gel For Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Sep 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it looks fabulous when styled properly, it can be challenging to manage, especially when it comes to finding the right products. That's where hair gel comes in. With the right gel, you can define your curls, reduce frizz, and achieve the perfect hairstyle. In this guide, we'll tell you everything you need to know about what gel for curly hair.

Description

Gel is a hair styling product that comes in a variety of forms, including thick and thin consistency, and varying levels of hold. It is commonly used to define curls, reduce frizz, and create a sleek, polished look. Gel is often made with water-soluble polymers, which means it can be easily washed out with water.

How to Use Gel for Curly Hair

The key to using gel for curly hair is to apply it when your hair is wet. Here's how:

  1. Start with freshly washed hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and moisturize your curls.
  3. Section your hair into manageable sections.
  4. Take a small amount of gel and apply it to each section, working it through your curls from root to tip.
  5. Scrunch your hair to encourage curl formation.
  6. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser to dry your curls.

Remember, less is more when it comes to gel. Start with a small amount and add more as needed.

Step by Step Guide on How to Choose the Right Gel for Your Curly Hair

Choosing the right gel for your curly hair can be a daunting task, but with these steps, you'll be able to find the perfect product for your hair.

Step 1: Determine Your Hair Porosity

Knowing your hair porosity is essential when choosing hair products. Hair porosity refers to how easily your hair can absorb and retain moisture. There are three types of hair porosity: low, medium, and high.

If you have low porosity hair, look for gels that are lightweight and won't weigh down your curls. If you have high porosity hair, look for gels that are moisturizing and can help retain moisture in your hair.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture

Curly hair comes in a variety of textures, from loose waves to tight coils. The texture of your hair will determine the type of gel you should use. If you have fine hair, look for gels that are lightweight and won't weigh down your curls. If you have coarse hair, look for gels that are thick and moisturizing.

Step 3: Look for the Right Ingredients

When choosing a gel for curly hair, look for products that contain ingredients that will help define your curls and reduce frizz. Some of the best ingredients to look for include:

  • Aloe vera
  • Glycerin
  • Shea butter
  • Jojoba oil
  • Castor oil
  • Hydrolyzed wheat protein

Step 4: Consider the Hold Level

Gels come in varying levels of hold, from light to strong. The level of hold you need will depend on the texture of your hair and the style you want to achieve. If you have fine hair, look for gels with a light hold. If you have thick hair, look for gels with a strong hold.

Tips for Using Gel for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when using gel for curly hair:

  • Apply gel to wet hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair before applying gel
  • Scrunch your hair to encourage curl formation
  • Avoid touching your hair while it dries to reduce frizz
  • Don't use too much gel, as it can weigh down your curls

FAQ

Can I use gel on dry hair?

While it's best to apply gel to wet hair, you can use it on dry hair to refresh your curls. Simply dampen your hair with water or a leave-in conditioner, apply a small amount of gel, and scrunch your hair to revive your curls.

How often should I use gel on my curly hair?

It's best to use gel on your curly hair every time you wash it. However, if you find that your hair becomes weighed down or greasy, try using gel every other wash day.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Using the right gel is just one part of caring for your curly hair. Here are some additional tips to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to reduce frizz
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils

With the right gel and hair care routine, you can achieve the perfect curls every time. Remember to experiment with different products until you find the perfect one for your hair type and texture.
