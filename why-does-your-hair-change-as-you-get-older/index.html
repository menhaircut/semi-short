Why Does Your Hair Change As You Get Older

Written by Aprilia Aug 16, 2023 · 4 min read

As we age, our body goes through many changes and one of those changes is our hair. If you have noticed that your hair is not the same as it was when you were younger, you are not alone. Many people experience changes in their hair as they get older. In this article, we will discuss why does your hair change as you get older and what you can do to keep your hair healthy and looking its best.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

As we age, our hair goes through a natural process of aging. As we get older, the hair follicles shrink, and the hair becomes thinner and weaker. The production of melanin, the pigment that gives our hair its color, also decreases. This results in gray or white hair. The oil glands that produce sebum, the natural oil that keeps our hair moisturized, also decrease. This can result in dry, brittle hair that is prone to breakage.

How To

There is no way to stop the natural aging process of our hair, but there are things we can do to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy. Foods that are good for your hair include leafy greens, eggs, nuts, and fish.

2. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Aim for at least eight glasses of water per day.

3. Use Gentle Hair Products

As we age, our hair becomes more fragile and prone to damage. Use gentle hair products that are designed for your hair type to avoid causing further damage.

4. Protect Your Hair From the Sun

The sun can damage your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

Step By Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for keeping your hair healthy as you age:

1. Wash Your Hair Less Frequently

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every two days.

2. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Using a deep conditioning treatment once a week can help keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Apply the treatment to your hair, leave it on for 10-15 minutes, and then rinse it out.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, keeping your hair healthy and strong.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for keeping your hair healthy as you age:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Avoid using them as much as possible, or use them on a lower heat setting.

2. Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep can help keep your hair healthy and reduce stress, which can contribute to hair loss.

FAQ

Q: Can stress cause hair loss?

A: Yes, stress can contribute to hair loss. When we are stressed, our body produces hormones that can affect the hair growth cycle.

Q: Is it normal to lose hair as you age?

A: Yes, it is normal to lose hair as you age. However, if you are experiencing excessive hair loss, you should talk to your doctor.

Hair Care

As we age, our hair requires more care to keep it healthy and looking its best. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful at any age.
