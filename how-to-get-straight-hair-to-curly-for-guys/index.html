How To Get Straight Hair To Curly For Guys A Step By Step Guide

In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to get straight hair to curly for guys.

How to Get Straight Hair to Curly for Guys

Before we dive into the specifics of how to get curly hair, let's briefly discuss what causes straight hair to be, well, straight. Hair gets its shape from the hair follicle, which determines whether the hair is straight, wavy, or curly. Straight hair typically has round hair follicles, while curly hair has more oval-shaped follicles.

So, how can you get curly hair if your follicles are naturally round? The answer lies in the use of heat and/or chemicals to temporarily or permanently alter the shape of your hair.

Methods for Getting Curly Hair

There are several methods you can use to get curly hair. These include:

  • Using a curling iron or wand to create temporary curls
  • Getting a perm to permanently alter the shape of your hair
  • Using heatless methods, such as braiding or twisting your hair, to create natural-looking curls

In the next section, we'll cover the steps for achieving curly hair using each of these methods.

Step-by-Step Guide

Using a Curling Iron or Wand

If you want to create temporary curls, using a curling iron or wand is a quick and easy method. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with dry, clean hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage from the heat.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Take one section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron or wand, starting at the base of the hair and working your way to the ends.
  5. Hold the hair on the curling iron or wand for 5-10 seconds, then release.
  6. Repeat steps 4-5 until all of your hair is curled.
  7. Use your fingers to loosen the curls for a more natural look.
  8. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep the curls in place.

Using a curling iron or wand can be damaging to your hair if you use it too often, so be sure to use a heat protectant spray and limit your use to once or twice a week.

Getting a Perm

If you want to permanently alter the shape of your hair, getting a perm is the way to go. Here's what to expect:

  1. Find a stylist who specializes in perms and schedule a consultation.
  2. During the consultation, discuss the type of curls you want and any concerns you have.
  3. If you decide to go ahead with the perm, your stylist will apply a solution to your hair that breaks down the bonds that give your hair its shape.
  4. Your hair will then be wrapped around perm rods to create the desired curl pattern.
  5. The perm solution will be rinsed out, and a neutralizer will be applied to set the curls.
  6. Your hair will be rinsed again, and you'll be given instructions for how to care for your new curls.

Getting a perm can be a long and sometimes uncomfortable process, but the results can last for several months. Be sure to follow your stylist's instructions for caring for your new curls to keep them looking their best.

Using Heatless Methods

If you want to create natural-looking curls without using heat, there are several methods you can try:

  • Braiding your hair: Divide your hair into sections and braid each section tightly. Leave the braids in overnight and undo them in the morning for natural-looking waves.
  • Twisting your hair: Divide your hair into sections and twist each section tightly. Leave the twists in overnight and undo them in the morning for defined curls.
  • Bantu knots: Divide your hair into sections and wrap each section around itself to create a knot. Leave the knots in overnight and undo them in the morning for tight ringlets.

Using heatless methods can be gentler on your hair than using a curling iron or getting a perm, but they can also take longer to achieve the desired result.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Once you have curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.

FAQs

Can I get curly hair if my hair is naturally straight?

Yes, you can use heat or chemicals to temporarily or permanently alter the shape of your hair.

Will getting a perm damage my hair?

Getting a perm can be damaging to your hair if it's not done properly or if you don't take care of your curls afterward. Be sure to find a stylist who specializes in perms and follow their instructions for caring for your new curls.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and how active you are. Some people with curly hair can go several days between washes, while others need to wash their hair daily. Experiment to find out what works best for you.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit more care than straight hair, but it's worth it for the beautiful, bouncy curls you'll have. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a gentle sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to wet hair to keep your curls hydrated and defined.
  • Avoid touching your hair too often, as this can cause frizz.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With the right care and styling, you can enjoy beautiful, healthy curls whether you have naturally curly or straight hair.
