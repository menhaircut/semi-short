How To Cut Layers In Long Hair Yourself

Description

If you're looking to add some volume, movement, and texture to your long hair, cutting layers is the perfect solution. But going to the salon can be expensive, and sometimes it's hard to describe to a stylist exactly what you want. The good news is that you can cut layers in long hair yourself at home, and it's easier than you might think.

In this article, we'll cover all the steps you need to know to cut layers in long hair yourself. We'll also provide some tips and answer some FAQs to help you achieve the perfect layered look.

How to Cut Layers in Long Hair Yourself

The first step in cutting layers in long hair yourself is to make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips. It's also a good idea to have a mirror that you can use to see the back of your head.

Next, you'll want to start with clean, dry hair. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles, and then part your hair down the middle.

From there, you'll want to divide your hair into sections. Start by separating the top section of your hair from the bottom section, and then clip the top section out of the way.

Now it's time to start cutting. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer, and hold it in between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the desired length and working your way up. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the bottom layer.

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, unclip the top layer of your hair and repeat the process. This time, you'll want to cut the hair at a slightly steeper angle to create more layers.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for cutting layers in long hair yourself:

  1. Make sure you have the right tools: sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.
  2. Start with clean, dry hair that has been combed through to remove tangles.
  3. Part your hair down the middle.
  4. Divide your hair into sections, starting with the bottom layer.
  5. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer, and hold it in between your fingers.
  6. Cut the hair at an angle, starting at the desired length and working your way up.
  7. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the bottom layer.
  8. Unclip the top layer of your hair and repeat the process, cutting at a slightly steeper angle.
  9. Once you've finished cutting, comb through your hair to make sure everything is even.
  10. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Long Hair Yourself

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when cutting layers in long hair yourself:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure you don't cut off too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Make sure you're cutting at an angle to create layers, rather than straight across.
  • Don't cut too much off at once – you can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • When cutting the top layer of hair, be sure to blend it with the bottom layer to create a seamless look.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting layers in long hair yourself:

  • How much hair should I cut off? It's up to you, but it's best to start with small sections and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • What if I make a mistake? Don't worry – mistakes happen. If you cut off too much hair, you can always go back and trim more off the longer pieces to create an even look.
  • Can I cut layers in curly hair? Yes, but it can be a bit more challenging. Make sure you cut each section of hair when it's dry, and use a diffuser when blow-drying to enhance your curls.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers in Long Hair Yourself

After cutting layers in long hair yourself, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Cutting layers in long hair yourself is a great way to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank. With the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can achieve a beautiful layered look right at home.
