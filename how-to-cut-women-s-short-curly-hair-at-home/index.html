How To Cut Women s Short Curly Hair At Home

Written by Bardi Jul 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts for your short curly hair? Have you considered cutting your hair at home but don't know where to start? Look no further, as we have compiled a comprehensive guide on how to cut women's short curly hair at home.

Description

Short curly hair can be challenging to cut, but with the right tools, techniques and a little patience, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your home. Cutting your hair at home can save you time and money, and it can also give you the freedom to experiment with different styles, without the fear of damaging your hair.

How to

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you need to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow-dry it. It's essential to have clean and dry hair before cutting it, as damp hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is.

Step 3: Section your hair

Divide your hair into small sections, using hair clips to hold the excess hair out of the way. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 4: Begin cutting

Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at the desired length, using the scissors. It's essential to cut your hair slowly and avoid cutting too much at once, as curly hair can spring up once it's cut.

Step 5: Repeat the process

Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up to the top layer. Be patient, and take breaks if needed to avoid getting frustrated or fatigued.

Step 6: Check your work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check your work in the mirror. Ensure that your hair is even and that there are no stray hairs that need trimming.

Tips

  • Invest in a good pair of sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it is.
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit room, with a mirror to help you see what you're doing.
  • Avoid using thinning shears on curly hair, as they can cause frizz and split ends.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with small snips and work your way up.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my curly hair when it's wet?

A: It's recommended to cut your curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it is.

Q: Do I need special scissors to cut my curly hair?

A: It's ideal to use a good pair of sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.

Q: Can I use thinning shears on my curly hair?

A: It's best to avoid using thinning shears on curly hair, as they can cause frizz and split ends.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, ensure that you take care of it to maintain its health and shape. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair. Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to avoid frizz, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Cutting your short curly hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve salon-worthy results and save time and money. Remember to be patient and take your time, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if needed.
