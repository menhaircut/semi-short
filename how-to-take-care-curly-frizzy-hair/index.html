How To Take Care Of Curly Frizzy Hair Tips Tricks And Faq

Written by Oliver Aug 05, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have curly frizzy hair, you know that it can be a struggle to keep your locks looking their best. But with the right care and maintenance, you can manage your curls and tame the frizz. In this article, we'll take a look at some tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions about how to take care of curly frizzy hair.

How to Take Care of Curly Frizzy Hair

Curly frizzy hair needs moisture to thrive. The natural oils in your scalp have a harder time getting to the ends of curly hair because of the twists and turns. This can lead to dryness and breakage. To keep your curls healthy, you need to hydrate and nourish them regularly.

Here are some tips for taking care of curly frizzy hair:

1. Shampoo Less Often

Over-shampooing can strip your curls of their natural oils, leaving them dry and frizzy. Instead, try to wash your hair every 2-3 days, or even less frequently if your hair is particularly dry. When you do shampoo, use a sulfate-free formula that won't dry out your curls.

2. Condition, Condition, Condition

Curly hair needs lots of moisture, so conditioning is key. Use a rich, hydrating conditioner every time you wash your hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner for extra moisture and to help detangle your curls.

3. Detangle Gently

Curly hair is more prone to tangling than straight hair, so it's important to be gentle when detangling. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently work through any knots. Avoid using a brush, as it can break up your curls and cause frizz.

4. Use a Microfiber Towel or T-Shirt to Dry Your Hair

Regular towels can be too rough on curly hair, causing frizz and breakage. Instead, use a microfiber towel or a soft t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry. Don't rub your hair vigorously, as this can cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for taking care of curly frizzy hair:

1. Shampoo your hair every 2-3 days with a sulfate-free formula.

2. Condition your hair every time you wash it with a rich, hydrating conditioner.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair for extra moisture and to help detangle your curls.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair.

5. Blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or soft t-shirt.

6. Apply a curl-defining product, such as a mousse or gel, to your hair.

7. Let your hair air-dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls.

Tips for Managing Curly Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing curly frizzy hair:

1. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

A satin pillowcase is gentler on your hair than a regular cotton pillowcase, which can cause friction and lead to breakage and frizz.

2. Use a Hair Mask Once a Week

A weekly hair mask can help nourish and hydrate your curls, keeping them healthy and frizz-free.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Whenever Possible

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can cause damage and lead to frizz. Try to air-dry your hair whenever possible, and use heat styling tools sparingly.

4. Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can lead to frizz.

FAQ

Q: How can I make my curls more defined?

A: To make your curls more defined, try using a curl-defining product, such as a mousse or gel. Apply the product to damp hair, scrunching your curls as you go. You can also try plopping your hair to encourage curl formation.

Q: How can I prevent frizz?

A: To prevent frizz, make sure to use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and blot your hair dry with a microfiber towel or soft t-shirt. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause frizz.

Q: Can I brush my curly frizzy hair?

A: It's best to avoid brushing your curly frizzy hair, as this can break up your curls and cause frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
