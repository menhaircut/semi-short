How To Cut Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Description

Curly hair can be a challenge to cut due to its unique texture and shape. If you're looking to cut your own curly hair or want to learn how to cut curly hair for others, this guide will provide you with the tips and techniques you need to get the job done.

How to Cut Curly Hair

Cutting curly hair requires a different approach than cutting straight hair. While straight hair can be cut evenly and with precision, curly hair needs to be cut in a way that takes into account its natural texture and shape. Here are some general guidelines for cutting curly hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair
  • Use sharp, high-quality scissors
  • Cut curly hair when it's in its natural state, not straightened
  • Don't cut curly hair too short, as it will appear even shorter once it dries
  • Consider cutting curly hair when it's wet, as it will be easier to handle and cut evenly

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut curly hair:

  1. Start by sectioning the hair. Divide the hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the back sections. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and comb through it with a wide-tooth comb. Use your fingers to twist the hair, creating a natural curl.
  3. Using your scissors, cut the hair at an angle, following the natural curve of the curl. Don't cut straight across, as this will create a blunt, uneven cut.
  4. Continue cutting small sections of hair, working your way up the back of the head. Remember to cut at an angle and follow the natural curve of the curl.
  5. Repeat this process on the other back section of hair.
  6. Move on to the front sections of hair. Take a small section of hair from one of the front sections, and repeat the same process of twisting the hair and cutting at an angle.
  7. Repeat this process on the other front section of hair.
  8. Check your work by running your fingers through the hair. Look for any uneven or blunt cuts and trim them as needed.
  9. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Always cut curly hair when it's dry or slightly damp. Cutting wet hair can result in a shorter cut than intended.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair if it's too dry.
  • Don't cut curly hair too often. It's best to wait at least six to eight weeks between cuts to allow the hair to grow and maintain its natural shape.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not confident in your ability to cut curly hair, seek the help of a professional stylist.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair:

Can you cut curly hair with regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors can result in a blunt, uneven cut that doesn't take into account the natural shape of curly hair. It's best to use sharp, high-quality scissors that are designed specifically for cutting curly hair.

How do you know how much to cut off?

It's best to start with small cuts and work your way up. Remember that curly hair will appear shorter once it dries, so it's better to err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need to.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful curly hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use products designed specifically for curly hair, such as sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage curly hair.
  • Avoid washing curly hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle curly hair.
  • Consider using a deep conditioner or hair mask once a week to keep curly hair hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to confidently cut curly hair and maintain its natural shape and beauty.
