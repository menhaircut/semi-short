How To Cut A Fade Mohawk Step By Step

Written by Eveline Sep 21, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you looking for a bold and edgy hairstyle that will make you stand out? Look no further than the fade mohawk. This style combines the classic mohawk with a fade for a modern twist. In this step-by-step guide, we’ll show you how to cut a fade mohawk at home.

Table of Contents

  • How To
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How To

Before we dive into the step-by-step instructions, let’s go over what you’ll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Trimming guides (1/8", 1/4", 3/8")
  • Hair gel or wax (optional)

Now that you have your tools, let’s get started!

Step by Step

Step 1: Prep the Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. You want to work with clean and dry hair for the best results. Comb through your hair to remove any tangles.

Step 2: Create the Mohawk

Using your scissors, cut a strip of hair down the center of your head. This should be about 2-3 inches wide. The length of the hair will depend on your personal preference. Use your comb to keep the hair in place as you cut.

Step 3: Cut the Sides

Now it’s time to create the fade. Use your clippers and the 1/8" trimming guide to cut the hair on the sides of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using an upward motion. Be sure to blend the hair as you go for a seamless transition.

Step 4: Adjust the Trimming Guide

Switch to the 1/4" trimming guide and repeat the same process, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Remember to blend as you go.

Step 5: Use the Final Trimming Guide

Finally, switch to the 3/8" trimming guide and repeat the process once more. This will create the longest length of hair on the sides of your head. Again, be sure to blend for a seamless transition.

Step 6: Clean Up the Edges

Using your scissors, trim any stray hairs along the edges of the mohawk and the sides of your head. Take your time and make small cuts to avoid any mistakes.

Step 7: Style the Mohawk

Now that your fade mohawk is complete, you can style it with hair gel or wax if desired. Use your fingers to shape the hair into a point at the top of your head.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way down for a more natural-looking fade.
  • Take breaks if needed to avoid fatigue or mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.

FAQ

How long does a fade mohawk last?

The length of time a fade mohawk lasts depends on how quickly your hair grows. It’s recommended to get a touch-up every 2-4 weeks to maintain the style.

Can I cut a fade mohawk on my own?

Yes, with the proper tools and technique, you can cut a fade mohawk on your own. However, it’s always recommended to visit a professional stylist for best results.

What hair type is best for a fade mohawk?

A fade mohawk can work for any hair type, but it’s best suited for thick and straight hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your fade mohawk, it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain the style. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Regular trims will also help maintain the shape of the mohawk.

Congratulations, you now know how to cut a fade mohawk step by step! With a little practice and patience, you can achieve this bold and edgy style at home.
