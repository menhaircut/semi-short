How To Low Fade Long Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Low fade haircuts are popular among men who want a stylish and low-maintenance hairdo. But what if you have long hair? Can you still rock a low fade? The answer is yes! In this article, we'll show you how to low fade long hair. From the tools you need to the step-by-step process, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Clipper
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair gel or pomade
  • Mirror

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair and comb it to remove any tangles or knots. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to your hair to make it easier to work with.

Sectioning Your Hair

Use a comb to create a parting line from the front hairline to the back of your head. This will divide your hair into two sections. Clip the top section of your hair up and out of the way.

Clipping Your Hair

Start with the longest clipper guard and work your way down to the shortest. Begin at the nape of your neck and work your way up to the parting line. Make sure to keep the clippers level with your head and use a fluid motion. Once you've finished the first pass, repeat the process with the next shortest clipper guard until you reach the desired length.

Fading Your Hair

Switch to the shortest clipper guard and create a line around the sides and back of your head. This will serve as your guide for the fade. Using an upward motion, fade the hair from the line upwards. Make sure to blend the hair evenly to avoid any harsh lines.

Blending Your Hair

Switch to your scissors and blend the hair from the top section with the faded hair. Start at the front and work your way towards the back. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle to blend the hair together. Use your comb to check for any uneven spots.

Final Touches

Use your clippers to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neckline. Check your hair in the mirror to make sure you're happy with the length and blend. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to style your hair as desired.

Expert Tips

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Take your time and work in small sections to avoid mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.

FAQs

Can I low fade long hair without clippers?

While clippers make the process easier, you can achieve a low fade with scissors and a comb. It will take longer, but the end result will be the same.

How often should I get my low fade haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain the style. On average, most men get their hair cut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

To maintain your low fade long hair, make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant spray if you do. Trim any split ends and get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy.

Congratulations! You've successfully learned how to low fade long hair. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a stylish and modern hairdo that's easy to maintain. Give it a try and show off your new look!
