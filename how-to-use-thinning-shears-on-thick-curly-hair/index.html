How To Use Thinning Shears On Thick Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. Thinning shears can be a great tool to help tame your curls and make your hair more manageable. In this article, we will go over how to use thinning shears on thick curly hair, step-by-step.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissor that has teeth on one blade, which helps to remove bulk from hair. This can be especially useful for people with thick curly hair, as it can help to reduce the amount of hair and weight, making it easier to style and manage. However, using thinning shears improperly can result in damaged hair, so it is important to use them correctly.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Thick Curly Hair

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before using thinning shears, it is important to start with clean and conditioned hair. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to help keep your curls hydrated and healthy.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. This will help to prevent breakage and damage when using thinning shears.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you are thinning your hair evenly.

Step 4: Use Thinning Shears

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at the mid-shaft and working your way down to the ends. Repeat this process on each section of hair, being careful not to cut too much at once.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After thinning your hair, use regular scissors to blend the hair and create a seamless look. This will help to prevent any choppy or uneven areas in your hair.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you have thinned and blended your hair, it is time to style it. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to help enhance your natural curls, or use a straightening iron to create a sleeker look.

Tips

  • Only use thinning shears on the mid-shaft and ends of your hair, not the roots.
  • Cut small sections of hair at a time to prevent cutting too much at once.
  • Use a light hand when using thinning shears, as cutting too much can result in damaged hair.
  • If you are unsure about using thinning shears, consult with a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but they are especially useful for people with thick hair.

Can thinning shears damage hair?

Yes, if thinning shears are not used correctly, they can cause damage to hair. It is important to use them properly and with a light hand.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

This will depend on your hair type and styling preferences. It is best to consult with a professional stylist to determine how often you should use thinning shears on your hair.

Hair Care

In addition to using thinning shears, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week, avoid using heat styling tools too often, and trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using thinning shears correctly, you can help to manage your thick curly hair and create a look that you love.
