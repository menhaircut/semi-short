Is It Normal For Baby To Lose Hair On Top Of Head

Written by Oliver Aug 12, 2023 · 4 min read

As a new parent, you might be wondering if it is normal for your baby to lose hair on top of their head. The answer is yes! In fact, it is quite common for babies to lose hair on the top of their head during the first few months of life. This hair loss is known as newborn hair loss, and it usually occurs between the ages of 2 and 6 months.

What is Newborn Hair Loss?

Newborn hair loss is the shedding of the fine, downy hair that covers a baby’s body and head during the first few months of life. This hair is known as lanugo, and it is completely normal for babies to lose it. In addition to lanugo, some babies may also lose some of the hair on the top of their head, which is known as baby baldness.

What is Baby Baldness?

Baby baldness is the shedding of hair on the top of a baby’s head, leaving small, bald patches. This is a different type of hair loss than newborn hair loss, and it is also normal. Baby baldness usually occurs between the ages of 3 and 6 months, but it can happen at any time during the first year of life.

How to Deal with Newborn Hair Loss and Baby Baldness

While newborn hair loss and baby baldness are completely normal, it can be concerning for some parents. Here are some tips on how to deal with it:

Don’t Worry

First and foremost, don’t worry! Newborn hair loss and baby baldness are normal and temporary. Your baby’s hair will eventually grow back.

Be Gentle

Be gentle when washing your baby’s head. Use a gentle shampoo and avoid rubbing their scalp too hard.

Massage Their Scalp

Massaging your baby’s scalp can help stimulate hair growth. Use your fingertips to gently massage their scalp for a few minutes each day.

Use a Soft Brush

Use a soft brush to gently brush your baby’s hair. This can help remove any loose hairs and stimulate hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will my baby’s hair grow back?

Your baby’s hair will start to grow back within a few months. By the time they are a year old, they should have a full head of hair.

Is there anything I can do to prevent newborn hair loss?

No, there is nothing you can do to prevent newborn hair loss. It is a natural part of your baby’s growth and development.

Should I be concerned if my baby’s hair loss is excessive?

If you are concerned about your baby’s hair loss, talk to your pediatrician. Excessive hair loss could be a sign of an underlying health condition.

Hair Care for Babies

Once your baby’s hair starts growing back, it is important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo specifically designed for babies. Avoid shampoos with harsh chemicals or fragrances.

Don’t Wash Their Hair Too Often

Babies don’t need to have their hair washed every day. Washing their hair too often can strip it of its natural oils.

Be Gentle

Be gentle when brushing or combing your baby’s hair. Use a soft brush or comb and be careful not to tug or pull on their hair.

Trim Their Hair

If your baby’s hair starts to get too long, you can trim it with baby scissors. Be very careful not to cut their skin.

In conclusion, it is completely normal for babies to lose hair on the top of their head. Newborn hair loss and baby baldness are both temporary and will eventually grow back. If you have any concerns about your baby’s hair loss, talk to your pediatrician.
