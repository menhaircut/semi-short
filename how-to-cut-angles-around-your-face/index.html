How To Cut Angles Around Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to change up your hairstyle, one way to add some edge and dimension is to cut angles around your face. This technique can help frame your face and accentuate your features, whether you have long or short hair. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut angles around your face.

Description

Cutting angles around your face involves creating layers and angles that frame your face and add dimension to your hair. This technique can help make your face appear slimmer and emphasize your cheekbones, jawline, and other features. Depending on your hair length and texture, there are various ways to cut angles around your face, including using a razor, scissors, or a combination of both.

How to Cut Angles Around Your Face

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before cutting angles around your face, it's important to prepare your hair by washing and drying it. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair and make it easier to cut. You can also apply some styling products, such as mousse or hairspray, to help hold your hair in place.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections, starting with the front section that frames your face. You can use clips or hair ties to keep the sections separate and make it easier to cut. If you're unsure how much hair to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up.

Step 3: Cut the Angles

Now it's time to cut the angles. Depending on your preference, you can use scissors or a razor to create the angles. Hold the hair at a 45-degree angle and make a diagonal cut, starting from the longest point and moving inward. Repeat this process until you've cut all the sections around your face.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

After cutting the angles, it's important to blend the layers to create a natural look. You can do this by using scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time and blending them together. This will help create a seamless transition between the layers and give your hair a polished look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've cut the angles and blended the layers, it's time to style your hair. You can use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to create the desired look. Experiment with different styling techniques and products to find the perfect style for you.

Tips for Cutting Angles Around Your Face

- Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

- Use a comb to help guide your scissors or razor and ensure a clean cut.

- Cut at a 45-degree angle to create a natural-looking angle.

- Blend the layers to create a seamless transition between the angles.

- Experiment with different styling techniques and products to find the perfect look for you.

FAQ

What's the best tool to use for cutting angles around your face?

There's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as the best tool to use depends on your hair texture and personal preference. Scissors are a common choice, but razors can also create a softer, more textured look. You may also want to consider using a combination of both tools for the best results.

Can I cut angles around my face if I have short hair?

Absolutely! Cutting angles around your face can work for both short and long hair. Just be sure to start with small sections and work your way up to avoid cutting too much hair at once.

How often should I cut angles around my face?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your style. Generally, you can cut angles around your face every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care for Angled Haircuts

If you've cut angles around your face, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are a few tips:

- Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils

- Apply a leave-in conditioner to help detangle and protect your hair from heat damage

- Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools

- Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully cut angles around your face and achieve a stylish, modern look that accentuates your features.
