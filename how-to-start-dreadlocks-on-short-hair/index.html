How To Start Dreadlocks On Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair and are looking to start dreadlocks, you may be wondering where to begin. Starting dreadlocks on short hair can be a bit tricky, as the hair needs to be long enough to form a dreadlock. However, it is not impossible, and with the right technique and some patience, you can start your journey to having beautiful, natural dreadlocks.

How to Start Dreadlocks on Short Hair

Before you begin, it is important to understand that starting dreadlocks on short hair will take time and commitment. You will need to be patient and willing to put in the effort to maintain your dreadlocks over time. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in starting dreadlocks on short hair is to wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup or residue from your hair and scalp, making it easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small, even sections. This will help you to create uniform dreadlocks that are evenly spaced and sized. You can use clips or rubber bands to secure each section.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Using a fine-toothed comb, backcomb each section of hair in a zig-zag motion. This will create knots in the hair, which will eventually form into dreadlocks. Be gentle and take your time, as backcombing too aggressively can damage your hair.

Step 4: Twist and Roll

After backcombing, twist and roll each section of hair between your palms to help shape the dreadlock. You can also use a dread wax or locking gel to help hold the hair in place.

Step 5: Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Once you have created your dreadlocks, it is important to maintain them properly. This includes washing them regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and retwisting them every few weeks to keep them tight and neat.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks on Short Hair

Starting dreadlocks on short hair can be challenging, but with these tips, you can make the process a little easier:

  • Be patient and take your time. Dreadlocks take time to form, so don't expect to have perfect locks overnight.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue from your hair.
  • Backcomb gently to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Twist and roll each section of hair between your palms to shape the dreadlocks.
  • Use a dread wax or locking gel to help hold the hair in place.
  • Maintain your dreadlocks properly to keep them looking neat and healthy.

FAQs About Starting Dreadlocks on Short Hair

Q: How long does my hair need to be to start dreadlocks?

A: Your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long to start dreadlocks.

Q: Can I start dreadlocks on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreadlocks on relaxed hair. However, it is important to be gentle and avoid damaging your hair during the process.

Q: How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

A: You should wash your dreadlocks at least once a week with a residue-free shampoo.

Q: Can I color my dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can color your dreadlocks. However, it is important to use a dye that is safe for dreadlocks and to avoid over-processing your hair.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful dreadlocks. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your dreadlocks regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Retwist your dreadlocks every few weeks to keep them tight and neat.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your dreadlocks while sleeping by wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet.

Starting dreadlocks on short hair is a process that requires patience and commitment. With the right technique and proper hair care, you can achieve beautiful, natural dreadlocks that will last for years to come.
