How To Put Your Hair Up With A Pencil Short Hair Edition

Written by Christine Oct 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of wearing the same old ponytail or bun? If you have short hair, you might think your styling options are limited. But fear not! With just a pencil and a few easy steps, you can create an effortless and chic updo. Here's how to put your hair up with a pencil if you have short hair.

Description

Putting your hair up with a pencil is a simple and versatile way to create an updo. It's perfect for when you're short on time or just want to switch up your hairstyle. Plus, it's a great way to make use of those extra pencils lying around your desk!

How to

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

For this hairstyle, you'll need a few things:

  • A pencil (preferably unsharpened)
  • A few bobby pins
  • Hairspray (optional)

Once you have these items, you're ready to get started!

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before you start putting your hair up, it's important to prep it properly. Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Then, apply a small amount of hair product, such as mousse or texturizing spray, to give your hair some grip and texture.

Step 3: Create a Low Ponytail

Using your fingers, gather your hair at the nape of your neck and create a low ponytail. Secure it with a hair tie or elastic band.

Step 4: Insert the Pencil

Take your unsharpened pencil and insert it into the base of your ponytail, just above the hair tie. Make sure the pencil is straight and centered.

Step 5: Roll Your Hair

Take the ends of your ponytail and roll them up and over the pencil, toward the back of your head. Keep rolling until you reach the base of your ponytail. Tuck any loose ends under the roll.

Step 6: Secure with Bobby Pins

Using bobby pins, secure the roll in place. You may need to use several pins to ensure it stays put. Make sure to hide the pins as much as possible for a polished look.

Step 7: Finish with Hairspray (Optional)

If you want extra hold, you can finish the hairstyle with a spritz of hairspray. This will help keep your hair in place all day.

Tips

  • Experiment with different types of pencils to see which works best for your hair texture and thickness.
  • If your hair tends to slip out of updos, try using bobby pins with a bit of texture or grip.
  • If you have layers in your hair, you may need to use more bobby pins to secure them.
  • Accessorize your updo with a headband or scarf for a pop of color or texture.

FAQ

Can I use a pen or other object instead of a pencil?

While a pencil works best for this hairstyle, you can try using other objects such as chopsticks, knitting needles, or even a pen if you don't have a pencil on hand.

Will this hairstyle work on curly or thick hair?

Yes! This hairstyle can work on all hair types and textures. Just make sure to prep your hair properly with product and use enough bobby pins to secure it in place.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Make sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools too often. If you have short hair, consider getting regular trims to keep it looking fresh and healthy.

Now you know how to put your hair up with a pencil if you have short hair. Whether you're heading to work, school, or a night out, this hairstyle is a simple and chic way to switch up your look. Give it a try and see how easy it is!
