Do Baby Bangs Suit Round Faces A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do baby bangs suit round faces? It's a question that has been popping up a lot lately. Baby bangs, also known as micro bangs, are a trendy hairstyle that has been popular for a while now. They are short, blunt bangs that sit above the eyebrows, giving the face a youthful and edgy look. However, not everyone can pull off this style, especially those with round faces. In this article, we will discuss whether or not baby bangs suit round faces, and if so, how to style them.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Before we dive into whether or not baby bangs suit round faces, let's first define what a round face shape is. A round face has soft, curved lines with the width and length in the same proportions. The cheekbones are the widest part of the face, and the jawline is rounded. Round faces lack angularity, so creating angles with hairstyles is key to balance the face shape.

Now, let's talk about baby bangs. Baby bangs are very short bangs that sit above the eyebrows. They are a trendy hairstyle that many women are embracing. Baby bangs can give the face a youthful and edgy look, but they're not for everyone. They are best suited for those with oval or heart-shaped faces, as they can create angles and balance out the face shape. But can they work for those with round faces? Let's find out.

How to

So, do baby bangs suit round faces? The answer is yes, but with some caveats. Round faces lack angularity, so creating angles with baby bangs is essential. Baby bangs can help to create the illusion of an elongated face, but they need to be styled correctly.

Face-framing Layers

Adding face-framing layers to your baby bangs can help to create angles and balance out a round face. These layers should start at the cheekbones and work their way down. This will give the illusion of a longer face and create angles.

Side-Swept Bangs

If you're not ready to commit to full-on baby bangs, side-swept bangs can be a great alternative. These bangs are longer and swept to the side, creating angles that can balance out a round face.

Asymmetrical Bangs

Asymmetrical bangs are another option for those with round faces. These bangs are shorter on one side and longer on the other, creating angles and balancing out the face shape.

Step by Step

If you've decided to go for baby bangs, here's how to style them:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section off the hair you want to be your baby bangs.
  3. Using a pair of sharp scissors, cut the hair straight across, just above the eyebrows.
  4. If you want to add face-framing layers, cut them at a slight angle starting at the cheekbones.
  5. Style your baby bangs using a flat iron or a round brush and blow dryer for a sleek look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when considering baby bangs for a round face:

  • Make sure to create angles with face-framing layers or asymmetrical bangs.
  • Consider side-swept bangs as an alternative to full-on baby bangs.
  • Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best bangs for your face shape.
  • Consider your hair texture when choosing baby bangs. They work best with straight or slightly wavy hair.
  • Keep your baby bangs trimmed regularly to maintain their shape.

FAQ

Can round faces pull off baby bangs?

Yes, but with some caveats. Baby bangs can work for round faces if they're styled correctly. Adding face-framing layers, side-swept bangs, or asymmetrical bangs can create angles and balance out a round face.

What face shape suits baby bangs?

Baby bangs are best suited for those with oval or heart-shaped faces. These face shapes already have angles and can handle the short, blunt bangs well.

Are baby bangs high maintenance?

Yes, baby bangs require regular trims to maintain their shape. They can also be more high maintenance when it comes to styling, as they need to be kept straight and sleek to avoid looking messy.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy is essential to pulling off baby bangs. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Limit heat styling to avoid damage to your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when heat styling.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to keep your hair nourished and healthy.

So, do baby bangs suit round faces? The answer is yes, but with some caveats. Adding face-framing layers, side-swept bangs, or asymmetrical bangs can create angles and balance out a round face. Consult with a hairstylist to determine the best bangs for your face shape, and keep your hair healthy with regular trims and good hair care practices. With the right styling and care, baby bangs can be a great look for those with round faces.
