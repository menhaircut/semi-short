Are Afros Unprofessional The Truth About Natural Hair In The Workplace

As a person of color, deciding how to wear your hair in the workplace can be a difficult decision. One common question that arises is whether or not afros are unprofessional. In this article, we'll explore this topic and provide tips on how to navigate natural hair in a professional setting.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Afros in the Workplace

  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Natural Hair

Description

Afros are a popular natural hairstyle that many people of color choose to wear. However, some may believe that afros are unprofessional in a workplace setting. This belief may stem from the idea that natural hair is unkempt, messy, or distracting. But the truth is, natural hair, including afros, can be just as professional and polished as any other hairstyle.

How to Wear Afros in the Workplace

When it comes to wearing afros in the workplace, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, consider the workplace culture and dress code. If your workplace has strict rules about appearance, it may be best to opt for a more conservative hairstyle. However, if your workplace is more relaxed, you may have more freedom to wear your hair in a style that feels comfortable and authentic to you.

Another factor to consider is the length and volume of your afro. If your afro is very large or voluminous, it may be best to style it in a way that is more contained and professional-looking. This may mean using hair accessories, such as hairpins or headbands, to keep your hair out of your face and tidy.

Step-by-Step Guide for Professional Afro Styles

Here are a few steps you can take to create a professional-looking afro:

  1. Start with clean, well-conditioned hair
  2. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair from the ends to the roots
  3. Apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel, to your hair
  4. Use a pick or afro comb to lift your hair at the roots and create volume
  5. Shape your afro as desired, using hairpins or headbands to keep it neat and tidy

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Natural Hair

Regardless of how you choose to wear your natural hair in the workplace, it's important to keep it healthy and nourished. Here are a few tips to help you maintain healthy natural hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often (once a week is usually sufficient)
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and soft
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often
  • Protect your hair from damage by wearing a satin or silk scarf to bed
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid using brushes, which can cause breakage

FAQs About Natural Hair in the Workplace

Q: Can my employer legally prohibit me from wearing my natural hair in the workplace?

A: In most cases, no. The CROWN Act (Creating a Respectful and Open World for Natural Hair) was passed in 2019 to prevent discrimination based on natural hairstyles in the workplace and in public schools. However, there are some exceptions, such as for safety reasons or if the hairstyle would interfere with the performance of the job.

Q: What if my natural hair is very long or voluminous?

A: If your hair is very long or voluminous, it may be best to style it in a way that is more contained and professional-looking. This may mean using hair accessories, such as hairpins or headbands, to keep your hair out of your face and tidy.

Q: Can I wear protective styles, such as braids or twists, in the workplace?

A: Yes, protective styles are a great way to maintain healthy natural hair and can be worn in the workplace. However, it's important to ensure that the style is neat and tidy, and complies with any workplace dress code policies.

Essential Hair Care Tips for Natural Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you care for your natural hair:

  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as relaxers or perms, on your hair
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental damage by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote hair growth and health
  • Consult with a professional stylist or natural hair expert for personalized hair care advice

In conclusion, afros are not inherently unprofessional, but rather a natural hairstyle that can be styled in a way that is neat, tidy, and professional-looking. It's important to consider your workplace culture and dress code, as well as the length and volume of your hair, when deciding how to wear your natural hair in the workplace. With these tips and tricks, you can feel confident and empowered to embrace your natural hair in the workplace.
