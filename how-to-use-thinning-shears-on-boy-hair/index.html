How To Use Thinning Shears On Boy Hair

Written by Oliver Sep 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning shears are an essential tool for any hair stylist, especially when it comes to working on boy hair. Thinning shears are designed to remove bulk and create texture, giving the hair a natural look. In this article, we will discuss how to use thinning shears on boy hair, step by step, and provide tips and FAQ to help you achieve the best results.

Description

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are a type of scissors with teeth or notches on one blade. These teeth or notches help to remove bulk from the hair, creating a textured look. Thinning shears are commonly used on thick hair, but they can also be used on fine hair to add volume and texture.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Boy Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Thinning Shears

The first step in using thinning shears on boy hair is to choose the right pair of shears. There are different types of thinning shears available, with varying tooth spacing and blade length. Choose a pair of thinning shears that matches the hair type and the desired result.

Step 2: Prepare the Hair

Before using thinning shears, it is important to prepare the hair. Start by washing and conditioning the hair, then towel-dry it. Comb the hair to remove any tangles and separate it into sections using hair clips.

Step 3: Begin Thinning

Start thinning the hair by taking a small section of hair and holding it between your fingers. Slide the thinning shears down the section of hair, starting from the mid-length to the ends. Repeat this process on all sections of hair, focusing on areas that need thinning or texturizing.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

After thinning the hair, it is important to blend the hair to create a natural look. Use regular shears to trim the ends of the hair and blend the layers.

Step 5: Finish the Hair

Finish the hair by styling it using your preferred hair product. You can use a hair gel, mousse, or wax to create the desired style.

Tips

  • Start with a small section of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use the thinning shears sparingly to avoid over-thinning the hair.
  • Always use sharp thinning shears to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Comb the hair regularly during the thinning process to ensure even thinning.
  • Use a mirror to check the progress and ensure that the hair is evenly thinned.

FAQ

Can I use thinning shears on any type of boy hair?

Thinning shears can be used on most types of boy hair, including thick, curly, and fine hair. However, it is important to choose the right type of thinning shears to match the hair type and desired result.

Can thinning shears damage the hair?

Thinning shears can damage the hair if used incorrectly or if the shears are not sharp. It is important to use sharp thinning shears and to use them sparingly to avoid over-thinning the hair.

Hair Care

After using thinning shears on boy hair, it is important to take care of the hair to maintain its health and texture. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when using them. Trim the hair regularly to maintain its shape and style.

Thinning shears are a great tool for creating texture and removing bulk from boy hair. By following the steps and tips outlined in this article, you can achieve a natural and stylish look for your client or your own hair.
