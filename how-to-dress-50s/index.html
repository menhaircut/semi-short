How to Dress 50s: Relaxed Guide, Step-by-Step, Tips, FAQ and Hair Care | SEO Blog Article

Are you looking to channel your inner Audrey Hepburn or Marilyn Monroe? Do you love the vintage style of the 1950s? Whether you're attending a themed party or simply want to incorporate some 50s flair into your everyday wardrobe, we've got you covered with this relaxed guide on how to dress 50s. Read on for step-by-step instructions, helpful tips, and answers to frequently asked questions. Plus, we'll share some hair care tips to complete your retro look!

Step 1: Choose Your Silhouette

The 50s were known for their feminine silhouettes, with fitted bodices and full skirts. Some popular styles include the swing dress, pencil skirt, and shirtwaist dress. Choose a silhouette that flatters your body type and makes you feel confident.

For a classic 50s look, opt for a full skirt that hits at or below the knee. You can pair it with a fitted top or sweater to balance out the volume. Alternatively, a pencil skirt can create a sleek and sophisticated look, especially when paired with a tailored blouse.

Step 2: Select Your Fabrics

When it comes to fabrics, think feminine and classic. Some popular choices include cotton, linen, silk, and wool. Look for fabrics with a bit of structure, as they will help maintain the shape of your silhouette.

Floral prints, polka dots, and gingham are all popular prints from the 50s era. Stripes and solid colors are also great options. Choose prints and colors that suit your personal style and skin tone.

Step 3: Accessorize with the Right Pieces

The right accessories can take your 50s-inspired outfit to the next level. Some classic pieces to consider include:

  • Pearls or other classic jewelry
  • A cardigan or sweater to layer over your dress or blouse
  • A pair of kitten heels or ballet flats
  • A structured handbag or clutch
  • A scarf or headband to tie in your hair

Don't be afraid to mix and match accessories to create a unique look that reflects your personal style.

Step 4: Opt for Classic 50s Hairstyles

To complete your 50s look, consider trying a classic hairstyle from the era. Some popular styles include:

  • The French twist
  • The beehive
  • The chignon
  • The bouffant

For a more relaxed look, try some loose waves or curls. You can also accessorize your hair with a scarf, headband, or hairpin.

Tips for Dressing 50s

Here are some additional tips for nailing the 50s look:

  • Experiment with layering pieces, like cardigans or sweaters, to add depth to your outfit.
  • If you're wearing a full skirt, consider wearing a petticoat underneath to create additional volume and movement.
  • Try a bold lip color, like red or coral, for a pop of color.
  • Play with texture by mixing fabrics like lace, tweed, or denim.

FAQs on How to Dress 50s

What shoes should I wear with a 50s outfit?

For a classic 50s look, consider wearing kitten heels or ballet flats. You can also try a pair of ankle boots or loafers for a more modern twist.

What if I don't want to wear a full skirt?

No problem! You can still channel the 50s with a pencil skirt or a shirtwaist dress. Just make sure to choose a silhouette that flatters your body type.

Can I wear pants in a 50s-inspired outfit?

While skirts and dresses were more common in the 50s, you can still incorporate pants into your outfit. Consider wearing a pair of high-waisted trousers with a tucked-in blouse or sweater.

Hair Care for 50s Styles

To keep your 50s-inspired hairstyle looking its best, consider these hair care tips:

  • Use a heat protectant when using hot tools, like curling irons or straighteners.
  • Invest in a quality hairspray to help hold your style in place.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner to keep your locks healthy and shiny.
  • Try using a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be ready to rock a 50s-inspired outfit with confidence!
