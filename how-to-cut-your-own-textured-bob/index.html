How To Cut Your Own Textured Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our tutorial on how to cut your own textured bob! If you’ve been wanting to switch up your hairstyle, but don’t want to spend a fortune at the salon, cutting your own hair is a great option. With a few tools and some patience, you can achieve a cute and trendy textured bob in the comfort of your own home. Follow our step-by-step guide for a successful haircut!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Textured Bob
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After a Textured Bob Cut

How to Cut Your Own Textured Bob

A textured bob is a popular hairstyle that features layers and texture throughout the hair. This style can be achieved with a variety of hair types and lengths, making it a versatile option for many people. Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a fun and rewarding experience.

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Mirror

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your tools, it’s time to start cutting! Follow these steps for a successful textured bob:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you begin cutting. This will make it easier to see your natural texture and ensure that you don’t accidentally cut too much off.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with a hair clip.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Starting with the back sections, take a small piece of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a textured effect. Work your way around the back of your head, cutting small sections at a time.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Move on to the front sections of your hair, starting with one side. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, pulling it down at an angle. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating texture. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve cut all sections of your hair, use your scissors to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless look and ensure that your hair is evenly textured.

Step 6: Check Your Hair in the Mirror

Use a mirror to check the back of your hair and make any necessary adjustments. You can also use the mirror to check the sides and make sure they’re even.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Now that your hair is cut, style it as desired. You can use a curling iron or straightener to add volume and texture, or leave it natural for a more relaxed look.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own textured bob:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much off
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Take breaks if you need to, cutting your own hair can be tiring
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting a textured bob:

Can I cut a textured bob with curly hair?

Yes! A textured bob can be achieved with any hair type, including curly hair. Just make sure to cut your hair when it’s dry and use a diffuser when styling to enhance your natural curls.

How often should I cut my textured bob?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your bob.

Can I cut my hair shorter than a bob?

Yes! If you want a shorter haircut, you can follow the same steps and adjust the length as desired.

Hair Care After a Textured Bob Cut

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain the texture and shape. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically for textured hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and texture of your bob
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy

We hope this guide on how to cut your own textured bob was helpful! Remember to take your time and be patient. With practice, you’ll be able to achieve a salon-worthy haircut at home.
