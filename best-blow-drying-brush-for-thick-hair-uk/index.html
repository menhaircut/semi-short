How to Use a Blow Drying Brush for Thick Hair

Using a blow drying brush on thick hair can seem daunting, but with the right technique, you can achieve a smooth, voluminous blowout at home. Here are some tips for using a blow drying brush on thick hair:

1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair

Make sure your hair is clean and towel-dried before you start blow drying. This will help to reduce frizz and make it easier to style.

2. Apply a heat protectant

Before you start blow drying, apply a heat protectant to your hair to help reduce damage from the heat. This is especially important if you have thick hair, which can be more prone to damage.

3. Section your hair

Section your hair into manageable sections before you start blow drying. This will make it easier to style and ensure that all of your hair gets evenly dried.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for using a blow drying brush on thick hair:

1. Section your hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using clips or hair ties.

2. Start at the roots

Start at the roots and use the brush to lift your hair away from your scalp. Hold the hair dryer above the brush and blow dry your hair while moving the brush down the length of your hair.

3. Repeat on each section

Repeat this process on each section of your hair until you have blow dried your entire head.

Tips for Using a Blow Drying Brush on Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for using a blow drying brush on thick hair:

1. Use a heat protectant

Always use a heat protectant before blow drying your hair to reduce damage from the heat.

2. Start with a lower heat setting

If you have thick hair, you may need to use a higher heat setting to fully dry your hair, but start with a lower heat setting and gradually increase it as needed.

3. Don't forget to cool down

After blow drying each section, use the cool setting on your hair dryer to cool down your hair and set the style.

FAQs

What is the best blow drying brush for thick hair in the UK?

Some of the best blow drying brushes for thick hair in the UK include the BaBylissPRO Thermal Ceramic Ionic Round Brush, the ghd Ceramic Vented Radial Brush Size 4, and the Tangle Teezer Blow-Styling Round Tool.

How do I choose the right size brush for my thick hair?

For thick hair, look for a brush with a larger barrel size to help tame unruly locks and speed up drying time.

How often should I blow dry my thick hair?

Blow drying your hair too often can lead to damage and breakage, so it's best to limit blow drying to no more than a few times a week.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

In addition to using a blow drying brush, here are some additional hair care tips for those with thick hair:

1. Use a deep conditioning treatment

Thick hair can be more prone to dryness and damage, so use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

2. Avoid using hot tools too often

Using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons too often can lead to damage and breakage, so try to limit their use as much as possible.

3. Use a wide-toothed comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb to help prevent breakage and tangles.

With the right tools and techniques, achieving a salon-worthy blowout at home is possible, even for those with thick hair. Invest in the best blow drying brush for thick hair in the UK and follow these tips for a smooth, voluminous style every time.
