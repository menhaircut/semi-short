What Kind Of Fringe Suits A Round Face

Written by Robby Jun 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle but not sure what kind of fringe suits a round face? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll go over the best fringe styles for those with round faces, as well as some tips and tricks for maintaining your new look.

Description

A fringe, also known as bangs, is a popular hairstyle that involves cutting the hair in front of the forehead. Fringes can be worn by anyone, but if you have a round face, it's important to choose a style that flatters your features.

How to Choose the Right Fringe

When choosing a fringe style, it's important to consider the shape of your face. If you have a round face, you'll want to choose a style that adds length and definition to your features.

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a fringe:

1. Avoid Heavy, Blunt Fringes

Heavy, blunt fringes can make a round face look even rounder. Instead, opt for a softer, more textured fringe that blends in with the rest of your hair.

2. Choose Wispy, Side-Swept Fringes

Side-swept fringes are a great option for those with round faces. They add length to the face and create a flattering, feminine look. Wispy, textured fringes are also a good choice as they create a softer, more natural look.

3. Consider a Curtain Fringe

A curtain fringe is a versatile style that can work well on those with round faces. This fringe style is longer in the middle and shorter on the sides, framing the face and creating a flattering, face-framing effect.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Cut a Wispy, Side-Swept Fringe

If you've decided that a wispy, side-swept fringe is the right choice for you, here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your own fringe at home:

1. Start with Dry Hair

It's best to cut your fringe when your hair is dry so that you can see exactly how it will fall. Use a comb to separate the hair that you want to cut into a fringe.

2. Determine the Length

Decide how long you want your fringe to be. Remember to start longer and gradually cut more if needed.

3. Cut at an Angle

Using scissors, cut the hair at an angle. This will create a softer, more natural look. Cut small sections at a time and check your progress frequently.

4. Blend the Edges

When you're finished cutting, use thinning shears to blend the edges of the fringe with the rest of your hair. This will create a seamless, natural look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fringe

Once you've got your new fringe, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for maintaining your fringe:

1. Wash and Blow-Dry Your Hair Regularly

Regular washing and blow-drying will help to keep your fringe looking fresh and clean. Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from heat styling tools.

2. Trim Your Fringe Every 4-6 Weeks

Regular trimming will help to keep your fringe looking neat and tidy. If you're not comfortable cutting your own fringe, visit a professional stylist for regular trims.

3. Use Hair Accessories to Change Up Your Look

Headbands, hair clips, and other accessories can be a great way to change up your fringe style without committing to a new cut.

FAQ

Q: Can I still wear a fringe if I have a round face?

A: Absolutely! Just make sure to choose a style that flatters your features.

Q: Will a heavy, straight fringe make my face look rounder?

A: Yes, heavy, straight fringes can make a round face look even rounder. Opt for a softer, more textured fringe instead.

Q: Can I cut my own fringe at home?

A: Yes, you can, but it's important to be careful and take your time. If you're not comfortable cutting your own fringe, visit a professional stylist.

Hair Care Tips for Fringe Styles

When it comes to maintaining your fringe, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips for fringe styles:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

Choose a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won't dry out your hair. Avoid shampoos with harsh chemicals, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to style.

2. Use a Heat Protectant Spray

When using heat styling tools, always use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair. This will help to keep your hair looking healthy and shiny.

3. Avoid Over-Styling

Avoid over-styling your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and let your hair air dry whenever possible.

By following these tips and choosing the right fringe style for your face shape, you can create a beautiful, flattering look that you'll love. Whether you opt for a wispy, side-swept fringe or a curtain fringe, you're sure to turn heads with your new hairstyle.
