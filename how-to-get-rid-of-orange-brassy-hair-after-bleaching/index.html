How To Get Rid Of Orange Brassy Hair After Bleaching A Complete Guide

Are you tired of looking at your orange brassy hair after bleaching? Don't worry; you're not alone. Many people face this issue after bleaching their hair. However, you don't need to worry anymore because we have got you covered. In this article, we'll discuss how to get rid of orange brassy hair after bleaching. So, let's get started!

How to Get Rid of Orange Brassy Hair After Bleaching

The first thing you need to do is to understand why your hair turned orange after bleaching. When you bleach your hair, it removes the natural pigments, and the yellow and orange tones become more prominent. So, to get rid of the orange brassy hair after bleaching, you need to tone it with a blue or purple toner.

There are many toners available in the market that you can use to get rid of orange brassy hair after bleaching. However, we recommend using a toner that is specifically designed for orange hair. You can check the packaging to see if it is suitable for your hair color.

Another option is to use a hair color with a blue or purple base. This will neutralize the orange tones and give you the desired hair color. However, make sure to choose a hair color that is suitable for your hair type and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know how to get rid of orange brassy hair after bleaching let's discuss the step-by-step guide:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  2. Choose a toner or hair color with a blue or purple base that is suitable for your hair color.
  3. Apply the toner or hair color to your hair, following the instructions on the packaging.
  4. Leave the toner or hair color on your hair for the recommended time.
  5. Rinse your hair with cool water to seal the cuticle and prevent color fading.
  6. Apply a conditioner or hair mask to nourish and hydrate your hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

After toning your hair or coloring it, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent hair color from fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as curling irons or straighteners.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to protect your hair from heat and nourish it.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to get rid of orange brassy hair after bleaching:

1. Can I tone my hair at home?

Yes, you can tone your hair at home using a toner or hair color with a blue or purple base. However, make sure to follow the instructions on the packaging and do a patch test before applying it to your hair.

2. How often should I tone my hair?

It depends on your hair type and the color of your hair. Generally, you can tone your hair once every four to six weeks to maintain the desired hair color.

3. Can I use a toner without bleaching my hair?

Yes, you can use a toner without bleaching your hair. However, it may not be as effective in neutralizing the orange or yellow tones in your hair.

Hair Care After Treatment

After toning or coloring your hair, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent hair color from fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as curling irons or straighteners.
  • Apply a hair serum or oil to protect your hair from heat and nourish it.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

In conclusion, getting rid of orange brassy hair after bleaching is not as difficult as it seems. With the right toner or hair color and proper hair care, you can achieve the desired hair color and maintain its health and shine. So, follow the step-by-step guide, take care of your hair, and flaunt your beautiful hair with confidence.
