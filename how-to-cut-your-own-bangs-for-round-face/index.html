How To Cut Your Own Bangs For Round Face A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of always going to the salon to get your bangs trimmed? Why not try cutting your own bangs at home? If you have a round face, cutting your own bangs can be a bit tricky, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own bangs for a round face shape.

Description

Before we dive into the process of cutting your own bangs, let's talk about what a round face shape is. A round face shape is characterized by its soft, circular features with a width and length that are roughly the same. If you have a round face shape, cutting your bangs too short or too blunt can make your face appear even rounder.

How to Cut Your Own Bangs for Round Face

Here are some things you will need before you start:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own bangs for a round face:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air-dry or blow-dry it. You want your hair to be dry before cutting it.
  2. Part your hair in the middle and comb it thoroughly to remove any tangles.
  3. Section off the hair you want to cut into a triangular shape. The base of the triangle should be at the crown of your head, and the point of the triangle should be at the middle of your forehead.
  4. Clip the rest of your hair away from your face so that it doesn't get in the way while you're cutting your bangs.
  5. Use your scissors to cut your bangs at an angle. Start from the middle of your forehead and work your way towards the outer edges of your face.
  6. Be sure to cut your bangs in small increments, checking the length after each cut. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  7. Once you've cut your bangs to the desired length, you can style them as you like. You can wear them straight, side-swept, or parted in the middle.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

Here are some tips to help you cut your own bangs for a round face:

  • Don't cut your bangs too short or too blunt. This can make your face appear even rounder.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut your bangs in small increments to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Start with longer bangs and gradually cut more if needed. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my bangs?

You should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

2. Can I cut my bangs when they're wet?

No, you should only cut your bangs when they are dry. Wet hair can shrink when it dries, causing you to cut more than you intended.

3. Can I cut my bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your bangs if you have curly hair. Just be sure to cut them when your hair is dry and in its natural state.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Bangs

After cutting your bangs, you should continue to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your bangs for the first few days after cutting them.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to keep your hair and bangs looking shiny and healthy.
  • Continue to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Cutting your own bangs can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it can also be a bit nerve-wracking. Follow these tips and steps to cut your own bangs for a round face, and you'll be sure to have a great new look in no time.
