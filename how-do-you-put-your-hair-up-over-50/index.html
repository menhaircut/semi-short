How Do You Put Your Hair Up Over 50

Written by Robby Aug 03, 2023 · 3 min read

As we age, our hair may become thinner or more delicate. This can make styling your hair a bit more challenging, especially if you want to put it up in a bun, ponytail, or other styles. In this article, we’ll discuss how to put your hair up over 50 and share some tips to ensure your hair stays healthy and strong.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Your Hair Up Over 50
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Putting Your Hair Up Over 50
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair

How to Put Your Hair Up Over 50

Putting your hair up over 50 can be a great way to switch up your hairstyle and keep your hair out of your face. Here are a few ways to put your hair up:

  • Ponytail
  • Bun
  • French Twist
  • Chignon
  • Braids

Each of these styles can be modified to fit your hair type and personal style. Keep reading for a step-by-step guide on how to achieve some of these looks.

Step-by-Step Guide

Ponytail

  1. Start by brushing your hair to remove tangles.
  2. Gather your hair into a high or low ponytail, depending on your preference.
  3. Tie your hair with an elastic band.
  4. If you have thin hair, use a hair tie that won't damage your hair.
  5. If you have thick hair, consider using a scrunchie or a larger hair tie to hold your hair in place.

Bun

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  4. If you have thin or delicate hair, use hair pins instead of bobby pins to avoid breakage.
  5. You can also use a hair donut to create a fuller bun.

French Twist

  1. Brush your hair to remove tangles.
  2. Gather your hair at the nape of your neck.
  3. Twist your hair upwards towards the crown of your head.
  4. Secure the twist with bobby pins.
  5. Use hairspray to set the style in place.

Tips for Putting Your Hair Up Over 50

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when putting your hair up over 50:

  • Use gentle hair ties and bobby pins to avoid breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Don't pull your hair too tight, as this can cause headaches and even hair loss over time.
  • Consider using hair accessories like headbands, hair clips, and scarves to add a pop of color and style to your hair.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for your hair type and face shape.

FAQs

Can putting my hair up cause hair loss?

While putting your hair up too tightly or too often can cause hair loss over time, occasional styling is unlikely to cause any damage.

What hair accessories work best for putting hair up over 50?

Headbands, hair clips, and scarves can all be great accessories for putting your hair up over 50. Look for gentle accessories that won't damage your hair or cause breakage.

Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair

As we age, our hair may become thinner, drier, or more brittle. Here are a few tips to keep your hair healthy and strong:

  • Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are formulated for aging hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to support hair growth and strength.
  • Consider taking supplements like biotin or collagen to support hair health.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

By following these tips and tricks, you can put your hair up over 50 with confidence and style.
