How To Stop Hair Thinning From Pcos

Written by Daniel Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome, commonly known as PCOS, is a hormonal disorder that affects many women. One of the most common symptoms of PCOS is hair thinning, which can be distressing and affect self-esteem. If you're dealing with hair thinning from PCOS, there are steps you can take to stop it. In this article, we'll go over how to stop hair thinning from PCOS, including tips, tricks, and FAQs.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Stop Hair Thinning from PCOS

There are several ways to stop hair thinning from PCOS, including:

  • Medical Treatments
  • Lifestyle Changes
  • Hair Care

Medical treatments for PCOS hair thinning include prescription medications such as birth control pills, spironolactone, and metformin. These medications can help regulate hormone levels and reduce hair loss. Some women may also benefit from low-level laser therapy or hair transplants.

Lifestyle changes such as eating a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and managing stress can also help reduce hair thinning from PCOS. PCOS is often associated with insulin resistance, so maintaining a healthy weight and eating a low-carb diet may be particularly beneficial.

Hair care is also an important aspect of preventing hair thinning from PCOS. Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and try to use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners. Regularly deep conditioning your hair can also help keep it healthy and prevent breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're dealing with hair thinning from PCOS, follow these steps:

  1. Consult with your doctor to determine the best treatment plan for your specific case.
  2. Make lifestyle changes such as eating a healthy diet, exercising regularly, and managing stress.
  3. Use gentle hair care products and avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  4. Regularly deep condition your hair to keep it healthy and prevent breakage.
  5. Consider low-level laser therapy or hair transplants if recommended by your doctor.

Tips for Preventing Hair Thinning from PCOS

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair thinning from PCOS:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids and ponytails.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as wet hair is more prone to breakage.
  • Take hair supplements such as biotin and iron, which can help promote hair growth.
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption, as these habits can contribute to hair loss.

PCOS and Hair Thinning FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about PCOS and hair thinning:

Why does PCOS cause hair thinning?

PCOS can cause hair thinning because it disrupts the normal hormonal balance in the body. High levels of androgens, or male hormones, can cause hair follicles to shrink and eventually stop producing hair.

Can hair thinning from PCOS be reversed?

Yes, hair thinning from PCOS can often be reversed with the right treatment plan. However, it's important to consult with your doctor to determine the best course of action for your specific case.

How long does it take to see results from PCOS hair thinning treatments?

The amount of time it takes to see results from PCOS hair thinning treatments can vary depending on the treatment and the individual. Some women may see results within a few weeks, while others may take several months to see improvement.

Hair Care for Women with PCOS

If you have PCOS, it's important to take extra care of your hair to prevent hair thinning and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips for hair care for women with PCOS:

  • Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids and ponytails.
  • Take hair supplements such as biotin and iron, which can help promote hair growth.

By following these tips and consulting with your doctor, you can stop hair thinning from PCOS and promote healthy hair growth.
