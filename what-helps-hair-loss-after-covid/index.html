What Helps Hair Loss After Covid A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 · 4 min read

COVID-19 has affected our lives in many ways. One of the lesser-known side effects of the virus is hair loss. If you have been experiencing hair loss after COVID, you are not alone. In this article, we will discuss what causes hair loss after COVID and how to treat it. We will also provide you with some tips for hair care to prevent further damage.

Table of Contents

  • What Causes Hair Loss After COVID
  • How to Treat Hair Loss After COVID
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss After COVID
  • Tips for Hair Care to Prevent Further Damage
  • FAQs

What Causes Hair Loss After COVID

There are several reasons why people experience hair loss after COVID. The virus can cause a lot of stress on the body, which can lead to hair loss. Additionally, COVID can cause inflammation, which can damage hair follicles. Some people also experience hair loss as a side effect of medications used to treat COVID.

Another reason for hair loss after COVID is telogen effluvium. This is a type of hair loss that occurs due to physical or emotional stress. It is usually temporary and can last up to six months.

How to Treat Hair Loss After COVID

If you are experiencing hair loss after COVID, there are several things you can do to treat it. The first step is to consult a dermatologist or a trichologist. They can diagnose the underlying cause of your hair loss and recommend a treatment plan.

Some common treatments for hair loss after COVID include:

  • Topical medications such as minoxidil
  • Oral medications such as finasteride
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy
  • Hair transplant surgery

It is important to note that not all treatments may be suitable for everyone. Your doctor will recommend the best treatment for your specific needs.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss After COVID

If you are looking for a step-by-step guide to treating hair loss after COVID, here are some general steps you can follow:

  1. Consult a dermatologist or a trichologist
  2. Follow their recommended treatment plan
  3. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails
  5. Avoid using hot styling tools such as straighteners and curling irons
  6. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair such as bleach and hair dyes
  7. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair
  8. Avoid using tight hats or headbands
  9. Get enough sleep and manage stress levels
  10. Be patient – it may take several months to see results

Tips for Hair Care to Prevent Further Damage

If you want to prevent further damage to your hair, here are some tips you can follow:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using too many styling products
  • Avoid exposing your hair to too much sun or chlorine
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQs

Q: Is hair loss after COVID permanent?

A: Hair loss after COVID can be temporary or permanent, depending on the underlying cause. If it is caused by telogen effluvium, it is usually temporary and can last up to six months. However, if it is caused by damage to hair follicles, it may be permanent.

Q: Can diet affect hair loss after COVID?

A: Yes, diet can have an impact on hair loss after COVID. Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and prevent further damage.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss after COVID?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss after COVID. The virus can cause a lot of stress on the body, which can lead to hair loss. Additionally, telogen effluvium is a type of hair loss that occurs due to physical or emotional stress.

Overall, hair loss after COVID can be a distressing experience. However, with the right treatment plan and hair care routine, you can promote hair growth and prevent further damage. Consult a dermatologist or a trichologist for personalized advice and follow their recommended treatment plan. Be patient and take care of your hair – it will thank you in the long run!
