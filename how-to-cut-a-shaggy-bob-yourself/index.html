How To Cut A Shaggy Bob Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a chic and low-maintenance haircut, a shaggy bob might just be the perfect choice for you. Not only is it trendy and versatile, but it's also relatively easy to achieve at home. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting a shaggy bob yourself, from the initial preparation to the finishing touches. Let's get started!

Preparation

Before you begin cutting your hair, it's important to gather all the necessary tools and materials. You will need:

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • Thinning shears (optional)
  • A comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • A mirror
  • A well-lit room with a clean surface (to catch any hair clippings)

It's also a good idea to wear an old shirt or towel to protect your clothes from hair clippings.

Sectioning

Once you have all your tools ready, it's time to section your hair. Start by dividing your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips or ties to secure each section.

Next, take the front section of hair and divide it into two subsections: a top and a bottom section. The top section should be about one inch wide and run from the top of your head to just above your ears. Clip the top section out of the way for now.

Cutting the Hair

With the bottom section of hair still clipped in place, take your scissors and cut the hair straight across, about an inch below your chin. This will serve as your guide for the rest of the haircut.

Release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Use your guide to cut the hair in a straight line across, following the natural curve of your head. Repeat this process on the other side of your head.

Once you have cut both sides, take out the clips and let your hair fall naturally. Use your scissors to trim any uneven pieces or longer strands, being careful not to cut too much.

Texturizing and Layering

To give your shaggy bob some texture and movement, you can add some layers. Take small sections of hair and use your thinning shears to remove some of the bulk. Start at the ends and work your way up, being careful not to take off too much at once.

You can also use your scissors to create longer layers by holding the hair at a 45-degree angle and snipping into the ends.

Trimming the Bangs

If you have bangs, now is the time to trim them. Comb your bangs straight down and cut them to your desired length. For a shaggy look, you can use your scissors to create some texture by snipping into the ends.

Final Touches

Take a step back and look at your haircut in the mirror. Check for any uneven pieces or areas that need more trimming. Once you're satisfied with the length and shape, you can style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Successful Shaggy Bob Cut

  • Start with clean, dry hair to make it easier to cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small sections and work slowly.
  • If you're unsure about a certain section, leave it for later and come back to it after you've cut the rest of your hair.
  • Use a comb to keep your sections neat and even.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and layers until you find the perfect look for you.

FAQs

How often should I trim my shaggy bob?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your shaggy bob.

Can I cut a shaggy bob on curly hair?

Absolutely! A shaggy bob can look great on curly hair. Just be sure to cut the hair when it's dry and avoid cutting too much to prevent frizz.

Do I have to use thinning shears?

No, thinning shears are optional. If you prefer a blunt look, you can skip this step and simply use your scissors to add layers.

Hair Care and Maintenance

To keep your shaggy bob looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type, and avoid over-washing to prevent dryness. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and minimize breakage. And don't forget to get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your shaggy bob!
