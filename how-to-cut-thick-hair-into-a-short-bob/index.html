How To Cut Thick Hair Into A Short Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are looking to switch up your hairstyle, a short bob may be just what you need. However, if you have thick hair, it can be a challenge to achieve the perfect cut. In this guide, we will walk you through how to cut thick hair into a short bob, including tips, tricks, and hair care advice.

How to Cut Thick Hair into a Short Bob

Before we begin, it's important to note that cutting hair can be tricky and requires a certain level of skill. If you're not confident in your ability to cut hair, it's always best to seek out a professional stylist. That being said, if you're up for the challenge, here's how to cut thick hair into a short bob:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's best to start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and shape of your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four equal parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate.

Step 3: Start with the back

Starting with the back section, comb your hair straight down and cut it to the desired length. It's best to start with small cuts and work your way up, as it's easier to cut more hair than to add it back in.

Step 4: Move to the sides

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the sides. Comb your hair straight down and cut it to the same length as the back section.

Step 5: Finish with the top

Finally, move on to the top section. Comb your hair straight up and cut it to the desired length. It's best to use a point-cutting technique, which involves cutting into the hair at an angle, to achieve a more textured look.

Step 6: Blend your hair

Once you've finished cutting all sections, it's time to blend your hair. Use a comb to blend the sections together, ensuring that there are no noticeable lines or unevenness.

Step 7: Style your hair

Once you're happy with the length and shape of your hair, it's time to style it. You can use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to achieve your desired look.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair into a Short Bob

Cutting thick hair can be challenging, but there are a few tips and tricks that can help make the process easier:

  • Use sharp scissors: Dull scissors can cause split ends and make it difficult to achieve a clean cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time: Cutting too much hair at once can lead to unevenness and mistakes.
  • Take breaks: Cutting hair can be tiring, so take breaks as needed to avoid making mistakes.

FAQ

Here are a few frequently asked questions about cutting thick hair into a short bob:

Can you cut thick hair into a short bob without layers?

Yes, you can cut thick hair into a short bob without layers. However, adding layers can help add texture and movement to the hair.

How short should a short bob be?

A short bob can vary in length, but generally falls anywhere between the chin and the shoulders.

Can you cut your own hair into a short bob?

It's possible to cut your own hair into a short bob, but it can be challenging. If you're not confident in your ability to cut hair, it's best to seek out a professional stylist.

Hair Care for Short Bobs

Once you've cut your hair into a short bob, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut your thick hair into a short bob like a pro. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll end up with a beautiful new hairstyle that you'll love.
