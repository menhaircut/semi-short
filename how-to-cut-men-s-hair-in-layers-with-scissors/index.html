How To Cut Men s Hair In Layers With Scissors A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to try something new with your hair? Layered haircuts are a great way to switch up your look and add some texture to your hair. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut men's hair in layers with scissors. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced barber, these step-by-step instructions will help you achieve a layered haircut that looks great on any man.

Description

A layered haircut involves cutting the hair into different lengths to create texture and volume. Men's layered haircuts are versatile and can be customized to suit any face shape and hair type. The layers can be subtle or dramatic, depending on the client's preference. A layered haircut is perfect for men who want to add some movement and life to their hair without sacrificing length.

How to Cut Men's Hair in Layers with Scissors

Before you start cutting, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and a cape to protect the client's clothing.

Step 1: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair into manageable sections. Use a comb to create a horizontal part across the back of the head, from ear to ear. Clip the top section of hair up and out of the way.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Take the bottom section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle, starting at the back and working your way to the front. This will create a gradual graduation of length, with the hair getting slightly longer towards the face.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Next, release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Take a section of hair from the middle of the head and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, following the length of the bottom layer. This will create a layered effect, with the middle layer slightly shorter than the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, take the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold a section of hair between your fingers and cut it at a slight angle, following the length of the middle layer. This will create a layered effect, with the top layer slightly shorter than the middle layer.

Step-by-Step Instructions

To recap, here are the step-by-step instructions for cutting men's hair in layers with scissors:

  1. Section the hair
  2. Cut the bottom layer
  3. Cut the middle layer
  4. Cut the top layer

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair in Layers

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting men's hair in layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp and easier to work with.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in uneven layers.
  • Use a comb to check for any uneven sections and trim as needed.
  • Finish with a light texturizing spray to enhance the layers.

FAQs

How often should I get a layered haircut?

The frequency of getting a layered haircut depends on the individual's hair type and growth rate. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

What face shapes are suited for layered haircuts?

Layered haircuts can be customized to suit any face shape. For example, men with round faces can benefit from longer layers that create the illusion of length, while men with square faces can benefit from shorter layers that soften the angles of the face.

Hair Care After a Layered Haircut

After getting a layered haircut, it's important to maintain the health and appearance of the hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too frequently, as this can damage the hair and create split ends. Regular trims every 4-6 weeks will help maintain the shape and style of the haircut.

Now that you know how to cut men's hair in layers with scissors, you can try this versatile and stylish haircut on your clients. With a little practice and patience, you can create a layered haircut that looks great and suits any face shape and hair type.
