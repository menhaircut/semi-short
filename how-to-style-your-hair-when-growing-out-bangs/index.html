How To Style Your Hair When Growing Out Bangs

Written by Oliver Jun 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of dealing with the awkward stages of growing out your bangs? Don't worry, you're not alone! Growing out bangs can be a tricky process, but with the right styling techniques, you can make it look effortless. In this article, we'll walk you through how to style your hair when growing out bangs, with tips, step by step instructions, and hair care advice.

  • How To Style Your Hair When Growing Out Bangs
  • Step By Step Instructions

How To Style Your Hair When Growing Out Bangs

There are several ways to style your hair when growing out bangs. Here are a few techniques:

1. Side-Swept Bangs

One of the easiest ways to style your hair when growing out bangs is to sweep them to the side. Use a round brush to blow dry your bangs, directing them to the side. Once dry, use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves. Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place.

2. Braids

Braids are a great way to hide your bangs while they're growing out. French braids, dutch braids, and fishtail braids all work well. Start the braid at your hairline and braid your bangs into the rest of your hair. Secure with an elastic band and use bobby pins to tuck any loose pieces into the braid. This style is perfect for second-day hair or when you don't have time to wash your hair.

3. Top Knot

A top knot is a simple and stylish way to keep your bangs out of your face. Gather your hair into a high ponytail and twist it into a bun. Secure with bobby pins and pull out a few face-framing pieces if you want. This style works well for both casual and formal occasions.

Step By Step Instructions

Here are step by step instructions on how to style your hair when growing out bangs:

1. Blow Dry Your Bangs

Start with clean, damp hair. Use a round brush to blow dry your bangs, directing them to the side. Use the brush to lift the roots and create volume.

2. Smooth With A Flat Iron

Once your bangs are dry, use a flat iron to smooth out any kinks or waves. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

3. Add Texture

If you want to add some texture, use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo. Spray it on your roots and scrunch with your fingers to create volume and texture.

4. Secure With Hairspray

Finish with a light hairspray to hold the style in place. Use a flexible hold hairspray to avoid any stiffness or crunchiness.

Tips For Styling Your Hair

Here are some tips to make styling your hair when growing out bangs easier:

1. Use Accessories

Headbands, hair clips, and bobby pins are all great accessories to help keep your bangs out of your face. Experiment with different styles and colors to find what works best for you.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every other day or every three days, depending on your hair type.

3. Get Regular Trims

Even when you're growing out your bangs, it's important to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Ask your stylist to trim your hair around your face to keep it looking neat and polished.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How Long Does It Take To Grow Out Bangs?

It can take anywhere from three to six months to grow out bangs, depending on how fast your hair grows. Be patient and try different styling techniques to make the process easier.

2. Can I Style My Bangs If They're Too Short?

If your bangs are too short to sweep to the side, try pinning them back with bobby pins or a headband. You can also try using a texturizing spray to create some volume and texture.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

1. Use A Heat Protectant Spray

Whenever you use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

2. Use A Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to nourish and hydrate your hair. Apply it to damp hair and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing out.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as ponytails or braids, for extended periods of time. This can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can style your hair with confidence while growing out your bangs. Remember to be patient, take good care of your hair, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
