How To Air Dry Thin Frizzy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have thin, frizzy hair, you may dread washing your hair because it can be a hassle to style. Blow drying can damage your hair and using heat styling tools too often can cause your hair to break and become even frizzier. However, air drying your hair can be a great alternative to avoid heat damage and keep your hair healthy. In this article, we will show you how to air dry thin frizzy hair in a few simple steps.

How to Air Dry Thin Frizzy Hair

Air drying your hair may seem like a simple task, but it requires some preparation and the right techniques to achieve the best results. Here are some tips to help you air dry your thin frizzy hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

When washing your hair, use a gentle shampoo that is sulfate-free and designed for thin, frizzy hair. This type of shampoo will help to minimize damage and frizz, leaving your hair smoother and more manageable.

2. Use a microfiber towel

After washing your hair, gently squeeze out the excess water using a microfiber towel. Avoid rubbing your hair with a regular towel, as it can cause friction and breakage, leading to more frizz.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair when it is still damp. This will help to detangle your hair and add moisture, making it easier to manage when it is dry.

4. Use a wide-toothed comb

When your hair is still damp, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair. Start from the ends and work your way up, being careful not to tug or pull on your hair. This will help to prevent breakage and minimize frizz.

5. Let your hair air dry

Let your hair air dry naturally, without using any heat styling tools. This may take some time, but it is worth the wait. Avoid touching your hair too much while it is drying, as this can cause frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to air drying thin frizzy hair:

Step 1:

Wash your hair with a gentle sulfate-free shampoo.

Step 2:

Gently squeeze out the excess water using a microfiber towel.

Step 3:

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair.

Step 4:

Detangle your hair using a wide-toothed comb.

Step 5:

Let your hair air dry naturally.

Tips for Air Drying Thin Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you air dry your thin frizzy hair:

  • Use a hair serum or oil to add shine and tame frizz.
  • Try using a hair mask once a week to add moisture and nourishment to your hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more frizzy.

FAQs

Can I use a hair dryer on low heat to speed up the drying process?

It is best to avoid using a hair dryer, even on low heat, as it can cause damage to your hair and make it more prone to frizz. If you need to dry your hair quickly, try using a hair towel that absorbs moisture quickly.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have thin frizzy hair, you may want to wash your hair every other day or every three days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. You can also use a dry shampoo in between washes to refresh your hair.

What can I do to prevent my hair from becoming frizzy?

There are several things you can do to prevent your hair from becoming frizzy, such as using a sulfate-free shampoo, applying a leave-in conditioner, using a wide-toothed comb, and avoiding heat styling tools. You can also try using a hair serum or oil to add shine and tame frizz.

Hair Care for Thin Frizzy Hair

Here are some hair care tips to help you keep your thin frizzy hair healthy:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo that is designed for thin frizzy hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to add moisture and detangle your hair.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to prevent breakage and minimize frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more frizzy.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to add moisture and nourishment to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to air dry thin frizzy hair, you can enjoy healthy, shiny, and manageable hair without using heat styling tools. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and follow these tips to achieve the best results.
