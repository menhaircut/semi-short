How To Use Texturizing Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you want to add volume and texture to your hair, texturizing scissors are a great tool to use. In this guide, we'll show you how to use texturizing scissors to achieve the perfect look. We'll also provide some tips and answer some frequently asked questions about texturizing scissors.

How to Use Texturizing Scissors

Texturizing scissors are designed to remove some of the bulk from your hair without taking off too much length. The scissors have teeth or notches on one blade, which cut the hair at different lengths, creating layers and texture. Follow these steps to use texturizing scissors:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before using texturizing scissors, it's important to wash and dry your hair. This will make it easier to cut and give you a better idea of how your hair will look once it's styled. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips or ties. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much. Start with the bottom layer of your hair and work your way up.

Step 3: Hold the Texturizing Scissors Correctly

Hold the texturizing scissors with your dominant hand and the regular scissors with your other hand. The teeth should be pointing upwards and away from your head. This will ensure that the scissors cut the hair at different lengths, creating layers and texture.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair using the texturizing scissors. Hold a section of your hair between your fingers and use the texturizing scissors to cut into the hair at various points. Work your way through each section of your hair, cutting at different angles to create layers and texture.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check your work in the mirror. Make sure that the layers are even and that you haven't cut too much off. If you need to make any adjustments, use the regular scissors to trim any uneven sections.

Tips for Using Texturizing Scissors

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using texturizing scissors:

  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually work your way up.
  • Use the texturizing scissors sparingly to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Practice on a mannequin head or with a friend's hair before trying it on your own hair.
  • Use the regular scissors to trim any uneven sections.
  • Always cut at an angle to create layers and texture.

FAQ About Texturizing Scissors

Here are some frequently asked questions about texturizing scissors:

Can I Use Texturizing Scissors on Curly Hair?

Yes, texturizing scissors can be used on curly hair. However, it's important to be careful not to cut too much off, as curly hair tends to shrink when it's cut.

How Often Should I Use Texturizing Scissors?

Texturizing scissors can be used as often as you like, depending on how much texture and volume you want in your hair. However, it's important to avoid overusing them, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Are Texturizing Scissors Expensive?

Texturizing scissors can be more expensive than regular scissors, but they are worth the investment if you want to achieve a textured, layered look.

Hair Care After Using Texturizing Scissors

After using texturizing scissors, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain the shape and texture of your hair.

Using texturizing scissors is a great way to add texture and volume to your hair. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect look. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at using texturizing scissors in no time!
