How To Fix A Crooked Haircut Easy And Practical Tips

Written by Petter Nov 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Crooked haircuts can happen to anyone, even to the most experienced hairstylist. If you've recently had a bad haircut, don't panic. There are plenty of ways to fix it without having to go back to the salon. Below, we'll give you some tips on how to fix a crooked haircut and make it look polished and stylish.

How to Fix a Crooked Haircut

The first thing you need to do is to identify the problem areas. Check if one side is longer than the other, or if the layers are uneven. Once you've identified the problem, you can start fixing it. Here are some ways to make a crooked haircut look better:

1. Trim it Yourself

If the problem is minor, you can fix it yourself by trimming the longer side to match the shorter one. Use a sharp pair of scissors, and make sure to cut small sections at a time. Start at the back and work your way up to the front. Don't forget to check the length from different angles to ensure that it's even.

2. Use a Flat Iron

If the problem is with the layers, you can use a flat iron to straighten them out. Start at the root, and pull the iron down to the ends. This will give your hair a sleek and polished look. Don't forget to use a heat protectant before using the flat iron to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Add Volume

If your hair is too short on one side, you can add volume to that side to make it appear longer. Use a volumizing mousse or spray, and blow-dry your hair with a round brush. This will give your hair some lift and add volume to the shorter side.

4. Style it Differently

If all else fails, you can style your hair differently to hide the problem areas. Part your hair on the other side, or try a different hairstyle that will cover up the uneven parts. You can also use accessories like headbands or scarves to distract from the problem areas.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to fix a crooked haircut:

Step 1: Identify the Problem Areas

Check if one side is longer than the other, or if the layers are uneven.

Step 2: Trim the Longer Side

If the problem is minor, trim the longer side to match the shorter one.

Step 3: Use a Flat Iron

If the problem is with the layers, use a flat iron to straighten them out.

Step 4: Add Volume

If your hair is too short on one side, add volume to that side to make it appear longer.

Step 5: Style it Differently

If all else fails, style your hair differently to hide the problem areas.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you fix a crooked haircut:

  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Use a mirror to check the length from different angles.
  • Don't hesitate to ask for help from a friend or family member.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles.
  • Use quality hair care products to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

FAQs

1. How can I prevent a crooked haircut?

Make sure to communicate clearly with your hairstylist about the style you want. Bring pictures or examples to help them understand your vision. It's also important to choose a skilled and experienced hairstylist.

2. How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy and even hair.

3. Can I fix a crooked haircut at home?

Yes, you can fix a crooked haircut at home using the tips we provided above. However, if the problem is severe, it's best to seek help from a professional hairstylist.

Hair Care

Keeping your hair healthy and strong is essential to prevent a crooked haircut. Here are some tips for healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools frequently, and always use a heat protectant.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips, you can maintain healthy and beautiful hair that's easy to style and maintain.
