How To Cut A Short Shaggy Bob Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jul 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and low-maintenance haircut, a short shaggy bob might be just what you need. Not only is it easy to style, but it's versatile enough to suit different face shapes and hair types. The best part? You can even cut it yourself at home, without having to book a salon appointment. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting a short shaggy bob yourself, from start to finish.

Description

A short shaggy bob is a haircut that's characterized by choppy layers, uneven ends, and lots of texture. It's typically shoulder-length or shorter and can be styled in different ways, such as messy, tousled, or sleek. This haircut is popular among women who want a low-maintenance yet fashionable look that doesn't require a lot of styling or upkeep.

How to

Here are the steps to follow if you want to cut a short shaggy bob yourself:

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to have the right tools on hand. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Mirror

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, so wash and blow-dry your hair before you start. Make sure your hair is completely dry, as wet hair can appear longer and lead to uneven cutting.

Step 3: Section your hair

Use the comb to section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Clip or tie each section separately so that they don't get in the way while you're cutting.

Step 4: Cut the back

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it straight across with the scissors. Repeat this process for the rest of the back section, working from bottom to top.

Step 5: Cut the sides

Move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it at an angle, starting from the length of the back section and gradually getting shorter towards the front. Repeat this process for the other side, making sure to keep the length and angle consistent.

Step 6: Cut the top

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and cut it at an angle, starting from the length of the sides section and gradually getting shorter towards the back. Repeat this process for the rest of the top section, making sure to blend the layers with the sides and back.

Step 7: Check for symmetry

Once you've finished cutting, use the mirror to check for symmetry and make any necessary adjustments.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut conservatively, as you can always cut more later.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends and uneven cutting.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can lead to uneven cutting.
  • Check for symmetry and balance throughout the cutting process.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair yourself, consider asking a friend or family member for help.

FAQ

How often should I trim a short shaggy bob?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6 to 8 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Can I style a short shaggy bob differently?

Yes, you can style a short shaggy bob in different ways, such as messy, tousled, or sleek. Experiment with different products and techniques to find a style that suits you.

Is a short shaggy bob suitable for all face shapes?

A short shaggy bob can be customized to suit different face shapes and hair types. Consult with your hairstylist or do some research to find a shaggy bob that flatters your features.

Hair Care

To keep your short shaggy bob looking its best, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat or styling products that can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Cutting a short shaggy bob yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a stylish and low-maintenance haircut that suits your personality and lifestyle. Happy cutting!
