How To Do A Mid Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a hairstyle that's both stylish and low maintenance, a mid taper fade might be just what you need. This versatile cut works well with a variety of hair types and can be tailored to your individual preferences. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a mid taper fade, as well as provide tips and answers to common questions.

How to Do a Mid Taper Fade

A mid taper fade is a haircut that gradually fades from a longer length on top to a shorter length on the sides and back. The fade begins at the midpoint of the head, hence the name "mid taper fade."

To achieve a mid taper fade, you'll need to use clippers of varying lengths and blend the different lengths together. It's important to note that this cut requires some skill, so if you're new to cutting hair, you may want to consider going to a professional stylist.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieve a mid taper fade:

Step 1: Determine the Length of the Top

Decide how long you want the hair on top to be. This will determine the starting length for the fade. Use scissors to cut the top to the desired length if necessary.

Step 2: Choose the Length of the Clippers

Choose the length of the clippers you want to start with. A good rule of thumb is to start with a longer length and work your way down. For example, you might start with a #4 clipper guard and work down to a #2 or #1. You can also use a fading comb to blend the lengths together.

Step 3: Start the Fade

Using your clippers, begin cutting the hair at the midpoint of the head. Start with the longest length and work your way down to the shortest length. Be sure to blend the different lengths together to create a smooth transition.

Step 4: Blend the Sides and Back

Once you've reached the desired length on the sides and back, use your clippers and fading comb to blend the hair into the fade. Be sure to take your time and blend the hair evenly for a seamless transition.

Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline

Use your clippers or a razor to clean up the neckline and create a defined edge. Be sure to follow the natural curve of the hairline.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect Mid Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect mid taper fade:

  • Start with a longer length and work your way down to create a gradual fade.
  • Use a fading comb to blend the different lengths together.
  • Take your time and be patient. This cut requires some skill and precision.
  • Use a mirror to check your work and make adjustments as necessary.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist if you're new to cutting hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a mid taper fade?

A: It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how neat you want to keep the cut. Generally, you can go 2-4 weeks between haircuts.

Q: Can I do a mid taper fade on curly hair?

A: Yes! A mid taper fade works well with curly hair. Just be sure to account for the hair's natural texture when cutting and blending.

Q: What should I do if I make a mistake?

A: Don't panic! If you make a mistake, try to blend the hair to hide the mistake. You can also use a longer clipper guard to even out the length.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Mid Taper Fade

Here are some tips for maintaining your mid taper fade:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair soft and moisturized.
  • Use a hair oil or serum to add shine and reduce frizz.
  • Trim your hair every 2-4 weeks to maintain the shape of the cut.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve a stylish mid taper fade that will turn heads. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to experiment and try new techniques. Good luck!
