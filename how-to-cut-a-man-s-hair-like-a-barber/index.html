How To Cut A Man s Hair Like A Barber A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're tired of spending money on haircuts, or just want to learn a new skill, cutting your own hair at home can be a great option. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without ever leaving your house. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to cut a man's hair like a barber.

Tools You'll Need

Before you can start cutting hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with guard attachments
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or old shirt
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Haircutting shears

With these tools in hand, you're ready to start your haircut.

Preparation

Before you start cutting, it's important to prepare your hair. Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it thoroughly. You want your hair to be damp, but not dripping wet.

Next, put on your haircutting cape or an old shirt to protect your clothes. Then, use your comb to part your hair where you normally wear it. This will help you create an even cut.

Sectioning the Hair

Once your hair is prepped, it's time to section it. Start by using your clippers with a longer guard attachment to trim the top of your hair. Then, switch to a shorter guard attachment to trim the sides and back.

After you've trimmed your hair with the clippers, use your scissors to cut the top of your hair. Take small sections of hair at a time and cut them at a slight angle to create layers.

Cutting the Hair

When you start cutting, it's important to remember to take your time. Use your comb to lift a section of hair, and then use your scissors to cut off any split ends or damaged hair.

As you work your way around your head, make sure to keep your cuts even. If you need to, use your comb to measure the length of your hair as you cut.

Blending the Hair

After you've cut the top of your hair, it's time to blend it with the sides and back. Use your scissors to make small, snipping motions to blend the hair together. Work in sections, starting at the back and moving forward.

Trimming the Sides and Back

Once your hair is blended, use your clippers with a shorter guard attachment to trim the sides and back of your hair. Use your comb to lift the hair and trim it in small sections. Make sure to trim any hair around your ears and neck.

Texturizing the Hair

If you want to add some texture to your hair, use your haircutting shears to create wispy ends. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle to create a choppy, textured look.

Cleaning Up

After you've finished cutting, use your comb to make sure all of your hair is even. Then, brush off any loose hair and clean up the area.

Tips for a Great Haircut

  • Invest in high-quality tools. A good pair of scissors and clippers will make all the difference.
  • Start with a longer guard attachment and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring, so don't be afraid to stop and rest.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from all angles.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if I have long hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair no matter the length. However, cutting long hair can be more difficult, so it's important to take your time and be patient.

Do I need to use guard attachments with my clippers?

Yes, guard attachments help you achieve an even cut and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After the Cut

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to cut a man's hair like a barber in no time. Remember to take your time, be patient, and practice regularly to improve your skills.
