How I Choose My Hairstyle Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Jun 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're like me, choosing a hairstyle can be a daunting task. With so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to find a style that fits your personality, face shape, and lifestyle. However, over the years, I've discovered a few tips and tricks that have helped me identify my perfect hairstyle. In this blog post, I'll be sharing my process with you to help you find your ideal look.

Identify Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a hairstyle is understanding your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong. Each shape has its own set of recommended hairstyles that can enhance your features and balance your proportions.

To identify your face shape, pull your hair back and look in the mirror. Use a measuring tape or ruler to measure the widest points of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Then, compare your measurements to the following guidelines:

  • Oval: Forehead and jawline are roughly the same width, with a slightly narrower chin.
  • Round: Face is nearly as wide as it is long, with full cheeks and a rounded chin.
  • Square: Forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are roughly the same width.
  • Heart: Forehead is wider than the cheeks and jawline, and the chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline.
  • Oblong: Face is longer than it is wide, with a high forehead and a narrow chin.

Once you've identified your face shape, you can start exploring hairstyles that will flatter your features. A good stylist will be able to recommend cuts and styles that suit your face shape and bring out your best features.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Another important factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle is your lifestyle. Think about how much time and effort you're willing to put into styling your hair each day, as well as any activities or events that may require a specific look.

For example, if you have a busy schedule and don't have much time to spend on your hair, a low-maintenance style like a bob or pixie cut may be a good choice. On the other hand, if you have a formal event coming up, you may want to opt for an updo or sleek blowout.

It's also important to consider your personal style and the image you want to project. Are you edgy and adventurous, or classic and sophisticated? Your hairstyle can say a lot about you, so choose one that reflects your personality and makes you feel confident.

Know Your Hair Type

When choosing a hairstyle, it's important to take your hair type into consideration. Factors like texture, thickness, and natural curl can all affect how a cut will look and perform.

If you have fine hair, for example, you may want to avoid styles that are too heavy or layered, as they can weigh your hair down and make it look limp. If you have curly hair, on the other hand, you may want to opt for a layered cut that enhances your natural texture.

Be honest with your stylist about your hair type and any challenges you've had in the past with certain styles. Together, you can choose a cut and style that works with your hair's natural tendencies.

Choose Your Length

Another key decision to make when choosing a hairstyle is the length. Do you want to keep your hair long and flowing, or are you ready for a dramatic chop?

Again, your face shape, lifestyle, and hair type can all play a role in this decision. For example, if you have a round face, a longer style with layers can help elongate your features. If you have thick, curly hair, a shorter cut may be more manageable.

It's also important to consider how often you're willing to get a trim. Shorter styles generally require more frequent maintenance to keep their shape, while longer styles can go longer between cuts.

Think About Color

Choosing a new hairstyle doesn't have to mean a full-on dye job, but adding some color can be a fun way to freshen up your look.

If you're considering a new color, think about your skin tone and natural hair color. Warm skin tones generally look best with warm hair colors like golden blonde or auburn, while cool skin tones pair well with cooler shades like ash blonde or chestnut brown.

You can also play with highlights or lowlights to add dimension and depth to your hair. Talk to your stylist about the best color options for your skin tone and desired look.

Play with Texture

Texture is another factor to consider when choosing a hairstyle. Do you want sleek and straight locks, or do you prefer a more tousled, beachy look?

Your hair type can again play a role here, but there are products and styling tools that can help you achieve your desired texture. A smoothing serum or flat iron can help tame frizz, while a texturizing spray can add volume and movement.

Experiment with different products and techniques to find the texture that works best for you.

Add Some Accessories

If you're not quite ready for a full-on hairstyle change, adding some accessories can be a fun way to switch up your look. Headbands, scarves, and hair clips can all add a pop of color or interest to your hair.

Accessories can also be a great way to make a simple hairstyle feel more special. A sleek ponytail can be dressed up with a sparkly hair clip, while a messy bun can be elevated with a silk scarf.

Visit Your Salon

Once you've done your research and identified your ideal hairstyle, it's time to visit your salon. Bring in pictures or examples of the style you're looking for, and be open to your stylist's suggestions.

Remember that your stylist is a trained professional who can offer valuable insights and advice. Together, you can create a look that suits your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Take Care of Your Hair

Finally, once you've chosen your hairstyle, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. This means using the right products for your hair type, avoiding excessive heat styling, and getting regular trims to maintain your shape.

Investing in quality products and tools can also make a big difference in the health and appearance of your hair. Look for products that are tailored to your hair type and concerns, and use heat protectant sprays or serums when using hot tools.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get a haircut?

A: This depends on the length and style of your hair, as well as your personal preferences. Generally, shorter styles require more frequent trims to maintain their shape, while longer styles can go longer between cuts. Talk to your stylist about the best schedule for your hair.

Q: What if I don't like my new hairstyle?

A: If you're not happy with your new look, don't be afraid to speak up. Your stylist wants you to be happy with your hair, and may be able to make adjustments or suggest a different style.

Q: Can I change my hairstyle frequently?

A: Absolutely! Your hair is a fun way to express your personality and style, so don't be afraid to mix things up. Just be sure to take care of your hair and avoid excessive heat styling or chemical treatments that can damage your strands.

Choosing a new hairstyle can be an exciting and rewarding experience. By considering your face shape, lifestyle, hair type, and personal preferences, you can find a style that makes you feel confident and beautiful. Remember to take care of your hair and have fun experimenting with different looks!
