How To Get Natural Korean Curls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 05, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to add some bounce and texture to your hair, natural Korean curls might be just what you need. This hairstyle has been popular in Korea for years, but it’s now gaining popularity in other parts of the world. In this article, we’ll show you how to get natural Korean curls at home, using simple tools and techniques.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Get Natural Korean Curls
  3. Step-by-Step Guide

What are Natural Korean Curls?

Natural Korean curls are soft, bouncy curls that are achieved without using any heat or chemicals. This hairstyle is popular in Korea because it’s easy to achieve and doesn’t damage the hair. Natural Korean curls can be worn by people with any hair type, but they work especially well on straight or slightly wavy hair.

How to Get Natural Korean Curls

Getting natural Korean curls is easy and can be done at home with some simple tools and techniques. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Hair gel or mousse
  • Hair spray

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to achieve natural Korean curls:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles.
  2. Section your hair and clip it up. Start with the bottom section of your hair and clip the rest of your hair up out of the way.
  3. Apply hair gel or mousse to the bottom section of your hair. Make sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.
  4. Twist your hair. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. Keep twisting until the hair starts to curl up on itself.
  5. Pin the curl in place. Use a hair clip to hold the curl in place. Repeat this process with the rest of the bottom section of your hair.
  6. Repeat the process with the rest of your hair. Take down the next section of your hair and repeat steps 3-5 until you’ve curled all of your hair.
  7. Let your hair dry. Leave your hair to dry for a few hours or overnight. The longer you leave it, the tighter the curls will be.
  8. Take out the hair clips. Once your hair is dry, take out the hair clips and gently separate the curls with your fingers.
  9. Apply hair spray. Spray your hair with some hair spray to hold the curls in place.

Tips for Getting Natural Korean Curls

Here are some tips to help you get the best results:

  • Use a small amount of hair gel or mousse. Using too much product can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Twist your hair tightly. The tighter you twist your hair, the tighter the curls will be.
  • Leave your hair to dry completely. If you try to take out the hair clips too soon, the curls may fall out.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to separate your curls. This will help to prevent frizz.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about natural Korean curls:

Q: Can I achieve natural Korean curls on short hair?

A: Yes, you can achieve natural Korean curls on any length of hair. However, the length of your hair will affect the size of the curls.

Q: Will natural Korean curls damage my hair?

A: No, natural Korean curls are achieved without using any heat or chemicals, so they won’t damage your hair.

Q: How long will the curls last?

A: The curls will last until you wash your hair or until the next time you style it.

Hair Care Tips for Natural Korean Curls

Here are some tips for taking care of your natural Korean curls:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can make it dry and brittle.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools. Heat can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so it’s best to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

With these tips and techniques, you can achieve natural Korean curls at home and enjoy a bouncy, playful hairstyle that’s easy to maintain. Give it a try and see how it works for you!
