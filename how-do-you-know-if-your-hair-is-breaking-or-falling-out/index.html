How Do You Know If Your Hair Is Breaking Or Falling Out

Written by Robby Jun 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

In this article, we will be discussing how to identify if your hair is breaking or falling out. Hair loss is a common problem that affects both men and women, but it can be difficult to determine the cause of it. Understanding whether your hair is breaking or falling out is important as it can help you to identify the underlying cause and take appropriate measures to address it.

Description

Hair breakage is when your hair becomes weak and brittle and breaks off easily when you comb or brush it. Hair fall, on the other hand, is when your hair falls out from the roots. The two conditions can look similar, but the underlying causes are different. Hair breakage is often caused by physical damage to the hair, such as heat styling, chemical treatments, or rough handling. Hair fall, on the other hand, can be caused by various factors such as genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and medical conditions.

How to Identify Whether Your Hair is Breaking or Falling Out

The easiest way to determine if your hair is breaking or falling out is to examine the hair that falls out. If you notice that the hair has a white bulb at the end, it is likely falling out from the root. If there is no bulb, it is likely breaking off. You can also examine the texture of the hair. If it feels weak and brittle, it is likely breaking. If it feels thin and wispy, it is likely falling out.

Step-by-Step Guide to Identifying Hair Loss

Step 1: Examine the hair that falls out. If it has a white bulb at the end, it is likely falling out from the root. If there is no bulb, it is likely breaking off.

Step 2: Examine the texture of the hair. If it feels weak and brittle, it is likely breaking. If it feels thin and wispy, it is likely falling out.

Step 3: Look for other signs of hair loss, such as a receding hairline, bald patches, or thinning hair.

Tips for Identifying Hair Loss

  • Pay attention to the amount of hair that falls out when you brush or wash your hair.
  • Look for signs of thinning hair, such as a wider part or a smaller ponytail.
  • Check your pillowcase and hairbrush for excessive hair.
  • Ask a friend or family member to check the back of your head for any signs of bald patches.

FAQs

Q: Is it normal to lose hair every day?

A: Yes, it is normal to lose up to 100 hairs per day.

Q: What can cause hair loss?

A: Hair loss can be caused by various factors such as genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and medical conditions.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Hair Loss

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Avoid chemical treatments such as perms and relaxers.
  • Avoid rough handling of your hair, such as tight hairstyles and vigorous brushing.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid wearing tight hats or headbands that can pull on your hair.

In conclusion, understanding whether your hair is breaking or falling out is important as it can help you to identify the underlying cause and take appropriate measures to address it. If you are experiencing excessive hair loss, it is recommended that you consult a dermatologist or a trichologist to determine the cause and receive proper treatment.
