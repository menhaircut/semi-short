Preventing Frizz When Blow Drying Curly Hair

Written by Petter Oct 31, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that frizz can be a constant battle. When you blow dry your hair, the heat and the air can cause your curls to become frizzy, making it difficult to achieve a smooth and polished look. However, there are steps you can take to prevent frizz when blow drying your curly hair. In this article, we’ll explore how to prevent frizz when blow drying curly hair, as well as some hair care tips and frequently asked questions.

How To Prevent Frizz When Blow Drying Curly Hair

Preventing frizz when blow drying curly hair requires a little bit of preparation and the right technique. Here are some steps you can take to prevent frizz:

1. Start with Clean, Conditioned Hair

Before you blow dry your hair, make sure it’s clean and conditioned. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for curly hair. This will help to keep your curls moisturized and reduce frizz.

2. Use a Heat Protectant

Before you blow dry your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or serum. This will help to protect your hair from the heat and reduce frizz.

3. Use a Diffuser Attachment

When blow drying curly hair, it’s important to use a diffuser attachment. This will help to distribute the air more evenly and reduce frizz. When using a diffuser attachment, hold it close to your scalp and gently scrunch your hair as you dry it.

4. Dry Your Hair in Sections

Blow drying curly hair in sections can help to reduce frizz. Use hair clips to section your hair and dry one section at a time. This will help to ensure that each section is dried evenly and reduce frizz.

5. Finish with a Cold Shot

When you’re finished blow drying your hair, finish with a blast of cold air. This will help to set your style and reduce frizz.

Step By Step: How To Prevent Frizz When Blow Drying Curly Hair

Here’s a step-by-step guide to preventing frizz when blow drying curly hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for curly hair. Rinse well and gently squeeze out excess water with a microfiber towel.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends and mid-lengths.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair with hair clips, starting from the bottom and working your way up.

Step 4: Attach the Diffuser

Attach the diffuser attachment to your blow dryer.

Step 5: Dry Your Hair

Hold the diffuser close to your scalp and gently scrunch your hair as you dry it. Continue this process until your hair is dry.

Step 6: Finish with a Cold Shot

Finish by blasting your hair with cold air to set your style and reduce frizz.

Tips for Preventing Frizz When Blow Drying Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for preventing frizz when blow drying curly hair:

1. Use a Microfiber Towel

Use a microfiber towel to gently squeeze out excess water before blow drying your hair. This will help to reduce frizz.

2. Don’t Touch Your Hair While It’s Drying

While you’re blow drying your hair, avoid touching it as much as possible. This can cause frizz.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb or Your Fingers

After washing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair. This will help to reduce breakage and frizz.

4. Avoid Over-Drying Your Hair

Don’t over-dry your hair when blow drying it. This can cause frizz and damage.

5. Use the Right Products

Use products that are specifically designed for curly hair, including shampoo, conditioner, and styling products.

FAQ: Preventing Frizz When Blow Drying Curly Hair

Q: Can I blow dry my hair if it’s wet?

A: Yes, you can blow dry your hair when it’s wet. However, make sure you use a heat protectant and don’t over-dry your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. Generally, you can wash your curly hair every 2-3 days.

Q: Will using a diffuser attachment damage my hair?

A: No, using a diffuser attachment won’t damage your hair. In fact, it can help to reduce frizz and promote healthy hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Preventing frizz when blow drying curly hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine for curly hair. Here are some additional tips for caring for your curly hair:

1. Use a Silk Pillowcase

Use a silk pillowcase to prevent breakage and reduce frizz.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling your hair as much as possible. This can cause damage and breakage.

3. Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

4. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protecting hair spray.

5. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

By following these tips and techniques, you can prevent frizz when blow drying your curly hair and promote healthy, beautiful curls.
