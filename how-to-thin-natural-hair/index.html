How To Thinning Natural Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have naturally thick hair that you wish to thin out? Thinning natural hair can be a challenge, especially if you're looking to maintain the length and health of your hair. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can thin out your natural hair without causing any damage or breakage. In this blog post, we'll show you how to thin your natural hair in a relaxed, step-by-step guide.

How to Thin Natural Hair

Thinning natural hair can be done in a variety of ways, from using thinning shears to razor cutting. However, it's important to note that thinning your hair too often or using the wrong tools can lead to damage and breakage. Here are some steps to follow to safely and effectively thin out your natural hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you begin thinning your natural hair, it's important to start with clean and conditioned hair. This will make it easier to comb and section your hair, and it will also ensure that your hair is healthy and strong enough to handle the thinning process.

Step 2: Comb and Section Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and conditioned, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Section your hair into four or more parts, depending on the thickness of your hair. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 3: Choose Your Thinning Tool

There are several tools that you can use to thin out your natural hair, including thinning shears, razor blades, and texturizing scissors. Choose a tool that you're comfortable using and that will give you the desired results.

Step 4: Thin Out Your Hair

Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. Take a small section of hair, and comb it straight. Use your thinning tool to cut into the section of hair, starting at the mid-lengths and working your way down to the ends. Make sure to only cut a few strands at a time, and avoid cutting too close to the scalp.

Repeat this process on each section of hair, making sure to blend the thinned sections with the un-thinned sections.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished thinning out your natural hair, style your hair as desired. You may notice that your hair feels lighter and has more movement than before.

Tips for Thinning Natural Hair

Thinning natural hair can be a tricky process, but there are several tips that you can follow to make the process easier and more effective:

  • Start with clean and conditioned hair
  • Use a tool that you're comfortable with
  • Only cut a few strands of hair at a time
  • Blend the thinned sections with the un-thinned sections
  • Use a light hand when thinning your hair

FAQs About Thinning Natural Hair

Can Thinning Natural Hair Cause Damage?

Thinning natural hair can cause damage if done too often or using the wrong tools. It's important to use a tool that is specifically designed for thinning hair, and to only thin out a few strands at a time.

How Often Should You Thin Out Your Natural Hair?

It's best to only thin out your natural hair once every few months, or as needed. Thinning your hair too often can lead to damage and breakage.

Can You Thin Out Natural Hair at Home?

Yes, you can thin out your natural hair at home using the right tools and techniques. However, if you're unsure about how to thin out your hair, it's best to visit a professional stylist.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Natural Hair

Thinning your natural hair can make it more prone to damage and breakage. Here are some hair care tips to follow to keep your thinned out hair healthy and strong:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair

By following these tips and techniques, you can safely and effectively thin out your natural hair without causing any damage or breakage. Remember to take your time and be gentle when thinning your hair, and always prioritize the health and strength of your hair.
