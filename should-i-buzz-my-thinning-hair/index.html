Should I Buzz My Thinning Hair

Written by Bardi Jul 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you experiencing thinning hair? Do you feel like your hair is becoming thinner and more difficult to style? If so, you may be wondering if buzzing your hair is the right choice for you. In this article, we will discuss the pros and cons of buzzing your hair and provide tips for maintaining healthy hair post-buzz.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Buzz Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Buzzing Guide
  • Buzzing Tips
  • FAQ
  • Post-Buzz Hair Care

Description

Buzzing your hair can be a liberating experience. It can make you feel powerful, confident and free. However, it is not the right choice for everyone. If you have thinning hair, buzzing your hair may be a good option for you. Buzzing your hair can help you regain control of your hair, and it can be the perfect solution for those who want a low maintenance hairstyle.

How to Buzz Your Hair

If you decide to buzz your hair, you can either do it yourself or go to a professional. If you want to do it yourself, you will need a set of clippers, a handheld mirror, and a steady hand. It is also important to choose the right clipper guard size. The size of the guard will determine how short your hair will be.

Step-by-Step Buzzing Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair.
2. Choose the right clipper guard size.
3. Begin at the back of your head and work your way up.
4. Move the clippers in an upward motion against the direction of hair growth.
5. Take your time, and be sure to go over each section of your head several times.
6. Pay special attention to the hairline around your ears and neck.
7. Use a handheld mirror to ensure that you haven't missed any spots.
8. Once finished, rinse your head with cool water to remove any loose hairs.
9. Pat your head dry with a towel.
10. Apply a moisturizer or oil to your scalp to prevent dryness.

Buzzing Tips

1. Start with a longer clipper guard size and work your way down if you want your hair shorter.
2. Be careful not to cut yourself with the clippers.
3. Use a handheld mirror to ensure that you haven't missed any spots.
4. Consider going to a professional if you're not confident in your ability to buzz your hair yourself.
5. Invest in a good pair of clippers to ensure that they last for years to come.

FAQ

Q: Will buzzing my hair make it grow back thicker?
A: No, buzzing your hair will not make it grow back thicker. It may appear thicker because the ends of the hair are blunt, but it will not change the thickness of your hair.

Q: How often should I buzz my hair?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows. Most people buzz their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Will buzzing my hair damage it?
A: No, buzzing your hair will not damage it. However, if you use the clippers incorrectly, you may cut your scalp.

Post-Buzz Hair Care

After buzzing your hair, it is important to take care of your scalp. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair post-buzz:

1. Moisturize your scalp regularly to prevent dryness.
2. Wear a hat or sunscreen in the sun to protect your scalp from damage.
3. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your scalp.
4. Massage your scalp regularly to promote healthy hair growth.
5. Consider using a hair growth serum or supplement to encourage hair growth.

In conclusion, buzzing your hair can be a great option for those with thinning hair. It is low maintenance and can help you regain control of your hair. However, it is important to weigh the pros and cons before making the decision to buzz your hair. If you do decide to buzz your hair, be sure to follow the step-by-step guide and take care of your scalp post-buzz.
