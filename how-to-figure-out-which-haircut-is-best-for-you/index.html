Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out which haircut is best for you is to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and long. To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back away from your face.

Take a close look at your face and determine which of the following descriptions best matches your features:

  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded hairline and jawline.
  • Round: Your face is roughly circular, with a wide hairline and full cheeks.
  • Square: Your face is roughly square, with a strong jawline and forehead.
  • Heart: Your face is widest at the temples and narrows at the jawline, with a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline.
  • Long: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a high forehead and narrow jawline.

Once youâve determined your face shape, you can start to look for haircuts that will flatter your features.

Considering Your Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut is your hair texture. Different hair textures require different haircuts to look their best.

If you have fine hair, you may want to consider a shorter haircut with layers to add volume and movement. If you have thick hair, a longer haircut with blunt ends can help to weigh down your hair and keep it under control.

If you have curly hair, you may want to consider a shorter haircut that will allow your curls to bounce and move freely. If you have straight hair, a longer haircut with layers can help to add volume and texture.

Personal Style and Lifestyle

Another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut is your personal style and lifestyle. Your haircut should not only flatter your features and hair texture, but it should also reflect your personal style and be easy to maintain.

If you have a busy lifestyle and donât have a lot of time to spend styling your hair, you may want to consider a shorter, low-maintenance haircut. If you prefer a more polished look, a longer, layered haircut may be a better fit for you.

  • Round: A layered haircut with volume at the crown can help to elongate your face and make it appear slimmer.
  • Square: Soft, layered haircuts or hairstyles with bangs can help to soften a square jawline.
  • Heart: A side-swept bang or chin-length bob can help to balance a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: A short, layered haircut or a pixie cut can help to flatter a diamond face shape.
  • Long: Long, layered haircuts or hairstyles with bangs can help to break up a long face shape.
