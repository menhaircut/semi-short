What Type Of Haircut Makes Hair Look Thicker

Written by Eveline Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have fine or thin hair, you know how frustrating it can be to achieve a voluminous, thick look. While hair products and styling techniques can certainly help, one of the easiest ways to make your hair appear thicker is by getting the right haircut. In this post, we'll explore what type of haircut makes hair look thicker and share some tips for achieving a fuller look.

How to Make Hair Look Thicker with a Haircut

The right haircut can make all the difference when it comes to creating the illusion of thicker hair. Here are some key factors to consider:

Layering

Layering is a great way to add volume and movement to fine hair. By cutting hair at different lengths, the hair appears fuller and more textured. Layers can be added throughout the hair or focused on specific areas, such as around the face or at the crown of the head.

Blunt Cuts

A blunt cut is another option for making hair appear thicker. This style involves cutting hair straight across, without any layers. The result is a full, heavy look that works particularly well for those with fine hair. Blunt cuts can be worn at any length, from a short bob to long, flowing locks.

Texturizing

Texturizing involves cutting into the hair to create movement and volume. This technique can be used in combination with layering or blunt cuts to create a tousled, messy look that gives the appearance of thicker hair. Texturizing can be done using scissors or a razor.

Step-by-step Guide to Getting a Haircut for Thicker Hair

Ready to get a haircut that makes your hair look thicker? Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Choose Your Cut

Consider the factors we discussed above, such as layering, blunt cuts, and texturizing, and choose a cut that will work best for your hair type and face shape.

Step 2: Find a Skilled Stylist

Look for a stylist who has experience working with fine or thin hair. A skilled stylist can help you choose the right cut and make adjustments to ensure that your hair looks as full as possible.

Step 3: Communicate Clearly

When you meet with your stylist, be sure to communicate your goals clearly. Let them know that you want to create the illusion of thicker hair and ask for their recommendations.

Step 4: Consider Color

Adding highlights or lowlights can also help create the illusion of thicker hair. Talk to your stylist about adding color to your hair to enhance the overall effect.

Step 5: Maintain Your Cut

Regular trims are important for maintaining a haircut that makes your hair look thicker. Be sure to visit your stylist every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking its best.

Tips for Maintaining Thicker Hair After a Haircut

Once you've gotten a haircut that makes your hair look thicker, there are a few things you can do to maintain the look:

  • Use volumizing hair products, such as mousse or texturizing spray, to enhance the fullness of your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume at the roots.
  • Avoid using heavy, oil-based hair products, which can weigh down fine hair.
  • Consider using a thickening shampoo and conditioner to help enhance the thickness of your hair.

FAQs

What's the best haircut for thin hair?

Layering, blunt cuts, and texturizing are all great options for making thin hair appear thicker. The best cut for you will depend on your hair type and face shape.

Can color make hair look thicker?

Yes, adding highlights or lowlights can help create the illusion of thicker hair. Talk to your stylist about adding color to enhance the overall effect.

What hair products can help make hair look thicker?

Volumizing hair products, such as mousse or texturizing spray, can help enhance the fullness of your hair. Thickening shampoos and conditioners can also be helpful.

Hair Care for Thicker Hair

Once you've achieved a haircut that makes your hair look thicker, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the look. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, which can damage hair and make it appear thinner.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid pulling or breaking strands.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in nutrients that promote hair health, such as protein, iron, and vitamin C.

By following these tips and getting a haircut that makes your hair look thicker, you can achieve the voluminous, full look you've always wanted.
