Why Does My Hair Shed So Much In The Shower

Written by Petter Aug 05, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of seeing clumps of hair in your shower drain? Shedding hair is a natural process, but excessive hair loss can be a sign of a problem. Many people experience hair shedding in the shower, but why does it happen? In this post, we will explore the reasons behind hair shedding in the shower and what you can do to prevent it.

Table of Contents:

  • Reasons Behind Hair Shedding In The Shower
  • How To Prevent Hair Shedding In The Shower
  • Step-by-Step Guide To Prevent Hair Shedding In The Shower
  • Tips To Reduce Hair Shedding In The Shower
  • FAQs About Hair Shedding In The Shower
  • Hair Care Tips To Prevent Hair Shedding

Reasons Behind Hair Shedding In The Shower:

There are several reasons why you may experience hair shedding in the shower, including:

  • Overwashing: Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dry, brittle hair that is more prone to breakage.
  • Chemical Treatments: Chemical treatments like hair dye and relaxers can weaken the hair and cause it to break off.
  • Poor Nutrition: A diet that is low in essential vitamins and minerals can lead to hair loss and shedding.
  • Stress: Stress can disrupt the hair growth cycle and cause more hairs to enter the shedding phase.
  • Hormonal Changes: Hormonal changes like pregnancy, menopause, and thyroid disorders can cause hair shedding.

It is important to identify the underlying cause of your hair shedding in order to address the problem effectively.

How To Prevent Hair Shedding In The Shower:

The good news is that there are several things you can do to prevent hair shedding in the shower:

  • Wash your hair less frequently: Try washing your hair every other day or every three days instead of daily.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Limit your use of chemical treatments like hair dye and relaxers.
  • Eat a balanced diet: Make sure you are getting enough vitamins and minerals in your diet, especially iron, biotin, and vitamin D.
  • Reduce stress: Practice stress-reducing techniques like meditation, yoga, or deep breathing.

Step-by-Step Guide To Prevent Hair Shedding In The Shower:

Follow these steps to prevent hair shedding in the shower:

  1. Wet your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  2. Apply a small amount of shampoo to your scalp and massage gently with your fingertips.
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  4. Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair and work it upward, avoiding the scalp.
  5. Let the conditioner sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out with warm water.
  6. Gently squeeze excess water out of your hair with a towel.
  7. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause breakage.
  8. Comb your hair with a wide-toothed comb, starting at the ends and working your way up.
  9. Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and flat irons, as they can damage your hair.
  10. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to reduce heat damage.

Tips To Reduce Hair Shedding In The Shower:

Here are some additional tips to reduce hair shedding in the shower:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can cause tension on the hair and lead to breakage.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase: These materials are less abrasive than cotton and can help prevent hair breakage while you sleep.
  • Be gentle with your hair: Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it is wet, as it is more prone to breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun: Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from UV damage.

FAQs About Hair Shedding In The Shower:

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair shedding in the shower:

  • Is it normal to lose hair in the shower? Yes, it is normal to lose some hair in the shower. The average person loses 50-100 hairs per day.
  • How much hair shedding is too much? If you notice clumps of hair in your shower drain or on your brush, you may be experiencing excessive hair shedding.
  • Can hair shedding in the shower be reversed? Yes, hair shedding in the shower can be reversed by identifying the underlying cause and taking steps to address it.

Hair Care Tips To Prevent Hair Shedding:

Here are some additional hair care tips to prevent hair shedding:

  • Use a gentle shampoo: Choose a shampoo that is free from harsh chemicals and sulfates.
  • Avoid heat styling: If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray or serum to minimize damage.
  • Get regular trims: Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.
  • Take supplements: Consider taking supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and iron to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can reduce hair shedding in the shower and promote healthy, strong locks.
