Should You Cut Hair Around Dogs Eyes

Written by Luffy Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a dog owner, you may have wondered whether you should cut the hair around your dog's eyes. This is a common question that many pet parents ask, and it's understandable why. After all, long hair around the eyes can cause irritation, infections, and even vision problems. In this article, we'll explore whether or not you should cut hair around dogs eyes and provide tips on how to do it safely.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Hair Around Dogs Eyes
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Before we dive into whether or not you should cut hair around dogs eyes, let's first understand why this is even a concern. Long hair around the eyes can cause irritation and discomfort. It can also lead to eye infections, as the hair can trap dirt and debris that can cause irritation.

In addition, long hair around the eyes can obstruct vision, making it difficult for your dog to see properly. This can be dangerous, especially if your dog is active or likes to play outside.

How to Cut Hair Around Dogs Eyes

If you've decided that cutting your dog's hair around the eyes is necessary, there are a few things to keep in mind:

1. Use the Right Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a pair of scissors specifically designed for pet grooming, as well as a comb to help separate the hair.

2. Make Sure Your Dog is Calm and Comfortable

It's important to make sure your dog is calm and comfortable before you start cutting. You may want to have someone hold your dog or distract them with treats or toys.

3. Be Careful When Cutting

When cutting the hair around your dog's eyes, be careful not to get too close to the eye itself. Use small, gentle snips and work slowly to avoid accidentally cutting your dog's skin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting hair around your dog's eyes:

Step 1: Brush Your Dog's Hair

Before you start cutting, brush your dog's hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Separate the Hair

Using a comb, separate the hair around your dog's eyes so that it's easier to cut.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Using your pet grooming scissors, start cutting the hair. Work slowly and carefully, making sure not to get too close to your dog's eye.

Step 4: Check Your Progress

Periodically check your progress to make sure you're getting the desired look. You can always trim more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.

Step 5: Reward Your Dog

Once you're finished, reward your dog with a treat or some extra love and attention.

Tips for Cutting Hair Around Dogs Eyes

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting hair around your dog's eyes:

1. Use Scissors Specifically Designed for Pet Grooming

Using scissors that are specifically designed for pet grooming will make the process easier and safer for both you and your dog.

2. Work Slowly and Carefully

Take your time when cutting your dog's hair, and work slowly and carefully to avoid accidentally cutting your dog's skin.

3. Don't Get Too Close to the Eye

When cutting hair around your dog's eyes, be careful not to get too close to the eye itself. This can cause injury or discomfort for your dog.

4. Reward Your Dog

After the grooming session, reward your dog with treats, toys, or extra love and attention. This will help make the experience more positive for them.

FAQs

Should I Cut Hair Around My Dog's Eyes?

If your dog has long hair around their eyes, it may be necessary to trim it to avoid irritation, infections, and vision problems. However, if you're not comfortable doing it yourself, it's best to take your dog to a professional groomer.

What Happens if I Accidentally Cut My Dog's Skin?

If you accidentally cut your dog's skin while trimming their hair, clean the area with antiseptic and apply pressure to stop any bleeding. If the cut is deep or doesn't stop bleeding, take your dog to the vet.

Dog Hair Care

In addition to trimming hair around the eyes, it's important to take good care of your dog's coat. Regular brushing, bathing, and grooming can help keep your dog's hair healthy and shiny. You may also want to talk to your vet about any specific hair care needs your dog may have based on their breed or coat type.

In conclusion, cutting hair around dogs eyes can be necessary for your pet's health and comfort. Use the right tools, work slowly and carefully, and reward your dog for their patience. If you're not comfortable doing it yourself, it's best to take your dog to a professional groomer.
