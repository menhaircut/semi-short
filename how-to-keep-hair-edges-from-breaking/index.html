How To Keep Hair Edges From Breaking Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Sep 02, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with damaged or broken hair edges? Whether you have natural hair or relaxed hair, keeping your edges smooth and healthy can be a challenge. But don't worry, we've got some tips and tricks to help you keep your hair edges from breaking. Read on to learn more.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Keep Hair Edges from Breaking
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Healthy Hair Edges
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Products for Healthy Edges

Description

Hair edges are the delicate hairs that grow around the perimeter of your hairline. They are often the first to break or become damaged due to styling, chemicals, or genetics. This can lead to hair loss or thinning around the hairline, which can be frustrating and challenging to repair. In this article, we'll explore how to keep hair edges from breaking and promote healthy growth.

How to Keep Hair Edges from Breaking

There are several ways to keep your hair edges from breaking. The first step is to avoid harsh chemicals and excessive heat styling. Chemical relaxers, hair dyes, and hot tools can weaken the hair and cause breakage. It's also important to maintain a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth. In addition, you can try the following:

1. Protective Styling

Protective styling involves wearing your hair in styles that protect the ends and edges from damage. This can include braids, twists, buns, or updos. Protective styles can help prevent breakage and promote healthy hair growth.

2. Moisturize

Dry hair is more prone to breakage, so it's important to keep your hair moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing oil to keep your edges hydrated and healthy. You can also try a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

3. Be Gentle

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, and be gentle when styling or brushing your edges. Avoid tight hairstyles that can put stress on your hairline.

4. Use Satin or Silk Scarf

Sleeping with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet can help protect your hair from breakage while you sleep. These materials are smoother and less likely to cause friction and breakage than cotton or other fabrics.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to keep your hair edges from breaking:

  1. Avoid harsh chemicals and excessive heat styling.
  2. Protect your hair with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet while you sleep.
  3. Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or moisturizing oil.
  4. Be gentle when detangling, styling, or brushing your edges.
  5. Try protective styling to prevent breakage.

Tips for Healthy Hair Edges

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy hair edges:

  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water.
  • Don't over-manipulate your hair or wear tight hairstyles that can cause stress on your edges.
  • Avoid wearing hats or headbands that rub against your hairline.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQs

Q. How often should I moisturize my edges?

A. It's best to moisturize your edges daily or as needed to keep them hydrated and healthy.

Q. Can I use heat on my edges?

A. It's best to avoid using heat styling tools on your edges, as this can cause damage and breakage. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Q. How do I know if my edges are damaged?

A. Signs of damaged edges include thinning hair, hair loss, and breakage. If you notice any of these signs, it's important to take steps to repair and protect your hair.

Hair Care Products for Healthy Edges

Here are some hair care products that can help promote healthy edges:

  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Moisturizing oil
  • Deep conditioning treatment
  • Satin or silk scarf or bonnet
  • Wide-tooth comb

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair edges healthy and prevent breakage. Remember to be gentle, moisturize regularly, and protect your hair from harsh chemicals and excessive heat styling. With a little care and attention, you can maintain healthy and beautiful hair edges.
