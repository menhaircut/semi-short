How To Cut Wavy Curly Hair At Home

Are you tired of spending hundreds of dollars on haircuts every year? Do you have wavy curly hair that seems impossible to style or cut yourself? Fear not, because with a few simple tips and tricks, you can learn how to cut wavy curly hair at home!

Description

Wavy curly hair can be tricky to cut, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a beautiful and personalized haircut from the comfort of your own home. This guide will walk you through the steps to cut wavy curly hair at home, and provide tips and tricks for achieving the perfect style.

How to Cut Wavy Curly Hair at Home

The key to cutting wavy curly hair is to work with the natural texture and shape of your hair. It's important to use sharp scissors and to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in a shorter cut than intended. Follow these steps to cut your wavy curly hair at home:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water or a leave-in conditioner

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way, leaving one section at a time to work on.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut the ends, trimming as little as possible to avoid removing too much length. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you have finished the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Release the next section of hair and comb it straight. Use the bottom layer as a guide and cut the top layer to match the length. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you have finished the top layer.

Step 5: Cut the Bangs (Optional)

If you have bangs, release them from the front sections of hair and trim them to the desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have finished cutting all of your hair, use your scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut into the ends to create a natural, blended look.

Tips for Cutting Wavy Curly Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair and trim as little as possible to avoid removing too much length.
  • Use your fingers to twist and shape your curls as you cut to ensure that you are working with the natural texture of your hair.
  • Consider cutting your hair in layers to add volume and definition to your curls.
  • Take breaks as needed to rest your arms and avoid fatigue.

FAQs

Can I cut my wavy curly hair when it's wet?

It's best to cut wavy curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in a shorter cut than intended.

Do I need special scissors to cut wavy curly hair?

Sharp scissors are important for any haircut, but you don't need special scissors specifically for wavy curly hair.

How often should I cut my wavy curly hair?

It's recommended to cut wavy curly hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy ends and promote hair growth.

Hair Care for Wavy Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful wavy curly hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl-defining cream to wet hair to enhance your natural curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently cut your wavy curly hair at home and achieve the perfect style for you. Remember to work with the natural texture of your hair and take your time to avoid making any drastic mistakes. Happy cutting!
