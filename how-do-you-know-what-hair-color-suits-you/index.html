How Do You Know What Hair Color Suits You

Written by Frank Aug 04, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your current hair color and want to switch things up? Choosing a new hair color can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure what shade will suit you best. But don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we'll give you tips on how to determine what hair color will suit you and provide you with some hair care tips to keep your new color looking vibrant and healthy.

  • How to Determine What Hair Color Suits You
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hair Color

How to Determine What Hair Color Suits You

When it comes to choosing the right hair color, there are a few factors you should consider. Here are some tips to help you determine the best hair color for you:

Skin Tone

Your skin tone is one of the most important factors to consider when choosing a hair color. If you have warm undertones, you should opt for warm hair colors like golden blonde, copper, or auburn. If you have cool undertones, you should choose cool hair colors like ash blonde, platinum blonde, or burgundy.

Natural Hair Color

Your natural hair color can also play a role in determining what hair color will suit you best. If you have a warm natural hair color like brown or red, warm hair colors will likely suit you best. If you have a cool natural hair color like black or ash brown, cool hair colors will look best on you.

Eye Color

Your eye color can also impact what hair color will suit you best. If you have blue or green eyes, warm hair colors like golden blonde or copper will make your eyes pop. If you have brown eyes, cool hair colors like ash brown or platinum blonde will look great on you.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing the Right Hair Color

Now that you know what factors to consider when choosing a hair color, here is a step-by-step guide to help you make the right choice:

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

There are a few ways to determine your skin tone. One way is to look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If your veins appear blue, you have cool undertones. If your veins appear green, you have warm undertones. If you can't tell whether your veins are blue or green, you likely have neutral undertones.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Color

Think about the color of your eyes and whether they are warm or cool. This will help you determine whether you should choose a warm or cool hair color.

Step 3: Look at Your Natural Hair Color

If you want a subtle change, choose a hair color that is close to your natural hair color. If you want a more dramatic change, choose a color that is several shades lighter or darker than your natural hair color.

Step 4: Consult with a Professional

If you're still unsure what hair color will suit you best, it's always a good idea to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help you determine the best color for you based on your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Hair Color

Once you've chosen your new hair color, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking vibrant and healthy. Here are some tips to help you maintain your new hair color:

Use Color-Safe Shampoo and Conditioner

Regular shampoo and conditioner can strip your hair of its color, so it's important to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help preserve your new hair color.

Avoid Hot Water

Hot water can also strip your hair of its color, so it's best to wash your hair in lukewarm water. If you can't stand cold showers, try rinsing your hair with cold water after washing it to help seal the cuticle and lock in the color.

Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners can also damage your hair and cause your color to fade. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Avoid Chlorine

If you're a regular swimmer, chlorine can also damage your hair and cause your color to fade. To avoid this, wear a swim cap or rinse your hair with clean water before and after swimming.

FAQs

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how noticeable your roots are. On average, most people touch up their roots every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair at home, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully and do a patch test first to avoid any allergic reactions.

Q: What should I do if I don't like my new hair color?

A: If you're not happy with your new hair color, don't panic! You can always go back to your natural hair color or try a different shade. It's best to wait a few weeks before making any drastic changes to give your hair time to recover from the dyeing process.


Choosing a new hair color can be a fun and exciting experience. By considering your skin tone, natural hair color, and eye color, you can determine what hair color will suit you best. Remember to take good care of your new hair color by using color-safe products and avoiding heat styling tools and chlorine. And if you're ever unsure, don't hesitate to consult with a professional hairstylist. Happy coloring!
