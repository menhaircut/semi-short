How To Cut My Own Hair Taper Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you want to learn how to cut your own hair taper fade, you've come to the right place. This step-by-step guide will teach you how to achieve an on-trend taper fade hairstyle from the comfort of your own home. We'll cover everything from the equipment you'll need to the technique you should use, so let's get started.

Equipment You'll Need

Before we get started, you'll want to make sure you have all the necessary equipment. Here's what you'll need:

  • Electric hair clippers with an adjustable blade
  • A comb
  • A pair of scissors
  • A handheld mirror or a wall-mounted mirror
  • A towel or cape to catch hair clippings
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in before you begin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all your equipment, let's jump into the steps for cutting your own hair taper fade:

Step 1: Determine the Length

Decide how long you want your hair to be on top. If you're not sure, start with a longer guard on your clippers and work your way down until you find the length you like.

Step 2: Set Your Clippers

Attach your chosen guard to your clippers and turn them on. Make sure the blade is adjusted to the shortest setting for the closest cut.

Step 3: Start at the Bottom

Begin cutting your hair at the nape of your neck, using the clippers to create a straight line. Move up the back of your head towards the crown, using the mirror to check your progress.

Step 4: Blend the Sides

Using the same guard, move to the sides of your head and use the clippers to create a gradient effect. Start with a longer setting at the bottom and work your way up to the shorter setting near the top.

Step 5: Create the Fade

Switch to a shorter guard and use the clippers to blend the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides. Use a flicking motion with your wrist to create a seamless transition.

Step 6: Define the Hairline

Use your clippers or scissors to define your hairline around your forehead and neck. Take your time and make sure the lines are straight and even.

Step 7: Taper the Sideburns

Taper your sideburns by starting with a longer setting and gradually working your way down to a shorter one. Use the comb and scissors to blend the hair into the rest of your hair.

Step 8: Clean Up the Edges

Use your clippers or scissors to clean up any stray hairs around your ears and the back of your neck.

Step 9: Check Your Work

Use the mirror to check your work from all angles. Make any necessary adjustments until you're happy with the results.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Apply a small amount of product to your hair and style it as desired.

Tips for Success

Here are a few tips to help you achieve the best possible results:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting your hair too short.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, but practice makes perfect.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take small, gradual steps than to make a big mistake.
  • Watch tutorial videos online for more guidance.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair taper fade if I've never done it before?

Yes, you can! Just be patient and follow the steps carefully. It may take a few tries to get the hang of it, but you'll get there.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always fix it by cutting the rest of your hair shorter to match or by blending the mistake into the rest of your hair.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it?

It's best to cut clean, dry hair. This will give you a more accurate idea of how your hair will look after the cut.

Hair Care After the Cut

Now that you've successfully cut your own hair taper fade, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a hairbrush or comb to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can make your hair look greasy.
  • Visit a professional barber every few months for a trim and to keep your hairstyle looking fresh.

Cutting your own hair taper fade may seem daunting, but with the right equipment and a bit of practice, you can achieve great results. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow the steps carefully. Good luck!
