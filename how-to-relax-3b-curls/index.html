How To Relax 3B Curls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have 3B curls, you know how challenging it can be to maintain them. While they are beautiful and unique, they can also require a lot of effort and attention. If you're looking for a way to relax your 3B curls, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll provide you with a step-by-step process to help you relax your curls while keeping them healthy and moisturized.

Description

3B curls are tight, spiral curls that are prone to frizz and dryness. They are known for their volume and bounce, but they can also be difficult to manage. Relaxing your curls can help to loosen them and make them easier to style. However, it's important to do it properly to avoid damaging your hair.

How to Relax 3B Curls

Step 1: Determine Your Hair's Porosity

Before you begin the relaxing process, it's important to determine your hair's porosity. This will help you choose the right products and techniques for your hair type. You can do a simple porosity test by placing a strand of hair in a glass of water. If it sinks to the bottom quickly, your hair has high porosity. If it floats for a while before sinking, your hair has low porosity.

Step 2: Choose a Relaxer

There are two types of relaxers: lye and no-lye. Lye relaxers are stronger and can be more damaging to your hair, while no-lye relaxers are gentler and better for sensitive scalps. Choose a relaxer that is specifically formulated for your hair type and porosity.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair

Before applying the relaxer, you'll need to prepare your hair. This involves washing and conditioning your hair to remove any dirt, oil, and product buildup. Be sure to detangle your hair carefully with a wide-tooth comb to avoid breakage.

Step 4: Apply the Relaxer

Follow the instructions on the relaxer package carefully. Apply the relaxer to your hair in small sections, starting at the back and working your way to the front. Be sure to wear gloves to avoid getting the relaxer on your skin. Leave the relaxer on your hair for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out thoroughly with warm water.

Step 5: Neutralize Your Hair

After rinsing out the relaxer, you'll need to neutralize your hair to stop the chemical process. Apply the neutralizing shampoo that came with your relaxer kit to your hair, working it into a lather. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 6: Deep Condition Your Hair

Relaxing your hair can be drying, so it's important to deep condition your hair to restore moisture. Apply a deep conditioner to your hair and leave it on for the recommended amount of time. Rinse it out thoroughly with warm water.

Tips for Relaxing 3B Curls

1. Don't relax your hair too often. Relaxing your hair too frequently can cause damage and breakage.

2. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.

3. Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as they can dry it out and cause damage.

4. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Q: Can I relax my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can relax your hair at home. However, it's important to follow the instructions carefully and use the right products for your hair type.

Q: How often should I relax my hair?

A: It's best to wait at least 8-10 weeks between relaxer treatments to avoid damage and breakage.

Hair Care for Relaxed 3B Curls

After relaxing your hair, it's important to care for it properly to keep it healthy and moisturized. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Deep condition your hair regularly to restore moisture, and avoid using heat tools to style your hair. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends, and protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions.
