How To Know What Haircut Suits You Male

Are you looking for a new haircut but not sure what style to choose? It can be overwhelming trying to decide on a haircut that suits your face shape, hair type, and personal style. In this article, we'll give you some tips on how to know what haircut suits you male.

  • Understanding Your Hair Type
  • Finding Your Personal Style

Determining Your Face Shape

Before you choose a haircut, it's important to determine your face shape. The most common face shapes are oval, round, square, and heart-shaped. Each face shape has a haircut that suits it best.

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Look at the shape of your face and compare it to the following descriptions:

  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a rounded jawline and forehead.
  • Round: Your face is roughly the same width and length, with a round chin and full cheeks.
  • Square: Your face is roughly the same width and length, with a strong jawline and forehead.
  • Heart-shaped: Your face is wider at the forehead and temples, with a pointed chin.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can choose a haircut that complements your features.

Understanding Your Hair Type

Another important factor to consider when choosing a haircut is your hair type. There are several different hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each hair type requires different care and styling techniques.

If you have straight hair, you may want a haircut that adds volume and texture. If you have wavy or curly hair, you may want a haircut that enhances your natural texture. And if you have coily hair, you may want a haircut that reduces frizz and defines your curls.

Talk to your hairstylist about your hair type and ask for recommendations on haircuts that will work well with your texture.

Finding Your Personal Style

In addition to your face shape and hair type, your personal style is also an important factor to consider when choosing a haircut. Do you prefer a classic, timeless look? Or are you more adventurous and willing to try something new?

Think about your wardrobe and the types of clothes you like to wear. Do you prefer a more casual or formal style? Your haircut should reflect your personal style and complement your overall look.

Haircut Ideas for Different Face Shapes and Hair Types

Now that you've determined your face shape, hair type, and personal style, it's time to choose a haircut. Here are some haircut ideas for different face shapes and hair types:

  • Oval face: A textured crop, slicked-back undercut, or side-parted quiff.
  • Round face: A short back and sides, textured fringe, or messy quiff.
  • Square face: A crew cut, buzz cut, or textured crop.
  • Heart-shaped face: A side-swept fringe, textured crop, or messy quiff.
  • Straight hair: A textured crop, slicked-back undercut, or side-parted quiff.
  • Wavy hair: A messy fringe, layered cut, or textured crop.
  • Curly hair: A curly undercut, textured crop, or side-swept fringe.
  • Coily hair: A short afro, curly undercut, or textured crop.

Remember, these are just suggestions. Talk to your hairstylist about what haircut will work best for your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Hair Care Tips to Maintain Your New Haircut

Once you've chosen a new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain your new look. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools like hair dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a styling product like pomade or wax to keep your hair in place and add texture.
  • Visit your hairstylist regularly for trims and touch-ups.

Frequently Asked Questions

What haircut suits a receding hairline?

A short back and sides, textured crop, or buzz cut can be good options for a receding hairline. Talk to your hairstylist about what haircut will work best for your hairline.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. On average, most men get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Can I bring a photo of a haircut I like to my hairstylist?

Yes, bringing a photo of a haircut you like can be helpful for your hairstylist to understand the look you're going for.

What haircut suits a round face?

A short back and sides, textured fringe, or messy quiff can be good options for a round face. Talk to your hairstylist about what haircut will work best for your face shape.

What haircut suits curly hair?

A curly undercut, textured crop, or side-swept fringe can be good options for curly hair. Talk to your hairstylist about what haircut will work best for your hair type.

Choosing a new haircut can be a fun and exciting experience. By considering your face shape, hair type, and personal style, you can choose a haircut that suits you best. Talk to your hairstylist for recommendations and hair care tips to maintain your new look.
