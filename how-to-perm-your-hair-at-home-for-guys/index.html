How To Perm Your Hair At Home For Guys

Written by Aprilia Sep 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Perming your hair at home can be a cost-effective and easy way to achieve curly locks. While most people associate perms with women, men can also rock a curly look. In this article, we will show you how to perm your hair at home for guys in relaxed English language. We will cover everything from how to prep your hair to how to care for it after the perm.

Description

A perm is a chemical treatment that adds curls or waves to your hair. Perms work by breaking down the bonds in your hair and then reforming them in a new shape. The process can take a few hours, but the results can last for several months. Perming your hair at home can be a cost-effective alternative to going to a salon, but it does require some preparation and caution.

How to Perm Your Hair at Home for Guys

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before perming your hair, it is essential to wash it thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any buildup or residue from your hair, which can interfere with the perm solution. Next, towel-dry your hair and comb it out to remove any tangles. It is also a good idea to apply a protective cream to your scalp to prevent any burning or irritation.

Step 2: Wrap Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections and wrap each section tightly around perm rods. The size of the perm rods will determine the size of your curls. It is best to use smaller rods for tighter curls and larger rods for looser waves. Be sure to wrap your hair securely around the rods to ensure an even curl pattern.

Step 3: Apply the Perm Solution

Once your hair is wrapped, it is time to apply the perm solution. Follow the instructions on the perm kit carefully, as the process can vary depending on the brand. Use a comb to apply the solution evenly to each rod, making sure to saturate each strand of hair. Be sure to wear gloves and avoid getting the solution on your skin or eyes.

Step 4: Process and Rinse

After applying the perm solution, cover your hair with a plastic cap and let it process for the recommended time. This can take anywhere from 10 to 30 minutes, depending on the strength of the solution and the desired curl pattern. Once the processing time is up, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water to remove the perm solution.

Step 5: Neutralize Your Hair

After rinsing, it is time to neutralize your hair. This step is critical, as it stops the perming process and locks in the new curl pattern. Apply the neutralizer solution to each rod, making sure to saturate each strand of hair. Let the neutralizer sit for the recommended time, then rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 6: Remove the Rods and Style

Once your hair is rinsed, it is time to remove the perm rods. Gently unwind each rod, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Do not pull or tug on your hair, as this can cause breakage or damage. Once all the rods are removed, style your hair as desired. You can use a diffuser to enhance your curls or a hair straightener to create a looser wave pattern.

Tips

  • Choose a perm kit that is specifically designed for men's hair.
  • Do a patch test before applying the perm solution to your entire head to avoid any allergic reactions or irritation.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 48 hours after perming to allow the curl pattern to set.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools or harsh chemicals on your permed hair, as this can cause damage.

FAQ

Can I perm my hair if it is already colored or highlighted?

Yes, but it is best to wait at least two weeks after coloring or highlighting your hair before perming it. This will allow your hair to recover and prevent damage.

How often should I perm my hair?

It is best to wait at least three months between perms to avoid damaging your hair. Perming your hair too frequently can cause breakage and dryness.

Can I perm my hair if it is already relaxed?

No, perming relaxed hair can cause severe damage and breakage. It is best to choose one chemical treatment or the other, but not both.

Hair Care

After perming your hair, it is essential to take good care of it to maintain its health and vitality. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for permed hair, and avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when it is wet, as this can cause breakage. Finally, consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair soft and moisturized.

Now that you know how to perm your hair at home for guys, you can achieve a curly look that is both stylish and affordable. Just remember to follow the instructions carefully, take good care of your hair, and enjoy your new look!
