How To Hide Bald Spot On Top Of Head Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you are experiencing hair loss and have a bald spot on the top of your head, you may be wondering how to hide it. The good news is that there are several ways to do so, without resorting to costly and invasive procedures. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to cover up your bald spot and restore your confidence.

Description

A bald spot on the top of your head can be caused by various factors such as genetics, aging, stress, or medical conditions. Regardless of the cause, it can be distressing to deal with hair loss, and it may affect your self-esteem. However, there are ways to conceal your bald spot and feel comfortable in your skin again.

How to Hide Bald Spot on Top of Head

1. Hairstyles

One of the easiest and most effective ways to hide a bald spot on the top of your head is to choose a suitable hairstyle. You can opt for a shorter haircut that incorporates layers or textured styles that add volume and depth to your hair. Avoid combing your hair straight back, as it can accentuate the balding area. Instead, consider a side part or a fringe that can cover the affected spot.

2. Hairpieces

If you want a quick fix for your bald spot, consider using a hairpiece. Hairpieces are available in various shapes, sizes, and materials, and can be attached to your hair using clips or adhesives. You can choose a hairpiece that matches your hair color and texture, and style it as you would your natural hair. However, keep in mind that hairpieces require maintenance and may need to be replaced periodically.

3. Hair Fibers

Hair fibers are another option to conceal a bald spot. They are made of keratin protein, the same material as your hair, and can be sprinkled on the affected area to create the illusion of thicker hair. Hair fibers adhere to your hair using static electricity and can last until your next shampoo. They come in various shades and can be blended to match your hair color.

4. Scalp Micropigmentation

Scalp micropigmentation is a non-invasive cosmetic procedure that involves tattooing tiny dots on your scalp to simulate hair follicles. This technique can create the appearance of a shaved head or a buzz cut and can be customized to match your skin tone and hair color. Scalp micropigmentation is a long-term solution that can last up to five years, but it can be costly and requires maintenance.

Step by Step

1. Choose a suitable hairstyle

Consult with your hairstylist to select a haircut that complements your face shape and covers your bald spot. Consider adding layers or texture to create volume and fullness. Avoid slicked-back hairstyles that expose the affected area.

2. Use hair fibers

Apply hair fibers to your hair using the applicator provided. Distribute evenly and blend with your fingers or a brush. Use a hair spray to set the fibers in place.

3. Try a hairpiece

Select a hairpiece that matches your hair color and texture. Place it on your head and secure it with clips or adhesive. Style it as you would your natural hair.

4. Consider scalp micropigmentation

Research reputable scalp micropigmentation clinics in your area. Schedule a consultation to discuss your options and expectations. Choose a color and density that matches your skin tone and hair color.

Tips

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools that can damage your hair.
  • Wear a hat or a headscarf to protect your scalp from the sun and wind.
  • Practice good hair care habits such as regular washing, conditioning, and brushing.

FAQ

1. Is scalp micropigmentation painful?

No, scalp micropigmentation is not painful. The procedure is done using a local anesthetic, and most people describe it as a mild discomfort.

2. Can hair fibers cause hair loss?

No, hair fibers do not cause hair loss. They are made of natural ingredients and do not interfere with your hair growth cycle.

3. How long does a hairpiece last?

A hairpiece can last up to six months, depending on the quality and maintenance. It may need to be replaced when it starts to show signs of wear and tear.

Hair Care

Regardless of the method you choose to hide your bald spot, it is essential to take care of your hair and scalp. Use gentle and nourishing hair products that promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals that support healthy hair growth. Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, as they can weaken your hair and scalp. Consult with a dermatologist or a hair specialist if you experience severe hair loss or scalp conditions.
