My Short Haired Dog Sheds A Lot How To Deal With Excessive Shedding

Written by Frank Sep 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're a pet owner, you know that shedding is one of the most common problems faced by dog owners. While all dogs shed, some breeds shed more than others. My short haired dog sheds a lot and it can be frustrating to deal with the constant shedding. But don't worry, there are several things you can do to manage your dog's shedding and keep your home clean and free of dog hair.

Description

Short haired dogs are known for their sleek and shiny coats. They are easy to groom and require minimal maintenance. However, they can also shed a lot, especially during the shedding season which can be twice a year. The shedding season can last for several weeks and during this time, your dog's shedding can be excessive. This can be a problem as dog hair can get everywhere, from your clothes, furniture, and carpets.

How to deal with excessive shedding

If you're wondering how to deal with your short haired dog's excessive shedding, here are some tips that you can follow:

1. Brush your dog regularly

Regular brushing can help remove loose hair from your dog's coat before it falls out. This can reduce the amount of hair that ends up on your furniture and carpets. Use a brush that is suitable for your dog's coat type to avoid damaging their coat or skin. You can also use a deshedding tool to remove loose hair effectively.

2. Bathe your dog regularly

Bathing your dog can help remove loose hair, dirt, and debris from their coat. Use a shampoo that is gentle and suitable for your dog's skin. Avoid over-bathing your dog as it can dry out their skin and cause more shedding.

3. Feed your dog a healthy diet

A healthy diet can help improve your dog's coat health and reduce shedding. Feed your dog with high-quality dog food that contains essential nutrients such as protein, vitamins, and minerals.

4. Keep your home clean

Regularly vacuum and dust your home to remove dog hair and dander. Use a vacuum cleaner with a HEPA filter to trap allergens and prevent them from circulating in the air. Wash your dog's bedding and toys regularly to keep them clean and free of hair.

Step by step guide to managing your dog's shedding

Here's a step by step guide to managing your short haired dog's shedding:

Step 1: Brush your dog's coat

Use a brush that is suitable for your dog's coat type and brush your dog regularly to remove loose hair.

Step 2: Bathe your dog

Bathe your dog with a gentle shampoo to remove loose hair, dirt, and debris from their coat.

Step 3: Feed your dog a healthy diet

Feed your dog with high-quality dog food that contains essential nutrients such as protein, vitamins, and minerals.

Step 4: Keep your home clean

Regularly vacuum and dust your home to remove dog hair and dander. Wash your dog's bedding and toys regularly to keep them clean and free of hair.

Tips to manage your dog's shedding

Here are some additional tips that can help you manage your dog's shedding:

1. Use a lint roller

Use a lint roller to remove dog hair from your clothes and furniture.

2. Cover your furniture

Use furniture covers to protect your furniture from dog hair and dander.

3. Use air purifiers

Use air purifiers to remove allergens and pet hair from the air.

FAQs

1. Why do short haired dogs shed a lot?

Short haired dogs shed a lot because they have a shorter hair cycle compared to long haired dogs. This means that their hair falls out more frequently, resulting in more shedding.

2. Is excessive shedding normal for short haired dogs?

Yes, excessive shedding is normal for short haired dogs, especially during the shedding season.

3. Can I stop my short haired dog from shedding?

No, you cannot stop your short haired dog from shedding completely. However, you can manage their shedding and reduce the amount of hair that ends up on your furniture and carpets.

Hair care for your short haired dog

Here are some tips for taking care of your short haired dog's coat:

1. Brush your dog regularly

Regular brushing can help remove loose hair and prevent matting.

2. Bathe your dog when necessary

Bathe your dog when they start to smell or their coat looks dirty.

3. Use a high-quality dog shampoo

Use a shampoo that is gentle and suitable for your dog's skin.

4. Dry your dog's coat thoroughly

Make sure to dry your dog's coat thoroughly to prevent skin irritation and matting.

5. Trim your dog's nails regularly

Trim your dog's nails regularly to prevent them from scratching their skin and causing irritation.

Overall, managing your short haired dog's shedding can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can keep your home clean and your dog healthy and happy. Remember to brush your dog regularly, bathe them when necessary, feed them a healthy diet, and keep your home clean.
