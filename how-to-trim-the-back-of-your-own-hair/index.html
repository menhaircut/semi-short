How To Trim The Back Of Your Own Hair A Beginner s Guide

Written by Bardi Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Trimming your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back of your head. But with the right tools and techniques, it's completely doable. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to trimming the back of your own hair, including tips and tricks to get the best results. Read on to learn more!

Description

Have you ever tried to trim the back of your own hair, only to end up with a choppy, uneven mess? Don't worry, you're not alone. Trimming the back of your head can be tricky, but it's not impossible. With a little practice and some helpful tips, you can achieve a clean, even cut that looks just like you went to the salon.

How to Trim the Back of Your Own Hair

Before you get started, you'll need a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Mirror (preferably a large one)

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to get started. Here are the steps to trimming the back of your own hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you get a better idea of how long your hair is and where it needs to be trimmed. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start trimming.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section off the hair at the back of your head. You can do this by creating a horizontal parting from ear to ear, or by creating two vertical parts on either side of your head. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Starting with the bottom section of hair, comb it straight down and trim the ends. Make sure to cut straight across and use small, precise cuts. Repeat this process with the other sections of hair.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

Once you've trimmed all the sections, it's time to check for evenness. Use your comb to create a center part and check that both sides are even. If one side is longer than the other, go back and make small adjustments until it's even.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

If you're using clippers, you can use them to blend the sides of your hair into the back. Start with a longer guard and work your way down until you reach your desired length. If you're not using clippers, you can use scissors to blend the sides into the back by making small, angled cuts.

Step 6: Clean up Around the Ears

Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs around your ears. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Once you've finished trimming the back of your hair, check your work in the mirror. Make any final adjustments as needed.

Tips for Trimming the Back of Your Own Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the best results:

  • Use sharp scissors to get a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to make small adjustments than to cut off too much.
  • Use a mirror that gives you a clear view of the back of your head.
  • If you're using clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to avoid cutting too much.
  • Take your time and be patient. Trimming your own hair can be time-consuming, but it's worth it for a great result.

FAQ

Can I trim my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can trim your own curly hair. Just be sure to comb it straight before trimming and make small adjustments to avoid cutting off too much.

Do I need to use clippers to trim the back of my hair?

No, you can use scissors if you prefer. Just make sure they're sharp and use small, precise cuts.

How often should I trim the back of my hair?

This will depend on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want it to be. As a general rule, it's a good idea to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking healthy and neat.

Hair Care

Trimming the back of your own hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. To keep your hair looking its best, be sure to:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a heat protectant when using styling tools.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a great-looking trim in the comfort of your own home.
