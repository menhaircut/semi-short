Hairstyles For Over 50 With Glasses And Thick Hair Tips And Ideas

Written by Frank Jul 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As we age, our hair and vision may change, but that doesn't mean we can't look fabulous with the right hairstyles for over 50 with glasses and thick hair. Whether you have short or long hair, straight or curly, there are plenty of options to flatter your face shape and enhance your features. In this article, we'll share some how-to steps, tips, and frequently asked questions to help you find the perfect haircut for your mature and stylish look.

Description

Thick hair is a blessing and a curse, especially when we reach our golden years. While it adds volume and texture to our locks, it can also make our hairstyles bulky and unmanageable. Plus, if we wear glasses, we need to consider their shape, size, and color when choosing our haircuts. That's why it's essential to find hairstyles that balance our hair and eyewear and complement our skin tone, face shape, and personality.

How to Choose the Right Hairstyle for Over 50 with Glasses and Thick Hair

Before we jump into the specific hairstyles for over 50 with glasses and thick hair, let's review some general guidelines on how to choose the best haircut for you:

Consider your face shape

Whether you have a round, square, oval, or heart-shaped face, there's a hairstyle that can accentuate your features and soften your angles. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to go for a layered bob that frames your cheeks and elongates your neck. If you have a square face, you may prefer a side-swept bang that softens your jawline and narrows your forehead.

Think about your glasses

Your glasses can be a fashion statement or a hindrance, depending on how well they match your hairstyle. If you have thick frames, you may want to avoid heavy bangs or voluminous hairstyles that compete with your glasses. If you have thin frames, you may want to go for bolder hairstyles that add contrast and dimension to your face.

Choose the right length and texture

Short haircuts can be liberating and low-maintenance, but they can also emphasize your wrinkles and age you prematurely. Long hair, on the other hand, can be romantic and youthful, but it can also weigh you down and require more upkeep. Therefore, it's crucial to find a length that suits your lifestyle, hair type, and face shape. As for texture, you may want to consider layers, curls, waves, or straightness to add depth and movement to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hairstyles for Over 50 with Glasses and Thick Hair

Now that you have some general ideas on how to choose your haircut, let's explore some specific hairstyles for over 50 with glasses and thick hair:

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic and daring hairstyle that can highlight your cheekbones, eyes, and lips. It's also low-maintenance and versatile, as you can style it sleek, tousled, or layered. To rock the pixie cut with glasses and thick hair, you may want to add some bangs, highlights, or texture to create contrast and balance. Here's how to get the pixie cut:

  • Wash and dry your hair thoroughly.
  • Section your hair into four parts: top, back, and sides.
  • Cut the top first, using scissors or clippers, to create a short and textured layer.
  • Blend the sides and back with the top, using a comb and scissors to create a tapered effect.
  • Trim the bangs to your desired length and shape, using scissors or a razor.
  • Style your pixie cut with some hair wax, gel, or spray, and adjust your glasses accordingly.

The Bob Cut

The bob cut is a timeless and elegant hairstyle that can flatter any face shape and hair type. It's also versatile and chic, as you can wear it straight, wavy, or curly. To rock the bob cut with glasses and thick hair, you may want to add some layers, side-swept bangs, or highlights to create dimension and movement. Here's how to get the bob cut:

  • Wash and dry your hair thoroughly.
  • Section your hair into four parts: top, back, and sides.
  • Cut the back first, using scissors or clippers, to create a blunt or angled line.
  • Blend the sides with the back, using a comb and scissors to create a seamless effect.
  • Cut the top to your desired length and shape, using scissors or a razor to add texture and layers.
  • Add some side-swept bangs, if you like, by sectioning the front and cutting it diagonally.
  • Style your bob cut with some heat protectant, curling iron, or flat iron, and adjust your glasses accordingly.

Tips for Hairstyles for Over 50 with Glasses and Thick Hair

Now that you know how to choose and create your hairstyles for over 50 with glasses and thick hair, here are some tips to make them last and shine:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that suit your hair type and color, and avoid products that contain sulfates, parabens, or alcohol.
  • Apply a hair mask or oil once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair, and prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Blow-dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and smoothness, and avoid using high heat or pulling too hard.
  • Use a hairspray or serum to set your hairstyle and add shine, but don't overdo it or your hair may look stiff or greasy.
  • Accessorize your hair with headbands, clips, or scarves that match your glasses and outfit, and add some flair to your look.

FAQs about Hairstyles for Over 50 with Glasses and Thick Hair

Q: Can I dye my hair if I wear glasses and have thick hair?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair if you wear glasses and have thick hair, but you may want to consult a professional hairstylist or colorist first. They can help you choose the right shade, tone, and technique that suit your skin tone, hair type, and glasses. They can also advise you on how to maintain your hair color and prevent damage or fading.

Q: What hairstyles should I avoid if I wear glasses and have thick hair?

A: You may want to avoid hairstyles that make your glasses look too heavy or clash with your face shape. For example, you may want to avoid bangs that cover your eyebrows or eyes, as they can make your glasses slide down or obscure your vision. You may also want to avoid hairstyles that make your face look wider or rounder, as they can make your glasses look smaller or outdated. Instead, you may want to opt for hairstyles that elongate your neck, lift your cheekbones, or highlight your jawline.

Q: How often should I cut my hair if I wear glasses and have thick hair?

A: The frequency of your haircuts depends on your hair type, length, and style, as well as your personal preference and budget. However, as a general rule, you may want to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain its shape and health. You may also want to get a deep conditioning treatment or a hair gloss every two to four months to revive your hair and enhance its shine.

With these tips and ideas, you're ready to rock your hairstyles for over 50 with glasses and thick hair with confidence and style. Whether you prefer a short and sassy pixie cut or a long and luscious bob cut, make sure you choose a hairstyle that makes you feel beautiful and comfortable, and reflects your unique personality and spirit. Happy styling!
