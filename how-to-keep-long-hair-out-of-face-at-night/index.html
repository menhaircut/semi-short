How To Keep Long Hair Out Of Face At Night Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Nov 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have long hair, you know how frustrating it can be to keep it out of your face at night. Not only can it be uncomfortable, but it can also lead to breakouts and skin irritation. Luckily, there are several ways to keep your long hair out of your face while you sleep. In this post, we will share some tips and tricks to help you achieve a peaceful night's sleep without any hair distractions.

How to Keep Long Hair Out of Face at Night

If you're tired of waking up with hair all over your face, there are several things you can do to keep your locks under control while you sleep. Here are some tips to help you keep long hair out of your face at night:

1. Tie Your Hair Up

The easiest way to keep your hair out of your face at night is to tie it up. You can use a hair tie or a scrunchie to create a loose ponytail or bun. Make sure the hair tie or scrunchie isn't too tight, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

2. Braid Your Hair

Braiding your hair before bed is another great way to keep it out of your face. A loose braid will prevent tangles and knots while you sleep, and it can also create beautiful waves in the morning.

3. Use a Headband

A headband can be a lifesaver when it comes to keeping your hair out of your face at night. Choose a soft, wide headband that won't dig into your scalp or damage your hair.

4. Try a Silk Pillowcase

Silk pillowcases are not only luxurious, but they can also help keep your hair in place while you sleep. Silk is a smooth, slippery fabric that won't create friction against your hair, which can cause breakage and tangles.

Step-by-Step Guide to Keeping Long Hair Out of Face at Night

If you're not sure where to start when it comes to keeping your long hair out of your face at night, follow this step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Before bed, make sure to brush your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to style your hair for bed and prevent any tangles from forming overnight.

Step 2: Choose Your Style

Decide which style will work best for you. Will you tie your hair up, braid it, or use a headband? Choose a method that is comfortable for you and won't cause any discomfort while you sleep.

Step 3: Secure Your Hair

Once you've chosen your style, secure your hair in place. Use a loose hair tie or scrunchie, a loose braid, or a soft headband to keep your hair out of your face.

Step 4: Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help keep your hair in place and prevent tangles and breakage. Make sure to use a clean pillowcase each night to avoid any skin irritation or breakouts.

Tips for Keeping Long Hair Healthy

Keeping your hair healthy is just as important as keeping it out of your face at night. Here are some tips for maintaining long, healthy hair:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause damage and breakage to your hair. Try to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, or use them sparingly.

2. Use a Deep Conditioner

A weekly deep conditioning treatment can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Look for a conditioner that contains natural oils and ingredients to nourish your hair.

3. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can sleeping with my hair down cause breakouts?

A: Yes, sleeping with your hair down can lead to breakouts and skin irritation. When your hair comes into contact with your face, it can transfer oils and bacteria, which can clog pores and cause breakouts.

Q: Should I brush my hair before bed?

A: Yes, brushing your hair before bed can help prevent tangles and knots. It can also distribute natural oils throughout your hair, which can help keep it healthy and shiny.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: The frequency of hair washing depends on your hair type and personal preferences. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others can go several days between washes. Try to find a washing schedule that works for you and your hair type.

Conclusion

Keeping long hair out of your face at night can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible. Try tying your hair up, braiding it, or using a headband to keep your hair in place while you sleep. And don't forget to maintain healthy hair habits, like avoiding heat styling, using a deep conditioner, and getting regular trims.
