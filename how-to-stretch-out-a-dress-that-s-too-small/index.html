How To Stretch Out A Dress That s Too Small

Written by Aprilia Oct 06, 2023 · 4 min read

If you've ever found yourself in a situation where your favorite dress doesn't fit anymore or you've bought a dress that's too small, don't worry! There are ways to stretch out a dress that's too small without damaging the fabric. In this article, we'll go through the steps you need to follow to stretch out a dress that's too small.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Stretch Out a Dress That's Too Small
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Stretching Out a Dress
  4. FAQs
  5. How to Take Care of Your Dress After Stretching It Out

How to Stretch Out a Dress That's Too Small

Before we get started, it's important to note that not all fabrics can be stretched out. It's best to avoid fabrics like silk, leather, and lace. If your dress is made of a stretchy material like spandex or elastane, you may not need to stretch it out at all.

The best way to stretch out a dress that's too small is to use a combination of heat and moisture. Moisture helps to relax the fibers in the fabric, while heat helps to loosen them up. Here's what you need to do:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Fill a Bathtub with Warm Water

Fill your bathtub with warm water, making sure that the temperature is not too hot. You don't want to damage the fabric by exposing it to high temperatures. Add a few drops of fabric softener to the water to help relax the fibers in the fabric.

Step 2: Soak Your Dress in the Water

Submerge your dress in the water, making sure that it's completely covered. Gently massage the fabric to help the water penetrate the fibers. Let the dress soak for about 30 minutes.

Step 3: Drain the Water and Gently Squeeze the Dress

After 30 minutes, drain the water from the bathtub and gently squeeze the dress to remove any excess water. Be careful not to wring the dress as this can damage the fabric.

Step 4: Lay the Dress on a Flat Surface

Lay your dress on a flat surface like a table or countertop. Smooth out any wrinkles and make sure that the dress is in its original shape.

Step 5: Stretch the Dress

Start stretching the dress gently, starting with the areas that are too tight. Use your hands to pull the fabric in different directions. Be gentle, and don't pull too hard as this can damage the fabric. Continue stretching the dress until it reaches the desired size.

Step 6: Let the Dress Air Dry

After you've stretched the dress, leave it on the flat surface to air dry. Don't hang the dress as this can cause it to stretch out of shape. Once it's dry, try it on to see if it's the right size. If it's still too small, repeat the process.

Tips for Stretching Out a Dress

  • Always test a small, inconspicuous area of the dress before stretching it out completely. This will help you avoid damaging the fabric.
  • Don't use a hair dryer or any other heat source to dry your dress as this can damage the fabric.
  • If your dress is made of a delicate fabric like cotton or linen, it's best to take it to a professional tailor to have it stretched out.
  • If you're in a hurry and don't have time to soak your dress, you can try using a steamer to relax the fibers in the fabric.

FAQs

Is it possible to stretch out a dress that's too small?

Yes, it is possible to stretch out a dress that's too small using a combination of heat and moisture. However, not all fabrics can be stretched out, so it's important to test a small area of the dress before stretching it out completely.

Can I use a hair dryer to dry my dress after stretching it out?

No, it's not a good idea to use a hair dryer or any other heat source to dry your dress after stretching it out. This can damage the fabric and cause it to shrink.

How to Take Care of Your Dress After Stretching It Out

After you've stretched out your dress, it's important to take care of it properly to ensure that it stays in good condition. Here are some tips:

  • Always read the care label on your dress before washing it.
  • Wash your dress in cold water and on a gentle cycle to avoid damaging the fabric.
  • Avoid using bleach or harsh detergents as these can damage the fabric.
  • Hang your dress to dry or lay it flat on a towel. Don't put it in the dryer as this can cause it to shrink.
  • Iron your dress on a low heat setting to avoid damaging the fabric.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your dress stays in good condition and lasts for a long time.
