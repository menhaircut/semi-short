Why Do Babies Lose The Hair They Are Born With

Written by Luffy Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a new parent, you may notice that your baby is losing the hair they were born with. This can be a bit concerning, but it's a completely normal process that most babies go through. In this article, we'll explore why babies lose their hair, when it typically happens, and what you can do to care for your baby's hair during this process.

Why Do Babies Lose the Hair They Are Born With?

Babies are born with a thin layer of hair on their heads, which is often referred to as "lanugo." This hair is different from the hair that will eventually grow in, as it's very fine and soft. While lanugo serves a purpose while a baby is in the womb, it's not necessary after birth. Over time, your baby's body will begin to shed the lanugo hair and grow in new hair that's better suited for life outside of the womb.

Another reason why babies lose their hair is due to hormonal changes. When a baby is born, they have high levels of hormones that were passed on from their mother. As these levels decrease, it can cause hair loss. This is especially common in baby girls, as they have higher levels of estrogen at birth.

When Do Babies Typically Lose Their Hair?

Babies typically start losing their lanugo hair within the first few weeks of life. This process can continue for several months, and it's not uncommon for babies to be completely bald for a period of time. After the lanugo hair falls out, new hair will begin to grow in. This new hair may be a different color or texture than the lanugo hair, and it may take several months for it to fully grow in.

How Can You Care for Your Baby's Hair During This Process?

While there's not much you can do to prevent your baby from losing their hair, there are a few things you can do to care for their scalp during this process. Here are a few tips:

  • Gently wash your baby's hair with a mild shampoo when needed.
  • Use a soft-bristled brush to gently massage your baby's scalp, which can help stimulate hair growth.
  • Avoid using hair accessories that are too tight or can pull on your baby's hair.

Tips for Dealing with Your Baby's Hair Loss

While it can be tough to watch your baby lose their hair, it's important to remember that this is a completely normal process. Here are a few tips for dealing with your baby's hair loss:

  • Don't worry too much about it. Your baby will grow new hair in due time.
  • Take lots of pictures during this process, as it can be a fun and memorable time in your baby's life.
  • Consider putting a cute hat or headband on your baby to cover up any bald spots.

FAQs

Can a baby be born with hair?

Yes, some babies are born with hair. However, it's typically very fine and soft, and it may fall out within the first few weeks of life.

Will my baby's hair grow back?

Yes, your baby's hair will grow back after they lose their lanugo hair. It may take several months for the new hair to fully grow in.

Is it normal for a baby to be completely bald?

Yes, it's completely normal for a baby to be completely bald for a period of time after they lose their lanugo hair.

Hair Care for Your Baby

Once your baby's new hair starts to grow in, it's important to take good care of it. Here are a few tips for caring for your baby's hair:

  • Use a mild shampoo that's designed for babies.
  • Brush your baby's hair gently with a soft-bristled brush.
  • Avoid using hair accessories that can pull on your baby's hair.
  • Consider using a conditioner that's designed for babies if your baby's hair is very dry or tangled.

By following these tips, you can help keep your baby's hair healthy and looking great!
