How To Trim Long Curly Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Nov 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have a little boy with long curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to keep it looking neat and tidy. Regular trims can help keep the curls under control and prevent tangles, but taking your child to the salon or barber can be expensive and time-consuming. With a little bit of practice, you can learn how to trim your child's hair at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of trimming long curly boy hair.

Description

Trimming long curly boy hair requires a bit of patience and practice, but it's not as difficult as you might think. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a neat, even cut that will keep your child's curls looking great. Whether you're dealing with ringlets, spirals, or waves, these tips and tricks will help you trim your child's hair like a pro.

How to Trim Long Curly Boy Hair

Follow these steps to trim your child's long curly hair:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you begin, you'll need to gather some basic tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. You may also want to use hair clips to section off the hair.

Step 2: Wash and Condition the Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your child's hair. This will help detangle the curls and make them easier to work with. Rinse the hair thoroughly and blot it dry with a towel.

Step 3: Section Off the Hair

Use hair clips to section off the hair. Start at the bottom and work your way up, clipping the top layers out of the way as you go. This will help you see the sections you're working on and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair.

Step 4: Comb Out the Curls

Use a fine-tooth comb to comb out the curls. This will help you see any split ends or uneven sections that need trimming. Spray the hair lightly with water to help the comb glide through the curls more easily.

Step 5: Trim the Ends

Using your scissors, trim the ends of the hair. Start with small snips and work your way up, checking the length as you go. Remember, it's better to cut too little than too much. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

If your child's hair has layers, you'll need to blend them together. Take small sections of hair and trim them at an angle, working your way up to the top layer. This will help create a more natural, even look.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've trimmed all the sections, check the hair for evenness. Comb through the curls and look for any sections that are longer or shorter than the rest. Trim these sections as needed.

Step 8: Finish with Styling

Once you're happy with the length and shape of the hair, you can finish with styling. Apply a bit of curl cream or gel to the hair and scrunch it with your hands. This will help define the curls and keep them looking neat and tidy.

Tips for Trimming Long Curly Boy Hair

Here are a few tips to help you trim your child's long curly hair:

  • Work slowly and carefully, taking small sections at a time.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to take your time and trim a little bit at a time.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Keep a spray bottle of water handy to help detangle the curls and make them easier to work with.
  • Consider using hair clips to section off the hair and keep it organized.

FAQ

Can I trim my child's curly hair myself?

Yes, with a bit of practice, you can learn how to trim your child's curly hair at home. Just be sure to work slowly and carefully, taking small sections at a time.

What tools do I need to trim long curly boy hair?

You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a fine-tooth comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Hair clips can also be helpful for sectioning off the hair.

How often should I trim my child's curly hair?

It depends on how quickly your child's hair grows and how quickly it becomes tangled or unruly. Generally, it's a good idea to trim curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Long Curly Boy Hair

Trimming your child's curly hair is just one part of a healthy hair care routine. To keep your child's curls looking their best, be sure to:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid brushing the hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define the curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage the hair and cause frizz.
  • Protect the hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or swim cap.

With a little bit of care and attention, your child's long curly hair can look great all year round.
