How To Cut Layered Hair With Ponytails A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of your long hair weighing you down? Do you want a fresh, new look without sacrificing too much length? Cutting your hair into layers can add texture and volume, making it easier to style and manage. And using ponytails to guide your cuts can help ensure an even, balanced result. In this article, we'll show you how to cut layered hair with ponytails, step by step.

Description

Layered hair is a versatile and popular style that can work for almost any hair type or length. By cutting shorter layers on top of longer ones, you can add movement and dimension to your hair. Ponytails can be a useful tool for cutting layers, as they allow you to see the length and shape of your hair more clearly. Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right guidance and tools, it's possible to achieve a salon-worthy result at home.

How to

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you begin, make sure you have all the necessary tools on hand. You'll need a sharp pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair ties, and clips. It's also a good idea to have a mirror or two set up so you can see the back of your head.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to see the shape and texture of your hair as you cut. If your hair tends to be frizzy or unruly, you may want to use a smoothing serum or oil before you start.

Step 3: Section your hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into sections. Start by making a part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide your hair into four sections: two in front of your ears, and two behind. Use clips or hair ties to keep each section separate.

Step 4: Create your ponytail guides

Starting with one of the back sections, gather the hair into a ponytail at the nape of your neck. Use your comb to smooth out any bumps or tangles, and make sure the ponytail is centered. Then, using your hair cutting scissors, cut the hair straight across at the desired length. This will be your guide for the rest of your layers. Repeat this step with the other back section, and then with the two front sections, making sure each ponytail is level with the others.

Step 5: Cut your layers

Starting with one of the front sections, take a small section of hair from the top of the ponytail and hold it straight up. Cut the hair at a slight angle, following the shape of the ponytail guide. This will create a shorter layer on top of the longer one. Continue cutting small sections in this way until you've reached the end of the ponytail. Then, repeat this process with the other front section, and finally with the back sections.

Step 6: Blend your layers

Once you've cut all your layers, it's time to blend them together. Remove the hair ties or clips from each section, and shake out your hair. Use your comb to gently blend the layers together, combing through from the top down. You may need to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections with your scissors.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair, especially if you're new to cutting your own hair.
  • Use a mirror or two to help you see the back of your head.
  • Don't cut too much off at once - you can always go back and trim more if needed.
  • If you're not confident with cutting your own hair, consider asking a friend or family member for help.

FAQ

Can I cut layered hair without ponytails?

Yes, you can. However, using ponytails can help you achieve a more even, symmetrical result. If you're comfortable cutting freehand, you can certainly do so.

How often should I cut my layered hair?

It depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut layers into curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, it's important to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink and become shorter once it dries. It's also a good idea to cut curly hair in its natural state, rather than straightening it first, as this can alter the shape and texture of the curls.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain its health and texture. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-washing or using hot tools too often. Regular trims can help prevent split ends and keep your layers looking fresh. And don't forget to protect your hair from the sun and other environmental damage by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.
