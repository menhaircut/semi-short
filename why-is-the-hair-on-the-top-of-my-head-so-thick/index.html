Why Is The Hair On The Top Of My Head So Thick

Written by Christine Oct 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering why the hair on the top of your head is thicker compared to the rest of your hair? This is a common question among many people, and in this blog post, we will be exploring the reasons why your hair may be thicker on the top of your head. We will also provide tips on how to care for thick hair and answer some frequently asked questions.

Description: Understanding Thick Hair

Thick hair is characterized by having a high number of hair strands on a person's head. This type of hair can be challenging to manage and style because it tends to be heavier and more voluminous than other hair types. People with thick hair often experience frizz, tangles, and difficulty drying their hair.

How to: Identify Thick Hair

If you are unsure whether you have thick hair or not, there are a few ways to identify it. Firstly, if you can feel a lot of hair when you run your fingers over the top of your head, you may have thick hair. Also, if you have to use a lot of shampoo and conditioner when washing your hair, it can be an indicator of thick hair.

Step by Step: Why Is the Hair on the Top of My Head So Thick?

1. Genetics

The thickness of your hair is mostly determined by your genetics. If your parents or grandparents have thick hair, it is likely that you inherited that trait. Thick hair is also more common in certain ethnicities, such as those of Mediterranean, African, or Asian descent.

2. Hormones

Hormonal changes in your body can affect the thickness of your hair. During puberty, for example, hormonal changes can cause hair to become thicker. Likewise, during pregnancy, some women experience thicker hair due to hormonal changes. However, after giving birth, they may experience hair thinning or shedding.

3. Age

As you age, your hair may become thinner due to the natural aging process. However, some people may experience thicker hair as they age. This is because the hair follicles on the top of the head may be less affected by hormonal changes that cause hair thinning.

Tips: How to Care for Thick Hair

Managing thick hair can be challenging, but here are some tips to help you care for your hair:

1. Use the Right Products

Use products that are designed for thick hair, such as shampoos and conditioners that are moisturizing and hydrating. Avoid products that can weigh down your hair, such as those containing silicones or sulfates.

2. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can cause your hair to become dry and brittle. Instead, try to wash your hair every two to three days.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb when detangling your hair to prevent breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I Make My Hair Thinner?

While it is not possible to change the thickness of your hair, you can make it appear thinner by getting a haircut that removes some of the bulk. You can also use hair styling products that help to reduce volume.

2. Does Coloring My Hair Affect Its Thickness?

Coloring your hair can cause damage, which can lead to hair breakage and thinning. However, if done correctly, coloring your hair should not affect its thickness.

3. Can a Poor Diet Affect the Thickness of My Hair?

Yes, a poor diet can affect the thickness of your hair. Eating a diet rich in vitamins and nutrients, such as iron, zinc, and biotin, can help to promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care: Take Care of Your Thick Hair

Thick hair can be challenging to manage, but with the right products and care, you can keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Remember to use products designed for your hair type, avoid overwashing your hair, and use a wide-tooth comb when detangling. By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking thick and luxurious.
