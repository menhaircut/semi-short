How To Take Care Of Short Permed Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Perming your hair can be a great way to add some texture and volume to your locks. However, short permed hair requires extra care and attention to maintain its health and shape. In this article, we’ll guide you through the best practices for taking care of your short permed hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Permed Hair Care
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Short Permed Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Products for Short Permed Hair

Description

Perming involves treating your hair with chemicals to create permanent curls or waves. Short permed hair can be a great way to add some texture and volume to your locks. However, permed hair requires extra care and attention to maintain its health and shape. Short permed hair, in particular, can be prone to breakage and damage if not properly cared for.

How to Permed Hair Care

Permed hair requires a unique set of care practices to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips for taking care of your short permed hair:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Permed hair is more fragile than non-permed hair, so it’s important to use gentle products that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for shampoos and conditioners specifically formulated for permed hair.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can cause damage to permed hair, so it’s best to avoid it whenever possible. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low.

3. Don’t over-wash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage. Aim to wash your hair no more than twice a week.

4. Use a wide-tooth comb

Permed hair can be more prone to tangling than non-permed hair, so it’s important to use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your locks. Avoid using brushes or fine-tooth combs, which can cause breakage.

5. Get regular trims

Regular trims can help keep your short permed hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide for taking care of your short permed hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Wash your short permed hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Step 2: Detangle with a wide-tooth comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help keep it moisturized.

Step 4: Air dry your hair

Avoid using heat styling tools and let your hair air dry.

Step 5: Get regular trims

Schedule regular trims to keep your short permed hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Tips for Maintaining Short Permed Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining your short permed hair:

1. Sleep on silk or satin pillowcases

Regular cotton pillowcases can cause friction and lead to breakage. Sleeping on silk or satin pillowcases can help prevent this.

2. Avoid tight hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that can pull on your hair, such as tight braids or ponytails. These can cause breakage and damage to your permed hair.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun can cause damage to your permed hair. Wear a hat or scarf when spending time outdoors to protect your locks.

FAQs

Q: How often should I get my short permed hair trimmed?

A: Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I color my permed hair?

A: Yes, you can color your permed hair. However, it’s best to wait at least a week after getting your perm before coloring your hair.
