How To Cut Your Hair To Shoulder Length

Written by Luffy Nov 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long hair weighing you down? Cutting your hair to shoulder length can be a great way to freshen up your look and give your hair some new life. But how do you do it yourself? In this article, we'll go over step-by-step instructions on how to cut your hair to shoulder length, as well as some tips and tricks for achieving the perfect cut.

Description

Shoulder length hair is a popular choice for many women because it's versatile, easy to style, and looks great on just about anyone. It's a length that's not too short, but not too long either. Cutting your hair to shoulder length can also help to get rid of any damaged ends and promote healthy hair growth.

How to Cut Your Hair to Shoulder Length

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a sharp pair of haircutting scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to section off your hair. Here's how to cut your hair to shoulder length:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry, so wash and blow-dry your hair before you start cutting. This will give you a better idea of how your hair will look once it's cut.

Step 2: Section off your hair

Use your comb to section off your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section out of the way so it's easier to work with.

Step 3: Cut the back section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight up with your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across at the desired length. Repeat this process, working your way up the back section of your hair.

Step 4: Cut the front section

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the front. Take a small section of hair from one side and hold it straight up with your fingers. Cut the hair straight across at the desired length. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Blend the layers

Using your scissors, blend the layers of your hair together by cutting small sections of hair at an angle. This will help to create a seamless transition from the longer layers to the shorter layers.

Step 6: Check for symmetry

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for symmetry by looking in the mirror and making sure both sides are even. Make any necessary adjustments.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair to shoulder length:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to the desired length. It's always better to cut less off than too much.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and damage to your hair.
  • If you're not comfortable cutting your hair, consider going to a professional stylist instead.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair to shoulder length if it's curly?

Absolutely! Shoulder length hair can look great on curly hair. Just make sure to cut it when it's dry and to cut it a little longer than you think you want it, as curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries.

How often should I cut my hair to maintain shoulder length?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the length and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair to shoulder length, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking and feeling its best:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

Now that you know how to cut your hair to shoulder length, it's time to give it a try! Follow these steps and tips, and you'll have a beautiful new hairstyle in no time.
