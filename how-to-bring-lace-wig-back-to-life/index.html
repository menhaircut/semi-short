How to Bring Lace Wig Back to Life | Step by Step Guide

If you are a fan of lace wigs, then you know that they can be quite expensive. And as much as we love them, they can lose their luster and become dull over time. But don't worry, there are ways to bring your lace wig back to life and make it look as good as new. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to revive your lace wig and keep it looking fresh.

Description

A lace wig is a type of wig that is made of human hair or synthetic hair that is tied by hand to a sheer lace base. The lace is then attached to the scalp, creating a natural-looking hairline. Lace wigs are popular because they look and feel like natural hair. However, over time, they can become dull and lifeless, making them look less realistic.

How to

Step 1: Wash Your Lace Wig

The first step in bringing your lace wig back to life is to wash it. Use a gentle shampoo and lukewarm water to cleanse the wig. Gently massage the shampoo into the hair, being careful not to tangle or mat the hair. Rinse the wig thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 2: Condition Your Lace Wig

After washing, condition your lace wig with a quality conditioner. Apply the conditioner to the hair, avoiding the lace. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out with lukewarm water. Conditioning your wig will help to restore its shine and softness.

Step 3: Dry Your Lace Wig

After washing and conditioning, gently pat your lace wig dry with a towel. Do not rub the wig vigorously as this can cause tangles and damage. Allow the wig to air dry on a wig stand or mannequin head. Do not use a hairdryer as this can damage the hair fibers.

Step 4: Detangle Your Lace Wig

Once your lace wig is dry, it's time to detangle it. Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently remove any tangles or knots in the hair. Work from the bottom up, starting at the ends of the hair and working your way up to the roots. Be gentle and patient, as rough handling can cause damage to the hair fibers.

Step 5: Style Your Lace Wig

Now that your lace wig is clean, conditioned, and detangled, it's time to style it. You can use a flat iron, curling iron, or hot rollers to create the look you want. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to the hair fibers.

Tips

  • Always use a wig stand or mannequin head to store your lace wig when you're not wearing it.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to detangle your wig, never use a regular brush.
  • Never sleep in your wig. Always remove it before going to bed.
  • Don't use hot water when washing your wig, as this can damage the hair fibers.
  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for wigs.

FAQ

How often should I wash my lace wig?

You should wash your lace wig every 10-14 days, depending on how often you wear it. If you wear it frequently, you may need to wash it more often.

Can I dye my lace wig?

If your lace wig is made of human hair, you can dye it. However, if it's made of synthetic hair, you cannot dye it.

How long will my lace wig last?

The lifespan of your lace wig depends on how well you care for it and how often you wear it. With proper care, a lace wig can last up to a year or more.

Hair Care

Caring for your lace wig is important if you want it to last and look good. Always store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when you're not wearing it. Use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to detangle it, and never use regular brushes. Wash and condition your wig regularly, and avoid using hot water or heat styling tools without a heat protectant spray. With proper care, your lace wig can look as good as new for a long time.
