How To Create 40S Hairstyles A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a fan of vintage hairstyles, you might be interested in learning how to create 40s hairstyles. These hairstyles are glamorous, sophisticated, and perfect for any occasion. In this article, we'll show you how to create several iconic 40s hairstyles, including victory rolls, pin curls, and the classic Hollywood wave. So, grab your curling iron and let's get started!

Victory Rolls

Victory rolls are one of the most iconic 40s hairstyles. They were popular during World War II and were named after the victory in Europe. Here's how to create victory rolls:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and applying a styling mousse. Blow dry your hair until it's completely dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into two sections: the front and back. Clip the back section out of the way.

Step 3: Create the Rolls

Take a small section of hair from the front section and roll it towards your scalp. Secure it in place with bobby pins. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Finish the Look

Spray your hair with hairspray to keep it in place. You can also add a headband or scarf for a more vintage look.

Pin Curls

Pin curls were another popular 40s hairstyle. They're easy to create and can be worn in a variety of ways. Here's how to create pin curls:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and applying a styling mousse. Blow dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections. Take one section and wrap it around your finger, starting at the ends and rolling upwards towards your scalp.

Step 3: Pin the Curls

Remove your finger and secure the curl in place with a bobby pin. Repeat with the rest of your hair.

Step 4: Let Your Hair Dry

Allow your hair to dry completely. You can either sleep on the curls or blow dry them on a low setting.

Step 5: Remove the Pins

Remove the pins and gently brush out your curls. You can add hairspray to keep the curls in place.

Hollywood Wave

The Hollywood wave is a classic 40s hairstyle that's still popular today. It's perfect for a formal occasion or a night out. Here's how to create the Hollywood wave:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and applying a styling mousse. Blow dry your hair until it's completely dry.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections. Use a curling iron to curl each section away from your face.

Step 3: Create the Waves

Take a section of hair and twist it in the opposite direction of the curl. Pin it in place and repeat with the rest of your hair.

Step 4: Let Your Hair Set

Allow your hair to set for at least 30 minutes. You can use a hair dryer on a low setting to speed up the process.

Step 5: Remove the Pins

Remove the pins and gently brush out your waves. You can add hairspray to keep the waves in place.

FAQs

Q: Can I create 40s hairstyles on short hair?

A: Yes, you can create 40s hairstyles on short hair. However, some styles may require hair extensions or hairpieces.

Q: Do I need special tools to create 40s hairstyles?

A: No, you don't need any special tools. A curling iron, bobby pins, and hairspray are all you need.

Q: Can I create 40s hairstyles without heat?

A: Yes, you can create 40s hairstyles without heat. Pin curls are a great heatless option.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. Use heat protectant products if you must use heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye.

With these tips and techniques, you can create beautiful 40s hairstyles that are sure to turn heads. Whether you're going for a retro look or just want to try something new, these hairstyles are a great choice. So, grab your curling iron and start experimenting!
