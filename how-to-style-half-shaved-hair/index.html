How To Style Half Shaved Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by William Jul 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you thinking of rocking a half shaved hairdo? Look no further! This article will provide you with all the information you need to style your half shaved hair with ease. From hair care to styling tips, we’ve got you covered.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Style Half Shaved Hair
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips and Tricks
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care

Description

Half shaved hair is a bold and edgy hairstyle that has been gaining popularity in recent years. It involves shaving one side of your head while leaving the other side long. This style can be customized to fit your personal taste and can be worn in many different ways. Whether you want a sleek and sophisticated look or a punk-inspired style, half shaved hair has got you covered.

How to Style Half Shaved Hair

Styling half shaved hair can seem intimidating, but it’s actually quite simple. The key is to work with the length and texture of your hair to create a look that complements your features.

Choose Your Style

Before you begin styling, decide what kind of look you want to achieve. Do you want a sleek and polished look, or do you prefer a more edgy and textured style? Once you have a vision in mind, you can begin the styling process.

Work with Your Hair Texture

The key to styling half shaved hair is to work with your hair’s natural texture. If you have naturally curly hair, embrace your curls and use a curl-enhancing product to define your curls. If you have straight hair, use a flat iron to create a sleek and polished look.

Use Product Sparingly

When it comes to styling half shaved hair, less is more. Use product sparingly to avoid weighing down your hair or creating a greasy appearance. Opt for lightweight products that provide hold without adding too much volume or texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to style your half shaved hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Towel-dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray if you plan on using heat styling tools.
  3. Blow dry your hair, using a round brush to add volume and shape.
  4. Use a flat iron or curling iron to style your hair, depending on the look you want to achieve.
  5. Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, focusing on the ends and avoiding the roots.
  6. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks to help you style your half shaved hair:

  • Invest in a high-quality flat iron or curling iron for long-lasting results.
  • Use a detangling spray or wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage when brushing your hair.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find the ones that work best for your hair type.
  • Consider adding highlights or lowlights to your hair to create depth and dimension.

FAQs

Can I style half shaved hair if I have short hair?

Yes, you can still rock a half shaved hairdo if you have short hair. Talk to your stylist about the best way to incorporate a shaved section into your current haircut.

How often do I need to touch up my shaved section?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how much of a contrast there is between the shaved section and the rest of your hair. On average, most people touch up their shaved section every 4-6 weeks.

Can I still wear my hair up if I have half shaved hair?

Absolutely! Half shaved hair can be worn in a variety of styles, including ponytails, buns, and braids.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential when it comes to maintaining a half shaved hairstyle. Here are a few tips to keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days, using a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your hairstyle.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to rock a half shaved hairdo with confidence and ease. Remember to work with your hair’s natural texture and choose a style that complements your features. Happy styling!
