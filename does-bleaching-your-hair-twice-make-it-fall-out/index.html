Does Bleaching Your Hair Twice Make It Fall Out The Truth Behind Double Bleaching

Written by Aprilia Jun 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re someone who loves to experiment with their hair color, you might be familiar with the term “double bleaching.” But what exactly is double bleaching, and does it make your hair fall out? In this article, we’ll answer all your questions regarding the safety of bleaching your hair twice.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Double Bleach Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Double Bleaching
  • Tips for Safe Double Bleaching
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Double Bleaching

Description

Double bleaching is a process of lightening your hair color by bleaching it twice in one session. This technique is often used to achieve a very light or platinum blonde color. However, double bleaching can be damaging to your hair, and many people wonder if it can cause hair loss.

The answer to the question is not straightforward. While bleaching your hair twice can cause damage and breakage, it doesn’t necessarily mean that your hair will fall out. The extent of damage depends on several factors, including your hair’s natural strength and the level of bleach used.

How to Double Bleach Your Hair

If you’re planning to double bleach your hair, it’s essential to understand the process involved. Typically, double bleaching involves applying bleach to your hair twice in one sitting. The first application lifts the color to a certain level, and the second application lightens it further.

It’s important to keep in mind that double bleaching can be damaging to your hair. The bleach can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. If you’re not careful, you might end up with damaged hair or even hair loss.

Step-by-Step Guide to Double Bleaching

Here’s a step-by-step guide to double bleaching your hair:

  1. Choose the right bleach: Look for a bleach that’s gentle on your hair and scalp. Avoid using bleach that’s too harsh, as it can cause more damage.
  2. Prepare the bleach mixture: Mix the bleach powder and developer according to the instructions on the package.
  3. Apply the first bleach: Section your hair and apply the bleach to your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Leave it on for the recommended time.
  4. Rinse and dry your hair: Rinse your hair thoroughly and dry it with a towel. Don’t use a hairdryer at this stage.
  5. Apply the second bleach: Repeat the process with the second bleach, making sure to apply it evenly to your hair.
  6. Rinse and condition your hair: Rinse your hair thoroughly, and apply a deep conditioner to restore moisture to your hair.

Tips for Safe Double Bleaching

Here are some tips to help you double bleach your hair safely:

  • Don’t double bleach your hair if it’s already damaged or weak.
  • Use a gentle bleach that’s suitable for your hair type.
  • Don’t leave the bleach on for longer than the recommended time.
  • Use a deep conditioner after double bleaching to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools immediately after double bleaching.
  • Limit double bleaching to once every few months to prevent over-processing.

FAQs

Does double bleaching cause hair loss?

Double bleaching can cause hair damage and breakage, but it doesn’t necessarily cause hair loss. However, if your hair is already weak or damaged, double bleaching can make the problem worse.

How long should I wait before double bleaching my hair again?

It’s best to wait at least a few months before double bleaching your hair again. Double bleaching can be damaging, and frequent bleaching can cause over-processing, which can lead to hair damage and breakage.

Can I double bleach my hair at home?

You can double bleach your hair at home, but it’s important to be careful and follow the instructions carefully. If you’re not confident about doing it yourself, it’s best to visit a professional hairdresser.

Hair Care After Double Bleaching

After double bleaching your hair, it’s essential to take care of it to prevent further damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Apply a deep conditioner regularly to restore moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends.
  • Avoid coloring your hair frequently, as this can cause further damage.

In conclusion, double bleaching can be damaging to your hair, but it doesn’t necessarily cause hair loss. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can double bleach your hair safely and prevent further damage.
