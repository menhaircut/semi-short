What To Do If You Burn Your Scalp With Bleach

Written by Petter Sep 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who loves to experiment with different hair colors, you've probably tried bleaching your hair at some point. However, bleaching your hair can sometimes lead to disastrous results, especially if you don't follow the instructions properly. One of the most common problems people face when bleaching their hair is scalp burns.

How to Identify a Scalp Burn

A scalp burn caused by bleach can be quite painful and uncomfortable. You may experience symptoms such as itching, redness, swelling, and even blisters. In severe cases, you may also experience hair loss or scarring. If you suspect that you have a scalp burn, it's essential to take immediate action to prevent further damage.

What to Do If You Burn Your Scalp with Bleach

If you've accidentally burned your scalp with bleach, don't panic. There are several things you can do to ease the pain and prevent further damage. Here are some steps you should take:

Step 1: Rinse Your Hair Immediately

The first thing you should do when you realize that you've burned your scalp with bleach is to rinse your hair immediately. Use cold water to rinse your hair thoroughly for at least 10-15 minutes. This will help to remove any residual bleach from your scalp and hair.

Step 2: Apply Aloe Vera Gel

Once you've rinsed your hair, apply aloe vera gel to the affected area. Aloe vera has soothing properties that can help to reduce inflammation and ease the pain. Apply the gel directly to your scalp and leave it on for a few hours.

Step 3: Take Over-The-Counter Painkillers

If you're experiencing a lot of pain, you can take over-the-counter painkillers such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen. These medications can help to reduce pain and inflammation. However, make sure you follow the dosage instructions carefully.

Step 4: Avoid Heat Styling

After you've burned your scalp with bleach, it's essential to avoid heat styling your hair. Heat can further damage your scalp and hair, making the burn worse. Avoid using hair dryers, flat irons, and curling irons until your scalp has completely healed.

Tips for Preventing Scalp Burns

The best way to deal with a scalp burn is to prevent it from happening in the first place. Here are some tips to help you prevent scalp burns:

Tip 1: Follow the Instructions Carefully

When using hair bleach, make sure you read the instructions carefully and follow them to the letter. Don't leave the bleach on your hair for longer than recommended, and don't apply it to your scalp.

Tip 2: Do a Patch Test

Before applying bleach to your hair, do a patch test to see how your skin reacts to the bleach. Apply a small amount of bleach to a small area of skin and wait for 24 hours to see if you have any adverse reactions.

Tip 3: Use Protective Gear

Wear gloves and a protective cape or towel when applying bleach to your hair. This will help to prevent the bleach from coming into contact with your skin.

FAQ

Q: Can I still bleach my hair if I've burned my scalp before?

A: It's not recommended to bleach your hair if you've burned your scalp before. Your scalp may be more sensitive to bleach, and you may be at a higher risk of experiencing a burn.

Q: How long does it take for a scalp burn to heal?

A: The healing time for a scalp burn can vary depending on the severity of the burn. It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks for the burn to heal completely.

Q: Can I color my hair after a scalp burn?

A: It's best to wait until your scalp has completely healed before coloring your hair. Coloring your hair can further irritate your scalp and delay the healing process.

Hair Care After a Scalp Burn

After you've burned your scalp with bleach, it's essential to take extra care of your hair. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle shampoo that's free from harsh chemicals and sulfates. A gentle shampoo will help to cleanse your hair without further irritating your scalp.

Tip 2: Moisturize Your Hair

After shampooing, apply a moisturizing conditioner to your hair. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent further damage.

Tip 3: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your scalp and cause further damage. Opt for loose hairstyles such as braids or a low ponytail.

Scalp burns caused by bleach can be quite painful and uncomfortable. However, with the right care and treatment, you can speed up the healing process and prevent further damage. Remember to follow the instructions carefully when using hair bleach and take extra care of your hair after a scalp burn.
