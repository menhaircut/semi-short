How To Cut Shaggy Hair Boy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 17, 2023 · 3 min read

Do you have a son or a young boy in your life with shaggy hair? Are you looking for a way to give them a fresh new cut without breaking the bank? Look no further, as we have compiled a step-by-step guide on how to cut shaggy hair boy!

Description

Shaggy hair is a popular hairstyle for young boys, but it can quickly become messy and unkempt. By cutting shaggy hair, you can give your child a fresh new look while still maintaining the length and texture of their hair. This guide is perfect for parents who want to save money on haircuts or for anyone who wants to learn how to cut hair.

How To

Before you begin cutting your child's hair, it's important to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clippers if you plan on using them. It's also a good idea to have a towel or cape to protect your child's clothes from hair clippings.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing your child's hair and letting it air dry or blow-drying it on a low setting.

2. Use the comb to separate the hair into sections, starting from the back of the head and moving forward.

3. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at the desired length. Use the comb to guide the scissors and make sure the cut is even.

4. Continue cutting the hair in sections until you have reached the desired length for each section.

5. If you want to use clippers, attach the desired guard and run it over the hair in the same direction as the growth of the hair. Use the comb to guide the clippers and make sure the cut is even.

6. Once you have finished cutting the hair, use the comb to trim any stray hairs or uneven areas.

7. Clean up the hair clippings and wash your child's hair again if necessary.

Tips

- Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.

- Use a mirror to check the back of your child's head.

- Take breaks if your child becomes restless or uncomfortable.

- Consider using a spray bottle to dampen the hair and make it easier to cut.

- If you're unsure about cutting your child's hair, consider watching tutorial videos or practicing on a mannequin head first.

FAQ

Q: How often should I cut my child's hair?

A: It depends on the length and texture of their hair. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is a good time frame to maintain a shaggy hairstyle.

Q: What if I make a mistake while cutting my child's hair?

A: Don't panic! Hair grows back and mistakes can be fixed. You can always take your child to a professional hairstylist to fix any mistakes.

Hair Care

After cutting your child's hair, it's important to maintain their hair with proper care. This includes washing their hair regularly, using a conditioner to keep it soft, and brushing or combing it gently to prevent tangles. It's also important to protect their hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray or avoiding excessive heat styling.

Now that you know how to cut shaggy hair boy, you can give your child a fresh new look at home. With the right tools and techniques, cutting hair can be a fun and rewarding experience for both you and your child. Happy cutting!
