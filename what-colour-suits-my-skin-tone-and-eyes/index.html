What Colour Suits My Skin Tone And Eyes A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Jun 26, 2023 · 5 min read

When it comes to choosing the right colours for your clothing, hair, and makeup, it can be overwhelming to figure out what looks best on you. However, understanding your skin tone and eye colour can make all the difference in helping you choose the perfect shades that enhance your natural beauty. In this article, we'll go over how to determine your skin tone and eye colour and provide tips on what colours work best for you.

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

Before you can determine what colours look best on you, you need to know your skin tone. There are three main categories of skin tone: cool, warm, and neutral. Here are some ways to figure out which category you fall into:

Look at Your Veins

Take a look at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they look blue or purple, you likely have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you likely have a warm skin tone. If you have a mix of both, you may have a neutral skin tone.

Check Your Jewellery

What type of jewellery looks best on you? If you notice that silver jewellery tends to flatter your skin, you likely have a cool skin tone. If gold jewellery looks best, you likely have a warm skin tone. If you can wear both without any issues, you may have a neutral skin tone.

Consider Your Sun Exposure

How does your skin react to the sun? If you burn easily and have a pink or rosy undertone, you likely have a cool skin tone. If you tan easily and have a yellow or golden undertone, you likely have a warm skin tone. If you have a mix of both or don't have any noticeable undertones, you may have a neutral skin tone.

How to Determine Your Eye Colour

Eye colour can also play a role in what colours look best on you. Here are the most common eye colours and what shades tend to complement them best:

Blue Eyes

For blue eyes, shades like copper, bronze, and champagne tend to make them pop. You can also try wearing navy, gray, and charcoal for a more dramatic look.

Green Eyes

For green eyes, shades like burgundy, plum, and forest green tend to complement them best. You can also try wearing gold, bronze, and peach for a softer look.

Brown Eyes

For brown eyes, shades like purple, bronze, and teal tend to be the most flattering. You can also try wearing gold, green, and copper for a more natural look.

Colours for Cool Skin Tones

If you have a cool skin tone, you'll likely look best in colours that have blue or pink undertones. Here are some shades to try:

  • Navy
  • Royal blue
  • Emerald green
  • Pink
  • Lavender
  • Plum
  • Gray
  • Silver

Colours for Warm Skin Tones

If you have a warm skin tone, you'll likely look best in colours that have yellow or gold undertones. Here are some shades to try:

  • Gold
  • Orange
  • Yellow
  • Coral
  • Peach
  • Olive green
  • Brown
  • Red

Colours for Neutral Skin Tones

If you have a neutral skin tone, you can pretty much wear any shade without it clashing with your skin. However, here are some colours that tend to be the most flattering:

  • Black
  • White
  • Gray
  • Pink
  • Peach
  • Lavender
  • Burgundy
  • Navy

Hair Care Tips for Enhancing Your Natural Colour

Your hair colour can also complement your skin tone and eye colour. Here are some tips for enhancing your natural colour:

  • If you have cool undertones, try shades like ash blonde, platinum, or dark brown.
  • If you have warm undertones, try shades like honey blonde, caramel, or auburn.
  • If you have neutral undertones, you can pretty much pull off any shade. However, shades like chocolate brown or golden blonde tend to be universally flattering.

FAQs

What if I have multiple undertones?

If you have a mix of warm and cool undertones, you likely have a neutral skin tone. If you have a mix of blue and green in your eyes, you can try wearing colours that complement both, such as bronze or gray.

What if I don't like the colours that are recommended for me?

At the end of the day, you should wear colours that make you feel confident and comfortable. If you don't like a certain shade, try experimenting with different hues until you find one that you love.

Can I wear black if I have a warm skin tone?

Yes! While black can be a harsh colour for some people, it can still look great on warm skin tones. If you find that black is too harsh, try wearing it in small doses, such as with a black blazer or accessories.

Choosing the right colours for your skin tone and eye colour can make a world of difference in how you look and feel. By following these tips and experimenting with different shades, you can find the perfect colours that enhance your natural beauty.
