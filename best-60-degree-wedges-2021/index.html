Best 60 Degree Wedges 2021 A Comprehensive Guide

If you're a golf enthusiast looking for the best 60 degree wedges to add to your golf bag, you've come to the right place. In this guide, we'll provide you with a list of the top 60 degree wedges in 2021 that will help you improve your short game and take your golfing skills to the next level.

Description

60 degree wedges are known for their versatility and ability to perform a variety of shots around the green. They are great for getting out of bunkers, hitting high flop shots, and getting the ball to stop quickly on the green. With the right 60 degree wedge, you can improve your scoring and make more birdies and pars.

How to Choose the Best 60 Degree Wedge

Choosing the right 60 degree wedge can be a daunting task with so many options available in the market. Here are some factors to consider when choosing the best 60 degree wedge:

1. Loft

The loft of a wedge is the angle between the face of the club and the ground. The higher the loft, the higher the ball will go. A 60 degree wedge is perfect for high, soft shots around the green.

2. Grind

The grind of a wedge refers to the shaping and design of the sole of the club. The right grind can help you hit different types of shots with ease.

3. Bounce

The bounce of a wedge is the angle between the leading edge and the trailing edge of the sole. A higher bounce angle can help prevent the club from digging into the turf and provide more forgiveness on shots.

Top 60 Degree Wedges in 2021

Here are some of the best 60 degree wedges in 2021:

1. Titleist Vokey SM8 Wedge

The Titleist Vokey SM8 Wedge is one of the most popular wedges on the market. It features a progressive center of gravity, which provides more control and consistency on shots. The SM8 also has a variety of grinds and bounce options to suit different playing styles.

2. Callaway Mack Daddy CB Wedge

The Callaway Mack Daddy CB Wedge is designed for golfers who want forgiveness and versatility. It has a cavity-back design that provides more forgiveness on off-center hits and a wide sole that makes it easier to hit out of bunkers.

3. Cleveland RTX ZipCore Wedge

The Cleveland RTX ZipCore Wedge features a new ZipCore technology that provides more consistency and distance control on shots. It also has a variety of sole grinds and bounce options to suit different playing styles.

Tips for Using a 60 Degree Wedge

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your 60 degree wedge:

1. Practice Your Technique

Using a 60 degree wedge requires a different technique than using other clubs. Practice hitting different types of shots with your wedge to improve your technique and feel around the green.

2. Choose the Right Loft and Bounce

Choosing the right loft and bounce for your wedge can help you hit better shots and improve your scoring. Experiment with different lofts and bounces to find the right combination for your game.

3. Use the Right Ball Position

The position of the ball in your stance can affect the trajectory and spin of your shots. Experiment with different ball positions to find the right one for your shot.

FAQs

1. What is the difference between a 56 degree and 60 degree wedge?

A 56 degree wedge has less loft than a 60 degree wedge, which means it will hit the ball lower and with less spin. A 60 degree wedge is ideal for hitting high, soft shots around the green.

2. Can I use a 60 degree wedge for full shots?

While a 60 degree wedge is primarily used for shots around the green, it can be used for full shots if necessary. However, it may not be as forgiving as other clubs and requires a good technique to hit solid shots.

3. How often should I replace my 60 degree wedge?

It's recommended to replace your wedges every 2-3 years or sooner if you notice significant wear and tear on the clubface or grooves.

Conclusion

A 60 degree wedge is an essential club for any golfer looking to improve their short game. With the right loft, grind, and bounce, you can hit a variety of shots around the green and improve your scoring. Consider the options we've provided in this guide and choose the best 60 degree wedge that suits your playing style and preferences.
