Best Way To Sleep With Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Christine Aug 19, 2023 · 5 min read

Having naturally curly hair is a blessing, but it can also be a hassle to maintain. One of the biggest challenges for curly-haired individuals is figuring out the best way to sleep without ruining those beautiful curls. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to sleep with naturally curly hair and wake up with gorgeous, defined curls.

Table of Contents

  • How to Sleep with Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Sleeping with Naturally Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Naturally Curly Hair

How to Sleep with Naturally Curly Hair

The best way to sleep with naturally curly hair is to protect it from friction and tangling. Here are some ways to do that:

1. Use a Silk or Satin Pillowcase

Silk and satin are smooth fabrics that reduce friction between your hair and the pillowcase. This means less tangling and frizz. Cotton pillowcases, on the other hand, can cause more friction and make your hair look messy in the morning.

2. Sleep with Your Hair in a Pineapple

A pineapple is a high ponytail that sits on top of your head. This style keeps your curls from getting crushed while you sleep. Use a scrunchie or a silk scarf to tie your hair loosely into a pineapple. Make sure the scrunchie or scarf is not too tight, as this can cause a dent in your curls.

3. Use a Silk or Satin Bonnet

A silk or satin bonnet is another option to protect your curls while you sleep. Simply put your hair in a loose ponytail or pineapple, and then cover it with the bonnet. This will keep your hair from rubbing against the pillowcase and causing tangles.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to sleep with naturally curly hair:

Step 1: Detangle Your Hair

Before you go to bed, make sure your hair is detangled. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to your curls before you go to bed. This will help to seal in moisture and prevent frizz. Be sure to use a product that is light enough to not weigh your curls down.

Step 3: Put Your Hair in a Pineapple or Bonnet

Choose your preferred method of protecting your curls. Either put your hair in a pineapple or use a silk or satin bonnet to cover your hair.

Step 4: Sleep Comfortably

Make sure you’re comfortable before you go to bed. If your bonnet or pineapple is too tight, adjust it until it feels comfortable. You want to make sure you get a good night’s rest without any discomfort.

Tips for Sleeping with Naturally Curly Hair

Here are a few more tips for sleeping with naturally curly hair:

1. Avoid Sleeping on Wet Hair

It’s best to avoid sleeping on wet hair, as this can cause more frizz and breakage. Allow your hair to air-dry before you go to bed, or use a diffuser to dry your hair on low heat.

2. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids and buns can cause tension on your scalp and lead to breakage. Opt for looser styles like a pineapple or a loose braid.

3. Use a Silk or Satin Scarf

If you don’t have a silk or satin pillowcase or bonnet, you can use a silk or satin scarf to wrap your hair. Simply tie the scarf around your head and tuck in any loose ends.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my naturally curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much product you use. Some people with curly hair can go a week without washing, while others need to wash every other day. Experiment with different wash schedules to find what works best for you.

Q: How do I refresh my curls in the morning?

A: To refresh your curls in the morning, mist your hair with water and scrunch your curls with your hands. You can also use a curl refresher spray or a leave-in conditioner to revive your curls.

Hair Care Tips for Naturally Curly Hair

Here are some hair care tips to keep your naturally curly hair healthy and beautiful:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it’s important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like shea butter or coconut oil.

2. Use a Deep Conditioner Once a Week

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help to nourish and hydrate your curls. Look for a deep conditioner that contains protein to strengthen your hair and reduce breakage.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can damage your curls and cause them to lose their shape. If you must use heat, use a diffuser on low heat or a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can sleep soundly and wake up with beautiful, defined curls. Remember to take care of your hair and experiment with different methods to find what works best for you.
