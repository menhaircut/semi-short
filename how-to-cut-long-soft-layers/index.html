How To Cut Long Soft Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and add some movement and dimension, cutting long soft layers may be the perfect solution. This style is versatile, low maintenance, and works well with all hair types, making it a popular choice among women of all ages.

What You'll Need

To cut long soft layers, you'll need a few essential tools:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean, dry, and tangle-free. Comb through your hair to remove any knots or tangles, and section it off into manageable pieces using sectioning clips.

Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how long you want your layers to be. For a subtle, natural-looking effect, aim for layers that are about 1-2 inches shorter than your overall hair length. If you're going for a more dramatic look, you can go shorter.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Starting at the front of your hair, take a small section and comb it straight down. Use your fingers or a comb to hold the section in place and cut straight across with your scissors. Repeat this process for each section, working your way towards the back of your head.

Make sure to cut each section at the same angle to create even layers. For a softer, more natural look, you can also use a point-cutting technique, which involves cutting into the hair at an angle instead of straight across.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of your sections, it's time to blend the layers together. Take a section of hair from the back of your head and hold it up at a 90-degree angle. Use your scissors to cut into the ends of the hair, creating a soft, feathered effect. Repeat this process for each section, blending the layers together for a seamless look.

Step 5: Finish with a Trim

To finish off your haircut, trim any stray hairs or uneven pieces with scissors. You can also use a razor to add more texture and movement to your layers, but be careful not to overdo it.

Tips for Cutting Long Soft Layers

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting long soft layers:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections as you gain more confidence.
  • Take breaks if you need to, and don't rush the process.
  • Always use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • When in doubt, start with longer layers and work your way up to shorter ones.

FAQs

How often should you cut your hair if you have long soft layers?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

Can you cut long soft layers on your own?

While it's possible to cut your own hair, it's always best to go to a professional stylist for the best results. If you do decide to cut your own hair, make sure to follow the steps carefully and take your time.

Will long soft layers work with curly hair?

Yes! Long soft layers work well with all hair types, including curly hair. Just make sure to have a stylist who is experienced in cutting curly hair to ensure the best results.

Hair Care for Long Soft Layers

To keep your long soft layers looking their best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of your hair to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.
