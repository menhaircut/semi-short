Which Hairstyle Suits Square Face Male

Are you struggling to find the perfect hairstyle that suits your square face? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through the best hairstyles for square face males, along with tips on how to maintain and care for your hair.

Description

A square face is characterized by a strong jawline, sharp angles and a wide forehead. The key to finding the right hairstyle is to soften these strong features and create a balanced look. The right hairstyle can help to enhance your facial features and make you look your best.

How To

Here are some of the best hairstyles for square face males:

1. Textured Crop

The textured crop is a popular hairstyle that works well for square faces. This style features short hair on the sides and back, with longer hair on top that is styled into messy, textured layers. This style softens the jawline and adds volume to the top of the head, creating a balanced look.

2. Side Part Haircut

The side part haircut is a classic style that suits square faces. This style features a deep side part with short hair on the sides and back, and longer hair on top that is swept to the side. This style helps to elongate the face and create a more balanced look.

3. Buzz Cut

The buzz cut is a low maintenance style that works well for square faces. This style features short hair all over the head, creating a clean and sharp look. This style accentuates the strong jawline and creates a masculine look.

Step by Step

Hereâs how to achieve the perfect textured crop:

Step 1: Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by washing your hair with a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. This will help to remove any dirt and oil from your hair and provide a healthy foundation for styling.

Step 2: Towel Dry

After washing your hair, towel dry it gently to remove excess water. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair, such as a texturizing paste or pomade. Work the product through your hair with your fingers, focusing on the top and sides.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Using a blow dryer and a round brush, style your hair into textured layers. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create a messy, textured look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain and care for your hair:

1. Use the Right Products

Choose hair products that are suited for your hair type and style. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals or alcohol, as these can damage your hair.

2. Get Regular Haircuts

Visit your hairstylist regularly to keep your hair looking neat and well-maintained. A good haircut can make all the difference in how your hairstyle looks.

3. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as these can damage your hair. If you must use them, always use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

FAQ

Q: Can square face males pull off long hair?

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have oily hair or live in a humid environment, you may need to wash your hair more often. However, washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause damage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Q: What should I do if I have thinning hair?

A: If you have thinning hair, try using volumizing products and styles that add texture and volume to your hair. Avoid hairstyles that flatten your hair and emphasize the thinning areas.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and strong hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help to nourish your hair from the inside out. Include plenty of fresh fruits and vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats in your diet.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

3. Avoid Overstyling

Overstyling your hair with heat tools and chemicals can cause damage and breakage. Try to limit your use of these tools and opt for natural styles instead.
