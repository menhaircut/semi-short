Is My Hair Actually Wavy

Written by Frank Jul 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of wondering if your hair is actually wavy or just frizzy? Do you find yourself constantly searching for ways to tame your unruly locks? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with identifying their hair type, especially when it comes to wavy hair. In this article, we'll help you determine if your hair is actually wavy and provide you with tips on how to care for it.

Description

Wavy hair is often confused with frizzy or curly hair. However, wavy hair has a unique pattern that sets it apart from other hair types. Wavy hair is characterized by loose, flowing curls that move with the wind. It's not as tightly coiled as curly hair, but it's not as straight as straight hair. Wavy hair is somewhere in between.

Identifying your hair type is crucial to finding the right hair care products and styling techniques. Using the wrong products or styling techniques can lead to frizz, breakage, and overall hair damage. So, let's figure out if your hair is actually wavy.

How to Determine If Your Hair Is Wavy

The easiest way to determine if your hair is wavy is to look at your natural hair pattern. If your hair forms an "S" shape when it dries naturally, then you have wavy hair. You can also look at old photos to see if your hair had a natural wave to it when you were younger.

Another way to determine if your hair is wavy is to do the "pull-test." Simply take a strand of hair and gently pull it straight. If the strand has a gentle wave to it, then you have wavy hair. If the strand is completely straight, then you have straight hair. If the strand is tightly coiled, then you have curly hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Checking Your Hair Type

Here's a step-by-step guide to checking your hair type:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would.
  2. Let your hair air dry naturally without any products or styling tools.
  3. Look at your hair in the mirror. Does it form an "S" shape?
  4. If it does, then you have wavy hair. If it doesn't, then you may have straight or curly hair.
  5. If you're still unsure, take a strand of hair and gently pull it straight. Does it have a gentle wave to it?
  6. If it does, then you have wavy hair. If it's completely straight, then you have straight hair. If it's tightly coiled, then you have curly hair.

Tips for Caring for Wavy Hair

Now that you know you have wavy hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for caring for wavy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can lead to breakage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use a heat protectant spray before using them.
  • Experiment with different styling products, such as mousse or curl-defining creams, to find what works best for your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I still straighten my wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can still straighten your wavy hair. However, it's important to use a heat protectant spray and to not straighten your hair too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage.

Q: Can I use the same products for wavy hair as I do for curly hair?

A: No, wavy hair and curly hair have different needs. Wavy hair is typically less dry than curly hair, so you may not need as heavy of a moisturizing product. Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.
