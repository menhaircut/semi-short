How To Cut Thick Hair Thinner A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick hair, you may feel like it's unmanageable at times. It can be hard to style, and it can feel heavy and overwhelming. One solution is to cut your hair thinner. This can give your hair a lighter, more manageable feel, and it can make it easier to style. But how do you cut thick hair thinner? In this blog post, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to do just that.

How to Cut Thick Hair Thinner

To cut thick hair thinner, you'll need a few tools, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Thinners or a razor

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to wash and dry your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you're cutting it at the right length.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, section it off into manageable sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step 3: Cut Your Hair with Scissors

Using sharp scissors, start cutting small sections of your hair at a time. Cut at an angle to create texture and thin out your hair. Be sure to cut evenly and take your time to avoid making any mistakes.

Step 4: Use Thinners or a Razor

Once you've cut your hair with scissors, you can use thinners or a razor to further thin out your hair. This will create a more natural, textured look and help blend any harsh lines. Use the thinners or razor on the ends of your hair, taking care not to cut too much at once.

Step 5: Blend Your Hair

After you've used the thinners or razor, use your scissors to blend your hair. This will help create a natural look and ensure that your hair is evenly thinned out.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting and thinning your hair, style it as desired. You'll likely find that your hair is easier to style and manage now that it's thinner.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair Thinner

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting thick hair thinner:

  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process.
  • Use thinners or a razor sparingly to avoid cutting too much at once.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting thick hair thinner:

Can I cut my hair thinner at home?

Yes, you can cut your hair thinner at home. However, if you're not experienced at cutting hair, it's best to go to a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my hair thinner?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it thickens. As a general rule, you should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and thickness.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, be sure to take good care of it. This includes washing it regularly, using a good conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. Additionally, be sure to eat a healthy diet and get plenty of rest to promote healthy hair growth.

Conclusion

Cutting thick hair thinner can be a great way to make your hair more manageable and easier to style. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can achieve a natural, textured look that's perfect for any occasion. Just be sure to take your time, use the right tools, and take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
