Best Side Bangs For Round Faces The Ultimate Guide

Do you have a round face and want to try out side bangs, but not sure which one will be the best fit for you? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through the best side bangs for round faces, how to style them, and how to take care of them.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Best Side Bangs for Round Faces
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Side Bangs for Round Faces

Description

Side bangs are a great way to frame your face and add some dimension to your hairstyle. For round faces, the key is to create angles that will help elongate the face and draw attention away from the cheeks. The best side bangs for round faces are those that are longer and tapered on the ends, rather than blunt and straight across. They should also be angled to create a diagonal line that will add length to the face.

How to Choose the Best Side Bangs for Round Faces

When choosing the best side bangs for round faces, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Length: Longer side bangs will help elongate the face and draw attention away from the cheeks. They should be tapered on the ends to create a natural look.
  • Angle: The bangs should be angled to create a diagonal line that will add length to the face.
  • Texture: Textured side bangs will add depth and dimension to the hairstyle.

Some great options for side bangs for round faces include:

Long Side Swept Bangs

Long side swept bangs are a great option for round faces. They are longer and tapered on the ends, creating a natural look that will help to elongate the face. They should be angled to create a diagonal line that will add length to the face.

Textured Side Bangs

Textured side bangs are a great option for those who want to add some depth and dimension to their hairstyle. They can be layered and tousled to create a natural, effortless look.

Asymmetrical Side Bangs

Asymmetrical side bangs are a great option for those who want to add some edge to their hairstyle. They are longer on one side and shorter on the other, creating a diagonal line that will help to elongate the face.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Side Bangs for Round Faces

Here is a step-by-step guide to styling your side bangs:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Blow dry your hair, using a round brush to create volume and shape.
  3. Section off your side bangs and clip the rest of your hair out of the way.
  4. Use a straightener or curling iron to style your side bangs. For a natural look, curl them away from your face.
  5. Apply a small amount of styling product to your side bangs to keep them in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Side Bangs

Here are some tips for maintaining your side bangs:

  • Trim your bangs regularly to keep them looking fresh and avoid split ends.
  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid over-washing your bangs.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your bangs, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your bangs between washes.

FAQs

What if I have curly hair?

Curly hair can work great with side bangs, but it's important to choose the right length and texture. Longer, tapered side bangs can work well with curly hair, as can textured side bangs that are layered and tousled.

Can I wear my side bangs straight down?

Straight, blunt side bangs can be a bit harsh on round faces, as they can accentuate the cheeks. It's best to opt for longer, tapered side bangs that are angled to create a diagonal line.

Hair Care Tips for Side Bangs

Here are some hair care tips for side bangs:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much product on your side bangs, as this can weigh them down and make them look greasy.

With these tips and options, you can now confidently choose the best side bangs for your round face and maintain them with ease. Remember to always consult with your hairstylist for personalized advice and to get your hair trimmed regularly for a fresh look. Happy styling!
