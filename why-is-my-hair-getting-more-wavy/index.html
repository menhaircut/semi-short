Why Is My Hair Getting More Wavy Understanding The Science Behind It

Written by Oliver Nov 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever noticed that your hair is getting more wavy than it used to be? This can happen for a variety of reasons, including changes in your hormones, genetics, or even the products you use on your hair. In this article, we'll explore the science behind why your hair is getting more wavy and what you can do to embrace and care for your new texture.

Description

Wavy hair is a beautiful and unique texture that can be difficult to manage if you're used to straighter hair. If you've noticed that your hair is getting more wavy, you might be wondering why and what you can do to take care of it. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your hair might be getting wavier and provide tips for embracing and caring for your new texture.

How to

Step 1: Understand the Science

The first step in understanding why your hair is getting more wavy is to understand the science behind it. Your hair texture is determined by the shape of your hair follicles, which are round in straight hair and oval in wavy or curly hair. If your hair follicles change shape, your hair texture can change as well. Hormonal changes, genetics, and the products you use on your hair can all affect the shape of your hair follicles and make your hair more wavy.

Step 2: Embrace Your New Texture

Once you've accepted that your hair is getting more wavy, it's time to embrace your new texture. Wavy hair can be beautiful and unique, so don't be afraid to experiment with new hairstyles and products that are specifically designed for wavy hair. Look for products that enhance your natural waves and provide moisture and hydration to your hair.

Step 3: Care for Your Wavy Hair

Caring for wavy hair can be a bit different than caring for straight hair. Wavy hair tends to be more prone to frizz and dryness, so it's important to use products that provide moisture and hydration. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural waves.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your wavy hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra moisture to your hair.
  • Try using a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for your new texture.

FAQ

Why is my hair getting more wavy?

Your hair can get more wavy due to hormonal changes, genetics, or the products you use on your hair. If your hair follicles change shape, your hair texture can change as well.

Is it normal for hair to change texture?

Yes, it's normal for hair to change texture over time due to hormonal changes, aging, or other factors.

What products should I use for wavy hair?

You should look for products that enhance your natural waves and provide moisture and hydration to your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for wavy or curly hair.
