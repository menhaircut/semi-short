How To Know Your Natural Hair Type A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Understanding your natural hair type is essential for choosing the right hair products and styling techniques. However, many people are unsure of their hair type and how to properly care for it. In this article, we will guide you through the process of determining your natural hair type and provide tips on how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Curl Pattern
  • Step 2: Assess Your Hair Density
  • Step 3: Evaluate Your Hair Porosity
  • Tips for Caring for Your Natural Hair
  • FAQs

Step 1: Determine Your Curl Pattern

The first step in determining your natural hair type is to figure out your curl pattern. There are four main types of curl patterns:

  • Type 1: Straight hair
  • Type 2: Wavy hair
  • Type 3: Curly hair
  • Type 4: Coily hair

To determine your curl pattern, examine your hair when it is wet and free of any products. If your hair is straight, you have Type 1 hair. If your hair has a slight "S" shape, you have Type 2 hair. If your hair has a defined "S" or "Z" shape, you have Type 3 hair. If your hair has a tight, corkscrew shape, you have Type 4 hair.

Step 2: Assess Your Hair Density

The second step in determining your natural hair type is to assess your hair density. Hair density refers to the amount of hair you have on your head. There are three main types of hair density:

  • Low density: Thin hair with visible scalp
  • Medium density: Average amount of hair
  • High density: Thick hair with little to no visible scalp

To assess your hair density, separate your hair into small sections and look at your scalp. If you can easily see your scalp, you have low density hair. If you can see some scalp, but it is not easily visible, you have medium density hair. If you cannot see your scalp at all, you have high density hair.

Step 3: Evaluate Your Hair Porosity

The final step in determining your natural hair type is to evaluate your hair porosity. Hair porosity refers to how well your hair can absorb and retain moisture. There are three main types of hair porosity:

  • Low porosity: Hair that is resistant to moisture
  • Medium porosity: Hair that can absorb and retain moisture easily
  • High porosity: Hair that absorbs moisture quickly but loses it just as fast

To evaluate your hair porosity, take a strand of your hair and place it in a glass of water. If the hair stays afloat, you have low porosity hair. If the hair sinks slowly, you have medium porosity hair. If the hair sinks quickly, you have high porosity hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Natural Hair

Now that you know your natural hair type, it's important to know how to care for it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Choose hair products specifically designed for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools frequently
  • Protect your hair at night by using a silk or satin pillowcase
  • Deep condition your hair regularly
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as relaxers or color treatments

FAQs

What is the best way to care for Type 4 hair?

Type 4 hair is very delicate and requires extra care. It's important to keep your hair moisturized, so use a leave-in conditioner daily. Avoid using hair brushes and instead use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Protective hairstyles, such as braids or twists, can also help prevent breakage.

Can I change my natural hair type?

No, your natural hair type is determined by genetics and cannot be changed. However, you can alter the appearance of your hair by using styling techniques or chemical treatments.

How often should I wash my natural hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with Type 4 hair prefer to wash their hair once a week, while others may wash their hair every few days. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and follow up with a moisturizing conditioner.

What is the best way to style Type 2 hair?

Type 2 hair looks great when styled in loose waves or curls. Use a curl-enhancing product and diffuse your hair with a blow dryer for best results. You can also braid your hair overnight for natural-looking waves.

By following these steps, you can determine your natural hair type and care for it properly. Remember to embrace your natural hair and celebrate its unique beauty!
