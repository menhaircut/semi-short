How To Keep A Newborn Hair From Falling Out Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Oct 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcoming a newborn baby into the world is an exciting and overwhelming experience for new parents. While you may be thrilled to have your little one in your arms, it’s normal to worry about their health and development, including their hair. It’s common for newborns to experience hair loss in the first few months of life. In this blog post, we’ll share some tips and tricks on how to keep your newborn’s hair from falling out.

How to Keep a Newborn Hair from Falling Out: The Basics

Before we dive into the specifics, it’s important to understand why newborns experience hair loss. Newborns are born with a fine, downy layer of hair called lanugo that covers their body. This hair is shed in the womb and replaced with vellus hair, which is soft and fine. Over time, vellus hair is replaced with terminal hair, which is thicker and coarser. However, this process takes time, and in the meantime, your baby may experience some hair loss.

Here are some basic tips on how to keep a newborn hair from falling out:

1. Be Gentle

Newborn hair is delicate, and rough handling can cause it to fall out. Be gentle when washing your baby’s hair, and use a soft baby brush or comb to detangle it. Avoid pulling or tugging on the hair, as this can cause breakage and hair loss.

2. Don’t Overwash

While it’s important to keep your baby clean, overwashing can strip their hair of its natural oils and cause it to fall out. Aim to wash your baby’s hair every 2-3 days, or as needed if they have a particularly messy diaper or spit-up.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles like ponytails or braids, as these can pull on the hair and cause breakage. Stick to loose hairstyles or simply let your baby’s hair be.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Keep a Newborn Hair from Falling Out

Now that you know the basics, here’s a step-by-step guide on how to keep your newborn’s hair from falling out:

Step 1: Wash Your Baby’s Hair

Start by washing your baby’s hair with a gentle baby shampoo. Use warm water and a soft washcloth to lather the shampoo into their hair, being careful not to tug or pull on the hair. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Dry Gently

After washing, gently pat your baby’s hair dry with a soft towel. Avoid rubbing or tugging on the hair, as this can cause breakage.

Step 3: Brush or Comb

Use a soft baby brush or comb to detangle your baby’s hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or tug on the hair.

Step 4: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

As mentioned earlier, avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair and cause breakage. Stick to loose hairstyles or simply let your baby’s hair be.

Tips for Keeping Your Baby’s Hair Healthy and Strong

While hair loss is a normal part of newborn development, there are things you can do to keep your baby’s hair healthy and strong:

1. Provide Proper Nutrition

A well-balanced diet is important for your baby’s overall health, including their hair. Make sure your baby is getting all the necessary nutrients for healthy hair growth, including protein, iron, and vitamin D.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle, baby-specific hair products that are free from harsh chemicals and fragrances. Look for products that are specifically formulated for newborns and infants.

3. Protect Your Baby’s Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your baby’s hair, so it’s important to protect their head from the sun. Use a hat or other protective head covering when spending time outdoors.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is it normal for newborns to lose their hair?

A: Yes, it’s normal for newborns to experience some hair loss in the first few months of life.

Q: Will my baby’s hair grow back?

A: Yes, your baby’s hair will grow back over time. Be patient and continue to care for their hair gently.

Q: Can I use adult hair products on my baby’s hair?

A: No, adult hair products are not suitable for babies and can be too harsh for their delicate hair and scalp. Use products specifically formulated for newborns and infants.

Hair Care for Newborns: Final Thoughts

Caring for a newborn’s hair can be overwhelming, but with these tips and tricks, you can help keep their hair healthy and strong. Remember to be gentle, avoid tight hairstyles, and use gentle, baby-specific hair products. With time, your baby’s hair will grow back and become thicker and stronger.
