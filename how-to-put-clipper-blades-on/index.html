How To Put Clipper Blades On A Step By Step Guide

Clipper blades are an essential tool for anyone who wants to maintain their hair. However, if you are new to using clippers, putting blades on can be a bit tricky. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to put clipper blades on. We will also provide some tips and answer some frequently asked questions to help you get the most out of your clippers.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Clipper Blades On
  • Step-By-Step Guide
  • Tips for Putting Clipper Blades On

How to Put Clipper Blades On

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's take a quick look at what clipper blades are and why they are important. Clipper blades are the metal pieces that sit at the bottom of the clipper. They are responsible for cutting the hair and come in different sizes to accommodate different hair lengths.

Now, let's get started with putting clipper blades on.

Step-By-Step Guide

Step 1: Turn off your clipper and remove any attachments that may be on it.

Step 2: Hold the clipper with the blade facing up.

Step 3: Take the blade and align the plastic piece on the blade with the metal notch on the clipper.

Step 4: Once aligned, gently press down on the blade until you hear a click. This means that the blade is locked in place.

Step 5: Turn on your clipper and test the blade to make sure it is cutting properly.

Tips for Putting Clipper Blades On

Tip 1: Make sure the clipper is turned off before attempting to put blades on.

Tip 2: Take your time when putting blades on to ensure they are properly aligned and locked in place.

Tip 3: If you are having trouble getting the blade to click into place, try applying a bit of pressure with your hand while pressing down on the blade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use any type of blade on my clipper?

A: No, you cannot use any type of blade on your clipper. You must use blades that are specifically designed for your clipper model.

Q: How often should I change my clipper blades?

A: It is recommended that you change your clipper blades every 6-8 months or every 200 haircuts.

Hair Care with Clippers

Using clippers is a great way to maintain your hair, but it is important to take care of your clippers and your hair to get the best results. Here are a few tips for hair care with clippers:

Tip 1: Clean your clipper blades after each use to prevent bacteria buildup.

Tip 2: Oil your clipper blades regularly to keep them running smoothly.

Tip 3: Use a comb to help guide the clippers through your hair and prevent uneven cuts.

By following these tips and using our step-by-step guide, you should be able to put clipper blades on with ease. Remember to take your time and be gentle when handling your clippers to ensure they last for years to come.
