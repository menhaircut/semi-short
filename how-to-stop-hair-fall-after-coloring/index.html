How To Stop Hair Fall After Coloring Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jul 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Coloring your hair can be a great way to change up your look and express your personal style. But if you've noticed that your hair is falling out more than usual after coloring, you're not alone. Hair fall after coloring is a common problem that many people experience. In this article, we'll show you how to stop hair fall after coloring with some helpful tips and tricks.

  • How to Stop Hair Fall after Coloring
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Stop Hair Fall after Coloring

If you're experiencing hair fall after coloring, there are a few things you can do to help prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

After coloring your hair, it's important to use a gentle shampoo that won't strip the color or damage your hair. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for color-treated hair and are sulfate-free. Sulfates can be harsh on your hair and scalp, causing dryness and irritation.

2. Avoid Hot Styling Tools

Hot styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers can damage your hair and cause it to fall out. If possible, avoid using these tools altogether. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and set them to a low or medium heat setting.

3. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Coloring your hair can dry it out, making it more prone to breakage and hair fall. To combat this, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Look for treatments that contain natural oils like coconut or argan oil.

4. Don't Overdo It

It's important to give your hair a break between coloring treatments. If you're coloring your hair every few weeks, you're putting a lot of stress on it, which can lead to hair fall. Try to space out your coloring treatments as much as possible, and give your hair time to recover between treatments.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you stop hair fall after coloring:

Step 1: Use a gentle shampoo

Choose a shampoo that's designed for color-treated hair and is sulfate-free. Massage it gently into your scalp and hair, and rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 2: Use a deep conditioning treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair once a week. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Step 3: Avoid hot styling tools

Avoid using hot styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and set them to a low or medium heat setting.

Step 4: Don't overdo it

Don't color your hair too frequently. Space out your coloring treatments as much as possible, and give your hair time to recover between treatments.

Tips for Preventing Hair Fall after Coloring

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair fall after coloring:

1. Choose a lighter color

Dark hair colors can be more damaging to your hair than lighter colors. If you're experiencing hair fall after coloring, try switching to a lighter color to see if it helps.

2. Use a hair mask

In addition to using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, try using a hair mask once a month. Hair masks can help repair and strengthen your hair, preventing hair fall.

3. Eat a healthy diet

Eating a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure you're getting enough protein, iron, and vitamin B12, as these are all important for healthy hair.

4. Don't brush your hair when it's wet

Wet hair is more prone to breakage than dry hair. To prevent hair fall, avoid brushing your hair when it's wet. Instead, use a wide-toothed comb to gently detangle your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can coloring your hair cause hair fall?

A: Yes, coloring your hair can cause hair fall. The chemicals in hair color can damage your hair, making it more prone to breakage and hair fall.

Q: How long should I wait between coloring treatments?

A: It's best to wait at least four to six weeks between coloring treatments to give your hair time to recover.

Q: Should I use a hair serum?

A: Yes, using a hair serum can help protect your hair from damage and prevent hair fall. Look for serums that contain natural oils like argan oil or jojoba oil.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips to help you maintain healthy, beautiful hair:

1. Trim your hair regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, keeping your hair healthy and strong.

2. Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can put stress on your hair and scalp, causing hair fall. If you must wear your hair in a tight style, try to loosen it up as much as possible.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can be damaging to your hair, causing dryness and breakage. If you're going to be spending time in the sun, wear a hat or use a hair product that contains SPF.

4. Be gentle with your hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and be gentle when brushing and styling it. Treat your hair with care, and it will stay healthy and beautiful.

By following these tips and tricks, you can stop hair fall after coloring and maintain healthy, beautiful hair. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid harsh chemicals and hot styling tools, and give your hair time to recover between coloring treatments.
