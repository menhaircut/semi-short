How To Cut Long Naturally Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours detangling and styling your long, curly locks? It may be time for a haircut! But cutting curly hair can be intimidating, especially if you're used to straight hair. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to cut long naturally curly hair, step by step.

Table of Contents

  2. How to Cut Long Naturally Curly Hair
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips

Description

Long naturally curly hair is beautiful, but it can also be challenging to maintain. Over time, your curls can become weighed down and lose their shape. A haircut can help revive your curls and give them new life. The key is to find a stylist who is experienced with cutting curly hair, or to learn how to do it yourself.

How to Cut Long Naturally Curly Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies:

  • Sharp hair-cutting scissors
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have your supplies, follow these steps:

Step by Step

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help remove any product buildup and make your curls easier to detangle.
  2. Detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be gentle to avoid damaging your curls.
  3. Part your hair in the middle or on the side, whichever you prefer.
  4. Take a small section of hair at the front of your head, and twist it tightly. This will be your guide for the length of your haircut.
  5. Using your hair-cutting scissors, cut the twisted section of hair to your desired length. Be sure to cut in a straight line, following the twist.
  6. Repeat steps 4 and 5 on the other side of your head.
  7. Now, it's time to trim the rest of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back of your head, and twist it tightly. Use the twist as your guide, and cut the hair to the same length as the front sections.
  8. Continue twisting and cutting small sections of hair, working your way around your head. Be sure to check your work in a mirror as you go.
  9. When you're finished, use a wide-tooth comb to gently comb out your curls. This will help create more volume and shape.
  10. Style your hair as desired, and enjoy your new haircut!

Tips

  • It's always a good idea to start with a small trim, and work your way up to a bigger cut if you're not sure how much you want to take off.
  • Be sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as curly hair can shrink up to an inch or more when wet.
  • If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, consider finding a stylist who specializes in curly hair. They can help you achieve the look you want without risking damage to your curls.
  • Regular trims every 6-8 weeks can help keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Can I cut curly hair with regular scissors?

No, it's important to use sharp hair-cutting scissors to avoid damaging your hair. Regular scissors can create split ends, which can lead to breakage and frizz.

How much should I cut off?

This depends on your personal preference and the current state of your hair. If your curls are weighed down and losing their shape, a shorter cut may help revive them. If you just want to remove split ends and maintain your length, a small trim may be all you need.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you're not happy with your cut, consider finding a stylist who can help you fix it.

Hair Care

Caring for your curly hair after a haircut is just as important as the cut itself. Here are some tips to keep your curls looking their best:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to damp hair to help define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle.
  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent tangles.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your long naturally curly hair at home. Remember to be patient, take your time, and have fun with it!
