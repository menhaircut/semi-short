Best Haircut For Oval Face Over 60 How To Choose

Written by Oliver Aug 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As we get older, our hair tends to change in texture and thickness. This means that the haircut we used to have may no longer be the best fit for us. If you're over 60 and have an oval face shape, it's important to find a haircut that flatters your features and brings out your best qualities. Here, we'll discuss some tips on how to choose the best haircut for oval face over 60, and some popular styles that you may want to try.

How to Choose the Best Haircut for Oval Face Over 60

When it comes to choosing the best haircut for oval face over 60, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, consider your hair texture and thickness. If you have thinning hair, you may want to opt for a shorter, layered cut that adds volume and movement. If you have thick hair, you may want to go for a longer, layered cut that thins out your hair and makes it more manageable.

Next, consider your face shape. If you have an oval face, you're in luck! Oval faces are considered the most versatile face shape and can pull off a variety of haircuts. However, you'll still want to choose a haircut that flatters your features and balances out your face shape. For example, if you have a longer face, you may want to avoid a long, straight cut that elongates your face even more. Instead, opt for a layered cut with volume at the top and sides to balance out your face shape.

Finally, consider your personal style and preferences. Do you prefer a low-maintenance haircut that you can wash and go, or do you enjoy styling your hair every day? Do you like a classic, timeless look, or do you prefer something more edgy and modern? These factors will also play a role in choosing the best haircut for oval face over 60.

Popular Haircuts for Oval Face Over 60

The Pixie Cut

The pixie cut is a classic, low-maintenance haircut that works well for oval face shapes. It's short and cropped, which means it won't weigh down your hair or make it look flat. Plus, it's easy to style and can be worn with or without bangs.

The Bob Cut

The bob cut is another popular haircut for oval face over 60. It's a versatile cut that can be customized to suit your hair texture and thickness. For thinning hair, a short, layered bob can add volume and movement. For thicker hair, a longer, one-length bob can thin out your hair and make it more manageable.

The Shag Cut

The shag cut is a layered haircut that adds volume and texture to your hair. It's a great option for oval face shapes because it adds width to your face and balances out your features. Plus, it's a low-maintenance cut that can be styled with minimal effort.

Tips for Maintaining Your Haircut

Once you've chosen the best haircut for oval face over 60, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips to help you care for your new haircut:

  • Visit your hairstylist regularly for trims and touch-ups
  • Use high-quality hair products that nourish and protect your hair
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, which can damage your hair

FAQ About Best Haircut for Oval Face Over 60

1. Can I still wear long hair if I have an oval face over 60?

Yes, you can! However, you may want to avoid a straight, one-length cut that can elongate your face even more. Instead, opt for a layered cut with volume at the top and sides to balance out your features.

2. What's the difference between a bob and a lob?

A bob is a short, chin-length haircut, while a lob is a longer, shoulder-length haircut. Both cuts can be customized to suit your hair texture and thickness.

3. How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every day. If you have dry hair, you may only need to wash it once or twice a week.

Conclusion

Choosing the best haircut for oval face over 60 can be a fun and exciting process. By considering your hair texture, face shape, and personal style, you can find a haircut that flatters your features and brings out your best qualities. Whether you opt for a pixie cut, bob cut, or shag cut, be sure to maintain your haircut with regular trims and high-quality hair products.
