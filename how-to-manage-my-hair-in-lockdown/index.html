How To Manage My Hair In Lockdown Tips And Tricks

Written by William Sep 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're struggling to manage your hair during the lockdown, you're not alone. With salons closed and limited access to hair care products, it's easy to feel overwhelmed. However, there are plenty of ways to keep your hair healthy and looking great, even while you're stuck at home. In this post, we'll share our top tips and tricks for managing your hair during the lockdown, from DIY haircuts to simple styling hacks.

How to Manage Your Hair in Lockdown

First things first, it's important to establish a hair care routine that works for you. This can include washing your hair regularly, using a good conditioner, and avoiding heat styling as much as possible. If you're struggling with dry or damaged hair, consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to restore moisture and prevent breakage.

When it comes to styling your hair, there are plenty of easy options that don't require any special tools or products. One of our favorites is the messy bun, which can be dressed up or down depending on the occasion. Simply pull your hair back into a loose ponytail, twist it into a bun, and secure with bobby pins. For a more polished look, try a sleek top knot or low bun.

If you're feeling adventurous, you might even consider giving yourself a haircut or trim. While we don't recommend attempting anything too drastic, a simple trim can help keep split ends at bay and give your hair a healthier, more polished look. Just be sure to use sharp scissors and take your time, working in small sections to avoid any mistakes.

Step-by-Step Guide to DIY Haircuts

If you're ready to take the plunge and give yourself a haircut, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb out any tangles and make sure your hair is evenly distributed.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut. This could be just the ends, or a specific area like your bangs.
  3. Use sharp scissors to trim the hair, working in small sections. Remember to cut less than you think you need, as hair tends to bounce back once it's dried.
  4. Check your work in a mirror, making any necessary adjustments. Remember, it's always better to cut too little than too much!

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Aside from regular washing and conditioning, there are a few things you can do to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Avoid using hot tools like hair straighteners and curling irons as much as possible. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase, which can help prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet that includes plenty of protein and vitamins. This can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss.

FAQs About Hair Care in Lockdown

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: While it is possible to dye your hair at home, we recommend against it unless you have experience with hair dye. Improper application can result in uneven color or even damage to your hair.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preferences. Generally, washing your hair every other day or every few days is sufficient for most people.

Q: What can I do about split ends?

A: The best way to prevent split ends is to regularly trim your hair. If you already have split ends, you can use a split end treatment or oil to help smooth the hair and prevent further damage.

Q: How can I make my hair grow faster?

A: While there are no magic solutions for faster hair growth, you can help promote healthy hair growth by eating a healthy diet, taking vitamins like biotin and folic acid, and avoiding heat styling and chemical treatments as much as possible.

Managing your hair in lockdown doesn't have to be a daunting task. With a little bit of effort and creativity, you can keep your hair looking healthy and stylish until salons reopen. Whether you're trying out a new DIY haircut or simply mastering the art of the messy bun, we hope these tips and tricks will help you feel more confident and in control of your hair care routine.
