The Reason Of Excessive Hair Fall Understanding The Root Cause

Written by Eveline Oct 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Experiencing hair fall can be distressing, especially when it becomes excessive. Hair fall can be caused by a variety of factors, ranging from genetics to lifestyle choices. Understanding the root cause of excessive hair fall is crucial in finding the most effective solution. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind excessive hair fall and provide tips on how to prevent it.

What is the Reason of Excessive Hair Fall?

There are several reasons why you may be experiencing excessive hair fall. Some of the most common reasons include:

1. Genetics

If your family has a history of baldness or hair thinning, it is likely that you are genetically predisposed to it. This type of hair fall is often gradual and may start as early as your twenties.

2. Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes, such as those experienced during pregnancy or menopause, can trigger hair fall. Hormonal imbalances caused by thyroid disorders or polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) can also contribute to excessive hair fall.

3. Stress

Stress can cause hair fall by disrupting the hair growth cycle. High levels of stress can trigger the hair follicles to enter the resting phase, leading to hair fall.

4. Nutritional Deficiencies

A lack of essential vitamins and minerals, such as iron, zinc, and vitamin D, can lead to hair fall. These nutrients are important for healthy hair growth and maintenance.

5. Hairstyling Practices

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can pull on the hair follicles and cause hair fall. Chemical treatments, such as hair coloring and perming, can also damage the hair and lead to hair fall.

How to Prevent Excessive Hair Fall?

Preventing excessive hair fall requires a multi-faceted approach. Here are some tips to help prevent hair fall:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help maintain healthy hair. Include foods such as leafy greens, eggs, nuts, and fish in your diet.

2. Reduce Stress

Managing stress through activities such as meditation and yoga can help prevent hair fall caused by stress.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid hairstyles that pull on the hair, such as tight braids and ponytails. Opt for looser hairstyles instead.

4. Use Gentle Hair Products

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair. Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.

5. Seek Professional Help

If your hair fall persists, seek the help of a professional. A dermatologist or trichologist can help diagnose the cause of your hair fall and provide treatment options.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is hair fall normal?

A: Yes, it is normal to lose up to 100 hairs per day. However, if you are experiencing excessive hair fall, it may be a cause for concern.

Q: Can hair fall be reversed?

A: Depending on the cause of your hair fall, it may be reversible. However, some types of hair fall, such as genetic hair loss, may not be reversible.

Q: Can hair fall be caused by medications?

A: Yes, certain medications, such as chemotherapy drugs and blood thinners, can cause hair fall. Speak to your doctor if you are experiencing hair fall as a result of medication.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to help maintain healthy hair:

1. Avoid Overwashing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and hair fall. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using brushes on wet hair, as it can lead to breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.

3. Use Heat Protectant Spray

If you use heat styling tools, such as a flat iron or curling iron, make sure to use a heat protectant spray. This can help prevent heat damage and hair fall.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair every six to eight weeks can help prevent split ends and breakage.

By understanding the reasons behind excessive hair fall and taking preventative measures, you can maintain healthy, luscious locks for years to come.
