How To Cut Interior Layers In Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a way to add volume and movement to your hair without losing length? Cutting interior layers can be a great solution. With the right technique and tools, you can achieve a chic and trendy look that complements your face shape and hair texture. In this blog post, we'll show you how to cut interior layers in hair in a relaxed English language that's easy to follow. Read on to learn more.

What are Interior Layers?

Interior layers, also known as invisible layers, are sections of hair that are cut shorter than the outer layers. They are typically located underneath the top layer of hair and create volume and texture without altering the overall length. Interior layers can be cut in various lengths and angles, depending on the desired effect.

How to Cut Interior Layers in Hair

Cutting interior layers is a technique that requires some skill and practice. If you're not confident in your abilities, it's best to visit a professional hairstylist. However, if you're up for the challenge, here's how to cut interior layers in hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Make sure it's free of tangles and knots.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into three sections: left, right, and back. Clip each section with a hairpin to keep them separate.

Step 3: Decide on the Length and Angle of the Layers

Decide on the length and angle of the layers you want to create. For a subtle effect, cut the layers only a few inches shorter than the outer layer. For a more dramatic effect, cut the layers at a steeper angle.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and use hair cutting scissors to cut the hair at the desired length and angle. Repeat this process for each section of hair, making sure to cut the hair evenly and at the same angle.

Step 5: Move to the Side Sections

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from the left or right side, depending on which one you're working on, and repeat the cutting process. Make sure the layers are even on both sides.

Step 6: Check for Balance and Adjustments

After you've finished cutting all the sections, check for balance and make any necessary adjustments. Use a mirror to check the back of your head and ensure that the layers are even on both sides.

Tips for Cutting Interior Layers in Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting interior layers in hair:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once to avoid making mistakes.
  • Use a comb to guide your cutting and make sure the layers are even.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated.

FAQs about Cutting Interior Layers in Hair

1. Can I cut interior layers by myself?

While it's possible to cut interior layers by yourself, it's not recommended unless you have experience and confidence in your abilities. It's best to visit a professional hairstylist to ensure a good result.

2. How often should I cut interior layers?

You can cut interior layers as often as you like, depending on how quickly your hair grows and how often you want to refresh your hairstyle. Typically, every 6 to 8 weeks is a good timeframe.

3. Can I cut interior layers on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut interior layers on curly hair. However, it's important to use a dry-cutting technique to avoid disrupting the natural curl pattern. It's best to visit a hairstylist who specializes in cutting curly hair.

Hair Care after Cutting Interior Layers

After cutting interior layers in hair, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray when you do.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your layers.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair looking and feeling great after cutting interior layers.

Conclusion

Cutting interior layers in hair is a great way to add volume and movement to your hair without losing length. With the right technique and tools, you can achieve a trendy and stylish look that complements your face shape and hair texture. Remember to take your time, follow the steps carefully, and seek professional help if you're not confident in your abilities. Happy cutting!
