How To Thin Out Thick Hair Without Thinning Scissors

Written by Oliver Oct 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Thick hair is a blessing, but it can also be a curse. It can take hours to dry, and styling can be a nightmare. One way to make thick hair more manageable is to thin it out. However, not everyone has access to thinning scissors or wants to use them. Fortunately, there are other ways to thin out your hair without thinning scissors. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about thinning out thick hair without thinning scissors.

Description

Thick hair can be difficult to manage, but thinning it out can make it more manageable. Thinning scissors are one way to thin out hair, but they are not the only way. There are other ways to thin out hair that do not require thinning scissors. In this article, we will cover how to thin out thick hair without thinning scissors.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair Without Thinning Scissors

There are several ways to thin out thick hair without thinning scissors:

1. Razor Cutting

Razor cutting is a method of thinning out hair using a straight razor. This method is best done by a professional hairdresser, but it can also be done at home with care. To razor cut your hair at home, you will need a sharp straight razor and a comb. Start by sectioning your hair and combing each section straight. Then, use the razor to cut the hair at an angle. This will create a tapered effect and thin out the hair.

2. Texturizing Shears

Texturizing shears are scissors with teeth that are designed to thin out hair. They work by cutting some hairs while leaving others untouched. This creates a natural-looking, textured effect. To use texturizing shears, section your hair and comb each section straight. Then, use the shears to cut the hair at an angle. This will thin out the hair without removing too much length.

3. Thinning Comb

A thinning comb is a comb with teeth that are spaced farther apart than a regular comb. This makes it easier to remove some hair while leaving other hair untouched. To use a thinning comb, section your hair and comb each section straight. Then, use the comb to remove some hair by combing through the section from the root to the tip.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin out thick hair without thinning scissors:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to comb and section.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with.

Step 3: Comb Each Section Straight

Using a regular comb, comb each section straight. This will make it easier to see which hairs need to be thinned out.

Step 4: Choose Your Method

Choose the method you want to use to thin out your hair (razor cutting, texturizing shears, or thinning comb).

Step 5: Thin Out Your Hair

Using your chosen method, thin out your hair by removing some hairs while leaving others untouched. Be careful not to remove too much hair at once.

Step 6: Blend Your Hair

Blend your hair by combing through it with a regular comb. This will ensure that the thinned-out sections blend in with the rest of your hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out thick hair:

  • Start by thinning out a small section of hair to see how it looks before moving on to the rest of your hair.
  • Be careful not to remove too much hair at once, as this can lead to uneven results.
  • If you are not confident in your ability to thin out your hair, it is best to seek the help of a professional hairdresser.
  • Be sure to use sharp tools to ensure a clean cut.
  • Always start with dry hair, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with.

FAQ

Can I thin out my hair with regular scissors?

You can use regular scissors to thin out your hair, but it is not recommended. Regular scissors can create a blunt cut, which can look uneven and unnatural.

How often should I thin out my hair?

The frequency with which you should thin out your hair depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, it is best to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

Thinning out your hair will not necessarily make it look thinner. It will simply make it more manageable by removing some of the bulk.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable, but it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can cause breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Thinning out thick hair can be a great way to make it more manageable, and there are several methods you can use to do so without thinning scissors. Whether you choose razor cutting, texturizing shears, or a thinning comb, be sure to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
