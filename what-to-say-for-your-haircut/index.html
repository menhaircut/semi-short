What To Say For Your Haircut A Relaxing Guide In 2023

Written by Christine Nov 19, 2023 · 4 min read

Getting a haircut can be a daunting experience, especially if you're not sure what to say to your hairdresser or barber. In this guide, we'll provide you with all the information you need to confidently communicate with your stylist and get the haircut you want. From describing the length you want to choosing a style that suits you, we've got you covered. Read on for our step-by-step guide on what to say for your haircut.

Description

When you arrive at the salon or barbershop, the first thing you should do is describe the hairstyle you want. This includes the length, style, and any specific details you want to include. Here are some keywords you can use to describe your ideal haircut:

  • Short
  • Medium
  • Long
  • Cropped
  • Layered
  • Buzzed
  • Tapered
  • Side-swept
  • Textured

Use these keywords to construct a simple sentence that describes your ideal haircut. For example, "I want a short, layered cut with a side-swept fringe." This will give your stylist a clear idea of what you want and help them create the perfect look for you.

How to

Once you've described the basic style you want, your stylist may ask you some follow-up questions to clarify your preferences. Here are some common questions you might hear:

  • How short do you want it?
  • Do you want any layers?
  • How would you like your fringe?
  • Do you want any texture or volume?
  • Would you like any specific details, such as a side part or undercut?

Answer these questions as clearly and honestly as possible. If you're unsure about any aspect of the cut, don't be afraid to ask your stylist for their advice. They're experts in their field and can provide valuable insights into what will work best for your hair type and face shape.

Step-by-Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to say for your haircut:

  1. Describe the basic style you want using keywords such as short, medium, or long.
  2. Add any specific details you want, such as layers, texture, or volume.
  3. Answer any follow-up questions your stylist may have, such as how short you want it or whether you want a side part.
  4. Ask for your stylist's advice if you're unsure about any aspect of the cut.
  5. Once your stylist has a clear idea of what you want, they'll start cutting your hair.
  6. Check in with them throughout the process to make sure you're happy with how it's going.
  7. Once the cut is complete, your stylist will style your hair and show you the finished result.
  8. If you're happy with the cut, thank your stylist and make another appointment for your next cut.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect haircut:

  • Bring a picture of the style you want to help your stylist visualize your ideal cut.
  • Be honest about your hair type and any concerns you have, such as thinning or split ends.
  • Communicate clearly and confidently with your stylist to avoid any miscommunications.
  • Trust your stylist's expertise and be open to their advice.
  • Don't be afraid to try something new – a new haircut can be a great way to refresh your look.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about what to say for your haircut:

What if I'm not sure what style I want?

If you're unsure about what style you want, ask your stylist for their advice. They can recommend styles that will suit your face shape and hair type.

What if I don't like the cut?

If you're unhappy with the cut, let your stylist know. They can make adjustments to the style to better suit your preferences.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and the style you want. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Once you've got your new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth and health.

By following these tips, you can maintain your new haircut and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

Now that you know what to say for your haircut, you can confidently communicate with your stylist and get the perfect cut for you. Remember to be clear and honest about your preferences, trust your stylist's expertise, and take care of your hair to keep it looking its best.
