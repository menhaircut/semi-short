How Can I Trim The Back Of My Own Hair

Written by Aprilia Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Trimming the back of your hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're doing it for the first time. However, with a little bit of practice and patience, you can easily trim the back of your hair at home. In this article, we'll walk you through a step-by-step guide on how to trim the back of your own hair.

Description

Trimming the back of your own hair is an essential skill, especially if you want to maintain your hairstyle at home. Whether you have long hair or short hair, trimming the back can help you achieve a polished and neat look without the need for frequent salon visits.

How to Trim the Back of Your Own Hair

Before we jump into the step-by-step guide, here are a few things you'll need:

  • A pair of sharp haircutting scissors
  • A handheld mirror
  • A flat surface, like a table or countertop, to place the mirror on
  • A hairbrush or comb

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start trimming.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Using a hairbrush or comb, comb your hair straight back from your forehead. Make sure your hair is smooth and tangle-free.

Step 3: Set Up Your Mirror

Place your handheld mirror on a flat surface, facing the larger mirror in your bathroom. Stand in front of the larger mirror and hold the handheld mirror up to the back of your head. Adjust the angle of the mirror until you can see the back of your head clearly.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into two parts: the top section and the bottom section. Clip the top section of your hair on top of your head.

Step 5: Trim the Bottom Section

Using your scissors, trim the bottom section of your hair. Take small sections of hair and trim them gradually. Start from the center and work your way towards the sides.

Step 6: Unclip the Top Section

Unclip the top section of your hair and comb it straight back.

Step 7: Blend the Top and Bottom Sections

Using your scissors, blend the top and bottom sections of your hair. Take small sections of hair and trim them gradually, making sure the top and bottom sections blend seamlessly.

Step 8: Check Your Work

Use your handheld mirror to check the back of your head. Make sure the hair is even and there are no uneven spots.

Step 9: Clean Up

Brush off any loose hair from your clothes and the floor. Clean your scissors thoroughly and put them away.

Tips

Here are a few tips to make trimming the back of your own hair easier:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up, as you can always trim more hair but you can't put it back once it's cut.
  • Use a smaller scissor to trim the hair around the neck area.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.
  • If you're not confident, ask a friend or family member to help you.

FAQ

Can I use regular scissors to trim my hair?

No. It's important to use sharp haircutting scissors to avoid split ends and uneven cuts.

Do I need to wet my hair before trimming it?

No. You can trim your hair when it's dry.

How often should I trim the back of my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, it's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care

Trimming the back of your own hair is just one aspect of hair care. To keep your hair healthy and shiny, make sure to:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using hot tools, like hair straighteners and curling irons, too often
  • Eat a balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals

By following these tips, you can maintain healthy, shiny, and well-trimmed hair at home.
