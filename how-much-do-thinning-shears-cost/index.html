How Much Do Thinning Shears Cost

Written by Luffy Jun 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Thinning shears are an essential tool for any hairstylist or barber. They are used for texturizing hair, removing bulk, and creating layers. However, if you're new to hairdressing, you may be wondering how much do thinning shears cost? In this article, we'll discuss the price range of thinning shears, factors that affect the cost, and tips on how to choose the right one for your needs.

Factors That Affect Thinning Shears Cost

The cost of thinning shears can vary depending on several factors:

Brand

The brand of thinning shears can affect the cost. Popular brands like Joewell, Kamisori, and Kasho may be pricier than lesser-known brands. However, it's important to remember that a higher price doesn't always guarantee better quality.

Material

The material used to make the thinning shears can also affect the price. Shears made from high-quality steel or titanium are typically more expensive than those made from stainless steel or other materials.

Number of Teeth

The number of teeth on the thinning shears can also affect the cost. The more teeth the shears have, the more expensive they may be. However, more teeth can also mean better precision and control.

Price Range of Thinning Shears

The price of thinning shears can range from under $10 to over $500. Here is a general breakdown of the price range:

  • Under $50: These are typically basic thinning shears made from stainless steel or other materials. They may have fewer teeth and are not as precise as more expensive shears.
  • $50-$150: These are mid-range thinning shears that are made from higher-quality materials and have more teeth. They may also have additional features like ergonomic handles or beveled edges.
  • Over $150: These are high-end thinning shears that are made from premium materials like titanium or Damascus steel. They may have a higher number of teeth and be more precise and durable.

How to Choose the Right Thinning Shears

When choosing thinning shears, it's important to consider your needs and budget. Here are some tips to help you choose the right shears:

Consider Your Skill Level

If you're a beginner, you may want to start with a basic pair of thinning shears until you gain more experience. If you're an experienced hairstylist or barber, you may want to invest in higher-end shears that offer more precision and control.

Think About Your Needs

Consider the type of hair you'll be working with and the style you want to achieve. If you're working with thick, coarse hair, you may want shears with more teeth. If you're creating a layered look, you may want shears with fewer teeth.

Read Reviews

Before buying thinning shears, read reviews from other hairstylists and barbers. This can give you an idea of the quality, durability, and performance of the shears.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've invested in a pair of thinning shears, it's important to take care of them to ensure they last. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Keep the shears clean and dry to prevent rusting
  • Store the shears in a dry, cool place
  • Oil the shears regularly to maintain their sharpness and prevent corrosion
  • Have the shears sharpened by a professional when needed

FAQ

Do thinning shears damage hair?

When used properly, thinning shears should not damage hair. However, if used incorrectly or too aggressively, they can cause split ends and breakage.

Can thinning shears be used on wet hair?

Thinning shears can be used on wet or dry hair. However, using them on wet hair can make it harder to control the amount of hair being removed.

How often should thinning shears be sharpened?

Thinning shears should be sharpened when they start to feel dull or when they are not cutting as smoothly as they should. This can vary depending on how often they are used and how well they are maintained.

In conclusion, the cost of thinning shears can vary depending on several factors. It's important to consider your needs and budget when choosing the right shears for you. Remember to take care of your shears to ensure they last and perform well. Happy cutting!
