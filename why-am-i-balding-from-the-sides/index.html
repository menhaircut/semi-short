Why Am I Balding From The Sides

Written by Oliver Nov 02, 2023 · 5 min read

If you are experiencing hair loss from the sides, you may be wondering why it's happening and what you can do about it. In this article, we will explore the reasons why you may be balding from the sides and provide tips on how to prevent further hair loss.

Description

Balding from the sides is a common problem for both men and women. It can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and certain medical conditions. The hair loss typically starts at the temples and progresses to the crown, leaving a horseshoe-shaped pattern of hair on the back and sides of the head.

How to

If you are experiencing hair loss from the sides, there are several things you can do to slow down or prevent further hair loss:

1. Maintain a healthy diet:

Eating a well-balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to include plenty of protein, iron, and vitamin C in your diet.

2. Avoid tight hairstyles:

Tight hairstyles like braids, cornrows, and ponytails can put too much tension on the hair follicles, causing them to weaken and eventually fall out. Stick to looser hairstyles that don't pull on your hair.

3. Use gentle hair products:

Harsh chemicals in hair products like shampoos and conditioners can damage your hair and scalp, leading to hair loss. Use gentle, sulfate-free hair products that are designed for your hair type.

Step by Step

If you are looking for a more proactive approach to preventing hair loss from the sides, here are some steps you can take:

1. Start using a hair loss treatment:

There are several over-the-counter and prescription hair loss treatments that can help slow down or even reverse hair loss. Minoxidil and finasteride are two of the most popular treatments for male pattern baldness.

2. Consider hair restoration surgery:

If you have significant hair loss from the sides, hair restoration surgery may be a good option for you. This involves transplanting hair follicles from other parts of your scalp to the balding areas.

3. Talk to your doctor:

If you are experiencing hair loss from the sides, it's important to talk to your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be causing it. They may also be able to recommend treatments or lifestyle changes that can help slow down or prevent further hair loss.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for preventing hair loss from the sides:

1. Don't smoke:

Smoking can damage your hair follicles and lead to hair loss. If you smoke, consider quitting to improve your overall health and the health of your hair.

2. Reduce stress:

Stress can cause hormonal changes in your body that can lead to hair loss. Try to reduce stress in your life by practicing relaxation techniques like meditation or yoga.

3. Protect your hair from the sun:

Exposure to the sun's UV rays can damage your hair and scalp, leading to hair loss. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair when you're outside for extended periods of time.

FAQ

Q: Is hair loss from the sides permanent?

A: Hair loss from the sides can be permanent if the hair follicles have been damaged beyond repair. However, there are treatments available that can help slow down or even reverse hair loss.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss from the sides?

A: Yes, stress can cause hormonal changes in your body that can lead to hair loss from the sides.

Q: Is hair restoration surgery a good option for hair loss from the sides?

A: Hair restoration surgery can be a good option for significant hair loss from the sides. However, it's important to talk to your doctor to determine if it's the right choice for you.

Hair Care

Whether you are experiencing hair loss from the sides or not, it's important to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

1. Wash your hair regularly:

Washing your hair regularly helps remove dirt and oil that can clog your hair follicles and lead to hair loss. Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.

2. Avoid heat styling:

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and lead to breakage and hair loss. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and avoid using them on wet hair.

3. Get regular haircuts:

Getting regular haircuts can help prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can help prevent hair loss from the sides and promote healthy hair growth.
