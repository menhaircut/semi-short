How To Know If A Haircut Will Look Good On You

Written by Frank Jul 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Getting a new haircut can be exciting, but it can also be nerve-wracking. How do you know if the style you want will actually look good on you? Here, we'll go through some tips and tricks for figuring out if a haircut will flatter your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Considering Your Hair Type
  • Matching Your Personal Style
  • Types of Haircuts to Consider
  • Tips for Communicating with Your Stylist

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in figuring out if a haircut will look good on you is to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a style that will flatter your features and balance out any asymmetries.

The most common face shapes include:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart-shaped
  • Diamond-shaped

Once you know your face shape, you can do some research on hairstyles that tend to look good on that shape. For example, if you have a round face, you might want to avoid styles that add volume to the sides and instead opt for something that elongates the face, like a long bob.

Considering Your Hair Type

The next thing to consider is your hair type. Different hair types require different types of cuts and styling techniques to look their best.

Some common hair types include:

  • Straight
  • Wavy
  • Curly
  • Thick
  • Thin

For example, if you have thick, curly hair, you might want to choose a layered cut that will help reduce bulk and add movement. If you have thin, straight hair, you might want to opt for a blunt cut that will give the appearance of more volume.

Matching Your Personal Style

Another important factor to consider is your personal style. Do you prefer a more classic, timeless look, or do you like to experiment with trends? Are you comfortable with a lot of maintenance and styling, or do you prefer a low-maintenance cut?

Make sure you choose a cut that fits with your personal style and that you'll feel comfortable wearing on a daily basis.

Types of Haircuts to Consider

There are many different types of haircuts to choose from, so it can be overwhelming to know where to start. Here are a few styles to consider:

  • Bob
  • Pixie
  • Long layers
  • Blunt cut
  • Shag

Do some research on each of these styles to see which one might work best for your face shape, hair type, and personal style.

Tips for Communicating with Your Stylist

Once you've decided on a style, it's important to communicate clearly with your stylist to ensure that you're both on the same page. Here are some tips for effective communication:

  • Bring in pictures of the style you want
  • Be specific about the length and layers you want
  • Ask for advice on how to style your new cut
  • Be open to suggestions from your stylist

Remember, your stylist is there to help you achieve the look you want, so don't be afraid to ask questions and get their input.

FAQs

How do I know if a short haircut will look good on me?

Short haircuts can be flattering on many different face shapes, but it's important to choose a style that works with your hair type and personal style. Consider factors like your hair texture and the amount of maintenance you're willing to do before deciding on a short cut.

Will a certain haircut make my face look slimmer?

There are certain haircuts that can help create the illusion of a slimmer face, such as long layers or a side-swept fringe. However, it's important to choose a style that flatters your features and makes you feel confident, rather than solely focusing on making your face look slimmer.

Hair Care After the Cut

Once you've gotten your new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and looks its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are appropriate for your hair type
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling
  • Get regular trims to keep your cut looking fresh
  • Consider using a hair mask or other treatments to keep your hair healthy

With the right care and maintenance, your new haircut is sure to look great for weeks to come!
