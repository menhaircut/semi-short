How To Cut Your Own Hair Medium Choppy Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Aug 17, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon? Why not try cutting your own hair at home? With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a trendy medium choppy layered haircut without leaving your house! In this article, we’ll show you how to cut your own hair medium choppy layers in relaxed English language. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Medium Choppy Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Your Own Hair Medium Choppy Layers

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let’s talk about what a medium choppy layered haircut is. This style is characterized by layers that start at the chin and gradually get longer towards the back. The layers are cut in a choppy, textured style to create volume and movement.

To achieve this style, you’ll need a few tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Mirror

Now, let’s get started!

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it’s completely dry before you start cutting.

2. Part your hair down the middle and clip the two sections to separate them.

3. Take a section of hair from the front of one side and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and use the scissors to cut it at an angle. Repeat this step on the other side.

4. Release the clipped sections and comb them straight down. Use the first sections you cut as a guide and cut the rest of the hair to the same length. Make sure to cut at an angle for a choppy look.

5. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it at an angle. This will create the first layer.

6. Take another small section of hair behind the first and cut it to the same length. Repeat this step until you’ve cut all the hair in the back.

7. Blend the layers by taking horizontal sections of hair and cutting them at a slight angle. This will create a textured, choppy look.

8. Check your work in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments. Remember, it’s always better to cut a little bit at a time than to cut too much!

9. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new medium choppy layered haircut!

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

- Start with clean, dry hair.

- Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.

- Cut a little bit at a time to avoid cutting too much.

- Use clips to separate sections of hair.

- Take breaks and step away from the mirror to avoid overthinking.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my hair wet?

A: It’s better to cut your hair when it’s dry, as wet hair can shrink and make your cuts uneven.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy ends.

Q: Should I cut my hair all at once or in sections?

A: It’s better to cut your hair in sections to ensure evenness and avoid cutting too much at once.

Hair Care Tips

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often.

- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair medium choppy layers, you can save money and experiment with different styles from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you’ll have a trendy new haircut in no time!
