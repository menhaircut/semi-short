How To Cut Your Hair To Give It More Volume

Written by Robby Nov 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have fine or thin hair, you may struggle with getting the volume you desire. While there are many styling products and techniques that can help, one way to give your hair more volume is by cutting it in a way that adds texture and movement. In this article, we will go over how to cut your hair to give it more volume, step by step.

Description

Cutting your hair to add volume involves creating layers and removing weight from the hair. This allows the hair to move more freely and creates the illusion of fullness. It's important to note that while cutting your hair can help with volume, it's not a guarantee. Your hair type, texture, and natural volume will all play a role in how much volume you can achieve.

How To

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls and moves. If your hair is wet, it may look longer and heavier than it actually is.

Step 2: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly and ensure that you don't miss any spots. Start by sectioning your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom.

Step 3: Cut the bottom section

Starting with the bottom section, take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle. This will create layers and remove weight from the hair. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and cut more if you need to.

Step 4: Move to the top section

Once you've finished cutting the bottom section, move on to the top section. Take horizontal sections of hair and cut them at an angle, just like you did with the bottom section. The key is to create layers that blend seamlessly into the rest of your hair.

Step 5: Check for balance

Once you've finished cutting your hair, check for balance. Make sure that both sides are even and that the layers blend well together. If you notice any uneven spots, go back and trim them.

Tips

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're not comfortable cutting your own hair, ask a friend or visit a salon.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Consider adding bangs to your haircut. Bangs can add volume and texture to your hair.

FAQ

Can cutting my hair really add volume?

Yes, cutting your hair can add volume by creating layers and removing weight from the hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go back and fix it or visit a salon for help.

How often should I cut my hair to maintain volume?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of your hair.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and voluminous, it's important to take care of it. This includes washing your hair regularly, using conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. You can also use volumizing products, such as mousse or root lifters, to help boost volume.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut your hair to give it more volume and achieve the full, voluminous look you desire. Remember to take your time, start small, and be patient. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time!
