How To Get Thick Wavy Hair Air Dry A Complete Guide

Are you tired of using heat tools to style your hair every day? Want to embrace your natural waves and give your hair a break from the damaging effects of heat styling? In this guide, we'll show you how to get thick wavy hair air dry, without any heat tools!

Description

Thick, wavy hair can be challenging to style, especially if you're used to using heat tools. But air drying your hair can give you beautiful, natural waves without any damage. The key to getting thick wavy hair to air dry is using the right products and techniques. In this guide, we'll show you how to get the perfect air-dried waves every time.

How to Get Thick Wavy Hair Air Dry

The first step to getting thick wavy hair to air dry is to start with clean, damp hair. You don't want your hair to be soaking wet, but it should be damp enough to work with. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by shampooing and conditioning your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub it too hard, which can cause damage and frizz.

Step 2: Apply Products

Apply a styling cream or mousse to your hair, focusing on the mid-lengths and ends. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly through your hair. This will help to define your waves and reduce frizz.

Step 3: Scrunch Your Hair

Use your hands to scrunch your hair, encouraging your natural waves to form. This will help to create volume and texture. Don't be afraid to be a little rough with your hair, but be gentle enough to avoid causing damage.

Step 4: Let Your Hair Air Dry

Once you've scrunched your hair, let it air dry. You can either let it dry naturally or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to speed up the process. Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your waves.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting thick wavy hair to air dry:

  1. Shampoo and condition your hair with a volumizing formula.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently.
  3. Apply a styling cream or mousse to your hair.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly.
  5. Scrunch your hair to encourage your natural waves.
  6. Let your hair air dry.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when air drying your thick wavy hair:

  • Use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to blot your hair dry, instead of rubbing it with a regular towel.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair before styling to help prevent frizz and add moisture.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to speed up the drying process without causing damage.
  • Try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or braid to create natural waves overnight.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your waves.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take for thick wavy hair to air dry?

A: The drying time will depend on the length and thickness of your hair. On average, it can take anywhere from 30 minutes to 2 hours for thick wavy hair to air dry.

Q: Can I use heat tools on my hair after air drying it?

A: Yes, you can use heat tools on your hair after air drying it, but be sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Q: Can I air dry my hair overnight?

A: Yes, you can air dry your hair overnight. Try sleeping with your hair in a loose bun or braid to create natural waves while you sleep.

Hair Care

To keep your thick wavy hair looking its best, be sure to follow a regular hair care routine. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools too often. Be sure to trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to add moisture and nourishment. With proper care, you can have healthy, beautiful waves that look great air dried!
