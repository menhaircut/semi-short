Is Blow Drying Good For Curly Hair

Written by Aprilia Sep 01, 2023 · 5 min read

Curly hair can be challenging to manage, and many people with curly hair struggle with frizz, dryness, and breakage. One question that often comes up is whether blow drying is good for curly hair. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of blow drying curly hair and provide some tips for achieving healthy, bouncy curls.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Blow Dry Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Blow Drying Curly Hair
  • Tips for Blow Drying Curly Hair

Description

Blow drying can be beneficial for curly hair if done correctly. The heat from the blow dryer can help to smooth the hair cuticle and reduce frizz, resulting in a smoother, more defined curl pattern. However, blow drying can also be damaging to curly hair if done too frequently or at too high of a heat setting. It's important to use the right tools and techniques to minimize damage and achieve healthy, bouncy curls.

How to Blow Dry Curly Hair

Before blow drying curly hair, it's important to prep the hair properly. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are specifically formulated for curly hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it's wet, being careful not to pull or tug too hard. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help protect your hair from heat damage and keep it moisturized.

Next, use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer. A diffuser is a tool that disperses the airflow over a larger area, which helps to reduce frizz and maintain your curl pattern. Start by using the blow dryer on a low heat setting and low speed, using the diffuser to gently lift and scrunch your hair. As you work, gradually increase the heat and speed settings, being careful not to overdo it. Finish by using a cool shot of air to set your curls.

Step-by-Step Guide to Blow Drying Curly Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to blow drying curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with products formulated for curly hair.
  2. Gently detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help protect your hair from heat damage and keep it moisturized.
  4. Attach a diffuser to your blow dryer and set it to a low heat and low speed setting.
  5. Start at the roots and use the diffuser to gently lift and scrunch your hair.
  6. As you work, gradually increase the heat and speed settings, being careful not to overdo it.
  7. Continue lifting and scrunching your hair until it's about 80% dry.
  8. Finish by using a cool shot of air to set your curls.

Tips for Blow Drying Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve healthy, bouncy curls when blow drying your hair:

  • Use a diffuser attachment to reduce frizz and maintain your curl pattern.
  • Start with a low heat and low speed setting and gradually increase as needed.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to protect your hair from heat damage and keep it moisturized.
  • Don't over-dry your hair - stop when it's about 80% dry to avoid damage.
  • Use a cool shot of air to set your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Avoid blow drying your hair every day - try to limit it to once or twice a week.
  • Consider using a heat protectant spray to further protect your hair from damage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about blow drying curly hair:

Can I blow dry my curly hair every day?

No, it's best to limit blow drying curly hair to once or twice a week to avoid damage.

What heat setting should I use on my blow dryer?

Start with a low heat and low speed setting and gradually increase as needed.

How can I reduce frizz when blow drying my curly hair?

Use a diffuser attachment and a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help reduce frizz and maintain your curl pattern.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Blow drying can be a useful tool for managing curly hair, but it's important to take care of your hair in other ways as well. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy, bouncy curls:

  • Wash your hair with products formulated for curly hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair while it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or styling product to help keep your hair moisturized and protected from damage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools too frequently.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In summary, blow drying can be beneficial for curly hair if done correctly. Use a diffuser attachment, start with a low heat and low speed setting, and be sure to use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to protect your hair from damage. By following these tips, you can achieve healthy, bouncy curls that you'll love.
