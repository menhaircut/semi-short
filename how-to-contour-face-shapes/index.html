How To Contour Face Shapes A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 25, 2023 · 6 min read

Contouring is a makeup technique that involves shading and highlighting to enhance the natural structure of your face. It's a popular trend that can help you achieve a more defined and sculpted look. However, contouring can be overwhelming, especially if you're new to the game. In this post, we'll walk you through how to contour face shapes like a pro.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Contour Face Shapes
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Contouring
  • FAQs About Contouring
  • Hair Care Tips to Complement Your Contoured Look

Description

Contouring is a makeup technique that involves using different shades of makeup to create the illusion of shadows and highlights on your face. The darker shades are used to create shadows, while the lighter shades are used to create highlights. When done correctly, contouring can help make your face appear more defined and sculpted.

How to Contour Face Shapes

If you're new to contouring, it can be challenging to know where to start. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

Determine Your Face Shape

Before you start contouring, it's essential to determine your face shape. There are six basic face shapes: round, oval, square, heart, diamond, and long. Each face shape requires a different contouring technique to achieve the desired look.

Choose the Right Products

Choosing the right products is essential when it comes to contouring. You'll need a contour shade that's a few shades darker than your natural skin tone and a highlight shade that's a few shades lighter. You'll also need a blending brush, a powder brush, and a beauty blender.

Apply the Contour Shade

Once you've chosen your products, it's time to apply the contour shade. Start by applying the contour shade to the areas of your face that you want to shade. These areas typically include the hollows of your cheeks, your temples, and your jawline.

Apply the Highlight Shade

After you've applied the contour shade, it's time to apply the highlight shade. Apply the highlight shade to the areas of your face that you want to highlight. These areas typically include the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow.

Blend, Blend, Blend

The key to successful contouring is blending. You'll want to blend the contour and highlight shades until there are no harsh lines or visible demarcations. Use your blending brush, powder brush, and beauty blender to blend the shades seamlessly into your skin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basics of contouring, here's a step-by-step guide to help you contour your face shape:

Round Face

If you have a round face, you'll want to create the illusion of angles and definition. Start by applying the contour shade to the hollows of your cheeks, your temples, and your jawline. Apply the highlight shade to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow. Blend the shades thoroughly, focusing on the edges of your face.

Oval Face

If you have an oval face, you're in luck! You have a naturally balanced face shape, so you don't need to do much contouring. Apply the contour shade to the hollows of your cheeks and your temples. Apply the highlight shade to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow. Blend the shades thoroughly for a natural look.

Square Face

If you have a square face, you'll want to soften your angles and create a more rounded look. Apply the contour shade to the corners of your jawline, the hollows of your cheeks, and your temples. Apply the highlight shade to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow. Blend the shades thoroughly, focusing on the edges of your face.

Heart-Shaped Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, you'll want to balance your forehead and create a more defined jawline. Apply the contour shade to the corners of your jawline and the temples. Apply the highlight shade to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow. Blend the shades thoroughly, focusing on the edges of your face.

Diamond Face

If you have a diamond face, you'll want to soften your angles and create a more rounded look. Apply the contour shade to the corners of your jawline, the hollows of your cheeks, and your temples. Apply the highlight shade to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow. Blend the shades thoroughly, focusing on the edges of your face.

Long Face

If you have a long face, you'll want to create the illusion of width and balance. Apply the contour shade to the corners of your jawline, the hollows of your cheeks, and your temples. Apply the highlight shade to the tops of your cheekbones, the bridge of your nose, and your cupid's bow. Blend the shades thoroughly, focusing on the edges of your face.

Tips for Contouring

Here are a few tips to help you master the art of contouring:

  • Start with a light hand and build up the product gradually.
  • Use natural light to ensure you're applying the shades correctly.
  • Blend, blend, blend!
  • Set your contour with a powder to ensure it lasts all day.
  • Practice makes perfect! Don't be discouraged if it takes a few tries to get it right.

FAQs About Contouring

Here are a few frequently asked questions about contouring:

How do I choose the right contour shade?

You'll want to choose a contour shade that's a few shades darker than your natural skin tone. Avoid shades that are too orange or warm-toned, as they can look unnatural.

Can I contour with cream products?

Yes! Cream products can be a great option for contouring, especially if you have dry skin. Just remember to blend thoroughly to avoid any harsh lines.

Do I need to contour every day?

It's entirely up to you! Contouring can take some time, so you may not want to do it every day. However, it can be a great option for special occasions or when you want to switch up your makeup look.

Hair Care Tips to Complement Your Contoured Look

Now that you know how to contour your face shape, it's time to complement your look with the right hairstyle. Here are a few hair care tips to help you achieve the perfect look:

  • Choose a hairstyle that complements your face shape.
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and body to your hair.
  • Apply a hair oil or serum to add shine and tame frizz.
  • Use a heat protectant before using hot tools to prevent damage.
  • Don't forget to trim your hair regularly to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Contouring can be a great way to enhance your natural structure and achieve a more defined look. Follow our step-by-step guide, practice, and experiment with different products to find the perfect contour for your face shape. Don't forget to complement your contoured look with the right hairstyle to complete your look.
