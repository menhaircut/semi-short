How To Trim Shih Tzu Eye Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Nov 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you own a Shih Tzu and find it challenging to trim their eye hair? Don't worry; you are not alone. Many Shih Tzu owners face this problem. Trimming Shih Tzu's eye hair is crucial as it prevents eye irritation, infections and keeps them looking neat and tidy. In this article, we will guide you through the process of trimming your Shih Tzu's eye hair step-by-step. We will also provide you with some tips and hair care techniques to make the process easier.

Description of Shih Tzu Eye Hair

Shih Tzus are popular pets known for their long, silky, and dense hair. Their hair also grows around their eyes, which can cause discomfort and irritation. The hair around their eyes needs to be trimmed regularly to prevent eye infections and ensure their comfort.

How to Trim Shih Tzu Eye Hair

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

Before you start the trimming process, make sure you have the necessary tools. You will need a pair of small and sharp scissors, a comb, and some treats. It is essential to use sharp scissors as dull scissors can cause discomfort to your Shih Tzu.

Step 2: Brush the Hair

Start by brushing your Shih Tzu's hair around their eyes to remove any tangles or mats. Brushing will also help you see the hair that needs trimming.

Step 3: Trim the Hair

Hold your Shih Tzu's head still and carefully trim the hair around their eyes. Start by trimming the hair on the top of the eye, and then move on to the hair underneath the eye. Be careful not to poke your Shih Tzu's eye with the scissors. Trim small amounts of hair at a time to prevent over-cutting.

Step 4: Reward Your Shih Tzu

Once you have finished trimming your Shih Tzu's eye hair, reward them with a treat. This will help them associate the trimming process with positive experiences and make it easier to trim their eye hair in the future.

Tips for Trimming Shih Tzu Eye Hair

Here are some tips to make the trimming process easier:

  • Trim your Shih Tzu's eye hair when they are relaxed and calm.
  • Make sure you have good lighting to see the hair clearly.
  • Use a comb to separate the hair before trimming.
  • Trim a little hair at a time to avoid over-cutting.
  • Use treats to reward your Shih Tzu after the trimming process.

FAQs

How often should I trim my Shih Tzu's eye hair?

You should trim your Shih Tzu's eye hair every two to three weeks. This will prevent the hair from growing too long and causing irritation to their eyes.

Can I use electric trimmers to trim my Shih Tzu's eye hair?

No, it is not recommended to use electric trimmers to trim your Shih Tzu's eye hair. Electric trimmers can be dangerous and cause discomfort to your Shih Tzu. It is best to use small, sharp scissors and trim the hair carefully.

Hair Care for Shih Tzu

Regular grooming is essential for Shih Tzus. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Brush your Shih Tzu's hair daily to prevent tangles and mats.
  • Trim their hair regularly, including their paw pads and around their anus.
  • Bathe your Shih Tzu every two to four weeks, depending on their activity level.
  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner designed for dogs.
  • Wipe their eyes daily with a damp cloth to prevent tear stains.

Conclusion

Trimming your Shih Tzu's eye hair is essential for their health and comfort. Follow the steps and tips in this article to make the trimming process easier. Remember to reward your Shih Tzu with treats and regular grooming to keep them looking and feeling their best.
