Can Guys Have Long Hair In The Air Force

Written by Luffy Jun 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you considering joining the Air Force but worried about having to cut your long locks? Fear not, because we’re here to answer the question: can guys have long hair in the Air Force?

Description

Many young men today prefer to have long hair, and there’s nothing wrong with that. However, when it comes to joining the military, there are certain regulations that need to be followed, including hair length requirements. In this blog post, we’ll discuss whether guys can have long hair in the Air Force, and if so, what the regulations are.

How to

The Air Force has strict grooming standards that all its members must adhere to. The guidelines for hair length vary depending on the individual’s position in the military. However, the general rule is that hair should be neatly groomed and not extend beyond the collar of the shirt or touch the ears, eyebrows, or upper part of the collar. The sides and back of the head should be tapered, with the hair length gradually decreasing from the crown to the hairline.

Step by Step

Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to ensure your hair meets the Air Force regulations:

  1. Make sure your hair is neatly groomed and trimmed regularly.
  2. Ensure that your hair does not extend beyond the collar of the shirt.
  3. Make sure your hair does not touch your ears, eyebrows, or the upper part of the collar.
  4. The sides and back of your head should be tapered, with the hair length gradually decreasing from the crown to the hairline.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your hair according to Air Force regulations:

  • Consider getting a haircut before joining the Air Force to ensure that your hair meets the regulations.
  • Invest in high-quality hair grooming products to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Make sure to trim your hair regularly to maintain the proper length.
  • Find a hairstylist who is familiar with military grooming standards.

FAQ

Q: Can guys have long hair in the Air Force?

A: Yes, but the hair should not extend beyond the collar of the shirt or touch the ears, eyebrows, or upper part of the collar. The sides and back of the head should be tapered, with the hair length gradually decreasing from the crown to the hairline.

Q: What happens if my hair is too long?

A: If your hair is too long, you may be asked to get a haircut to meet the Air Force regulations. If you refuse to comply, you may face disciplinary action.

Q: Can I have a ponytail or a man bun?

A: No, the Air Force regulations do not allow for ponytails or man buns. Your hair should be neatly groomed and tapered at the back and sides.

Hair Care

It’s important to take care of your hair to ensure it meets the Air Force regulations. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Use conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

So, can guys have long hair in the Air Force? The answer is yes, but with certain regulations in place. As long as your hair is neatly groomed and does not extend beyond the collar of the shirt or touch the ears, eyebrows, or upper part of the collar, you should be fine. Remember to maintain your hair regularly to ensure that it meets the Air Force regulations, and invest in high-quality hair grooming products to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
