How To Cut A Mullet For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Nov 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to rock a bold and unique hairstyle, the mullet might just be for you. But cutting a mullet can seem intimidating to beginners. Don't worry, with our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve the perfect mullet in no time.

How to Cut a Mullet: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of scissors, clippers, a comb, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create a horizontal part at the back of your head, separating the top from the bottom. Clip the top section up and out of the way. Then, create another horizontal part about halfway down the back of your head, separating the bottom into two sections.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Section

Using your clippers, create a graduated cut by starting with a longer length at the back and gradually getting shorter towards the front. Make sure to blend the longer and shorter sections together for a seamless transition.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Unclip the top section and use your scissors to create layers. Start by cutting the top layer shorter than the bottom layer and gradually getting longer towards the back. This will create the classic "business in the front, party in the back" mullet shape.

Step 6: Add Texture

To give your mullet some extra edge, use your scissors to add texture by cutting into the hair vertically. This will create a choppy, layered effect.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use your clippers to clean up the edges at the nape of your neck and around your ears.

Step 8: Style Your Mullet

Once you've finished cutting, style your mullet with some hair gel or wax.

Tips for Cutting a Mullet

  • Start with a longer length and gradually work your way shorter to avoid any mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head as you cut.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're unsure about any step.
  • Remember, practice makes perfect!

FAQ

Can I cut a mullet on my own?

Yes, you can cut a mullet on your own as long as you have the necessary tools and follow our step-by-step guide.

How long does a mullet take to grow back?

It depends on your hair's natural growth rate, but on average, hair grows about half an inch per month.

What face shape is best for a mullet?

Mullets can work for any face shape, but they tend to look best on oval and heart-shaped faces.

Hair Care for Your Mullet

To keep your mullet looking its best, make sure to wash and condition your hair regularly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and invest in a good quality heat protectant spray.

Cutting a mullet may seem daunting at first, but with our guide, you'll be able to achieve the perfect look. Remember to take your time, follow the steps carefully, and don't be afraid to ask for help. Happy cutting!
