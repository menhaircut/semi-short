How To Get Red Tones Out Of Brown Hair At Home

Description

If you have brown hair with red tones, you may be looking for ways to get rid of the red and achieve a more natural look. Luckily, there are several methods you can use to achieve this at home. Whether you want to cover up highlights or simply tone down the red, there are several techniques you can use to achieve the desired result.

How to Get Red Tones Out of Brown Hair at Home

The first step in getting rid of red tones in brown hair is to identify the underlying cause. This could be due to a variety of factors, such as exposure to sunlight or using hair products that contain harsh chemicals. Once you’ve identified the cause, you can then choose the appropriate method to remove the red tones.

One method is to use a hair dye that is specifically designed to neutralize red tones. This will typically involve choosing a hair dye that is one or two shades lighter than your natural hair color, as this will help to tone down the redness. You can also use a color correcting shampoo or conditioner to help neutralize the red tones.

If you want to avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, you can also try using natural remedies to remove the red tones. One option is to use vinegar or lemon juice to help neutralize the redness. Simply mix one part vinegar or lemon juice with two parts water, and apply it to your hair. Leave it on for 15-20 minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get red tones out of brown hair at home:

  1. Identify the underlying cause of the redness.
  2. Choose the appropriate method to remove the red tones.
  3. If using hair dye, choose a shade one or two shades lighter than your natural hair color.
  4. Apply the hair dye according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  5. If using a color correcting shampoo or conditioner, use it regularly to help neutralize the red tones.
  6. If using vinegar or lemon juice, mix one part vinegar or lemon juice with two parts water, and apply it to your hair.
  7. Leave the vinegar or lemon juice mixture on your hair for 15-20 minutes.
  8. Rinse the mixture out with cool water.
  9. Repeat as necessary until the desired result is achieved.
  10. Continue to use hair care products that are designed to maintain your new color and prevent red tones from returning.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your hair after removing red tones:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your new color.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to sunlight or other sources of UV rays, as this can cause the color to fade.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip away natural oils and cause the color to fade faster.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause the color to fade.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of red tones in brown hair:

  • Is it safe to use hair dye to remove red tones? Yes, as long as you use a hair dye that is specifically designed to neutralize red tones and follow the manufacturer’s instructions, it should be safe to use.
  • Can I use natural remedies to remove red tones? Yes, you can use natural remedies such as vinegar or lemon juice to help neutralize red tones. However, these may take longer to achieve the desired result compared to using a hair dye.
  • How often should I repeat the treatment? This will depend on the severity of the red tones and the method you are using. It’s best to follow the manufacturer’s instructions or consult with a hair care professional for guidance.

Hair Care and Products

Here are some recommended hair care products for maintaining your new color:

  • Color-safe shampoo and conditioner: Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically designed for color-treated hair to help maintain your new color and prevent fading.
  • Deep conditioning treatment: Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Hair oil: Use a hair oil to help protect your hair and prevent damage from hot tools or other environmental factors.
  • Leave-in conditioner: Use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and protect it from damage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can successfully remove red tones from your brown hair and achieve a more natural look. Remember to choose the appropriate method for your specific hair type and consult with a hair care professional if you have any concerns.
