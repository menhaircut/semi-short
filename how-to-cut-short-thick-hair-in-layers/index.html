How To Cut Short Thick Hair In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting short thick hair can be tricky, especially if you're trying to add layers. But don't worry, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to cut short thick hair in layers. Whether you're a professional hairdresser or a DIY enthusiast, you can achieve a layered haircut that looks great on anyone.

Description

Layered haircuts are a popular choice for those with thick hair. Layers can add dimension, texture, and movement to the hair, making it more manageable and stylish. However, it's important to cut layers correctly to avoid a choppy or uneven look. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut short thick hair in layers using the right technique and tools.

How to Cut Short Thick Hair in Layers

The first step in cutting short thick hair in layers is to choose the right length for your layers. You can go for long layers or short layers depending on your preference. Short layers are ideal for those who want to add volume and movement to their hair, while long layers are great for those who want a more subtle look.

Tools You'll Need

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle
  • Hairdryer

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair thoroughly and towel-drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into sections using sectioning clips. Start by creating a middle part and then sectioning off the hair on both sides of your head.

Step 3: Start Cutting Your Layers

Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle to create a layered look. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top layer.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, it's time to blend them. Comb your hair down and use the scissors to cut any stray hairs that may be sticking out.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Use a hairdryer to style your hair and see how the layers look. You can also use a straightener or curling iron to add more texture and volume to your hair.

Tips for Cutting Short Thick Hair in Layers

  • Always use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small sections at a time.
  • Make sure your hair is dry before you start cutting.
  • Use sectioning clips to keep your hair organized and easy to work with.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting hair in layers can be time-consuming, but the results are worth it.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own hair in layers?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair in layers. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps carefully.

Q: How often should I get a layered haircut?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the layers grow out. On average, you should get a layered haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Will layers make my hair look thinner?

A: No, layers can actually make your hair look thicker and more voluminous. By removing some of the weight from your hair, layers can add more movement and body.

Hair Care After Layered Haircut

After getting a layered haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.
  • Consider using hair masks or oils to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered haircut that looks great on short thick hair. Whether you're cutting your own hair or going to a professional, make sure to take your time and be patient for the best results.
