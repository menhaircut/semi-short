How To Cut A Basic Fade A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to cut a basic fade. If you're looking to give your hair a stylish and trendy makeover, a fade is a great option. Whether you're a professional barber or just looking to cut your own hair at home, learning how to cut a basic fade is a skill that can come in handy. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting a basic fade step by step. Let's get started!

Table of Contents:

  • Description
  • How to Cut a Basic Fade
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting a Basic Fade

Description

A fade is a haircut where the hair gradually gets shorter from the top of the head down to the sides and back. The hair is usually cut shorter on the sides and back using clippers, while the top is left longer. The fade is a popular hairstyle for men of all ages and can be customized to suit individual preferences.

How to Cut a Basic Fade

Cutting a basic fade is a simple process, but it requires some skill and practice. You'll need a few tools to get started:

  • Clipper with different guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Haircut cape or towel

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut a basic fade:

  1. Start by washing and drying the hair.
  2. Use the clipper with the longest guard to cut the hair on the top of the head. Cut the hair evenly, working from the front to the back.
  3. Change to a shorter guard and use the clipper to cut the hair on the sides and back. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using a flicking motion to create a smooth transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides and back.
  4. Blend the hair using different guard sizes to create a gradual fade. Use a longer guard to blend the hair around the temples and a shorter guard to blend the hair at the nape of the neck.
  5. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a neat, clean look.
  6. Use the comb to check for any uneven spots and trim as needed.
  7. Finally, use the mirror to check the back and sides of the head for any missed spots. Make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting a Basic Fade

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting a basic fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard to create a smooth transition.
  • Use a flicking motion when cutting the hair to create a natural-looking fade.
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as needed.
  • Use a mirror to check your work from different angles.
  • Practice makes perfect - the more you cut fades, the better you'll get.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut a fade on my own?

A: Yes, you can cut a basic fade on your own with the right tools and some practice. However, it's always best to consult a professional barber if you're unsure or want a more complicated style.

Q: What's the difference between a fade and a taper?

A: A fade is a gradual decrease in hair length from the top of the head down to the sides and back, while a taper is a gradual decrease in hair length from the top of the head down to the neckline.

Q: How often should I get a fade?

A: How often you should get a fade depends on your individual hair growth and style preferences. Some people get a fade every two weeks, while others go several weeks or even months between haircuts.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain a healthy and stylish look. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much product, which can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using styling tools.

Cutting a basic fade is a great way to update your look and stay on trend. With the right tools and some practice, you can achieve a stylish fade that suits your individual style. Follow the steps in this guide and don't be afraid to experiment with different lengths and styles to find your perfect fade. Good luck!
