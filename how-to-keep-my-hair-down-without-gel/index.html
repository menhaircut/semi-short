How To Keep My Hair Down Without Gel A Guide For Relaxed Hair

Written by Petter Nov 28, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of using gel to keep your hair down? Do you want a more natural-looking hairstyle that doesn't involve harsh chemicals? If so, you are in the right place. In this article, we will show you how to keep your hair down without gel. From simple hair care tips to step-by-step instructions, we've got you covered.

Table of Contents:

  • Hair Care
  • How to Keep Your Hair Down Without Gel
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs

Hair Care

Before we dive into how to keep your hair down without gel, it's important to take care of your hair. Healthy hair is easier to manage and style, which means you won't need as much product to keep it down.

Here are a few tips for taking care of your hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair gently.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

How to Keep Your Hair Down Without Gel

Now that you have healthy hair, let's talk about how to keep it down without gel. There are a few different methods you can try, depending on your hair type and the look you want to achieve.

Method 1: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

One of the easiest ways to keep your hair down without gel is to use a leave-in conditioner. This will help to hydrate your hair and keep it smooth and sleek.

Here's how to use a leave-in conditioner:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently.
  3. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  4. Comb through your hair to distribute the product evenly.

Method 2: Blow-Dry Your Hair

If you want a sleek, straight look, you can blow-dry your hair to keep it down. This method works best for those with straight or wavy hair.

Here's how to blow-dry your hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair.
  3. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush, pulling the brush down as you go.
  4. Once your hair is dry, use a straightening iron to smooth out any kinks.

Method 3: Use Hair Clips

If you have curly or wavy hair, you can use hair clips to keep your hair down. This method works best for those who want to maintain their natural texture.

Here's how to use hair clips:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently.
  3. Part your hair into sections.
  4. Clip each section down using hair clips.
  5. Leave the clips in until your hair is completely dry.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know the different methods for keeping your hair down without gel, let's go through a step-by-step guide.

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual. This will help to keep your hair healthy and easy to manage.

Step 2: Apply Leave-In Conditioner or Heat Protectant Spray

If you're using a leave-in conditioner, apply it to your hair after towel-drying. If you're blow-drying your hair, apply a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

Step 3: Style Your Hair

Choose the method that works best for your hair type and the look you want to achieve. Blow-dry your hair, use hair clips, or try another method to keep your hair down.

Step 4: Finish with Hair Oil or Serum

To keep your hair looking sleek and shiny, finish with a hair oil or serum. Apply a small amount to the ends of your hair and work your way up.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few additional tips to help you keep your hair down without gel:

  • Use a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Try a different hairstyle, such as a braid or bun, to keep your hair down.

FAQs

Can I use hairspray instead of gel to keep my hair down?

Yes, you can use hairspray instead of gel to keep your hair down. However, hairspray can be drying and can leave your hair feeling stiff.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. Some people can go several days without washing their hair, while others need to wash it every day.

What can I do if my hair is still frizzy?

If your hair is still frizzy, try using a hair oil or serum to smooth it down. You can also try using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to maintain your natural texture.

Keeping your hair down without gel is easier than you might think. With a few simple hair care tips and the right styling techniques, you can achieve a sleek, natural-looking hairstyle. Try out these methods and see what works best for you.
