What Hair Type Do I Have Black Male

Written by William Nov 22, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a black male who's looking to take care of your hair, you might be wondering what hair type you have. Understanding your hair type is crucial if you want to choose the right products and styling techniques. In this post, we'll go over the different types of hair that black men can have, and how to determine which one you have.

What Are the Different Types of Hair?

Before we dive into how to determine your hair type, let's take a look at the different types of hair that black men can have:

Straight Hair

Straight hair is the rarest type of hair for black men. It's characterized by hair that is completely straight, with no curl or wave.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by hair that has a slight wave pattern. It's not as curly as kinky hair, but it's not completely straight either.

Curly Hair

Curly hair is characterized by hair that has a distinct curl pattern. The curls can range from loose to tight, and the hair may be difficult to manage.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair is characterized by hair that has a tight curl pattern. It's the most common hair type for black men, and it can be difficult to manage if not properly cared for.

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Now that you know the different types of hair, it's time to determine which one you have. Here are the steps you can take:

Step 1: Examine Your Hair

Take a close look at your hair in the mirror. Determine if it's straight, wavy, curly, or kinky. If you're not sure, ask a friend or family member for their opinion.

Step 2: Feel Your Hair

Run your fingers through your hair. If it feels smooth and silky, you likely have straight or wavy hair. If it feels coarse and rough, you likely have curly or kinky hair.

Step 3: Check for Elasticity

Take a small section of your hair and gently stretch it. If it snaps back into place, you likely have kinky hair. If it stretches and bounces back, you likely have curly hair. If it stretches but doesn't bounce back, you likely have wavy hair. If it doesn't stretch at all, you likely have straight hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Hair Type

Once you've determined your hair type, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

Straight Hair

Straight hair can be prone to oiliness, so it's important to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid heavy products that can weigh down your hair.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair can be prone to frizz, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Use a lightweight styling product to define your waves.

Curly Hair

Curly hair can be prone to dryness, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls hydrated, and a curl-defining styling product to enhance your curls.

Kinky Hair

Kinky hair can be prone to breakage, so it's important to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated, and a moisturizing styling product to prevent breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can I have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it's possible to have hair that is a combination of two or more types. In this case, you'll need to take care of your hair based on the type that is most dominant.

Q: Can I change my hair type?

A: No, you can't change your hair type. However, you can use styling techniques and products to enhance your hair's natural texture.

Hair Care

Now that you know what hair type you have, it's important to take care of it properly. With the right products and techniques, you can keep your hair looking healthy and stylish.

Remember to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated, and a styling product that's appropriate for your hair type to enhance your natural texture.
