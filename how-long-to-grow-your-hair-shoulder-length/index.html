How Long Does It Take To Grow Your Hair Shoulder Length

Written by Aprilia Jun 26, 2023 · 5 min read

Whether you’re looking to grow out a short haircut or just want to switch up your style, achieving shoulder-length hair can be a lengthy process. In this post, we’ll explore how long it typically takes to grow your hair shoulder length, as well as some tips for healthy hair growth and maintenance along the way.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow Your Hair Shoulder Length
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Healthy Hair Growth
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Shoulder-Length Hair

How to Grow Your Hair Shoulder Length

The length of time it takes to grow your hair shoulder length depends on a variety of factors, including your genetics, age, and overall health. On average, hair grows about 0.5 inches per month, so it can take anywhere from 12-24 months to grow out your hair to shoulder length.

However, there are some things you can do to help speed up the process and promote healthy hair growth:

1. Eat a Balanced Diet

What you eat can have a big impact on the health of your hair. Make sure your diet is rich in nutrients like protein, iron, and vitamins A, C, and E to promote healthy hair growth.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage your hair and slow down the growth process. Try to limit your use of these tools as much as possible, and when you do use them, use a heat protectant spray.

3. Get Regular Trims

While it might sound counterintuitive, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow faster. Trimming off split ends and damaged hair can prevent breakage and keep your hair healthy, which can lead to faster growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you’re looking to grow your hair shoulder length, here’s a step-by-step guide:

1. Start with a Healthy Foundation

Before you start growing out your hair, make sure your hair is healthy and free from damage. Get a trim to remove split ends, and start using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

2. Be Patient

Growing out your hair takes time, so be patient and don’t get discouraged if you don’t see results right away. Avoid the temptation to cut your hair short again or try drastic hair growth treatments, as these can often do more harm than good.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from damage by avoiding heat styling tools, wearing a hat or scarf when you’re out in the sun, and using a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent breakage while you sleep.

4. Get Regular Trims

As mentioned earlier, getting regular trims can actually help your hair grow faster by preventing breakage and split ends. Aim to get a trim every 8-12 weeks.

Tips for Healthy Hair Growth

Here are some additional tips to promote healthy hair growth:

1. Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to the hair follicles, which can promote healthy hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes each day.

2. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Using a wide-tooth comb instead of a brush can help prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to prevent pulling and tugging.

3. Take a Hair Growth Supplement

If you’re struggling to get all the nutrients you need for healthy hair growth from your diet alone, consider taking a hair growth supplement. Look for one that contains vitamins and minerals like biotin, folic acid, and iron.

FAQs

How often should I wash my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp gets. Generally, it’s best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

How can I prevent split ends?

Prevent split ends by getting regular trims, using a deep conditioning treatment once a week, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible.

Do hair growth shampoos actually work?

While there’s no magic formula for instant hair growth, some hair growth shampoos can help support healthy hair growth by nourishing the scalp and hair follicles. Look for one that contains biotin, keratin, or other hair-healthy ingredients.

Hair Care Tips for Shoulder-Length Hair

Once you’ve achieved shoulder-length hair, it’s important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Gentle Shampoo

Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Look for one that’s formulated for your hair type.

2. Condition Regularly

Use a conditioner after every shampoo to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Focus on the mid-lengths and ends of your hair, and avoid applying conditioner to your scalp.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can damage your hair and lead to dryness and breakage. Wear a hat or scarf when you’re out in the sun, or use a spray-on UV protectant for your hair.

4. Experiment with Styles

Shoulder-length hair is versatile and can be styled in a variety of ways. Experiment with different styles like braids, top knots, and loose waves to find what works best for you.

5. Get Regular Trims

Keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends by getting regular trims every 8-12 weeks.


In conclusion, growing your hair shoulder length can take anywhere from 12-24 months, depending on your genetics, age, and overall health. To promote healthy hair growth, eat a balanced diet, avoid heat styling tools, and get regular trims. Once you’ve achieved shoulder-length hair, take good care of it by using a gentle shampoo, conditioning regularly, and protecting it from the sun. With patience and consistency, you can achieve the hair length and style you’ve been dreaming of.
