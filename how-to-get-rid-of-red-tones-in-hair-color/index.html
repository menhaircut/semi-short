How To Get Rid Of Red Tones In Hair Color

Written by Daniel Jul 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have ever tried to dye your hair, you know that it can be a tricky process. One of the most common problems that people face is getting red tones in their hair color, which can be frustrating and difficult to get rid of. But don't worry, there are several ways that you can fix this issue and get the hair color that you want.

Description

Red tones can be caused by a variety of factors, including the type of dye that you use, the condition of your hair, and the amount of time that you leave the dye in your hair. While some people love the look of red hair, others prefer a different color. If you fall into the latter category, you may be wondering how to get rid of those red tones.

How to Get Rid of Red Tones in Hair Color

Here are some steps that you can take to get rid of red tones in your hair color:

Step 1: Use a Clarifying Shampoo

If you have recently dyed your hair and noticed that it has turned red, you can try using a clarifying shampoo. This type of shampoo is designed to remove build-up and residue from your hair, which can help to remove the red tones. Be sure to follow the instructions on the bottle and rinse your hair thoroughly after shampooing.

Step 2: Use a Toner

If clarifying shampoo doesn't work, you can try using a toner. A toner is a product that is designed to neutralize unwanted tones in your hair, including red. You can find toners at most beauty supply stores, and they come in a variety of colors. Be sure to choose a toner that is appropriate for your hair color and follow the instructions carefully.

Step 3: Color Your Hair Again

If neither of the first two steps work, you may need to color your hair again. This time, be sure to choose a hair dye that is designed to counteract red tones. Look for dyes that have cool tones, such as ash or platinum, and avoid warm tones, such as gold or copper.

Tips

Here are some additional tips that can help you get rid of red tones in your hair color:

  • Make sure that your hair is in good condition before you dye it. If your hair is damaged or dry, it may not take the dye evenly, which can lead to red tones.
  • Follow the instructions on the hair dye package carefully. Leaving the dye in your hair for too long can cause red tones to develop.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist if you are having trouble getting rid of red tones. They can help you choose the right products and techniques to achieve the hair color that you want.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting rid of red tones in hair color:

Q: Can I use lemon juice to remove red tones from my hair?

A: Lemon juice can be effective at lightening hair, but it may not be the best choice for removing red tones. It is also important to note that lemon juice can be drying to your hair, so be sure to use a deep conditioner afterwards.

Q: How long should I leave toner in my hair?

A: The amount of time that you should leave toner in your hair will depend on the product that you are using. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and don't leave the toner in for longer than recommended.

Hair Care

Getting rid of red tones in your hair color can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, it is possible. Be sure to take care of your hair before and after dyeing it, and follow the instructions carefully. With a little patience and perseverance, you can achieve the hair color that you want.
