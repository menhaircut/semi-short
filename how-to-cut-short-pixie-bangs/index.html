How To Cut Short Pixie Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Nov 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting short pixie bangs might just be the change you need. Short pixie bangs are a trendy and versatile style that can suit anyone. But, before you grab the scissors, it's essential to know the proper technique. In this guide, we'll take you through step-by-step on how to cut short pixie bangs.

How to Cut Short Pixie Bangs

Cutting short pixie bangs may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and technique, it's simple. Here are the steps you should follow.

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you need to gather the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp haircutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Brush and Section Your Hair

Brush your hair to remove any knots or tangles, and then create a triangular section from the hairline to the top of your head. Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the Length

Decide on the length of your bangs by using your eyebrows as a guide. For shorter bangs, cut just above the brow line, and for longer bangs, cut just below.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Start cutting in the middle of your triangular section, and work your way outwards. Use the comb to hold the hair taut and cut small sections at a time. Remember to cut in a straight line and check your progress frequently.

Step 5: Blend the Bangs

Blend the bangs into the rest of your hair to create a seamless transition. Use the scissors to create feathered ends that gradually blend into your hair.

Step 6: Style Your Bangs

Once you've finished cutting, style your bangs as desired. You can use a flat iron to straighten them, or a curling iron to add some texture.

Tips for Cutting Short Pixie Bangs

Cutting short pixie bangs may seem simple, but there are a few tips that can make the process smoother.

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Cut small sections at a time and check your progress frequently.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. You can always trim more later, but you can't glue it back on!
  • If you're unsure, start with longer bangs and gradually cut more until you're happy with the length.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short pixie bangs.

Can I cut my own short pixie bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own short pixie bangs, but it's important to follow the proper technique and use the right tools.

How often do I need to trim my short pixie bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain the length. As a general rule, trim your bangs every four to six weeks.

Can I style my short pixie bangs differently?

Yes, you can style your short pixie bangs in different ways. Experiment with straightening or curling them, or try sweeping them to the side for a different look.

Hair Care for Short Pixie Bangs

To keep your short pixie bangs looking their best, it's essential to take care of your hair. Here are some tips.

  • Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and pollution by wearing a hat or scarf.

Cutting short pixie bangs is a fun and easy way to switch up your hairstyle. With the right tools and technique, anyone can do it. Follow these steps, and you'll have a trendy new look in no time.
