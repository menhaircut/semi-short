How To Cut Heavy Bangs At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle without breaking the bank, cutting your own bangs at home can be a great option. However, cutting heavy bangs can be a bit tricky, and it's important to know what you're doing before you start snipping away. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to achieve a perfect set of heavy bangs that will frame your face and elevate your look. So, let's get started!

Description

Heavy bangs are a popular style that works well with most face shapes. They can help to highlight your eyes, create a more youthful appearance, and even make your face look slimmer. However, getting your heavy bangs cut at a salon can be expensive, and it's not always easy to maintain the look. By learning how to cut heavy bangs at home, you can save money and have more control over your hairstyle.

How To

Before you start cutting your heavy bangs at home, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. You might also want to have some hair clips on hand to help section off your hair.

Step 1: Choose the Right Length

The first step in cutting your heavy bangs at home is to decide on the length you want. Heavy bangs can range from just above your eyebrows to just below your eyebrows, so it's important to choose a length that you're comfortable with. Use your fingers to measure where you want your bangs to start and end, and mark the spot with a comb.

Step 2: Section Off Your Hair

Once you've decided on the length of your heavy bangs, it's time to section off your hair. Use a comb to separate your bangs from the rest of your hair, and clip the rest of your hair back. This will make it easier to cut your bangs without accidentally snipping other parts of your hair.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Now it's time to start cutting. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle, starting from the center of your bangs and working your way out. Be sure to cut slowly and carefully, as it's easy to make a mistake when cutting heavy bangs. Keep checking your progress in the mirror to make sure your bangs are even.

Step 4: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your heavy bangs, it's time to check your work. Comb your bangs down and check to make sure they're even. If you notice any uneven spots, you can always go back and make adjustments.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect heavy bangs:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to fix a mistake.
  • Be patient. Cutting heavy bangs takes time and precision.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so it's okay to take a break and come back to it later.

FAQ

Can I cut heavy bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut heavy bangs if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut your bangs when your hair is dry, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your heavy bangs, don't panic. You can always go back and make adjustments. If you're really unhappy with how your bangs turned out, you can always visit a salon to have them fixed.

How often should I trim my heavy bangs?

You should trim your heavy bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh and healthy.

Hair Care

Once you've cut your heavy bangs at home, it's important to take good care of them. You can use a small amount of hair oil to keep your bangs looking shiny and healthy. You should also avoid using heat styling tools on your bangs too often, as this can damage the hair.

Cutting heavy bangs at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and a little bit of patience, you can achieve a perfect set of bangs that will elevate your look and save you money. So, grab your scissors and get cutting!
