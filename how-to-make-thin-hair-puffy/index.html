How To Make Thin Hair Puffy Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Bardi Jun 15, 2023 · 4 min read

For those with thin hair, getting it to look voluminous and puffy can be a challenge. But fear not, there are plenty of ways to add body and fullness to your hair without damaging it. In this article, we’ll explore various tips, tricks, and hair care routines to help you achieve the puffy, voluminous hair of your dreams.

Table of Contents

  • How to Make Thin Hair Puffy
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ

How to Make Thin Hair Puffy

There are several ways to add volume and fullness to thin hair. Here are a few:

Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down will give it a lift at the roots, creating a puffy, voluminous look. Use a round brush to further add volume, but be careful not to overdo it, as this can cause damage to your hair.

Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

When washing your hair, use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and fullness. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and collagen, which can help strengthen your hair and add volume.

Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair at the roots can create the illusion of fuller, puffy hair. Use a teasing comb or brush to gently backcomb your hair, starting at the roots and working your way down. Be careful not to over-tease, as this can cause damage and breakage.

Use Hair Extensions

If you’re looking for a more dramatic way to add volume to your hair, consider using hair extensions. Look for extensions that are made from real human hair and match your hair color for a natural look. Clip-in extensions are a great option for those who don’t want to commit to a permanent change.

Step by Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to making thin hair puffy:

  1. Wash your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Blow dry your hair upside down, using a round brush to add volume.
  3. Tease your hair at the roots, starting at the crown and working your way down.
  4. If desired, use hair extensions to add extra volume and length.
  5. Finish with a volumizing hairspray to hold the style in place.

Tips and Tricks

Here are a few tips and tricks to help you achieve the puffy, voluminous hair of your dreams:

  • Avoid using heavy hair products, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Consider getting a layered haircut, as this can add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote hair growth and strength.

FAQ

Q: Will teasing my hair damage it?

A: Teasing your hair can cause damage and breakage if done excessively or with too much force. Be gentle when teasing, and use a wide-tooth comb or teasing brush to minimize damage.

Q: Can I use volumizing products on wet hair?

A: Yes, you can use volumizing products on wet hair. Apply the product to your roots and work it through your hair with your fingers or a comb.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. Generally, it’s best to wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.

Hair Care Routine

In addition to the tips and tricks outlined above, it’s important to have a good hair care routine to keep your hair healthy and strong. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

By following these tips, tricks, and hair care routines, you can achieve the puffy, voluminous hair of your dreams. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid damaging hot tools, and eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and strength. With a little patience and care, you’ll have the full, puffy hair you’ve always wanted.
