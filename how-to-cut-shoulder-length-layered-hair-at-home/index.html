How To Cut Shoulder Length Layered Hair At Home

Written by Aprilia Sep 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and give your hair a fresh new look, cutting your shoulder length layered hair at home can be a great option. With a little bit of practice and the right tools, you can achieve professional-looking results without ever leaving your house.

Description

Layered hair is a popular style that adds volume and movement to your hair. It involves cutting different lengths of hair to create layers that fall in a flattering way. Shoulder length layered hair is a versatile style that works well for most face shapes and hair types. It can be styled in many different ways and is easy to maintain.

How to Cut Shoulder Length Layered Hair at Home

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a good quality pair of hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting it.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section of hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the First Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Repeat this process for each section of hair in the bottom layer.

Step 4: Cut the Second Layer

Release the clips for the second layer of your hair. Take a section of hair from the first layer and use it as a guide for the length of the second layer. Cut each section of hair in the second layer at an angle.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the clips for the top layer of your hair. Use the second layer as a guide for the length of the top layer. Cut each section of hair in the top layer at an angle.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use the scissors to blend the layers together. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle to create a seamless transition between each layer.

Step 7: Trim the Ends

Use the scissors to trim any split ends or uneven sections of hair. Take small sections of hair and cut them at an angle to create a natural-looking finish.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it as desired. You can blow dry it straight, curl it, or leave it natural.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take breaks if you start to feel tired or frustrated.
  • Watch tutorials online to get a better idea of the process.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair at home. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be careful not to cut too much off.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine.

Cutting your shoulder length layered hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. With a little bit of practice and patience, you can achieve a fresh new look that you'll love.
