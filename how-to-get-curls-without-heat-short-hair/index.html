How To Get Curls Without Heat For Short Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have short hair and want to add some curls to your look? Are you tired of using heat styling tools that damage your hair? Don't worry, you can get curls without heat even if you have short hair. In this article, we will guide you through the process of getting curls without heat for short hair. From preparation to styling, we will cover everything you need to know.

Table of Contents

  1. Preparation
  2. Wet Your Hair
  3. Apply a Styling Product
  4. Twist Your Hair
  5. Pin Your Hair
  6. Let Your Hair Dry
  7. Unpin Your Hair
  8. Fluff Your Curls

  10. FAQs

Preparation

Before you start curling your hair without heat, you need to prepare your hair. Make sure your hair is clean and dry. If your hair is wet, it will take longer to dry and the curls may not hold their shape.

Additionally, you may want to detangle your hair with a wide-tooth comb to ensure that your hair is free from knots and tangles. This will make it easier to style your hair later on.

Wet Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and dry, it's time to wet your hair. You can either wet your hair in the shower or use a spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your hair is damp but not soaking wet.

Apply a Styling Product

Next, apply a styling product to your hair. This can be a curl enhancing cream, mousse, or gel. The product will help define your curls and hold them in place.

Make sure to apply the product evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends of your hair where the curls will be most prominent.

Twist Your Hair

Now it's time to start twisting your hair. Take a small section of hair and twist it tightly. The tighter you twist your hair, the more defined your curls will be.

Continue twisting small sections of hair until you have twisted all of your hair.

Pin Your Hair

Once you have twisted all of your hair, it's time to pin your hair. Use bobby pins or hair clips to secure the twisted sections of hair to your scalp. This will help the curls set and hold their shape.

Let Your Hair Dry

Now it's time to let your hair dry. You can either air dry your hair or use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer. Make sure not to touch your hair while it's drying.

Unpin Your Hair

Once your hair is completely dry, it's time to unpin your hair. Remove the bobby pins or hair clips and gently unravel each twisted section of hair.

Fluff Your Curls

Finally, it's time to fluff your curls. Use your fingers to gently separate and fluff your curls. You can also use a wide-tooth comb to add volume to your curls.

Hair Care Tips

Getting curls without heat is a great way to avoid damaging your hair. However, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it stays healthy. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQs

1. How long will the curls last?

The duration of your curls depends on your hair type and the products you use. However, curls without heat tend to last longer than curls created with heat. You can extend the life of your curls by using a hairspray or a curl-enhancing product.

2. Can I do this on dry hair?

No, it's best to start with damp hair. This will help the curls set and hold their shape.

3. Can I brush my hair after I've styled it?

Avoid brushing your hair after you've styled it. This can cause the curls to loosen and lose their shape. Instead, use your fingers or a wide-tooth comb to gently separate and fluff your curls.

Now that you know how to get curls without heat for short hair, you can try this styling technique at home. It's a great way to add some volume and texture to your hair without damaging it with heat styling tools.
