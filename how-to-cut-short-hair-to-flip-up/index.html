How To Cut Short Hair To Flip Up

Written by Eveline Jul 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair and want to add some volume and texture, flipping up the ends is a great way to achieve a playful and fun look. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting short hair to flip up, step by step.

Description

Flipping up the ends of short hair can make it look fuller and bouncier. This hairstyle works well for those with fine hair, as it adds volume and texture. It's also a versatile look that can be dressed up or down, depending on the occasion.

How to Cut Short Hair to Flip Up

Cutting short hair to flip up is a relatively easy process that can be done at home with the right tools and techniques. Follow these steps to achieve the perfect flip:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that may interfere with the cutting process. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add extra body to your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separate and out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Back

Start with the back section. Use a pair of sharp scissors to cut your hair straight across at the desired length. Then, take small sections of hair and hold them straight up with your fingers. Cut the ends of the hair at a slight angle, moving towards the center of the back of your head. This will create a graduated effect, which will help the hair to flip up.

Step 4: Cut the Sides

Move on to the side sections. Use the same technique as the back, cutting the hair at a slight angle towards the center. Be sure to blend the sides with the back, creating a seamless transition.

Step 5: Cut the Top

Finally, cut the top section of your hair. Use the same technique as the sides and back, cutting the hair at a slight angle towards the center. This will create layers and volume, which will help the hair to flip up.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it using a blow dryer and round brush. Use the brush to lift the roots of your hair and create volume. Blow dry your hair in an upward motion, directing the hair towards the crown of your head. Use a flat iron to flip up the ends of your hair, creating a playful and fun look.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect flip:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Start with a longer length and trim as needed to avoid cutting too much off at once.
  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add extra body to your hair.
  • Blow dry your hair in an upward motion to create volume.
  • Use a flat iron to flip up the ends of your hair.

FAQ

How often should I cut my hair to maintain the flip?

We recommend getting a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the flip and keep your hair healthy.

Can I still wear my hair straight with this cut?

Yes, you can still wear your hair straight with this cut. Simply use a flat iron to straighten the ends instead of flipping them up.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, be sure to use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner that is free from sulfates and parabens. Avoid using hot tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray before styling. Additionally, try to avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Cutting short hair to flip up is a fun and playful way to add volume and texture to your look. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect flip, and maintain healthy and beautiful hair.
