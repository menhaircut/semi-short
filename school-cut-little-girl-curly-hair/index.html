School Cut Little Girl Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a parent struggling to manage your little girl’s curly hair? Do you want to get her a school haircut but don’t know how to handle those curls? Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered! In this guide, we’ll provide you with step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you cut your little girl’s curly hair for school.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Little Girl Curly Hair?
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Little Girl Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Little Girl Curly Hair

Description

Curly hair can be challenging to manage, especially for children. Little girls with curly hair require regular haircuts to keep their locks healthy and manageable. A school haircut is a popular choice among parents as it is easy to maintain and looks neat and tidy. However, cutting little girl curly hair requires patience, precision, and the right tools. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut your little girl’s curly hair for school, so she looks her best every day.

How to Cut Little Girl Curly Hair?

Cutting little girl curly hair requires a different approach than straight hair. Curly hair tends to shrink when dry, making it difficult to gauge the length accurately. It’s essential to use the right technique and tools to ensure a clean and neat cut. Here are the steps to cut little girl curly hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and condition the hair to detangle and soften the curls.
  2. Divide the hair into sections using hair clips.
  3. Start with a small section at the back of the head and comb the hair straight using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush.
  4. Hold the hair between your fingers, about ½ inch from the ends, and cut straight across using hair cutting scissors.
  5. Continue with the next section, making sure to match the length with the previous section.
  6. Trim the hair around the face using the same technique.
  7. Check for any unevenness and adjust as necessary.
  8. Style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Little Girl Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting little girl curly hair:

  • Use hair cutting scissors instead of regular scissors as they are sharp and precise.
  • Don’t cut too much at once, as curly hair tends to shrink when dry.
  • Use a spray bottle to wet the hair if it starts to dry during cutting.
  • Keep a comb and detangling brush handy to smooth out tangles and knots.
  • Trim the hair when it is dry to prevent shrinkage.

FAQs

1. How often should I cut my little girl’s curly hair?

It depends on how fast her hair grows and how long you want it to be. A school haircut typically lasts for six to eight weeks.

2. Can I cut curly hair with regular scissors?

No, regular scissors are not sharp enough and can cause split ends and damage to curly hair. Use hair cutting scissors instead.

3. Should I cut my little girl’s curly hair when it is wet or dry?

Cut curly hair when it is dry to prevent shrinkage and ensure an accurate length.

Hair Care for Little Girl Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some hair care tips for little girl curly hair:

  • Wash and condition the hair once or twice a week with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to comb out tangles and knots gently.
  • Avoid brushing curly hair as it can cause frizz and breakage.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to moisturize and define the curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage curly hair.

By following these tips and using the right technique and tools, you can cut your little girl’s curly hair for school and keep it healthy and manageable. Remember to be patient and take your time to ensure a clean and neat cut.
