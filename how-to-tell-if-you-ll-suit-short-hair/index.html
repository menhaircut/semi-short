How to Tell If You'll Suit Short Hair

In this article, we'll be discussing how to tell if you'll suit short hair. Short hair can be a big change, and it's important to make sure it's a change you'll be happy with. We'll cover everything from how to determine your face shape to tips for maintaining your new hairstyle.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider when deciding if you'll suit short hair is your face shape. Different face shapes suit different hairstyles, and short hair can be particularly unforgiving if it doesn't suit your face shape. There are several ways to determine your face shape:

  • Measurements: Measure your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, and compare their widths. If your forehead and jawline are roughly the same width, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face, you likely have a heart-shaped face. If your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all roughly the same width, you likely have a square face. If your jawline is the narrowest part of your face, you likely have a diamond-shaped face. If your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at the forehead and jawline, you likely have an oval face. If your forehead is the widest part of your face, and your jawline is narrow, you likely have a round face.
  • Tracing: Stand in front of a mirror and trace the outline of your face onto the mirror with lipstick or a washable marker. Step back and examine the shape you've traced.
  • Online tools: There are several online tools that can help you determine your face shape, such as the Elle Face Shape Quiz.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can start considering whether short hair will suit you.

Considering Your Hair Texture

Another important factor to consider when deciding if you'll suit short hair is your hair texture. Some hair textures work better with short hair than others. Here are some tips for different hair textures:

  • Straight hair: Straight hair can look great in a sleek, short bob or a pixie cut.
  • Wavy hair: Wavy hair can look great in a tousled, textured bob or a layered pixie cut.
  • Curly hair: Curly hair can look great in a voluminous, curly bob or a short, curly pixie cut.
  • Thick hair: Thick hair can be difficult to manage in a short hairstyle, but a layered bob or a shaggy pixie cut can work well.
  • Thin hair: Thin hair can look great in a short, layered bob or a textured pixie cut.

Keep in mind that these are just general guidelines, and there are no hard and fast rules when it comes to hair texture and short hair. It's always best to consult with a stylist to determine what will work best for your hair.

Evaluating Your Personal Style

Another important factor to consider when deciding if you'll suit short hair is your personal style. Short hair can be very versatile, but it's important to make sure it fits with your overall aesthetic. Here are some things to consider:

  • Clothing style: Do you prefer a more classic, sophisticated style, or do you prefer something more edgy and alternative? Short hair can work with both, but you may want to choose a different style depending on your overall look.
  • Makeup style: Do you prefer a natural, minimal makeup look, or do you like to experiment with bold, colorful makeup? Short hair can work with both, but you may want to choose a different style depending on your makeup.
  • Lifestyle: Do you have a busy, active lifestyle that requires low-maintenance hair, or do you have more time to devote to styling your hair? Short hair can be low-maintenance, but it can also require more frequent trips to the salon for upkeep.

Again, these are just general guidelines, and it's always best to consult with a stylist to determine what will work best for you.

Short Haircut Ideas

Here are some popular short haircuts to consider:

  • Pixie cut: A classic short haircut that's perfect for those who want a low-maintenance style.
  • Bob: A versatile style that can be customized to suit your face shape and hair texture.
  • Lob: A longer bob that's perfect for those who want a short style but still want some length.
  • Shag: A layered style that's perfect for those with thick hair.

Remember, these are just a few popular styles, and there are many more to choose from. Consult with a stylist to determine what will work best for you.

Tips for Maintaining Short Hair

Short hair can be low-maintenance, but it still requires some upkeep. Here are some tips for maintaining your short hair:

  • Get regular trims: Short hair can lose its shape quickly, so it's important to get regular trims to keep it looking neat.
  • Invest in quality products: Use products that are specifically designed for your hair type and texture, and invest in high-quality styling tools.
  • Experiment with styling: Short hair is versatile, so don't be afraid to experiment with different styling techniques.

FAQ

Will short hair suit me?

Short hair can suit anyone, regardless of face shape or hair texture. The key is to choose a style that works best for you.

Will short hair make me look older?

Short hair can actually make you look younger, as it can give you a fresh, modern look.

Do I need to change my makeup when I get short hair?

Not necessarily, but you may want to experiment with different makeup looks to find what works best with your new style.

Hair Care Tips

Whether you have short hair or long hair, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some general hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly: How often you wash your hair will depend on your hair type and texture, but it's important to keep your scalp clean and healthy.
  • Use conditioner: Conditioner can help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling can damage your hair, so try to avoid it as much as possible.
  • Eat a healthy diet: A healthy diet can help keep your hair healthy and strong.

By following these tips, you can help keep your hair looking its best.
