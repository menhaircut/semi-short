Is There Surgery To Change Your Face Shape

Written by Oliver Oct 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're unhappy with the shape of your face, you may be wondering if there's a surgical solution. The good news is that there are a variety of procedures available that can help you achieve the look you want. In this article, we'll go over some of the most popular options for changing your face shape, as well as some important things to keep in mind before undergoing any type of cosmetic surgery.

Description

There are a few different reasons why someone might want to change the shape of their face. Some people are born with a face shape that they don't like, while others may have experienced changes due to aging or weight gain/loss. Whatever the reason, there are several surgical procedures that can help:

Rhinoplasty

Rhinoplasty, also known as a nose job, is a popular surgical procedure that can help reshape the nose. This can be especially helpful for people who feel that their nose is too large or too wide, or who have a bump or other asymmetry that they'd like to correct. During the procedure, the surgeon will make incisions inside the nostrils or across the columella (the tissue between the nostrils) to access the underlying bone and cartilage. They can then reshape these structures and/or remove excess tissue to achieve the desired results.

Facelift

A facelift is a surgical procedure that can help tighten and lift sagging skin on the face and neck. This can be especially helpful for people who have lost significant weight or who are experiencing the effects of aging. During the procedure, the surgeon will make incisions around the ears and/or along the hairline to access the underlying tissue. They can then lift and tighten this tissue, remove excess skin, and reposition the fat pads to create a more youthful and defined appearance.

Chin Implant

A chin implant is a surgical procedure that can help improve the appearance of the chin and jawline. This can be especially helpful for people who have a weak or recessed chin, or who feel that their face lacks definition. During the procedure, the surgeon will make a small incision under the chin or inside the mouth to insert an implant made of silicone or other material. This can help create a more balanced and proportionate facial profile.

How To

If you're interested in undergoing one of these procedures, the first step is to schedule a consultation with a reputable cosmetic surgeon. During this initial appointment, the surgeon will evaluate your face and discuss your goals and expectations. They'll also go over the risks and benefits of the procedure, as well as the recovery process and any other important information you need to know. If you decide to move forward, the surgeon will schedule your surgery and provide you with instructions on how to prepare.

Step by Step

Here's a general overview of what you can expect during each of these procedures:

Rhinoplasty

  1. You'll receive anesthesia to keep you comfortable during the surgery.
  2. The surgeon will make incisions inside the nostrils or across the columella.
  3. They'll reshape the bone and/or cartilage as needed.
  4. They may add or remove tissue to achieve the desired look.
  5. The incisions will be closed with sutures or surgical tape.
  6. You'll be monitored in a recovery area before being released to go home.

Facelift

  1. You'll receive anesthesia to keep you comfortable during the surgery.
  2. The surgeon will make incisions around the ears and/or along the hairline.
  3. They'll lift and tighten the underlying tissue, remove excess skin, and reposition the fat pads.
  4. The incisions will be closed with sutures or surgical tape.
  5. You'll be monitored in a recovery area before being released to go home.

Chin Implant

  1. You'll receive anesthesia to keep you comfortable during the surgery.
  2. The surgeon will make a small incision under the chin or inside the mouth.
  3. They'll insert a silicone or other material implant to augment the chin.
  4. The incision will be closed with sutures or surgical tape.
  5. You'll be monitored in a recovery area before being released to go home.

Tips

If you're considering any type of cosmetic surgery, it's important to do your research and choose a reputable surgeon with a lot of experience. You should also be prepared to follow all of their instructions before and after the surgery, including any dietary restrictions, medication schedules, and activity limitations. Finally, be prepared for some downtime and discomfort during the recovery process, as it can take several weeks to fully heal from these types of procedures.

FAQ

Is it safe to change your face shape with surgery?

Surgery always carries some risks, but if you choose a reputable surgeon and follow all of their instructions, the risks can be minimized. It's important to be aware of the potential risks and complications before undergoing any type of surgery.

How long does it take to recover from these procedures?

The recovery time can vary depending on the specific procedure and your individual healing process, but you should expect to take at least a few weeks off from work or other activities. You may also experience swelling, bruising, and discomfort during this time.

Will insurance cover the cost of these procedures?

In most cases, cosmetic surgery is considered elective and won't be covered by insurance. However, you may be able to finance the cost through a payment plan or medical credit card.

Hair Care

If you're undergoing a facelift or chin implant, you may need to be careful with your hair during the recovery process. Your surgeon will provide specific instructions, but in general, it's best to avoid pulling your hair back tightly or using hair accessories that could interfere with the healing incisions. You may also want to consider getting a shorter haircut before the surgery to make it easier to care for your hair during the recovery period.
