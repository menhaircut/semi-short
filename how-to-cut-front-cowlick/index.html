How To Cut Front Cowlick Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have a front cowlick that just won't behave? Tired of trying to style your hair around it every day? Cutting your front cowlick might be the solution you've been looking for. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your front cowlick with ease.

Description

A front cowlick is a section of hair that grows in a different direction than the rest of your hair, usually at the front of your head. It can be frustrating to deal with, especially when trying to style your hair. Cutting your front cowlick can help make styling your hair easier and give you a more polished look.

How to Cut Front Cowlick: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. This will help make your hair easier to work with and ensure that it's clean before you start cutting.

Step 2: Towel dry your hair

Use a towel to dry your hair until it's damp. This will make it easier to see where your cowlick is and how it's growing.

Step 3: Section your hair

Using a comb, section off the hair around your cowlick. This will help you focus on the area you want to cut and prevent you from accidentally cutting too much hair.

Step 4: Determine the length you want

Decide how short you want your cowlick to be. It's best to start with a little bit of hair and gradually cut more if needed. Remember, you can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's cut.

Step 5: Cut the hair

Using sharp scissors, start cutting your cowlick. Cut small sections of hair at a time and check your progress frequently. It's best to cut a little at a time and make sure you like the length before cutting more.

Step 6: Blend the hair

Once you've cut your cowlick to the desired length, blend the hair around it to make it look natural. Use thinning shears or scissors to blend the hair around the cowlick, so it looks like it's part of your hair.

Step 7: Style your hair

Now that you've cut your cowlick, style your hair as usual. You'll find that your hair is easier to style, and your cowlick is no longer a problem.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you cut your front cowlick like a pro:

  • Use sharp scissors to make clean cuts
  • Cut a little at a time to avoid cutting too much hair
  • Blend the hair around your cowlick to make it look natural
  • Use a comb to make sure you're cutting the hair evenly

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my cowlick myself?

A: Yes, you can cut your cowlick yourself. Just make sure you're using sharp scissors, cutting a little at a time, and blending the hair around your cowlick to make it look natural.

Q: Can cutting my cowlick damage my hair?

A: Cutting your cowlick shouldn't damage your hair if you're using sharp scissors and cutting a little at a time. Just make sure to take care of your hair after cutting it by using a good conditioner and avoiding too much heat styling.

Q: What if I cut too much hair?

A: If you cut too much hair, don't panic. You can always cut more hair to even it out, or you can visit a hairstylist to fix it.

Hair Care

After cutting your cowlick, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips to help you care for your hair:

  • Use a good shampoo and conditioner that works for your hair type
  • Avoid using too much heat styling, which can damage your hair
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy
  • Eat a healthy diet to nourish your hair from the inside out

Cutting your front cowlick doesn't have to be difficult. With these simple steps and tips, you can cut your cowlick like a pro and enjoy a more polished look. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to keep it healthy and looking its best.
