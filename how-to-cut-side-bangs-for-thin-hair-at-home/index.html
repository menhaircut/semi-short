How To Cut Side Bangs For Thin Hair At Home

Written by Robby Jul 10, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have thin hair and want to try a new look, cutting side bangs is a great option. Not only does it add dimension to your hair, but it can also frame your face and give you a fresh, youthful appearance. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of cutting side bangs for thin hair at home, so you can achieve the look you want without breaking the bank.

How to Cut Side Bangs for Thin Hair at Home

Cutting side bangs for thin hair can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it’s actually quite simple. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips to section your hair
  • A mirror

Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Decide on the Length and Style of Your Bangs

Before you start cutting, you need to decide on the length and style of your bangs. Take into account your face shape, hair type, and personal style. If you’re not sure what style will suit you best, do some research online or consult with a hairstylist.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair by pulling your hair up into a ponytail and then parting it down the middle. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Cut Your Bangs

Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across using sharp scissors. Repeat this process, working your way from the center of your head to the side. Remember to cut a little at a time to avoid cutting too much off.

Step 4: Check Your Work

When you’ve finished cutting your bangs, check your work in the mirror. If you’re happy with the length and style, you’re done! If you need to make any adjustments, go back and trim your bangs until they’re perfect.

Tips for Cutting Side Bangs for Thin Hair at Home

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting side bangs for thin hair at home:

  • Don’t cut your bangs when they’re wet, as they’ll look shorter when they dry.
  • Use sharp scissors, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Always cut a little at a time, as it’s easier to trim more hair off than it is to add it back on.
  • When cutting your bangs, hold your scissors vertically instead of horizontally for a softer, more natural look.

FAQ about Cutting Side Bangs for Thin Hair at Home

Can I cut my side bangs myself?

Yes, you can cut your side bangs yourself. Just make sure you have the right tools and follow the steps outlined in this article.

How often should I trim my side bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to look uneven. As a general rule, aim to trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you cut too much off, don’t panic! You can always wear your hair in a side part or pin your bangs back until they grow out.

Hair Care for Thin Hair with Side Bangs

To keep your thin hair healthy and looking its best, follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as they can weigh your hair down and make it look flat.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to add volume.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and make it look even thinner.

With these tips, you’ll be able to cut side bangs for thin hair at home with confidence. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
