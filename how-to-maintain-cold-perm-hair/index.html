How To Maintain Cold Perm Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 15, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cold perm is a type of perm that is gentler on your hair compared to hot perm. It is a great option for those who want to add volume and texture to their hair without damaging it. However, just like any other chemical treatment, cold perm requires proper maintenance to keep your hair healthy and beautiful. In this article, we will give you a step-by-step guide on how to maintain cold perm hair.

  • How to Maintain Cold Perm Hair

How to Maintain Cold Perm Hair

Maintaining cold perm hair requires a different approach compared to maintaining natural hair or relaxed hair. Here are some tips that you can follow to keep your cold perm hair healthy:

1. Use sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfate is a harsh chemical that can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid damaging your cold perm hair. Look for a shampoo that is specifically formulated for permed hair.

2. Condition your hair regularly

Permed hair needs extra moisture to prevent it from becoming dry and frizzy. Use a deep conditioner at least once a week to keep your hair hydrated. You can also use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra moisture throughout the day.

3. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your cold perm hair. If you must use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to minimize the damage. You can also air dry your hair instead of blow-drying it.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

The sun's UV rays can damage your cold perm hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Use a hat or a scarf to protect your hair when you are out in the sun. You can also use a hair serum that contains SPF to provide extra protection.

5. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet

Wet hair is more fragile and prone to breakage. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet to prevent damage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to maintain cold perm hair:

1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo

Use a sulfate-free shampoo to gently cleanse your hair. Massage your scalp with your fingertips to stimulate blood flow.

2. Apply a deep conditioner

Apply a deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for at least 5 minutes before rinsing it off.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to provide extra moisture throughout the day.

4. Avoid heat styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize the damage.

5. Protect your hair from the sun

Use a hat or a scarf to protect your hair from the sun's UV rays. You can also use a hair serum that contains SPF.

6. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet

Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Tips for Maintaining Cold Perm Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining cold perm hair:

1. Trim your hair regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

2. Sleep on a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent tangles and breakage.

3. Use a protein treatment

Use a protein treatment once a month to strengthen your hair.

4. Avoid using hair products with alcohol

Hair products that contain alcohol can dry out your hair, making it more prone to breakage. Look for products that are alcohol-free.

FAQs

1. How long does a cold perm last?

A cold perm can last up to 6 months, depending on how well you maintain it.

2. Can I color my hair after getting a cold perm?

It is best to wait at least 2 weeks before coloring your hair after getting a cold perm.

3. Can I swim with cold perm hair?

Yes, you can swim with cold perm hair. However, make sure to rinse your hair with fresh water after swimming in chlorinated or saltwater.
