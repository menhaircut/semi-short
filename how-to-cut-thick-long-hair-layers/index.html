How To Cut Thick Long Hair Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 02, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your long and thick hair weighing you down? Layers can be the perfect solution for adding movement, shape, and volume to your locks. However, cutting thick long hair layers can seem like a daunting task. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process step by step, including tips and tricks to make sure you get the perfect cut.

How to Cut Thick Long Hair Layers

Cutting thick long hair layers requires some patience and skill. It’s important to have the right tools and techniques to achieve the desired look. Here are the basic steps for cutting thick long hair layers:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, gather all the necessary tools. You’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors or shears
  • Clips to section hair
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle with water

Step 2: Determine the Length of Your Layers

Decide how many layers you want and how short you want them to be. It’s best to start with longer layers and work your way up if you want more. Remember that you can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 4: Cut the Bottom Layer

Take the bottom section of your hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut across the ends. It’s best to cut small sections at a time to achieve an even cut.

Step 5: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the next section of hair and comb it straight down. Use the previous layer as a guide and cut the new layer slightly shorter. Continue cutting small sections until you reach the desired length.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle layer as a guide and cut the top layer slightly shorter. Continue cutting small sections until you reach the desired length.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Blend the layers together by combing the hair and checking for any uneven spots. Use your scissors to trim any sections that need it.

Step 8: Add Finishing Touches

Check your layers for any uneven spots and trim as necessary. You can also add some face-framing layers by cutting the front sections of your hair slightly shorter.

Tips for Cutting Thick Long Hair Layers

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Start with small sections of hair to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • Use sharp scissors or shears to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure your hair is dry before cutting to avoid any surprises when it dries.
  • Check your layers for any uneven spots and trim as necessary.
  • When in doubt, cut less. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.

FAQs

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own hair?

While it’s possible to cut your own hair, it’s recommended to go to a professional stylist to avoid any mistakes.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always go to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Long Hair Layers

Here are some tips to keep your thick long hair layers looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when wet.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when wet to prevent breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to cut thick long hair layers like a pro. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you’ll end up with a beautiful and voluminous hairstyle.
