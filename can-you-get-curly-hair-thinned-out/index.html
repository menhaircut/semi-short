Can You Get Curly Hair Thinned Out

Written by Christine Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to manage it. From frizz to tangles, curly hair can sometimes feel like a never-ending battle. One way to make your curls more manageable is by thinning them out. But can you get curly hair thinned out? The answer is yes. In this article, we'll discuss how to do it, step by step, and offer some tips along the way.

Description

Thinning out curly hair means removing some of the bulk and weight from your curls. This process can help make your hair more manageable and easier to style. However, it's important to note that not all hair types are suitable for thinning. If your hair is already fine or thinning, thinning it out further could cause damage or breakage.

How to Thin Out Curly Hair

Here are the steps you can follow to thin out your curly hair:

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair as usual. This will help to remove any buildup and make your hair easier to work with.
  2. Once your hair is dry, section it off into small sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to work with.
  3. Using thinning shears, start at the bottom of each section and snip away small sections of hair at a time. Be sure to only cut a small amount of hair at a time, as you can always cut more later.
  4. Continue to work your way up each section, cutting small sections of hair until you've thinned out the entire section.
  5. Repeat this process for each section of your hair until you've thinned out all of your curls.
  6. Once you've finished thinning your hair, wash and condition it again to remove any loose hairs.

Tips for Thinning Out Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when thinning out your curly hair:

  • Be patient. Thinning out curly hair can be a time-consuming process, but it's important to take your time to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the right tools. Thinning shears are specifically designed for thinning out hair, so be sure to use them instead of regular scissors.
  • Work in small sections. Thinning out your hair in small sections will help you to control the amount of hair you're cutting and ensure that you don't cut too much at once.
  • Consult a professional. If you're not confident in your ability to thin out your hair, consider consulting a professional stylist who can do it for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out curly hair:

Is thinning out curly hair damaging?

Thinning out curly hair can be damaging if not done properly. It's important to take your time and only cut small sections of hair at a time to avoid damaging your curls.

Can you thin out curly hair with regular scissors?

You should never attempt to thin out curly hair with regular scissors. Thinning shears are specifically designed for thinning out hair and will give you the best results.

How often should you thin out curly hair?

The frequency at which you should thin out your curly hair depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should only thin out your hair once every few months to avoid damaging it.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Curly Hair

After thinning out your curly hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid using heat styling tools. Heat can damage your curls and make them frizzy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your curls hydrated.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your curls.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid damaging your curls.

Conclusion

Thinning out curly hair can be a great way to make your curls more manageable and easier to style. By following the steps outlined in this article and taking care of your hair after thinning it out, you can achieve healthy, beautiful curls that you'll love.
