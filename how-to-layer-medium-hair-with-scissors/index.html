How To Layer Medium Hair With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Are you looking for a way to add texture and volume to your medium-length hair? Layering your hair with scissors is a great way to achieve this look. In this article, we will guide you through the steps of layering your hair with scissors, as well as provide tips and advice on hair care.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQs

How to Layer Medium Hair with Scissors

Layering your hair with scissors involves cutting different lengths of hair to create depth and volume. It is important to use sharp scissors and to work on dry hair to achieve the best results.

Before you start, make sure you have a clear idea of the type of layers you want. Do you want long layers or short layers? Do you want them to start at the chin or the cheekbones? Once you have a clear idea, you can start the process.

Tools You Will Need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Sectioning clips
  • Comb

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: front, back, and two sides. Use sectioning clips to keep the hair in place.

Step 2: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a section of hair from the back and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Cut the hair at a slight angle, making sure to keep the scissors parallel to the hair.

Repeat this process for the rest of the bottom layer. Make sure to cut the hair at the same angle for a uniform look.

Step 3: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you have finished the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a section of hair from the back and hold it between your fingers, making sure it is at the same level as the bottom layer.

Use the same cutting technique as before, but this time, cut the hair at a slightly steeper angle. This will create more depth and volume.

Repeat this process for the rest of the middle layer.

Step 4: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer of your hair. Take a section of hair from the front and hold it between your fingers, making sure it is at the same level as the middle layer.

Use the same cutting technique as before, but this time, cut the hair at an even steeper angle. This will create the most depth and volume.

Repeat this process for the rest of the top layer.

Tips for Layering Medium Hair with Scissors

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Work on dry hair to achieve accurate results.
  • Start with longer lengths and gradually work your way up to shorter lengths.
  • Remember that less is more. It is easier to go back and cut more hair than it is to fix a mistake.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to uneven cuts.

FAQs

Q: Can I layer my own hair?

A: It is possible to layer your own hair, but it can be difficult. It is recommended to seek the help of a professional stylist for the best results.

Q: How often should I get my hair layered?

A: It is recommended to get your hair layered every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to enhance the volume of your hair.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and texture of your hair.

Layering your medium-length hair with scissors is a great way to add texture and volume to your hair. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a beautiful and stylish look. Remember to take your time, be patient, and seek the help of a professional stylist if needed.
