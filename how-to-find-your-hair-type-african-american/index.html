How To Find Your Hair Type African American

Written by William Jul 26, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Are you an African American struggling to figure out your hair type? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through the steps of finding your hair type and provide tips on how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Understanding Hair Types
  • Step 2: Examining Your Hair
  • Step 3: Determining Your Hair Type
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQs

Step 1: Understanding Hair Types

There are four main hair types: Type 1 (straight), Type 2 (wavy), Type 3 (curly), and Type 4 (kinky/coily). Within each type, there are subcategories that range from A to C.

Type 1 hair is straight and often lacks volume. Type 2 hair has a slight wave to it and may be prone to frizz. Type 3 hair is curly and can range from loose curls to tight coils. Type 4 hair is kinky and coily, with a zig-zag pattern.

Step 2: Examining Your Hair

Now it's time to examine your hair. Take a strand of hair and stretch it out. If it's straight with no curve, you have Type 1 hair. If it has a slight wave, you have Type 2A hair. If it has a more pronounced wave, you have Type 2B hair. If it's very wavy with loose curls, you have Type 2C hair.

If your hair has tight curls, it's likely Type 3. Type 3A hair has loose curls, Type 3B hair has tighter curls, and Type 3C hair has corkscrew curls. If your hair is very kinky and coily, you have Type 4 hair. Type 4A hair has tightly coiled curls, Type 4B hair has a zig-zag pattern, and Type 4C hair has a tight, crimped pattern.

Step 3: Determining Your Hair Type

Now that you know the different hair types and have examined your hair, it's time to determine your hair type. Remember, hair types are not set in stone and can change over time, so don't worry if you're not sure.

If your hair is straight, you have Type 1 hair. If it's wavy, you have Type 2 hair. If it's curly, you have Type 3 hair. If it's kinky/coily, you have Type 4 hair.

Within each type, you can determine the subcategory based on the characteristics of your hair. For example, if you have very tight coils, you likely have Type 4A hair.

Tips for Hair Care

Once you've determined your hair type, it's important to care for it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly, especially if you have Type 3 or Type 4 hair.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, which can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair while sleeping by using a satin or silk bonnet or pillowcase.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

FAQs

Can my hair type change over time?

Yes, hair types can change due to hormonal changes, age, and other factors.

What products should I use for my hair type?

It's important to use products formulated for your hair type. Look for products that cater to Type 1, Type 2, Type 3, or Type 4 hair.

How often should I wash my hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, Type 1 hair can be washed every other day, while Type 3 and Type 4 hair may only need to be washed once a week.

Now that you know how to find your hair type African American, you can start caring for your hair properly. Remember to embrace your natural hair and enjoy the journey!
