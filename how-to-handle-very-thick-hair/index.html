How To Handle Very Thick Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Jun 27, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have thick hair, you know that it can be both a blessing and a curse. While thick hair can look voluminous and healthy, it can also be difficult to manage and style. In this article, we'll explore how to handle very thick hair, from tips on washing and conditioning to styling and maintenance.

Table of Contents

  • Washing and Conditioning
  • Drying Techniques
  • Brushing and Combing
  • Styling Tips
  • Maintenance Tips
  • FAQs

Washing and Conditioning

When it comes to washing and conditioning very thick hair, it's important to use the right products. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for thick or coarse hair. These products will help to nourish and moisturize your hair, while also making it easier to manage.

When washing your hair, be sure to use warm water, as hot water can strip your hair of its natural oils. Gently massage the shampoo into your scalp, focusing on the roots. Rinse thoroughly and then apply conditioner, focusing on the ends of your hair. Leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

Tip:

Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Drying Techniques

Drying very thick hair can take a long time, but there are some techniques you can use to speed up the process. Towel-drying your hair before blow-drying can help to remove excess moisture. Once you've towel-dried your hair, use a hair dryer on a low heat setting, starting at the roots and working your way down.

Avoid using high heat, as this can damage your hair and cause split ends. If you have the time, air-drying your hair is also a great option. Simply comb your hair and let it dry naturally.

Tip:

Consider using a microfiber towel to dry your hair, as this will help to reduce frizz and breakage.

Brushing and Combing

When it comes to brushing and combing very thick hair, it's important to be gentle. Use a wide-toothed comb or paddle brush to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. Avoid tugging or pulling on your hair, as this can cause breakage.

Once your hair is detangled, you can use a boar bristle brush to smooth and style it. Brush your hair in sections, starting at the roots and working your way down. This will help to distribute your hair's natural oils and reduce frizz.

Tip:

Consider using a leave-in conditioner or detangling spray to make brushing and combing easier.

Styling Tips

When it comes to styling very thick hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy. Instead, opt for lightweight products, such as mousse or light-hold gel.

When blow-drying your hair, use a round brush to add volume and shape. Twist your hair around the brush and use the hair dryer to heat it up. Once the section of hair is dry, blast it with cool air to set the style.

Tip:

Consider using hot rollers or a curling iron to add texture and volume to your hair.

Maintenance Tips

Maintaining very thick hair can be a challenge, but there are some things you can do to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. First, consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Second, avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools too often, as these can damage your hair and cause breakage. Finally, be sure to protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside.

Tip:

Consider using a hair mask once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.

FAQs

Q: What causes very thick hair?

A: Thickness is largely genetic, but it can also be influenced by factors such as diet, stress, and hormonal changes.

Q: Can I thin out my very thick hair?

A: Yes, you can ask your stylist to thin out your hair using special scissors or a razor. This can help to reduce bulk and make your hair easier to manage.

Q: How often should I wash my very thick hair?

A: This will depend on your hair type and lifestyle. Some people may need to wash their hair every day, while others can go a few days between washes. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care Products

  • OGX Thick & Full Biotin & Collagen Shampoo
  • TRESemmé Thick & Full Shampoo
  • Pantene Pro-V Smooth & Sleek Shampoo

In addition to shampoo and conditioner, consider using a leave-in conditioner, detangling spray, or hair mask to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

With these tips and tricks, you can learn how to handle very thick hair like a pro. Whether you're looking to reduce frizz, add volume, or just keep your hair healthy and vibrant, there are plenty of things you can do to care for your luscious locks.
