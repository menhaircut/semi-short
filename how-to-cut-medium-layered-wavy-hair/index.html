How To Cut Medium Layered Wavy Hair

Written by Frank Dec 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your medium layered wavy hair and want to switch things up? Perhaps you're looking for a new cut that will complement your natural waves and add some depth and dimension to your look. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting medium layered wavy hair, step-by-step. We'll cover everything from how to prepare for the cut to tips for maintaining your new style. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Tools Needed
  • Sectioning the Hair

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, it's essential to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair and conditioning it thoroughly. This will help to remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup that might make it harder to cut. Once you've washed and conditioned your hair, towel dry it and comb it gently to remove any tangles.

It's also a good idea to decide on the length and style you want before you start cutting. Look at pictures of hairstyles that you like and consider your face shape and hair type. This will help you to achieve the desired look and avoid any mistakes.

Tools Needed

To cut medium layered wavy hair, you'll need a few essential tools, including:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips
  • Water spray bottle
  • Mirror

Make sure that your scissors are sharp and suitable for cutting hair. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve a clean cut.

Sectioning the Hair

Once you have all the necessary tools, it's time to section your hair. Start by dividing your hair into three parts: the top, middle, and bottom sections. Clip the top section away, leaving the middle and bottom sections loose.

Next, divide the middle section into two parts, creating a horizontal parting at the back of your head. Clip the top part away and work on the bottom section first. You can use the comb to create vertical partings to guide you as you cut.

Cutting the Hair

When cutting medium layered wavy hair, it's essential to work with small sections to achieve a more precise cut. Start by taking a small section of hair from the bottom section and combing it straight. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut the desired length. Repeat this process for all the sections in the bottom layer.

Once you've finished the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the top layer and cut it to the same length as the bottom layer. Continue this process until you've finished cutting all the sections in the middle layer.

Finally, unclip the top section and cut it to the same length as the other layers. You can also create some face-framing layers or a side-swept bang, depending on your preference.

Texturizing the Hair

Texturizing your hair can add some volume and movement to your cut. To do this, take small sections of hair and point cut into them with the scissors. This will create some texture and movement in your hair.

Styling the Hair

Once you've cut and texturized your hair, it's time to style it. You can air dry your hair to emphasize your natural waves or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to enhance them. You can also use a curling iron or flat iron to create more defined waves or straighten your hair.

Tips for Maintaining the Style

  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it and make it harder to style.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

FAQs

Q: How often should I cut my medium layered wavy hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut my own medium layered wavy hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair, but it's essential to have the right tools and follow the proper technique to avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to keep your medium layered wavy hair healthy and beautiful. Make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type and avoid using too much heat or chemicals on your hair. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and shiny.

That's it! We hope this article has helped you learn how to cut medium layered wavy hair. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow the proper technique to achieve the desired look. Happy cutting!
