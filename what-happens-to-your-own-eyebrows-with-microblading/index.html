What Happens To Your Own Eyebrows With Microblading

Written by Robby Jun 30, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending hours in front of the mirror every day trying to get your eyebrows just right? If so, microblading may be the solution for you. Microblading is a semi-permanent tattooing technique that can give you full, natural-looking eyebrows. But what happens to your own eyebrows with microblading? Let's take a closer look.

Description

Microblading is a process that involves using a small handheld tool to create tiny, hair-like strokes in the skin beneath your eyebrows. These strokes are filled in with pigment, creating the illusion of full, natural-looking eyebrows. The process typically takes about two hours to complete, and the results can last anywhere from one to three years.

How to

The first step in the microblading process is to schedule a consultation with a professional technician. During this consultation, the technician will discuss your desired eyebrow shape and color, as well as any concerns you may have. Once you've agreed on the details, the technician will numb your eyebrows with a topical anesthetic to minimize any discomfort during the procedure.

Next, the technician will use the handheld tool to create the hair-like strokes in your eyebrows. They will then apply pigment to the strokes, and you'll be able to see the results immediately. The entire process typically takes about two hours, and you'll be able to return to your normal activities right away.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step breakdown of what happens during the microblading process:

Step 1: Consultation

During your consultation, you'll discuss your desired eyebrow shape and color with the technician. They'll also go over any concerns you may have and answer any questions you might have.

Step 2: Numbing

The technician will apply a topical anesthetic to your eyebrows to minimize any discomfort during the procedure.

Step 3: Microblading

The technician will use a handheld tool to create tiny, hair-like strokes in your eyebrows. They will then fill in the strokes with pigment to create a natural-looking eyebrow shape.

Step 4: Results

You'll be able to see the results of your microblading immediately. The entire process typically takes about two hours from start to finish.

Tips

If you're considering microblading, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Research technicians in your area and choose one with plenty of experience and positive reviews.
  • Follow all pre- and post-care instructions to ensure the best possible results.
  • Be prepared for some redness and swelling in the eyebrow area immediately following the procedure.
  • Avoid getting your eyebrows wet for the first week after the procedure to allow them to heal properly.
  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning beds for at least two weeks after the procedure to prevent fading.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about microblading:

Q: Is microblading painful?

A: The numbing cream applied before the procedure typically minimizes any discomfort, but some people may still feel a slight pinch or pressure during the process.

Q: How long does microblading last?

A: Microblading typically lasts anywhere from one to three years, depending on factors like skin type and aftercare.

Q: Is there any downtime after microblading?

A: There is typically no downtime after microblading, though you may experience some redness and swelling in the eyebrow area for a few days following the procedure.

Hair Care

After your microblading procedure, it's important to take good care of your eyebrows to ensure the best possible results. Here are a few hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid getting your eyebrows wet for the first week after the procedure to allow them to heal properly.
  • Avoid using any harsh face washes or scrubs on your eyebrows for the first week after the procedure.
  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning beds for at least two weeks after the procedure to prevent fading.
  • Avoid picking at any scabs that may form on your eyebrows during the healing process.
  • Apply a thin layer of ointment to your eyebrows twice a day for the first week to keep them moisturized.

Overall, microblading can be a great way to achieve natural-looking eyebrows without the daily hassle of filling them in with makeup. If you're considering microblading, be sure to do your research and choose a reputable technician with plenty of experience. Follow all pre- and post-care instructions to ensure the best possible results.
