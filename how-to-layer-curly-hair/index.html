How To Layer Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Layering curly hair can help add volume, shape, and definition to your curls. It can also make styling and maintaining your hair easier. In this guide, we'll show you how to layer curly hair in a relaxed English language. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your hair for layering to tips on maintaining your beautiful curls.

Table of Contents

  • Preparing your hair for layering
  • The cutting process
  • Styling your layered curls
  • Tips for maintaining your curls
  • FAQs
  • Hair care for curly hair

Preparing your hair for layering

Before you start layering your curly hair, you need to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your curls moisturized and prevent them from becoming frizzy. After washing, gently detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Once your hair is detangled, let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair. Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz and damage to your curls. You can also apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and keep them moisturized.

The cutting process

When layering curly hair, it's important to work with your natural curl pattern. This means cutting your hair when it's dry, so you can see how your curls fall naturally. Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: the front, sides, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Starting with the back section, take a small section of hair and twist it around your finger. This will help you see where your curls fall naturally and where to cut. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to trim the ends of your curls. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working from the back to the front.

When layering curly hair, you want to create long layers that blend seamlessly into each other. Avoid cutting your hair too short or creating choppy layers, as this can make your hair look uneven and frizzy.

Styling your layered curls

Once you've layered your curly hair, it's time to style it. You can either let your curls air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair. Avoid using a brush or comb on your curls, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your natural curl pattern.

You can also apply a styling product, such as a curl cream or gel, to help define your curls and keep them in place. Apply the product evenly throughout your hair, scrunching your curls as you go. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to help set your curls.

Tips for maintaining your curls

Maintaining your curly hair can be a challenge, but with a few simple tips, you can keep your curls looking their best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using a towel to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz and damage to your curls.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, as this can cause damage to your curls.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz and maintain your curls.

FAQs

How often should I layer my curly hair?

You should layer your curly hair every 6 to 8 weeks, depending on how quickly your hair grows. This will help maintain your curls' shape and prevent them from becoming weighed down.

Can I layer my curly hair at home?

While it's possible to layer your curly hair at home, we recommend visiting a professional stylist. They have the experience and knowledge to create a layered look that works best for your natural curl pattern.

Will layering my curly hair make it look shorter?

No, layering your curly hair shouldn't make it look shorter. In fact, layering can help add volume and shape to your curls, making them appear longer and fuller.

Hair care for curly hair

Caring for your curly hair is essential to keeping it healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for maintaining your curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as straighteners or curling irons, as this can cause damage to your curls.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and keep them moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up.
  • Sleep on a satin pillowcase to help reduce frizz and maintain your curls.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide on how to layer curly hair, you'll be able to achieve beautiful, defined curls that are easy to maintain. Remember to work with your natural curl pattern and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your curls.
