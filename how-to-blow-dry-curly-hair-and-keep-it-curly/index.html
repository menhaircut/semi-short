How To Blow Dry Curly Hair And Keep It Curly

Written by Petter Nov 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. It looks great when styled properly, but can be a nightmare to deal with when it comes to blow drying. If you're not careful, you can easily end up with frizzy, lifeless curls. However, with the right techniques, you can blow dry your curly hair and keep it looking curly and bouncy. In this blog post, we'll share some tips on how to blow dry curly hair and keep it curly.

Description

Blow drying curly hair is not as simple as just pointing a hair dryer at your head and going to town. Curly hair requires specific techniques to keep it looking curly and well-defined. If you don't take the right steps, you can easily end up with frizz, tangles, and limp curls. In this post, we'll walk you through the process of blow drying curly hair and share some tips to help you keep your curls looking their best.

How to Blow Dry Curly Hair and Keep it Curly: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Start with Clean, Detangled Hair

Before you begin blow drying your curly hair, you need to make sure it's clean and detangled. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and use a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles. You may also want to use a leave-in conditioner to help detangle your hair and keep it moisturized.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before you start blow drying your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or cream to your hair. This will help protect your hair from damage caused by the heat of the blow dryer. Make sure to apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, divide your hair into sections using hair clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to blow dry your hair and ensure that you don't miss any sections. Start by dividing your hair into two sections: top and bottom. Then, divide each of those sections into two more sections: left and right.

Step 4: Use a Diffuser Attachment

If your blow dryer comes with a diffuser attachment, use it. A diffuser will help distribute the heat evenly throughout your hair and reduce frizz. If your hair dryer doesn't come with a diffuser, you can purchase one separately.

Step 5: Start Blow Drying

Start blow drying your hair on the lowest heat setting. Hold the hair dryer about six inches away from your hair and use the diffuser attachment to dry your hair in sections. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends of your hair. Use your fingers to scrunch your curls as you dry them.

Step 6: Finish with Cold Air

Once your hair is mostly dry, switch your hair dryer to the cool setting and use the diffuser attachment to finish drying your hair. The cool air will help set your curls and reduce frizz.

Tips for Blow Drying Curly Hair and Keeping it Curly

Here are some additional tips to help you blow dry your curly hair and keep it curly:

  • Don't over-dry your hair. Curly hair is prone to dryness, so avoid over-drying your hair with the blow dryer.
  • Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair before blow drying. This will help reduce frizz.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much while blow drying. This can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.
  • Finish with a curl defining cream or gel. This will help enhance your curls and reduce frizz.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you blow dry curly hair without a diffuser?

While it's possible to blow dry curly hair without a diffuser, it's not recommended. A diffuser helps distribute the heat evenly throughout your hair and reduce frizz. If you don't have a diffuser attachment, you can purchase one separately.

How often should you blow dry curly hair?

Blow drying curly hair too often can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. It's best to limit blow drying to once or twice a week. On other days, opt for air-drying or diffusing your hair instead.

How can you prevent frizz when blow drying curly hair?

To prevent frizz when blow drying curly hair, make sure to use a heat protectant, a diffuser attachment, and finish with cool air. You can also use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair before blow drying and avoid touching your hair too much while drying.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

If you have curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Conclusion

Blow drying curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques, you can keep your curls looking curly and bouncy. Use a diffuser attachment, start with clean, detangled hair, and finish with cool air to reduce frizz and enhance your curls. Remember to take care of your curly hair properly by using sulfate-free shampoo, deep conditioning treatments, and avoiding heat styling tools too often.
