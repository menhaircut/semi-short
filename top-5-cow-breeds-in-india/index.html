Top 5 Cow Breeds In India

Written by Eveline Aug 03, 2023 · 4 min read

If you are looking for the best cow breeds in India, then you have come to the right place. In this article, we will discuss the top 5 cow breeds in India that are popular for their milk production, meat quality, and overall usefulness. We will also explore the characteristics, benefits, and challenges of each breed, as well as some tips for raising and caring for them.

Table of Contents

  1. Gir Cow
  2. Sahiwal Cow
  3. Murrah Buffalo
  4. Tharparkar Cow
  5. Red Sindhi Cow

1. Gir Cow

The Gir cow is one of the most popular cow breeds in India, known for its high milk production and docile temperament. This breed is native to the Gir forest in Gujarat and is also called Bhadawari or Desan. The milk from Gir cows is rich in fat, protein, and minerals, making it ideal for dairy products like ghee, butter, and cheese.

Gir cows have a distinctive hump on their shoulders and a reddish-brown coat. They are well-adapted to hot and humid climates and can graze on rough terrain. However, they require good quality feed and clean water to maintain their health and productivity. Gir cows are also susceptible to diseases like mastitis and foot-and-mouth disease, so regular veterinary check-ups are necessary.

2. Sahiwal Cow

The Sahiwal cow is another popular breed in India, originally from Punjab and Haryana. This breed is known for its high milk yield, hardiness, and adaptability. Sahiwal cows have a reddish-brown coat with white markings and a distinctive hump. They are resistant to diseases like tick fever and are well-suited to grazing on arid land.

The milk from Sahiwal cows is high in fat and protein and has a creamy texture. It is also rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a nutritious food source. Sahiwal cows are easy to handle and have a calm disposition, but they require good quality feed and clean water to maintain their health and productivity.

3. Murrah Buffalo

The Murrah buffalo is a breed of water buffalo found in Punjab, Haryana, and Uttar Pradesh. It is known for its high milk yield, meat quality, and draught power. Murrah buffaloes have a black coat with white markings and a broad forehead. They are well-adapted to hot and humid climates and can graze on low-quality fodder.

The milk from Murrah buffaloes is high in fat and protein and has a creamy texture. It is also used to make dairy products like paneer, yogurt, and ice cream. Murrah buffaloes are docile and easy to handle, but they require regular bathing and grooming to maintain their hygiene and health.

4. Tharparkar Cow

The Tharparkar cow is a breed of cattle found in Rajasthan and Gujarat. It is known for its high milk yield, hardiness, and adaptability. Tharparkar cows have a white coat with black markings and a hump. They are resistant to diseases like foot-and-mouth disease and can graze on arid land.

The milk from Tharparkar cows is high in fat and protein and has a sweet taste. It is also used to make dairy products like ghee, butter, and cheese. Tharparkar cows are easy to handle and have a calm temperament, but they require good quality feed and clean water to maintain their health and productivity.

5. Red Sindhi Cow

The Red Sindhi cow is a breed of cattle found in Sindh province of Pakistan and Rajasthan and Punjab states of India. It is known for its high milk yield, meat quality, and hardiness. Red Sindhi cows have a reddish-brown coat and a hump. They are resistant to diseases like tick fever and are well-suited to grazing on arid land.

The milk from Red Sindhi cows is high in fat and protein and has a rich flavor. It is also used to make dairy products like butter and cheese. Red Sindhi cows are docile and easy to handle, but they require good quality feed and clean water to maintain their health and productivity.

Conclusion

In conclusion, these are the top 5 cow breeds in India that are popular for their milk production, meat quality, and overall usefulness. Each breed has its own characteristics, benefits, and challenges, so it is important to choose the right one based on your needs and resources. Whether you are a dairy farmer, a meat producer, or a hobbyist, raising cows can be a rewarding and fulfilling experience.
