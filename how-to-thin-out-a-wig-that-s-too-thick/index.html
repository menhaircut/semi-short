How To Thin Out A Wig That s Too Thick

Written by Aprilia Aug 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you having trouble with your wig being too thick? If so, you're not alone. Many people have experienced the same issue, and it can be frustrating when your wig doesn't look the way you want it to. But don't worry, there are ways to thin out a wig that's too thick. In this blog post, we will be discussing how to thin out a wig that's too thick, step by step, along with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect look.

Description

Thick wigs can be a headache to manage, especially if you're new to the wig game. They can look unnatural and bulky, which can be a dead giveaway that you're wearing a wig. Thinning out your wig can help to create a more natural look and make the wig easier to style. Thinning out a wig involves removing some of the hair strands from the wig to create a lighter and more natural-looking style.

How to Thin Out a Wig That's Too Thick

Step 1: Brush Your Wig

The first step to thinning out your wig is to brush it. This helps to remove any tangles and knots that may have formed in the wig. Use a wide-tooth comb or a wig brush to gently brush through the wig. Make sure to start at the ends of the wig and work your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Section Your Wig

Once you've brushed your wig, the next step is to section it. Use hair clips to divide your wig into sections. You can create as many sections as you need, depending on the thickness of your wig. This will help you to thin out the wig more evenly.

Step 3: Thin Out the Wig

Now it's time to start thinning out your wig. Use thinning shears to cut the strands of hair on each section. Start from the ends of the hair and work your way upwards. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can make your wig look uneven. Continue thinning out each section until you've achieved your desired thickness.

Step 4: Style Your Wig

Once you've thinned out your wig, it's time to style it. You can use a curling iron, straightener, or other styling tools to create your desired look. If you're unsure how to style your wig, you can always consult a professional stylist or watch tutorial videos online.

Tips

- Use a wig stand when styling your wig to keep it in place.

- Be patient when thinning out your wig. Take your time and make sure you're not cutting too much hair at once.

- Invest in high-quality thinning shears to make the process easier.

FAQ

Can I thin out a synthetic wig?

It's not recommended to thin out a synthetic wig as it can damage the fibers. If you're looking to thin out your synthetic wig, consider taking it to a professional stylist.

Can I thin out my wig if it's already styled?

Yes, you can thin out your wig even if it's already styled. However, it's recommended to thin out your wig before styling it to achieve the best results.

How often should I thin out my wig?

It depends on how often you wear your wig and how quickly it grows out. It's recommended to thin out your wig every 6-8 weeks to maintain its thickness.

Hair Care

Thinning out your wig is just one part of wig maintenance. In order to keep your wig looking its best, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

- Store your wig on a wig stand when not in use to prevent tangling.

- Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to prevent damage to the fibers.

- Wash your wig with a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and soft.

Conclusion

Thinning out a wig that's too thick can seem daunting, but it's actually a simple process. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can achieve a natural-looking wig that's easy to style. Remember to be patient and take your time, and your wig will look great in no time.
