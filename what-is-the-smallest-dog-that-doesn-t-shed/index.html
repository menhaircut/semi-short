What Is The Smallest Dog That Doesn t Shed

If you're looking for a small dog that doesn't shed, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll be discussing what is the smallest dog that doesn't shed, and provide you with some helpful tips and tricks for taking care of your new furry friend.

Description

The smallest dog that doesn't shed is the Yorkshire Terrier. Also known as Yorkies, these little dogs are known for their small size, playful personalities, and hypoallergenic coats. They typically weigh between four and seven pounds, and have a lifespan of 12 to 15 years.

How to

If you're interested in getting a Yorkie, the first step is to find a reputable breeder. Look for a breeder who is registered with a kennel club, and who has a good reputation in the community. Once you've found a breeder, it's important to ask questions about the dog's health history, temperament, and any potential health issues that may arise.

When you bring your Yorkie home, it's important to provide them with a comfortable and safe living space. This can be a crate or a small bed, depending on your dog's preference. You'll also need to provide them with food and water, as well as toys and treats to keep them entertained.

Step by Step

1. Find a reputable breeder

2. Ask questions about the dog's health history and temperament

3. Provide a comfortable and safe living space

4. Provide food, water, toys, and treats

Tips

1. Yorkies require regular grooming to keep their coats healthy and free of tangles. This includes brushing their hair daily, trimming their nails, and cleaning their ears.

2. Yorkies are prone to dental issues, so it's important to brush their teeth regularly and provide them with dental treats and toys.

3. Yorkies are energetic dogs, so it's important to provide them with plenty of exercise and playtime. This can include walks, trips to the park, and playing fetch.

FAQ

Q: Are Yorkies good with children?

A: Yes, Yorkies are good with children, but they should always be supervised when playing together.

Q: Do Yorkies bark a lot?

A: Yorkies can be vocal dogs and may bark to alert their owners of potential danger. However, proper training can help to minimize excessive barking.

Hair Care

Yorkies have a long, silky, and hypoallergenic coat that requires regular grooming. This includes daily brushing to prevent tangles and mats, as well as regular baths to keep their coat clean and shiny.

You'll also need to trim their hair regularly, especially around their eyes and ears, to prevent irritation and infection. It's important to use sharp scissors and to be careful not to cut their skin.

In addition to grooming, you should also provide your Yorkie with a healthy diet and plenty of exercise to keep their coat and skin healthy from the inside out.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the smallest dog that doesn't shed is the Yorkshire Terrier. They are playful, hypoallergenic, and make great companions for those with allergies. If you're interested in getting a Yorkie, be sure to find a reputable breeder and provide them with proper care and grooming to keep them happy and healthy for years to come.
