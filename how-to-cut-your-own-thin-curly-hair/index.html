How To Cut Your Own Thin Curly Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thin curly hair. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting your own thin curly hair, from preparation to finishing touches. So, let's get started!

  3. Sectioning Your Hair

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure your hair is clean and dry. It's also important to have a clear idea of the type of haircut you want to achieve. You can find inspiration from magazines, social media, or even consult with a professional hairstylist.

Tools You Will Need

To cut your own thin curly hair, you will need the following tools:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage to your hair.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is crucial to achieving an even haircut. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start by dividing your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back.
  2. Clip the sections you are not currently working on to keep them out of the way.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Follow these steps:

  1. Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the sections you are working on.
  2. Comb the hair straight down and hold it between your index and middle fingers.
  3. Using your scissors, cut the hair at a slight angle, starting from the outside and working your way in.
  4. Repeat this process for the rest of the section, making sure to cut at the same angle.
  5. Continue with the other sections until you have cut all of your hair.

Trimming Your Bangs

If you have bangs, here's how to trim them:

  1. Comb your bangs straight down.
  2. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at a slight angle, starting from the outside and working your way in.
  3. Repeat this process until you have achieved the desired length.

Finishing Touches

Once you have finished cutting your hair, it's time to make sure everything is even. Here's what to do:

  1. Comb your hair straight down and check for any uneven areas.
  2. If you notice any uneven areas, take small sections of hair and trim them until everything is even.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger cuts.
  • Take breaks if you get tired or if your hands start to cramp.
  • If you're not sure about cutting too much hair, start with a smaller amount and work your way up.
  • Don't cut your hair too short. You can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair if I have never done it before?

Yes, you can. However, it's important to start with small cuts and work your way up to larger cuts. You can also seek advice from a professional hairstylist.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on your hair type and the style you are trying to achieve. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut my hair while it's wet?

It's not recommended to cut your hair while it's wet because wet hair tends to look longer than it actually is. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut.

Cutting your own thin curly hair can be a rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut in the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and seek advice if you're unsure. Happy cutting!
