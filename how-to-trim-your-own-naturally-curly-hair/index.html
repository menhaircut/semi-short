How To Trim Your Own Naturally Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 02, 2023 · 4 min read

As someone with naturally curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to maintain your locks. One of the most challenging aspects of curly hair care is trimming your hair. Many people with curly hair avoid getting haircuts because they don't trust a stylist to properly handle their curls. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can learn how to trim your own naturally curly hair.

Description

Trimming your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're new to the process. However, with the right guidance, anyone can learn how to cut their own hair. This article will walk you through the steps of trimming your own naturally curly hair, from start to finish. We'll also provide tips and advice on how to care for your hair after you trim it.

How to Trim Your Own Naturally Curly Hair

Step One: Gather Your Tools

The first step in trimming your own curly hair is to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. You may also want to use hair clips to help section your hair.

Step Two: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start trimming your hair, it's important to wash and condition it. This will help remove any buildup or product from your hair, making it easier to work with. Be sure to use a conditioner that is designed for curly hair, as this will help keep your curls hydrated and defined.

Step Three: Detangle Your Hair

Once your hair is clean and conditioned, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls. This will help you see the natural shape of your curls and determine where you need to trim.

Step Four: Section Your Hair

Using hair clips, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. This will make it easier to work with each section and ensure that you don't miss any spots.

Step Five: Trim Your Hair

Starting with one section at a time, take a small section of hair and twist it gently. Use your scissors to trim the ends of the twisted hair, cutting no more than 1/4 inch at a time. Repeat this process on all sections of your hair.

Step Six: Check Your Work

Once you've finished trimming your hair, take a moment to check your work. Make sure that your hair is even and that you haven't missed any spots. If you notice any areas that need more trimming, repeat the process until you're satisfied with the results.

Tips for Trimming Your Own Naturally Curly Hair

Trimming your own curly hair can be tricky, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve a great look:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Trim your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is
  • Don't cut too much at once; it's better to trim a little at a time
  • Take breaks if you need to; trimming your own hair can be tiring

FAQ

Q: How often should I trim my curly hair?

A: It's recommended that you trim your hair every 8-12 weeks to maintain healthy ends.

Q: Can I use regular scissors to trim my hair?

A: No, regular scissors can damage your hair and cause split ends. Be sure to use scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair.

Hair Care After Trimming

After trimming your hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain healthy curls. Be sure to use a conditioner that is designed for curly hair, and avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. You may also want to use a leave-in conditioner or curl-defining cream to enhance your curls.

Conclusion

Trimming your own naturally curly hair is a great way to save money and maintain healthy locks. With the right tools and techniques, anyone can learn how to cut their own hair. Just be sure to take your time, use sharp scissors, and follow the tips and advice provided in this article.
