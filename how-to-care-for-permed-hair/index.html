How To Care For Permed Hair Tips Tricks And Faqs

Written by Christine Sep 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Perming your hair is a great way to add texture and volume to otherwise limp and lifeless hair. But, like any chemical treatment, it requires a bit of extra care and attention to maintain its health and vitality.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQs

Description

Perming your hair involves using chemicals to break down the bonds in your hair, allowing it to be reshaped into curls or waves. This process can be damaging to your hair and scalp, so it's essential to take extra care of your permed hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.

How to

Caring for permed hair is all about gentle treatment and regular maintenance. Here are some tips to help you keep your permed hair looking its best:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Permed hair is more fragile than natural hair, so it's essential to use a gentle, pH-balanced shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons can be damaging to permed hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low.

3. Don't overwash your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day, and use a dry shampoo in between washes to freshen up your hair.

4. Moisturize your hair regularly

Permed hair is more prone to dryness than natural hair, so it's essential to keep it moisturized with regular deep conditioning treatments and leave-in conditioners.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to care for permed hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair with a gentle, pH-balanced shampoo and conditioner.

Make sure to use lukewarm water and massage the shampoo gently into your scalp.

Step 2: Towel-dry your hair gently.

Avoid rubbing your hair with a towel, as this can cause friction and damage to your hair. Instead, gently blot your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner.

Permed hair needs extra moisture, so apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 4: Style your hair gently.

Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to gently style your hair, avoiding any tugging or pulling. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low.

Step 5: Deep condition your hair regularly.

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your permed hair healthy and moisturized.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help you care for your permed hair:

  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and damage to your hair while you sleep.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause it to break and become damaged.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your permed hair, such as tight ponytails or braids.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my permed hair?

A: You should wash your permed hair no more than every other day, as washing it too often can strip it of its natural oils.

Q: Can I color my permed hair?

A: Yes, you can color your permed hair, but it's best to wait at least two weeks after perming your hair to allow it to settle and recover.

Q: Can I still use regular shampoo and conditioner on my permed hair?

A: Yes, but make sure to use a gentle, pH-balanced shampoo and conditioner that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

Hair Care

Caring for permed hair requires a bit of extra care and attention, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your permed hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid heat styling, and moisturize regularly to keep your permed hair looking its best.
