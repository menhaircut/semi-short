Best Hair Color For Older Olive Skin A Guide For 2023

Written by Robby Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're an older woman with olive skin, you may be wondering what hair color will best suit you. Choosing the right hair color can make a big difference in how you look and feel, so it's important to choose wisely. In this article, we'll be discussing the best hair color for older olive skin and providing you with tips on how to maintain your hair's health and vibrancy.

Description

As we age, our skin tone changes, and so should our hair color. For those with olive skin, it's important to choose a hair color that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty. Olive skin is characterized by a yellow or green undertone, which can make it difficult to find the right hair color.

How To Choose The Best Hair Color

When choosing a hair color, it's important to consider your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color. For those with olive skin, warm tones like caramel, honey, and golden blonde work best. These colors complement the yellow and green undertones in olive skin and provide a natural-looking glow.

If you're looking to go darker, opt for rich browns like chocolate, chestnut, or mahogany. These colors add depth and dimension to your hair while still complementing your skin tone. Avoid cool-toned colors like ash blonde or platinum, as these colors can make olive skin look washed out.

Step by Step Guide

1. Determine your skin tone - If you're not sure what your skin tone is, hold a piece of white paper next to your face in natural light. If your skin appears yellow or green, you have olive skin.

2. Choose warm-toned colors - When choosing a hair color, opt for warm tones that complement your skin tone. Golden blonde, caramel, and honey work well for those with olive skin.

3. Consider going darker - If you're looking to go darker, choose rich browns like chocolate or chestnut. These colors add depth and dimension to your hair while still complementing your skin tone.

4. Avoid cool-toned colors - Cool-toned colors like ash blonde or platinum can make olive skin look washed out. Stick to warm-toned colors for the best results.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair's Health and Vibrancy

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo - Sulfates can be harsh on hair and strip it of its natural oils. Use a sulfate-free shampoo to keep your hair healthy and vibrant.

2. Deep condition regularly - Olive skin tends to have thicker hair, so it's important to deep condition regularly to keep your hair soft and manageable.

3. Protect your hair from the sun - The sun can be damaging to hair, especially if it's already colored. Use a hat or scarf to protect your hair when you're outside.

FAQ

Q: Can I dye my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair at home, but it's important to follow the instructions carefully and choose the right color for your skin tone.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how noticeable the roots are. Generally, it's recommended to touch up roots every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to using sulfate-free shampoo and deep conditioning regularly, there are other things you can do to keep your hair healthy and vibrant:

1. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair

2. Don't use too much heat when styling your hair

3. Use a heat protectant spray when using hot tools

4. Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Choosing the best hair color for older olive skin can be a challenge, but with the right tips and tricks, you can find a color that complements your skin tone and enhances your natural beauty. Remember to choose warm-toned colors, avoid cool-toned colors, and take care of your hair to keep it healthy and vibrant.
