What Is The Difference Between An Undercut And A Fade

Written by Oliver Jul 20, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, you might be considering an undercut or a fade. While both of these haircuts involve shorter sides and longer hair on top, there are some key differences to keep in mind. In this article, we'll break down the difference between an undercut and a fade, as well as provide tips for styling and caring for your new haircut.

What is an Undercut?

An undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the sides and back of the head is cut very short, while the hair on the top is left longer. The hair on the top can be styled in a variety of ways, including slicked back, side swept, or messy. The contrast between the short sides and longer top creates a bold, edgy look.

What is a Fade?

A fade is a haircut where the hair gradually gets shorter from the top of the head down to the neckline. The hair on the sides and back of the head is buzzed or shaved very short, while the hair on the top is left longer. The transition between the short sides and longer top is seamless, creating a clean, polished look.

The Difference Between an Undercut and a Fade

While both an undercut and a fade involve shorter sides and longer hair on top, there are a few key differences to keep in mind. The main difference is in the way the sides are cut. With an undercut, the sides are buzzed very short or shaved, while with a fade, the hair gradually gets shorter from the top down to the neckline.

Another difference is in the styling options. With an undercut, the longer hair on top can be styled in a variety of ways, while with a fade, the hair on top is usually styled in a specific way, such as a slicked back or side swept look. Additionally, a fade tends to have a cleaner, more polished look, while an undercut has a more edgy, bold look.

How to Style an Undercut or Fade

Styling an undercut or fade is relatively simple. For an undercut, simply apply a small amount of pomade or styling cream to the longer hair on top and style as desired. For a fade, use a comb to style the hair on top into your preferred look, such as a slicked back or side swept look. You can also add a small amount of pomade to help hold the style in place.

Tips for Maintaining an Undercut or Fade

To keep your undercut or fade looking great, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First, be sure to get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your haircut. Second, use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and clean. Finally, consider using a styling product, such as pomade or styling cream, to help hold your hairstyle in place.

FAQs

Can an undercut or fade work for any hair type?

Yes, an undercut or fade can work for any hair type, including straight, wavy, and curly hair. However, the styling options may vary depending on your hair type.

How often should I get a trim for an undercut or fade?

You should get a trim for an undercut or fade every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.

Do I need to use a specific type of shampoo or conditioner for an undercut or fade?

While you don't necessarily need to use a specific type of shampoo or conditioner for an undercut or fade, it's important to use a high-quality product that will help keep your hair healthy and clean.

Final Thoughts

Whether you're considering an undercut or a fade, both haircuts offer a stylish, modern look. By understanding the difference between the two, as well as how to style and care for your new haircut, you'll be able to rock your new look with confidence.
