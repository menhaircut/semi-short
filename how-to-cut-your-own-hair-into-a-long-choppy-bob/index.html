How to Cut Your Own Hair into a Long Choppy Bob

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want to save some money, cutting your own hair into a long choppy bob is a great option. This style is trendy and easy to maintain, making it a popular choice for many women. In this article, we'll go over how to cut your own hair into a long choppy bob, step by step.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair into a Long Choppy Bob
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut Your Own Hair into a Long Choppy Bob

The first step in cutting your own hair into a long choppy bob is to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair ties. It's also a good idea to have a handheld mirror or a mirror that you can position behind you, so you can see the back of your head.

Next, decide how long you want your hair to be. A long choppy bob typically falls anywhere between your chin and your collarbone. Once you've determined the length, start by sectioning off your hair. Use a comb to create a part down the middle of your head, and then section off the sides of your hair, leaving the back section down.

Step by Step Guide

1. Take a small section of hair from the back and pull it forward over your shoulder.

2. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and cut it straight across.

3. Repeat this process, working your way up your head, and be sure to cut the hair at an angle to create the choppy effect.

4. Once you've finished the back section, move on to the sides, taking small sections of hair and cutting them at an angle.

5. Finally, trim any uneven pieces and style as desired.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

- Start with small sections of hair and work your way up, so you don't accidentally cut off too much.

- Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.

- Cut at an angle to create the choppy effect.

- Take breaks if you need to, and don't rush the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cut my hair into a long choppy bob if I have curly hair?

A: Yes, you can still cut your hair into a long choppy bob if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that the length may appear shorter than intended due to the curls.

Q: How often should I trim my hair if I have a long choppy bob?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care Tips

- Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type.

- Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can damage your hair.

- Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

- Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.

Cutting your own hair into a long choppy bob may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Remember to take your time and start with small sections of hair, and you'll be able to achieve a trendy and stylish new look.
