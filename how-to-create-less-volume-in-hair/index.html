How To Create Less Volume In Hair

Written by Robby Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you have naturally voluminous hair that you struggle to manage? Or do you find that your hair becomes too voluminous after washing or styling? Well, fret not! In this blog post, we will share with you some tips and tricks on how to create less volume in hair.

Description: Why Does Hair Become Voluminous?

Before we dive into the tips and tricks, let's understand why hair becomes voluminous in the first place. Hair becomes voluminous due to factors such as genetics, humidity, and styling techniques. When hair is exposed to humidity, it absorbs moisture from the air, causing the hair shafts to swell and creating more volume. Similarly, certain styling techniques such as blow-drying and teasing can also add volume to hair.

How to Create Less Volume in Hair: Tips and Tricks

1. Use a Clarifying Shampoo

If you have naturally oily hair, the excess oil can contribute to voluminous hair. To combat this, use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any buildup of oil and product in your hair.

2. Use a Hair Straightener

A hair straightener is a great tool to use if you want to create less volume in your hair. By straightening your hair, you are essentially flattening the hair shafts, which reduces the amount of volume in your hair.

3. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

While it may seem counterintuitive, using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can actually help to reduce volume in your hair. These products contain ingredients that coat the hair strands, making them appear smoother and flatter.

4. Blow-Dry Your Hair Using a Round Brush

When blow-drying your hair, use a round brush to smooth out the hair shafts and create a sleeker look. This will help to reduce volume in your hair.

5. Avoid Teasing Your Hair

Teasing your hair can add volume, but it can also cause damage to your hair over time. Instead, try using hair accessories such as hair clips or headbands to create volume-free hairstyles.

Step by Step: How to Create Less Volume in Hair

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any excess oil and buildup of product.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Conditioner

Apply a volumizing conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Avoid applying conditioner to your roots, as this can contribute to voluminous hair.

Step 3: Blow-Dry Your Hair

Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to smooth out the hair shafts and create a sleeker look.

Step 4: Use a Hair Straightener

Use a hair straightener to straighten your hair, which will help to reduce volume.

Step 5: Use Hair Accessories

Use hair accessories such as hair clips or headbands to create volume-free hairstyles.

Tips for Maintaining Less Volume in Hair

1. Use a Dry Shampoo

Using a dry shampoo can help to absorb excess oil and keep your hair looking less voluminous.

2. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends, which can contribute to voluminous hair.

3. Avoid Humidity

Avoiding humidity as much as possible can help to prevent your hair from becoming voluminous due to moisture absorption.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use a volumizing styling product?

A: Yes, you can use a volumizing styling product, but be sure to use it sparingly and avoid applying it to your roots.

Q: Can I still curl my hair?

A: Yes, you can still curl your hair, but be sure to use a hair straightener or flat iron to smooth out the hair shafts before curling.

Q: Is it safe to use a hair straightener every day?

A: It is not recommended to use a hair straightener every day, as it can cause damage to your hair over time. Instead, try to limit your use to once or twice a week.

Hair Care: Taking Care of Your Hair

Remember to take care of your hair by using a nourishing hair mask once a week, and avoiding harsh chemicals such as bleach or hair dye. Additionally, be sure to protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.

With these tips and tricks, you can create less volume in your hair and achieve a sleek, polished look. By following a consistent hair care routine and using the right techniques and products, you can manage your voluminous hair with ease.
