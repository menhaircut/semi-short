How To Use Thinning Shears On Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Thinning shears are a useful tool for achieving a variety of hairstyles. They can help you achieve a layered look, remove bulk from thick hair, and blend different lengths of hair together. However, using thinning shears requires a bit of skill and knowledge to avoid damaging your hair. In this guide, we’ll show you how to use thinning shears on hair safely and effectively.

Table of Contents

  • How to Use Thinning Shears on Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Use Thinning Shears on Hair

Thinning shears are a type of scissors with teeth on one or both blades that cut some of the hair while leaving some untouched. They are used to remove bulk from thick hair, blend different lengths of hair together, or create a layered look.

Before using thinning shears, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can stretch and make it difficult to cut evenly. Also, make sure your shears are clean and sharp. Dull or dirty shears can damage your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s how to use thinning shears on hair:

  1. Section your hair into manageable parts. Thinning shears work best on small, manageable sections of hair. Use clips to secure the rest of your hair away from the section you’re working on.
  2. Comb the section of hair you’re working on. Make sure it’s tangle-free and smooth.
  3. Hold the section of hair between your fingers, close to the root. The amount you hold will depend on how much hair you want to remove. The more hair you hold, the more you’ll remove.
  4. Open the thinning shears and rest the teeth against the section of hair. The teeth should be facing down towards the ends of your hair.
  5. Close the shears on the section of hair, sliding them down towards the ends. The teeth should cut some of the hair while leaving some untouched. Repeat this process until you’ve removed the desired amount of hair.
  6. Repeat steps 2-5 on the rest of your hair, section by section.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Start with a small amount of hair. You can always cut more, but you can’t put hair back once it’s been cut.
  • Don’t use thinning shears on wet hair. Wet hair can stretch and make it difficult to cut evenly.
  • Use sharp shears. Dull or dirty shears can damage your hair.
  • Only cut in a downward motion. Cutting in an upward motion can damage your hair and create uneven layers.
  • Keep the shears at an angle. Holding the shears at a 45-degree angle can create a more natural look.

FAQs

Can thinning shears damage my hair?

Yes, if not used properly, thinning shears can damage your hair. Make sure your hair is clean and dry before using thinning shears and use sharp, clean shears.

Can I use thinning shears on curly hair?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on curly hair. However, it’s important to be careful not to overdo it, as too much thinning can cause curly hair to lose its shape.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

It depends on your hair type and the style you’re trying to achieve. If you have thick hair, you may need to use thinning shears more often. However, it’s important not to overdo it, as too much thinning can damage your hair.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain its shape.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and looking great after using thinning shears.
