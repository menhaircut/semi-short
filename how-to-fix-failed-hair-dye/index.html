How To Fix Failed Hair Dye A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you dealing with hair dye that didn't turn out as expected? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people experience this issue, and it can be frustrating. However, there are ways to fix failed hair dye without having to go to a salon. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to correct the problem and achieve the desired color.

What Causes Failed Hair Dye?

Before we dive into how to fix it, let's talk about why hair dye can fail. There are several reasons why this can happen:

  • The dye wasn't left on long enough
  • The dye wasn't mixed properly
  • The dye was old or expired
  • The hair wasn't prepped correctly

How to Fix Failed Hair Dye

Now, let's get into the steps to fix failed hair dye:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Take a good look at your hair and determine what went wrong. Is the color too light? Too dark? Did it turn out brassy or orange? Once you know what you're dealing with, you can move on to the next step.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

The first thing you should do is wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will help remove any excess dye and prepare your hair for the next steps.

Step 3: Apply a Color Remover

If the color is too dark, you can use a color remover to lighten it. Make sure to follow the instructions carefully and be patient. It may take a few applications to achieve the desired result.

Step 4: Use a Toner

If your hair has turned brassy or orange, you can use a toner to neutralize the color. Look for a toner with a blue or purple base, as these colors will cancel out the orange tones.

Step 5: Redye Your Hair

If the color is too light, you can try redyeing your hair with a darker shade. Make sure to choose a color that is at least one shade darker than the original color.

Tips for Fixing Failed Hair Dye

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Always follow the instructions on the hair dye box
  • Don't leave the dye on for too long
  • Make sure to mix the dye thoroughly
  • Use a timer to keep track of how long the dye has been on
  • If you're unsure about a color, do a strand test first

FAQs

Can I fix failed hair dye without going to a salon?

Yes, you can fix failed hair dye at home by following the steps outlined above.

How long should I leave the color remover on?

Make sure to follow the instructions on the product carefully. The amount of time will vary depending on the brand.

Can I dye my hair again right after using a color remover?

No, you should wait at least 24 hours before dyeing your hair again.

Hair Care After Fixing Failed Hair Dye

Once you've fixed your failed hair dye, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the color. Here are some tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid washing your hair too often
  • Protect your hair from the sun
  • Avoid heat styling as much as possible

With these tips and steps, you should be able to fix any failed hair dye mishaps. Remember to be patient and follow the instructions carefully. Good luck!
