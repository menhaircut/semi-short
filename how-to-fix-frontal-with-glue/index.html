How To Fix Frontal With Glue Your Ultimate Guide

Written by Petter Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your frontal constantly shifting and slipping? Gluing your frontal in place may be the solution you've been looking for. In this post, we'll walk you through the steps on how to fix frontal with glue. From preparation to application and maintenance, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • Application
  • Maintenance
  • Tips for a Successful Fix
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

Preparation

Before you start gluing your frontal, it's important to properly prepare your hairline and frontal. Here's what you'll need:

  • Alcohol or Witch Hazel
  • Cotton Balls or Pads
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Frontal Glue
  • Blow Dryer

Start by cleaning your hairline with alcohol or witch hazel to remove any oils or residue. Then, use scissors to trim any excess lace on your frontal. Comb your hair back and away from your hairline to ensure a smooth and even application. Finally, use a blow dryer on low heat to dry any excess moisture.

Application

It's time to apply the glue to your frontal. Here's how to do it:

  • Apply a thin layer of glue along the hairline of your frontal. Be sure to avoid getting glue on your hair.
  • Use a blow dryer on cool air to dry the glue for about 30 seconds.
  • Apply another thin layer of glue over the first layer.
  • Repeat the blow dryer process until the glue becomes tacky.
  • Carefully place the frontal onto your hairline, starting from the center and working your way outwards.
  • Use a comb or brush to press the frontal into place and smooth out any bubbles or creases.
  • Use a blow dryer on low heat to fully dry the glue and ensure a secure hold.

Maintenance

Now that your frontal is in place, it's important to properly care for it to ensure it lasts. Here are some maintenance tips:

  • Avoid getting your hair wet or sweating excessively for at least 24 hours after application.
  • Avoid using oil-based products near the hairline, as they can break down the glue.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on the frontal, as this can cause it to lift.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to prevent friction and tangling while sleeping.

Tips for a Successful Fix

Here are some additional tips to ensure a successful frontal fix:

  • Choose a high-quality glue specifically designed for frontal application.
  • Apply the glue in thin layers to avoid a bulky and unnatural appearance.
  • Allow each layer of glue to fully dry before applying the next layer.
  • Avoid applying too much glue, as this can cause the glue to seep through the lace and onto your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing frontal with glue:

How long will the glue hold?

The longevity of the glue hold will depend on a variety of factors, such as the quality of the glue, your hair type, and your maintenance routine. However, with proper care, you can expect the glue hold to last for about 2-4 weeks.

Can I swim or shower with a glued frontal?

Avoid getting your hair wet or sweating excessively for at least 24 hours after application. After that, you can shower and swim as usual. However, avoid submerging your head underwater for extended periods of time, as this can cause the glue to loosen.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to maintaining a healthy and beautiful frontal. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly with products designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using high heat on your hair, such as flat irons and curling irons, as this can damage the hair and cause the frontal to lift.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep the hair moisturized and prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Trim your ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

Now that you know how to fix frontal with glue, you can confidently rock your frontal without any worries. Remember to properly prepare, apply, and maintain your frontal for a successful fix. Happy styling!
