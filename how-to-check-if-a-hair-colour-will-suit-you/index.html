How To Check If A Hair Colour Will Suit You A Guide For 2023

Written by Aprilia Jul 10, 2023 · 4 min read

Changing your hair colour can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look. However, it can also be daunting to commit to a new hue. With so many colours and shades to choose from, how do you know which one will suit you best? In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to check if a hair colour will suit you and offer tips for finding the perfect shade.

Table of Contents

  • How to Check if a Hair Colour Will Suit You
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

How to Check if a Hair Colour Will Suit You

There are a few factors to consider when choosing a hair colour that will suit you:

  • Your skin tone
  • Your natural hair colour
  • Your eye colour
  • Your personal style

Your skin tone is the most important factor to consider when choosing a hair colour. It can be warm, cool or neutral, and this will affect which colours will look best on you. If you have a warm skin tone, you’ll look best in warm colours like golden blonde, copper, and auburn. If you have a cool skin tone, you’ll look best in cool colours like ash blonde, cool brown, and black. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can wear both warm and cool colours.

Your natural hair colour should also be taken into account. If you have naturally dark hair, it may be difficult to achieve a light blonde without damaging your hair. If you have lighter hair, you may be able to experiment with a wider range of colours.

Your eye colour can also be a helpful guide. If you have blue or green eyes, warm colours will make them pop, while cool colours will enhance brown or hazel eyes.

Finally, your personal style should be considered. Do you prefer a bold look or something more natural? Are you willing to commit to regular maintenance or do you want something low-maintenance? These factors should all be taken into account when choosing a hair colour.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s how to check if a hair colour will suit you:

  1. Research different hair colours and shades to get an idea of what you like.
  2. Determine your skin tone by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. If you can’t tell, you likely have a neutral skin tone.
  3. Consider your natural hair colour and eye colour.
  4. Use online resources like virtual hair colour try-ons or consult with a professional stylist to see how different colours will look on you.
  5. Take into account your personal style and maintenance preferences.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when choosing a hair colour:

  • Stick to shades that are within two levels of your natural hair colour to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Consider your lifestyle and how much time you’re willing to commit to maintenance. Some colours require more upkeep than others.
  • Ask for professional advice if you’re unsure about which colour to choose.
  • Remember that the lighting in a salon can be different from natural lighting. Ask to see your hair colour in natural light before committing.

FAQs

Q: Can I dye my hair a completely different colour from my natural hair colour?

A: Yes, but keep in mind that the process may be more damaging to your hair and it may require more maintenance.

Q: Should I dye my hair myself or go to a professional?

A: It’s recommended to go to a professional for the best results, especially if you’re making a drastic change.

Q: How often should I touch up my roots?

A: This will depend on the colour and how quickly your hair grows. Generally, root touch-ups are needed every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

Once you’ve found the perfect hair colour, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking fresh and vibrant. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the colour.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners, as this can also cause fading.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine with a hat or hair product specifically designed for this purpose.

By following these tips and considering your skin tone, natural hair colour, eye colour, personal style, and maintenance preferences, you’ll be able to find a hair colour that suits you perfectly.
