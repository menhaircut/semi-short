How To Cut The Top Of Your Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide For Women

Written by Bardi Oct 02, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money on haircuts or simply want to try cutting your own hair, you might be wondering how to cut the top of your hair yourself. While cutting your own hair can seem intimidating, it's actually a relatively simple process if you follow the right steps. In this post, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut the top of your hair yourself, as well as some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to ensure you get the best results possible.

Table of Contents

  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  4. FAQs About Cutting Your Own Hair
  5. Hair Care Advice After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut the Top of Your Hair Yourself

Before we get into the step-by-step guide, it's important to note that cutting your own hair can be risky, especially if you're not experienced. If you're unsure about cutting your own hair, we recommend seeking the help of a professional hair stylist.

That being said, if you're determined to cut the top of your hair yourself, here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary tools, it's time to start cutting. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut the top of your hair yourself:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair thoroughly. This will help you see the natural shape and texture of your hair.
  2. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up. Make sure the section is no wider than the length of your scissors.
  3. Hold the section of hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch away from your scalp. This will ensure you don't cut too much hair off.
  4. Using your scissors, cut the hair in a straight line. Make sure to cut only a small amount at a time, as you can always cut more later.
  5. Repeat steps 2-4 with small sections of hair until you've cut the entire top of your head.
  6. Stand back and assess your work. If you notice any uneven sections, use your comb and scissors to even them out.

Remember, it's always better to cut less hair than more. You can always go back and cut more later if necessary.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting the top of your hair yourself:

  • Start with dry hair, as wet hair can be deceiving in terms of length.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member if you're struggling to cut the back of your head.

FAQs About Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair:

1. Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair, but it's important to be cautious. Curly hair can be tricky to cut, as it often bounces up and appears shorter once it's dry. We recommend starting with small cuts and working your way up to a desired length.

2. How often should I cut the top of my hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, we recommend cutting the top of your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and style.

3. What if I mess up?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. It's just hair, and it will grow back. If the mistake is minor, you may be able to fix it yourself. If it's more significant, you may need to seek the help of a professional stylist.

Hair Care Advice After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting the top of your hair yourself, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure it stays healthy and looks its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Trim the ends of your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

Cutting the top of your hair yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience, as long as you follow the right steps and take care of your hair afterwards. Happy cutting!
