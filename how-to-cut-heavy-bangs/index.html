How To Cut Heavy Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Description

If you're looking to update your hairstyle, cutting heavy bangs can be a great way to do so. Bangs are a versatile and trendy way to add interest to your hair while framing your face. However, cutting heavy bangs can be intimidating, especially if you've never done it before. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting heavy bangs and share some tips and tricks to help you get the perfect look.

How to Cut Heavy Bangs

Before we get started, let's talk about what you'll need to cut your heavy bangs:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips to hold back the rest of your hair

It's also a good idea to have a photo of the type of bangs you want to achieve for reference.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your supplies, let's get started:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb your hair straight down and use clips to secure the rest of your hair back.
  2. Take a small section of your hair from the front. This will be the section you cut into bangs.
  3. Decide on the length of your bangs. It's best to start longer and gradually cut more if needed.
  4. Hold the hair section between your fingers where you want to make the first cut.
  5. Make small cuts at a slight angle, working your way across the section of hair. Be sure to use sharp scissors to avoid ragged edges.
  6. Once you've made your initial cuts, check the length and adjust as needed.
  7. Continue cutting small sections of hair until you have the desired length and thickness.
  8. Blend the bangs into the rest of your hair by point-cutting or using thinning shears.
  9. Style your bangs as desired.
  10. Clean up any stray hairs and enjoy your new look!

Tips for Cutting Heavy Bangs

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect heavy bangs:

  • Start with longer bangs and gradually cut more if needed. It's easier to take off more than to add it back.
  • Make small cuts and check the length frequently to avoid cutting too much at once.
  • For a more natural look, cut your bangs when your hair is dry and styled as you normally wear it.
  • Use thinning shears or point-cutting to blend your bangs into the rest of your hair for a seamless look.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting heavy bangs:

  • What type of bangs are best for my face shape? Different face shapes look best with different types of bangs. Consult with a hairstylist or do some research to find the best bangs for your face shape.
  • How often should I trim my bangs? You should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking their best.
  • Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair? Yes, but be sure to cut your bangs when your hair is dry and styled as you normally wear it. Curly hair can be unpredictable when wet, so it's best to avoid cutting it then.

Hair Care for Bangs

Now that you have your new bangs, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your bangs frequently to avoid oil buildup.
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to style them.
  • Avoid touching your bangs throughout the day to prevent oil transfer.
  • Use dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh between washes.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut your own heavy bangs with confidence. Remember to start slowly and check the length often to avoid any mistakes. Happy cutting!
