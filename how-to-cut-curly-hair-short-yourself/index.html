How To Cut Curly Hair Short Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a curly-haired person who wants to cut your hair short, you might be hesitant to go to a salon. Maybe you're trying to save money, or you just prefer to do things yourself. Whatever the reason, cutting curly hair short yourself is totally doable, even if you've never done it before. With the right tools, a little bit of patience, and some guidance, you can give yourself a stylish new look.

How to Cut Curly Hair Short Yourself

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some key terms you should know:

  • Layers: A haircut that involves cutting the hair at different lengths. Layers can add volume and movement to curly hair.
  • Bangs: Hair that hangs over the forehead. Bangs can be blunt or textured.
  • Clipper: A tool used to cut hair with the help of a comb attachment. Clippers are often used to create a buzz cut or a fade.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let's get started with the actual cutting process:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. Curly hair should be cut when it's dry, but it's easier to detangle and comb through when it's wet. So, wash your hair and let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry it gently.
  2. Section your hair. Use clips or hair ties to divide your hair into sections. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly. Start at the back and work your way forward.
  3. Decide on the length. Use a mirror to determine how short you want to cut your hair. Remember that curly hair will shrink as it dries, so it's better to err on the side of caution and cut less than you think you need to.
  4. Start cutting. Use sharp hair cutting scissors to trim each section of hair. Cut in small sections and work your way up. Use the point cutting technique to create texture and movement.
  5. Trim the bangs. If you want bangs, cut them last. Use your fingers to measure the length and cut them straight across. If you want textured bangs, cut into them at an angle.
  6. Blend the layers. If you want layers, use the point cutting technique to blend them together. This will create a seamless transition from one layer to the next.
  7. Clean up the edges. Use clippers or scissors to clean up the hair around your ears and neck.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Short

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind as you cut your curly hair short:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. Using regular scissors or kitchen scissors can damage your hair.
  • Take your time. Cutting curly hair is a slow process, but it's worth it to get it right.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're having trouble cutting the back of your head, ask a friend or family member for assistance.
  • Experiment with different lengths and styles. Cutting your hair short is a great opportunity to try something new.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting curly hair short:

  • Can I cut my curly hair myself? Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself. Just make sure you have the right tools and take your time.
  • How often should I cut my curly hair? It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. As a general rule, curly hair should be trimmed every 6-8 weeks.
  • How do I know if I need a trim? If your curls are losing their shape, your ends are frayed, or your hair is difficult to detangle, it's probably time for a trim.

Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

Now that you've cut your curly hair short, it's important to take care of it properly:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your curls from drying out.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle it.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to keep your curls hydrated and defined.
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your curly hair short yourself. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
