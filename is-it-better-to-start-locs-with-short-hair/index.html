Is It Better To Start Locs With Short Hair

Written by William Jul 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you considering starting locs, but wondering if it's better to do so with short hair? This is a common question among those who are new to the loc journey. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of starting locs with short hair and provide you with some tips and tricks to ensure that your loc journey is a success.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Locs with Short Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Locs with Short Hair

Description

Starting locs with short hair can be a great option for those who want to avoid the long and sometimes tedious process of growing out their hair before starting their loc journey. It can also be a good option for those who are looking for a low-maintenance hairstyle that requires minimal upkeep.

However, there are some potential downsides to starting locs with short hair. For example, it may take longer for your locs to mature and fully develop if you start with short hair. Additionally, your locs may be thinner than if you start with longer hair.

How to Start Locs with Short Hair

If you've decided that you want to start locs with short hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind:

  • Choose a starting method that works for your hair type and texture.
  • Be patient - it may take longer for your locs to mature if you start with short hair.
  • Be gentle with your hair and avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products.
  • Consider working with a professional stylist who has experience with starting locs.

Starting Methods for Short Hair

There are several methods for starting locs with short hair, including:

  • Two-Strand Twists: This method involves twisting small sections of hair together to create locs. It's a popular method for those with shorter hair.
  • Comb Coils: This method involves using a small comb to twist the hair into small coils. It's a good option for those with tightly coiled or kinky hair.
  • Interlocking: This method involves using a small tool to thread the hair through itself to create locs. It's a good option for those with tightly coiled or kinky hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Starting Locs with Short Hair

Here's a step-by-step guide to starting locs with short hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair until it's damp.
  3. Choose a starting method that works for your hair type and texture.
  4. Section your hair into small, even sections.
  5. Apply a natural oil or locking gel to each section of hair.
  6. Twist, coil, or interlock each section of hair to create your locs.
  7. Allow your hair to air dry or sit under a hooded dryer.
  8. Repeat the twisting, coiling, or interlocking process every 4-6 weeks to maintain your locs.

Tips for Starting Locs with Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you successfully start your loc journey with short hair:

  • Be patient - it may take longer for your locs to mature if you start with short hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products on your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Consider using a natural oil or locking gel to help your locs form and maintain their shape.
  • Work with a professional stylist who has experience with starting locs.

FAQs About Starting Locs with Short Hair

How long should my hair be before I start locs?

There's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. Some people prefer to wait until their hair is at least 6 inches long before starting locs, while others are comfortable starting with shorter hair. Ultimately, it's up to you to decide what length of hair you're comfortable starting with.

Will my locs be thinner if I start with short hair?

Possibly. If you start with short hair, your locs may be thinner than if you start with longer hair. However, this isn't always the case, and there are steps you can take to help your locs thicken over time.

Can I start locs with relaxed hair?

Yes, you can start locs with relaxed hair. However, keep in mind that relaxed hair may take longer to lock than natural hair, and your locs may be thinner and weaker if you start with relaxed hair.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Your Locs

Once you've started your loc journey, it's important to take good care of your hair to ensure that your locs stay healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips for maintaining your locs:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or styling products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Protect your hair at night by wearing a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.
  • Retwist your locs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.

Starting locs with short hair can be a great option for those who want a low-maintenance hairstyle or who want to avoid the long process of growing out their hair. By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can ensure that your loc journey is a success.
