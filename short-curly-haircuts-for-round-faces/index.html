Short Curly Haircuts For Round Faces A Complete Guide

If you have a round face and curly hair, it can be challenging to find the perfect haircut that flatters your features. But fear not! With the right haircut and styling techniques, you can enhance your natural curls and show off your beautiful face shape. In this guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know about short curly haircuts for round faces, including how to choose the right cut, styling tips, and hair care advice.

How to Choose the Right Short Curly Haircut for Your Round Face

When choosing a haircut for a round face, it's essential to consider the overall length, volume, and shape. Here are some tips to keep in mind when looking for a short curly haircut that flatters your face shape:

Consider the Length

Short haircuts that end just above or below the chin tend to work best for round faces. This length helps to elongate the face and create the illusion of a more defined jawline. Avoid haircuts that end at the widest part of your face, as this can make your face appear even rounder.

Opt for Layers

Layered haircuts are ideal for adding volume and movement to your curls, which can help balance out a round face shape. Layers that start at the cheekbones or chin can help to create the illusion of a more defined jawline. Avoid layers that start at the top of your head, as this can make your face appear wider.

Avoid Heavy Bangs

While bangs can be a great way to switch up your look, they can be tricky for those with round faces. Heavy bangs can shorten the face and make it appear rounder. Instead, opt for side-swept bangs that fall just above the eyebrows, as this can help to create the illusion of a longer face shape.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Curly Hair for Round Faces

Now that you have the right haircut, it's time to style your curls. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect look:

Step 1: Start with Wet Hair

Begin by washing your hair and applying a curl-enhancing product while it's still wet. This will help to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Diffuse Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair while scrunching your curls. This will help to enhance your natural texture and create volume.

Step 3: Define Your Curls

Once your hair is dry, use a curling wand or curling iron to define any sections that need more definition. Be sure to wrap your hair around the curling wand or iron in small sections to avoid creating tight, ringlet curls.

Step 4: Finish with Hairspray

Finish off your look with a light-hold hairspray to help set your curls and keep them in place.

Tips for Maintaining Short Curly Haircuts for Round Faces

Here are a few tips to help you maintain your short curly haircut:

Use a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help to reduce frizz and prevent breakage, which can help your curls look their best.

Avoid Overwashing Your Hair

Curly hair tends to be drier than straight hair, so you don't need to wash it as often. Overwashing can strip your hair of its natural oils, making it more prone to frizz and breakage.

Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning your hair once a week can help to restore moisture and keep your curls looking their best. Look for a deep conditioning treatment that's specifically designed for curly hair.

FAQs About Short Curly Haircuts for Round Faces

What's the best haircut for a round face with curly hair?

Short haircuts that end just above or below the chin tend to work best for round faces with curly hair. Layered haircuts can also help to add volume and balance out your face shape.

Can I wear bangs with a round face and curly hair?

While bangs can be tricky for those with round faces, side-swept bangs can be a great way to add some texture and dimension to your hair.

How often should I trim my curly hair?

It's recommended to trim your curly hair every 6-8 weeks to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Short Curly Haircuts for Round Faces

Here are some additional hair care tips to help keep your short curly haircut looking its best:

Avoid Heat Styling

Curly hair is prone to damage from heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. Try to air dry your hair as often as possible and limit your use of heat styling tools.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can be damaging to your hair, especially if you have color-treated hair. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant hair product to help shield your hair from the sun.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Curly hair is more prone to tangling than straight hair, so it's essential to use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to avoid causing breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can rock a short curly haircut that flatters your round face and enhances your natural curls. Remember to choose the right haircut, style with care, and maintain your hair with the right products and techniques for a look that's always on point.
