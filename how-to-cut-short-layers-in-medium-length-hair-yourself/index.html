How To Cut Short Layers In Medium Length Hair Yourself

Do you want to add some texture and volume to your medium length hair? Cutting short layers is a great way to achieve this look. Plus, you can save money by doing it yourself at home. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut short layers in medium length hair yourself, as well as some helpful tips and hair care advice.

How to Cut Short Layers in Medium Length Hair Yourself

Cutting short layers in medium length hair may seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily at home. Here is what you will need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Small mirror
  • Spray bottle with water

Once you have gathered your tools, follow these simple steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, back, and two sides. Use hair clips to secure each section.
  3. Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle. Repeat this step for all sections of the back of your head.
  4. Move to the sides of your head. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle towards the back of your head. Repeat this step for both sides.
  5. Finally, move to the top section of your head. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle towards the back of your head. Repeat this step for all sections of the top of your head.
  6. Check your layers in the mirror. If any sections are too long, trim them again.
  7. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Short Layers in Medium Length Hair Yourself

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers in medium length hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to mistakes and uneven layers.
  • Always cut at an angle to create a natural, blended look.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Keep a small mirror nearby to check the back of your head.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short layers in medium length hair:

Q: Can I cut short layers in curly hair?

A: Yes, you can cut short layers in curly hair. However, keep in mind that the layers may appear shorter once the hair is dry and styled.

Q: How often should I cut my short layers?

A: It's recommended to trim your short layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and prevent split ends.

Q: Can I cut short layers in thin hair?

A: Yes, you can cut short layers in thin hair. Short layers can add volume and texture to thin hair.

Hair Care Advice for Short Layered Hair

After cutting short layers in your medium length hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the shape of your short layers.

Now that you know how to cut short layers in medium length hair yourself, give it a try! With the right tools, technique, and care, you can achieve a trendy and stylish look without leaving your home.
