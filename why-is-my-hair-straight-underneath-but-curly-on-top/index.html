Why Is My Hair Straight Underneath But Curly On Top

Written by Daniel Oct 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you ever looked in the mirror and wondered why your hair is straight underneath but curly on top? This is a common hair type that many people have, and it can be difficult to deal with. However, with the right knowledge and tips, you can learn to embrace and care for your unique hair type. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this hair type, how to style it, and how to care for it.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Style Your Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Hair that is straight underneath and curly on top is often referred to as "mixed texture" hair. This is because the hair on the underside is usually finer and straighter, while the hair on the top is thicker and curlier. This can make styling difficult, as the hair can appear uneven and frizzy. However, with the right styling techniques and hair care, this hair type can be stunning and unique.

How to Style Your Hair

When it comes to styling mixed texture hair, it's important to use the right products and techniques. Here are some tips for styling your hair:

1. Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help to detangle your hair, reduce frizz, and add moisture. Look for a leave-in conditioner that is lightweight and won't weigh down your hair.

2. Apply a Curl Enhancing Product

To enhance your curls and reduce frizz, apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair while it's damp. Look for a product that contains ingredients like shea butter, jojoba oil, or coconut oil.

3. Use a Diffuser

A diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help to enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Use it on a low or medium heat setting, and scrunch your hair while diffusing.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide for styling your mixed texture hair:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are designed for your hair type.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair while it's damp, focusing on the ends and avoiding the roots.

3. Apply a Curl Enhancing Product

Apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair while it's damp, focusing on the areas where your hair is curlier.

4. Diffuse Your Hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your curls and reduce frizz. Scrunch your hair while diffusing on a low or medium heat setting.

5. Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. You can wear your hair down, in a half-up half-down style, or in a ponytail or bun.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your mixed texture hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots. This can help to prevent breakage and damage.

2. Avoid Over-Washing Your Hair

Over-washing your hair can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair no more than every other day.

3. Protect Your Hair While You Sleep

Sleeping with your hair loose can cause tangles and breakage. Consider wearing your hair in a loose bun or braid while you sleep to protect it.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can straighten your curly hair with a flat iron or a blow dryer. However, it's important to use heat protectant products to prevent damage.

Q: How often should I get my hair trimmed?

A: It's recommended to get your hair trimmed every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and breakage.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips for maintaining your mixed texture hair:

1. Deep Condition Your Hair

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to add moisture and nourishment to your hair.

2. Protect Your Hair from Heat

Use heat protectant products when styling your hair with heat tools, and avoid using high heat settings.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid wearing your hair in tight hairstyles, as this can cause tension and breakage.

By following these tips and techniques, you can learn to embrace and care for your mixed texture hair. Remember, every hair type is unique and beautiful in its own way!
