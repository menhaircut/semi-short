How To Wear Hair Up And Cover Ears A Step By Step Guide

Description

Many people want to wear their hair up and cover their ears for various reasons. Some may want to show off their earrings, while others may want to keep their ears warm in cooler weather. Whatever the reason, there are many ways to wear your hair up and cover your ears that are both functional and fashionable. In this article, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to do so.

How To

Before we get started, it's important to note that there are many different ways to wear your hair up and cover your ears. The method you choose will depend on your hair type, the occasion, and your personal style. Here are some popular options:

  • Ponytail
  • Bun
  • Braids
  • Top Knot
  • Half-Up, Half-Down

Step by Step

Ponytail

To create a ponytail that covers your ears, follow these steps:

  1. Brush your hair back and gather it into a ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Pull the hair tie up towards your ears, leaving a few inches of hair below the tie.
  3. Fluff out the hair above the tie to create volume and cover your ears.

Bun

To create a bun that covers your ears, follow these steps:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail at the crown of your head.
  2. Twist the hair around the base of the ponytail to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  4. Tuck any loose hair around your ears into the bun.

Braids

To create braids that cover your ears, follow these steps:

  1. Part your hair down the middle and create two equal sections.
  2. French braid each section, starting at the top of your head and working your way down.
  3. Secure the ends of the braids with hair ties.
  4. Tuck any loose hair around your ears into the braids.

Top Knot

To create a top knot that covers your ears, follow these steps:

  1. Gather your hair into a high ponytail at the crown of your head.
  2. Twist the hair around the base of the ponytail to create a bun.
  3. Secure the bun with bobby pins.
  4. Fluff out the hair above the bun to create volume and cover your ears.

Half-Up, Half-Down

To create a half-up, half-down style that covers your ears, follow these steps:

  1. Part your hair down the middle.
  2. Take the top section of your hair and pull it back, securing it with a hair tie at the back of your head.
  3. Tuck any loose hair around your ears into the hair tie.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when wearing your hair up and covering your ears:

  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose hair around your ears.
  • Add accessories like headbands, scarves, or hats to enhance your look.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.
  • Use hair products like hairspray or texturizing spray to add volume and hold your style in place.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about wearing your hair up and covering your ears:

Q: Can I wear my hair up and cover my ears if I have short hair?

A: Yes! Try a half-up, half-down style or a messy bun with bobby pins to secure any loose hair around your ears.

Q: Will wearing my hair up and covering my ears damage my hair?

A: It shouldn't if you use gentle hair ties and don't pull your hair too tightly.

Q: What if I have curly hair?

A: Braids and messy buns can work well with curly hair. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care

Wearing your hair up and covering your ears can be a great way to switch up your look and keep your ears warm. However, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid pulling your hair too tightly when securing it with hair ties or bobby pins.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat tools like flat irons or curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.

By following these tips and experimenting with different styles, you can wear your hair up and cover your ears with confidence.
