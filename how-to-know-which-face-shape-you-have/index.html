How to Determine Your Face Shape

There are six common face shapes: oval, round, square, oblong, diamond, and heart. To determine your face shape, youâll need to take a few measurements of your face.

Step 1: Measure Your Forehead

Using a measuring tape, measure the width of your forehead from one temple to the other.

Step 2: Measure Your Cheekbones

Measure the widest part of your cheekbones, just below the eyes.

Step 3: Measure Your Jawline

Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jawbone begins to angle upwards.

Step 4: Measure Your Face Length

Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you have your measurements, you can determine your face shape using the following guide:

Oval Face Shape

An oval face shape is longer than it is wide, with a rounded hairline and jawline. The forehead is slightly wider than the chin.

Round Face Shape

A round face shape is characterized by a wide hairline and full cheeks. The width and length of the face are almost equal.

Square Face Shape

A square face shape is characterized by a strong, square jawline and a forehead, cheekbones, and jawline that are almost equal in width.

Oblong Face Shape

An oblong face shape is longer than it is wide, with a long, straight cheekline and forehead. The jawline is slightly rounded.

Diamond Face Shape

A diamond face shape is characterized by a narrow forehead and jawline, with wide cheekbones.

Heart Face Shape

A heart face shape is characterized by a wider forehead and cheekbones, with a narrow, pointed chin.

Tips for Styling Your Hair Based on Your Face Shape

Now that you know your face shape, here are some tips for styling your hair:

Oval Face Shape

Lucky you! An oval face shape can pull off almost any hairstyle. Try a layered cut, a bob, or long waves.

Round Face Shape

Round faces look great with hairstyles that add height at the crown, like a high ponytail or top knot. Long, layered cuts also work well.

Square Face Shape

Soft, wavy hairstyles soften a square jawline. Try a side-swept bang or a shoulder-length cut with layers.

Oblong Face Shape

Long bobs or shoulder-length cuts with layers add volume to an oblong face shape. Avoid straight, long hair.

Diamond Face Shape

Side-swept bangs and layers around the chin soften a diamond face shape. Avoid short hairstyles that add volume at the crown.

Heart Face Shape

Side-swept bangs and chin-length bobs are great for heart-shaped faces. Avoid adding volume at the crown.

Hair Care for Different Face Shapes

Regardless of your face shape, itâs important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

Oily Hair

Wash your hair every day or every other day to keep it from looking greasy.

Dry Hair

Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils. Deep condition once a week.

Curly Hair

Avoid brushing curly hair, as this can cause frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb and a leave-in conditioner to keep curls defined.

Straight Hair

Avoid using too much heat on straight hair, as this can cause damage. Use a heat protectant spray and let your hair air-dry whenever possible.
