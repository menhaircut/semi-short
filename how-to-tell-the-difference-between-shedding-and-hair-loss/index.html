How To Tell The Difference Between Shedding And Hair Loss

Written by Petter Jul 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're noticing more hair on your brush or in the shower drain, you may be wondering if you're experiencing shedding or hair loss. Shedding is a natural process where hair falls out as part of its growth cycle, while hair loss is the result of hair falling out and not regrowing. It's important to understand the difference between the two, as they have different causes and treatments. In this article, we'll go over how to tell the difference between shedding and hair loss, as well as provide tips on how to care for your hair.

How to Tell if You're Shedding

Shedding is a normal process where hair falls out as part of its growth cycle. On average, people shed around 50-100 hairs per day. If you're experiencing shedding, you'll notice hair falling out when you brush or wash your hair, but you won't see any bald spots or thinning areas. Shedding can be caused by a variety of factors, including hormonal changes, stress, and diet.

Tips to Manage Shedding:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to minimize hair damage.
  • Avoid using heat tools, like blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage hair and make shedding worse.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth.
  • Manage stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.

How to Tell if You're Experiencing Hair Loss

Hair loss is the result of hair falling out and not regrowing. If you're experiencing hair loss, you may notice thinning or bald spots on your scalp. Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and medical conditions.

Tips to Manage Hair Loss:

  • Consult with a dermatologist or hair loss specialist to determine the cause of your hair loss and the best treatment options.
  • Consider using hair growth products, like minoxidil or finasteride, under the guidance of a medical professional.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, like braids and ponytails, as they can cause further hair damage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth.

FAQ:

Is shedding the same as hair loss?

No, shedding is a natural process where hair falls out as part of its growth cycle, while hair loss is the result of hair falling out and not regrowing.

How much shedding is normal?

On average, people shed around 50-100 hairs per day.

What causes hair loss?

Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hormonal changes, and medical conditions.

Hair Care Tips:

Regardless of whether you're experiencing shedding or hair loss, it's important to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools, like blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support hair growth.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, like braids and ponytails, as they can cause hair damage.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or brush to minimize hair damage.

In conclusion, shedding and hair loss are two different things that require different treatments. If you're experiencing shedding, focus on managing the underlying causes, like stress and diet. If you're experiencing hair loss, consult with a dermatologist or hair loss specialist to determine the cause and the best treatment options. Regardless of whether you're experiencing shedding or hair loss, taking care of your hair is important for promoting healthy growth.
