What Hairstyle Was Popular In The 70S

Written by Daniel Jul 17, 2023 · 3 min read

The 70s was a decade of growth, self-expression, and free spirit. It was a time of bell-bottom jeans, platform shoes, and of course, big hair. The 70s was a time when hairstyles were bold, daring, and expressive. In this blog post, we will explore what hairstyle was popular in the 70s, how to achieve the look, tips and tricks, and hair care.

Description

The 70s was a decade of experimentation, liberation, and individuality. Women and men alike were embracing their natural hair texture and were experimenting with various hairstyles. From afros to shags, the 70s was a decade of the bohemian, hippie, and disco era. Women were growing their hair long and men were sporting mustaches and sideburns.

How to Achieve the Look

There are various ways to achieve the 70s look. First, you can grow your hair long and let your natural texture shine. Second, you can go for a shag haircut, which was popular in the 70s. A shag haircut is a layered haircut with choppy ends that gives you a messy yet stylish look. Third, you can opt for an afro or a fro. An afro is a natural hairstyle that involves picking the hair out to create a full, round shape. Fourth, you can go for a feathered haircut. Feathered haircuts were popular in the 70s, and involve layers that start at the cheekbones and curve outward.

Step by Step

If you want to achieve the shag haircut, here are the steps:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Separate your hair into sections
  3. Cut the layers starting from the back to the front
  4. Use a razor or scissors to create a choppy, messy look
  5. Style your hair with a texturizing spray or mousse

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when trying to achieve the 70s look:

  • Embrace your natural hair texture
  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Get regular haircuts to maintain the shape and style
  • Experiment with accessories like headbands and scarves

FAQ

Q: What kind of hair was popular in the 70s?

A: The 70s was a time when natural hair texture was embraced. People with curly, wavy, and straight hair were all popular in the 70s.

Q: What is a shag haircut?

A: A shag haircut is a layered haircut with choppy ends that gives you a messy yet stylish look.

Q: How do I maintain the 70s look?

A: To maintain the 70s look, get regular haircuts to maintain the shape and style. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, and experiment with accessories like headbands and scarves.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and shiny, use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant spray when you do. Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage. Lastly, protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or applying a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

In conclusion, the 70s was a decade of bold, daring, and expressive hairstyles. From shags to afros, the 70s was a time of self-expression and individuality. To achieve the 70s look, embrace your natural hair texture, experiment with various hairstyles, and accessorize with headbands and scarves. With these tips and tricks, you can rock the 70s look with confidence and style.
