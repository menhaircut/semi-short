How To Choose Concealer Tone A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Whether you're looking to brighten up your under-eye area or cover up blemishes, choosing the right concealer tone is crucial to achieving a flawless finish. With so many different shades and undertones available, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. That's why we've put together this complete guide to help you choose the right concealer tone for your skin.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Skin Undertone
  • Step 2: Choose the Right Shade
  • Step 3: Test the Concealer
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Concealer Tone

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Undertone

The first step in choosing the right concealer tone is to determine your skin undertone. This is the underlying color that affects the overall look of your skin. Undertones can be warm, cool, or neutral.

To determine your skin undertone, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist:

  • If your veins appear green, you likely have a warm undertone.
  • If your veins appear blue or purple, you likely have a cool undertone.
  • If your veins appear blue-green, you likely have a neutral undertone.

Another way to determine your undertone is to look at the jewelry you prefer to wear:

  • If you look best in gold jewelry, you likely have a warm undertone.
  • If you look best in silver jewelry, you likely have a cool undertone.
  • If you can wear both gold and silver jewelry, you likely have a neutral undertone.

Step 2: Choose the Right Shade

Once you've determined your undertone, it's time to choose the right shade of concealer. Look for shades that match your skin tone as closely as possible. If you're not sure which shade to choose, go for a shade that's slightly lighter than your skin tone.

If you're covering up under-eye circles, choose a shade that's one to two shades lighter than your skin tone. This will help brighten up the area and make you look more awake.

If you're covering up blemishes or redness, choose a shade that matches your skin tone exactly. Using a lighter shade can actually draw more attention to the area you're trying to cover up.

Step 3: Test the Concealer

Before you buy a concealer, it's important to test it out to make sure it's the right shade for you. Apply a small amount of concealer to the back of your hand or your jawline and blend it in. If it blends seamlessly into your skin, you've found the right shade.

If the concealer looks too light or too dark, try a different shade. Don't be afraid to ask for help from a makeup artist or sales associate at the store.

Tips for Choosing the Right Concealer Tone

  • When in doubt, go for a shade that's slightly lighter than your skin tone.
  • Choose a concealer with a yellow or peach undertone to help counteract dark circles.
  • For the most natural-looking finish, blend the concealer outwards towards the edges of your face.
  • If you're using a color-correcting concealer, make sure to apply foundation on top to even out your skin tone.

FAQs

What's the difference between warm, cool, and neutral undertones?

Warm undertones have a yellow, peach, or golden hue. Cool undertones have a pink, red, or blue hue. Neutral undertones have a mix of warm and cool hues.

What's the best way to apply concealer?

The best way to apply concealer is to dot it onto the areas you want to cover up and blend it in with a brush or your fingertips. Make sure to blend it outwards towards the edges of your face for a seamless finish.

How do I know if I need to use color-correcting concealer?

If you have dark circles or redness that can't be covered up with regular concealer, you may need to use a color-correcting concealer. These concealers are designed to neutralize specific colors on the skin.

How to Care for Your Skin While Using Concealer

While concealer can be a lifesaver for covering up imperfections, it's important to take care of your skin as well. Make sure to cleanse your face thoroughly at the end of the day to remove any makeup or dirt that may have built up. Use a gentle cleanser and moisturizer to keep your skin healthy and hydrated.

It's also a good idea to take a break from makeup every once in a while to let your skin breathe. Use a face mask or exfoliating scrub to give your skin a deep cleanse and remove any buildup.

By following these tips and choosing the right concealer tone, you can achieve a flawless finish that looks natural and beautiful.
