How To Give Yourself A Bald Fade Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Nov 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a fresh haircut that's both stylish and low-maintenance, a bald fade might be the perfect choice for you. This popular haircut is characterized by a gradual taper from the top of the head down to the neckline, leaving the sides and back of the head shaved close to the scalp. While getting a bald fade haircut from a professional barber is always an option, it's also possible to achieve this look at home with the right tools and techniques. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of giving yourself a bald fade haircut, along with some tips and tricks to help you achieve the best results.

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have these essential tools on hand:

  • Clippers with adjustable guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Mirror or second person for assistance

How to Give Yourself a Bald Fade Haircut

Step 1: Determine Your Starting Point

Start with dry, clean hair. Use a comb to determine where you want your fade to begin. For a low fade, start at the bottom of your hairline. For a high fade, start about 2 inches above your ears. Use your clippers with a #2 guard to shave the hair below this starting point, going all the way around your head.

Step 2: Cut the Top of Your Hair

Use your scissors to cut the top of your hair to your desired length. You can use a comb to help guide your cutting, or simply cut freehand. Make sure to cut evenly and check your work as you go.

Step 3: Create a Fade Line

Attach a #1 guard to your clippers and create a fade line around your head, about an inch above your starting point. Use your clippers without a guard to create a bald line underneath the fade line. This will create the beginning of your bald fade.

Step 4: Trim the Sides and Back

Use your clippers with a #2 guard to trim the sides and back of your head, going up to the fade line. Make sure to keep your clippers level and use smooth, even strokes to avoid any uneven patches or lines.

Step 5: Blend the Fade

Attach a #1.5 guard to your clippers and use it to blend the fade line into the rest of your hair. Work in small sections and use a back-and-forth motion to blend the hair evenly. Repeat with a #1 guard and then a #0.5 guard to gradually blend the hair until you achieve the desired fade.

Tips for a Perfect Bald Fade

  • Take your time and work in small sections to ensure an even, gradual fade.
  • Use a mirror or a second person to check your work from all angles.
  • Don't be afraid to adjust the guard sizes to achieve the perfect fade for your head shape and hair type.
  • Practice makes perfect! Don't worry if your first attempt isn't perfect â keep practicing and you'll get better each time.

FAQs

Can I give myself a bald fade if I have long hair?

A bald fade is typically most effective with shorter hair, but it's still possible to achieve the look if you have longer hair. However, you may need to spend more time blending the fade and using different guard sizes to achieve the desired effect.

Can I use hair clippers without guards to achieve a bald fade?

While it is possible to use clippers without guards to achieve a bald fade, it's generally not recommended unless you have a lot of experience with hair cutting. Using a guard will help you achieve a more even and controlled cut, and reduce the risk of accidentally cutting too much hair.

Hair Care After Your Bald Fade Haircut

Caring for your hair after a bald fade haircut is relatively easy. Keep your hair clean and moisturized with a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep it hydrated. Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your haircut, and consider using a styling product like hair wax or gel to add texture and hold.

With a little practice and patience, giving yourself a bald fade haircut can be a great way to achieve a stylish and low-maintenance look. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect bald fade, and enjoy your new haircut!
