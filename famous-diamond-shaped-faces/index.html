Description

Diamond shaped faces are characterized by narrow foreheads and chins, with wider cheekbones. Famous diamond shaped faces include Rihanna, Halle Berry, and Audrey Hepburn. Knowing your face shape is important when it comes to hair care, as it can help you choose the right hairstyle and hair products to enhance your features.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Take a close look at the outline of your face and compare it to the following shapes:

  • Diamond: Narrow forehead and chin, wide cheekbones
  • Oval: Forehead is slightly wider than chin, with gently rounded hairline
  • Round: Equal width and length, with soft, rounded features
  • Square: Prominent jawline and forehead, with a straight hairline
  • Heart: Wider forehead and cheekbones, with a narrow chin
  • Triangle: Narrow forehead, wide jawline and chin

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Care for Diamond Shaped Faces

When it comes to hair care for diamond shaped faces, the goal is to balance out the widest part of the face (the cheekbones) with the rest of the features. Here are some tips for achieving this:

1. Choose the Right Haircut

Hairstyles that add width to the forehead or chin can help balance out the cheekbones. This can be achieved with a layered haircut that adds volume to the top of the head, or a chin-length bob that draws attention to the jawline. Avoid hairstyles that emphasize the width of the cheekbones, such as blunt bangs or one-length cuts.

2. Add Volume to the Crown

Adding volume to the crown of the head can help elongate the face and balance out the cheekbones. This can be achieved with a volumizing mousse or spray, or by backcombing the hair at the crown.

3. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can help soften the angles of a diamond shaped face, while drawing attention to the eyes. Avoid blunt bangs, as they can make the face appear more angular.

4. Emphasize the Jawline

Emphasizing the jawline can help balance out the width of the cheekbones. This can be achieved with a chin-length bob, or by wearing the hair sleek and straight to draw attention to the jawline.

5. Avoid Center Parts

A center part can make a diamond shaped face appear even more angular. Instead, opt for a side part that helps soften the angles of the face.

Tips for Diamond Shaped Faces

Here are some additional tips for hair care for diamond shaped faces:

1. Experiment with Layers

Layers can add volume and movement to the hair, which can help balance out the width of the cheekbones. Experiment with different lengths and textures to find the perfect look for your face shape.

2. Play with Texture

Adding texture to the hair can help soften the angles of a diamond shaped face. This can be achieved with a texturizing spray, or by using a curling iron or flat iron to create waves or curls.

3. Consider Highlights

Highlighting the hair can add dimension and depth, which can help balance out the width of the cheekbones. Consider highlights that are lighter around the face, which can draw attention away from the cheekbones.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear my hair in a ponytail?

A: Yes, a high ponytail can elongate the face and balance out the cheekbones.

Q: Are there any hairstyles I should avoid?

A: Avoid hairstyles that add width to the cheekbones, such as blunt bangs or one-length cuts.

Q: Can I wear my hair curly?

A: Yes, curly hair can add volume and texture, which can help balance out the width of the cheekbones.

Q: Should I use a straightening iron?

A: A straightening iron can be used to create a sleek, straight look that emphasizes the jawline.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Generally, it is recommended to wash the hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping the natural oils from the scalp.

Remember, hair care is all about enhancing your natural features. With the right haircut and styling techniques, you can make the most of your diamond shaped face and feel confident in your own skin.
