How To Cut And Fade Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to save some money and time on haircuts, or you simply prefer to do it yourself, then learning how to cut and fade your own hair is a valuable skill to have. With a few basic tools and some patience, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut right at home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of cutting and fading your own hair, as well as provide some tips and tricks to help you get the best results.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut and Fade Your Own Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQ

How to Cut and Fade Your Own Hair

Before we dive into the specifics of how to cut and fade your own hair, let's first review the basic tools you'll need:

  • Clipper with guards
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to catch hair

Once you have these tools, you're ready to get started. The first step is to decide on the length and style you want. Look up pictures of hairstyles you like and take note of the length and any specific details, such as sideburns or a part.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut and fade your own hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner, then dry it completely. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Choose the Right Guard

Attach the desired guard to your clipper, depending on the length you want. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if needed.

Step 3: Start at the Bottom

Begin at the bottom of your head and work your way up, using the clipper to cut your hair in a straight line. Use the comb to lift sections of hair and guide the clipper. Be sure to go over each section several times to ensure an even cut.

Step 4: Remove the Guard

Remove the guard from the clipper and use it to trim around your ears and the back of your neck. Be careful not to cut too close or you may nick yourself.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

Use the scissors to blend the sides of your hair into the top, using a comb to guide the scissors. This will create a natural-looking transition between the top and sides.

Step 6: Fade the Sides

To fade the sides, switch to a shorter guard and use the clipper to create a gradual transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides. Start with a longer guard and work your way down until you achieve the desired length.

Step 7: Define the Hairline

Use the clipper or scissors to define your hairline at the front and back of your head. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can alter the shape of your face.

Step 8: Clean Up

Use the comb and scissors to remove any stray hairs and create a clean, polished look.

Tips and Tricks

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down. It's easier to remove more hair than to add it back.
  • Take breaks and step back from the mirror to check your progress. This will help you avoid overcutting.
  • If you're unsure about a certain area, ask a friend or family member for help or seek the advice of a professional stylist.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to shrink when dry, so you may want to cut it slightly longer than you would with straight hair.

How often should I cut my hair?

This depends on your hair type and the style you're going for. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 4-8 weeks to maintain a healthy length and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking its best, be sure to follow a regular hair care routine. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a heat protectant when styling, and avoiding harsh chemicals and excessive heat.

Now that you know how to cut and fade your own hair, you can save time and money while still looking your best. With some practice and patience, you'll become a pro in no time!
