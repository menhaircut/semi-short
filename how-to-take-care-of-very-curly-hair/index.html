How To Take Care Of Very Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have very curly hair, then you know that it can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, your curls are unique and beautiful, but on the other hand, they can be difficult to manage and maintain. In this article, we'll be giving you a step-by-step guide on how to take care of very curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Determine Your Hair Type
  • Washing Your Hair
  • Conditioning Your Hair
  • Styling Your Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

How to Determine Your Hair Type

Before we get into the nitty-gritty of hair care, it's important to determine your hair type. This will help you choose the right products and techniques for your curls. There are four main types of curly hair:

  • Type 2: Wavy
  • Type 3: Curly
  • Type 4: Coily
  • Type 5: Kinky

To determine your hair type, look at the curl pattern when your hair is wet and without any product. You can also use a hair typing chart for reference.

Washing Your Hair

Washing your hair is an important part of your hair care routine, but it's important to not overdo it. Curly hair is naturally drier than other hair types, so washing too frequently can strip your hair of its natural oils. We recommend washing your hair once or twice a week.

When washing your hair, use a sulfate-free shampoo specifically designed for curly hair. Sulfates can be harsh and drying on your curls. Focus on your scalp and roots, and avoid rubbing your hair too vigorously. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Conditioning Your Hair

Conditioning your hair is just as important as washing it. Curly hair needs moisture to stay healthy and defined. We recommend using a deep conditioning treatment once a week.

After washing your hair, apply conditioner to the lengths and ends of your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls. Rinse thoroughly with cool water to seal the cuticles and prevent frizz.

Styling Your Hair

Styling your curly hair can be a bit tricky, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls. Here are some tips:

  • Use a leave-in conditioner to moisturize your curls and prevent frizz.
  • Apply a curl defining cream or gel to enhance your curls.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls without causing frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your curls, as this can disrupt the curl pattern. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls.
  • Pineapple your hair at night to preserve your curls and prevent frizz.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

In addition to the above tips, there are some general tips you can follow to maintain healthy, beautiful curls:

  • Protect your hair from heat styling tools, as high temperatures can damage your curls.
  • Wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet at night to prevent breakage and frizz.
  • Avoid using hair elastics with metal parts, as these can cause breakage. Instead, use cloth-covered elastics or hair ties made specifically for curly hair.
  • Be gentle when handling your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your curls.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I trim my curly hair?

A: We recommend trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

Q: Can I color my curly hair?

A: Yes, you can color your curly hair, but it's important to use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent color fading and damage.

Q: How can I revive my curls on second-day hair?

A: You can revive your curls on second-day hair by spritzing them with water or a curl refreshing spray, and scrunching them with your hands.

Final Thoughts

Taking care of very curly hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can achieve beautiful, healthy curls. Remember to be gentle when handling your hair, and don't be afraid to experiment with different products and styles to find what works best for you.
