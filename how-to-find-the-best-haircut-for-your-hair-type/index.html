How To Find The Best Haircut For Your Hair Type

Written by Aprilia Nov 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of getting haircuts that just don't work for your hair type? It can be frustrating to walk out of a salon feeling like your hair looks worse than when you went in. But don't worry, there are ways to find the best haircut for your hair type. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you find a haircut that works for you.

Determining Your Hair Type

The first step in finding the best haircut for your hair type is to determine what type of hair you have. This includes the thickness, texture, and density of your hair. Knowing your hair type will help you choose a haircut that will work with your hair instead of against it.

For example, if you have thick, curly hair, you'll want to avoid haircuts that are too short or have too many layers. On the other hand, if you have fine, straight hair, you may want to add layers to create more volume.

Matching the Haircut to Your Face Shape

Your face shape can also play a role in determining the best haircut for you. Different face shapes look better with different hairstyles. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to avoid haircuts that add width to your face, such as blunt bangs or one-length cuts. Instead, opt for a haircut with layers that add height to your hair.

Matching the Haircut to Your Hair Texture

The texture of your hair can also affect the type of haircut that will work best for you. If you have curly hair, you may want to avoid haircuts that require a lot of styling, such as blunt bangs or a bob. Instead, opt for a haircut with long layers that will enhance your natural texture.

Choosing the Right Haircut Length

The length of your hair can also play a role in determining the best haircut for your hair type. If you have long hair, you may want to add layers to create more movement and volume. If you have short hair, you may want to keep your haircut simple and easy to maintain.

Layering vs. One-Length Cuts

Deciding between a layered haircut and a one-length cut can be tough. Layered haircuts can add volume and movement to your hair, while one-length cuts can create a sleek and polished look. Ultimately, the decision comes down to your personal style and hair type.

To Bang or Not to Bang

Bangs can be a great way to change up your look, but they aren't for everyone. If you're considering bangs, think about your face shape and hair type. Blunt bangs can make a round face look even rounder, while side-swept bangs can create a more flattering shape. If you have curly hair, you may want to avoid bangs altogether.

Styling Your Haircut

Once you've found the perfect haircut for your hair type, it's important to know how to style it. Talk to your hairstylist about the best products and techniques to use for your hair type. This will help you maintain your haircut and keep it looking great between appointments.

Tips for Communicating with Your Hairstylist

Communication is key when it comes to getting the perfect haircut. Make sure you're clear about what you want and bring pictures if necessary. Don't be afraid to ask questions or speak up if you're not happy with the results.

FAQs

What if I don't know my hair type?

If you're not sure what type of hair you have, ask your hairstylist. They can help you determine your hair type and recommend haircuts that will work for you.

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on your hair type and how fast your hair grows. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every six to eight weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and maintained.

Can I change my haircut if I don't like it?

If you're not happy with your haircut, talk to your hairstylist. They may be able to make adjustments to the cut or recommend a different style that will work better for you.

Hair Care Tips

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining a great haircut. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and looking great:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

Finding the best haircut for your hair type can take some trial and error, but with these tips and tricks, you'll be on your way to finding a cut that works for you.
