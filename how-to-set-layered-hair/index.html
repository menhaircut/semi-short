How To Set Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Layered hair is a great way to add dimension and movement to your locks. However, styling layered hair can be tricky, especially if you're not sure where to start. In this post, we'll walk you through the steps on how to set layered hair, including some tips and tricks to make your hair look its best.

Table of Contents

  2. Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant
  3. Step 3: Section Your Hair
  4. Step 4: Curl Your Hair

  7. FAQs
  8. Hair Care for Layered Hair

Step 1: Start with Clean Hair

The first step to setting layered hair is to start with clean hair. Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would, and then towel dry your hair until it's damp. Avoid using a blow dryer as this can cause damage to your hair.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant

Before you start styling your hair, it's important to apply a heat protectant to your locks. This will help reduce damage caused by heat styling tools. Apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, paying extra attention to the ends which are the most vulnerable to damage.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Once you've applied the heat protectant, it's time to section your hair. Divide your hair into 1-2 inch sections, and clip the rest of your hair out of the way. This will make it easier to curl your hair and ensure that you don't miss any sections.

Step 4: Curl Your Hair

Now it's time to curl your hair. Take one section of your hair, and wrap it around the barrel of your curling iron. Hold the curling iron in place for 5-10 seconds, and then release the curl. Repeat this process for each section of your hair, alternating the direction of the curls to create a more natural look.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Once you've curled all of your hair, it's time to finish with hairspray. Spray a light mist of hairspray all over your hair to help hold the curls in place. Avoid using too much hairspray as this can make your hair look stiff and unnatural.

Tips for Styling Layered Hair

  • Use a curling iron with a barrel size that's appropriate for your hair length. If you have short hair, use a smaller barrel size, and if you have long hair, use a larger barrel size.
  • Don't curl your hair too tightly as this can make your curls look unnatural. Instead, aim for loose, beachy waves.
  • Experiment with different curling techniques, such as wrapping your hair around the curling iron in different directions, to create a more natural look.

FAQs

Can I set layered hair with a flat iron?

Yes, you can set layered hair with a flat iron. Follow the same steps as you would with a curling iron, but instead of wrapping your hair around the barrel, clamp your hair between the plates of the flat iron and twist the iron as you glide it down your hair.

How often should I set my layered hair?

It's best to set your layered hair no more than once a week to avoid damage caused by heat styling tools.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

To keep your layered hair looking its best, it's important to take proper care of your locks. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent your hair from becoming dry and brittle, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Additionally, be sure to get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and healthy.

Now that you know how to set layered hair, it's time to give it a try! With a little practice and the right tools, you'll be able to create beautiful, bouncy curls that will make your hair look its best.
