How Long Does Hair Loss Last After Pregnancy

Written by Daniel Oct 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a new mom? Are you experiencing hair loss? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many new moms experience hair loss after giving birth. This is known as postpartum hair loss. In this article, we will discuss how long postpartum hair loss lasts and ways to cope with it.

Why Does Hair Loss Occur After Pregnancy?

Hormonal changes during pregnancy can cause an increase in hair growth. This is due to an increase in estrogen levels, which prolongs the growth phase of hair follicles. However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop, and hair follicles return to their normal growth cycle, which can result in hair loss.

Postpartum hair loss is a normal occurrence and typically resolves on its own within a few months. However, some women may experience more severe hair loss, which can take longer to resolve.

When Does Hair Loss Occur After Pregnancy?

Postpartum hair loss typically occurs within the first three months after giving birth. However, it can occur up to six months after giving birth. Hair loss can last for several months before hair growth returns to normal.

How to Prevent Hair Loss After Pregnancy

While postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, there are ways to minimize its effects. These include:

  • Eating a balanced and healthy diet
  • Taking prenatal vitamins
  • Getting enough sleep
  • Reducing stress
  • Avoiding harsh hair treatments and styles

How to Treat Hair Loss After Pregnancy

Most postpartum hair loss resolves on its own without treatment. However, there are some things you can do to help promote hair growth, including:

  • Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Using a hair growth serum or treatment
  • Massaging the scalp to increase blood flow
  • Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth
  • Using a hair loss concealer product

Hair Care Tips for New Moms

As a new mom, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and promote hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using hair dryers and other heat styling tools
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle hair
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on hair
  • Protect hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray
  • Trim split ends to prevent further damage

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for several months before hair growth returns to normal.

Is postpartum hair loss permanent?

No, postpartum hair loss is not permanent. Hair growth will eventually return to normal.

Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, but its effects can be minimized by taking care of your hair and scalp and eating a healthy diet.

When should I see a doctor about postpartum hair loss?

If you are experiencing severe hair loss or hair loss beyond six months after giving birth, you should see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Can breastfeeding cause hair loss?

No, breastfeeding does not directly cause hair loss. However, the hormonal changes that occur during breastfeeding can contribute to postpartum hair loss.

Overall, postpartum hair loss is a common occurrence that typically resolves on its own within a few months. However, taking care of your hair and scalp and eating a healthy diet can help minimize its effects. If you are experiencing severe hair loss or hair loss beyond six months after giving birth, you should see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.
