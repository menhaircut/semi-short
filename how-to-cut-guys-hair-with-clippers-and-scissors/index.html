How To Cut Guys Hair With Clippers And Scissors A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending money at the barber every few weeks? Do you want to learn how to cut your own hair or your partner's? Look no further, because we have a comprehensive guide on how to cut guys hair with clippers and scissors. In this article, we will cover everything from the tools you need, how to section the hair, and tips for a successful cut. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Sectioning the Hair
  • The Clipper Cut
  • The Scissor Cut
  • Blending the Cut
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting, you will need to gather the necessary tools. Here is a list of what you will need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Cape or towel
  • Water spray bottle (optional)

Make sure your clippers and scissors are sharp and well-maintained. Dull tools can lead to uneven cuts and frustration. It's also a good idea to have a water spray bottle on hand to dampen the hair and make it easier to cut.

Sectioning the Hair

Sectioning the hair is an important step to ensure an even cut. Start by dividing the hair into four sections: top, sides, back, and nape. Use a clip or hair tie to hold each section in place. The top section should extend from the hairline to the crown of the head, while the sides should extend from the temples to the ears. The back section should be from the crown to the nape of the neck, and the nape section should be the hair at the bottom of the neck.

The Clipper Cut

Start with the clipper cut by selecting the guard size you want. Use a higher guard for a longer cut and a lower guard for a shorter cut. Begin with the sides by starting at the bottom and moving the clippers up to the desired length. Use the same technique on the back section, starting at the nape and moving up. For the top section, use a comb to lift the hair and cut with the clippers in an upward motion. Repeat until you have achieved the desired length.

The Scissor Cut

Now it's time for the scissor cut. Start with the top section by combing the hair straight up and cutting across in a straight line. Use the previously cut hair as a guide. For the sides and back, comb the hair down and cut in a straight line. It's important to keep the comb and scissors parallel to each other for an even cut.

Blending the Cut

To blend the cut, use the clippers without a guard or with a very low guard. Use a flicking motion to blend the longer and shorter hair together. Start at the bottom and work your way up, making sure to blend the hair evenly.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the mirror to check your progress and make sure the cut is even.
  • Don't forget to trim the sideburns and the hair around the ears.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting hair can be tiring and it's important to stay focused.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my own hair with clippers and scissors?

A: Yes, with the right tools and technique, cutting your own hair is possible. It may take some practice, but it can be a money-saving and rewarding skill to learn.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and style. Generally, every 4-6 weeks is recommended for maintaining a short hairstyle.

Hair Care After the Cut

After the cut, it's important to take care of your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type and style. If you have a shorter cut, you may need to style it with a pomade or gel. Avoid using too much heat on your hair and use a heat protectant if you do use heat styling tools. Remember to schedule your next cut in a few weeks to maintain your fresh look.

Now that you have a comprehensive guide on how to cut guys hair with clippers and scissors, you can confidently try it out at home. Remember to take your time and follow the steps carefully. With practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
