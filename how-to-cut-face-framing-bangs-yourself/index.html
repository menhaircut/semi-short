How To Cut Face Framing Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 28, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of paying a fortune for a simple haircut? Want to try cutting your own bangs but don't know where to start? Look no further! In this article, we'll show you how to cut face framing bangs yourself in just a few simple steps.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Cut Face Framing Bangs Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Bangs
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Face framing bangs are a great way to add a little extra style and interest to your haircut. They can help soften your features, make your eyes pop, and take years off your look. But getting them cut at a salon can be expensive, and it can be hard to maintain the length and shape you want between appointments. That's why we're here to show you how to do it yourself!

How to Cut Face Framing Bangs Yourself

Before we get started, there are a few things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • A towel to drape over your shoulders

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Comb out any tangles and make sure your hair is dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section off the hair you want to cut into bangs. This will depend on the style you're going for, but typically you'll want to section off a triangle shape that starts at the center of your forehead and ends at the outer corners of your eyebrows.

Step 3: Wet Your Bangs

Use your spray bottle to wet the section of hair you want to cut. This will help you see the natural fall of your hair and make it easier to cut evenly.

Step 4: Cut Your Bangs

Starting in the center of your forehead, use your scissors to cut a straight line across the section of hair. Don't worry if it's not perfect - you can always go back and adjust it later. Then, using a comb as a guide, angle your scissors downwards and cut the outer edges of your bangs so they frame your face.

Step 5: Check Your Length

Once you've cut your bangs, check the length in the mirror. If they're too long, you can always go back and trim them a little shorter. If they're too short, you'll need to wait for them to grow out.

Step 6: Adjust the Shape

If you want a softer, more layered look, you can use your scissors to cut into the ends of your bangs. This will create a more textured, natural look.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Once you're happy with the length and shape of your bangs, style them as you normally would. You can blow dry them straight or use a curling iron or flat iron to add some waves or curls.

Tips for Cutting Bangs

  • Start with small snips and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair off than it is to add it back on.
  • Use your fingers or a comb to hold your bangs in place while you cut. This will help you get a more even cut.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short! It's always better to err on the side of caution and cut them a little longer than you think you want them.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but with a little practice and patience, you can do it!

FAQs

Can I cut bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes! Just be sure to cut your bangs a little longer than you want them, since curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries.

How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how fast your bangs start to look shaggy. As a general rule, you'll probably want to trim them every 4-6 weeks.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! It's just hair, and it will grow back. If you make a mistake, try to even it out as best you can and then wait for it to grow out before trying again.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've mastered the art of cutting your own bangs, it's important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it more brittle.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or oil to help nourish your hair and prevent breakage.

Congratulations - you've now learned how to cut face framing bangs yourself! With a little practice and patience, you can save yourself time and money by doing it at home. Just remember to take it slow, start with small snips, and use a mirror to check your progress as you go. Happy cutting!
