How To Get Thin Curly Hair Thicker A Complete Guide

If you have thin curly hair, you may feel like you're stuck with limp, lifeless locks. But don't despair! With the right techniques and products, you can add volume and thickness to your curls for a fuller, more voluminous look. In this guide, we'll share our top tips for getting thin curly hair thicker and more voluminous.

How to Get Thin Curly Hair Thicker

Getting thicker, fuller curls starts with proper hair care. Here are some tips to help you achieve the look you want:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Look for shampoos and conditioners that are specifically formulated to add volume and thickness to hair. These products contain ingredients that help lift the hair cuticle, giving your curls more body and bounce. Avoid using heavy, moisturizing products that can weigh down your hair.

2. Apply a Hair Mask

Once a week, apply a hair mask to help strengthen and nourish your hair. Look for masks that contain ingredients like keratin, biotin, and panthenol, which can help add volume and thickness to your curls. Leave the mask on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Use a Root-Lifting Spray

To give your curls an extra boost of volume, use a root-lifting spray on damp hair. Apply the spray to the roots of your hair, then blow dry with a diffuser attachment to help create more volume and texture.

4. Avoid Brushing Your Hair When Dry

Brushing your hair when it's dry can cause breakage and damage, which can make your hair look even thinner. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.

5. Add Layers to Your Haircut

If you're looking to add more volume to your curls, consider getting a haircut with layers. Layers can help create the illusion of thicker hair by adding movement and texture to your curls.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get thicker, fuller curls:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair with a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Massage the shampoo into your scalp, then rinse thoroughly. Apply the conditioner to the lengths of your hair, avoiding the roots, then rinse out.

Step 2: Apply a Hair Mask

Once a week, apply a hair mask to help strengthen and nourish your hair. Leave the mask on for at least 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

Step 3: Apply a Root-Lifting Spray

Apply a root-lifting spray to damp hair, focusing on the roots. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to help create more volume and texture.

Step 4: Avoid Brushing Your Hair When Dry

When your hair is dry, avoid brushing it. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls.

Step 5: Add Layers to Your Haircut

If you're looking to add more volume to your curls, consider getting a haircut with layers. Layers can help create the illusion of thicker hair by adding movement and texture to your curls.

Tips for Thicker Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get thicker, fuller curls:

1. Use a Diffuser Attachment on Your Blow Dryer

A diffuser attachment can help create more volume and texture in your curls. Use it on a low heat setting to avoid damaging your hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it look thinner. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look thinner.

FAQs

Q: Can I use hair extensions to make my hair look thicker?

A: Yes, hair extensions can be a great way to add volume and thickness to your curls. Look for extensions that are made from high-quality human hair and have a natural curl pattern that matches your own hair.

Q: Can changing my diet help make my hair thicker?

A: Eating a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help improve the overall health of your hair, but it's unlikely to make a significant difference in the thickness of your curls.

Hair Care for Thin Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for thin curly hair:

1. Don't Overwash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it look thinner. Try to wash your hair no more than every other day.

2. Use a Microfiber Towel to Dry Your Hair

Using a microfiber towel to dry your hair can help prevent frizz and breakage, which can make your hair look even thinner.

3. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent tangles and breakage, which can make your hair look thinner.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve thicker, fuller curls that you'll love showing off. Remember to be patient and consistent in your hair care routine, and you'll see the results you want in no time.
