How To Do A High Taper Fade Step By Step Guide

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, a high taper fade is definitely worth considering. This haircut is characterized by a gradual fade from the top of the head to the sides and back, creating a sleek and modern look. In this article, we'll show you how to do a high taper fade step by step, along with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

How to Do a High Taper Fade

A high taper fade is a haircut that gradually fades from a longer length at the top to a shorter length on the sides and back, creating a dramatic and stylish look. It's a popular choice for men of all ages and hair types, and it's relatively easy to achieve with the right tools and technique.

Step by Step Guide

Here's how to do a high taper fade:

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair. This will help you get a better idea of your natural hair texture and shape, which will make it easier to cut.
  2. Next, use a comb to separate the hair on top of your head from the hair on the sides and back. Use hair clips to keep the top section of hair out of the way.
  3. Choose the length of the fade you want. A high taper fade typically starts at the temples and gradually gets shorter towards the ears and neck. You can use different clipper guard sizes to achieve different lengths, depending on your preference.
  4. Begin the fade by using clippers with the longest guard size. Start at the temples and work your way down towards the ears and neck, using a smooth and even motion. Repeat this process with progressively shorter guard sizes until you reach your desired length.
  5. Blend the fade by using a clipper without a guard. Start at the bottom of the fade and work your way up, using a smooth and even motion to create a seamless transition between the longer hair on top and the shorter hair on the sides and back.
  6. Use scissors to trim any remaining hair on top of your head to your desired length.
  7. Finish the haircut by using a trimmer to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neck.
  8. Style your hair as desired, using a hair product like wax, pomade, or gel to keep it in place.

Remember, practice makes perfect when it comes to cutting hair. Don't be afraid to experiment with different clipper guard sizes and techniques to find the look that's right for you.

Tips for a Perfect High Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect high taper fade:

  • Invest in a good quality pair of clippers and scissors. This will make it easier to achieve a clean and precise cut.
  • Use a comb to help guide your clippers and scissors. This will help you create a more even and consistent cut.
  • Take your time and work slowly. Rushing the process can lead to mistakes and uneven fades.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or advice. If you're unsure about how to cut your hair, or if you're having trouble achieving the look you want, ask a professional barber or stylist for guidance.

FAQs

Here are some common questions and answers about high taper fades:

  • Q: How often do I need to get a high taper fade?
  • A: This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how well you maintain your fade. Generally, you'll need to get a touch-up every 2-4 weeks to keep your fade looking sharp.
  • Q: Can I do a high taper fade at home?
  • A: Yes, you can do a high taper fade at home with the right tools and technique. However, if you're new to cutting hair or if you're unsure about how to achieve the look you want, it's best to seek professional guidance.
  • Q: What hair types work best with a high taper fade?
  • A: A high taper fade works well with most hair types, including straight, wavy, curly, and coily hair. However, it may be more difficult to achieve a sharp and defined fade with very fine or thin hair.

Hair Care Tips for a High Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you care for your high taper fade:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help keep your hair and scalp clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to maintain your fade.
  • Use a hair product like wax or pomade to keep your hair in place and add shine. However, be careful not to use too much product, as this can make your hair greasy or heavy.
  • Visit your barber or stylist regularly for touch-ups and maintenance. This will help keep your fade looking sharp and fresh.

With these tips and techniques, you should be able to achieve a flawless high taper fade that looks great and is easy to maintain. Remember to take your time, practice regularly, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and techniques.
