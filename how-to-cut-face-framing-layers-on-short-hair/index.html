How To Cut Face Framing Layers On Short Hair

Written by Frank Jul 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have short hair, you may think that it's impossible to add some layers to create a more dynamic look. However, with face framing layers, you can add some dimension to your short hair without sacrificing its length. In this article, we'll discuss how to cut face framing layers on short hair step by step, as well as some tips and FAQs.

Description

Face framing layers are layers of hair that are cut around your face to create a more flattering look. These layers can help to soften your features and add some dimension to your hair. They're perfect for those who have short hair and want to add some style without sacrificing its length.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers on Short Hair: Step by Step

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step to cutting face framing layers on short hair is to wash and dry your hair. This will help to remove any dirt and oil that may be on your hair, and it will also make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into two parts: the top and the bottom. Use a hair tie or clip to hold the top section in place.

Step 3: Create a Guide

Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and use it as a guide for the rest of your layers. This section should be the length that you want your layers to be.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Start cutting the first layer of hair by holding the guide section at a 45-degree angle and cutting the hair underneath it. Make sure to cut the hair in small sections, so you have more control over the length.

Step 5: Cut the Rest of the Layers

Use the first layer as a guide for the rest of your layers. Cut each layer at a 45-degree angle, making sure to cut the hair underneath the previous layer.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

After you have cut all of your layers, use a pair of scissors to blend them together. This will create a more natural look and make your layers less noticeable.

Tips

  • Make sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Use thinning shears to remove some of the bulk from your hair.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as you may end up with a style that is too short.
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Can I cut face framing layers on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut face framing layers on curly hair. However, make sure to cut the hair when it's dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is. You may also want to consult with a professional stylist who has experience cutting curly hair.

How often should I cut my face framing layers?

You should aim to cut your face framing layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and length.

Can I cut face framing layers on my own?

You can cut face framing layers on your own, but it's recommended to consult with a professional stylist if you're unsure about cutting your own hair.

Hair Care

After cutting your face framing layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and shape. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. You may also want to use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

By following these steps and tips, you can easily cut face framing layers on short hair and create a more dynamic look. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to maintain its health and shape.
