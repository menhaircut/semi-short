How To Fix Fly Away Short Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Christine Sep 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have short hair, you know how frustrating it can be when those pesky flyaways start sticking up. Fly away hair can ruin an otherwise perfect hairstyle, making you look unkempt and unprofessional. Fortunately, there are several ways to fix fly away short hair, and we’ve got all the tips and tricks you need to know.

Description

Fly away hair is a common problem faced by people with short hair, especially those with fine hair. It occurs when the hair cuticles are lifted, allowing the hair to stick up and away from the scalp. Fly away hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including dryness, static electricity, and damage from heat styling tools.

How to Fix Fly Away Short Hair

Here are some simple steps you can take to fix fly away short hair:

1. Use a Hydrating Shampoo and Conditioner

Dry hair is more prone to flyaways, so using a hydrating shampoo and conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and less likely to stick up. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

2. Apply a Leave-in Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help smooth down the hair cuticles and prevent flyaways. Apply a small amount to your hair after washing and conditioning, and comb through to distribute evenly.

3. Use a Hair Oil

Hair oils like argan oil or jojoba oil can help smooth down flyaways and add shine to your hair. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends, to help keep your hair smooth and shiny throughout the day.

4. Use a Hair Serum

Hair serums are designed to smooth down flyaways and add shine to your hair. Apply a small amount to your hair, focusing on the ends, to help keep your hair smooth and shiny throughout the day.

5. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it more prone to flyaways. If possible, avoid using these tools or use them sparingly. If you must use them, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to help minimize damage.

6. Use a Humidifier

Dry indoor air can contribute to flyaway hair. Using a humidifier can help add moisture to the air and prevent your hair from drying out.

Step by Step: How to Fix Fly Away Short Hair

Here’s a step-by-step guide to fixing fly away short hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-in Conditioner

Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair, combing through to distribute evenly.

Step 3: Apply a Hair Oil or Serum

Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to your hair, focusing on the ends.

Step 4: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a comb or brush to smooth down any flyaways.

Tips for Fixing Fly Away Short Hair

Here are some additional tips for fixing fly away short hair:

1. Use a Boar Bristle Brush

A boar bristle brush can help smooth down flyaways and distribute natural oils throughout your hair.

2. Wear a Silk or Satin Scarf to Bed

Wearing a silk or satin scarf to bed can help prevent your hair from rubbing against your pillow and causing flyaways.

3. Use a Hair Mask

A weekly hair mask can help nourish and hydrate your hair, making it less prone to flyaways.

FAQ

Q. What causes flyaway hair?

A. Flyaway hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including dryness, static electricity, and damage from heat styling tools.

Q. How can I prevent flyaways?

A. Using a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, applying a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, and avoiding heat styling tools can help prevent flyaways.

Q. Can I use hairspray to fix flyaway hair?

A. Yes, you can use hairspray to help smooth down flyaways. However, be careful not to use too much, as it can make your hair stiff and crunchy.

Hair Care for Fly Away Short Hair

In addition to the tips and tricks listed above, taking good care of your hair can help prevent flyaways. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, avoid over-brushing, and protect your hair from damage caused by heat styling tools.

By following these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to flyaway short hair and hello to a smooth, polished hairstyle.
