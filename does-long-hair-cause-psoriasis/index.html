Does long hair cause psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune disease that affects the skin. It causes red, scaly patches on the skin that can be itchy and painful. Some people believe that long hair can cause psoriasis. In this article, we will explore whether there is any truth to this belief.

How to know if long hair causes psoriasis

There is no scientific evidence to suggest that long hair can cause psoriasis. However, some people with psoriasis may find that their symptoms are worse when they have long hair. This may be because long hair can trap heat and sweat against the scalp, which can irritate the skin and trigger a psoriasis flare-up.

Step by step guide to managing psoriasis with long hair

If you have psoriasis and long hair, there are some steps you can take to manage your symptoms:

  1. Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt, oil, and sweat from your scalp. Use a gentle shampoo that is free from sulfates and fragrances.
  2. Avoid using hot styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and scalp.
  3. Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull on your scalp and cause irritation.
  4. Use a soft brush or comb to avoid scratching your scalp.
  5. Avoid exposing your scalp to extreme temperatures, such as cold weather or hot sun.
  6. If you notice any changes in your psoriasis symptoms, such as increased redness or itching, speak to your doctor or dermatologist.

Tips for managing psoriasis with long hair

Here are some additional tips for managing psoriasis with long hair:

  • Avoid using hair products that contain harsh chemicals, such as sulfates and parabens.
  • Consider using a scalp treatment, such as a medicated shampoo or a scalp serum, to help manage your psoriasis symptoms.
  • Avoid scratching or picking at your scalp, as this can worsen your psoriasis symptoms.
  • Try wearing your hair in loose styles, such as a low bun or a loose braid, to avoid putting too much pressure on your scalp.
  • Consider visiting a dermatologist who specializes in psoriasis to get personalized advice on managing your symptoms.

Hair care tips for people with psoriasis

If you have psoriasis, it's important to take good care of your hair and scalp. Here are some hair care tips for people with psoriasis:

  • Use a gentle shampoo that is free from sulfates and fragrances.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, as they can pull on your scalp and cause irritation.
  • Use a soft brush or comb to avoid scratching your scalp.

In conclusion, there is no evidence to suggest that long hair causes psoriasis. However, long hair can irritate the scalp and trigger psoriasis flare-ups in some people. If you have psoriasis and long hair, it's important to take good care of your hair and scalp to manage your symptoms.
