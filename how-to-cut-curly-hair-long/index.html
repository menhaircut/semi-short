How To Cut Curly Hair Long A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be tricky to cut, especially if you want to keep it long. You don't want to end up with uneven layers or a choppy look that will take months to grow out. But don't worry, with the right technique and some helpful tips, you can achieve a beautiful, long cut that enhances your natural curls.

  2. Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Curly Hair Long

Before you start cutting your curly hair, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a wide-tooth comb, and some hair clips to section your hair.

When it comes to cutting curly hair, the key is to cut it when it's dry. That way, you can see how the curls will fall and avoid any unwanted shrinkage. It's also important to cut curly hair in its natural state, meaning you should avoid straightening or blowing it out before cutting.

Another important thing to keep in mind is that curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries, so you'll want to cut off less than you think you need to. It's always better to start with a little and work your way up.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. Be gentle to avoid pulling or breaking your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start with one section at a time, and begin cutting small sections of hair at a time. Use your fingers to guide the scissors and cut at an angle to create layers. Remember to cut less than you think you need to.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections of hair, use the scissors to blend the layers together. Be careful not to cut off too much or create any choppy lines.

Step 6: Check the Length

Check the length of your hair by pulling a few strands down. If you need to cut more, do so in small sections until you're happy with the length.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair Long

  • Cut curly hair when it's dry to avoid shrinkage.
  • Use a pair of sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Cut less than you think you need to.
  • Start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Blend the layers together to avoid any choppy lines.

FAQs

Can I cut my curly hair myself?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair yourself. Just be sure to follow the steps above and take your time.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to keep your hair healthy and avoid split ends.

What if I mess up my curly hair cut?

Don't worry, hair grows back! If you're not happy with your cut, visit a professional stylist to fix it or wait for it to grow out.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Caring for curly hair is important to maintain its health and beauty. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, or use them sparingly.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid frizz and breakage.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry to avoid breakage.

By following these tips and this step-by-step guide, you can achieve a beautiful, long cut for your curly hair. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you'll have gorgeous, healthy curls in no time.
