Best Straightener For Thick Curly Hair 2020 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Oct 24, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy, unruly hair every day? If you have thick curly hair, you know how challenging it can be to style and manage. One of the best solutions to tame your curls and achieve sleek, straight locks is by using a straightener. However, not all straighteners are created equal, especially when it comes to thick curly hair. In this guide, we'll help you find the best straightener for thick curly hair 2020, and provide tips on how to use it effectively.

How to Choose the Best Straightener for Thick Curly Hair

Before we dive into the top straighteners for thick curly hair, let's discuss the factors you should consider when choosing one.

Plate Material

The plate material is one of the most important factors to consider when buying a straightener. It determines how well the straightener will work on your hair, and its durability.

For thick curly hair, ceramic or ceramic tourmaline plates are the best option. They distribute heat evenly, reduce frizz, and leave your hair shiny and smooth. Titanium plates are also a good choice, but they can get very hot, which can damage your hair if not used properly.

Plate Width

The plate width is another important factor to consider. If you have thick hair, you should choose a straightener with wider plates. This will help you cover more hair with each pass, which will save you time and reduce heat damage.

Temperature Settings

Thick curly hair requires higher temperatures to straighten effectively. Look for a straightener with adjustable temperature settings, so you can find the right heat level for your hair type.

How to Straighten Thick Curly Hair

Now that you have the right straightener for your hair, it's time to learn how to use it effectively. Here are some tips to help you get the best results.

1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Straightening dirty or wet hair can cause damage and frizz. Make sure your hair is clean and completely dry before using your straightener.

2. Use Heat Protectant

Applying a heat protectant before straightening can help reduce damage and keep your hair healthy. Spray it evenly throughout your hair, focusing on the ends.

3. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections, starting at the bottom. This will make it easier to straighten each strand and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

4. Use a Comb

Before straightening each section, use a comb to detangle it. This will help you achieve a smoother finish and prevent snagging.

5. Take Your Time

Straightening thick curly hair can take time, so be patient. Don't rush through the process, as this can cause damage and reduce the effectiveness of the straightener.

FAQs

1. Can I use a straightener on wet hair?

No, it's not recommended to use a straightener on wet hair. Wet hair is more susceptible to damage, and using a straightener on it can cause breakage and frizz.

2. How often should I straighten my hair?

It's best to limit straightening to once or twice a week to prevent damage.

3. Can I straighten my hair without a heat protectant?

No, using a heat protectant is essential to prevent damage and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Curly Hair

Aside from using the right straightener, taking care of your hair is also important. Here are some tips to keep your thick curly hair healthy and manageable.

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Thick curly hair is prone to dryness, so using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner can help keep it hydrated. Look for products that contain natural oils, such as coconut or argan oil.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

If possible, avoid heat styling your hair frequently. This can cause damage and make your hair more difficult to manage.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using a brush on your hair, as this can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

4. Deep Condition Once a Week

Deep conditioning once a week can help nourish your hair and keep it healthy. Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. Leave it on for at least 15 minutes before rinsing.

5. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Wearing your hair in tight hairstyles, such as ponytails or braids, can cause breakage and damage. Try to avoid these styles or wear them loosely.

With the right straightener and proper hair care, you can achieve sleek, straight locks with ease. Follow these tips and recommendations to find the best straightener for thick curly hair 2020 and keep your hair healthy and manageable.
