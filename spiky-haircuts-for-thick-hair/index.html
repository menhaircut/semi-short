Spiky Haircuts For Thick Hair A Guide For Men In 2023

Written by Luffy Dec 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick hair and want to add some edge to your look, spiky haircuts may be the perfect option for you. This style has been popular for years and continues to be a go-to for men who want to add some texture and dimension to their hair. In this guide, we will discuss how to achieve spiky haircuts for thick hair, tips for styling and maintenance, and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Achieve Spiky Haircuts for Thick Hair

The first step in achieving a spiky haircut for thick hair is to find a skilled barber who can help you achieve your desired look. They will be able to provide recommendations based on your hair type, face shape, and personal style. Once you have found the right barber, you can discuss different haircuts that will work for you, such as a classic spiky cut or a more modern textured look.

One of the most important things to keep in mind when getting a spiky haircut is that you need to have enough length for the hair to stand up. This means that you may need to grow out your hair for a few weeks or months before getting the cut. Your barber can provide advice on the ideal length for your hair type and style.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Spiky Haircuts for Thick Hair

Once you have your spiky haircut, it's time to start styling your hair on a daily basis. Here is a step-by-step guide to achieving the perfect spiky look:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of hair gel or wax to your fingertips.
  3. Work the product into your hair, starting at the roots and working your way up.
  4. Use a comb or your fingers to create spikes, working in small sections.
  5. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your spikes in place.

Remember, less is more when it comes to styling products. Too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy or clumpy. Stick to a small amount of gel or wax and build up as needed.

Tips for Maintaining Spiky Haircuts for Thick Hair

Now that you have achieved the perfect spiky haircut, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Here are some tips to help you keep your spiky hair looking great:

  • Wash your hair every other day to prevent product buildup.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or hot irons, as this can cause damage.
  • Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to keep it in shape.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a month to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

With proper maintenance, your spiky haircut can stay looking great for weeks or even months.

FAQs About Spiky Haircuts for Thick Hair

Q: Can I achieve a spiky haircut if I have thin hair?

A: Spiky haircuts work best on thick hair, but you can still achieve a similar look with thin hair by using a lightweight styling product and backcombing your hair to add volume.

Q: How often should I wash my hair if I have a spiky haircut?

A: It's best to wash your hair every other day to prevent product buildup, but you can adjust the frequency based on your hair type and personal preference.

Q: Can I style my spiky hair differently for formal occasions?

A: Yes, you can style your spiky hair in a more polished look for formal occasions by using a stronger hold product and smoothing your hair down with a comb.

Hair Care for Spiky Haircuts for Thick Hair

When it comes to hair care for spiky haircuts, it's important to use products that are designed for your hair type and style. Look for lightweight gels, waxes, and hairsprays that provide a strong hold without weighing your hair down.

In addition, make sure to use a shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for thick hair to keep your hair healthy and moisturized. Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or hot irons, as this can cause damage and make it more difficult to style your hair into spikes.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can achieve a stylish and edgy spiky haircut that will turn heads wherever you go.
