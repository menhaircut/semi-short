How To Make A Messy Bun Hairstyle A Relaxed Guide

Written by Robby Oct 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking for an easy and stylish way to keep your hair out of your face, a messy bun hairstyle might just be the perfect solution for you. Not only is it effortless to create, but it's also a versatile hairstyle that can be worn for any occasion, whether it's a casual day out or a formal event. In this article, we'll show you step-by-step how to make a messy bun hairstyle and provide you with some tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you maintain your messy bun looking fresh and fabulous.

How to Make a Messy Bun Hairstyle

The beauty of a messy bun hairstyle is that it doesn't have to be perfect. In fact, the messier, the better. Here are the basic steps to create this effortless style:

Step 1: Gather Your Hair

Start by gathering your hair into a high ponytail. You can use a brush or your fingers to smooth out any bumps or knots.

Step 2: Twist and Wrap

Next, twist your ponytail and wrap it around the base of your ponytail. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place. Don't worry if it's not perfect; remember, the messier, the better.

Step 3: Loosen It Up

To create a messy look, gently pull on the hair around the bun to loosen it up. You can also pull out a few strands of hair around your face to frame it.

Step 4: Set It in Place

Finally, set your messy bun in place by using hairspray or a light hold styling product. And voila! You now have a messy bun hairstyle that's perfect for any occasion.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to creating a messy bun hairstyle:

Step 1: Brush Your Hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 2: Gather Your Hair

Next, gather your hair into a high ponytail. Use a hair tie to secure it in place. Make sure it's tight enough to hold your hair in place, but not so tight that it causes discomfort.

Step 3: Twist Your Hair

Now, twist your ponytail until it starts to coil around itself. Keep twisting until you reach the end of your ponytail.

Step 4: Wrap Your Hair

Wrap the twisted ponytail around the base of your ponytail. Use bobby pins to secure the bun in place. You can use as many pins as you need to make sure the bun is secure.

Step 5: Pull Out Strands of Hair

Once your bun is secure, gently pull out some strands of hair around your face to frame it. You can also pull out some strands from the bun to create a messy look.

Step 6: Secure the Look

Finally, secure your messy bun in place with hairspray or a light hold styling product. This will help keep your hair in place and prevent frizz.

Tips for Making the Perfect Messy Bun

Here are some tips to help you make the perfect messy bun:

  • Use a hair tie that matches your hair color to avoid it being visible in your bun.
  • Tease your ponytail before twisting it to create more volume and texture.
  • Use bobby pins to secure any loose strands of hair in place.
  • Don't be afraid to use hair accessories like headbands, scarves, or hair clips to add some personality to your messy bun.
  • Experiment with different bun placements, such as a low bun or a side bun, to switch up your look.

FAQs

How long does it take to make a messy bun hairstyle?

A messy bun hairstyle can take anywhere from 2 to 5 minutes to create, depending on your hair length and thickness.

Can you create a messy bun hairstyle with short hair?

Yes, you can create a messy bun hairstyle with short hair. You may need to use some bobby pins to secure any loose strands in place.

Can you wear a messy bun hairstyle to a formal event?

Yes, you can wear a messy bun hairstyle to a formal event. However, make sure to style it in a way that looks intentional and polished, rather than messy and unkempt.

Hair Care Advice for Messy Buns

Here are some hair care tips to help keep your messy bun looking fresh and healthy:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and dryness. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray before styling.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Avoid using too many hair products, as they can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Don't wash your hair too often, as it can strip it of its natural oils. Instead, opt for dry shampoo or a refreshing hair mist to freshen up your hair between washes.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to make a messy bun hairstyle, go ahead and try it out for yourself. With a little practice, you'll be able to create the perfect messy bun that's both effortless and chic. Remember, the key is to embrace the messiness and have fun with it!
