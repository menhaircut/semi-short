How To Blow Dry Your Hair To Make It Look Thicker

Written by Petter Jul 21, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have fine, thin hair, you may feel like you’re stuck with limp, lifeless locks. But fear not, there are ways to make your hair look thicker and fuller – and it all starts with your blow drying technique. In this blog post, we’ll show you how to blow dry your hair to make it look thicker, with step-by-step instructions and expert tips for achieving the best results.

  • Step-by-Step Instructions

How to Blow Dry Your Hair to Make it Look Thicker

The key to making your hair look thicker with a blow dryer is to add volume at the roots. This will create lift and fullness, giving the appearance of thicker hair. Here’s how to do it:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub it too hard, as this can cause damage and breakage. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to your roots, working it through with your fingers.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to blow dry each section and ensure that you don’t miss any areas. Start with the bottom sections of your hair first, working your way up to the crown.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a round brush, blow dry each section of your hair, focusing on the roots. Start by holding the brush at the roots and pointing the blow dryer nozzle down towards your scalp. Use the brush to lift the hair upwards, creating volume at the roots. Once the roots are dry, continue blow drying the rest of the section, using the brush to smooth out any tangles and create a polished finish.

Step 4: Finish Your Style

Once you’ve blow dried your entire head, finish your style with a light hairspray or texturizing spray. This will help to hold the volume and create a more natural, tousled look.

Expert Tips for Making Your Hair Look Thicker

Here are some additional tips for making your hair look thicker:

  • Use a root lifting spray or powder to add extra volume at the roots.
  • Blow dry your hair upside down to create more lift and volume.
  • Use a boar bristle brush to smooth out your hair and create shine.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.

FAQs

Can I blow dry my hair every day?

While it’s okay to blow dry your hair every day, it’s important to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using the highest heat setting on your blow dryer. Overheating your hair can cause damage and breakage, which can make your hair appear thinner.

What’s the best type of blow dryer to use?

A ceramic or tourmaline blow dryer is best for fine, thin hair, as it will distribute heat evenly and help to prevent damage. Look for a blow dryer with multiple heat and speed settings, so you can customize your blow drying routine to your specific hair type.

Hair Care Tips for Fine, Thin Hair

In addition to using the right blow drying technique, there are several other things you can do to care for your fine, thin hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends, which can make your hair appear thinner.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients, such as B vitamins, iron, and biotin, which are essential for healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and using the right blow drying technique, you can make your hair look thicker and fuller, giving you the confidence to rock any hairstyle.
