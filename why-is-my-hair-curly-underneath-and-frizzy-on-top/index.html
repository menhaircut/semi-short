Why Is My Hair Curly Underneath And Frizzy On Top

Written by Oliver Sep 07, 2023 · 5 min read

It can be frustrating to deal with hair that's curly underneath and frizzy on top. Many people struggle with this issue, and it can be difficult to figure out the root cause. In this article, we'll explore the reasons why your hair might be behaving this way and provide tips on how to manage it.

Table of Contents

  • How to Manage Curly and Frizzy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. People with curly hair have follicles that are more oval-shaped, which causes the hair to curl. Frizz, on the other hand, is caused by a lack of moisture in the hair. When the hair is dry and lacks moisture, it can become frizzy and unruly.

If you have curly hair, it's common for the hair on the top of your head to be straighter and frizzier than the hair underneath. This can be due to a number of factors, including genetics, styling habits, and environmental factors.

Some people are born with hair that's naturally curly underneath and frizzy on top, while others develop this issue over time. Regardless of the cause, there are ways to manage curly and frizzy hair to make it more manageable and healthy.

How to Manage Curly and Frizzy Hair

If you're struggling with curly hair that's frizzy on top, there are several things you can do to manage it:

1. Use the Right Products

Using the right products is key when it comes to managing curly and frizzy hair. Look for products that are designed specifically for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos, conditioners, and styling products. These products are formulated to provide moisture and hold for curly hair, which can help reduce frizz and define your curls.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can be damaging to curly hair, and it can also contribute to frizz. Try to avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners, curling irons, and blow dryers as much as possible. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low to minimize damage.

3. Embrace Your Natural Texture

Instead of trying to fight your natural texture, embrace it! Work with your curls and use products that enhance and define them. This can help minimize frizz and make your hair more manageable.

4. Protect Your Hair from Environmental Factors

Environmental factors like humidity, wind, and pollution can all contribute to frizz. To protect your hair from these factors, try wearing a hat or scarf when you're outside, and avoid spending too much time in humid environments.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for managing curly and frizzy hair:

1. Shampoo and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner. These products are formulated to provide moisture and hold for curly hair, which can help reduce frizz and define your curls.

2. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After washing your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to provide additional moisture and protection. This will help keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

3. Style Your Hair

Use a curl-enhancing styling product to define your curls and hold your style in place. Apply the product to damp hair and use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair gently.

4. Touch Up as Needed

Throughout the day, you may need to touch up your hair to keep it looking its best. Use a curl-enhancing spray or cream to refresh your curls and tame any frizz.

Tips for Managing Curly and Frizzy Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing curly and frizzy hair:

1. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent damage and breakage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

2. Avoid Brushing Your Hair

Brushing curly hair can cause it to become frizzy and lose definition. Instead, use your fingers to gently comb through your hair and separate your curls.

3. Sleep on a Satin Pillowcase

Sleeping on a satin pillowcase can help prevent frizz and breakage. Satin is smoother than cotton, which means your hair will glide over it instead of becoming tangled.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair if it's curly underneath and frizzy on top?

A: While you can straighten your hair, it's important to use caution and avoid heat styling as much as possible. Heat styling can be damaging to curly hair, and it can also contribute to frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat setting on low to minimize damage.

Q: Can I color my hair if it's curly and frizzy?

A: Yes, you can color your hair if it's curly and frizzy. However, it's important to use caution and choose a gentle, non-damaging hair color formula. Coloring your hair can make it more prone to dryness and breakage, so be sure to use a deep conditioning treatment regularly.

Hair Care Products for Curly and Frizzy Hair

If you're struggling with curly hair that's frizzy on top, here are some hair care products that can help:

  • Curl-enhancing shampoo
  • Curl-enhancing conditioner
  • Leave-in conditioner
  • Curl-enhancing styling product
  • Curl-enhancing spray or cream

By using the right products and following the tips outlined in this article, you can manage curly and frizzy hair and keep it healthy and beautiful.
