Is Cutting Asian Hair Different

Written by Bardi Aug 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're a hairstylist or someone who's interested in cutting hair, you might be wondering if cutting Asian hair is different from cutting other hair types. The answer is yes, it is different. In this article, we'll discuss why Asian hair is different, and offer tips on how to cut and care for this hair type.

Why is Asian Hair Different?

Asian hair is different for several reasons. Firstly, Asian hair is generally thicker and coarser than other hair types. This means that it can be more difficult to cut and style, and can take longer to dry. Asian hair also tends to be straighter than other hair types, which can make it harder to create volume and texture.

Another reason why Asian hair is different is because of its color. Asian hair tends to be darker than other hair types, which can make it harder to see the individual strands when cutting. This can make it more difficult to achieve a precise cut.

How to Cut Asian Hair

If you're cutting Asian hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind. Firstly, it's important to use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage the hair. Secondly, it's important to cut the hair when it's dry. This will help you to see the natural fall of the hair, and will make it easier to achieve a precise cut.

When cutting Asian hair, it's also important to take into account the natural shape of the head. Asian hair tends to be flatter at the crown, so it's important to add volume to this area. You can do this by using layers or by cutting the hair into a graduated bob.

Step by Step Guide:

Here's a step by step guide to cutting Asian hair:

  1. Start by washing and drying the hair.
  2. Section the hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back.
  3. Starting at the back, take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers.
  4. Using sharp scissors, cut the hair at an angle, taking care not to cut too much off.
  5. Repeat this process with each section of hair, working your way towards the front.
  6. When you reach the front of the hair, take a small section of hair and cut it at an angle, following the natural shape of the head.
  7. Repeat this process with each section of hair at the front.
  8. Finish by trimming the ends of the hair to create a neat, finished look.

Tips for Cutting Asian Hair

Here are some tips for cutting Asian hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Cut the hair when it's dry to achieve a precise cut.
  • Take into account the natural shape of the head when cutting the hair.
  • Add volume to the crown of the head to create a more balanced look.
  • Use layers to create texture and movement in the hair.

FAQs

Can I use the same techniques to cut Asian hair as I would with other hair types?

No, Asian hair requires different techniques than other hair types. It's important to take into account the thickness, texture, and natural shape of the head when cutting Asian hair.

What's the best way to care for Asian hair?

The best way to care for Asian hair is to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and to avoid using heat styling tools too often. It's also important to trim the ends of the hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Asian Hair

Here are some tips for caring for Asian hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to the hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Trim the ends of the hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle the hair.
  • Avoid pulling the hair too tight when styling, as this can damage the hair follicles.

Conclusion

Cutting Asian hair is different from cutting other hair types, but with the right techniques and tools, it can be done successfully. By taking into account the thickness, texture, and natural shape of the head, you can create a beautiful, precise cut that complements the natural features of the individual. With the right care, Asian hair can be healthy, strong, and beautiful.
