How To Cut Your Own Hair Medium Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Oct 08, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim? Or maybe you just want to try something new and give yourself a fresh look? Whatever your reason may be, cutting your own hair can seem like a daunting task, especially if you’re looking to add some layers. But fear not, we’ve got you covered with this easy-to-follow guide on how to cut your own hair medium layers.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Medium Layers
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

How to Cut Your Own Hair Medium Layers

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let’s go over some of the tools you’ll need to get started. Make sure you have a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips or ties, and a mirror.

When it comes to cutting your own hair, it’s important to remember that less is more. Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always trim more later, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it’s completely dry before you start cutting.

2. Use your comb to part your hair down the middle, creating two even sections.

3. Take one section of hair and clip or tie the other section out of the way.

4. Starting at the front of your hair, take a small section of hair between your fingers and hold it straight up.

5. Use your scissors to make small snips into the ends of your hair. Remember, less is more.

6. Continue working your way back, taking small sections of hair and snipping into the ends.

7. Once you’ve finished one side, repeat on the other side.

8. Once you’ve added the layers, use your comb to blend them together.

9. Check both sides in the mirror to make sure they’re even.

10. Finish with a light hairspray or styling product to keep your layers in place.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

- Start with small snips and work your way up.

- Use a mirror to check your progress as you go.

- If you’re unsure about cutting too much, start with a smaller section of hair.

- Remember that hair will look shorter when it’s wet, so be cautious when cutting.

- Don’t rush the process. Take your time and be patient.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair while it’s wet?

It’s best to cut your hair when it’s dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with and can lead to uneven cuts.

How often should I trim my hair?

It’s recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don’t panic. You can always trim more or visit a professional for a fix.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your own hair, it’s important to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that’s suitable for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often.

Trimming your own hair can be a fun and empowering experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful, layered look without ever leaving your home.
