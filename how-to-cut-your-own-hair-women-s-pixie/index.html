How To Cut Your Own Hair Women s Pixie A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Aug 19, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're looking to save some money on salon visits or simply want to try a new look, cutting your own hair can be a great option. With the right tools and techniques, you can easily achieve a stylish and flattering pixie cut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll take you through the steps of how to cut your own hair women's pixie, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Handheld mirror or two mirrors
  • Electric clippers (optional)

It's important to use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair and creating uneven cuts. If you have longer hair, you may also want to invest in electric clippers to help with the initial trimming.

Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure it's completely dry before you begin cutting to avoid any unexpected shrinkage. It's also a good idea to wear a shirt with a loose neckline that won't interfere with your movements.

Sectioning Your Hair

Begin by sectioning your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips or elastics to keep each section in place.

Start with the back section, using the handheld mirror or two mirrors to see the back of your head. Use the clippers or scissors to trim the hair to your desired length, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up.

Once you've finished the back section, move on to the sides. Take a small section of hair and hold it straight out from your head, then use the scissors to cut it to the desired length. Repeat this process with each section of hair on the sides.

Finally, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair from the front and hold it straight up, then cut it to the desired length. Work your way from front to back, taking small sections and cutting them to the same length. Make sure to blend the top section with the sides and back to create a seamless look.

Cutting Your Hair

When cutting your hair, it's important to take small sections and cut them gradually. This will help you avoid any major mistakes and allow you to make adjustments as you go. Remember, you can always cut more hair, but you can't put it back once it's been cut.

If you're using electric clippers, start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one until you reach your desired length. Use the comb attachment to blend the hair and create a more natural look.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. You may also want to use thinning shears to create texture and volume.

Style your hair as desired using a blow dryer or styling products. A pixie cut can be styled in many different ways, from sleek and smooth to tousled and messy.

Tips for a Successful Pixie Cut

  • Start with longer hair and work your way down to a shorter length to avoid any major mistakes.
  • Take small sections of hair and cut them gradually to achieve your desired length.
  • Blend the top section with the sides and back to create a seamless look.
  • Use thinning shears to create texture and volume.
  • Experiment with different styling products to find what works best for your hair type and desired look.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to manage and may shrink up once it dries.

How often should I trim my pixie cut?

Most pixie cuts require a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape and style.

Can I still style my hair in different ways with a pixie cut?

Absolutely! A pixie cut can be styled in many different ways, from sleek and smooth to tousled and messy. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find what works best for you.

Hair Care and Maintenance

To keep your pixie cut looking its best, it's important to maintain a regular hair care routine. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type, and avoid over-washing to prevent dryness and breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle wet hair, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims will also help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, and with these tips and techniques, you can easily achieve a stylish and flattering pixie cut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, start with small sections, and make adjustments as needed to achieve your desired look.
