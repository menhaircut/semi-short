How To Cancel Brassy Tones In Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Brassy tones in hair can be frustrating, especially if you've just dyed your hair blonde or have highlights. But fear not! There are several ways to cancel out those unwanted warm tones and achieve the perfect shade of blonde. In this guide, we will go over how to cancel brassy tones in hair, step by step, and provide tips for maintaining your newly toned locks.

How to Cancel Brassy Tones in Hair

Before we dive into the steps, let's first understand what causes brassy tones in hair. Brassy tones occur when the cool tones in your hair color start to fade, leaving behind warm, yellow or orange tones. This can happen due to various factors, such as exposure to sun, chlorine, hard water, or even the type of shampoo you use.

Now, let's get into the steps for canceling out those brassy tones:

Step 1: Use a purple shampoo

Purple shampoo is a game-changer when it comes to canceling out brassy tones. The purple pigments in the shampoo neutralize the warm tones, leaving behind a cool-toned blonde. Use it once a week or as needed, but don't overuse it, as it can turn your hair purple.

Step 2: Try a toner

If your hair is severely brassy, a toner may be a better option. Toners are specifically designed to cancel out unwanted tones and can be found in various shades of blonde. Consult with a professional stylist to find the right toner for your hair.

Step 3: Use a clarifying shampoo

Hard water or excessive product build-up can also contribute to brassy tones. Using a clarifying shampoo once a month can help remove any build-up and restore your hair's natural shine.

Step 4: Use a leave-in treatment

Leave-in treatments can help protect your hair from environmental factors that contribute to brassy tones. Look for products with UV protection or those that are specifically designed to cancel out brassy tones.

Tips for Maintaining Your Toned Hair

Now that you've canceled out those brassy tones, it's important to maintain your newly toned hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping the color
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as it can cause damage and fade the color
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in treatment with UV protection
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cancel out brassy tones without using chemicals?

A: Yes, you can use natural remedies such as chamomile tea, apple cider vinegar, or lemon juice to cancel out brassy tones. However, these remedies may not be as effective as chemical products.

Q: How often should I use a purple shampoo?

A: Use a purple shampoo once a week or as needed, but don't overuse it, as it can turn your hair purple.

Q: Can I tone my hair at home?

A: It's possible to tone your hair at home, but it's recommended to consult with a professional stylist to avoid damaging your hair or achieving undesirable results.

Hair Care

Lastly, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and prevent damage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip natural oils and cause dryness
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair, as it can cause damage
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals for healthy hair growth

With these tips and steps, you can cancel out those brassy tones and achieve the perfect shade of blonde. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain its health and beauty.
