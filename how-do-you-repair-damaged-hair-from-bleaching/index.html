How To Repair Damaged Hair From Bleaching A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 24, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’ve ever bleached your hair, you know that the process can leave your locks dry, brittle, and damaged. But don’t fret – there are ways to repair and restore your hair to its former glory. In this post, we’ll walk you through how to repair damaged hair from bleaching, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Repair Damaged Hair from Bleaching
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Repairing Damaged Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Routine to Prevent Future Damage

Description

Bleaching your hair is a chemical process that removes the natural pigment from your strands. While it can give you a beautiful, bright blonde or pastel hair color, it can also cause significant damage. Bleaching can weaken the hair shaft, making it more prone to breakage and split ends. Additionally, it can cause the hair to become dry and brittle, making it difficult to style and manage.

How to Repair Damaged Hair from Bleaching

If you have damaged hair from bleaching, there are a few things you can do to repair and restore it. Here are some steps you can take:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Trim your hair – If your hair is severely damaged, the best thing you can do is to cut off the damaged ends. This will prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft and causing more damage. If you’re not ready for a drastic chop, consider getting a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your ends healthy.
  2. Use a protein treatment – Bleaching can strip the hair of its natural protein, which can lead to breakage and weakened strands. Using a protein treatment can help rebuild the hair’s protein structure, making it stronger and more resilient. Look for products that contain hydrolyzed keratin, which can penetrate the hair shaft and rebuild the hair from the inside out.
  3. Moisturize your hair – Bleaching can also strip the hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Using a moisturizing treatment can help restore the hair’s moisture balance, making it softer and more manageable. Look for products that contain natural oils like coconut oil, argan oil, or jojoba oil.
  4. Avoid heat styling – Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can further damage bleached hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature on low.
  5. Avoid harsh chemicals – Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, like hair dyes and relaxers, as they can further damage your strands. If you must use these products, make sure to use them sparingly and follow the instructions carefully.

Tips for Repairing Damaged Hair

Here are some additional tips for repairing damaged hair:

  • Wash your hair less frequently – Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair no more than two to three times per week.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb – Using a wide-tooth comb can help prevent breakage and damage while detangling your hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles – Tight hairstyles like braids and ponytails can put stress on the hair, causing it to break and become damaged. Try to avoid these styles or wear them loosely.
  • Eat a healthy diet – Eating a diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth. Foods like salmon, avocados, and sweet potatoes are great for promoting hair health.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about repairing damaged hair:

  • Can damaged hair be repaired? Yes, damaged hair can be repaired with the right treatment and care.
  • How long does it take to repair damaged hair? It can take several weeks or months to repair damaged hair, depending on the severity of the damage.
  • Can you fix damaged hair without cutting it? Yes, you can repair damaged hair without cutting it, but trimming off the damaged ends can help prevent further damage.
  • What is the best treatment for damaged hair? The best treatment for damaged hair depends on the type and severity of the damage. Protein treatments and moisturizing treatments are great for repairing bleached hair.

Hair Care Routine to Prevent Future Damage

Once you’ve repaired your damaged hair, it’s essential to take steps to prevent future damage. Here are some tips for a healthy hair care routine:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients
  • Avoid over-processing your hair with chemicals and treatments

Repairing damaged hair from bleaching can take time and effort, but with the right treatment and care, you can restore your hair to its former glory. Follow these steps, tips, and tricks to get your hair looking healthy and beautiful once again!
