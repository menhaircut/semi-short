How To Make Your Hair Slick Back Naturally

Written by Frank Jun 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a sleek and stylish hairstyle that will make you stand out from the crowd, a slicked-back look might be the perfect choice for you. While many people turn to hair products like gels and pomades to achieve this look, it's actually possible to slick back your hair naturally, without any chemicals or artificial ingredients. In this article, we'll explore how to make your hair slick back naturally, step by step.

How to Slick Back Your Hair Naturally

Here's a step-by-step guide to achieving a natural slicked-back hairstyle:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step in creating any hairstyle is to start with clean hair. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt, oil, and product buildup. This will make it easier to style your hair and keep it looking fresh throughout the day.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

After washing your hair, gently towel dry it to remove excess moisture. Avoid using a blow dryer, as this can damage your hair and make it harder to style.

Step 3: Apply a Natural Styling Product

While you don't need to use a traditional hair gel or pomade to slick back your hair, you may want to use a natural styling product to help hold your hair in place. Try using a small amount of coconut oil, aloe vera gel, or flaxseed gel to help tame flyaways and create a sleek, shiny look.

Step 4: Comb Your Hair Back

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently comb your hair back from your forehead. Start at the front of your hairline and work your way back, making sure to smooth down any flyaways or bumps along the way.

Step 5: Pin Your Hair in Place

If you have particularly unruly hair, you may want to use bobby pins or hair clips to help hold your hair in place. Simply slide the pins or clips into your hair at the sides and back of your head, making sure to hide them from view.

Tips for Slicking Back Your Hair Naturally

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve the perfect slicked-back hairstyle:

Tip 1: Use Natural Hair Products

When possible, try to use natural hair products to avoid exposing your hair and scalp to harsh chemicals and artificial ingredients. Look for products that are free of sulfates, parabens, and synthetic fragrances.

Tip 2: Don't Overdo It

While a slicked-back hairstyle can be impressive, it's important not to overdo it. Avoid using too much product or pulling your hair back too tightly, as this can lead to breakage and discomfort.

Tip 3: Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different slicked-back styles to find the one that works best for you. Try slicking your hair straight back, to one side, or even in a low ponytail.

FAQs

Is it possible to slick back curly hair naturally?

Yes, it's possible to slick back curly hair naturally. You may need to use a bit more product to help hold your curls in place, but the same basic steps apply.

Can I slick back my hair if it's short?

Yes, you can slick back short hair. Just use a small amount of product and a fine-tooth comb to help shape your hair into the desired style.

Hair Care Tips for Slicked-Back Hairstyles

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep in mind when sporting a slicked-back hairstyle:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair Regularly

Regularly washing your hair will help keep it clean and healthy, and prevent any product buildup that can lead to breakage and damage.

Tip 2: Use a Gentle Shampoo and Conditioner

When washing your hair, use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type.

Tip 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and hair dryers, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to style. Instead, let your hair air dry naturally whenever possible.

By following these steps and tips, you can easily achieve a sleek and stylish slicked-back hairstyle without any harsh chemicals or artificial ingredients. So why not give it a try and see how it works for you?
