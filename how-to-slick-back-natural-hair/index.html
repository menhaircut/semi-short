How To Slick Back Natural Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Slicking back natural hair can be a challenge, but with the right techniques and products, it's definitely doable. Whether you're looking to achieve a sleek, professional look for work or a glamorous style for a night out, slicked back hair can be a versatile option for any occasion. In this blog post, we'll be sharing our top tips and tricks on how to slick back natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • What You'll Need
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Slicking Back Natural Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

What You'll Need

Before you start slicking back your natural hair, make sure you have the right tools and products. Here's a list of what you'll need:

  • Wide tooth comb
  • Boar bristle brush
  • Gel or pomade
  • Hair ties or bobby pins
  • Hair scarf or wrap

Step by Step Guide

Now that you have all the necessary items, let's get started on how to slick back natural hair:

Step 1: Cleanse Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup from your hair and ensure that it's clean and ready for styling.

Step 2: Apply Gel or Pomade

Take a small amount of gel or pomade and apply it evenly throughout your hair. Make sure you distribute the product from root to tip, focusing on the areas that tend to frizz the most.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Using a wide tooth comb, gently comb your hair back in the direction of your desired style. Make sure to smooth out any bumps or flyaways and create a sleek, neat look.

Step 4: Brush Your Hair

Next, use a boar bristle brush to further smooth out your hair and create a polished finish. Brush your hair back in the same direction as before, focusing on the crown of your head and the sides.

Step 5: Tie or Pin Your Hair

Once you've achieved your desired look, use hair ties or bobby pins to secure your hair in place. You can either tie your hair back in a ponytail or bun, or pin it back in sections for a more intricate style.

Step 6: Wrap Your Hair

Finally, wrap your hair with a scarf or wrap to set the style in place and prevent any frizz or flyaways. Leave the wrap on for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a more defined look.

Tips for Slicking Back Natural Hair

Slicking back natural hair can be a bit tricky, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve a flawless look:

  • Use a small amount of product at a time. Too much gel or pomade can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles. Slicked back hair can be worn in a variety of ways, from a sleek ponytail to a messy bun.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf to wrap your hair. These materials are gentle on your hair and won't cause any breakage or damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can cause damage to your hair and disrupt its natural texture.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about slicking back natural hair:

  • Can I slick back my hair if it's short or curly?

    • Absolutely! Slicking back hair works for all hair types and lengths. If you have shorter or curlier hair, you may need to use more product and spend more time smoothing out your hair.

  • How often should I slick back my hair?

    • It's best to avoid slicking back your hair every day, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Instead, try to limit it to once or twice a week.

  • Do I need to use a specific type of gel or pomade?

    • Not necessarily. You can use any type of styling product that works well for your hair type and texture. Just make sure to use a small amount at a time and apply it evenly throughout your hair.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and nourished.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

Slicking back natural hair can be a fun and stylish way to switch up your look. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve a sleek and polished style in no time.
