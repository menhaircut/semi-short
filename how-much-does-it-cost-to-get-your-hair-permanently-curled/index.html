Are you tired of straight hair and considering getting your hair permanently curled? Before you take the plunge, it's important to know how much it will cost. In this article, we will break down the costs associated with getting your hair permanently curled, as well as provide tips on how to care for your new curls.

How to Get Your Hair Permanently Curled

Permanent hair curling is a chemical process that changes the structure of your hair. It's important to note that this process is permanent, meaning that the curls will remain until your hair grows out. If you're interested in getting your hair permanently curled, the first step is to find a reputable salon that specializes in this service.

Once you find a salon, you'll need to make an appointment for a consultation. During the consultation, the stylist will assess your hair type and determine the best type of perm for your hair. There are several types of perms available, including:

  • Alkaline perm
  • Acid perm
  • Exothermic perm
  • Spiral perm

The stylist will also discuss the process with you and answer any questions you may have. If you decide to move forward, you'll need to schedule an appointment for the perm.

Cost of Permanent Hair Curling

The cost of permanent hair curling varies depending on several factors, including the salon you choose, your location, and the length of your hair. On average, you can expect to pay anywhere from $100 to $300 for a permanent hair curling service.

It's important to note that this cost does not include the cost of a consultation or any additional hair treatments that may be required before or after the perm.

Step-by-Step Guide to Permanent Hair Curling

Here is a general step-by-step guide to the permanent hair curling process:

  1. The stylist will start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.
  2. Your hair will then be wrapped around perm rods in the desired curl pattern.
  3. The stylist will apply a perm solution to your hair, which will break down the bonds in your hair to create the curls.
  4. Your hair will be covered with a plastic cap to help the solution penetrate your hair.
  5. After a set amount of time, the stylist will rinse your hair with warm water to remove the perm solution.
  6. A neutralizer will then be applied to your hair to stop the chemical process.
  7. The perm rods will be removed, and your hair will be rinsed again.
  8. The stylist will then apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to help restore moisture.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Curls

Here are some tips for maintaining your new curls:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after the perm to allow the curls to set.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your new curls.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to help maintain your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help enhance your curls.

FAQs About Permanent Hair Curling

Here are some frequently asked questions about permanent hair curling:

Q: Is permanent hair curling safe?

A: Yes, permanent hair curling is safe when done by a professional stylist. However, it's important to note that the chemicals used in the process can be harsh, so it's important to follow the stylist's instructions for aftercare.

Q: How long does a perm last?

A: A perm typically lasts 3-6 months, depending on the rate of your hair growth.

Q: Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

A: It's best to wait at least two weeks before coloring your hair after getting a perm. This will give your hair time to recover from the chemical process.

Hair Care After Permanent Hair Curling

It's important to take extra care of your hair after getting a permanent hair curl. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dyes or bleach.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your curls moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as they can damage your curls.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it's wet.

Now that you know how much it costs to get your hair permanently curled, as well as how to care for your new curls, you can make an informed decision about whether this service is right for you. Remember to choose a reputable salon and follow your stylist's instructions for aftercare to ensure the best possible results.
