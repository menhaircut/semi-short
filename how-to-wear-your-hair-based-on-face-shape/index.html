Whether you have a round, square, oval, or heart-shaped face, finding the perfect hairstyle can be a challenge. With so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. In this guide, we'll break down the best hairstyles for each face shape, including how to determine your face shape and tips on how to care for your hair.

Determining Your Face Shape

Before you can choose a flattering hairstyle, you need to determine your face shape. Here's how:

  1. Stand in front of a mirror with your hair pulled back.
  2. Use a ruler or measuring tape to measure the following:
    • Forehead: Measure from the widest point of your hairline to the widest point of your eyebrows.
    • Cheeks: Measure across your cheekbones, starting and ending at the pointiest part.
    • Jawline: Measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your earlobe where your jawline begins to angle up.
    • Face length: Measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin.
  3. Use these measurements to determine your face shape:
    • Round: Forehead, cheek, and jawline measurements are similar in length. Face length measurement is shorter than the other measurements.
    • Square: Forehead, cheek, and jawline measurements are similar in length. Face length measurement is about the same as the other measurements.
    • Oval: Face length measurement is longer than the other measurements. Forehead and jawline measurements are similar in length. Cheek measurement is the widest part of the face.
    • Heart-shaped: Forehead measurement is wider than the cheek and jawline measurements. Cheek measurement is the widest part of the face. Jawline is narrow.

Hairstyles for Round Faces

If you have a round face, you want to elongate your face and create the illusion of angles. Here are some hairstyles to try:

  • Long layers: This style adds movement and dimension to your hair, making your face appear longer.
  • Side-swept bangs: A deep side part and long, sweeping bangs create angles and draw attention away from the roundness of your face.
  • Messy updo: A textured updo with loose pieces around your face can add height and elongate your face.

Hairstyles for Square Faces

If you have a square face, you want to soften your strong jawline and create the illusion of length. Here are some hairstyles to try:

  • Soft layers: Layers that start at your jawline and frame your face can soften your features.
  • Side-parted bob: A bob that's shorter in the back and longer in the front, with a deep side part, can elongate your face.
  • Loose curls: Curls that are loose and tousled can add volume and soften your jawline.

Hairstyles for Oval Faces

If you have an oval face, you're luckyâyou can pull off almost any hairstyle! Here are some ideas:

  • Long layers: Long layers can add movement and texture to your hair.
  • Bangs: Straight, blunt bangs or side-swept bangs can add interest to your face.
  • Pixie cut: A short, cropped cut can highlight your features and show off your face shape.

Hairstyles for Heart-Shaped Faces

If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to balance your wide forehead with your narrow chin. Here are some hairstyles to try:

  • Side-swept bangs: Long, sweeping bangs that start at the side of your forehead can draw attention away from your wide forehead.
  • Bob with volume: A bob that's shorter in the back and longer in the front, with volume at the crown, can balance your features.
  • Low ponytail: A low ponytail can draw attention to your narrow chin and show off your cheekbones.

Hair Care Tips

No matter what your face shape is, healthy hair is always in style. Here are some tips for keeping your hair looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before styling.
  • Get regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Don't over-wash your hairâjust wash it when it needs it.

FAQs

Q: Can I still wear a style that's not recommended for my face shape?

Q: What if I have a combination of face shapes?

A: Many people have a mix of face shapes, so don't worry too much about fitting into one specific category. Focus on finding styles that flatter your features and make you feel good.

Q: How do I know if a certain hairstyle will work for me?

A: Try it out! Experimenting with different styles is a great way to find what works best for you. Don't be afraid to ask your stylist for advice, too.


By following these tips, you'll be able to find a hairstyle that flatters your face shape and showcases your unique beauty. Remember, the most important thing is that you feel confident and comfortable in your own skin!
