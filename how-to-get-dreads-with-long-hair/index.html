How to Get Dreads with Long Hair: A Step by Step Guide

Dreadlocks have been around for thousands of years and are a popular hairstyle for many people today. If you have long hair and are interested in getting dreads, there are a few things you need to know before you begin. In this article, we will provide you with a step by step guide on how to get dreads with long hair, including tips on hair care and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Dreads with Long Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Dreads
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Dreads

How to Get Dreads with Long Hair

Before you begin the process of getting dreads, there are a few things you should consider. Firstly, it's important to have healthy hair, as dreadlocks require a lot of maintenance and can cause damage if your hair is already damaged. Secondly, it's important to have patience, as the process of getting dreads can take several months to a year depending on your hair type and the method you choose.

Choose Your Method

There are several methods to choose from when getting dreads. Some of the most popular methods include backcombing, twist and rip, and the neglect method. Each method has its own pros and cons, so it's important to do your research and choose the method that is best for you.

Choose Your Size

The size of your dreads will depend on your personal preference and the method you choose. Some people prefer thicker dreads, while others prefer thinner dreads. It's important to choose a size that you feel comfortable with, as you will be wearing them for several months to a year.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you begin the process of getting dreads, it's important to wash your hair thoroughly. This will help to remove any dirt or product buildup, which can interfere with the process of creating dreads.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, you will need to section your hair into small sections. This will make it easier to create each dread individually. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separated.

Step 3: Begin the Process

Now it's time to begin the process of creating your dreads. Depending on the method you choose, you will need to backcomb, twist and rip, or simply let your hair grow out and separate naturally.

Step 4: Maintenance

Once your dreads are created, it's important to maintain them properly. This includes washing them regularly, using a residue-free shampoo, and palm rolling them to keep them tight.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Dreads

Tip 1: Use a Residue-Free Shampoo

Using a residue-free shampoo is important for maintaining healthy dreads. This will help to prevent buildup, which can cause your dreads to become heavy and uncomfortable.

Tip 2: Palm Roll Your Dreads

Palm rolling your dreads regularly will help to keep them tight and prevent them from becoming too loose. This can also help to prevent breakage and damage.

Tip 3: Be Gentle

When washing and styling your dreads, it's important to be gentle. Avoid pulling or twisting your dreads too hard, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to get dreads with long hair?

A: The process of getting dreads can take several months to a year, depending on your hair type and the method you choose.

Q: Can I get dreads with naturally straight hair?

A: Yes, you can get dreads with naturally straight hair. However, it may take longer and require more maintenance than other hair types.

Q: Can I still wash my hair with dreads?

A: Yes, you can still wash your hair with dreads. In fact, it's important to wash your dreads regularly to keep them healthy and clean.

Hair Care for Dreads

Tip 1: Use a Silk Pillowcase

Using a silk pillowcase can help to prevent your dreads from becoming tangled or frizzy while you sleep.

Tip 2: Avoid Using Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy products in your hair, as this can cause buildup and make your dreads heavy and uncomfortable.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to prevent breakage and damage, and keep your dreads looking neat and tidy.

By following these tips and step by step guide, you can create beautiful and healthy dreadlocks with your long hair. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair throughout the process, and you'll have gorgeous dreads in no time!
