How To Cut Thick Hair Shoulder Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting thick hair can be a challenge, especially if you're looking to achieve a shoulder-length style. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a stunning haircut that suits your features and personality. In this blog post, we'll guide you through the process of cutting thick hair shoulder length, from preparing your hair to styling it after the cut. We'll also provide tips on how to maintain your hair's health and beauty.

Description

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it provides volume and texture, it can also be heavy and difficult to manage. If you're looking to lighten up your hair and achieve a shoulder-length style, you'll need to choose the right cut and technique. Shoulder-length hair is versatile and flattering, and it can be styled in various ways, from loose waves to sleek updos.

How to Cut Thick Hair Shoulder Length

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to prepare it properly. Start by washing your hair with a nourishing shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type. Towel-dry your hair and comb it gently to remove any tangles. Part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference. Divide your hair into two equal sections, one on each side of your head, and clip them to keep them separate.

Step 2: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair from the back. Take a section of hair from the nape of your neck and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it straight across, starting from the desired length and working your way up. Repeat this process with the rest of your hair, working your way up to the crown of your head. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in uneven lengths.

Step 3: Trim the Sides

Next, trim the sides of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the front of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the section between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting from the desired length and working your way down. Repeat this process with the rest of your hair, working your way towards the back. Be sure to cut the hair at an angle to create layers and texture.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections of your hair, it's time to blend the layers. Take a comb and run it through your hair, starting from the top and working your way down. Use scissors to trim any uneven lengths and create a seamless look. Be sure to blend the layers at the back and sides of your head, as well as the top.

Step 5: Check for Any Uneven Lengths

After you've finished cutting your hair, check for any uneven lengths or choppy sections. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a polished look. Be sure to check the back and sides of your head in a mirror to ensure that your hair is even.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting thick hair shoulder length:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be deceiving in length
  • Take breaks if you're feeling fatigued or frustrated
  • Ask a friend or family member for help if you're unsure about cutting your hair
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're not confident in your skills

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my thick hair shoulder length using a razor?

A: While a razor can create texture and movement in thick hair, it's not recommended for cutting hair shoulder length. A razor can create choppy ends and uneven lengths, which can be difficult to fix.

Q: How often should I trim my shoulder-length hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health. Regular trims can prevent split ends and breakage, and keep your hair looking fresh and vibrant.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair serum to protect your hair from environmental stressors
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength
  • Get enough sleep and exercise regularly to maintain overall health and wellbeing

Cutting thick hair shoulder length can be a fun and rewarding experience. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a stunning haircut that flatters your features and personality. Remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it healthy and beautiful. Happy cutting!
