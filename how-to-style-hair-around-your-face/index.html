How To Style Hair Around Your Face Tips And Tricks

When it comes to styling hair, the way you frame your face can make all the difference. Whether you have long hair, short hair, curly hair, or straight hair, there are many ways to style your hair around your face. In this article, we'll be sharing some tips and tricks on how to style hair around your face to flatter your features and enhance your overall look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Hair Around Your Face
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Style Hair Around Your Face

Before we dive into the specifics of how to style hair around your face, it's important to understand the different face shapes and which hairstyles are most flattering for each one. Here are the most common face shapes:

  • Round
  • Oval
  • Square
  • Heart
  • Diamond

To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and examine your face in the mirror. Once you know your face shape, you can choose a hairstyle that complements your features.

Round Face

If you have a round face, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that elongates your face and creates the illusion of length. Some great options include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Angled bob

Oval Face

If you have an oval face, you're in luck! This face shape is considered the most versatile and can pull off a variety of hairstyles. Some great options include:

  • Short pixie cut
  • Messy bun
  • Long beach waves

Square Face

If you have a square face, you'll want to soften your features with a hairstyle that has lots of texture and movement. Some great options include:

  • Soft layers
  • Curly bob
  • Side-swept bangs

Heart Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, you'll want to balance your features with a hairstyle that has volume around the jawline. Some great options include:

  • Long layers
  • Side-swept bangs
  • Half-up half-down style

Diamond Face

If you have a diamond-shaped face, you'll want to create width at the forehead and chin to balance out your features. Some great options include:

  • Side-swept bangs
  • Voluminous curls
  • Half-up half-down style

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know which hairstyles are most flattering for your face shape, here's a step-by-step guide on how to style hair around your face:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Choose a hairstyle that complements your face shape
  3. Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools
  4. Section your hair and start styling from the bottom up
  5. Use a small amount of hair product to add texture and hold
  6. Finish with hairspray to lock in your style

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to help you style hair around your face:

  • Invest in quality hair tools to prevent damage and achieve a polished look
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find what works best for you
  • Don't be afraid to try new hair products to achieve the perfect look
  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup and keep your hair healthy
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair looking its best

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people wash their hair every day, while others only wash it once a week. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it more frequently.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools on wet hair?

A: No, it's not recommended to use heat styling tools on wet hair. Wet hair is more fragile and prone to damage. Always use heat styling tools on dry hair and use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Q: What's the best way to prevent frizz?

A: The best way to prevent frizz is to keep your hair moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture to your hair. You can also use a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry your hair instead of a regular towel, which can cause frizz.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some additional hair care tips to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent damage
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and overall health
  • Protect your hair from the sun with a hat or scarf
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to style hair around your face in a way that flatters your features and enhances your overall look. Remember to choose a hairstyle that complements your face shape, use quality hair tools and products, and take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.
