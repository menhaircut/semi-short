How To Curl Very Fine Thin Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Daniel Jul 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have very fine and thin hair, you may think that curling it is impossible or that it won't hold. However, with the right techniques and tools, you can create beautiful curls that will last all day. In this article, we'll show you how to curl very fine thin hair step by step, as well as provide tips and answer some common questions.

How to Curl Very Fine Thin Hair

Before we start, it's important to note that you should always use heat protectant spray before curling your hair to prevent damage. Here's how to curl very fine thin hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is too oily, the curls may not hold.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. The smaller the sections, the tighter the curls will be.
  3. Take a small section of hair and spray it with heat protectant spray.
  4. Wrap the hair around the curling iron, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Hold for 5-10 seconds and release.
  5. Repeat with all sections of hair.
  6. Once you've curled all sections, flip your head upside down and shake out the curls to create volume.
  7. Finish with hairspray to hold the curls in place.

Remember to use a curling iron with a smaller barrel size, as larger barrels may not create tight enough curls for very fine thin hair.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step by step guide on how to curl very fine thin hair:

  1. Wash and blow dry your hair. If your hair is too oily, the curls may not hold. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use hair clips to keep the sections separate. The smaller the sections, the tighter the curls will be.
  3. Spray each section with heat protectant spray. Make sure to cover the entire section of hair.
  4. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron. Start at the roots and work your way down to the ends. Hold for 5-10 seconds and release.
  5. Repeat with all sections of hair.
  6. Once you've curled all sections, flip your head upside down and shake out the curls to create volume.
  7. Finish with hairspray to hold the curls in place. Don't use too much hairspray, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Tips for Curling Very Fine Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you curl very fine thin hair:

  • Use a curling iron with a smaller barrel size, such as 1 inch or less.
  • Don't curl your hair too tightly, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Use a lower heat setting on your curling iron to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Don't use too much product on your hair before curling, as this can weigh down your hair and make it harder to curl.
  • If you have trouble keeping your curls in place, spray your hair with hairspray before curling.

FAQs

How long will the curls last?

The length of time your curls will last depends on your hair type and the products you use. Generally, curls should last at least 8 hours, but they may fall out sooner if you have very fine thin hair.

What type of curling iron should I use?

You should use a curling iron with a smaller barrel size, such as 1 inch or less. This will create tighter curls that will hold better in very fine thin hair.

What should I do if my curls won't hold?

If your curls won't hold, try using a higher heat setting on your curling iron or spraying your hair with hairspray before curling. You can also try using smaller sections of hair or curling your hair in different directions.

Hair Care for Fine Thin Hair

If you have very fine thin hair, it's important to take care of it to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair. Avoid using heavy conditioners that can weigh down your hair.
  • Don't brush your hair too often, as this can cause breakage. Use a wide-tooth comb instead.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

With these tips and tricks, you can create beautiful curls that will last all day in very fine thin hair. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage, and enjoy your new curls!
