What To Use On Scalp During Chemo A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Nov 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Dealing with cancer and undergoing chemotherapy can be incredibly tough on the body, and one of the side effects that many patients experience is hair loss. While hair loss can be difficult to deal with emotionally, it can also be uncomfortable physically. Without hair to protect the scalp, it can become dry, itchy, and even painful. That's why it's essential to know what to use on your scalp during chemo. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know, including tips, FAQs, and more.

What to Expect During Chemo

Chemotherapy is a treatment designed to kill cancer cells. Unfortunately, it also kills healthy cells in the body, including those responsible for hair growth. Hair loss typically begins within a few weeks of starting chemotherapy, and it can occur all over the body, including the scalp, eyebrows, and eyelashes. The severity of hair loss can vary from person to person, but it's important to remember that it's a temporary side effect.

How to Care for Your Scalp During Chemo

Once you start experiencing hair loss, it's essential to take care of your scalp to prevent discomfort and irritation. Here are some tips for caring for your scalp during chemo:

1. Keep Your Scalp Clean and Moisturized

Wash your scalp regularly to keep it clean and free of bacteria. Use a gentle, unscented shampoo and conditioner, and avoid any harsh chemicals that could further irritate your scalp. Also, use a moisturizer to keep your scalp hydrated and prevent dryness and itching.

2. Use a Satin or Silk Pillowcase

Avoid using a cotton pillowcase, as it can cause friction on your scalp and further irritate it. Instead, use a satin or silk pillowcase, which is gentler on your skin.

3. Wear a Soft Hat or Scarf

Wearing a soft hat or scarf can protect your scalp from the sun and keep it warm. Choose a hat or scarf made from soft, breathable fabric to avoid further irritation.

4. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using heat styling tools like hair dryers, curling irons, and straighteners, as they can further damage your hair and scalp. Instead, opt for gentle styling techniques like air-drying your hair or using a wide-tooth comb.

What to Use on Your Scalp During Chemo

There are several products that you can use on your scalp during chemo to soothe and moisturize it. Here are some of the best options:

1. Aloe Vera Gel

Aloe vera gel is a natural moisturizer that can soothe dry, irritated skin. Apply a small amount of aloe vera gel to your scalp to hydrate and calm it.

2. Jojoba Oil

Jojoba oil is a lightweight, non-greasy oil that can help moisturize your scalp. Apply a few drops of jojoba oil to your scalp and massage it in gently.

3. Calendula Cream

Calendula cream is a natural cream that can help soothe dry, itchy skin. Apply a small amount of calendula cream to your scalp to moisturize and calm it.

Tips for Dealing with Hair Loss

Hair loss can be difficult to deal with emotionally, but there are several things you can do to make the process a little easier:

1. Get a Haircut Before Your Hair Starts Falling Out

Getting a shorter haircut before your hair starts falling out can make the transition easier. It can also make it easier to manage any hair that does fall out.

2. Try a Wig or Hairpiece

If you're uncomfortable with your hair loss, consider trying a wig or hairpiece. There are many different options available, so you're sure to find something that suits your style.

3. Embrace Your Baldness

Remember that hair loss is a temporary side effect of chemotherapy. Try to embrace your baldness and see it as a symbol of your strength and courage.

FAQs

Q: Is it safe to shave my head during chemo?

A: Yes, it's safe to shave your head during chemo. However, you should use an electric razor rather than a traditional razor to avoid any nicks or cuts.

Q: Will my hair grow back after chemo?

A: Yes, your hair will grow back after chemo. The rate of regrowth can vary, but most people will start to see new hair growth within a few months of finishing treatment.

Q: Can I dye my hair during chemo?

A: It's generally not recommended to dye your hair during chemo, as the chemicals can further irritate your scalp. However, you can consider using a temporary hair color product to add some color to your hair if you'd like.

Conclusion

Dealing with hair loss during chemo can be challenging, but taking care of your scalp and using the right products can help make the process more comfortable. Remember to keep your scalp clean and moisturized, and consider using products like aloe vera gel, jojoba oil, or calendula cream to soothe and hydrate your skin. And don't be afraid to experiment with different hairstyles or hairpieces to find something that makes you feel confident and comfortable.
