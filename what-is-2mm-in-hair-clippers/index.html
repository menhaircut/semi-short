What Is 2Mm In Hair Clippers

Written by Daniel Oct 11, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re thinking about cutting your hair at home, you’ll need the right hair clipper. But with so many different sizes and settings, it can be hard to know what you need. One important measurement to know is 2mm. In this article, we’ll explain what 2mm means in hair clippers and how you can use it to get the perfect haircut.

Description

2mm is a measurement of the length of hair that will be left after you use your hair clippers. In other words, it’s the length of the guard or attachment that you’ll need to use to achieve a 2mm haircut. This is a very short haircut, and it’s often used for buzz cuts, crew cuts, and other short hairstyles.

How to

To achieve a 2mm haircut, you’ll need to use a hair clipper with a 2mm guard or attachment. Here are the steps you can follow:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Attach the 2mm guard or attachment to your hair clipper.
  3. Turn on your hair clipper and start at the back of your head.
  4. Move the clipper up towards the crown of your head, cutting against the direction of hair growth.
  5. Continue moving the clipper over your head, working from the back towards the front.
  6. Use a mirror to check for any missed spots or uneven areas.
  7. Trim your sideburns and neckline with a shorter guard or attachment.
  8. Clean your hair clipper and attachments after use.

Step by Step

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting a 2mm haircut:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is curly or wavy, you may want to straighten it first to make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Attach the 2mm Guard

Attach the 2mm guard or attachment to your hair clipper. Make sure it clicks into place securely.

Step 3: Turn on Your Hair Clipper

Turn on your hair clipper and start at the back of your head. Hold the clipper with the blade facing downwards and the teeth of the guard touching your scalp.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Move the clipper up towards the crown of your head, cutting against the direction of hair growth. Keep the clipper flat against your head to avoid cutting too short.

Step 5: Continue Cutting

Continue moving the clipper over your head, working from the back towards the front. Use a mirror to check for any missed spots or uneven areas. If you’re not sure how short you want to go, start with a longer guard and work your way down.

Step 6: Trim Your Sideburns and Neckline

Use a shorter guard or attachment to trim your sideburns and neckline. Use a comb or your fingers to guide the clipper and create a natural-looking line.

Step 7: Clean Your Hair Clipper

Clean your hair clipper and attachments after use. Use a small brush or cloth to remove any hair or debris, and oil the blades to keep them lubricated.

Tips

Here are some tips to get the best results when cutting a 2mm haircut:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you’re not sure how short you want to go.
  • Use a mirror to check for any missed spots or uneven areas.
  • Trim your hair when it’s dry to avoid cutting too much off.
  • Clean your hair clipper and attachments after use to keep them in good condition.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about 2mm hair clippers:

What other haircuts can I achieve with a 2mm guard?

A 2mm guard can be used to achieve a variety of short haircuts, including buzz cuts, crew cuts, and military haircuts.

Can I use a 2mm guard on wet hair?

It’s best to use a 2mm guard on dry hair to avoid cutting too much off. Wet hair can also be harder to cut evenly.

How often should I replace my hair clipper blades?

You should replace your hair clipper blades every 6 to 12 months, depending on how often you use them and how well you care for them.

Hair Care

Cutting your hair at home can be a great way to save money and try out new styles. But it’s important to take care of your hair and scalp to keep them healthy. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Condition your hair to keep it hydrated and soft.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind with a hat or scarf.
  • Eat a healthy diet to promote hair growth and strength.

By following these tips and using the right hair clippers and guards, you can achieve a great-looking 2mm haircut at home. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and practice good hair care habits.
