How To Curl Your Hair 70 S Style A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jul 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to take a trip down memory lane and rock some vintage curls? The 70s were all about big, bouncy curls that were full of life and movement. In this article, we'll show you how to curl your hair 70's style, complete with tips and tricks for achieving the perfect look. So grab your curling iron and let's get started!

Description

The 70's were a decade of self-expression and bold fashion statements. One of the most iconic hairstyles of the time was voluminous, curly hair. This style was achieved using a variety of techniques and tools, from hot rollers to curling irons. With a little bit of practice and some patience, you can recreate this retro look in no time.

How to Curl Your Hair 70's Style

Before we get started, it's important to note that the key to achieving 70's style curls is volume. The bigger, the better. So, if you have fine or thin hair, you may want to consider using a volumizing product before you begin. Here's how to curl your hair 70's style:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Towel dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from hot tools. If you have fine or thin hair, apply a volumizing mousse or spray to the roots of your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Use hair clips to keep each section in place as you work.

Step 3: Heat Up Your Curling Iron

Choose a curling iron with a barrel size that matches the size of the curls you want. Heat up the curling iron and let it sit for a few minutes to ensure it's hot enough to create the curls.

Step 4: Curl Your Hair

Starting with the bottom section of your hair, take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron barrel. Hold the curling iron in place for 10-15 seconds, then release the curl. Repeat this process for each section of hair, working your way up to the top of your head.

Step 5: Shake It Out

Once you've curled all of your hair, use your fingers to gently shake out the curls. This will help to create more volume and a more natural-looking curl.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect 70's Style Curls

Here are some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect 70's style curls:

  • Don't brush your curls out too much, as this can cause them to lose their shape and volume.
  • If you want tighter curls, use a smaller curling iron barrel.
  • For looser curls, use a larger barrel size.
  • Use a hair spray or finishing spray to help your curls hold their shape.
  • Don't forget to use a heat protectant spray before using any hot tools on your hair.

FAQ

What kind of curling iron should I use?

It's best to use a curling iron with a ceramic or tourmaline barrel, as these materials help to distribute heat evenly and prevent damage to your hair.

How long should I hold the curling iron in place?

Hold the curling iron in place for 10-15 seconds, depending on the thickness and texture of your hair.

Can I achieve 70's style curls with short hair?

Absolutely! You may need to use a smaller barrel size and focus on creating curls that are closer to your scalp, but you can still achieve the 70's style look with short hair.

Hair Care

To keep your 70's style curls looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips for keeping your curls healthy and strong:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and prevent damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking healthy.

Now that you know how to curl your hair 70's style, it's time to rock that retro look! With a little bit of practice and some patience, you can recreate this iconic hairstyle and channel your inner disco diva. So grab your curling iron and get ready to boogie!
