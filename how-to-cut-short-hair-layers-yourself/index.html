How To Cut Short Hair Layers Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Nov 21, 2023 · 5 min read

If you have short hair and are looking to switch up your look, adding layers can be a great way to do so. However, going to a salon to get your hair cut can be expensive and time-consuming. In this article, we will show you how to cut short hair layers yourself in a relaxed English language. We’ll cover everything from preparation to aftercare. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  2. Sectioning Your Hair
  3. Cutting the First Layer
  4. Cutting the Second Layer

  8. FAQs

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have a pair of sharp scissors, hair clips, and a comb. You should also have a clear idea of the type of layer you want, whether it’s a long or short layer. It’s also essential to have a mirror that allows you to see the back of your head.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly. It’s important to cut your hair when it’s dry to ensure that you get the right length.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. This will make it easier to section your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into two parts, using a comb to create a parting in the middle of your head. Clip one side of your hair to keep it out of the way while you work on the other side.

Sectioning Your Hair

Now that you’ve prepared your hair, it’s time to start sectioning it.

Step 1: Start at the Front

Start at the front of your hairline and section off a small piece of hair. This will be the first layer.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Determine the length of the layer you want to cut. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the hair at the desired length.

Step 3: Create Guide Sections

Create guide sections by cutting small pieces of hair from the first layer. These pieces will serve as a guide for the next layers, ensuring that they are cut evenly.

Cutting the First Layer

Now that you’ve sectioned your hair, it’s time to start cutting the layers.

Step 1: Cut the First Layer

Take the first section of hair that you cut and use it as a guide to cut the rest of the first layer. Cut the hair at the same length as the guide section.

Step 2: Cut the Remaining Sections

Continue cutting the remaining sections of hair in the first layer, using the first section as a guide.

Cutting the Second Layer

Now that you’ve finished cutting the first layer, it’s time to move on to the second layer.

Step 1: Section Off the Second Layer

Section off the second layer by taking a small section of hair from the middle of your head.

Step 2: Determine the Length

Determine the length of the second layer by using the first layer as a guide. Hold the section of hair between your fingers and cut it at the desired length.

Step 3: Create Guide Sections

Create guide sections by cutting small pieces of hair from the second layer. These pieces will serve as a guide for the next layers, ensuring that they are cut evenly.

Blending the Layers Together

Now that you’ve cut the first and second layers, it’s time to blend them together.

Step 1: Comb Through Your Hair

Comb through your hair to remove any tangles or knots.

Step 2: Cut the Blending Sections

Cut the blending sections by taking small sections of hair from both layers and cutting them to blend the two layers together.

Trimming the Ends

Now that you’ve cut the layers and blended them together, it’s time to trim the ends.

Step 1: Trim the Ends

Trim the ends of your hair to ensure that they are even.

Tips for Cutting Short Hair Layers Yourself

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut them gradually to avoid cutting off too much hair.
  • When cutting the layers, remember that the hair at the back of your head should be shorter than the hair at the front.
  • Use hair clips to keep the sections of hair you’re not working on out of the way.

FAQs

Can I cut short hair layers myself?

Yes, you can cut short hair layers yourself. However, it’s important to have the right tools and to follow the steps carefully.

What kind of scissors should I use?

Use sharp scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair.

Do I need to wash my hair before cutting it?

Yes, it’s important to wash and dry your hair before cutting it.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Hair Layers Yourself

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to ensure that it stays healthy and looks great.

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.

Cutting short hair layers yourself can be a great way to switch up your look without breaking the bank. By following these steps and taking care of your hair after cutting it, you can achieve a great new hairstyle that you’ll love.
