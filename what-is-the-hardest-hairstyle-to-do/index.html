What Is The Hardest Hairstyle To Do

Written by Robby Oct 15, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking for a hairstyle that will challenge your styling skills? Whether you're a professional stylist or just want to try something new, there are some hairstyles that are notoriously difficult to achieve. In this article, we'll explore the hardest hairstyle to do and provide tips and tricks to help you master it.

Description: What Makes a Hairstyle Difficult?

Before we dive into the hardest hairstyle to do, let's talk about what makes a hairstyle difficult in the first place. Some factors that can make a hairstyle challenging include:

  • Length of hair
  • Texture of hair
  • Complexity of the style
  • Tools and products required
  • Level of skill needed

Keep these factors in mind as we explore the hardest hairstyle to do.

How to: Achieving the Hardest Hairstyle

The hardest hairstyle to do is the beehive. This iconic hairstyle from the 1960s requires a great deal of skill, patience, and a variety of tools and products. Here's how to achieve the beehive:

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Begin by washing your hair and blow-drying it straight. Apply a volumizing mousse to your roots and tease your hair at the crown to create volume.

Step 2: Create the Beehive

Take a section of hair at the crown of your head and tease it until it's big and voluminous. Smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section and secure it with bobby pins. Continue teasing and smoothing sections of hair until you've created a high, rounded beehive shape.

Step 3: Finish the Style

Once you've created the beehive, use a hairspray to hold it in place. Smooth any flyaways or frizz and secure the style with more bobby pins if necessary.

Tips: Mastering the Hardest Hairstyle

The beehive is a challenging hairstyle, but with the right tips and tricks, you can master it. Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect beehive:

  • Use a fine-tooth comb to tease your hair gently - aggressive teasing can lead to breakage and damage
  • Be patient - creating a beehive takes time and practice
  • Invest in good quality bobby pins and hairspray to keep your style in place
  • Experiment with different variations of the beehive, such as a half-up style or a more modern, textured version

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions about the beehive hairstyle:

Q: Can the beehive be done on short hair?

A: The beehive is typically done on medium to long hair, but it can be adapted for shorter hair with some creative styling and the right products.

Q: Is the beehive suitable for all hair types?

A: The beehive can be achieved on most hair types, but it may be more difficult to achieve on very fine or very thick hair.

Hair Care: Maintaining Your Style

Once you've achieved the beehive, you'll want to maintain your style for as long as possible. Here are some tips for keeping your beehive looking fresh:

  • Avoid touching your hair too much - this can flatten the beehive and cause it to lose its shape
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your roots and absorb excess oil
  • Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or use a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage

Conclusion: The Hardest Hairstyle to Do

The beehive is undoubtedly the hardest hairstyle to do, but with practice and patience, it can be achieved. Whether you're a fan of retro styles or just looking for a challenge, the beehive is a hairstyle that will test your skills and leave you feeling accomplished.
