How To Start Dreads On Relaxed Hair Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 09, 2023 · 4 min read
How to Start Dreads on Relaxed Hair | Step by Step Guide 2023

If you have relaxed hair and want to start dreadlocks, it is possible! In fact, many people with relaxed hair choose to start dreads because it is a low-maintenance, stylish hairstyle. However, starting dreads on relaxed hair requires a different approach than starting dreads on natural hair. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start dreads on relaxed hair.

How to Start Dreads on Relaxed Hair

Step 1: Decide on the Size of Your Dreads

The first step in starting dreads on relaxed hair is to decide on the size of your dreads. The size of your dreads will determine how long it takes for your hair to lock and mature. You can choose to start with small, medium, or large dreads. Small dreads will take longer to mature, while large dreads will mature faster.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Before starting your dreads, it is important to wash your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product build-up or residue from your hair. This will help your hair to lock and mature faster.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections should be based on the size of the dreads you want. Use hair clips to secure each section of hair.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Take a section of hair and hold it at the ends. Using a comb, backcomb the hair from the ends towards the roots. This will create knots and tangles in the hair, which will eventually form into dreadlocks.

Step 5: Twist and Seal Your Dreads

After backcombing your hair, twist the section in the direction you want your dread to form. Use a small amount of dread wax or locking gel to seal the ends of the dread. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 6: Maintain Your Dreads

Once you have started your dreads, it is important to maintain them properly. Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair and avoid using heavy oils or conditioners. You can also palm roll your dreads to help them lock and mature faster.

Tips for Starting Dreads on Relaxed Hair

- Be patient. It takes time for dreads to lock and mature, especially on relaxed hair.

- Avoid using heavy oils or conditioners on your hair, as this can prevent your dreads from locking.

- Protect your hair while you sleep by wearing a satin bonnet or using a satin pillowcase.

- Consider getting professional help from a loctician to start your dreads.

FAQs

Q: Can I start dreads on relaxed hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreads on relaxed hair. However, it requires a different approach than starting dreads on natural hair.

Q: How long does it take for dreads to lock on relaxed hair?

A: It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year for dreads to fully lock and mature on relaxed hair.

Relaxed Hair Care for Dreads

Starting dreads on relaxed hair requires you to change your hair care routine. Here are some tips for caring for your relaxed hair while you have dreads:

- Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair.

- Avoid using heavy oils or conditioners on your hair.

- Wear a satin bonnet or use a satin pillowcase to protect your hair while you sleep.

- Palm roll your dreads to help them lock and mature faster.

Starting dreads on relaxed hair is a low-maintenance, stylish hairstyle that requires patience and proper care. By following our step-by-step guide and tips, you can start your journey to having beautiful, mature dreadlocks on relaxed hair.
