How To Fade Male Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 22, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking for a trendy and stylish haircut, then a fade haircut might be the perfect option for you. Fading your hair involves blending the hair on the sides and back of your head with the hair on top of your head. This creates a sharp and clean look that is perfect for any occasion. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of how to fade male haircut in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

How to Fade Male Haircut

Here are the steps to follow when fading your male haircut:

Step 1: Choose the Right Clippers

The first step to fading your hair is to choose the right clippers. You want to make sure that your clippers have different guard sizes that can help you achieve the desired length of your hair.

Step 2: Decide on the Length of Your Hair

Before you start fading your hair, you need to decide on the length of your hair. This will help you determine which guard size to use. If you're not sure what length to go for, it's always best to start with a longer guard size and work your way down until you achieve the desired length.

Step 3: Start with the Highest Guard Size

Once you've decided on the length of your hair, start with the highest guard size on your clippers. This will ensure that you don't cut your hair too short in the beginning.

Step 4: Start at the Bottom of Your Head

Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up. Use the clippers to create a straight line around your head.

Step 5: Use a Lower Guard Size

Once you've finished with the highest guard size, switch to a lower guard size and repeat the process. Remember to blend the hair properly to create a smooth transition.

Step 6: Use the Lowest Guard Size

Once you've blended the hair with the lower guard size, switch to the lowest guard size and repeat the process. This will create a faded look that is sharp and clean.

Step 7: Blend the Top of Your Hair

After you've finished fading the sides and back of your head, it's time to blend the top of your hair. Use scissors or a comb to blend the hair properly.

Step 8: Clean Up the Edges

Use a trimmer to clean up the edges around your ears and neckline. This will give your haircut a polished look.

Step 9: Wash and Style Your Hair

Once you've finished fading your hair, it's time to wash and style your hair. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Tips for Fading Male Haircut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when fading your male haircut:

  • Take your time and be patient. Fading your hair requires precision and attention to detail.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from different angles.
  • Practice makes perfect. Don't be discouraged if you don't get it right the first time.
  • Experiment with different guard sizes to find the perfect length for your hair.
  • Don't forget to clean your clippers after each use to keep them in good condition.

FAQs About Fading Male Haircut

Here are some frequently asked questions about fading male haircut:

1. How often should I fade my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to maintain your haircut. As a general rule, you should aim to fade your hair every 2-3 weeks.

2. Can I fade my hair at home?

Yes, you can fade your hair at home. However, it's important to have the right tools and to follow the steps carefully.

3. What is the difference between a fade and a taper?

A fade involves blending the hair on the sides and back of your head with the hair on top of your head. A taper involves gradually decreasing the length of your hair from the top of your head to your neckline.

Hair Care for Faded Haircuts

To maintain your faded haircut, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow-drying or straightening.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and length of your haircut.
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair, as it can weigh it down and make it look greasy.

Now that you know how to fade male haircut, it's time to give it a try. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to experiment with different guard sizes and lengths. With a little practice, you'll be able to achieve a sharp and clean look that is perfect for any occasion.
