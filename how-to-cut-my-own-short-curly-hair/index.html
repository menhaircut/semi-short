How To Cut My Own Short Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 20, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're tired of spending a fortune on haircuts or just want to try something new, cutting your own short curly hair can be a great option. But before you grab those scissors, it's important to know what you're doing. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting your own short curly hair, from start to finish.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Cut My Own Short Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Short Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Cutting

How to Cut My Own Short Curly Hair:

The first thing you need to do is gather your supplies. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. It's also a good idea to have a mirror in front of you and behind you, so you can see your hair from all angles.

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and conditioned. This will help you get a more accurate cut and make your hair easier to work with.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. This will help prevent breakage and make it easier to section your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section up with a hair clip, leaving one section down to start cutting.

Step 4: Cut the First Section

Starting with the first section, use your fingers to pull your hair straight up and trim off any split ends or damaged hair. Be sure to cut small sections at a time, and use the comb to guide your scissors.

Step 5: Move on to the Other Sections

Once you've finished the first section, let down the next section and repeat the same process. Continue until you've cut all four sections.

Tips for Cutting Short Curly Hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Cut small sections at a time to avoid making mistakes.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can look shorter when it dries.
  • Take breaks if you need to, as cutting your own hair can be tiring.

FAQs:

Q: Can I cut my own short curly hair without any experience?

A: Yes, you can! Just be sure to start with small sections and take your time. You can also watch tutorials online for more guidance.

Q: How often should I cut my short curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Hair Care After Cutting:

After you've finished cutting your hair, be sure to wash and condition it again to remove any loose hairs. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or styling product to enhance your curls and add definition.

Cutting your own short curly hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it's important to take your time and be patient. With these tips and steps, you'll be on your way to a great haircut in no time!
