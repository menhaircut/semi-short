How to Get Rid of Brassy Hair at Home

1. Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with blonde hair. It contains purple pigments that neutralize the yellow and orange tones in your hair, leaving you with a cooler, more neutral tone. Simply swap out your regular shampoo for a purple shampoo and use it once or twice a week.

2. Apple Cider Vinegar Rinse

Apple cider vinegar is a natural hair clarifier that can help to remove buildup and restore your hairâs natural shine. Mix 1 part apple cider vinegar with 2 parts water and apply it to your hair after shampooing. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

3. Baking Soda and Shampoo

Baking soda is a natural exfoliant that can help to remove buildup from your hair. Mix a tablespoon of baking soda with your shampoo and apply it to your hair. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing it out.

4. Lemon Juice

Lemon juice is a natural hair lightener that can help to remove brassiness from your hair. Squeeze some lemon juice into a spray bottle and spritz it onto your hair. Leave it on for a few hours before washing it out.

Step-by-Step Guide

Hereâs a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy hair using purple shampoo:

  1. Wet your hair and apply a generous amount of purple shampoo.
  2. Massage the shampoo into your hair and scalp, focusing on the areas with the most brassiness.
  3. Leave the shampoo on for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

  6. Repeat once or twice a week, depending on how brassy your hair is.

Tips for Preventing Brassy Hair

Here are some tips for preventing brassiness in your hair:

  • Avoid overexposure to sunlight and chlorine.
  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair.
  • Wash your hair with cool water instead of hot water.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
