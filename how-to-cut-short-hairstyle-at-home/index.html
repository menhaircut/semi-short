How To Cut Short Hairstyle At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to try out a new look? Maybe you're tired of your current hairstyle and want to switch it up. Whatever the reason may be, cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you're not a professional. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting short hair at home can be a fun and easy way to change up your look. In this article, we'll go over the steps you need to take to successfully cut your short hair at home.

Table of Contents:

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Finishing Touches
  • Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair at Home

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Mirror

Make sure that your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to prepare it. Start by washing and drying your hair. Make sure that it's completely dry before you begin cutting. If your hair is naturally curly, straighten it before you begin cutting to ensure that it's easier to work with.

Sectioning Your Hair

Next, section your hair. Start by parting your hair down the middle. Then, using your comb, part your hair horizontally from ear to ear. Clip the top section of your hair away, leaving only the bottom section to work with.

Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting. Begin at the back of your head and work your way forward. Take small sections of hair and use your comb to hold them taut. Then, using your scissors, make small cuts until you reach the desired length.

Be sure to cut your hair slowly and carefully, taking small sections at a time. It's better to cut less hair than you want to, as you can always go back and make more cuts if necessary.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's time to add some finishing touches. Use your comb to blend any uneven sections and ensure that your hair is even all around. If you want to add some texture or layers, you can do so by making small cuts at an angle.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up to larger cuts.
  • Use a mirror to see the back of your head as you cut.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring and may take some time.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair if it's wet?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be harder to work with and may result in uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended that you cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair at Home

After you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Be sure to wash and condition your hair regularly, and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep it healthy and shiny. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially if you're looking to switch up your look. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a professional-looking cut from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps and tips to successfully cut your short hair at home.
