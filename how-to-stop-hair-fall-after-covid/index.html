How To Stop Hair Fall After Covid A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have recently recovered from COVID-19, you may have noticed that your hair is falling out more than usual. This is a common side effect of the virus, and it can be frustrating and stressful to deal with. But don't worry, there are steps you can take to stop hair fall after COVID and promote healthy hair growth. In this article, we will guide you through everything you need to know about how to stop hair fall after COVID.

Table of Contents

  • How to Stop Hair Fall After COVID
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Stop Hair Fall After COVID

The first step to stopping hair fall after COVID is to understand why it's happening. COVID-19 can cause physical and emotional stress, which can lead to hair loss. Additionally, the virus can impact the immune system and disrupt the hair growth cycle. Here are some ways to stop hair fall after COVID:

1. Practice Good Hair Hygiene

Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can damage your hair. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid tight hairstyles that can cause tension on your hairline.

2. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a healthy diet can help promote hair growth and prevent hair fall. Include foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals, such as leafy greens, nuts, eggs, and fatty fish. Drink plenty of water to keep your body and hair hydrated.

3. Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair fall, so it's important to find ways to manage it. Practice relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga. Get enough sleep, and make time for activities that bring you joy and relaxation.

4. Consult with a Medical Professional

If your hair fall persists, consult with a dermatologist or other medical professional. They can evaluate your hair and scalp, and recommend treatments or medications that can help promote hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to stopping hair fall after COVID:

Step 1: Evaluate Your Hair Care Routine

Assess your hair care routine and make changes as needed. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid harsh chemicals and styling products. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid tight hairstyles.

Step 2: Eat a Balanced Diet

Incorporate foods that are rich in vitamins and minerals into your diet. Drink plenty of water to keep your body and hair hydrated.

Step 3: Manage Stress

Practice relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga. Get enough sleep, and make time for activities that bring you joy and relaxation.

Step 4: Consult with a Medical Professional

If your hair fall persists, consult with a dermatologist or other medical professional. They can evaluate your hair and scalp, and recommend treatments or medications that can help promote hair growth.

Tips for Hair Care After COVID

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair after COVID:

1. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair and contribute to hair fall.

2. Use a Hair Mask

Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. You can make a DIY mask with ingredients like coconut oil, honey, and avocado.

3. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles, such as tight braids or ponytails, as they can cause tension and contribute to hair fall.

4. Be Patient

Remember that hair growth takes time, so be patient and consistent with your hair care routine. It may take several months to see significant results.

FAQs

1. Is hair fall after COVID permanent?

No, hair fall after COVID is usually temporary, and hair will typically grow back once the body has fully recovered.

2. Will hair fall stop on its own?

In many cases, hair fall will stop on its own as the body recovers from COVID. However, if hair fall persists, it's important to consult with a medical professional.

3. Can stress cause hair fall?

Yes, stress can contribute to hair fall. It's important to find ways to manage stress, such as through relaxation techniques or counseling.

By following these tips and consulting with a medical professional if needed, you can stop hair fall after COVID and promote healthy hair growth.
