Why Is Colored Hair Seen As Unprofessional

Introduction

Colored hair has been a fashion trend for many years. However, it is still considered unprofessional in many workplaces. This can be frustrating for people who want to express their personal style but also want to maintain a professional appearance.

So, why is colored hair seen as unprofessional? In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this perception and provide tips for coloring your hair in a professional manner.

How to Color Your Hair Professionally

When it comes to coloring your hair for a professional setting, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Choose natural-looking colors, such as brown, black, or blonde.
  • Avoid bright or neon colors.
  • Consider highlights or lowlights rather than all-over color.
  • Consult with a professional stylist to ensure the best results.

Choosing Natural-Looking Colors

Natural-looking hair colors are those that mimic the shades found in nature. These include brown, black, blonde, and red. If you want to add some dimension to your hair, consider highlights or lowlights that are only a few shades lighter or darker than your natural color.

Avoiding Bright or Neon Colors

Bright or neon hair colors are typically associated with youth culture and are not considered professional in many settings. If you want to add a pop of color to your hair, consider using a temporary hair color spray or hair chalk instead of permanent dye.

Considering Highlights or Lowlights

Highlights and lowlights can add dimension to your hair without the commitment of all-over color. They can also be more subtle and natural-looking. Consider getting highlights or lowlights that are only a few shades lighter or darker than your natural color.

Consulting with a Professional Stylist

A professional stylist can help you choose the right hair color for your skin tone and personal style. They can also ensure that the color is applied evenly and looks natural. Be sure to bring photos of the color you want and communicate your expectations clearly.

Step-by-Step Guide to Coloring Your Hair Professionally

Here is a step-by-step guide to coloring your hair professionally:

  1. Choose a natural-looking color: As mentioned earlier, choose a hair color that looks natural and complements your skin tone.
  2. Prepare your hair: Wash your hair and avoid using any styling products before coloring.
  3. Protect your skin: Apply petroleum jelly or a barrier cream around your hairline and ears to prevent staining.
  4. Follow the instructions: Read the instructions carefully and follow them exactly.
  5. Rinse and condition: Rinse your hair thoroughly and apply conditioner to keep it soft and healthy.
  6. Dry and style: Blow-dry and style your hair as usual.

Tips for Maintaining Colored Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining your colored hair:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to the sun for long periods of time.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

FAQs about Colored Hair in the Workplace

Q: Why is colored hair seen as unprofessional?

Colored hair is seen as unprofessional because it is often associated with youth culture and rebellion. In many workplaces, a conservative appearance is preferred to convey a professional image.

Q: Can I have colored hair in the workplace?

It depends on the workplace. Some companies have strict dress codes that prohibit colored hair, while others are more lenient. If you are unsure, it is best to check with your HR department.

Q: What should I do if my workplace prohibits colored hair?

If your workplace prohibits colored hair, you have a few options. You can either abide by the dress code or look for a job with a company that allows colored hair.

Hair Care for Colored Hair

Colored hair requires special care to maintain its vibrancy and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often.
  • Avoid hot tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, too often.
  • Avoid exposing your hair to the sun for long periods of time.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, which can fade color.

In conclusion, colored hair is often seen as unprofessional because it is associated with youth culture and rebellion. However, there are ways to color your hair professionally and maintain a professional appearance in the workplace. By choosing natural-looking colors, consulting with a professional stylist, and taking care of your colored hair, you can express your personal style while still conforming to workplace dress codes.
