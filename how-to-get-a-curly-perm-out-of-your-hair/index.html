How to Get a Curly Perm Out of Your Hair

If you've recently gotten a curly perm and you're not loving the results, don't worry, there are ways to remove it. This article will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to get a curly perm out of your hair. Read on for more information.

Description

A curly perm is a chemical treatment that creates permanent waves in hair. The process involves applying a solution to the hair, which breaks down the hair's natural structure and allows it to be reformed. While many people love the look of a curly perm, others find that it's not for them. If you're in the latter camp, don't worry, there are ways to get rid of a curly perm.

How to Get a Curly Perm Out of Your Hair

There are two main methods for removing a curly perm: cutting it off or using a perm solution to relax the curls. The latter method is less drastic and can be done at home. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

You'll need a perm solution, a neutralizer, gloves, a comb, and a towel.

Step 2: Apply the Perm Solution

Put on your gloves and apply the perm solution to your hair. Follow the instructions on the package, but typically you'll need to saturate your hair with the solution and then comb it through to ensure even distribution.

Step 3: Wait and Rinse

After applying the perm solution, you'll need to wait the recommended amount of time before rinsing it out. This can vary depending on the brand you're using, so make sure to read the instructions carefully. Once the time is up, rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.

Step 4: Apply the Neutralizer

After rinsing out the perm solution, apply the neutralizer to your hair. This will stop the chemical process and prevent further damage to your hair. Leave the neutralizer on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out with warm water.

Step 5: Condition Your Hair

Finally, condition your hair to restore moisture and hydration. This will help to prevent damage and breakage.

Tips

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when removing a curly perm:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools until your hair has fully recovered from the perm.
  • Consider getting a haircut to remove any damaged or frizzy ends.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about removing a curly perm:

  • Will my hair be damaged after removing a curly perm? It's possible for your hair to be damaged after a perm, but using a perm solution to relax the curls can help to minimize damage.
  • How long will it take for my hair to recover from a perm? It can take several months for your hair to fully recover from a perm. During this time, be gentle with your hair and avoid using heat styling tools.
  • Can I remove a curly perm at home? Yes, you can remove a curly perm at home using a perm solution. Just make sure to follow the instructions carefully and take precautions to protect your hair.

Hair Care

Remember to take good care of your hair after removing a curly perm. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoid heat styling tools, and use a deep conditioning treatment regularly. With a little patience and care, your hair will be back to its natural state in no time.
