How To Cut Your Own Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Bardi Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to freshen up your hairstyle without spending a lot of money, cutting your own hair with thinning shears is a great option. Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. They are designed to remove some of the bulk from your hair, creating a softer, more textured look. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your own hair with thinning shears, step by step.

Description

Thinning shears are a popular tool among hairstylists because they allow for precision cutting and texturizing. They have a unique blade that has teeth on one or both blades, which helps to remove bulk and create a softer, more textured look. Thinning shears are especially useful for people with thick hair, as they can help to thin out the hair and make it more manageable.

How to Cut Your Own Hair with Thinning Shears

Before you begin, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of thinning shears, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water, as wet hair is easier to cut with thinning shears.

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

The first step is to wash and dry your hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you begin cutting. Wet hair is more difficult to cut with thinning shears, and it can also cause the shears to rust.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Next, comb your hair to remove any tangles or knots. Make sure your hair is completely detangled before you begin cutting.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will make it easier to cut your hair with thinning shears. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and start cutting. Make sure to cut in small sections, and avoid cutting too much at once. It's better to cut a little at a time and check your progress as you go.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Check your progress as you go by looking in the mirror. Make sure your hair is even and that you're happy with the texture. If you need to make any adjustments, go back and cut a little more.

Step 6: Finish Up

Once you've finished cutting your hair with thinning shears, comb through it one more time to make sure it's even. Style your hair as desired, and enjoy your new look!

Tips

  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area
  • Use sharp thinning shears
  • Don't cut too much at once
  • Check your progress as you go
  • Use a mirror to see the back of your head

FAQ

Can I use thinning shears on any hair type?

Thinning shears work best on people with thick, coarse hair. If you have thin or fine hair, it's best to avoid using thinning shears as they can create too much texture and make your hair look thin and wispy.

Can I use regular scissors instead of thinning shears?

You can use regular scissors to cut your hair, but they won't create the same textured look as thinning shears. Regular scissors will simply cut your hair straight across, while thinning shears remove some of the bulk and create a softer, more textured look.

Hair Care

To keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant, it's important to take good care of it. This includes washing it regularly, using a conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools whenever possible. You should also get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair with thinning shears can be a fun and easy way to change up your look. With a little practice and some patience, you can achieve a salon-worthy hairstyle right at home.
