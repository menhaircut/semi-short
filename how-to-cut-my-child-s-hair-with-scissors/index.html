How To Cut My Child s Hair With Scissors A Simple Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 02, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're a parent, you know how expensive it can be to take your child to a hair salon. Plus, with the current pandemic situation, it's ideal to avoid crowded places as much as possible. One way to save money and keep your child safe is by cutting their hair at home. Cutting your child's hair with scissors may seem daunting, but with a little practice, you can do it like a pro. In this article, we'll provide you with a simple guide on how to cut your child's hair with scissors.

How to Cut My Child's Hair with Scissors

Before we start, here's what you need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Towel or cape

Step-by-Step Guide to Cutting Your Child's Hair with Scissors

Follow these steps to cut your child's hair with scissors:

  1. Start by washing your child's hair and towel-drying it.
  2. Comb their hair and divide it into sections using hair clips. Start with the back of the head and work your way forward.
  3. Choose the length you want to cut and hold the hair between your fingers. Use the scissors to trim the hair at the desired length. Make sure to cut straight across.
  4. Move to the next section and repeat the process.
  5. After you've cut all the sections, comb the hair to remove any tangles and trim any uneven spots.

Tips for Cutting Your Child's Hair with Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind while cutting your child's hair with scissors:

  • Start by cutting small sections of hair. You can always cut more if needed, but you can't put it back once you've cut too much.
  • Make sure your child is sitting still and not moving their head around too much.
  • Use the comb to keep the hair in place while cutting.
  • Use the spray bottle to dampen the hair if it's too dry or frizzy.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and be patient.

FAQs About Cutting Your Child's Hair with Scissors

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your child's hair with scissors:

1. How often should I cut my child's hair?

It depends on how fast their hair grows. On average, children's hair grows about half an inch per month. You can cut their hair every 2-3 months to keep it at a manageable length.

2. What if I make a mistake while cutting my child's hair?

Don't worry if you make a mistake. Hair grows back, and it's not the end of the world. You can always take your child to a salon to fix any mistakes.

3. Can I use regular scissors to cut my child's hair?

No, you should use scissors designed specifically for cutting hair. Regular scissors can damage the hair and make it look uneven.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Child's Hair with Scissors

After cutting your child's hair with scissors, make sure to wash their hair to remove any loose hair. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep their hair healthy and shiny. You can also use a detangling spray to make combing their hair easier. Avoid using heat styling tools on their hair, as it can damage the hair and make it look dry and brittle.

Cutting your child's hair with scissors may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, you can do it easily. Follow our step-by-step guide, keep our tips in mind, and don't be afraid to make mistakes. Your child's hair will look great, and you'll save money on salon visits.
