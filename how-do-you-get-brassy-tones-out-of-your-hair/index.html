How To Get Rid Of Brassy Tones In Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of dealing with brassy tones in your hair? Whether you have recently dyed your hair or have naturally blonde hair, brassy tones can be a frustrating problem. However, there are ways to remove these tones and achieve the perfect hair color. In this blog post, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to get rid of brassy tones in your hair.

What Causes Brassy Tones in Hair?

Before we dive into how to get rid of brassy tones, it is important to understand what causes them. When you lighten your hair, whether through dyeing or bleaching, the color pigments in your hair are removed. This process can leave your hair with warm, brassy tones, especially if you have naturally dark hair. The brassy tones are caused by the underlying pigment that is exposed after the color pigments are removed.

How to Get Rid of Brassy Tones in Hair

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

The first step to getting rid of brassy tones in your hair is to use a purple shampoo. Purple shampoo is specifically designed to neutralize brassy tones in blonde or lightened hair. The purple pigments in the shampoo counteract the yellow and orange tones in your hair, leaving you with a cooler, more neutral tone.

Step 2: Tone Your Hair

If your hair is still brassy after using a purple shampoo, the next step is to tone your hair. Toning your hair involves using a hair toner, which is a demi-permanent hair color that helps to neutralize brassy tones. Hair toners come in a variety of shades, but for brassy hair, you will want to use a toner with cool undertones, such as ash or platinum.

Step 3: Use a Hair Mask

After toning your hair, it is important to use a hair mask to nourish and hydrate your hair. Bleaching and dyeing can leave your hair dry and damaged, and a hair mask can help to restore moisture and shine. Look for a hair mask specifically designed for color-treated hair, as these products are formulated to protect and enhance your hair color.

Tips for Preventing Brassy Tones

While there are ways to remove brassy tones from your hair, prevention is always the best strategy. Here are a few tips for preventing brassy tones:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray when spending time in the sun
  • Avoid swimming in chlorinated water without a swim cap

FAQ

1. What is a purple shampoo and how does it work?

A purple shampoo is a shampoo that contains purple pigments. These pigments neutralize brassy tones in blonde or lightened hair by counteracting the yellow and orange tones in your hair.

2. Can I use a hair mask instead of a conditioner?

While a hair mask can be used in place of a conditioner, it is not recommended to do so on a regular basis. Hair masks are formulated to provide deep conditioning and nourishment to your hair, and using them too frequently can weigh down your hair and make it greasy.

3. How often should I tone my hair?

The frequency of toning your hair depends on a variety of factors, including the color of your hair, the type of toner you are using, and how quickly your hair fades. In general, it is recommended to tone your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain your desired hair color.

Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is essential for maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are a few tips for hair care:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends
  • Eat a balanced diet to promote hair health
  • Stay hydrated to prevent dry, brittle hair

By following these tips and using the steps outlined in this blog post, you can get rid of brassy tones in your hair and achieve the perfect hair color. Remember to take care of your hair to maintain healthy, beautiful locks.
