How To Put Layers In Shoulder Length Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to add some volume and dimension to your shoulder length hair? Layers could be the answer! However, going to a salon can be expensive and time-consuming. Why not try putting layers in your hair at home? With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy look without breaking the bank.

How to Put Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

Before we dive into the steps, it's important to understand the different types of layers. There are three main types:

  • Short Layers: These are placed around the crown of the head and add volume to the top of the hair.
  • Medium Layers: These are placed around the middle of the hair and add dimension and movement.
  • Long Layers: These are placed around the ends of the hair and add texture and movement.

For shoulder length hair, medium layers are usually the most flattering. Now, onto the steps!

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Comb through any tangles.
  2. Divide your hair into sections. Use clips to secure the top section of your hair out of the way.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom layer. Hold it straight up and use hair cutting scissors to trim the ends. This will be your guide section.
  4. Take another small section of hair from the bottom layer. Use the guide section to cut the ends to the same length. This will create your first layer.
  5. Continue taking small sections of hair and using the guide section to create layers. Work your way up to the middle layer of your hair.
  6. Release the top section of your hair and repeat the process, starting with the bottom layer and working your way up to the middle layer.
  7. Blend the layers by combing through your hair with a fine-tooth comb. This will remove any harsh lines.
  8. Use hair cutting scissors to add texture to the ends of your hair. This will create a softer, more natural look.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Putting Layers in Shoulder Length Hair

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting scissors. This will make the process easier and ensure clean cuts.
  • Start with small sections of hair. You can always take more off, but you can't put it back on!
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can appear longer than it actually is, which can lead to uneven layers.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your hair, start with just a few layers and work your way up to more.

FAQs

Can I put layers in curly hair?

Absolutely! However, it's important to cut curly hair when it's dry. This will ensure that the layers are evenly distributed and won't disrupt the natural curl pattern.

How often should I get my layers trimmed?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your layers lose their shape. On average, getting a trim every 6-8 weeks will keep your layers looking fresh.

Will layers add volume to my hair?

Yes! Layers can add volume and movement to your hair, especially if you opt for short or medium layers.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and nourished.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Invest in a good dry shampoo to extend the life of your layers in between washes.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.

Putting layers in your shoulder length hair is a great way to switch up your look and add some dimension. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve the perfect layered haircut at home. Happy cutting!
