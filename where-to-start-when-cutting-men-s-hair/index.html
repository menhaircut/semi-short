Where To Start When Cutting Men s Hair A Guide For Beginners

Written by Petter Sep 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're interested in cutting men's hair, whether it's for your friends and family or as a career, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. There are different techniques, tools, and hairstyles to consider. But don't worry, this guide will give you a step-by-step process and some tips to get started.

Description

Cutting men's hair is not just about using scissors and a comb. It requires a certain level of skill, knowledge, and creativity. You need to understand the basics of hair anatomy, texture, and growth patterns. You also need to be familiar with different haircut styles, from classic to trendy. Additionally, you need to have the right tools, such as clippers, trimmers, shears, and combs.

How to

The first step in cutting men's hair is to prepare your tools and workspace. Make sure your tools are clean, sharp, and well-maintained. You don't want to use dull blades or dirty combs on someone's hair. Also, make sure your workspace is well-lit, comfortable, and free of distractions. You need to focus on the task at hand.

The second step is to assess your client's hair. Ask them what kind of haircut they want and show them some pictures or examples. Look at their hair texture, length, and density. Determine if they have any cowlicks or hairlines that require special attention. Use your hands to feel their hair and visualize the end result.

The third step is to section the hair. Use a comb to create clean and even sections. Start from the bottom and work your way up. Use clips to hold the sections in place. This will help you control the hair and prevent uneven cutting.

The fourth step is to cut the hair. Use your tools according to the haircut style and the hair section. For example, use clippers for the sides and back, and shears for the top. Use scissors-over-comb or clipper-over-comb techniques for blending and fading. Use thinning shears or razors for texture and layers. Keep checking your work as you go and make adjustments as needed.

The fifth step is to finish the haircut. Use a blow dryer and a brush to style the hair. Apply some product if needed. Use a mirror to show your client the back of their head and ask for their feedback. Make any final touches and clean up the hair around the ears and neck.

Step by step

Step 1: Prepare your tools and workspace

Clean and sharpen your tools, and make sure your workspace is comfortable and free of distractions.

Step 2: Assess your client's hair

Ask your client what kind of haircut they want, and evaluate their hair texture, length, and density.

Step 3: Section the hair

Create clean and even sections using a comb and clips.

Step 4: Cut the hair

Use the appropriate tools and techniques for the haircut style and hair section, and check your work as you go.

Step 5: Finish the haircut

Style the hair using a blow dryer, brush, and product, and show your client the back of their head for feedback.

Tips

  • Practice on mannequin heads or willing friends and family members before cutting someone's hair professionally.
  • Watch tutorial videos or take classes to learn new techniques and styles.
  • Communicate clearly with your client and listen to their preferences and concerns.
  • Be patient and take your time, especially if you're a beginner.
  • Clean and sanitize your tools after each use to prevent infections and diseases.

FAQ

  1. Do I need a license to cut men's hair?

    2. It depends on your state or country's regulations. Some places require a cosmetology or barbering license to cut hair professionally. Check with your local government or licensing board for more information.

  2. What if I make a mistake while cutting someone's hair?

    3. Don't panic. Mistakes happen, even to experienced hairstylists. Try to fix the mistake if possible, or offer to give a discount or refund. Apologize and learn from the experience.

  3. How often should men get their hair cut?

    4. It depends on their hair type, length, and style. Generally, men should get their hair cut every 4-8 weeks to maintain the shape and health of their hair.

Hair Care

Cutting men's hair is not just about the haircut itself. It's also about promoting healthy and attractive hair. Encourage your clients to follow a good hair care routine, such as washing and conditioning their hair regularly, using heat protectant products, avoiding harsh chemicals, and protecting their hair from the sun and wind. Also, recommend some styling products that suit their hair type and style.

Now that you know where to start when cutting men's hair, don't be afraid to try new things and express your creativity. Cutting hair can be a rewarding and fun skill to have.
