How To Fade Your Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of paying a hefty amount of money to get your hair faded? Do you want to learn how to fade your own hair at home? Look no further, because we have got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the process of fading your own hair in a relaxed English language.

How to Fade Your Own Hair

Fading your own hair can be intimidating, but it is not impossible. The key to a successful fade is to take your time and be patient. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking fade at home.

What You Will Need

Before we dive into the process, here are the tools you will need:

  • Clipper
  • Guard sizes (1, 2, 3, 4)
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Mirror
  • Towel
  • Barber cape or an old shirt
  • Clipper oil
  • Hair trimmer

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to fade your own hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with shampoo and conditioner. This will make it easier to cut and style your hair.

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size

Decide on the fade you want and choose the appropriate guard size. Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller size for a more gradual fade.

Step 3: Start with the Sides

Start with the sides of your head. Use the larger guard size and trim the hair around your ears and the nape of your neck. Work your way up to the temples, using a smaller guard size as you go.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Blend the top of your hair with the sides. Use scissors to blend the hair on the top of your head with the hair on the sides. Be sure to use a comb to keep the hair in place while you cut.

Step 5: Use the Trimmer

Use the hair trimmer to create a clean line around your hairline and sideburns. This will give your fade a professional finish.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Use a mirror to check your work. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure your fade is even on both sides.

Step 7: Clean Up

Clean up any loose hair with a towel and brush off your shoulders. Remove the guard from your clippers and oil them to keep them in good condition.

Tips for Fading Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when fading your own hair:

  • Take your time and be patient.
  • Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller size.
  • Blend the top of your hair with the sides using scissors.
  • Use a mirror to check your work and make any necessary adjustments.
  • Clean your clippers and trimmer after each use to keep them in good condition.

FAQ

Can I fade my own hair if I have never done it before?

Yes, you can. It may take some practice, but with patience and the right technique, you can achieve a professional-looking fade at home.

What guard sizes should I use to fade my own hair?

Start with a larger guard size and work your way down to a smaller size for a more gradual fade.

How long does a fade last?

A fade typically lasts 2-3 weeks before it starts to grow out. You can maintain your fade by trimming it every 2-3 weeks.

Hair Care After Fading

After fading your hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain a healthy and shiny look. Here are some tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as straighteners and curling irons, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using hair products with harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

Now that you know how to fade your own hair, you can save money and achieve a professional-looking fade at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow the steps and tips we have outlined in this article. Happy fading!
