How To Care For A Natural Hair Wig Tips And Tricks

Written by William Nov 16, 2023 ¬∑ 4 min read

Wearing a natural hair wig can be a great way to switch up your hairstyle without damaging your natural hair. However, taking care of a natural hair wig can be a bit challenging if you're new to it. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to care for a natural hair wig so that it lasts longer and stays looking beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • How to Care for a Natural Hair Wig
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Wig
  • FAQs

How to Care for a Natural Hair Wig

A natural hair wig is made from real hair, which means that it needs to be treated with care just like your own hair. Here are some general tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to wash your wig
  • Avoid using hot water or heat styling tools on your wig
  • Avoid sleeping in your wig, and take it off before going to bed
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head when not in use

Now, let's dive into the step-by-step guide on how to care for your natural hair wig.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to keep your natural hair wig looking beautiful:

Step 1: Detangle Your Wig

Before washing your wig, use a wide-tooth comb or wig brush to gently detangle any knots or tangles. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up to the roots.

Step 2: Wash Your Wig

Fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Gently swirl the wig in the water, being careful not to rub or scrub the hair too much. Rinse the wig thoroughly with cool water.

Step 3: Condition Your Wig

Apply a small amount of wig conditioner to the hair, avoiding the roots. Let the conditioner sit for a few minutes, then rinse it out with cool water.

Step 4: Dry Your Wig

Use a towel to gently pat the wig dry, then lay it flat on a towel to air dry. Do not use a hair dryer or apply any heat to the wig.

Step 5: Style Your Wig

Once your wig is dry, you can style it using a wig brush or your fingers. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage the hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Wig

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when caring for your natural hair wig:

  • Use a wig cap to protect your natural hair and keep your wig in place
  • Avoid wearing your wig in extreme temperatures or humid conditions
  • Store your wig in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as they can weigh down the hair

FAQs

How often should I wash my wig?

It's generally recommended to wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or oily.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my wig?

No, you should use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically designed for wigs. Regular hair products can be too harsh and can damage the hair.

Can I swim or shower in my wig?

No, you should not wear your wig in the shower, pool, or ocean. Chlorine and salt water can damage the hair, and water can cause the wig to become tangled.

General Hair Care Tips

Whether you're wearing a natural hair wig or taking care of your own hair, here are some general hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using hot styling tools on your hair
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do use heat styling tools
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair when brushing or styling it
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your natural hair wig looking beautiful and healthy for as long as possible. Remember to be gentle with your wig and take care of it just like you would your own hair.
