How To Style Hair Growing Out After Chemo

Written by Petter Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you or someone you know has been through chemotherapy, you are aware of the toll it can take on your body, including hair loss. But once you have finished your treatment and your hair starts growing back, it's time to start thinking about how to style hair growing out after chemo. In this article, we'll provide you with tips and tricks to help you make the most of your new hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Style Hair Growing Out After Chemo
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips to Keep in Mind
  • Frequently Asked Questions

How to Style Hair Growing Out After Chemo

The first thing you should do is embrace the new texture of your hair. As it grows back, it may be curlier, thicker, or even a different color than your original hair. This means that you may need to adjust your styling routine and products to accommodate the changes.

One of the easiest ways to style hair growing out after chemo is to go for a short haircut. This will help you get rid of any damaged hair and give you a fresh start. A pixie cut or a bob can be a great option, as they are easy to maintain and can be styled in a variety of ways.

If you prefer longer hair, you can try growing it out gradually. This will allow you to experiment with different lengths and styles as your hair grows. You can also try adding layers to create volume and movement.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style hair growing out after chemo:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. If you have short hair, apply a small amount of styling product, such as gel or pomade, to add texture and hold.
  3. If you have longer hair, use a styling cream or mousse to add volume and control frizz.
  4. If you want to add some waves or curls, use a curling iron or hot rollers to create loose, natural-looking curls.
  5. If you prefer a straight style, use a flat iron to smooth out your hair.
  6. You can also try braiding your hair or putting it up in a ponytail or bun for a quick and easy style.
  7. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips to Keep in Mind

Here are some tips to help you style hair growing out after chemo:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid using harsh chemicals, such as hair dye or bleach, until your hair is fully grown out.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products to find what works best for you.
  • Consider using a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about styling hair growing out after chemo:

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

A: It can take several months for hair to start growing back after chemo, and up to a year or more for it to fully grow back.

Q: Can I color or bleach my hair while it's growing back?

A: It's best to wait until your hair is fully grown out before using any harsh chemicals on it.

Hair Care for Growing Out Hair After Chemo

Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair while it's growing out after chemo:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are free of sulfates and parabens.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as hair dryers and curling irons, as much as possible.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish and strengthen your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

Remember, growing out your hair after chemo can be a journey, but with a little patience and some styling tricks, you can make the most of your new hair. Embrace the changes and have fun experimenting with different styles!
