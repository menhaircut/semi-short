Description

Determining your hair type number is important when it comes to choosing the right hair products and styling techniques. There are three main hair types: straight, wavy, and curly. Each hair type has its own subcategories and characteristics that help determine which hair type number you fall under.

In this article, we will guide you on how to determine your hair type number and provide you with tips on how to take care of your hair based on your hair type.

How to Determine Your Hair Type Number

The easiest way to determine your hair type number is by examining your hair when it's dry and clean. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Look at the Shape of Your Hair Strands

Take a single hair strand and examine its shape. If your hair is straight, the strand will be cylindrical and have no bends or curves. If your hair is wavy, the strand will have an S-shape. If your hair is curly, the strand will have a tighter curl pattern and may resemble a coil or zigzag.

Step 2: Determine the Thickness of Your Hair

Take a closer look at the strand you're holding and determine its thickness. If your hair strand is thin and almost invisible, you have fine hair. If your hair strand is thicker and more visible, you have coarse hair. If it's somewhere in between, you have medium hair.

Step 3: Evaluate Your Hair's Porosity

Porosity refers to your hair's ability to absorb and retain moisture. To determine your hair's porosity, take a hair strand and place it in a glass of water. If the strand sinks to the bottom, you have high porosity hair. If the strand floats on top of the water, you have low porosity hair. If the strand sinks slowly or stays in the middle, you have normal porosity hair.

Tips for Determining Your Hair Type Number

Here are some additional tips to help you determine your hair type number:

  • Observe your hair's behavior when it's wet and dry.
  • Take note of how your hair reacts to different products and styling techniques.
  • Ask a hair stylist to help you determine your hair type number.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about determining hair type number:

Q: Can my hair type change over time?

A: Yes, your hair type can change due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

Q: Can I have more than one hair type?

A: Yes, it's possible to have more than one hair type on your head. For example, you may have wavy hair on top and curly hair on the bottom.

Q: Is it necessary to determine my hair type number?

A: While it's not necessary, determining your hair type number can help you choose the right hair products and styling techniques for your hair.

Hair Care Based on Hair Type Number

Once you've determined your hair type number, it's important to take care of your hair accordingly. Here are some hair care tips based on hair type:

Straight Hair

Straight hair tends to become oily quickly, so it's important to wash it frequently. Use a lightweight shampoo and conditioner to avoid weighing down your hair. Avoid using heavy styling products, as they can make your hair look greasy.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is prone to frizz, so it's important to use a leave-in conditioner to keep it moisturized. Use a curl-enhancing product to define your waves and avoid brushing your hair when it's dry.

Curly Hair

Curly hair needs plenty of moisture, so use a deep conditioning treatment once a week. Use a curl-defining product to help your curls keep their shape and avoid using heat styling tools.

By following these hair care tips and determining your hair type number, you can keep your hair healthy, shiny, and beautiful.
