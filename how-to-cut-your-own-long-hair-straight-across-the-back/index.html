How To Cut Your Own Long Hair Straight Across The Back A Diy Haircut Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 12, 2023 · 4 min read
How to Cut Your Own Long Hair Straight Across the Back | DIY Haircut Guide

Long hair can be beautiful, but it can also be a hassle to maintain. One of the most common haircuts for long hair is to cut it straight across the back. This style is simple but elegant and can be achieved with just a few tools and some basic knowledge. In this article, we will show you how to cut your own long hair straight across the back in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

  • Step-by-Step Guide

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a straight cut across the back of your head. This style is perfect for those who want a simple, low-maintenance haircut that still looks great.

How to Cut Your Own Long Hair Straight Across the Back

Before we get started, here are the tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Large mirror
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Once you have your tools ready, it's time to get started. Follow our step-by-step guide below.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you get an even cut.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

After your hair is dry, comb it straight down your back. Make sure there are no tangles or knots.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section off the hair at the nape of your neck. This will be the hair you cut first.

Step 4: Cut the Hair at the Nape of Your Neck

Take the hair you clipped up and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across. Use the shorter hair as a guide for the rest of your hair.

Step 5: Cut the Rest of Your Hair

Release the next section of hair and use the previously cut hair as a guide to cut the new section. Repeat this process until you have cut all of your hair.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check to make sure it is even. Use a mirror to look at the back of your head and adjust any uneven pieces.

Step 7: Trim Any Flyaways

Use your scissors to trim any flyaways or uneven pieces.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure an even cut.
  • Use hair clips to keep hair out of the way while you cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. Just make sure you cut it when it's dry and use small sections to ensure an even cut.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Hair

After cutting your hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and moisturized. Avoid using heat styling tools too often and use a heat protectant when you do.

Cutting your own long hair straight across the back can be a simple and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a beautiful and low-maintenance haircut. Follow our guide and tips to get started today!
