Starting Locs On Long Curly Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Oliver Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Locs, also known as dreadlocks, have been around for centuries and continue to be a popular hairstyle choice for many individuals. However, starting locs on long curly hair can be a bit more challenging than on other hair types. If you're considering starting your loc journey on long curly hair, then you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start locs on long curly hair, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice to help you achieve beautiful, healthy locs.

How to Start Locs on Long Curly Hair

Before you start the process of locking your hair, it's important to consider a few things. Firstly, make sure you have the time and dedication to commit to the process. Secondly, it's important to have a clear understanding of the type of locks you want to achieve. You can choose from traditional, freeform, or sisterlocks. Finally, it's important to have the right products and tools on hand to help you achieve your desired look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Starting locs on long curly hair is a gradual and natural process that requires patience and care. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue.
  2. Separate your hair into sections using a comb or your fingers.
  3. Apply a locking gel, wax, or cream to the section of hair you want to start with.
  4. Use a comb or your fingers to twist the hair in a clockwise or counterclockwise direction. Repeat this process until the entire section is twisted.
  5. Secure the end of the twisted section with a hair tie or rubber band.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 for each section of hair.
  7. Allow your hair to air dry, or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting.
  8. After your hair is completely dry, remove the hair ties or rubber bands from each section.
  9. Use a crochet hook to pull any loose hair or frizz into the twists. This will help to tighten the locks and create a neater appearance.
  10. Repeat steps 7-9 every 4-6 weeks until your locs are fully formed.

Tips for Starting Locs on Long Curly Hair

Starting locs on long curly hair can be a bit more challenging than on other hair types. Here are a few tips to help make the process easier:

  • Be patient. Starting locs on long curly hair is a gradual process that requires time and patience.
  • Avoid using heavy products. Heavy products can weigh down your hair and make it difficult for the locks to form.
  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup and residue from your hair.
  • Avoid using a comb to detangle your hair. Instead, use your fingers to gently separate any knots or tangles.
  • Protect your hair while you sleep by wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting locs on long curly hair:

  • How long does it take to start locs on long curly hair? It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year for locs to fully form on long curly hair.
  • Can I start locs on relaxed hair? Yes, you can start locs on relaxed hair. However, it's important to note that the process may take longer and require more maintenance.
  • Can I wash my hair while starting locs? Yes, you can wash your hair while starting locs. However, it's important to use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup and residue.
  • Do I need to retwist my hair while starting locs? Yes, retwisting your hair every 4-6 weeks will help to keep your locs neat and tidy.

Hair Care Tips for Locs on Long Curly Hair

Once your locs are fully formed, it's important to take care of them to keep them healthy and looking their best. Here are a few hair care tips for locs on long curly hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair.
  • Retwist your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep your locs neat and tidy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Starting locs on long curly hair can be a bit more challenging than on other hair types, but with patience, dedication, and the right products, you can achieve beautiful, healthy locs. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this guide, and you'll be well on your way to achieving the locs of your dreams.
