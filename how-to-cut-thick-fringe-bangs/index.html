Description

Cutting thick fringe bangs can be a daunting task, especially if you have never done it before. However, with the right tools and technique, you can achieve the perfect look you desire. Whether you want to add some excitement to your hairstyle or want to try a new look, cutting thick fringe bangs can be fun and easy. In this blog post, we will guide you on how to cut thick fringe bangs step-by-step and give you some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to Cut Thick Fringe Bangs

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Prep your hair - Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Comb your hair straight and ensure it is free of tangles.

Step 2: Section your hair - Divide your hair into three sections: the top, middle, and bottom. Clip the top and middle sections away, leaving only the bottom section out.

Step 3: Determine the length - Decide how long you want your bangs to be. Remember, it's always better to start longer and gradually cut shorter. Use your fingers to measure the desired length from the tip of your nose to the bottom of your eyebrows.

Step 4: Cut the hair - Using sharp scissors, cut straight across the hair from one side to the other. Start at the center of the hair and work your way outward.

Step 5: Check the length - After you have cut the hair, check the length of your bangs. If they are too long, trim them slightly until you reach the desired length.

Step 6: Cut the sides - Using the same technique as before, cut the sides of the bangs at an angle. This will create a softer, more natural look.

Step 7: Blend the edges - To make your bangs look more natural, use thinning shears to blend the edges. This will soften the blunt cut and give your bangs a more natural look.

Step 8: Style your bangs - After you have finished cutting your bangs, style them as desired.

Tips for Cutting Thick Fringe Bangs

Tip 1: Invest in good scissors - Make sure you use sharp, high-quality scissors to cut your bangs. This will ensure a clean cut and prevent split ends.

Tip 2: Start with longer bangs - It's always better to start with longer bangs and gradually cut shorter. This will give you more control over the length and prevent any mistakes.

Tip 3: Cut your bangs when dry - It's easier to cut your bangs when they are dry. Wet hair tends to stretch, making it difficult to cut evenly.

Tip 4: Don't cut too much at once - It's better to cut a little at a time and check the length instead of cutting too much at once. This will prevent any mistakes and ensure you achieve the desired length.

FAQ about Cutting Thick Fringe Bangs

Q: Can I cut my own bangs?

A: Yes, you can cut your own bangs. However, it's important to have the right tools and technique to achieve the desired look.

Q: How often should I cut my bangs?

A: It's recommended to cut your bangs every 4-6 weeks to maintain the desired length and shape.

Q: Can I cut my bangs when they are wet?

A: It's not recommended to cut your bangs when they are wet. Wet hair tends to stretch, making it difficult to cut evenly.

Hair Care after Cutting Thick Fringe Bangs

After cutting your bangs, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the desired length and shape. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Wash your hair regularly - Regular washing will keep your hair clean and free of dirt and oil.

Tip 2: Use a conditioner - Conditioner will keep your hair moisturized and prevent split ends.

Tip 3: Style your hair gently - Avoid using harsh styling products or pulling your hair too tightly. This can damage your hair and cause breakage.

Tip 4: Get regular trims - Regular trims will keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Cutting thick fringe bangs can be a fun and easy way to add some excitement to your hairstyle. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve the perfect look you desire. Follow our step-by-step guide and use our tips and tricks for a flawless cut. Don't forget to take care of your hair after cutting your bangs to maintain the desired length and shape.
