How To Cut Wavy Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts? Do you have wavy hair that needs a trim but don't want to go to a salon? Cutting your own hair can be intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve great results. In this article, we'll share our tips and tricks for how to cut wavy hair at home.

Table of Contents

  • Supplies You'll Need
  • How to Cut Wavy Hair at Home
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Great Haircut

Supplies You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools:

  • Sharp scissors: It's important to use scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. Blunt scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Comb: A wide-tooth comb is best for detangling hair before cutting.
  • Hair clips: These will help you section your hair and keep it out of the way while you work.
  • Mirror: You'll need a mirror to see the back of your head.
  • Towel or cape: To protect your clothes from hair clippings.

How to Cut Wavy Hair at Home

Cutting wavy hair can be tricky because the waves can make the hair appear shorter or longer than it actually is. It's important to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries. Here's how to cut wavy hair at home:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry naturally.
  2. Section your hair: Divide your hair into four sections by creating a center part and then parting your hair from ear to ear.
  3. Clip the top sections of hair out of the way.
  4. Start with the bottom back section of hair: Comb through the hair and hold it between two fingers at the length you want to cut it. Use the scissors to cut the hair in a straight line.
  5. Move on to the bottom side sections of hair: Comb through the hair and hold it between two fingers at the length you want to cut it. Use the scissors to cut the hair in a straight line.
  6. Release the top sections of hair and repeat the process, starting with the back section, then the side sections.
  7. Check your work: Holding a mirror, check the back of your head to make sure the hair is even.
  8. Trim any uneven pieces: Use the scissors to trim any pieces that are longer than the rest.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Great Haircut

Here are some additional tips to help you achieve a great haircut:

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to make small cuts and check your work as you go.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries, so it's best to cut it when it's dry.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring, so take breaks if you need to rest your arms.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting wavy hair at home:

Can I cut my hair when it's wet?

We don't recommend cutting your hair when it's wet, as wet hair can shrink up to an inch when it dries. It's best to cut your hair when it's dry.

Do I need to use hair clippers?

No, you don't need to use hair clippers to cut wavy hair. Scissors designed for cutting hair are sufficient.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are some tips for healthy, wavy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

Cutting wavy hair at home is possible with the right tools and techniques. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips for a great haircut. Remember to take care of your hair with a healthy hair care routine. Happy cutting!
