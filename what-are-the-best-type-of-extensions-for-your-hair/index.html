What Are The Best Type Of Extensions For Your Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 13, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you looking for a way to add length or volume to your hair? Extensions might be the perfect solution for you! But with so many different types of extensions available, it can be hard to know which one is right for your hair type and lifestyle. In this article, we'll explore the best types of extensions for your hair, and give you tips on how to care for them.

Table of Contents

  • Tape-In Extensions
  • Sew-In Extensions
  • Micro-Link Extensions

Clip-In Extensions

Clip-in extensions are a great option if you're looking for a temporary way to add length or volume to your hair. They're easy to apply and remove, and you can do it yourself at home. Clip-in extensions come in a variety of lengths, textures, and colors, so you can easily find a match for your hair. They're also affordable, making them a great choice if you're on a budget.

How to Apply Clip-In Extensions

Step 1: Section off the top half of your hair and clip it out of the way.

Step 2: Tease the roots of the hair in the bottom half of your head to create a secure base for the clips.

Step 3: Clip the extensions onto the teased hair, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up.

Step 4: Release the top half of your hair and blend it with the extensions.

Tips:

  • Make sure to match the color and texture of the extensions to your natural hair.
  • Tease the roots of your hair to create a secure base for the clips.
  • Don't clip the extensions too close to your scalp, as this can cause discomfort.

Tape-In Extensions

Tape-in extensions are a great option if you're looking for a longer-term solution to add length or volume to your hair. They're easy to apply and can last up to 8 weeks with proper care. Tape-in extensions come in a variety of lengths, textures, and colors, so you can easily find a match for your hair. They're also comfortable and lightweight, making them a great choice for everyday wear.

How to Apply Tape-In Extensions

Step 1: Section off a small section of your hair.

Step 2: Apply the tape-in extension to the root of the section.

Step 3: Sandwich the extension with another piece of hair, using a hair straightener to seal the bond.

Step 4: Repeat the process until all extensions are applied.

Tips:

  • Make sure to match the color and texture of the extensions to your natural hair.
  • Don't apply the extensions too close to your scalp, as this can cause discomfort.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 48 hours after application to allow the bonds to set.

Sew-In Extensions

Sew-in extensions are a great option if you're looking for a longer-term solution to add length or volume to your hair. They're applied by braiding your natural hair and sewing the extensions onto the braids. Sew-in extensions come in a variety of lengths, textures, and colors, so you can easily find a match for your hair. They're also comfortable and can last up to 3 months with proper care.

How to Apply Sew-In Extensions

Step 1: Braid your natural hair into cornrows.

Step 2: Sew the extensions onto the cornrows using a needle and thread.

Step 3: Cut and style the extensions to blend with your natural hair.

Tips:

  • Make sure to match the color and texture of the extensions to your natural hair.
  • Don't braid your hair too tightly, as this can cause discomfort.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 48 hours after application to allow the braids to set.

Micro-Link Extensions

Micro-link extensions are a great option if you're looking for a longer-term solution to add length or volume to your hair. They're applied by attaching small sections of hair to your natural hair using small metal beads. Micro-link extensions come in a variety of lengths, textures, and colors, so you can easily find a match for your hair. They're also comfortable and can last up to 3 months with proper care.

How to Apply Micro-Link Extensions

Step 1: Section off a small section of your hair.

Step 2: Thread the micro-link bead onto the hair extension and slide it onto your natural hair.

Step 3: Use pliers to clamp the bead shut, securing the extension to your natural hair.

Step 4: Repeat the process until all extensions are applied.

Tips:

  • Make sure to match the color and texture of the extensions to your natural hair.
  • Don't apply the extensions too close to your scalp, as this can cause discomfort.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 48 hours after application to allow the bonds to set.

Fusion Extensions

Fusion extensions are a great option if you're looking for a longer-term solution to add length or volume to your hair. They're applied by melting a keratin bond onto your natural hair using a hot tool. Fusion extensions come in a variety of lengths, textures, and colors, so you can easily find a match for your hair. They're also comfortable and can last up to 6 months with proper care.

How to Apply Fusion Extensions

Step 1: Section off a small section of your hair.

Step 2: Apply a small amount of keratin glue to the tip of the extension.

Step 3: Use a hot tool to melt the keratin glue and attach the extension to your natural hair.

Step 4: Repeat the process until all extensions are applied.

Tips:

  • Make sure to match the color and texture of the extensions to your natural hair.
  • Don't apply the extensions too close to your scalp, as this can cause discomfort.
  • Avoid washing your hair for 48 hours after application to allow the bonds to set.

Hair Care Tips

Regardless of which type of extensions you choose, it's important to take proper care of them to ensure they last as long as possible. Here are some tips:

  • Use a soft-bristled brush to gently detangle your hair, starting at the bottom and working your way up.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your extensions, as this can cause damage.
  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent drying out the extensions.
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair, as this can cause tangling.
  • Don't pull or tug on your extensions, as this can cause them to become loose or fall out.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair extensions:

Q: How long do hair extensions last?

A: The lifespan of hair extensions depends on the type of extensions and how well you care for them. Clip-in extensions typically last up to a year, while semi-permanent extensions can last up to 6 months.

Q: Can I color my hair extensions?

A: It's not recommended to color your hair extensions, as this can cause damage to the hair and the bonds. If you want to change the color of your hair, it's best to do so before applying the extensions.

Q: Can I swim with hair extensions?

A: It's best to avoid swimming with hair extensions, as chlorine and saltwater can cause damage to the hair and the bonds. If you do swim with extensions, make sure to wash and condition them immediately afterwards.
