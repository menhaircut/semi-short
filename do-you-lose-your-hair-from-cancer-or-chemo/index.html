Do You Lose Your Hair From Cancer Or Chemo

Written by Frank Aug 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about hair loss during cancer treatment. If you or a loved one has been diagnosed with cancer, you may have heard that hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy. In this article, we will answer the question: do you lose your hair from cancer or chemo? We’ll also provide helpful tips for hair care during treatment.

Table of Contents

  1. What Causes Hair Loss During Cancer Treatment?
  2. Do All Cancer Treatments Cause Hair Loss?
  3. How Long Does Hair Loss Last?

  6. FAQs

What Causes Hair Loss During Cancer Treatment?

Hair loss during cancer treatment is caused by chemotherapy drugs. These drugs target rapidly dividing cells, including cancer cells, but also hair follicles. As a result, hair falls out from the scalp, eyebrows, eyelashes, and other parts of the body. It’s important to remember that hair loss is not a sign that the treatment is not working.

Do All Cancer Treatments Cause Hair Loss?

No, not all cancer treatments cause hair loss. Radiation therapy may cause hair loss in the area being treated, but it is typically temporary. Some newer chemotherapy drugs may also be less likely to cause hair loss. However, most traditional chemotherapy drugs do cause hair loss.

How Long Does Hair Loss Last?

Hair loss typically starts a few weeks after the first chemotherapy treatment and continues throughout the course of treatment. Hair may start to grow back a few months after treatment ends, but it may be different in texture or color. It can take up to a year for hair to fully regrow.

Can You Prevent Hair Loss During Cancer Treatment?

Unfortunately, there is no guaranteed way to prevent hair loss during cancer treatment. Some people have tried scalp cooling caps, which are worn during chemotherapy and help to reduce blood flow to the scalp. This may help to reduce hair loss, but it is not always effective. It’s important to discuss any concerns about hair loss with your healthcare provider.

How to Care for Your Hair During Cancer Treatment

Here are some tips for caring for your hair during cancer treatment:

Wash hair less often.

Washing hair less often can help to reduce hair loss. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot water or harsh chemicals on your hair.

Avoid heat styling.

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons. These can damage hair and make it more prone to breakage.

Use a soft brush.

Use a soft brush or wide-tooth comb to gently detangle hair. Avoid pulling or tugging on hair, as this can cause it to break.

Protect your scalp.

Protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather by wearing a hat or scarf. Be gentle when shampooing your scalp, and avoid using hair products that contain alcohol or other harsh chemicals.

FAQs

Does everyone lose their hair during chemotherapy?

No, not everyone loses their hair during chemotherapy. The likelihood of hair loss depends on the type of chemotherapy drugs being used.

Does hair grow back after chemotherapy?

Yes, hair may start to grow back a few months after chemotherapy ends. However, it may be different in texture or color, and it can take up to a year for hair to fully regrow.

Can I dye my hair during chemotherapy?

It’s generally not recommended to dye your hair during chemotherapy, as it may be more sensitive and prone to damage. Wait until after treatment is over to dye your hair.


Thank you for reading our blog post about hair loss during cancer treatment. Remember, hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, but it is temporary. Take care of your hair during treatment, and don’t be afraid to discuss any concerns with your healthcare provider.
