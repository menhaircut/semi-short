How To Keep Permed Hair Healthy A Step By Step Guide

Permed hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to your locks. However, it can also be quite damaging if not treated properly. In this post, we'll be sharing our top tips on how to keep permed hair healthy and looking its best.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Keep Permed Hair Healthy
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Permed Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Permed Hair Care

How to Keep Permed Hair Healthy

If you've recently gotten a perm or are considering getting one, there are a few things you can do to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to keep your permed hair healthy:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are specifically formulated for permed hair, as they will be less harsh and won't strip your hair of its natural oils.
  2. Avoid using hot tools on your hair, such as curling irons and straighteners. Permed hair is already prone to damage, and using hot tools will only make things worse. If you must use a hot tool, make sure to apply a heat protectant spray first.
  3. Get regular trims to prevent split ends. Split ends can travel up the hair shaft and cause even more damage. By getting regular trims, you'll keep your hair looking healthy and prevent further damage.
  4. Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine. If you're going to be spending time in the sun or swimming in a pool, make sure to wear a hat or use a swim cap. Chlorine can be very damaging to permed hair, so it's important to take precautions.
  5. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized. Permed hair can be quite dry, so it's important to keep it moisturized. Look for a leave-in conditioner that's specifically formulated for permed hair.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Permed Hair

Here are a few additional tips for maintaining healthy permed hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as hair dye and bleach. These chemicals can be very damaging to permed hair.
  • Don't brush your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle your hair.
  • Avoid tying your hair up in tight ponytails or buns, as this can cause breakage and damage to your permed hair.
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are a few frequently asked questions about permed hair:

How long does a perm last?

A perm typically lasts for several months, depending on how quickly your hair grows. However, the curls will start to loosen over time, so you may need to get a touch-up after a few months.

Can you color permed hair?

Yes, you can color permed hair, but it's important to be careful. Coloring your hair can be quite damaging, so make sure to use a gentle dye and follow the instructions carefully.

Can you straighten permed hair?

You can straighten permed hair, but it's not recommended. Straightening your hair can be quite damaging, and it can also cause the curls to loosen or fall out.

Permed Hair Care

Here are a few additional tips for taking care of your permed hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Consider using a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy

By following these tips, you can keep your permed hair healthy and looking its best. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if you're unsure about how to care for your permed hair.
