How to Start Freeform Dreads with Long Hair

The first step in starting freeform dreads with long hair is to stop combing or brushing your hair. This will allow your hair to naturally form into dreadlocks.

It's important to note that freeform dreads are not created with any manipulation or products. This means that you should avoid using any wax, gel, or other products that may hinder the natural formation of your dreads.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to start freeform dreads with long hair:

Step 1: Stop Combing or Brushing Your Hair

As mentioned earlier, the first step is to stop combing or brushing your hair. This will allow your hair to naturally form into dreadlocks.

Step 2: Separate Your Hair

As your hair starts to form into dreads, it's important to separate them to prevent them from combining into larger, thicker dreads.

Use your fingers to gently separate any sections that are starting to combine.

Step 3: Patience

Starting freeform dreads with long hair takes time and patience. It's important to let your hair do its thing and not try to force any manipulation or use any products that may hinder the process.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you start freeform dreads with long hair:

Tip 1: Be Patient

As mentioned earlier, starting freeform dreads with long hair takes time and patience. Don't get discouraged if your hair doesn't start to form into dreads right away.

Tip 2: Separate Your Hair

It's important to separate your hair to prevent your dreads from combining into larger, thicker dreads. Use your fingers to gently separate any sections that are starting to combine.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starting freeform dreads with long hair:

Q: Can I use products to start my freeform dreads?

A: No, freeform dreads are created naturally without any manipulation or products. Using products may hinder the natural formation of your dreads.

Q: How often should I separate my dreads?

A: It depends on your hair type and how quickly your hair is forming into dreads. As a general rule, you should separate your dreads every 2-3 days.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining your freeform dreads:

Tip 1: Wash Your Hair

It's important to keep your dreads clean to prevent buildup and odors. Use a residue-free shampoo and wash your hair once a week or as needed.

Tip 2: Dry Your Hair

Make sure to thoroughly dry your hair after washing to prevent mold and mildew from forming inside your dreads.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair

When sleeping, wear a silk or satin scarf to protect your dreads and prevent them from rubbing against your pillowcase.

Starting freeform dreads with long hair is a fun and exciting journey. Remember to be patient, let your hair do its thing, and follow these tips and tricks to maintain your dreads.
