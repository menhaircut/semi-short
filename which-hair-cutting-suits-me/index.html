Which Hair Cutting Suits Me

Written by Petter Sep 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and want to try something new? Choosing the right haircut can be daunting, especially if you've never experimented with different styles. In this article, we'll help you figure out which hair cutting suits you best.

Description

Your hair is your crowning glory, and a good haircut can enhance your features and make you feel confident. However, not all haircuts suit everyone. Your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle all play a role in determining which haircut will flatter you the most.

How to

Before you book an appointment with your hairstylist, you need to determine your face shape. The most common face shapes are oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. Once you know your face shape, you can narrow down the hairstyles that will suit you.

If you have an oval face shape, you're lucky because you can pull off almost any haircut. However, if you have a round face, you want to avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face. Instead, opt for haircuts that elongate your face, such as a long bob or a layered cut.

If you have a square face, you want to soften your features with a haircut that adds volume to the top of your head, like a pixie cut or a shaggy bob. Heart-shaped faces look great with side-swept bangs and layered cuts that add volume to the bottom of the hair. Diamond-shaped faces can benefit from short haircuts that accentuate the cheekbones.

Step by Step

Once you've determined your face shape and the haircut that suits you, it's time to book an appointment with your hairstylist. Here's what you should do:

  1. Bring a photo of the hairstyle you want. This will give your hairstylist a better idea of what you're looking for.
  2. Be clear about your lifestyle. If you're someone who doesn't have the time or patience to style your hair every day, you want a low-maintenance haircut.
  3. Communicate your hair texture. If you have curly hair, you want a haircut that works with your curls.
  4. Ask for advice. Your hairstylist is an expert and can give you suggestions on how to style your new haircut.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different haircuts. Your hair will grow back, and you can always change your hairstyle.
  • Consider your hair color. Some haircuts look better with certain hair colors.
  • Take care of your hair. A good haircut won't look great if your hair is damaged or unhealthy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about haircuts:

How often should I get a haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and the style of your haircut. Generally, it's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks.

Can I cut my hair myself?

It's not recommended to cut your hair yourself, especially if you're trying a new style. Leave it to the professionals.

Will a haircut make my hair grow faster?

No, a haircut won't make your hair grow faster. However, getting regular haircuts can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care

Finally, taking care of your hair is essential to maintaining your haircut. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suited for your hair type.
  • Don't wash your hair every day. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a heat protectant when styling your hair with heat tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, choosing the right haircut can make a huge difference in your appearance and confidence. Determine your face shape, communicate with your hairstylist, and take care of your hair to maintain your new haircut. Happy styling!
