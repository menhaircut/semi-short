How To Keep Wig From Slipping On Bald Head

Written by Daniel Jul 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're wearing a wig on a bald head, you may find that it's slipping around and not staying in place. This can be frustrating and embarrassing. Fortunately, there are several ways to keep your wig from slipping on your bald head. In this blog post, we'll cover some tips and tricks to help you keep your wig securely in place.

How to Keep Wig from Slipping on Bald Head

Here are some tips and tricks to help you keep your wig from slipping:

1. Use Wig Clips

Wig clips are small combs that you can sew onto the inside of your wig. These clips will help to anchor your wig to your head and prevent it from slipping. You can purchase wig clips at most beauty supply stores or online.

2. Use Wig Tape

Wig tape is another option for keeping your wig in place. You can apply the tape directly to your scalp or to the inside of your wig. Just make sure that you don't use too much tape, as it can irritate your scalp.

3. Wear a Wig Cap

A wig cap is a thin cap that you wear underneath your wig. It can help to keep your wig in place and also protect your scalp from irritation. Wig caps come in a variety of materials, including nylon and cotton.

4. Use Bobby Pins

You can use bobby pins to secure your wig to your head. Just be careful not to poke yourself in the scalp with the pins. You can also use hairpins or hair clips if you prefer.

5. Try a Wig Grip

A wig grip is a band that you wear around your head. It helps to keep your wig securely in place and prevents it from slipping. Wig grips come in a variety of materials, including silicone and velvet.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to keeping your wig from slipping:

1. Choose Your Method

Decide which method you want to use to keep your wig in place. You can use wig clips, wig tape, a wig cap, bobby pins, or a wig grip.

2. Prepare Your Scalp

Clean your scalp and make sure it's dry before you put on your wig. If you have oily skin, you may want to use a scalp primer or powder to help your wig stick better.

3. Put on Your Wig

Put on your wig and adjust it until it's in the right position. Make sure that it's straight and sitting securely on your head.

4. Secure Your Wig

Use your chosen method to secure your wig in place. Make sure that it's not too tight or too loose. You should be able to move your head freely without your wig slipping.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for keeping your wig from slipping:

  • Try different methods to see which one works best for you.
  • Make sure that your wig is the right size for your head.
  • Don't use too much product on your scalp, as it can make your wig slip.
  • Be gentle when putting on and taking off your wig.
  • Invest in a good quality wig that fits well and is comfortable to wear.

FAQ

Q: Can I wear a wig if I have a bald head?

A: Yes, you can wear a wig if you have a bald head. In fact, many people with hair loss wear wigs to help boost their confidence and self-esteem.

Q: How do I know if my wig is too tight?

A: If your wig is too tight, you may experience headaches or discomfort. You may also notice that your wig is slipping or sliding around on your head.

Hair Care

Remember to take care of your wig to keep it looking and feeling great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your wig regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as curling irons or straighteners.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand to help it keep its shape.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to direct sunlight or heat.

With these tips and tricks, you should be able to keep your wig from slipping on your bald head. Remember to be patient and experiment with different methods until you find what works best for you. Good luck!
