How To Get Rid Of Lice In Thick Curly Hair

Written by Bardi Jul 30, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you dealing with lice in your thick curly hair? We know it can be a frustrating and stressful situation, but don't worry - we're here to help! In this article, we'll provide you with all the information you need to get rid of lice in your hair, including step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Getting rid of lice in thick curly hair requires a bit more effort than in straight hair. Here are the steps you should follow:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a lice-killing shampoo. Look for products that contain pyrethrin or permethrin, which are both effective at killing lice.
  2. After washing your hair, use a lice comb to comb out any remaining lice and nits (lice eggs). Make sure you comb your hair thoroughly, paying extra attention to the roots and behind your ears.
  3. Repeat the shampoo and combing process every three days for two weeks. This will ensure that you get rid of any lice that may have hatched from surviving nits.
  4. Wash all of your bedding, clothes, and towels in hot water to kill any lice that may have spread.
  5. Use a vacuum cleaner to clean your furniture, carpets, and car seats. Lice can survive for up to two days off of a human host, so it's important to eliminate any potential hiding places.

It's important to note that lice-killing shampoos can be harsh on your hair, so make sure to use a deep conditioner afterwards to keep your hair moisturized.

Tips for Getting Rid of Lice

Here are some additional tips to help you get rid of lice in your thick curly hair:

  • Use a detangling spray or leave-in conditioner to make combing easier.
  • Don't share combs, brushes, hats, or other hair accessories with anyone else.
  • Consider cutting your hair shorter to make the lice removal process easier.
  • Use a natural remedy like tea tree oil or coconut oil to help kill lice and soothe your scalp.

FAQs

How do I know if I have lice?

Common signs of lice include itching, red bumps on your scalp, and the presence of nits in your hair.

Can lice live in thick curly hair?

Yes, lice can live in any type of hair. However, they may be more difficult to detect and remove in thick curly hair.

Can I get lice from my pets?

No, lice are species-specific and can only survive on humans.

Hair Care Advice

After getting rid of lice, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent future infestations. Here are some tips:

  • Use a lice-repelling shampoo and conditioner to keep lice away.
  • Wash your hair regularly with warm water.
  • Avoid sharing hair accessories with others.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and strong.

By following these steps and tips, you can successfully get rid of lice in your thick curly hair and prevent future infestations. Remember to be patient and persistent - getting rid of lice takes time and effort.
