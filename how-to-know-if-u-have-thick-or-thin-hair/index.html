How To Know If You Have Thick Or Thin Hair

Written by Daniel Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you unsure if you have thick or thin hair? Knowing the thickness of your hair can help you choose the right hair products, styling tools and cuts to enhance your hair. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of determining the thickness of your hair.

Table of Content:

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Before we dive into the details, let's understand what thick and thin hair means. Thick hair means that you have a lot of hair strands on your head, and each strand is thick in diameter. Thin hair means you have fewer hair strands on your head, and each strand is thin in diameter. The thickness of your hair can depend on various factors, including genetics, ethnicity, age, and hormonal changes.

How to

Here are some ways to determine if you have thick or thin hair:

1. Look at the Volume:

If you have a lot of hair, your hair is most likely thick. Thick hair appears fuller, with more volume and body. If your hair is flat and lacks volume, it is likely to be thin.

2. Check the Ponytail:

A ponytail can be a good indicator of hair thickness. If your ponytail is thick and feels heavy, you have thick hair. If your ponytail is thin and feels light, you have thin hair.

3. Measure the Strand:

Take a single strand of hair and measure its diameter. If it's more than 2mm, you have thick hair. If it's less than 2mm, you have thin hair.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to determine your hair thickness:

Step 1:

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.

Step 2:

Dry your hair completely using a towel. Do not use any styling products or tools.

Step 3:

Create a ponytail at the crown of your head. If the ponytail is thick and full, you have thick hair. If it's thin and sparse, you have thin hair.

Step 4:

Take a single strand of hair and measure its diameter using a ruler or tape measure. If it's more than 2mm, you have thick hair. If it's less than 2mm, you have thin hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you manage your hair based on its thickness:

For Thick Hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to enhance body and volume.
  • Avoid heavy styling products that weigh down your hair.
  • Use a blow dryer with a diffuser attachment to add volume to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain healthy and manageable hair.

For Thin Hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and thickness.
  • Avoid heavy conditioners and styling products that weigh down your hair.
  • Use a round brush while blow-drying to add volume to your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain healthy and fuller-looking hair.

FAQ

1. Can you have a combination of thick and thin hair?

Yes, it's possible to have a combination of thick and thin hair. You may have thick hair at the crown of your head and thin hair at the nape of your neck.

2. Can hair thickness change over time?

Yes, hair thickness can change over time due to hormonal changes, aging, and other factors.

3. Is it necessary to know your hair thickness?

Knowing your hair thickness can help you choose the right hair products, cuts, and styling tools to enhance your hair's natural texture.

Hair Care

Regardless of your hair thickness, taking care of your hair is essential to maintain healthy, shiny, and manageable hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and health.

Now that you know how to determine your hair thickness, you can choose the right hair products and styling tools to enhance your hair's natural texture. Remember to take care of your hair by following a healthy hair care routine, and your hair will thank you for it!
