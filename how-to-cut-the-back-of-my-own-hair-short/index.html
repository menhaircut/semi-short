How To Cut The Back Of My Own Hair Short A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Oct 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting the back of your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a stylish and professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of cutting the back of your own hair short, providing you with tips and tricks along the way.

Description

Before you begin, it is important to have the right tools on hand. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, a mirror, and a spray bottle filled with water. It is also helpful to have a friend or family member nearby to assist you if needed.

How To

Step 1: Preparation

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Once your hair is dry, use the comb to part your hair down the middle, creating two even sections. Use the spray bottle to mist your hair with water, making it easier to work with.

Step 2: Cutting the Hair

Begin by trimming the hair at the nape of your neck. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, using the comb to create tension. Cut the hair using the scissors, being careful not to cut too much at once. Continue working your way up the back of your head, taking small sections of hair and trimming them as needed.

It is important to remember to cut your hair in a straight line, using the comb as a guide. If you are unsure about the length, start by cutting less and gradually work your way up. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

Step 3: Checking Your Progress

As you work, use a hand mirror to check the back of your head. This will allow you to see any areas that you may have missed or need to touch up. If you have a friend or family member nearby, ask them to check your progress as well.

Step 4: Finishing Touches

Once you are satisfied with the length and shape of your haircut, use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven sections. You can also use the comb to create layers or texture, depending on your desired style.

Tips

  • Start by cutting less hair than you think you need to.
  • Use the comb as a guide to ensure a straight line.
  • Ask a friend or family member to check your progress.
  • Take breaks if you feel tired or frustrated.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors for the best results.

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair if I have never done it before?

Yes, you can! It may take some practice, but with the right tools and techniques, anyone can cut their own hair.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always go back and fix it. If you are unsure about how to fix it, seek the help of a professional stylist.

How often should I cut the back of my own hair?

This will depend on your individual hair type and style. As a general rule, it is recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it is important to take care of it to ensure it stays healthy and looking its best. This includes regular washing and conditioning, avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, and using a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and most importantly, have fun!
