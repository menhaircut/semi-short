How To Start Dreads On Soft Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have soft hair and have been considering starting dreadlocks, you may be wondering how to get started. Dreadlocks are a popular and low-maintenance hairstyle that can look great on all hair types, including soft hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to starting dreads on soft hair, including tips for hair care and maintenance.

Table of Contents

  • How to Start Dreads on Soft Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Start Dreads on Soft Hair

Before you start the process of creating dreadlocks, it's important to understand the basic principles of how dreads are formed. Essentially, dreadlocks form when hair is allowed to mat together and form into rope-like strands. This process is achieved through a combination of time, patience, and maintenance.

Soft hair can be more challenging to lock than other hair types, but it's still possible to create beautiful, healthy dreads. Here are some tips for starting dreads on soft hair:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Cleanse Your Hair

Before starting the locking process, it's important to cleanse your hair thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup, and follow up with a deep conditioning treatment to moisturize and prepare your hair for the locking process.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into equal-sized parts. The size of your sections will determine the size of your dreads, so choose a size that works for you.

Step 3: Backcomb Your Hair

Starting at the roots of each section, use a comb to backcomb your hair. This process involves combing your hair backwards towards your scalp, creating a tangled, matted texture. Continue backcombing each section until all of your hair has been treated.

Step 4: Twist and Roll

Once your hair has been backcombed, use your fingers to twist and roll each section of hair. This will help to further tangle your hair and encourage the formation of dreadlocks.

Step 5: Secure Your Dreads

Once you've twisted and rolled each section of hair, use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end of each dread. This will help to hold the shape of your dreads as they continue to form.

Tips for Hair Care and Maintenance

Once your dreads have been formed, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for hair care and maintenance:

  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can weigh down your hair and prevent the locking process.
  • Be gentle with your hair when washing and drying, as rough handling can cause your dreads to unravel.
  • Use a crochet hook or interlocking tool to tighten your dreads as they grow and mature.
  • Regularly wash your dreads with a residue-free shampoo to remove buildup and keep them clean.

FAQs

Can you start dreads on soft hair?

Yes, you can start dreads on soft hair. While it may take longer for your dreads to form, it's still possible to create healthy, beautiful dreads on soft hair.

How long does it take to start dreads on soft hair?

The time it takes to start dreads on soft hair can vary depending on your hair type and the locking method you use. In general, it can take anywhere from 3-12 months for dreads to fully form.

Can you wash your hair when you have dreads?

Yes, you can and should wash your hair regularly when you have dreadlocks. Use a residue-free shampoo and be gentle when washing and drying your hair to avoid causing damage or unraveling your dreads.

Starting dreadlocks on soft hair may take a bit more time and patience than other hair types, but the end result can be worth it. By following these steps and taking care of your hair properly, you can create beautiful, healthy dreads that will last for years to come.
