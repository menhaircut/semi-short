What Was The Style Of Clothing In The 1960S

Written by Bardi Aug 07, 2023 Â· 4 min read

The 1960s was a decade of significant cultural revolution, and fashion played a crucial role in expressing the new attitudes and social changes of the time. This article will explore the style of clothing that dominated the 1960s and how it influenced fashion and culture to this day.

Table of Contents

  1. Introduction
  2. Women's Clothing
  3. Men's Clothing
  4. Accessories
  5. Hairstyles
  6. Influence on Today's Fashion
  7. FAQs

Introduction

The 1960s was a time of social and political upheaval, and the fashion of the time reflected that. Traditional gender roles were being challenged, and clothing became a way to express individuality and rebellion. The styles of the 1960s were bold, colorful, and often incorporated new materials such as plastic and vinyl.

Women's Clothing

Women's clothing in the 1960s was all about breaking the rules. The traditional feminine silhouette was replaced with more form-fitting styles that emphasized the natural shape of the body. Mini skirts, hot pants, and go-go boots became popular, and bright colors and bold patterns were everywhere. The shift dress, which was simple and comfortable, was also a staple of women's fashion in the 1960s.

One of the most iconic images of 1960s fashion is the mod look, which was characterized by short, geometric dresses and bold, graphic prints. Mary Quant was one of the most famous mod designers, and her designs were worn by many of the era's most fashionable women.

Men's Clothing

Men's fashion in the 1960s was also influenced by the cultural changes of the time. Traditional masculine dress was challenged, and men's clothing became more colorful and experimental. The mod look was also popular among men, and suits became more fitted and often featured bold patterns.

Another popular style for men in the 1960s was the hippie look, which was characterized by loose, flowing clothing and natural materials such as hemp and cotton. The Beatles were one of the most influential bands of the time, and their style of collarless jackets and straight leg trousers became a popular look for young men.

Accessories

Accessories were an essential part of 1960s fashion, and they often reflected the bold, colorful style of the time. Large, statement jewelry was popular, and plastic and metal materials were often used. Sunglasses were also an important accessory, and oversized frames were popular.

Handbags became smaller and more streamlined in the 1960s, and the clutch bag became a popular evening accessory. Go-go boots were the shoe of choice for many women, and men often wore Chelsea boots or loafers.

Hairstyles

Hairstyles in the 1960s were just as bold and experimental as the clothing. The beehive, which was a high, voluminous style, was popular among women, and the pixie cut, which was short and choppy, was also a popular look. Men's hair became longer and more unkempt, with the shaggy look becoming popular among hippies.

Influence on Today's Fashion

The fashion of the 1960s had a significant influence on today's fashion. Many of the styles that were popular in the 1960s, such as mini skirts, mod dresses, and bold prints, continue to be worn today. The influence of the 1960s can also be seen in the popularity of vintage clothing and the resurgence of bohemian and hippie styles.

FAQs

Q: What was the mod look?

A: The mod look was a style of clothing characterized by short, geometric dresses, and bold, graphic prints.

Q: What were go-go boots?

A: Go-go boots were a type of boot that was popular in the 1960s. They were typically made of vinyl or patent leather and were often worn with mini skirts.

Q: What was the hippie look?

A: The hippie look was a style of clothing characterized by loose, flowing clothing and natural materials such as hemp and cotton.

Q: What were some popular hairstyles in the 1960s?

A: Popular hairstyles in the 1960s included the beehive, the pixie cut, and long, shaggy hair for men.

Q: How did the fashion of the 1960s influence today's fashion?

A: The fashion of the 1960s continues to influence today's fashion, particularly in the popularity of vintage clothing and the resurgence of bohemian and hippie styles.

In conclusion, the style of clothing in the 1960s was all about breaking the rules and challenging traditional gender roles. The fashion of the time was bold, colorful, and often incorporated new materials and styles. The influence of the 1960s can still be seen in today's fashion, and many of the styles that were popular then continue to be worn today.
