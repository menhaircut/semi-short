Description

Centre parting hairstyles have been a popular trend for decades. This hairstyle can be worn by anyone, regardless of their face shape. However, certain face shapes can look more flattering with this hairstyle. In this article, we will discuss what face shape suits a centre parting, how to part your hair in the center, step-by-step guide, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care tips for centre parting.

How to Part Your Hair in the Center

Before we discuss what face shape suits a centre parting, let us first learn how to part our hair in the center. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with shampoo and conditioner to make it clean and smooth. Use a towel to dry your hair.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to part your hair in the center.

Step 3: Find Your Natural Parting

Stand in front of a mirror and look at your hairline. Use your fingers to create a parting in the center of your hairline. This will help you find your natural parting.

Step 4: Part Your Hair

Use the end of a comb to create a straight line down the center of your head. Start from the front of your hairline and go all the way to the back of your head.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Use a hair dryer to dry your hair and style it as desired. You can add curls, waves, or leave it straight.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on what face shape suits a centre parting:

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step is to determine your face shape. The most common face shapes are oval, round, square, heart, and diamond.

Step 2: Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you are in luck! This face shape suits a centre parting perfectly. The symmetry of your face will be enhanced by the centre parting.

Step 3: Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, a centre parting can elongate your face and make it appear slimmer. This hairstyle also draws attention away from the cheeks and towards the eyes.

Step 4: Square Face Shape

For those with a square face shape, a centre parting can soften the angles of your face. This hairstyle also highlights your cheekbones.

Step 5: Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, a centre parting can balance out your forehead and chin. This hairstyle also accentuates your eyes and cheekbones.

Step 6: Diamond Face Shape

For those with a diamond face shape, a centre parting can make your face appear more symmetrical. This hairstyle also draws attention to your cheekbones.

Tips for a Perfect Centre Parting

Here are some tips for a perfect centre parting:

  • Use a tail comb for a clean, straight parting.
  • Use hairspray to keep your hair in place.
  • Blow-dry your hair in the direction of the parting to create a sleek look.
  • Add volume to your hair by teasing the roots.
  • Accessorize your hair with a headband or hair clips.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can anyone wear a centre parting?

A: Yes, anyone can wear a centre parting. However, certain face shapes can look more flattering with this hairstyle.

Q: Is a centre parting suitable for short hair?

A: Yes, a centre parting can be suitable for short hair. It can create a sleek, sophisticated look.

Q: Can I part my hair in the center if I have a widow's peak?

A: Yes, you can part your hair in the center if you have a widow's peak. You can use a tail comb to create a straight parting that avoids the peak.

Hair Care Tips for Centre Parting

Here are some hair care tips for centre parting:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair smooth and shiny.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too frequently as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using a hot styling tool.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

In conclusion, a centre parting can be worn by anyone, but certain face shapes can look more flattering with this hairstyle. If you are unsure about what face shape suits a centre parting, follow the step-by-step guide in this article. With the right styling tools and hair care tips, you can rock a perfect centre parting!
