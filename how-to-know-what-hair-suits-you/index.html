How To Know What Hair Suits You A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 11, 2023 · 4 min read

Knowing what hair suits you is crucial for creating a look that compliments your features and personality. However, with so many haircuts, styles, and colors out there, it can be overwhelming to decide what works best for you. In this guide, we’ll break down how to determine what hair suits you and give you tips for maintaining your new look.

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in determining what hair suits you is to identify your face shape. Your face shape can help you choose a haircut that will flatter and enhance your features. Here are the different face shapes and what haircuts work best for each:

Round Face

If you have a round face, choose a haircut that adds height and lengthens your face. A layered bob or a long, straight cut with side-swept bangs are great options. Avoid blunt cuts and chin-length bobs.

Square Face

If you have a square face, opt for a haircut that softens your jawline. A layered cut with soft, side-swept bangs or a long bob with layers are great options. Avoid blunt cuts and center parts.

Heart Face

If you have a heart-shaped face, choose a haircut that balances your forehead and chin. A long, layered cut or a bob with side-swept bangs are great options. Avoid short, choppy cuts and blunt bangs.

Oval Face

If you have an oval face, consider yourself lucky – you can pull off almost any haircut! However, a layered cut or a long bob with side-swept bangs are great options to enhance your features.

Step 2: Consider Your Hair Texture and Type

Your hair texture and type can also help determine what hair suits you. Here are some general guidelines:

Straight Hair

Straight hair can pull off blunt cuts and bobs. Consider adding layers for movement and volume.

Curly Hair

Curls look great in layered cuts that add volume and movement. Avoid blunt cuts and super-short styles.

Thick Hair

Thick hair can handle a lot of volume and layers. Consider a long, layered cut or a choppy bob.

Thin Hair

Thin hair benefits from shorter styles that add volume and texture. Consider a pixie cut or a layered bob.

Step 3: Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle can also play a role in determining what hair suits you. If you’re low-maintenance, choose a style that requires minimal styling. If you’re active, choose a style that can be easily tied back. If you work in a professional environment, choose a style that looks polished and put-together.

Step 4: Look for Inspiration

Once you’ve determined your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle, look for inspiration to bring to your stylist. Consider looking at celebrity hairstyles, Pinterest boards, or even consulting with a stylist for their professional opinion.

Tips for Maintaining Your New Look

Once you’ve found the perfect haircut, here are some tips for maintaining your new look:

  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims to keep your haircut looking fresh.
  • Invest in quality hair products to keep your hair healthy and shiny.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling.
  • Consider getting a gloss or color treatment to enhance your hair’s natural shine.

FAQs

How do I know if a haircut will suit me?

Determine your face shape, hair texture and type, and lifestyle to find a haircut that will enhance your features and fit your needs.

How often should I get my hair trimmed?

Visit your stylist every 6-8 weeks for trims to keep your haircut looking fresh and healthy.

What products should I use to maintain my new hairstyle?

Invest in quality hair products that are tailored to your hair type and needs. Consider using a shampoo and conditioner that’s formulated for your hair type, as well as a heat protectant spray and styling products that will enhance your new look.

Hair Care Tips

In addition to maintaining your new haircut, it’s important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on a daily basis to prevent heat damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

Knowing what hair suits you can seem overwhelming, but by following these steps and tips, you’ll be able to find a haircut that enhances your features and fits your lifestyle. Remember to consult with a stylist for their professional opinion and to maintain your new look with quality hair products and regular trims.
