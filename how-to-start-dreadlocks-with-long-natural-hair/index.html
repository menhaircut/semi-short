How To Start Dreadlocks With Long Natural Hair

Written by William Oct 11, 2023 · 4 min read
How to Start Dreadlocks with Long Natural Hair - Step by Step Guide

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and want to try something new? Dreadlocks may be the perfect solution for you! In this step-by-step guide, we’ll discuss how to start dreadlocks with long natural hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Start Dreadlocks with Long Natural Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Starting Dreadlocks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Description

Dreadlocks, also known as dreads or locs, are a hairstyle that involves the matting and twisting of hair strands. This style has been worn by many different cultures throughout history and has become increasingly popular in recent years. Starting dreadlocks with long natural hair can be a bit more challenging than with shorter hair, but it is definitely possible with the right techniques.

How to Start Dreadlocks with Long Natural Hair

The first step in starting dreadlocks with long natural hair is to make sure your hair is healthy and free of any product build-up. It’s important to avoid using any chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair to prevent damage. Once your hair is in good condition, you can start the process of creating dreadlocks.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product build-up or oils. This will help your hair to knot up more easily.

2. Towel-dry your hair and separate it into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the thickness of your hair and the size of the dreadlocks you want to create.

3. Starting at the roots, twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction until it starts to knot up. Use a rubber band or hair tie to secure the end of each section.

4. Leave your hair in these twists for a few days to allow the knots to form and tighten. You can also use a locking gel or wax to help your hair knot up more quickly.

5. After a few days, remove the rubber bands or hair ties and gently separate the sections of hair. You should now have dreadlocks starting to form.

6. Continue to twist and knot each section of hair as it grows and matures. You can also use a crochet hook or interlocking tool to tighten up the knots and keep them looking neat.

Tips for Starting Dreadlocks

- Be patient. It can take several months or even years for your dreadlocks to fully mature.

- Keep your hair clean and moisturized to prevent breakage and damage.

- Avoid using any products that contain alcohol, sulfates, or other harsh chemicals that can damage your hair.

- Wear a scarf or hat to protect your hair from the sun and wind.

- Consider getting your hair professionally twisted or interlocked to ensure the best results.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I start dreadlocks with relaxed or chemically treated hair?

A: Yes, you can start dreadlocks with relaxed or chemically treated hair, but it may take longer for your hair to knot up and form dreadlocks.

Q: Can I wash my hair after starting dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can wash your hair after starting dreadlocks, but you should avoid using any products that can leave residue or build-up in your hair.

Q: How often should I retwist my dreadlocks?

A: It’s best to retwist your dreadlocks every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once you’ve started your dreadlocks, it’s important to take good care of them to keep them healthy and looking great. Here are some tips for caring for your dreadlocks:

- Wash your hair regularly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up or residue.

- Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair moisturized.

- Avoid using any products that contain alcohol, sulfates, or other harsh chemicals.

- Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage.

With these tips and techniques, you can start your journey to beautiful, healthy dreadlocks with long natural hair. Remember to be patient, take good care of your hair, and enjoy the process!
