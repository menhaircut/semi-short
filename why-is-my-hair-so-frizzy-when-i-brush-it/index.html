Why Is My Hair So Frizzy When I Brush It

Written by Aprilia Jun 10, 2023 · 3 min read

If you're someone who struggles with frizzy hair, you're not alone. Many people experience frizz at some point in their lives, and it can be frustrating to deal with. One of the most common times that people notice frizz is when they brush their hair. So, why is your hair so frizzy when you brush it?

What Causes Frizzy Hair?

There are a few different factors that can contribute to frizzy hair, including:

  • Humidity: When there's a lot of moisture in the air, it can cause your hair to absorb that moisture and swell, leading to frizz.
  • Dryness: On the other hand, when your hair is too dry, it can become brittle and prone to frizz.
  • Damaged Hair: If your hair is damaged from heat styling, chemical treatments, or other factors, it may be more prone to frizz.
  • Cuticle Damage: When the outer layer of your hair (the cuticle) is damaged, it can't lay flat, which can lead to frizz.

Why Does Brushing Make Frizz Worse?

When you brush your hair, you're essentially pulling and stretching it. This can cause the cuticle to lift, which allows moisture to penetrate the hair shaft and lead to frizz. Additionally, brushing can create static electricity, which can make hair stand up and look even more frizzy.

How Can You Reduce Frizz When Brushing?

If you're tired of dealing with frizzy hair every time you brush, there are a few things you can do to minimize the problem:

  1. Use the Right Brush: Choose a brush that's designed for your hair type, and avoid using rough or abrasive brushes that can damage the cuticle.
  2. Brush Less Frequently: Try to avoid over-brushing your hair, as this can exacerbate frizz. Instead, brush only when necessary, and use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle whenever possible.
  3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner: Before brushing, apply a leave-in conditioner or anti-frizz serum to help smooth the hair and reduce frizz.
  4. Avoid Heat Styling: Whenever possible, avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, or curling irons, as these can damage the hair and lead to frizz.

Frequently Asked Questions About Frizzy Hair

1. Can genetics play a role in frizzy hair?

Yes, genetics can play a role in hair texture and how prone it is to frizz. If other members of your family have frizzy hair, you may be more likely to experience it as well.

2. Can diet affect frizz?

Yes, a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep hair healthy and reduce frizz. Additionally, staying hydrated can help prevent dryness, which can contribute to frizz.

3. Is there a way to permanently get rid of frizz?

No, there's no way to permanently get rid of frizz. However, there are treatments like keratin treatments or Brazilian blowouts that can help smooth the hair and reduce frizz for several months.

Final Thoughts

Frizzy hair can be frustrating to deal with, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to minimize the problem. By using the right brush, brushing less frequently, and applying leave-in conditioner, you can reduce frizz and keep your hair looking healthy and smooth.
