How To Get Your Curls Back After Permanent Straightening

Are you tired of having straight hair and want to rock your natural curls again? If you've had permanent straightening done, you may think that your curls are gone for good. But don't worry, there are ways to get your curls back without damaging your hair. In this article, we'll show you how to get your curls back after permanent straightening.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Your Curls Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Getting Your Curls Back
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Hair

How to Get Your Curls Back

If you've had permanent straightening done, your curls may be temporarily lost, but they're not gone forever. Here are some ways to get your curls back:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo: Clarifying shampoos can help remove any buildup from your hair that may be weighing it down and preventing your curls from bouncing back.
  • Deep condition your hair: Use a deep conditioner to nourish your hair and restore moisture. This will help your curls bounce back and be more defined.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it harder for your curls to come back. Try to avoid using them as much as possible.
  • Use hair rollers: Hair rollers can help you achieve bouncy curls without heat styling. Choose the size of the rollers based on the size of the curls you want.
  • Try a curl-enhancing product: There are many curl-enhancing products available that can help define your curls and make them more bouncy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get your curls back after permanent straightening:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup.
  2. Apply a deep conditioner and leave it on for at least 30 minutes before rinsing it off.
  3. Towel dry your hair gently by squeezing the excess water out. Do not rub your hair vigorously with the towel as this can cause damage.
  4. Apply a curl-enhancing product to your hair and comb it through with a wide-toothed comb.
  5. Section your hair and start rolling it up using hair rollers. Make sure to roll the hair tightly to create defined curls.
  6. Leave the rollers in for at least an hour or until your hair is completely dry.
  7. Remove the rollers gently and use your fingers to separate the curls.
  8. Finish off with a curl-defining spray to hold the curls in place.

Tips for Getting Your Curls Back

Here are some additional tips that can help you get your curls back:

  • Be patient: It may take some time for your curls to fully bounce back, so be patient and don't give up.
  • Trim your hair regularly: Trimming your hair regularly can help remove any damaged ends and promote healthy hair growth.
  • Protect your hair while sleeping: Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent friction and protect your curls while you sleep.
  • Avoid using hairbrushes: Hairbrushes can cause breakage and damage to your hair. Use a wide-toothed comb instead to detangle your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting your curls back after permanent straightening:

  • Will my curls come back after permanent straightening? Yes, your curls will eventually come back, but it may take some time and effort.
  • How long does it take for curls to come back after permanent straightening? It varies from person to person, but it can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months.
  • Can I get my curls back without damaging my hair? Yes, you can get your curls back without damaging your hair by using gentle products and avoiding heat styling tools.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Now that you've got your curls back, it's important to take care of them to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for hair care for curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your curls gently.
  • Avoid over-brushing your hair as this can break up your curls and cause frizz.

With these tips and tricks, you can get your curls back after permanent straightening and keep them looking their best. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair to achieve healthy, bouncy curls.
