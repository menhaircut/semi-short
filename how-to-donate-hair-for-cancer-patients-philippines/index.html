How To Donate Hair For Cancer Patients Philippines A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy often lose their hair due to the treatment. Donating hair can help these patients regain their confidence and feel a sense of normalcy. Hair donations are also used to create wigs for cancer patients in need. If you're interested in donating your hair for a good cause, here's a step-by-step guide on how to donate hair for cancer patients in the Philippines.

Description

Donating hair for cancer patients is a great way to show your support for those who are battling the disease. Not only does it help cancer patients regain their confidence, but it also helps them feel more comfortable during treatment. In the Philippines, there are several organizations that accept hair donations and use them to create wigs for cancer patients in need. By donating your hair, you can make a difference in someone's life.

How to Donate Hair for Cancer Patients Philippines: Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Check the Hair Donation Requirements

Before donating your hair, make sure that it meets the requirements of the organization you plan to donate it to. Most organizations require hair that is at least 10 inches long, clean, and free from any hair products. Some organizations also require that the hair is not dyed or bleached.

Step 2: Find a Hair Donation Organization

There are several organizations in the Philippines that accept hair donations for cancer patients. Some of these organizations include Hair for Hope, Philippine Cancer Society, and Hair to Share. Do some research to find an organization that you feel comfortable donating to.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair for Donation

Before donating your hair, make sure that it is clean, dry, and free from any hair products. Tie your hair into a ponytail or braid it to keep it secure. Use a rubber band to tie the hair at the top and bottom of the ponytail or braid.

Step 4: Cut Your Hair

Use sharp scissors to cut your hair above the rubber band. Make sure that the hair is cut in a straight line to ensure that it can be used to create a wig.

Step 5: Package and Send Your Hair Donation

Place your hair donation in a plastic bag and seal it tightly. Place the plastic bag in a padded envelope and send it to the organization you have chosen to donate to. Make sure to include your name, address, and contact information so that the organization can thank you for your donation.

Tips for Donating Hair for Cancer Patients Philippines

Here are some tips to keep in mind when donating hair for cancer patients in the Philippines:

  • Make sure that your hair meets the donation requirements of the organization you plan to donate to.
  • Choose an organization that you feel comfortable donating to.
  • Make sure that your hair is clean, dry, and free from any hair products before donation.
  • Cut your hair above the rubber band in a straight line to ensure that it can be used to create a wig.
  • Include your name, address, and contact information when sending your hair donation.

FAQs

Can I donate colored or bleached hair?

Some organizations may not accept colored or bleached hair. Make sure to check the donation requirements of the organization you plan to donate to before donating your hair.

How long does my hair need to be?

Most organizations require hair that is at least 10 inches long. Make sure to check the donation requirements of the organization you plan to donate to before donating your hair.

Will my hair be used to create a wig for a cancer patient?

Yes, your hair donation will be used to create a wig for a cancer patient in need.

Hair Care Tips

If you're planning to donate your hair, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
