How To Take Care Of Straight Hair Dreads

Written by Daniel Jun 11, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you've got straight hair and want to try out dreadlocks, you might be wondering how to take care of them. While dreadlocks are relatively low-maintenance, they still require some upkeep to stay healthy and looking great. In this article, we'll go over the steps you need to take to care for your straight hair dreads.

How to Make Straight Hair Dreads

To make dreadlocks with straight hair, you'll need to use a special technique called backcombing. Here's how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Section your hair into small, even pieces.
  3. Take one section of hair and backcomb it using a fine-toothed comb. This means combing the hair back towards the roots, creating a tangled, matted texture.
  4. Once you've backcombed the hair, roll it between your palms to form a tight, compact dreadlock.
  5. Continue this process on all sections of hair until you've created a full head of dreadlocks.

It's important to note that making dreadlocks can be a time-consuming process, so be prepared to spend several hours working on your hair. It's also a good idea to have a friend or professional help you with the process to ensure the best results.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Straight Hair Dreads

Once you've made your straight hair dreads, it's important to take care of them properly to keep them looking great. Here's a step-by-step guide to caring for your dreads:

  1. Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo. This will help keep them clean and prevent buildup.
  2. Avoid using heavy conditioners or oils on your dreads, as these can weigh them down and make them look greasy.
  3. Dry your dreads thoroughly after washing to prevent mold or mildew growth.
  4. Use a crochet hook or dread comb to tighten any loose hairs or sections of your dreads.
  5. Wear a scarf or hat at night to protect your dreads and prevent them from getting tangled.

By following these steps, you can keep your straight hair dreads looking healthy and beautiful.

Tips for Maintaining Straight Hair Dreads

Here are a few additional tips for maintaining your straight hair dreads:

  • Be patient. It can take several months for your dreads to fully mature and lock up.
  • Avoid using rubber bands or hair ties on your dreads, as these can cause breakage and damage.
  • If you're experiencing itching or scalp irritation, try using a tea tree oil rinse or scalp treatment.
  • Consider using a locking gel or wax to help your dreads stay in place.

By following these tips, you can ensure that your straight hair dreads stay healthy and beautiful for years to come.

FAQ about Straight Hair Dreads

Here are a few frequently asked questions about straight hair dreads:

Can you get dreads with straight hair?

Yes, it is possible to get dreadlocks with straight hair using the backcombing technique.

How often should you wash your straight hair dreads?

It's generally recommended to wash your dreads at least once a week, but you may need to adjust this based on your hair type and lifestyle.

Can you swim with straight hair dreads?

Yes, you can swim with dreads, but it's important to rinse your hair thoroughly afterwards to remove any chlorine or saltwater.

General Hair Care Tips for Straight Hair Dreads

Here are a few additional tips for taking care of your hair, whether you have dreadlocks or not:

  • Use a heat protectant when using hot styling tools like flat irons or curling irons.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can damage your hair and scalp.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair and scalp hydrated.

By following these general hair care tips, you can help keep your hair healthy and beautiful, whether you have straight hair dreads or not.
