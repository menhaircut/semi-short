How To Grow Out A High Top Fade

If you're looking to grow out your high top fade, you're in luck! With a few simple steps and some patience, you can achieve the look you're going for. In this article, we'll go over how to grow out a high top fade, as well as some tips and hair care advice.

How to Grow Out a High Top Fade

Growing out a high top fade is a process that takes time, but it's definitely doable. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Stop Getting Fades

If you're trying to grow out your high top fade, you'll need to stop getting fades. This means that you'll need to let your hair grow out some before you can start styling it in a different way.

Step 2: Trim the Sides

While you're waiting for your hair to grow out, you can trim the sides to keep them looking neat. This will also help your hair grow out more evenly.

Step 3: Experiment with Different Styles

You don't have to stick to a high top fade while you're growing out your hair. Experiment with different styles, such as a low fade or a buzz cut, to see what works for you.

Step 4: Be Patient

Growing out your hair takes time, so be patient. It may take several months or even a year to achieve the look you're going for.

Step by Step

Here's a more detailed look at the steps involved in growing out a high top fade:

Step 1: Stop Getting Fades

The first step is to stop getting fades. This means that you'll need to let your hair grow out some before you can start styling it in a different way. Depending on how short your hair is when you start, this could take several weeks or even a few months.

Step 2: Trim the Sides

While you're waiting for your hair to grow out, you can trim the sides to keep them looking neat. This will also help your hair grow out more evenly. Use clippers or scissors to trim the sides to your desired length.

Step 3: Experiment with Different Styles

While you're growing out your hair, you don't have to stick to a high top fade. Experiment with different styles, such as a low fade or a buzz cut, to see what works for you. This will also give your hair some variety and help prevent you from getting bored with the growing-out process.

Step 4: Be Patient

Growing out your hair takes time, so be patient. It may take several months or even a year to achieve the look you're going for. In the meantime, continue to trim the sides and experiment with different styles.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind while you're growing out your high top fade:

  • Be patient. Growing out your hair takes time.
  • Trim the sides regularly to keep your hair looking neat.
  • Experiment with different styles to prevent boredom.
  • Use hair products, such as pomade or gel, to style your hair as it grows out.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing out a high top fade:

Q: How long does it take to grow out a high top fade?

A: It can take several months or even a year to grow out a high top fade, depending on how short your hair was when you started.

Q: Can I still get haircuts while I'm growing out my high top fade?

A: Yes, you can still get haircuts while you're growing out your high top fade. Just be sure to communicate with your stylist and let them know that you're trying to grow out your hair.

Q: Do I need to use special hair products while I'm growing out my high top fade?

A: You don't necessarily need to use special hair products, but using a pomade or gel can help you style your hair as it grows out.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important when you're growing out a high top fade. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as a flat iron or curling iron, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and being patient, you can successfully grow out your high top fade and achieve the look you're going for.
