Description

Dreadlocks, or dreads, are a popular hairstyle that has been around for centuries. They are created by allowing the hair to mat and tangle together into long, rope-like strands. While many people think that dreads are only for those with naturally kinky or coily hair, anyone can get them, including those with relaxed hair.

If you're interested in getting long dreads with relaxed hair, you may be wondering how long it will take. The truth is, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. The time it takes to get long dreads depends on a variety of factors, including your hair type, the thickness of your hair, and how fast your hair grows.

How to

Before you start the process of getting dreads, it's important to know that it takes time and patience. Here's a general overview of the steps involved in getting long dreads:

Step 1: Start with clean hair

Wash your hair thoroughly with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or product residue. Make sure to rinse your hair well and towel dry it.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small, equal-sized squares. This will help your dreads form evenly.

Step 3: Backcomb your hair

Starting at the roots, use a small comb to backcomb each section of hair. This involves combing the hair backwards towards the scalp, which creates tangles and knots that will eventually lead to dreadlocks.

Step 4: Roll and twist your hair

Once you've backcombed your hair, roll and twist each section tightly. This will help to further tangle the hair and create the beginnings of your dreads. You can also use a crochet hook to help tighten and shape your dreads.

Step 5: Maintain your dreads

Once your dreads have formed, you'll need to maintain them regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy. This involves palm rolling and separating your dreads to prevent them from merging together.

Step by Step

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide to getting long dreads:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup or residue that may be in your hair.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small, equal-sized squares. Make sure to part your hair in a way that will allow your dreads to fall in a natural-looking pattern.

Step 3: Backcomb your hair

Starting at the roots, use a small comb to backcomb each section of hair. Work your way down to the ends of your hair, making sure to create tight knots and tangles. The more backcombing you do, the faster your dreads will form.

Step 4: Roll and twist your hair

Once you've backcombed your hair, roll and twist each section tightly. This will help to further tangle the hair and create the beginnings of your dreads. You can also use a crochet hook to help tighten and shape your dreads.

Step 5: Maintain your dreads

After your dreads have formed, you'll need to maintain them regularly to keep them looking neat and tidy. This involves palm rolling and separating your dreads to prevent them from merging together.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get long dreads faster:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup or residue in your hair.
  • Backcomb your hair thoroughly to create tight knots and tangles.
  • Roll and twist your hair tightly to help the dreads form.
  • Use a crochet hook to help tighten and shape your dreads.
  • Maintain your dreads regularly by palm rolling and separating them to prevent them from merging together.

FAQ

Here are some common questions people have about getting long dreads with relaxed hair:

Hair Care

To keep your dreads healthy, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using too much product in your hair, as this can cause buildup.
  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as this can damage your dreads.

With time and patience, you can achieve long, beautiful dreads with relaxed hair. Just remember to take good care of your hair and maintain your dreads regularly to keep them looking their best.
