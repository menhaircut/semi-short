Should Curly Hair Be Thinned Out

Written by Petter Jun 29, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it looks beautiful and unique. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. One common question that people with curly hair ask is whether or not they should thin it out. In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of thinning out curly hair and provide some tips for taking care of curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • Pros of Thinning Out Curly Hair
  • Cons of Thinning Out Curly Hair
  • How to Thin Out Curly Hair
  • Tips for Taking Care of Curly Hair

Pros of Thinning Out Curly Hair

Thinning out curly hair can have several benefits:

  • Reduces Volume: Curly hair tends to be voluminous, which can be a challenge to manage. Thinning out the hair can reduce the volume and make it easier to style.
  • Reduces Frizz: Thinning out curly hair can also reduce frizz, which is a common problem for people with curly hair.
  • Makes Hair More Manageable: Thinning out curly hair can make it more manageable and easier to style.

Cons of Thinning Out Curly Hair

While there are benefits to thinning out curly hair, there are also some downsides:

  • Can Change Curl Pattern: Thinning out curly hair can change the natural curl pattern, which can be a concern for some people.
  • Can Cause Damage: Thinning out curly hair can cause damage, especially if not done properly.
  • Can Make Hair Look Thinner: Thinning out curly hair can make the hair look thinner, which may not be desirable for some people.

How to Thin Out Curly Hair

If you decide to thin out your curly hair, it's important to do it properly to avoid damage. Here are some steps to follow:

  1. Consult with a Professional: It's always best to consult with a professional stylist who has experience with curly hair. They can help you determine the best way to thin out your hair.
  2. Use the Right Tools: A professional stylist will use special thinning shears to thin out curly hair. Make sure the stylist you choose has the right tools.
  3. Start with Small Sections: It's best to start with small sections of hair and gradually thin out more as needed.
  4. Don't Overdo It: It's important not to thin out too much hair at once as this can cause damage.
  5. Take Care of Your Hair: After thinning out your hair, it's important to take good care of it to avoid damage and maintain the health of your hair.

Tips for Taking Care of Curly Hair

Whether or not you decide to thin out your curly hair, it's important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a Wide-Toothed Comb: Curly hair is more delicate than straight hair, so it's important to use a wide-toothed comb to avoid damaging it.
  • Avoid Heat: Heat can damage curly hair, so it's best to avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  • Use a Deep Conditioner: Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a deep conditioner to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid Brushing: Brushing curly hair can cause frizz, so it's best to avoid it.

FAQ

Can I thin out curly hair myself?

It's best to have a professional stylist thin out curly hair to avoid damaging it. However, if you decide to do it yourself, make sure to use the right tools and start with small sections of hair.

Will thinning out my curly hair change the curl pattern?

Thinning out curly hair can change the natural curl pattern, so it's important to consider this before making the decision to thin out your hair.

How often should I deep condition my curly hair?

It's a good idea to deep condition curly hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

Hair Care

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and beautiful. Whether or not you decide to thin out your hair, it's important to take good care of it. Use a wide-toothed comb to avoid damaging it, avoid heat, and use a deep conditioner to keep it moisturized. With the right care, you can enjoy beautiful, healthy curly hair.
