How To Cut Straight Boy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jun 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your son's hair can be a daunting task, especially if you're not a professional hairdresser. But with the right tools and techniques, you can easily give your little boy a fresh cut without spending a lot of money at the salon. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut straight boy hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Straight Boy Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Straight Boy Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Boys

How to Cut Straight Boy Hair

Before you start cutting your son's hair, you'll need a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clipper or scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Cape or towel to protect your child's clothes

Once you have all the necessary tools, you can start cutting your son's hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your son's hair:

  1. Start by washing and combing your son's hair. Wet hair is easier to cut and combing will help you see any tangles or knots that need to be removed.
  2. Section your son's hair into four parts: the front, back, and two sides. Use hair ties or clips to hold each section in place.
  3. Start cutting from the back of the head. Use a clipper or scissors to trim the hair to your desired length.
  4. Move on to the sides and the front, using the same technique to trim the hair to the desired length.
  5. Blend the top and sides together by combing the hair upwards and using scissors to cut across the hair vertically. This will create a natural-looking blend.
  6. Trim the bangs or fringe to the desired length, using the same technique as the sides and back.
  7. Once you're done cutting, use a comb to check for any uneven areas and trim accordingly.
  8. Remove the hair ties or clips and style the hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Straight Boy Hair

Here are some tips to help you cut straight boy hair like a pro:

  • Start with a longer length and gradually trim the hair shorter. You can always go back and trim more, but you can't undo a bad cut.
  • Use a spray bottle filled with water to dampen the hair before cutting. This will make it easier to see what you're doing and prevent flyaways.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. Cutting hair can be stressful, but it's important to stay calm and focused.
  • Invest in a good quality clipper or scissors. This will ensure a clean and smooth cut.
  • Watch tutorials or ask for advice from professional hairdressers to learn new techniques and tricks.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting straight boy hair:

How often should I cut my son's hair?

It depends on how fast his hair grows and the length you prefer. Generally, boys' hair should be cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain a neat and tidy appearance.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my son's hair?

Don't panic! Mistakes happen, but they can usually be fixed. Wait for the hair to dry and then trim the uneven areas with scissors. If you're unsure, take your son to a professional hairdresser for help.

Hair Care Tips for Boys

Here are some tips to keep your son's hair healthy and strong:

  • Wash hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and moisturized.
  • Brush hair regularly to prevent tangles and knots.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, as they can damage hair.
  • Trim hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Encourage your son to eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting straight boy hair may seem intimidating at first, but with the right tools and techniques, you can easily give your son a fresh new look. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun with it!
