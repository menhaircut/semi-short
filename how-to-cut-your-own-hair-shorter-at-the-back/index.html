How To Cut Your Own Hair Shorter At The Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 08, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back of your head. But with a little practice and patience, it's possible to achieve a shorter, neater look without having to go to a salon. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut your own hair shorter at the back, along with some tips and hair care advice to ensure a successful cut.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Shorter at the Back
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut Your Own Hair Shorter at the Back

Before we get started, it's important to gather the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a handheld mirror to view the back of your head.

Once you have your tools ready, follow these steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by wetting your hair and combing it straight back.
  2. Using the handheld mirror, hold it up to the back of your head so you can see the hair you want to cut.
  3. Using your comb, separate a small section of hair at the nape of your neck. The smaller the section, the easier it will be to cut.
  4. Hold the section of hair between your fingers, with the hair between your index and middle fingers.
  5. Using your scissors, cut the hair straight across. Be sure to cut at a slight angle, so the hair doesn't look blunt.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5, working your way up the back of your head, until you've cut all the desired sections of hair.
  7. Check the back of your head in the handheld mirror to make sure the cut is even.
  8. Trim any uneven pieces with the scissors.
  9. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for a Successful Cut

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair shorter at the back:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up. It's easier to cut more hair than it is to fix a bad cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Cut at a slight angle to avoid a blunt look.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and stressful.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own hair shorter at the back:

  • Can I cut my hair while it's dry? It's best to cut your hair while it's wet, as it's easier to see the lines and angles.
  • What if I mess up? If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim a little more hair to even it out or go to a salon for help.
  • How often should I cut my hair? It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. Generally, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your own hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of moisture.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, like a blow dryer or flat iron, as they can damage the hair.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep the hair healthy and nourished.

Cutting your own hair doesn't have to be a scary or intimidating task. With the right tools, technique, and care, you can achieve a shorter, neater look at the back of your head. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it.
