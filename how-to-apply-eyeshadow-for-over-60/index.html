How to Apply Eyeshadow for Over 60 | Tips and Tricks

As we age, our skin changes, and so does our approach to makeup. One of the most significant changes is the way we apply eyeshadow. Applying eyeshadow for over 60 requires a different technique than younger women. In this article, we will share with you some tips and tricks on how to apply eyeshadow for over 60.

How to Apply Eyeshadow for Over 60

When it comes to applying eyeshadow for over 60, less is more. The key is to enhance your eyes' natural beauty without looking too overdone. Stick to neutral colors such as browns, taupes, and grays. Avoid bright colors, glitter, and shimmer. These can magnify fine lines and wrinkles and draw attention to areas we want to conceal.

Choose the Right Eyeshadow

Choose eyeshadows that are formulated for mature skin. Look for products that contain moisturizing agents and antioxidants. Cream eyeshadows are an excellent option because they are easy to apply, blend well, and don't settle into fine lines.

Use Primer

Before applying eyeshadow, use a primer to create a smooth base. A primer will help the eyeshadow last longer and prevent creasing. It will also make the colors appear more vibrant.

Invest in Good Quality Brushes

Invest in good quality brushes that are soft and gentle on the skin. Use a flat brush to apply the eyeshadow and a fluffy brush to blend it out. Avoid using your fingers to apply eyeshadow, as the oils in your skin can cause the eyeshadow to crease and smudge.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to apply eyeshadow for over 60:

  1. Apply a primer to your eyelids.
  2. Choose a neutral eyeshadow shade that complements your skin tone.
  3. Using a flat brush, apply the eyeshadow to your eyelids, starting from the inner corner and working your way outwards.
  4. Blend the eyeshadow using a fluffy brush to soften any harsh lines.
  5. Apply a darker shade of eyeshadow to the outer corner of your eyelids to add depth.
  6. Apply a highlighter shade to the inner corner of your eyes to brighten them up.
  7. Finish off with mascara to define your lashes.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to keep in mind when applying eyeshadow for over 60:

  • Use a matte eyeshadow instead of a shimmer or glitter to avoid highlighting fine lines and wrinkles.
  • Avoid using black eyeliner, as it can look too harsh. Opt for brown or gray instead.
  • Apply a light-colored eyeshadow to your brow bone to lift your eyes and make them appear bigger.
  • Use a cotton swab to clean up any mistakes or smudges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I still wear eyeshadow if I have wrinkles?

Absolutely! Just make sure to choose neutral colors and avoid anything too glittery or shimmery.

What's the best way to apply eyeshadow for over 60?

Use a primer, invest in good quality brushes, and stick to neutral colors. Apply the eyeshadow in light layers and blend well to avoid any harsh lines.

Hair Care

As we age, our hair also changes. To keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant, use hair care products that are formulated for mature hair. Look for products that contain ingredients such as keratin, biotin, and argan oil. These ingredients will help to strengthen your hair, improve its texture, and add shine.
