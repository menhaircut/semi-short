Best Hairstyles For Fine Straight Hair 2020 A Comprehensive Guide

Are you tired of struggling with fine, straight hair that never seems to hold a style? Do you find yourself constantly searching for the perfect hairstyle that will give your hair volume and texture? Look no further! In this guide, we will cover the best hairstyles for fine straight hair in 2020. From short and edgy cuts to long and flowing styles, we have you covered. Read on for our top picks and expert tips on how to achieve these looks.

The Bob

The bob is a classic hairstyle that works well with fine, straight hair. This style can be customized to fit any face shape and hair texture, making it a versatile and popular choice. Whether you opt for a sleek and straight bob or a textured and layered bob, this style is sure to give your hair the volume and body it needs.

To achieve a sleek and straight bob, start by blow-drying your hair with a round brush to smooth out any kinks or waves. Then, use a flat iron to straighten your hair from root to tip. Finish with a shine spray or serum to add a glossy finish.

For a more textured and layered bob, ask your stylist to add layers throughout your hair to create movement and volume. Use a texturizing spray or mousse to add even more texture and definition.

The Pixie

The pixie cut is a bold and daring hairstyle that works well with fine, straight hair. This short style can be customized to fit any face shape and hair texture, making it a popular choice for those looking to make a statement.

To achieve a classic pixie cut, ask your stylist to cut your hair short on the sides and back, leaving a bit more length on top. Use a styling cream or paste to add texture and definition to your hair, and finish with a hairspray to keep your style in place.

The Lob

The lob, or long bob, is a versatile and stylish hairstyle that works well with fine, straight hair. This style can be customized to fit any face shape and hair texture, making it a popular choice for those looking to switch up their look.

To achieve a classic lob, ask your stylist to cut your hair to just above your shoulders. Use a round brush to blow-dry your hair, adding volume and texture. Finish with a curling iron or flat iron to add waves or straighten your hair, depending on your desired look.

Layers

If you have fine, straight hair, adding layers is a great way to create movement and volume. Layers can be added throughout your hair, from short layers around your face to longer layers throughout your hair.

To achieve a layered look, ask your stylist to add layers throughout your hair, focusing on the areas where you want to add volume and texture. Use a texturizing spray or mousse to add even more definition.

Bangs

Bangs are a great way to add interest and texture to fine, straight hair. Whether you opt for classic blunt bangs or side-swept bangs, this style is sure to give your hair the volume and body it needs.

To achieve a classic blunt bang look, ask your stylist to cut your bangs straight across your forehead. Use a flat iron to straighten your hair, and finish with a hairspray to keep your bangs in place.

For a more relaxed and flowing look, opt for side-swept bangs. Sweep your bangs to one side of your face and use a flat iron to create a smooth and sleek look.

Curls

If you have fine, straight hair, adding curls is a great way to add volume and texture. Whether you opt for loose waves or tight curls, this style is sure to give your hair the body and movement it needs.

To achieve loose waves, use a curling iron to create soft, beachy waves throughout your hair. For tight curls, use a smaller curling iron and wrap small sections of hair around the barrel.

Updos

Updos are a great way to add sophistication and style to fine, straight hair. Whether you opt for a classic bun or a sleek ponytail, this style is sure to give your hair the volume and body it needs.

To achieve a classic bun, start by pulling your hair back into a ponytail. Twist your hair into a bun and secure with bobby pins. Use a hairspray to keep your style in place.

For a sleek and sophisticated ponytail, use a straightening iron to create a smooth and sleek look. Pull your hair back into a low ponytail and secure with an elastic. Finish with a shine spray or serum to add a glossy finish.

Braids

Braids are a great way to add interest and texture to fine, straight hair. Whether you opt for a classic French braid or a trendy fishtail braid, this style is sure to give your hair the volume and body it needs.

To achieve a classic French braid, start by dividing your hair into three sections. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section. Continue this pattern, adding more hair to each section as you go. Secure the end of your braid with an elastic.

For a trendy fishtail braid, start by dividing your hair into two sections. Take a small piece of hair from the outer edge of one section and cross it over to the other section. Repeat this pattern, alternating sides, until you reach the end of your hair. Secure with an elastic.

Accessories

Accessories are a great way to add interest and texture to fine, straight hair. Whether you opt for a headband, hair clip, or scarf, these accessories can add a pop of color and style to your look.

To achieve a classic headband look, start by placing a headband on top of your hair, just above your forehead. Use bobby pins to secure the headband in place.

For a trendy hair clip look, start by pulling a small section of hair back from your face. Secure the section with a hair clip, adding interest and texture to your look.

FAQs

Q: What is the best hairstyle for fine straight hair?

A: The best hairstyle for fine straight hair depends on your personal preferences and face shape. However, bob cuts, pixie cuts, and layered cuts tend to work well with fine, straight hair.

Q: How can I add volume and texture to my fine, straight hair?

A: Adding layers, using texturizing products, and using a curling iron or flat iron can all help add volume and texture to fine, straight hair.

Q: What are some tips for maintaining fine, straight hair?

A: To maintain fine, straight hair, use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, avoid using too many styling products, and protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray.

Hair Care Tips for Fine Straight Hair

Now that you know the best hairstyles for fine straight hair in 2020, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some expert tips on how to care for fine straight hair:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and texture to your hair.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, which can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using hot tools.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and free of split ends.
  • Try not to wash your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look flat.
