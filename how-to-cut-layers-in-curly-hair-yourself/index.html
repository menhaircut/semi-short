How To Cut Layers In Curly Hair Yourself

Written by Christine Sep 09, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Curly hair can be tricky to cut, especially when it comes to layers. But with the right tools and technique, you can achieve a beautiful layered look without having to go to the salon. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting layers in curly hair yourself.

Description

Curly hair can be challenging to work with, but with the right techniques, you can create a beautiful, layered look. Layers can add volume, texture, and movement to curly hair, making it more manageable and easy to style. By following the tips and techniques in this guide, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Layers in Curly Hair Yourself

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start cutting, you'll need to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a spray bottle filled with water. Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and create split ends.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut curly hair when it's clean and dry. Wash your hair as you normally would, then let it air dry or use a diffuser to dry it. Avoid using a brush or comb to detangle your hair, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your curls.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use your comb to section your hair into four parts: the top, sides, and back. Clip each section up and out of the way, so you can focus on one section at a time.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom of the back section and comb it straight. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Avoid cutting too much at once, as this can create uneven layers. Repeat this process for each section, working your way up to the top section.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, release your hair from the clips and check your work in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to even out the layers.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting layers in curly hair yourself:

  • Start with small sections of hair
  • Don't cut too much at once
  • Use sharp scissors
  • Work slowly and methodically
  • Check your work as you go

FAQ

1. How do I know how much hair to cut?

It's best to start with small sections of hair and cut a little at a time. You can always go back and cut more if needed, but it's harder to fix a haircut that's too short.

2. Can I cut layers in my hair if it's wet?

It's best to cut curly hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and create an inaccurate cut.

3. Can I cut my own hair if it's very curly?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if it's curly. Just be sure to follow the tips and techniques outlined in this guide, and work slowly and carefully.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair, and avoid using heat styling tools whenever possible. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your curls moisturized and frizz-free.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve a beautiful layered haircut in your curly hair without having to go to the salon. With a little practice, you'll be a pro at cutting your own hair in no time!
