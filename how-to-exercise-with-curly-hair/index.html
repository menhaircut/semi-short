How To Exercise With Curly Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver Aug 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, especially during exercise. Sweat, humidity, and friction from workout equipment can cause frizz and damage to your hair. However, this shouldn't stop you from staying active and healthy. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to exercise with curly hair without sacrificing your hair's health and beauty.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How to Exercise with Curly Hair
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQ

Hair Care

Before we dive into how to exercise with curly hair, let's discuss the importance of hair care. Curly hair is naturally drier and more prone to breakage than straight hair. Therefore, it's crucial to keep your hair moisturized and protected from damage. Here are some tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Choose a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for curly hair. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients such as shea butter, coconut oil, and glycerin. Avoid products that contain sulfates, which can strip your hair of its natural oils.

2. Deep condition regularly

Deep conditioning helps to restore moisture and strength to your hair. Use a deep conditioner once a week or as needed. Apply the conditioner to your hair, put on a shower cap, and leave it on for at least 30 minutes. Rinse with cool water.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner

Leave-in conditioners help to detangle and moisturize your hair. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your damp hair before styling.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can cause damage and breakage to your hair. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and set your tools to a lower temperature.

How to Exercise with Curly Hair

Now that you know how to take care of your curly hair, let's talk about how to exercise with it. Here are some steps:

1. Choose the right hairstyle

Choose a hairstyle that will keep your hair out of your face and off your neck. This will help to prevent sweat and friction from damaging your hair. Some good options include a high ponytail, braids, or a bun.

2. Use a sweatband

A sweatband can help to absorb sweat and prevent it from dripping onto your hairline. Choose a soft, absorbent fabric such as cotton or bamboo.

3. Protect your hair from friction

Friction from workout equipment such as weight machines and mats can cause breakage and frizz. Protect your hair by using a headband or wrapping a scarf around your head.

4. Don't touch your hair

Avoid touching your hair during exercise, as this can transfer sweat and oil from your hands onto your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for exercising with curly hair:

1. Apply a hair oil

Apply a hair oil such as coconut oil or argan oil to your hair before exercise. This will help to protect your hair from humidity and dryness.

2. Use a diffuser

If you choose to blow-dry your hair after exercise, use a diffuser attachment to minimize frizz and maintain your curls' shape.

3. Wash your hair after exercise

Wash your hair after exercise to remove sweat and oil buildup. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot water, which can dry out your hair.

FAQ

Q: Can I exercise with wet hair?

A: It's not recommended to exercise with wet hair, as wet hair is more fragile and prone to breakage. If your hair is still damp after washing, use a diffuser to dry it gently.

Q: Can I wear a wig or hair extensions during exercise?

A: It's not recommended to wear wigs or hair extensions during exercise, as they can be heavy and uncomfortable. If you must wear them, choose a lightweight and breathable option and secure them tightly to prevent them from slipping.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and activity level. If you have dry hair or exercise frequently, you may need to wash your hair more often. Aim to wash your hair at least once a week.

By following these tips and tricks, you can exercise with curly hair without sacrificing your hair's health and beauty. Remember to take care of your hair before and after exercise, and choose hairstyles and accessories that protect your hair from damage. Happy exercising!
