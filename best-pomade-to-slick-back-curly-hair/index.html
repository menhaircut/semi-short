Best Pomades for Slicking Back Curly Hair

When it comes to selecting the best pomade for slicking back curly hair, there are a few key factors to consider. First, you'll want to choose a pomade that provides a strong hold to keep your curls in place. Second, you'll want a pomade that won't dry out your hair or leave it feeling greasy or weighed down. Finally, you'll want a pomade that's easy to apply and won't require a lot of fuss or styling.

After researching and testing various pomades on the market, we've narrowed down the top picks for slicking back curly hair:

Suavecito Pomade Firme Hold

Suavecito Pomade Firme Hold is a water-soluble pomade that provides a strong hold without leaving your hair feeling heavy or greasy. It's easy to apply and can be used on damp or dry hair. This pomade is also affordable and comes in a variety of sizes, so you can choose the one that's right for you.

Reuzel Pink Pomade

Reuzel Pink Pomade is a classic oil-based pomade that provides a strong hold and a high-shine finish. It's made with natural ingredients like beeswax and petroleum, which help to nourish and protect your hair. This pomade is also water-resistant, so it will hold up well in humid or rainy conditions.

Baxter of California Clay Pomade

Baxter of California Clay Pomade is a matte-finish pomade that provides a strong hold and a natural, textured look. It's made with natural ingredients like beeswax and clay, which help to absorb excess oil and add volume to your hair. This pomade is also easy to wash out and won't leave your hair feeling greasy or weighed down.

How to Slick Back Curly Hair with Pomade

Slicking back curly hair with pomade may seem intimidating, but it's actually quite simple. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step-by-Step Guide to Slicking Back Curly Hair with Pomade

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to cleanse and moisturize your hair.
  2. Towel dry your hair until it's slightly damp.
  3. Take a small amount of pomade and rub it between your palms to warm it up.
  4. Starting at the front of your hairline, work the pomade through your hair in a backward motion, using your fingers to comb and shape your curls.
  5. Continue to work the pomade through your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly from roots to tips.
  6. Use a comb to slick back your hair, starting at the front and working your way back.
  7. Smooth any flyaways or frizz with your fingers.
  8. Allow your hair to dry completely. You can use a hair dryer on a low heat setting to speed up the process.
  9. If desired, apply a small amount of pomade to the ends of your hair to add definition and texture.
  10. Finish with a light hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Your Slicked-Back Curly Hair

Once you've achieved the perfect slicked-back style, it's important to maintain your hair's health and texture. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils and lead to dryness and breakage.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and moisturize your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause it to lose its natural curl pattern.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or using a scarf.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQ About Slicking Back Curly Hair with Pomade

Q: Can I use pomade on dry hair?

A: Yes, you can use pomade on dry hair, but it's best to apply it to slightly damp hair for easier styling and better hold.

Q: How much pomade should I use?

A: It's best to start with a small amount of pomade and add more as needed. Too much pomade can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Q: Will pomade make my hair look oily?

A: No, if you choose the right pomade and apply it correctly, it shouldn't make your hair look oily. Look for water-soluble pomades that won't leave a heavy residue on your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

In addition to using the right pomade, there are a few key things you can do to keep your curly hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners that can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as this can dry it out and cause frizz.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to moisturize and protect your hair.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair and cause damage.

With these tips and the right pomade, you can easily achieve a sleek, polished look for your curly hair. Experiment with different products and techniques to find the style that works best for you, and don't be afraid to mix and match products to achieve the perfect look.
