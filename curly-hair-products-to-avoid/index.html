Curly Hair Products To Avoid In 2023

Written by Christine Jul 17, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Curly hair can be stunningly beautiful, but it can also be a challenge to manage. Finding the right products to keep your curls looking their best can be a daunting task, especially with so many products on the market. Unfortunately, not all hair products are created equal, and some can actually do more harm than good. In this article, we'll discuss the curly hair products to avoid in 2023 to help you keep your curls healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • Silicones
  • Sulfates
  • Alcohol
  • Heat Protectants
  • Brushes

Silicones

Silicones are a type of synthetic oil that are often found in hair care products. While they can make your hair feel soft and smooth, they can also cause buildup that can be difficult to remove. This buildup can weigh down your curls and make them look limp and lifeless. If you have curly hair, it's best to avoid products that contain silicones. Look for products that are silicone-free, or use a clarifying shampoo once a month to remove any buildup.

Sulfates

Sulfates are a type of detergent that are often found in shampoos. They can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. If you have curly hair, it's best to avoid sulfates, as they can cause your curls to lose their shape and become frizzy. Look for sulfate-free shampoos, or try co-washing (washing your hair with conditioner instead of shampoo) to keep your curls moisturized.

Alcohol

Alcohol is often used in hair care products as a solvent or preservative. While some alcohols are okay for curly hair (such as cetyl alcohol), others can be drying and damaging. Avoid products that contain alcohol denat, ethanol, or isopropyl alcohol, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to become dry and brittle.

Heat Protectants

While heat protectants can be a great way to protect your hair from damage caused by heat styling tools, they can also be damaging to curly hair. Heat protectants often contain silicones, which can cause buildup on curly hair. Additionally, the high heat from styling tools can cause your curls to lose their shape and become frizzy. If you must use heat styling tools, be sure to use a heat protectant that is specifically formulated for curly hair.

Brushes

Brushes can be damaging to curly hair, as they can cause breakage and frizz. Instead of using a brush, try using a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your curls. This will help to minimize damage and keep your curls looking their best.

Hot Tools

Hot tools, such as curling irons and flat irons, can be damaging to curly hair. The high heat can cause your curls to lose their shape and become frizzy. If you must use hot tools, be sure to use a heat protectant that is specifically formulated for curly hair, and use the lowest heat setting possible.

Glitter

While glitter may seem like a fun way to add some sparkle to your hair, it can be damaging to curly hair. Glitter can get stuck in your curls, causing tangles and breakage. Additionally, the chemicals used to make glitter can be harsh on your hair and scalp. It's best to avoid glitter and other hair accessories that can be damaging to your curls.

Heavy Oils

While oils can be a great way to moisturize curly hair, some oils can be too heavy and weigh down your curls. Avoid oils that are high in oleic acid, such as olive oil and avocado oil. Instead, look for oils that are high in linoleic acid, such as grapeseed oil and safflower oil. These oils are lighter and won't weigh down your curls.

Parabens

Parabens are a type of preservative that are often found in hair care products. While they can help to extend the shelf life of products, they can also be harmful to your health. Parabens have been linked to a number of health problems, including breast cancer. If possible, it's best to avoid products that contain parabens. Look for products that are paraben-free, or make your own hair care products using natural ingredients.

Formaldehyde

Formaldehyde is a chemical that is often used in hair straightening treatments. While these treatments can make your hair straight and smooth, they can also be damaging to your health. Formaldehyde has been linked to a number of health problems, including cancer. If you're considering a hair straightening treatment, be sure to do your research and choose a treatment that is formaldehyde-free.

Conclusion

Curly hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right products, it can also be stunningly beautiful. When shopping for hair care products, be sure to read the labels carefully and avoid products that contain the ingredients listed above. By using the right products and taking good care of your curls, you can keep them healthy and beautiful for years to come.
