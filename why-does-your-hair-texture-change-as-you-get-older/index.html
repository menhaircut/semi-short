Why Does Your Hair Texture Change As You Get Older

Written by Bardi Aug 20, 2023 Â· 5 min read

As we age, our bodies go through various changes, and one of the most noticeable changes is in our hair texture. Many people experience a change in their hair texture as they get older, and this can be due to a variety of reasons. In this article, we will explore why your hair texture changes as you get older, and what you can do to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

Table of Contents

  • What Causes Hair Texture Changes?
  • How to Identify Hair Texture Changes
  • Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair

What Causes Hair Texture Changes?

There are several factors that can contribute to changes in hair texture as you age. One of the main reasons is hormonal changes. As we get older, our bodies produce less estrogen, which can lead to a decrease in oil production in the scalp. This can cause hair to become dry and brittle, and more prone to breakage.

Another reason for changes in hair texture is genetics. Some people are born with naturally curly or straight hair, but as they age, their hair may become more or less curly or straight. This is because of changes in the hair follicles, which can affect the shape of the hair shaft.

Environmental factors can also play a role in hair texture changes. Exposure to sun, wind, and pollution can cause damage to the hair, making it more prone to breakage and frizz. Using harsh chemicals on the hair, such as hair dyes and relaxers, can also contribute to changes in texture.

How to Identify Hair Texture Changes

If you're not sure whether your hair texture has changed, there are a few signs to look out for. One of the most obvious signs is a change in the way your hair feels. If it feels dry and brittle, or if it's more prone to breakage, this could be a sign that your hair texture has changed.

You may also notice changes in the way your hair looks. If it's become more frizzy or less shiny, this could be due to changes in texture. If you're not sure whether your hair texture has changed, you can consult with a hair stylist or dermatologist.

Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair

If you're experiencing changes in your hair texture, there are several things you can do to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Moisturizing Shampoo and Conditioner

As we age, our hair becomes drier, so it's important to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that contain natural oils, such as coconut oil or argan oil, which can help to nourish and hydrate the hair.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, can cause damage to the hair, making it more prone to breakage and frizz. If you must use heat styling tools, use them sparingly and always use a heat protectant spray.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

Exposure to the sun can cause damage to the hair, so it's important to protect your hair from the sun's harmful rays. Wear a hat or use a scarf to cover your hair, and use a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help to nourish your hair from the inside out. Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, which are rich in vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy hair.

FAQs

Q: Can changes in hair texture be reversed?

A: While it's not possible to reverse changes in hair texture, there are things you can do to improve the health of your hair. Using a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling, and protecting your hair from the sun can all help to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful.

Q: Can changes in hair texture be prevented?

A: While it's not possible to prevent changes in hair texture entirely, there are things you can do to minimize the damage. Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, eat a healthy diet, and protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.

Q: Should I see a dermatologist if I'm experiencing changes in my hair texture?

A: If you're experiencing changes in your hair texture and you're not sure why, it's a good idea to consult with a dermatologist. They can help to identify the cause of the changes and recommend appropriate treatment options.

Q: Can stress cause changes in hair texture?

A: Yes, stress can cause changes in hair texture. Stress can cause hair to become dry and brittle, making it more prone to breakage and frizz. If you're experiencing stress, try practicing relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing or yoga, to help reduce your stress levels.

By following these tips, you can help to keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful, no matter what your age.
