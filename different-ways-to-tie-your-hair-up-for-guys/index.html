Different Ways To Tie Your Hair Up For Guys

Written by Bardi Nov 30, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of your hair constantly getting in your face? Do you want to switch up your hairstyle? Tying your hair up can be a great solution. In this article, we’ll discuss different ways to tie your hair up for guys.

Man Bun

The man bun has been a popular hairstyle for guys with long hair. To achieve this look, gather your hair at the crown of your head and tie it with a hairband. You can leave some hair out in the front for a more casual look or slick it back for a more polished look. The man bun can be worn low or high on the head, depending on your preference.

Top Knot

The top knot is similar to the man bun, but instead of gathering all of your hair at the crown of your head, you only gather the top half. This creates a smaller knot that sits on top of your head. This hairstyle is perfect for guys who want to keep their hair out of their face but still want to show off their length.

Braids

Braids are a great way to add some texture and style to your hair. There are a variety of braids to choose from, including French braids, Dutch braids, and fishtail braids. To braid your hair, start at the top of your head and work your way down. You can braid all of your hair or just a section of it. Once you’ve finished braiding, tie it off with a hairband.

Ponytail

The ponytail is a classic hairstyle that is perfect for guys with long hair. To create a ponytail, gather your hair at the nape of your neck and tie it with a hairband. You can leave it straight or add some texture by curling or braiding it. The ponytail can be worn high or low on the head, depending on your preference.

Half Up Half Down

The half up half down hairstyle is perfect for guys who want to keep their hair out of their face but still want to show off their length. To achieve this look, gather the top half of your hair and tie it with a hairband. Leave the bottom half of your hair down. This hairstyle is great for a casual or formal look.

Messy Bun

The messy bun is a great hairstyle for guys who want a more relaxed look. To achieve this look, gather your hair at the crown of your head and tie it with a hairband. Pull out some pieces of hair to create a messy look. This hairstyle is perfect for a day at the beach or a casual night out.

Slicked Back

The slicked back hairstyle is a great way to keep your hair out of your face while still looking polished. To achieve this look, apply some pomade or gel to your hair and comb it back. You can leave it down or tie it in a low ponytail or bun. This hairstyle is perfect for a formal event or a day at the office.

Quiff

The quiff is a classic hairstyle that has been popular for decades. To achieve this look, comb your hair back and up at the front to create a small bump. You can leave the rest of your hair down or tie it in a low ponytail or bun. This hairstyle is perfect for a night out or a special occasion.

Dreadlocks

Dreadlocks are a great way to show off your personality and style. To achieve this look, you’ll need to get your hair twisted or braided and then let it grow out. The length and size of your dreadlocks are up to you. This hairstyle is perfect for guys who want a low-maintenance look.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I tie my hair up if it’s short?

Yes, you can still tie your hair up if it’s short. You may need to use bobby pins or hair clips to keep it in place.

2. How do I prevent my hair from getting damaged when tying it up?

Make sure to use a hairband that won’t damage your hair. Avoid using rubber bands or hair ties with metal clasps. You can also use a hair oil or serum to protect your hair.

3. How often should I wash my hair when it’s tied up?

You should still wash your hair regularly even when it’s tied up. Depending on your hair type, you may need to wash it every other day or every few days.

Hair Care Tips

It’s important to take care of your hair when tying it up. Here are some tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a hair oil or serum to protect your hair from damage
  • Avoid using rubber bands or hair ties with metal clasps
  • Wash your hair regularly
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Now that you know different ways to tie your hair up for guys, you can switch up your hairstyle and keep your hair out of your face. Whether you prefer a polished look or a more relaxed style, there’s a hairstyle for you.
