Asian Men s Haircut Trends 2018 A Comprehensive Guide

Are you looking to switch up your hairstyle this year? Look no further than the top Asian men's haircut trends of 2018. From classic cuts to modern styles, we've got you covered. Read on for our guide to the best Asian men's haircuts of 2018.

Classic Cuts

The classic Asian men's haircut never goes out of style. These timeless cuts are characterised by short, clean sides and a slightly longer top. The top can be styled in a variety of ways, including slicked back, messy, or textured. This look is perfect for men who want a low-maintenance haircut that still looks sharp.

One classic cut that's been making a comeback in recent years is the buzz cut. This ultra-short cut is perfect for men who want a no-fuss hairstyle. The buzz cut can be worn with or without a fade, and looks great on all face shapes.

Modern Styles

If you're looking for something a little more trendy, there are plenty of modern Asian men's haircuts to choose from. One popular style is the textured crop. This cut features short, choppy layers on top, with a fade or undercut on the sides. The textured crop is perfect for men who want a messy, disheveled look that still looks put-together.

Another modern style that's been taking the hair world by storm is the man bun. This look is characterised by long hair pulled back into a bun or ponytail. The man bun can be worn low or high on the head, and looks great with a beard or stubble.

How to Achieve These Haircuts

The key to achieving any Asian men's haircut is finding the right barber or stylist. Look for someone who has experience cutting Asian hair, as this type of hair can be thicker and more difficult to work with than other hair types.

When you're at the salon, be sure to bring a photo of the haircut you want. This will help your stylist understand exactly what you're looking for.

Classic Cut How-To:

To achieve the classic Asian men's haircut, ask your stylist for short, tapered sides and a slightly longer top. The top can be textured, slicked back, or messy. Use a small amount of pomade or wax to keep your hair in place.

Textured Crop How-To:

To achieve the textured crop, ask your stylist for short, choppy layers on top and a fade or undercut on the sides. Use a texturising spray or paste to create a messy, disheveled look.

Man Bun How-To:

To achieve the man bun, grow your hair out to a length that's long enough to pull back into a bun or ponytail. Use a hair tie or elastic band to secure your hair in place. Use a small amount of styling product to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Tips for Maintaining Your Asian Men's Haircut

Once you've achieved your perfect Asian men's haircut, it's important to maintain it properly. Here are some tips for keeping your hair looking great:

  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much styling product, as this can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

FAQs

What is the best Asian men's haircut for thinning hair?

A buzz cut or crew cut can be a great choice for men with thinning hair. These cuts create the illusion of thicker hair, and are low-maintenance.

What is the best Asian men's haircut for a round face?

A classic Asian men's haircut with short, clean sides and a slightly longer top is a great choice for men with round faces. This cut helps to elongate the face and make it appear slimmer.

What is the best way to style a man bun?

To style a man bun, simply pull your hair back into a bun or ponytail and secure it with a hair tie or elastic band. Use a small amount of styling product to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Hair Care for Asian Men

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, great-looking hair. Here are some tips for keeping your Asian hair in top condition:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot water when washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add moisture to your hair.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
