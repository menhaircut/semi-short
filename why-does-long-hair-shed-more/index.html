Why Does Long Hair Shed More

Written by Oliver Jul 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Long hair can be beautiful and versatile, but it can also be a source of frustration for those who experience excessive shedding. Shedding is a natural process that occurs when hair reaches the end of its growth cycle and falls out. However, when long hair sheds more than usual, it can be a sign of underlying issues that need to be addressed. In this article, we will explore why long hair sheds more and what you can do to minimize shedding.

  • Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding

Description

Long hair sheds more than short hair for several reasons. Firstly, long hair is older and has been subjected to more wear and tear. As hair ages, it loses strength and elasticity, making it more prone to breakage and shedding. Secondly, long hair is heavier and puts more strain on the hair follicles. The weight of the hair can cause the follicles to weaken and eventually break, leading to shedding. Thirdly, long hair is more likely to get tangled and knotted, which can cause breakage and shedding when you try to comb or brush it out.

How to Minimize Shedding

The good news is that there are several things you can do to minimize shedding and keep your long hair looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips:

1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner

Harsh shampoos and conditioners can strip your hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and prone to breakage. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for your hair type and texture.

2. Avoid heat styling tools

Heat styling tools like flat irons, curling irons, and hair dryers can damage your hair and cause shedding. Try to limit your use of these tools, or use them on a low heat setting.

3. Use a wide-tooth comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair than a brush and can help prevent tangles and knots. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being careful not to pull or tug on your hair.

4. Trim your hair regularly

Regular trims can help prevent split ends and breakage, which can lead to shedding. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed.

Step by Step Guide

If you're experiencing excessive shedding, here's a step-by-step guide to help you minimize it:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse your hair well to remove any product buildup.

Step 2: Use a wide-tooth comb

Gently comb your hair with a wide-tooth comb to remove any tangles or knots. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. This will help prevent breakage and shedding.

Step 4: Air dry your hair

Avoid using heat styling tools and let your hair air dry. If you must use a hair dryer, use it on a low heat setting.

Step 5: Trim your hair

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks, or as needed, to prevent split ends and breakage.

Tips to Prevent Hair Shedding

Here are some additional tips to help prevent hair shedding:

1. Eat a healthy diet

A healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

2. Drink plenty of water

Drinking enough water is essential for healthy hair growth. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water a day.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause shedding. Wear a hat or use a UV-protective spray when you're outside for long periods of time.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to shed hair?

A: Yes, shedding is a natural process that occurs when hair reaches the end of its growth cycle and falls out. However, excessive shedding can be a sign of underlying issues.

Q: How much hair shedding is normal?

A: On average, people shed between 50-100 hairs a day. If you're shedding more than that, it may be a sign of an underlying issue.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair shedding. When you're stressed, your body produces hormones that can disrupt the hair growth cycle.

Hair Care for Long Hair

Long hair requires extra care to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

1. Use a silk pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent tangles and knots, which can lead to shedding.

2. Avoid tight hairstyles

Tight hairstyles like braids, ponytails, and buns can put strain on your hair follicles and cause shedding. Try to wear your hair down or in loose styles.

3. Protect your hair at night

Wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet at night to prevent tangles and knots.

4. Be gentle with your hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, and be gentle when brushing or combing it.

In conclusion, long hair sheds more than short hair due to age, weight, and tangles. However, there are many things you can do to minimize shedding and keep your hair healthy and beautiful. Follow the tips and steps outlined in this article, and you'll be well on your way to healthier, stronger hair!
