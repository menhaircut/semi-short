How To Hide Front Thinning Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Nov 05, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling with front thinning hair? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people, both men and women, experience hair loss or thinning hair in the front of their head. But the good news is, there are ways to conceal it and boost your confidence. In this article, we'll show you how to hide front thinning hair with some tips and tricks.

Table of Contents

  • How to Hide Front Thinning Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Hide Front Thinning Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips to Prevent Front Thinning Hair

How to Hide Front Thinning Hair

The easiest way to hide front thinning hair is to change your hairstyle. There are several hairstyles that can help you conceal thinning hair, such as:

  • Side Swept Bangs: This hairstyle can cover up the thinning hair in the front and give a stylish look.
  • Bangs: A full set of bangs can also cover up the thinning hair in the front and give a youthful look.
  • Layered Bob: A layered bob can add volume to your hair and make it look fuller.
  • Pixie Cut: A pixie cut can give you a chic and edgy look while hiding the thinning hair in the front.

Another way to hide front thinning hair is to use hair fibers. Hair fibers are made of keratin protein, the same protein that hair is made of. These fibers cling to your existing hair and create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair. You can find them in various shades to match your hair color. Just sprinkle the fibers on the thinning areas and style your hair as usual.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to hide front thinning hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume to the roots.
  3. Create a side part or a center part, whichever suits your face shape.
  4. If you have bangs, style them to cover up the thinning hair in the front.
  5. If you don't have bangs, use hair fibers to cover up the thinning hair. Just sprinkle the fibers on the thinning areas and pat them gently to blend with your hair.
  6. Style your hair as usual.

Tips to Hide Front Thinning Hair

Here are some additional tips to hide front thinning hair:

  • Avoid using hair products that weigh your hair down, such as heavy gels and oils.
  • Use a round brush while blow-drying your hair to add volume.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hairline and make the thinning hair more noticeable.
  • Consider using a scalp concealer to cover up the visible scalp.
  • Wear a hat or a headband to cover up the thinning hair when you're in a hurry.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about hiding front thinning hair:

Can hair fibers damage my hair?

No, hair fibers are safe to use and won't damage your hair. They're made of natural ingredients and won't clog your scalp or pores.

Can I use hair fibers on wet hair?

No, it's recommended to use hair fibers on dry hair. Wet hair can make the fibers clump together and look unnatural.

Do I need to wash out the hair fibers?

No, hair fibers can be left in your hair until you wash it out with shampoo.

Hair Care Tips to Prevent Front Thinning Hair

Prevention is always better than cure. Here are some hair care tips to prevent front thinning hair:

  • Eat a balanced diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals, especially iron and biotin.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hairline.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as it's more prone to breakage.
  • Use a scalp massage brush to stimulate blood flow to your scalp and promote hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can hide your front thinning hair and boost your confidence. Remember, it's not about how much hair you have, but how you feel about it.
