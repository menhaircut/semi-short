How To Cut Short Layers On Sides Of Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you're looking to add some dimension and texture to your hair, cutting short layers on the sides can be a great option. This style is versatile and can work for a variety of hair types and lengths. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting short layers on the sides of your hair.

How to Cut Short Layers on Sides of Hair

Before you start cutting, it's important to gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips to keep sections separated. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

It's best to cut hair when it's clean and dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual and let it air dry or use a blow dryer to dry it completely.

Step 2: Part your hair

Use a comb to part your hair where you normally wear it. If you're not sure where to part your hair, try a middle part or a side part that's opposite of your usual part. This will help create more volume and texture.

Step 3: Clip the top section of your hair

Using hair clips, separate the top section of your hair from the sides and bottom. This will help you focus on one area at a time.

Step 4: Cut the bottom layer

Begin cutting the bottom layer of hair on one side. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting from the length you want your layers to begin. Repeat this process on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the middle layer

Release the middle section of hair and repeat the same process as the bottom layer. This layer should be slightly shorter than the bottom layer to create a gradual gradient effect.

Step 6: Cut the top layer

Finally, release the top section of hair and cut it to the desired length. This layer should be the shortest and will add volume and texture to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a breakdown of the steps to follow when cutting short layers on the sides of your hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair
  2. Part your hair
  3. Clip the top section of your hair
  4. Cut the bottom layer
  5. Cut the middle layer
  6. Cut the top layer

Tips for Cutting Short Layers on Sides of Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting short layers on the sides of your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Make sure to cut at an angle to create a gradual gradient effect.
  • Take breaks if your arms get tired or if you're feeling unsure about the process.

FAQs

Can I cut short layers on the sides of my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can still cut short layers on the sides of your hair if it's curly. However, it's important to cut it when it's dry and to cut it using the curl pattern as a guide.

How often should I trim my short layers?

It's recommended to trim your short layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Hair Care for Short Layered Hair

Once you've cut your short layers on the sides of your hair, it's important to take proper care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Get regular trims every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your short layers.

By following these tips and using this step-by-step guide, you can confidently cut short layers on the sides of your hair for a trendy and stylish look.
