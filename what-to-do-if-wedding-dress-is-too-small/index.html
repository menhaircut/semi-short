What To Do If Your Wedding Dress Is Too Small

Written by Luffy Sep 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Welcome to your ultimate guide on what to do if your wedding dress is too small. Your wedding day is one of the most important days of your life and you want everything to be perfect, including your dress. Unfortunately, sometimes things don't go as planned and your dress doesn't fit as well as it should. Don't worry, there are several things you can do to fix this problem. Here's a step-by-step guide on what to do if your wedding dress is too small.

Description

It's not uncommon for brides to experience dress fitting issues. Perhaps you lost some weight or your dress was ordered in the wrong size. Whatever the reason, it's important to address the issue as soon as possible to ensure that your dress fits perfectly on your big day.

How to Fix a Small Wedding Dress

If your wedding dress is too small, there are several things you can do to fix the issue. Here are some of the most popular solutions:

Alterations

If your dress is only slightly too small, alterations may be the best option. A skilled seamstress can take your dress in or let it out in the necessary areas to ensure a perfect fit. Be sure to schedule your alterations appointment as soon as possible to allow time for multiple fittings if necessary.

Corset Back

If your dress has a zipper or buttons, consider having a corset back added. This will give you more flexibility with the size of the dress and allow you to adjust it as needed throughout the day. A corset back can also add a beautiful detail to your dress.

Add Panels

If your dress is too small in specific areas, such as the bust or hips, consider having panels added. A skilled seamstress can add panels of fabric to the necessary areas, allowing your dress to fit comfortably and beautifully.

Step-By-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to do if your wedding dress is too small:

  • Contact the bridal salon or boutique where you purchased the dress.
  • Schedule an appointment to try on the dress and assess the fit.
  • If alterations are necessary, schedule an appointment with a skilled seamstress.
  • If a corset back or panels are necessary, discuss options with your seamstress.
  • Attend multiple fittings to ensure a perfect fit.
  • Pick up your dress and enjoy your perfect fit on your big day!

Tips

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind if your wedding dress is too small:

  • Don't panic! There are several solutions to this problem.
  • Address the issue as soon as possible to ensure enough time for alterations.
  • Be open to options such as a corset back or added panels.
  • Attend multiple fittings to ensure a perfect fit.
  • Remember that your dress should make you feel beautiful and confident, no matter the size.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about what to do if your wedding dress is too small:

What if my dress is too big?

If your dress is too big, alterations can still be a great option. A skilled seamstress can take your dress in to ensure a perfect fit. Be sure to schedule your alterations appointment as soon as possible to allow time for multiple fittings if necessary.

What if I can't afford alterations?

If alterations are not in your budget, consider selling your current dress and purchasing a new one in the correct size. There are also several websites and stores that sell pre-owned wedding dresses at a discounted price.

Hair Care

Don't forget about your hair on your big day! Whether you're wearing your hair up or down, it's important to take care of it in the months leading up to your wedding. Here are some tips:

  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Consider taking hair and nail supplements to promote healthy growth.
  • Schedule your hair trial appointment well in advance to ensure you love your look on your big day.

Congratulations on your upcoming wedding! We hope this guide helps you fix any dress fitting issues and feel confident and beautiful on your big day.
