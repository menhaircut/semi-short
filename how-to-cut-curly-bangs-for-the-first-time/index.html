How To Cut Curly Bangs For The First Time A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Sep 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle and want to switch things up by adding some curly bangs to your look? Cutting your own curly bangs for the first time may seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be easily done at home. In this article, we’ll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut curly bangs for the first time, along with some useful tips and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You Will Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Bangs
  • Styling Your Bangs
  • Useful Tips
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Curly Bangs

Tools You Will Need

Before you start cutting your curly bangs, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle filled with water

These tools will help you achieve the best results when cutting your curly bangs.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting your bangs. Wet hair tends to shrink, and this can affect the length of your bangs.

Once your hair is dry, use a comb to detangle your curls. This will make it easier to section your hair and cut your bangs.

Sectioning Your Hair

Divide your hair into three sections: two on the sides and one at the back. Use clips or hair ties to secure the two side sections out of the way. This will help you focus on cutting your bangs without cutting the rest of your hair.

Take the remaining section at the front of your head and comb it straight down. This will be your bangs section. Use your fingers to determine how wide you want your bangs to be. Then, use the comb to create a straight line across your forehead where you want to cut your bangs.

Cutting Your Bangs

Take the scissors and start cutting your bangs in a straight line. It’s best to start cutting your bangs a little longer than you want them to be, as you can always trim them later if needed. Remember to cut your bangs while they’re dry, as wet hair can be deceiving and may result in uneven bangs.

If you want to add some texture to your bangs, you can use the point-cutting technique. This involves cutting into the hair at an angle with the scissors, rather than straight across. This will create a more natural and textured look.

Styling Your Bangs

Once you’ve cut your bangs, you can style them as you wish. For curly bangs, it’s best to use a hair cream or gel to define your curls and keep your bangs in place. Apply the product to your bangs, scrunching them gently with your fingers to enhance your curls.

Useful Tips

  • Start by cutting your bangs a little longer than you want them to be, as you can always trim them later if needed.
  • Use the point-cutting technique to add texture to your bangs.
  • Don’t cut your bangs when they’re wet, as this can result in uneven bangs.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut my curly bangs with regular scissors?

A: While you can technically use regular scissors to cut your curly bangs, it’s best to use sharp scissors specifically designed for cutting hair. This will give you more precision and better results.

Q: How often should I trim my curly bangs?

A: This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs lose their shape. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Hair Care for Curly Bangs

To keep your curly bangs looking their best, it’s important to take good care of them. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for curly hair to keep your curls hydrated and defined.

When styling your curly bangs, avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause frizz. Instead, embrace your natural curls and use a hair cream or gel to define them and keep them in place.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own curly bangs for the first time and achieve a fresh new look. Remember to take your time, start with small cuts, and always use sharp scissors for the best results.
