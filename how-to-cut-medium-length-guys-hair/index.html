How To Cut Medium Length Guys Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Oct 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a fresh new haircut that will make you stand out from the crowd? One popular option is medium length hair for guys. This style can suit a variety of face shapes and hair types, and can be versatile enough to wear in a variety of styles. But before you try to cut your own hair, it's important to know the proper technique. In this guide, we'll go over how to cut medium length guys hair in a relaxed English language.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Medium Length Guys Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for a Great Cut
  4. FAQs about Cutting Medium Length Guys Hair
  5. Hair Care for Medium Length Hair

How to Cut Medium Length Guys Hair

The first step in cutting medium length guys hair is to gather the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep the hair damp as you work.

Before you start cutting, decide on the length you want. Medium length hair is typically around 3-4 inches long, but you can adjust this based on your personal preference. Keep in mind that it's always better to start with a longer length and gradually trim more off as needed.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, damp hair

Wash your hair and towel dry it before you start cutting. If you have curly or wavy hair, use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Comb out any tangles

Using a wide-tooth comb, gently comb out any tangles or knots in your hair. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 3: Section your hair

Divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, divide each side into two sections by parting it horizontally from ear to ear. Clip each section out of the way with hair clips.

Step 4: Start cutting

Start with one of the back sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to trim the ends, working from the bottom up. Continue working in small sections until you've trimmed all the hair in that section. Repeat on the other back section.

Move on to the front sections, starting with the side closest to you. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to trim the ends, working from the bottom up. Continue working in small sections until you've trimmed all the hair in that section. Repeat on the other front section.

Step 5: Blend the layers

After you've trimmed all the sections, use your comb to blend the layers together. This will help create a more natural look.

Tips for a Great Cut

  • Use sharp scissors to get a clean cut.
  • Don't cut too much off at once. It's better to start with a longer length and gradually trim more off as needed.
  • Work in small sections to ensure an even cut.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Take breaks to rest your arms and avoid fatigue.

FAQs about Cutting Medium Length Guys Hair

Q: Is it easy to cut medium length guys hair?

A: Cutting hair can be challenging, especially if you're not experienced. However, with the right tools and technique, it's possible to achieve a great cut.

Q: How often should I trim my medium length hair?

A: It's recommended to trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care for Medium Length Hair

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.

By following these tips, you can keep your medium length hair looking great between cuts.

Cutting medium length guys hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools, technique, and care, you can achieve a great cut that suits your personal style.
