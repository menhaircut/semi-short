How To Get Rid Of Yellow Brassy Hair At Home

Written by Frank Nov 08, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’ve ever colored your hair blonde, you know that it can be a tricky process. Not only do you have to worry about damage and upkeep, but you also have to contend with the dreaded yellow brassiness that can crop up over time. Fortunately, there are a few easy ways to get rid of yellow brassy hair at home without breaking the bank. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks for banishing brassiness and keeping your blonde locks looking fresh and vibrant.

Description

Yellow brassy hair is a common problem for those with blonde hair, particularly those who color their hair at home. The brassiness can be caused by a variety of factors, including exposure to chlorine, hard water, or even just the natural aging process. However, there are a few simple steps you can take to get rid of yellow brassiness and restore your blonde hair to its former glory.

How to Get Rid of Yellow Brassy Hair at Home

Step 1: Use a Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with blonde hair. This specialized shampoo contains purple pigments that help to neutralize yellow and orange tones in your hair. Simply swap out your regular shampoo for a purple variety, and use it once or twice a week to keep your blonde hair looking bright and fresh.

Step 2: Try a Toning Treatment

If your hair is particularly brassy, you may want to try a toning treatment to get rid of the yellow tones. There are a variety of toning treatments available, from DIY vinegar rinses to professional-grade toners. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully, and use the treatment sparingly to avoid over-toning your hair.

Step 3: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Blonde hair is notoriously prone to dryness and damage, so it’s important to use a deep conditioning treatment regularly. Look for a product that is specifically formulated for blonde hair, and use it once or twice a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Step 4: Protect Your Hair from the Elements

Exposure to the sun, wind, and other elements can all contribute to yellow brassiness in your hair. To protect your hair from damage, be sure to wear a hat or use a protective spray when you’re outside for extended periods of time. You should also avoid swimming in chlorinated water, as this can cause serious damage to your hair.

Tips for Preventing Yellow Brassy Hair

While there’s no surefire way to prevent yellow brassiness in your hair, there are a few steps you can take to minimize the risk. Here are some tips for preventing yellow brassy hair:

  • Use a color-safe shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid over-washing your hair
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair

FAQ

Q: Can I use lemon juice to get rid of yellow brassiness?

A: While lemon juice is often touted as a natural remedy for yellow brassiness, it can actually do more harm than good. Lemon juice is highly acidic, and can cause serious damage to your hair if used too frequently. Stick to professional-grade toners and purple shampoos instead.

Q: How often should I use a toning treatment?

A: It depends on the severity of the brassiness in your hair. If your hair is only slightly yellow, you may only need to use a toning treatment once every few weeks. If your hair is very brassy, you may need to use the treatment once a week or more.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy, Vibrant Hair

In addition to following the tips outlined above, there are a few other things you can do to keep your hair looking its best. Here are some hair care tips for healthy, vibrant hair:

  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using hot tools on wet hair
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate hair growth
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals

By following these tips, you can banish yellow brassiness from your blonde hair and keep it looking healthy and vibrant for years to come.
