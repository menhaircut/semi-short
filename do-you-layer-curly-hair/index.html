Do You Layer Curly Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jul 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have naturally curly hair, you might be wondering if layering is a good option for you. Layering can add volume, definition, and shape to your curls, but it's important to understand the process before you commit. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about layering curly hair, including how to do it, step-by-step instructions, tips, FAQs, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Layering Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

How to Layer Curly Hair

Layering curly hair can be a great way to add bounce and shape to your curls. However, it's important to approach it with caution, as too much layering can lead to frizz and a lack of definition. Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your curly hair:

1. Consider the Shape of Your Face

Before you decide to layer your curly hair, consider the shape of your face. Different face shapes will look better with different types of layers. For example, if you have a round face, long layers might be more flattering, while if you have a square face, shorter layers might work better.

2. Consult with a Stylist

If you're not sure what type of layers would work best for your face shape and hair texture, it's a good idea to consult with a stylist. A professional stylist can help you determine the best type of layers for your hair, as well as the best placement for those layers.

3. Choose the Right Type of Layers

There are several types of layers you can choose from when layering curly hair. Some options include:

  • Long layers
  • Short layers
  • Face-framing layers
  • Choppy layers

The type of layers you choose will depend on your hair texture, face shape, and personal style.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are the step-by-step instructions for layering curly hair:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products designed for curly hair. This will help to hydrate and define your curls, making them easier to work with.

2. Detangle Your Hair

Once you've washed and conditioned your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls. This will help to remove any knots or tangles, making it easier to layer your hair.

3. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. This will help you to work on one section at a time, making it easier to create even layers.

4. Start with the Bottom Layer

Begin layering your hair by starting with the bottom layer. Use hair scissors to cut the hair at an angle, creating a soft, layered effect.

5. Move on to the Next Layer

Once you've finished the bottom layer, move on to the next layer. Use the same technique to cut the hair at an angle, creating a layered effect. Continue working your way up the head, layering each section of hair as you go.

6. Blend the Layers

Once you've finished layering your hair, use your fingers to blend the layers together. This will help to create a seamless, natural look.

Tips for Layering Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering curly hair:

  • Use hair scissors, not regular scissors, to avoid damaging your hair
  • Start with small, subtle layers and build up to more dramatic layers over time
  • Consider using a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your curls
  • Avoid layering your hair too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage

FAQs

Can I Layer My Curly Hair at Home?

While it's possible to layer your curly hair at home, it's always a good idea to consult with a stylist first. A professional stylist can help you determine the best type of layers for your hair, as well as the best placement for those layers.

How Often Should I Layer My Curly Hair?

It's best to avoid layering your hair too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage. Generally, it's best to layer your hair every 6-12 months, depending on your hair's texture and growth rate.

Will Layering My Curly Hair Make It Frizzy?

If done correctly, layering your curly hair can actually help to reduce frizz and enhance your curls. However, if you layer your hair too much or in the wrong places, it can lead to frizz and a lack of definition.

Hair Care for Layered Curly Hair

Once you've layered your curly hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for caring for your layered curly hair:

  • Use products designed for curly hair, such as leave-in conditioners and curl-enhancing creams
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and lead to frizz
  • Sleep on a silk pillowcase to reduce frizz and prevent breakage
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your layers

With these tips and tricks, you can create beautiful, voluminous layers in your curly hair. Just remember to approach the process with caution, and consult with a stylist if you're not sure where to start.
