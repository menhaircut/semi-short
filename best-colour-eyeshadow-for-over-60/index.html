The Best Colour Eyeshadow For Over 60 Tips And Tricks

If you're over 60 and looking to enhance your eyes, choosing the right eyeshadow can make all the difference. With so many colours to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide which shade will complement your skin tone and eye colour. In this article, we'll explore the best colour eyeshadow for over 60, along with tips and tricks for application and care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Choose the Best Colour Eyeshadow for Over 60
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Eyeshadow

How to Choose the Best Colour Eyeshadow for Over 60

With age, our skin tone and eye colour can change, so it's essential to choose eyeshadow colours that complement our current features. Here are some tips for selecting the best colour eyeshadow for over 60:

Consider your skin tone

If you have fair skin, go for light shades like champagne, pink, or peach. Medium skin tones look great with bronze, taupe, and beige. Dark skin tones can rock deep blues, greens, and purples.

Match your eye colour

For blue eyes, try warm shades like orange, gold, or copper. Green eyes pop with purples, browns, and pinks. Brown eyes can handle almost any colour, but shades like navy, burgundy, and emerald will make them stand out even more.

Avoid shimmer

Matte eyeshadows are best for mature skin, as they don't accentuate fine lines and wrinkles. Avoid glitter or shimmer, as they can draw attention to crepiness.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Eyeshadow

Now that you've chosen your eyeshadow, it's time to apply it. Follow these steps for a natural, youthful look:

Step 1: Prime your eyelids

Using an eyeshadow primer will help your eyeshadow stay put and prevent creasing. Apply a small amount to your eyelids and let it dry before applying any eyeshadow.

Step 2: Apply a light base colour

Using a large eyeshadow brush, apply a light, neutral colour like beige or taupe across your eyelid and up to your brow bone.

Step 3: Add depth with a darker colour

Using a smaller brush, apply a darker shade like brown or navy to the outer corner of your eyelid and blend it into the crease. This will add depth and dimension to your eyes.

Step 4: Highlight your brow bone

Using the same light shade from step 2, apply a small amount to your brow bone just underneath your eyebrow. This will highlight your eyes and make them appear more lifted.

Step 5: Add eyeliner and mascara

Apply eyeliner to your upper lash line and mascara to your lashes. This will define your eyes and make them stand out even more.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Eyes and Skin

Now that you know how to apply eyeshadow, here are some tips for maintaining healthy eyes and skin as you age:

Wear sunglasses

Protect your eyes from the sun's harmful UV rays by wearing sunglasses whenever you're outside. Look for a pair that blocks 100% of both UVA and UVB rays.

Stay hydrated

Drinking enough water is essential for healthy skin and eyes. Aim for at least eight glasses of water per day.

Eat a healthy diet

Include plenty of fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins in your diet to nourish your skin and eyes from the inside out.

FAQs

What eyeshadow colours should I avoid?

Avoid bright, neon colours, as they can look garish on mature skin. Stick to neutral shades like beige, taupe, and brown.

Can I wear eyeliner if I have sensitive eyes?

Yes, but look for a hypoallergenic formula that's gentle on your eyes.

How often should I replace my eyeshadow?

Eyeshadow should be replaced every six months to prevent bacterial growth.

Bonus: Hair Care Tips for Over 60s

As we age, our hair can become thinner and more fragile. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy and lustrous:

Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid over-processing your hair with harsh chemicals like bleach and perm solution. Instead, opt for gentle, natural products that won't damage your hair.

Use a deep conditioner

Use a deep conditioner once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. Leave it on for at least 15 minutes before rinsing.

Get regular trims

Regular trims will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you can choose the best colour eyeshadow for over 60 and enhance your eyes for a youthful, radiant look.
