How To Easily Cut Layers In Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 23, 2023 · 5 min read

If you're looking to add some dimension and movement to your hair, cutting layers is a great option. However, going to a salon can be expensive, especially if you need frequent touch-ups. Fortunately, cutting layers in hair is something you can easily do at home with the right tools and techniques. In this guide, we'll show you how to easily cut layers in hair with step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Layers in Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair
  • FAQs About Cutting Layers in Hair
  • Hair Care After Cutting Layers

How to Cut Layers in Hair

Cutting layers in hair involves creating different lengths of hair throughout the head. This technique adds volume, texture, and dimension to the hair. Here are the basic steps:

1. Gather the Right Tools

You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section the hair.

2. Wash and Dry Your Hair

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

3. Decide on the Length of the Layers

Think about how much length you want to take off and where you want the layers to start. This will depend on your hair type, face shape, and personal preference.

4. Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into manageable sections. Start by dividing your hair into two sections, left and right.

5. Cut the First Layer

Start by cutting the first layer. Take a small section of hair at the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to where you want the layer to end. Use the scissors to cut the hair at that length.

6. Cut the Rest of the Layers

Continue cutting layers by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at the same length as the first layer. Make sure to cut each layer evenly and symmetrically on both sides of your head.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for cutting layers in hair:

Step 1: Gather the Right Tools

Make sure you have a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section the hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Shampoo and condition your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 3: Decide on the Length of the Layers

Think about how much length you want to take off and where you want the layers to start. This will depend on your hair type, face shape, and personal preference.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into manageable sections. Start by dividing your hair into two sections, left and right.

Step 5: Cut the First Layer

Take a small section of hair at the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your fingers and slide your fingers down to where you want the layer to end. Use the scissors to cut the hair at that length.

Step 6: Cut the Rest of the Layers

Continue cutting layers by taking small sections of hair and cutting them at the same length as the first layer. Make sure to cut each layer evenly and symmetrically on both sides of your head.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use a comb to blend them together. This will create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 8: Check for Symmetry

Check the layers on both sides of your head to make sure they're even and symmetrical.

Step 9: Trim the Ends

If necessary, trim the ends of your hair to make sure they're even.

Step 10: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired to show off your new layers.

Tips for Cutting Layers in Hair

Here are some tips to make cutting layers in hair easier:

Use Sharp Scissors

Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and create uneven cuts.

Don't Cut Too Much at Once

It's better to cut a little at a time and build up to the desired length. This will help you avoid cutting off too much hair.

Keep Your Hair Straight

Cut your hair while it's straight to ensure even, symmetrical layers.

Start at the Top

Start cutting layers at the top of your head and work your way down to the bottom.

FAQs About Cutting Layers in Hair

Can I Cut Layers in Curly Hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in curly hair. However, it's important to cut curly hair when it's dry to avoid uneven cuts. It's also important to cut curly hair in its natural state to ensure the layers complement the curls.

How Often Should I Cut Layers in My Hair?

How often you cut your layers will depend on how fast your hair grows and how you want your layers to look. It's generally recommended to cut layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain their shape and volume.

Can I Cut Layers in Short Hair?

Yes, you can cut layers in short hair. However, the layers may be more subtle than they would be in longer hair.

Hair Care After Cutting Layers

After cutting layers in your hair, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some hair care tips:

Use a Heat Protectant

If you use heat tools to style your hair, make sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage.

Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it hydrated and healthy.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape of your layers and prevent split ends.

By following these steps and tips, you can easily cut layers in your hair at home. Remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a beautiful, layered hairstyle in no time!
