How To Choose A Flattering Haircut Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jul 03, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of getting haircuts that don't suit your face shape or personality? Choosing the right haircut can be a daunting task, but with the right knowledge and guidance, you can choose a flattering haircut that will enhance your features and make you feel confident. In this article, we will provide you with tips and tricks on how to choose a flattering haircut that suits your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle.

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing a flattering haircut is determining your face shape. Your face shape will help you choose a haircut that will enhance your features and balance out any asymmetry. The most common face shapes are round, oval, square, heart, and diamond.

For example, if you have a round face shape, you can opt for a layered haircut that adds volume and height to your hair. If you have a square face shape, you can choose a haircut with soft layers and angles to soften your jawline.

How to Determine Your Face Shape

To determine your face shape, follow these steps:

  1. Measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline with a tape measure.
  2. Determine the length of your face by measuring from your hairline to your chin.
  3. Compare your measurements to the following face shape descriptions:
  • Round: Your face is roughly as wide as it is long, and your cheekbones are the widest part of your face.
  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, and your forehead is slightly wider than your jawline.
  • Square: Your face is roughly as wide as it is long, and your jawline is the most prominent feature of your face.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your jawline, and your chin is pointed.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones, and your forehead and jawline are narrower.

Consider Your Hair Type

Another important factor in choosing a flattering haircut is considering your hair type. Your hair type will affect how your haircut looks and how easy it is to style and maintain. The most common hair types are straight, wavy, curly, and coily.

If you have straight hair, you can choose a blunt bob or long layers to add volume and movement to your hair. If you have curly hair, you can opt for a layered haircut that enhances your curls and adds volume.

Think About Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle is also an important factor in choosing a flattering haircut. Your haircut should suit your lifestyle and be easy to maintain. If you have a busy lifestyle, you may want to choose a low-maintenance haircut that requires minimal styling.

For example, if you're a busy mom, you may want to choose a short bob or pixie cut that's easy to style and maintain. If you're an athlete, you may want to choose a ponytail-friendly haircut that keeps your hair out of your face.

Choosing the Right Length

The length of your haircut will also affect how flattering it is. The right length will enhance your features and balance out your face shape. The most common lengths are short, medium, and long.

If you have a round face shape, you can opt for a short or medium-length haircut that adds height and volume to your hair. If you have an oval face shape, you can choose a long, layered haircut that enhances your cheekbones.

Adding Layers

Adding layers to your haircut can enhance your features and add movement and volume to your hair. Layers are also a great way to balance out asymmetry in your face shape.

If you have a square face shape, you can choose a layered haircut with soft angles to soften your jawline. If you have a heart-shaped face, you can opt for a layered haircut that frames your face and balances out your chin.

Adding Bangs

Bangs can also enhance your features and add a stylish touch to your haircut. Bangs can balance out your face shape and draw attention to your eyes.

If you have a round face shape, you can choose side-swept bangs that elongate your face. If you have an oval face shape, you can opt for blunt bangs that enhance your forehead.

Maintenance

Before choosing a haircut, consider how much maintenance it requires. Some haircuts require daily styling and frequent trims, while others are low-maintenance and can be styled quickly and easily.

If you have a busy lifestyle, you may want to choose a low-maintenance haircut that requires minimal styling. If you have the time and desire to style your hair regularly, you can choose a more high-maintenance haircut.

Consultation with Your Stylist

Before getting a haircut, it's important to consult with your stylist. Your stylist can provide you with personalized advice on what haircut will suit your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle.

Bring pictures of haircuts you like and be honest about your styling preferences and maintenance routine. Your stylist can also provide you with tips on how to style and maintain your new haircut.

Frequently Asked Questions

What haircut suits a round face?

A layered haircut with volume and height will flatter a round face shape. Avoid haircuts that add width to your face, such as blunt bobs and straight bangs.

What haircut suits a square face?

A layered haircut with soft angles will soften a square jawline. Avoid haircuts that add width to your face, such as blunt bobs and straight bangs.

What haircut suits an oval face?

An oval face shape can pull off most haircuts. A long, layered haircut that enhances your cheekbones is a great option.

Hair Care Tips

After getting a new haircut, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and style. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to maintain its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair sunscreen.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut's shape and prevent split ends.

Choosing a flattering haircut can be a fun and exciting experience. By considering your face shape, hair type, lifestyle, and maintenance routine, you can choose a haircut that enhances your features and makes you feel confident.
