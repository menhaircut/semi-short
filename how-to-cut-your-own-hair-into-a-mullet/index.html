How To Cut Your Own Hair Into A Mullet A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jun 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you feeling adventurous and want to try out a new haircut? Why not try out the iconic mullet hairstyle? This 80s-inspired haircut is making a comeback and is perfect for those who want to add some edge to their look. Plus, you can save money by cutting your own hair! In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to cut your own hair into a mullet.

Description

The mullet is a hairstyle that is shorter on the top and sides and longer in the back. It's a bold, edgy look that has been popularized by many celebrities, including Billy Ray Cyrus and Joe Exotic. While it may seem like a difficult haircut to achieve, it's actually quite easy, especially if you follow our guide.

How to Cut Your Own Hair into a Mullet

Step 1: Gather your tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of scissors, hair clippers, a comb, and hair ties or clips to section your hair.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will make it easier to cut and style. So, wash and dry your hair before you start cutting.

Step 3: Section your hair

Using your comb, section your hair into three parts: the top, sides, and back. Use hair ties or clips to keep each section separate.

Step 4: Cut the sides

Using your hair clippers, start cutting the sides of your hair. Use a shorter guard to create a fade, or a longer guard for a more gradual transition. Start at the bottom and work your way up, blending the hair as you go. Repeat on the other side.

Step 5: Cut the top

Take the top section of your hair and comb it straight up. Hold it between your fingers and cut straight across, trimming off any split ends or excess length. You can also add layers and texture by cutting into the hair with your scissors.

Step 6: Cut the back

For the back section, comb your hair straight down and use your scissors to cut the length. Start at the bottom and work your way up, cutting in a straight line. You can also add layers and texture to the back by using your scissors to cut into the hair.

Step 7: Blend the sections

Once you've cut all three sections, it's time to blend them together. Use your scissors to trim any uneven areas and to blend the hair between the sections. Take your time with this step to ensure a seamless transition between the sections.

Step 8: Style your mullet

Now that your hair is cut, it's time to style your mullet. You can use hair gel or wax to add texture and hold, or you can leave it natural for a more relaxed look.

Tips

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if you want a more dramatic mullet
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your hair as you cut
  • Take breaks if you need to, cutting your own hair can be tiring

FAQ

Can I cut my own hair into a mullet?

Yes, you can! With the right tools and our step-by-step guide, cutting your own hair into a mullet is easy and fun.

What kind of hair is best for a mullet?

A mullet can work on any hair type, but it's best for those with straight or wavy hair. Curly hair can be more difficult to style in a mullet.

How often do I need to trim my mullet?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want to keep your mullet. Generally, you should trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape.

Hair Care

Now that you have a mullet, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Use a shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type, and avoid using too much heat when styling your hair. Regular trims will also help keep your hair looking its best.
