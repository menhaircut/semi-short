How To Make Your Natural Hair Smell Good Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by Eveline Sep 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your natural hair smelling bad? Does your hair have an odor that just won't go away, no matter how much you wash it? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with making their natural hair smell good, but there are some simple tips and tricks to help you achieve fresh-smelling locks. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to make your natural hair smell good, from hair care to DIY remedies.

Table of Contents

  • Hair Care
  • How To
  • Tips
  • FAQ

Hair Care

The first step in making your natural hair smell good is proper hair care. Keeping your hair clean and healthy is essential for eliminating odors. Here are some tips for proper hair care:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly is crucial for maintaining a fresh scent. How often you should wash your hair depends on your hair type, but generally, washing your hair 2-3 times a week is recommended. Use a gentle shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils.

Condition Your Hair

Conditioning your hair is just as important as washing it. A good conditioner will help keep your hair moisturized and smelling great. Be sure to choose a conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.

Avoid Overstyling

Overstyling your hair can lead to buildup and odors. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as well as hair products like gels and hairsprays.

How To

Now that you know the importance of proper hair care, let's talk about how to make your natural hair smell good. Here are some easy DIY remedies:

Apple Cider Vinegar Rinse

Mix 1 part apple cider vinegar with 2 parts water and pour it over your hair after shampooing. Let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out with water. Apple cider vinegar helps to balance the pH of your scalp, which can eliminate odors.

Lemon Juice and Water Spray

Mix equal parts lemon juice and water in a spray bottle and spritz it over your hair. The citric acid in lemon juice can help to neutralize odors.

Baking Soda and Water Paste

Mix baking soda and water to create a paste and apply it to your hair. Let it sit for a few minutes before rinsing it out with water. Baking soda is a natural deodorizer and can help to eliminate odors.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for making your natural hair smell good:

  • Avoid smoking and being around smokers, as the smoke can cling to your hair and leave a bad odor.
  • Avoid using too much hair oil, as it can attract dirt and odors.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent your hair from absorbing odors while you sleep.
  • Try using essential oils like lavender or tea tree oil to add a fresh scent to your hair.

FAQ

Q: Why does my natural hair smell bad?

A: There are many reasons why your natural hair may have an odor, including sweat, product buildup, and bacteria on the scalp.

Q: How often should I wash my natural hair?

A: How often you should wash your hair depends on your hair type, but generally, washing your hair 2-3 times a week is recommended.

Q: Can I use perfume on my hair to make it smell good?

A: It's not recommended to use perfume directly on your hair, as it can be too harsh and drying. Instead, try using a hair mist or essential oils to add a fresh scent to your hair.

With these tips and tricks, you can make your natural hair smell good and feel fresh. Remember, proper hair care is the key to eliminating odors, so be sure to take care of your hair with regular washing and conditioning. Try out some of these DIY remedies and see which ones work best for you. Happy styling!
