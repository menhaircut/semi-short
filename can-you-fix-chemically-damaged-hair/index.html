Can You Fix Chemically Damaged Hair

Written by Aprilia Nov 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're someone who regularly colors, perms, or relaxes their hair, you know how important it is to maintain the health of your hair. Unfortunately, chemically treating your hair can leave it damaged, dry, and brittle. But the good news is that in most cases, you can fix chemically damaged hair.

Description

Chemically damaged hair is hair that has been weakened by chemical treatments such as coloring, perming, or relaxing. These treatments can damage the hair's cuticle, which can make it more susceptible to breakage, split ends, and frizz. Over time, this damage can become more severe, making it harder to repair.

How to Fix Chemically Damaged Hair

The key to fixing chemically damaged hair is to nourish and repair the hair from the inside out. Here's how:

1. Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help strengthen the hair and prevent further damage. Look for a treatment that contains keratin, which is a protein that helps repair and protect the hair.

2. Deep Condition Regularly

Deep conditioning treatments can help moisturize and nourish the hair. Look for a treatment that contains ingredients such as coconut oil, argan oil, or shea butter.

3. Trim Split Ends

Trimming split ends regularly can help prevent further damage and breakage. It's important to get regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons can further damage chemically treated hair. Try to avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before using them.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Assess the Damage

Before you can start repairing your chemically damaged hair, you need to assess the damage. Look for signs of dryness, breakage, split ends, and frizz.

Step 2: Use a Protein Treatment

Apply a protein treatment to your hair according to the package instructions. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out.

Step 3: Deep Condition

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair according to the package instructions. Leave it on for the recommended amount of time, then rinse it out.

Step 4: Trim Split Ends

Trim any split ends using hair cutting scissors. Only cut the very tips of the hair to avoid making the split ends worse.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to help prevent further damage.

Tips for Fixing Chemically Damaged Hair

Here are a few additional tips for fixing chemically damaged hair:

1. Use a Sulfate-Free Shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on chemically treated hair, so use a sulfate-free shampoo to help keep your hair healthy.

2. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can damage your hair, so wear a hat or use a hair protectant spray when you're outside for extended periods of time.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help promote hair growth and repair. Eat plenty of fruits, vegetables, and protein-rich foods.

FAQs

Q. Can you fix chemically damaged hair?

A. Yes, in most cases you can fix chemically damaged hair with the right treatments and care.

Q. How long does it take to fix chemically damaged hair?

A. It can take several weeks or months to fully repair chemically damaged hair, depending on the severity of the damage.

Q. Can you color chemically damaged hair?

A. It's best to avoid coloring chemically damaged hair until it's been fully repaired. Coloring can further damage the hair.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

If you want healthy hair, it's important to take care of it. Here are a few tips:

- Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
- Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
- Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible
- Get regular trims to prevent split ends
- Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals

By following these tips and using the right treatments, you can fix chemically damaged hair and keep your hair healthy and strong.
