What Are The 3 Layers Of Hair

Written by Bardi Nov 20, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Understanding the different layers of your hair is essential for proper hair care. Each layer has its own purpose and function, and knowing how to care for each one can help you achieve healthy, shiny, and beautiful hair. In this article, we will explore the three layers of hair and share some tips on how to care for them.

Table of Contents

  • Epidermis
  • Cortex
  • Medulla
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQ

1. Epidermis

The epidermis is the outermost layer of the hair shaft. It serves as a protective barrier against external factors such as UV radiation, heat, and chemicals. The epidermis is made up of flat, thin cells called cuticles, which overlap like the shingles on a roof. The cuticles protect the inner layers of the hair and help to retain moisture.

When the cuticles are healthy, they lay flat and smooth, giving your hair a shiny and healthy appearance. However, if the cuticles are damaged or lifted, your hair can become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Common causes of cuticle damage include heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental factors such as sun exposure and pollution.

2. Cortex

The cortex is the middle layer of the hair shaft. It is responsible for the hair's strength, color, and texture. The cortex contains long, twisted protein structures called keratin fibers. These fibers give the hair its elasticity and help it to withstand tension and stretching.

The cortex also contains melanin, which gives your hair its natural color. As we age, the melanin production in our hair decreases, leading to gray hair. Chemical treatments such as hair dye can also affect the melanin in your hair and change its color.

3. Medulla

The medulla is the innermost layer of the hair shaft. It is a small, central core that runs through the middle of the hair. Not all hair types have a medulla, and its function is not fully understood. Some experts believe that the medulla helps to regulate the hair's moisture levels and temperature, while others think it has no significant function.

Hair Care Tips

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, it's essential to care for each layer properly. Here are some tips:

Epidermis:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals such as bleach or relaxers.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent cuticle damage.
  • Protect your hair from UV radiation by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

Cortex:

  • Minimize heat styling and use a heat protectant spray when necessary.
  • Use a protein-rich hair mask to strengthen the cortex.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Medulla:

  • Nothing specific is required for medulla care, but keeping your hair healthy overall will benefit all layers of your hair.

FAQ

What is the purpose of the hair cuticle?

The cuticle protects the inner layers of the hair from damage and helps to retain moisture. It also gives the hair its shiny and healthy appearance.

What causes cuticle damage?

Common causes of cuticle damage include heat styling, chemical treatments, and environmental factors such as sun exposure and pollution.

What is the function of the hair cortex?

The cortex is responsible for the hair's strength, color, and texture. It contains keratin fibers that give the hair its elasticity and help it to withstand tension and stretching.

Do all hair types have a medulla?

No, not all hair types have a medulla. The function of the medulla is not fully understood, and some experts believe it has no significant function.

How can I keep my hair healthy overall?

Eat a healthy diet rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals, and avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and protect your hair from UV radiation.
