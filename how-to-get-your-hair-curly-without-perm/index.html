Method 1: Twist Outs

Twist outs are a simple and effective way to get defined curls without heat or chemicals. Hereâs how to do it:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly.
  4. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on low heat.
  5. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the twists and fluff your curls.

This method works best for those with naturally curly or wavy hair.

Method 2: Bantu Knots

Bantu knots are a traditional African hairstyle that can create beautiful, defined curls. Hereâs how to do it:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections and twist each section tightly.
  4. Wrap each twist around itself to create a small knot.
  5. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on low heat.
  6. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the knots and fluff your curls.

This method works best for those with medium to long hair length.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the methods, hereâs a step-by-step guide to help you get curly hair without a perm:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your damp hair.
  3. Divide your hair into small sections.
  4. Choose your preferred method (twist outs or bantu knots) and twist or knot each section.
  5. Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on low heat.
  6. Once your hair is completely dry, unravel the twists or knots and fluff your curls.
  7. Apply a curl-defining cream or gel to set your curls.
  8. Enjoy your new curly hair!

Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing or combing your curls when they are dry as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.

  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a regular basis.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or swim cap.
