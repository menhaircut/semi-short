How To Wear Thinning Hair Up Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Jul 04, 2023 · 5 min read

Thinning hair can be a real challenge, but there are ways to style it that can help you feel confident and beautiful. In this article, we’ll go over some tips and tricks for wearing thinning hair up. Whether you’re looking for a quick and easy style or something more complex, we’ve got you covered. So let’s get started!

Description

Thin hair is often associated with aging or health issues, but it can also be genetic. Thinning hair can be a frustrating and stressful experience for many people. However, there are many ways to style thin hair that can make it look fuller and more voluminous. In this article, we’ll explore different techniques for wearing thinning hair up, so you can feel confident and stylish.

How to Wear Thinning Hair Up

Step 1: Start with a Good Haircut

Before you start styling your thinning hair, it’s important to start with a good haircut. A skilled stylist can create a cut that will make your hair look thicker and fuller. Look for a stylist who has experience working with thinning hair, and bring pictures of styles you like to your appointment. A good haircut can make all the difference in how your hair looks and feels.

Step 2: Use the Right Products

The right hair products can help you achieve the look you want. Use volumizing shampoos and conditioners that are designed to add body and fullness to your hair. Avoid heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Opt for lightweight mousses, gels, and sprays that will help your hair hold its shape without weighing it down.

Step 3: Try a Top Knot

A top knot is a simple and stylish way to wear thinning hair up. Start by pulling your hair into a high ponytail, then twist the ponytail into a bun and secure it with bobby pins. Use your fingers to gently pull the bun apart and create volume. Finish with hairspray to hold the style in place.

Step 4: Experiment with Braids

Braids are a great way to add volume and texture to thinning hair. Try a simple French braid or a fishtail braid for a stylish and sophisticated look. You can also try braiding just the top section of your hair and leaving the rest down for a half-up, half-down style.

Step 5: Go for a Messy Bun

A messy bun is a great way to wear thinning hair up without looking too formal. Start by pulling your hair into a low ponytail, then twist the ponytail into a loose bun and secure it with bobby pins. Use your fingers to pull out a few strands of hair around your face and at the nape of your neck for a relaxed, effortless look.

Tips for Wearing Thinning Hair Up

Here are some additional tips for styling thinning hair:

  • Use a teasing comb to add volume to the roots of your hair.
  • Try a volumizing powder or spray to add texture and body to your hair.
  • Use bobby pins that match your hair color to secure your style.
  • Experiment with different styles until you find what works best for you.
  • Consider adding hair extensions to add volume and length to your hair.

FAQs

What causes thinning hair?

Thinning hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, aging, hormonal changes, and health issues. If you’re concerned about your thinning hair, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist to rule out any underlying health issues.

Can I wear my thinning hair down?

Yes, you can wear your thinning hair down. However, wearing your hair up can help create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair. Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

What should I do if my hair is thinning?

If you’re concerned about your thinning hair, talk to your doctor or a dermatologist. They can help you determine the underlying cause of your hair loss and recommend a treatment plan.

Hair Care for Thinning Hair

In addition to styling your hair, it’s important to take care of your hair to prevent further thinning. Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils. Avoid heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, which can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on it. And don’t forget to eat a healthy, balanced diet to support strong, healthy hair.

Conclusion

Wearing thinning hair up can be a great way to create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair. By starting with a good haircut, using the right products, and experimenting with different styles, you can find a look that works for you. And don’t forget to take care of your hair to prevent further thinning. With a little bit of effort and creativity, you can feel confident and beautiful with your thinning hair.
