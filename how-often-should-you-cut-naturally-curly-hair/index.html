How Often Should You Cut Naturally Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 26, 2023 · 4 min read

Curly hair can be both beautiful and challenging to maintain. One of the most important aspects of keeping your curls healthy is ensuring that you get regular haircuts. But how often should you cut naturally curly hair? In this article, we’ll explore the answer to this question and provide some tips on how to care for your curly locks.

Table of Contents

  • How To Cut Naturally Curly Hair
  • Step By Step Guide
  • Tips For Cutting Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care For Curly Hair

How To Cut Naturally Curly Hair

When it comes to cutting curly hair, it’s important to find a stylist who is experienced with curly hair. Curly hair can be temperamental and requires a different cutting technique than straight hair. After finding a stylist, it’s important to discuss the frequency of haircuts.

The general rule of thumb is to get a trim every 6-8 weeks. However, this can vary depending on the individual’s hair type, length, and personal preference. Some people may prefer to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain a specific length or style. Others may be able to go longer between haircuts without experiencing any adverse effects.

Step By Step Guide

Once you’ve determined the frequency of haircuts, it’s important to understand what to expect during the haircutting process. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Detangle your hair using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  3. Discuss your desired length and style with your stylist.
  4. Allow your stylist to cut your hair while it’s dry. This will allow them to see the natural curl pattern and prevent any surprises once your hair dries.
  5. After the haircut, your stylist may choose to add additional layers or thin out the hair to prevent any excess bulk.
  6. Style your hair as usual.

Tips For Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for cutting curly hair:

  • Don’t cut curly hair when it’s wet. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer than it actually is, which can result in a shorter cut than intended.
  • Use a sharp pair of scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and damage to the hair.
  • Consider getting a DevaCut or a haircut specifically designed for curly hair. These cuts take into account the natural curl pattern and can result in a more flattering and manageable style.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting naturally curly hair:

Q: Can you cut curly hair with a razor?

A: It’s not recommended to cut curly hair with a razor as it can cause frizz and damage to the hair.

Q: Should I cut my own curly hair?

A: It’s generally not recommended to cut your own curly hair as it can be difficult to achieve a balanced and even cut.

Q: What if I don’t want to cut my curly hair?

A: If you don’t want to cut your curly hair, it’s still important to maintain its health by regularly washing, conditioning, and styling it. Consider getting regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

Hair Care For Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy and beautiful curly hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to provide extra hydration and prevent tangles.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat on low.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry as this can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

In conclusion, the frequency of cutting naturally curly hair can vary depending on the individual’s hair type, length, and personal preference. It’s important to find a stylist who is experienced with curly hair and to discuss your desired length and style. Remember to maintain your curly hair’s health by regularly washing, conditioning, and styling it.
