When Does Hair Stop Falling After Chemo

Written by Robby Jul 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you or your loved one have recently undergone chemotherapy, you may be wondering when your hair will stop falling out. Losing hair can be a traumatic and emotional experience, and it's important to understand the process and timeline of hair regrowth after chemo. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about when hair stops falling out after chemo, including tips for hair care during and after treatment.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare for Hair Loss During Chemo
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Regrowth After Chemo
  • Tips for Hair Care During and After Chemo

How to Prepare for Hair Loss During Chemo

Hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, and it can be helpful to prepare for this before treatment begins. Here are a few things you can do to prepare for hair loss during chemo:

  • Cut your hair short before treatment begins. Shorter hair is easier to manage and can be less traumatic when it starts to fall out.
  • Invest in a wig or head covering. Many cancer centers offer wig fittings, and there are also online resources for finding wigs and head coverings.
  • Talk to your doctor about scalp cooling. Scalp cooling systems can help reduce hair loss during chemo by lowering the temperature of the scalp and reducing blood flow to the hair follicles.

Step-by-Step Guide to Hair Regrowth After Chemo

Hair regrowth after chemo can be a slow and unpredictable process. Here's a step-by-step guide to what you can expect:

Step 1: Shedding

In the first few weeks after chemotherapy, you may notice an increase in hair shedding. This is normal and is a sign that the hair follicles are preparing to regrow hair.

Step 2: Resting

After the shedding phase, the hair follicles enter a resting phase that can last several weeks to several months. During this time, you may notice little to no hair growth.

Step 3: Regrowth

Finally, the hair follicles will begin to regrow hair. This can take several months or even up to a year. The new hair may be a different texture or color than your previous hair, but it will eventually grow back to its normal state.

Tips for Hair Care During and After Chemo

Taking good care of your hair during and after chemotherapy can help promote healthy regrowth. Here are a few tips:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Use a soft-bristled brush or wide-tooth comb, and avoid using heat styling tools.
  • Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.
  • Avoid coloring or perming your hair until it has fully regrown.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or scarf.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for hair to stop falling out after chemo?

Hair loss usually begins within 2-4 weeks after starting chemotherapy, and it can continue for several weeks after treatment ends. Hair typically stops falling out within 1-2 months after treatment ends.

Will my hair grow back the same after chemo?

Hair may grow back differently after chemotherapy, and it may take several months or even up to a year for it to return to its normal state. The texture and color of the new hair may be different than your previous hair.

Can scalp cooling prevent hair loss during chemo?

Scalp cooling systems can help reduce hair loss during chemotherapy by lowering the temperature of the scalp and reducing blood flow to the hair follicles. However, not all cancer centers offer scalp cooling, and it may not be effective for everyone. Talk to your doctor to see if scalp cooling is an option for you.

Hair loss during chemotherapy can be a difficult experience, but understanding the process of hair regrowth can help you feel more prepared and confident. With the right care and patience, your hair will eventually grow back to its normal state.
