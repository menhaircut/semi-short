How To Cut Hair In Layers With Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Oct 19, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a way to add some dimension and texture to your hair, cutting it in layers with scissors is a great option. This technique can help create volume, movement, and shape to your hair, and can work for any hair length and type. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of how to cut hair in layers with scissors, from start to finish.

How to Cut Hair in Layers with Scissors

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start with freshly washed and dried hair. This will make it easier to see the natural texture and shape of your hair, and make it easier to cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, create a center part and divide your hair into two sections. Then, take one section and divide it horizontally into two more sections, clipping the top section out of the way.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Take the bottom section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold it between your fingers at the length you want to cut it. Use your scissors to cut straight across, making sure to keep your fingers parallel to the hair.

Next, release the top section of hair and comb it straight down over the bottom section. Hold it between your fingers at the length you want to cut it, but make sure to hold it slightly higher than the bottom section. Again, use your scissors to cut straight across, keeping your fingers parallel to the hair.

Repeat this process, working your way up the back of your head and then moving to the sides, until you've cut all of the sections you've divided.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all of the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Take your comb and run it through your hair, combing the layers together. You may need to do some additional cutting to blend any uneven layers.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Finally, you can add some finishing touches to your layered haircut. You can use your scissors to add some texture to the ends of your hair, or use a razor to create a more choppy look.

Tips for Cutting Hair in Layers with Scissors

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting hair in layers with scissors:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Work in small sections to ensure even layers.
  • Don't pull the hair too tight when cutting, as this can lead to uneven layers.
  • Cut less hair than you think you need to, as you can always go back and cut more.

FAQs

Can I cut my hair in layers with regular scissors?

While it's possible to cut your hair in layers with regular scissors, it's not recommended. Hair cutting scissors are designed to create clean, precise cuts, while regular scissors can lead to jagged, uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my hair in layers?

It's recommended to cut your hair in layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your haircut.

Hair Care for Layered Hair

Once you've cut your hair in layers, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for hair care for layered hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and texture of your haircut.

With these tips and techniques, you can successfully cut your hair in layers with scissors and enjoy a new, fresh look. Happy cutting!
