How To Cut Men s Hair Back Of Head Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Jun 27, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts for men? With a little bit of practice and patience, you can learn how to cut men's hair back of head at home. Not only will you save money, but you will also have complete control over the haircut style and length. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut men's hair back of head in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Description

The back of the head is a tricky area to cut, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. Cutting men's hair back of head requires a bit of the skill set and knowledge of different haircut styles. We will walk you through the entire process of cutting men's hair back of head, from the necessary tools to the final touches.

How to Cut Men's Hair Back of Head: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather the Necessary Tools

The first step in cutting men's hair back of head is to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, a hair clipper with various guards, and a mirror.

Step 2: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before starting the haircut, make sure the hair is clean and completely dry. Wet hair can be tricky to cut, and it may not provide an accurate length measurement. Make sure the hair is completely dry before starting the trim.

Step 3: Comb and Section the Hair

Once the hair is dry, comb it to remove tangles and knots. Divide the hair into sections using the comb and hair clips. Starting from the nape of the neck, clip the top section of the hair and leave the bottom section loose.

Step 4: Start with the Clippers

Begin the haircut by using the hair clippers and the desired guard size. Start from the bottom of the hairline and move upwards slowly. Avoid pushing the clippers too hard against the scalp, as it may cause cuts or uneven haircuts. Repeat this step on the other side of the head.

Step 5: Cut the Hair with Scissors

After using the hair clippers, use the hair cutting scissors to trim the hair on the back of the head. Start from the bottom and trim the hair in small sections, moving upwards. Make sure to blend the cut hair with the uncut hair, creating a seamless look.

Step 6: Check the Length

After cutting the hair, check the length in the mirror. If there are any uneven spots or long strands, use the scissors to trim them. Repeat this step until you achieve the desired length.

Step 7: Cut the Hairline

The hairline is a crucial part of the haircut. Use the hair clippers without a guard to trim the hairline. Create a natural curve around the ears and the back of the neck.

Step 8: Finish the Haircut

After trimming the hairline, use the scissors to clean up any stray hairs. Comb the hair and check the length one last time. If you are satisfied with the haircut, you can proceed to style it as desired.

Tips for Cutting Men's Hair Back of Head

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect haircut:

  • Start with a higher guard size and work your way down for a more gradual haircut.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head constantly.
  • Trim the hair in small sections to avoid mistakes.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as it may be difficult to correct.
  • Always use sharp scissors and clean the hair clippers after each use.

FAQs

Q: Can I cut men's hair back of head with regular scissors?

A: No, regular scissors may not be sharp enough to cut through thick hair. Use hair cutting scissors for best results.

Q: How often should I cut men's hair back of head?

A: It depends on the desired length and haircut style. On average, men should get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care

After cutting the hair, make sure to wash it with a mild shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using hot tools, such as blow dryers or straighteners, as they can damage the hair. Use hair products, such as gel or hairspray, as desired.

Now that you know how to cut men's hair back of head, you can save money and have complete control over the haircut style. With a bit of practice, you will be able to achieve a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home.
