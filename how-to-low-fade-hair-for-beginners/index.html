How To Low Fade Hair For Beginners A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Oct 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and easy-to-maintain haircut, a low fade might just be the perfect choice for you. A low fade haircut is where the hair gradually tapers from a longer length on top to a shorter length on the sides and back, creating a clean and sharp look. In this beginner's guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a low fade haircut, as well as provide some tips and hair care advice to keep your new style looking fresh.

How to Low Fade Hair for Beginners: Step by Step

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair and Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools and products on hand. You'll need a pair of clippers with different guard sizes, a comb, scissors, and hair clips. You might also want to use some hair wax or pomade to style your hair afterwards. Begin by washing your hair and towel-drying it until it's damp but not soaking wet.

Step 2: Create the Guideline

Next, you'll need to create a guideline. This is the line that separates the longer hair on top from the shorter hair on the sides and back. The guideline should be straight and even all the way around your head. You can create the guideline using the clippers with a longer guard, or with scissors if you prefer.

Step 3: Start Fading

Now it's time to start fading. Begin with the longest guard size and work your way down to the shortest, gradually tapering the hair shorter as you move down towards the bottom. Remember to blend the hair as you go, using the comb to create a smooth transition between the different lengths. Pay close attention to the hairline around the ears and neck, as these areas can be tricky to get right.

Step 4: Blend the Top

Once you've finished fading the sides and back, it's time to blend the top. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a neat and even shape. You can also use the clippers with a longer guard to trim the top if needed. Make sure the top is slightly longer than the sides and back for a classic low fade look.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Finally, it's time to style your hair. Apply a small amount of hair wax or pomade to your hands and work it through your hair, shaping it into the desired style. You can use a comb or your fingers to create texture and volume, or slick it back for a more formal look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Low Fade Haircut

Now that you've achieved the perfect low fade haircut, it's important to maintain it to keep it looking fresh and sharp. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Get regular trims every 4-6 weeks to keep the fade looking clean and neat.
  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days with a gentle shampoo to avoid stripping away natural oils.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Use a heat protectant if you use styling tools like a hair dryer or straightener.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

FAQ

What's the difference between a low fade and a high fade?

A low fade starts the tapering process towards the bottom of the head, while a high fade starts higher up on the sides and back. A low fade is a more subtle and classic look, while a high fade is more dramatic and modern.

Can I achieve a low fade haircut at home?

Yes, with the right tools and some practice, you can achieve a low fade haircut at home. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's always best to visit a professional barber or stylist.

Final Thoughts

A low fade haircut is a stylish and versatile option for anyone looking for a clean and sharp look. With this beginner's guide, you should have all the information you need to achieve the perfect low fade haircut at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and most importantly, have fun!
