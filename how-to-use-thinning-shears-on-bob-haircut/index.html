How To Use Thinning Shears On Bob Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Sep 18, 2023 · 4 min read

If you’re looking to add texture and dimension to your bob haircut, thinning shears are a great tool to have in your arsenal. While it may seem intimidating to use them on your own, with the right technique and some practice, you can achieve a salon-worthy look from the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we’ll walk you through how to use thinning shears on your bob haircut, as well as provide some tips and tricks for maintaining healthy hair.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth-like notches on one side, which allow you to cut hair in a way that removes some of the bulk while still leaving the length intact. They’re commonly used in hair salons to create textured, layered looks, but can also be used at home to maintain a bob haircut or add some extra oomph to your style.

How to

Step 1: Prep Your Hair

Before you get started, make sure your hair is clean, dry, and tangle-free. Comb or brush through your hair to remove any knots, and consider applying a heat protectant spray if you plan on using any hot tools.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using clips or hair ties, section your hair into manageable pieces. Start at the bottom of your head and work your way up, clipping up the top layers of hair so that you can focus on the bottom layers first.

Step 3: Begin Cutting

Hold the thinning shears at a slight angle to your hair and start cutting small sections at a time. Make sure to use a gentle, sawing motion rather than a straight cut. You can also vary the length of the sections you cut to create a more natural, textured look.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Once you’ve finished cutting the bottom layers, release the top layers and blend them into the shorter, textured hair below. Use the thinning shears sparingly on the top layers to avoid removing too much hair.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

Throughout the cutting process, check your progress in a mirror to make sure you’re achieving the desired look. Remember, it’s always easier to cut more hair than it is to add it back, so start with small sections and work your way up.

Tips

- Use sharp, high-quality thinning shears to avoid damaging your hair
- Cut in a well-lit area to ensure even cutting
- Start with small sections and work your way up to avoid removing too much hair
- Experiment with different cutting angles to create a more natural look
- Practice on a mannequin head before trying it on your own hair

FAQ

Can thinning shears be used on any hair type?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but they work best on hair that is thick or voluminous. If you have fine hair, be cautious when using thinning shears as they can cause your hair to look wispy or thin.

How often should I use thinning shears on my bob haircut?

This depends on your individual hair growth and maintenance needs. Some people may need to use thinning shears every few weeks to keep their bob looking fresh, while others may only need to use them once every few months. Pay attention to how your hair is growing and adjust accordingly.

Hair Care

Using thinning shears can be a great way to add texture and dimension to your bob haircut, but it’s important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and vitality. Make sure to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated. Avoid using hot tools too often, and always use a heat protectant spray when you do. With the right care and attention, you can enjoy a beautiful, textured bob haircut for years to come.
