Should Bridesmaids Wear Their Hair Up Or Down

Written by Eveline Jul 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a bridesmaid, one of the many decisions you'll need to make is how to wear your hair. Should you wear it up or down? It's a common question that many bridesmaids ask themselves. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of wearing your hair up or down on the big day.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Wear Your Hair Up or Down
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Wearing Your Hair Up or Down
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Description

Before we delve into the details of how to wear your hair on the big day, let's discuss why this decision is important. Your hairstyle can affect your overall look and feel on the day. It can also impact your comfort level. For example, wearing your hair up may be more comfortable if you're attending an outdoor wedding in hot weather.

How to Wear Your Hair Up or Down

The decision to wear your hair up or down ultimately comes down to personal preference and the style of the wedding. If the bride has a specific hairstyle in mind for her bridesmaids, you'll need to follow her lead. However, if the bride is open to suggestions, here are some things to consider:

Wearing your hair up:

  • Can be more comfortable in hot weather
  • Can be more formal and elegant
  • Can be easier to maintain throughout the day
  • Can showcase a beautiful neckline or back of the dress
  • Can be styled in a variety of ways, such as a classic chignon or a messy bun

Wearing your hair down:

  • Can be more casual and relaxed
  • Can be more playful and youthful
  • Can be flattering for certain face shapes
  • Can be styled in a variety of ways, such as loose waves or a sleek blowout

Step-by-Step Guide

Whether you're wearing your hair up or down, here are some steps to follow to ensure your hairstyle looks great all day:

Wearing your hair up:

  1. Wash your hair the night before and let it air dry
  2. Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add body to your hair
  3. Create a center or side part, depending on your preference
  4. Gather your hair at the nape of your neck and secure it with an elastic band
  5. Twist or braid your hair, depending on the style you're going for
  6. Secure the twist or braid with bobby pins
  7. Spray your hair with a strong-hold hairspray to keep it in place

Wearing your hair down:

  1. Wash your hair the night before and let it air dry
  2. Use a heat protectant spray before using any hot styling tools
  3. Curl or straighten your hair, depending on the style you're going for
  4. Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair
  5. Finish with a light-hold hairspray to keep your style in place

Tips for Wearing Your Hair Up or Down

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when deciding how to wear your hair:

  • Consider the neckline and back of your dress when choosing a hairstyle
  • Make sure your hairstyle complements the overall theme and style of the wedding
  • Bring pictures of hairstyles you like to your hair trial appointment
  • Use hair accessories, such as a headband or hair clip, to add interest to your hairstyle
  • Consider getting hair extensions if you want to add length or volume to your hair

FAQs

Q: Should bridesmaids all have the same hairstyle?

A: It's up to the bride to decide if she wants her bridesmaids to have matching hairstyles. Some brides prefer a uniform look, while others want their bridesmaids to choose a style that suits them best.

Q: Can I wear my hair half-up, half-down?

A: Yes, a half-up, half-down hairstyle can be a great compromise if you can't decide between wearing your hair up or down.

Q: Should I get my hair cut or colored before the wedding?

A: It's best to get any major hair changes done at least a few weeks before the wedding. This will give your hair time to settle and for any color to fade slightly, if needed.

Hair Care Tips

Whether you're wearing your hair up or down, it's important to take care of your hair in the lead up to the wedding. Here are some tips:

  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, which can cause damage
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using hot styling tools
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils

Ultimately, the decision to wear your hair up or down on the big day is a personal one. Consider your comfort level, the style of the wedding, and your own preferences when making your decision. With these tips, you'll be sure to have a beautiful hairstyle that looks great all day.
