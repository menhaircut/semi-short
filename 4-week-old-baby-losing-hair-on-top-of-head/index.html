What To Do If Your 4 Week Old Baby Is Losing Hair On Top Of Head

Written by Christine Jul 01, 2023 · 4 min read

If you have a 4 week old baby, you may notice that they are losing hair on top of their head. This can be concerning for new parents, but it is actually quite common and nothing to worry about. In this article, we will discuss why babies lose hair, what you can do to help, and when to see a doctor.

Why Do Babies Lose Hair?

Babies can lose hair for a variety of reasons. One of the most common reasons is simply due to the hormones they were exposed to while in the womb. These hormones can cause the hair to fall out and be replaced with new hair. Another reason is that babies often rub their heads against their bedding or car seat, which can cause hair to fall out.

How to Help Your Baby

If your baby is losing hair on top of their head, there are a few things you can do to help. First, make sure that your baby is not rubbing their head against anything. This may mean adjusting their bedding or car seat so that they are not in contact with the top of their head. You can also try using a soft hat or headband to protect their head.

Another thing you can do is make sure that your baby's scalp is clean and healthy. Use a mild baby shampoo to wash their hair, and gently massage their scalp to increase circulation. This can help promote healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to helping your baby if they are losing hair on top of their head:

Step 1: Check for Rubbing

Make sure that your baby is not rubbing their head against anything.

Step 2: Adjust Bedding or Car Seat

If necessary, adjust their bedding or car seat so that they are not in contact with the top of their head.

Step 3: Use a Soft Hat or Headband

You can also try using a soft hat or headband to protect their head.

Step 4: Wash Their Hair

Use a mild baby shampoo to wash their hair, and gently massage their scalp to increase circulation.

Tips for Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your baby's hair:

  • Use a mild baby shampoo
  • Gently massage their scalp to increase circulation
  • Do not use adult hair products on your baby
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on their hair
  • Use a soft brush or comb to detangle their hair

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about babies losing hair:

Is it normal for babies to lose hair?

Yes, it is normal for babies to lose hair. It is often due to hormonal changes or rubbing against bedding or car seats.

Will my baby's hair grow back?

Yes, your baby's hair will grow back. It may take some time, but most babies will have a full head of hair by the time they are 1 year old.

When should I see a doctor?

If you are concerned about your baby's hair loss or if they are experiencing other symptoms, such as a rash or redness on their scalp, you should see a doctor.

Conclusion

If you have a 4 week old baby who is losing hair on top of their head, there is no need to worry. This is a common occurrence and will likely resolve on its own. By following the tips and steps outlined in this article, you can help promote healthy hair growth and ensure that your baby's scalp is clean and healthy.
