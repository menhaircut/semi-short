What Size Round Brush For Thick Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 11, 2023 · 4 min read

When it comes to styling thick hair, a round brush can be your best friend. But with so many sizes to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know which one is right for you. In this guide, we’ll break down what size round brush for thick hair you should be using and provide tips for achieving salon-worthy results.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Right Size Round Brush for Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Thick Hair with a Round Brush
  • Tips for Using a Round Brush on Thick Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it has volume and body, it can also be difficult to manage and style. That’s where a round brush comes in. A round brush is a must-have tool for creating smooth, bouncy blowouts and adding curl and volume to your hair.

However, not all round brushes are created equal. The size of your brush is crucial to achieving the desired look. A smaller brush will create tighter curls and more volume, while a larger brush will create looser curls and a smoother finish.

How to Choose the Right Size Round Brush for Thick Hair

Choosing the right size round brush for your thick hair depends on the length of your hair and the style you’re trying to achieve. Here’s a general guide:

  • For short hair (< 6 inches), use a small brush (1-1.5 inches in diameter) to create volume and texture.
  • For medium-length hair (6-10 inches), use a medium brush (1.5-2 inches in diameter) to create loose curls and waves.
  • For long hair (> 10 inches), use a large brush (2-3 inches in diameter) to create smooth, bouncy curls.

Keep in mind that the larger the brush, the longer it will take to dry your hair. If you’re short on time, opt for a smaller brush.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Thick Hair with a Round Brush

Now that you’ve chosen the right size round brush for your hair, here’s how to use it:

  1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  2. Section your hair into manageable pieces. Use clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.
  3. Starting with the bottom section, place the brush at the roots and roll it down to the ends while blow-drying. Repeat until the section is dry.
  4. Move on to the next section, working your way up to the top of your head.
  5. Once all sections are dry, use the brush to style your hair as desired. For volume, brush your hair up and away from your scalp. For curls, wrap your hair around the brush and hold for a few seconds before releasing.
  6. Finish with a blast of cool air to set your style.

Tips for Using a Round Brush on Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for using a round brush on thick hair:

  • Use a high-quality brush with a mix of natural and synthetic bristles. This will help distribute heat evenly and prevent breakage.
  • Blow-dry your hair in the direction you want it to lay. For example, if you want volume at the roots, blow-dry your hair up and away from your scalp.
  • Don’t use too much product. Too much product can weigh down your hair and make it difficult to style.
  • Always use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage from heat styling.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I have thick, curly hair?

If you have thick, curly hair, a round brush can still be a great tool for smoothing and defining your curls. Use a large brush to smooth out your curls while blow-drying, or use a smaller brush to create tighter curls.

Can I use a round brush on wet hair?

No, it’s not recommended to use a round brush on wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to breakage and using a round brush on wet hair can cause damage. Make sure your hair is at least 80% dry before using a round brush.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

In addition to using the right size round brush, there are some hair care tips you can follow to keep your thick hair healthy and manageable:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically formulated for thick hair.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils and make it more difficult to style.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage. Let your hair air-dry as often as possible and use heat styling tools sparingly.

By following these tips and using the right size round brush, you can achieve salon-worthy results at home and keep your thick hair looking its best.
