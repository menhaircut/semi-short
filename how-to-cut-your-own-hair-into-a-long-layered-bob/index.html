How To Cut Your Own Hair Into A Long Layered Bob

Written by Bardi Nov 13, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle and want to try a long layered bob, you don't have to go to a salon and spend a lot of money. With the right tools and techniques, you can cut your own hair into a long layered bob at home. In this guide, we will show you how to achieve this look step by step.

Description

A long layered bob is a versatile and stylish haircut that suits many face shapes and hair types. This hairstyle features shorter layers at the back and longer layers at the front, creating a flattering and natural-looking shape. The layers also add volume and movement to the hair, making it look more dynamic and textured. By cutting your own hair into a long layered bob, you can save money and have more control over the final result.

How to Cut Your Own Hair into a Long Layered Bob

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle of water

Having the right tools is essential to achieving a good result.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using any styling products.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Divide your hair into four sections: two at the front and two at the back. Use hair clips to secure each section. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting from the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at an angle and cut upwards to create shorter layers. Repeat this process on each section of hair, working your way up to the crown of your head. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven cut.

Step 5: Cut the Front Layers

Once you have finished cutting the back, move on to the front. Take a small section of hair from each side and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at an angle and cut upwards to create longer layers. Make sure the layers are even on both sides.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check it in the mirror to make sure it's even and balanced. Make any necessary adjustments by trimming any uneven or longer pieces.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair and gradually work your way up to larger sections.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can take longer than going to a salon.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven cut.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair into a long layered bob if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair into a long layered bob if you have curly hair. Make sure you cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state, as wet curly hair can shrink and look shorter than it actually is.

Can I cut my hair into a long layered bob if I have thin hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair into a long layered bob if you have thin hair. The layers will add volume and texture to your hair, making it look fuller and more dynamic.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair into a long layered bob, it's important to take care of it to maintain its shape and health. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat when styling your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Apply a hair mask or treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.

Cutting your own hair into a long layered bob can be a fun and rewarding experience. With these tips and techniques, you can achieve a stylish and flattering haircut at home. Just remember to be patient, take your time, and have fun!
