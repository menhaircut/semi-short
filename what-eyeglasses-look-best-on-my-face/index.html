What Eyeglasses Look Best On My Face

Written by Daniel Dec 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Welcome to our guide on choosing the perfect eyeglasses for your face shape. Choosing the right eyeglasses can be a daunting task, but with the right knowledge, you can easily find the perfect pair that complements your facial features. In this article, we will discuss what eyeglasses look best on your face, how to choose the right pair, and some tips on how to care for your glasses. So, let's dive in!

Table of Contents

  • Determining Your Face Shape
  • Choosing a Frame Shape
  • Choosing the Right Frame Size
  • Choosing the Right Color
  • Choosing the Right Material

Determining Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing the right eyeglasses for your face is to determine your face shape. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and triangle. Here's how to determine which one you have:

  • Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently rounded forehead and jawline.
  • Round: Your face is about as wide as it is long, with a rounded forehead and jawline.
  • Square: Your face is about as wide as it is long, with a strong, square jawline and forehead.
  • Heart: Your face is wider at the forehead and gradually narrows to a pointed chin.
  • Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones and narrows at both the forehead and jawline.
  • Triangle: Your face is wider at the jawline than at the forehead.

Once you have determined your face shape, it's time to choose a frame shape that complements it.

Choosing a Frame Shape

The key to choosing the right frame shape is to choose one that contrasts with your face shape. For example, if you have a round face, choose a square or rectangular frame. If you have a square face, choose a round or oval frame. Here are some tips for each face shape:

  • Oval: Oval faces can wear almost any frame shape, but square or rectangular frames will add definition to your softer features.
  • Round: Round faces should choose frames with sharp angles and straight lines to add definition to the face. Avoid round frames.
  • Square: Square faces should choose frames with round or oval shapes to soften the strong angles of the face. Avoid square frames.
  • Heart: Heart-shaped faces should choose frames with wider bottoms to balance out the narrow chin. Avoid frames with embellishments or heavy top bars.
  • Diamond: Diamond-shaped faces should choose frames with rounded edges to soften the cheekbones. Avoid narrow frames.
  • Triangle: Triangle faces should choose frames with a strong top line to balance out the jawline. Cat-eye frames are a great choice.

Choosing the Right Frame Size

Choosing the right frame size is just as important as choosing the right shape. Frames that are too large or too small can throw off the balance of your face. Here are some tips for choosing the right size:

  • The frame should be no wider than the widest part of your face.
  • The top of the frame should follow the curve of your eyebrows.
  • The bottom of the frame should not rest on your cheeks.
  • The temples should fit comfortably and not press into your head.

Choosing the Right Color

When choosing the color of your eyeglasses, consider your skin tone, hair color, and personal style. Here are some tips:

  • Warm skin tones look best with gold, brown, or honey-colored frames.
  • Cool skin tones look best with silver, black, or blue frames.
  • Blonde hair looks best with warm-colored frames, while darker hair looks best with cool-colored frames.
  • For a classic look, choose neutral colors like black, brown, or tortoiseshell.
  • For a bold look, choose bright colors or patterns.

Choosing the Right Material

Eyeglass frames come in a variety of materials, including plastic, metal, and titanium. Here are some pros and cons of each:

  • Plastic: Lightweight and affordable, but can be less durable than other materials.
  • Metal: Sleek and durable, but can be heavier and more expensive than plastic.
  • Titanium: Lightweight and durable, but can be more expensive than other materials.

Taking Care of Your Glasses

Proper care of your glasses can extend their lifespan and keep them looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Clean your glasses daily with a microfiber cloth and lens cleaner.
  • Avoid using paper towels or clothing to clean your glasses, as they can scratch the lenses.
  • Store your glasses in a case when not in use.
  • Avoid leaving your glasses in direct sunlight or extreme temperatures.

FAQs

What if I have a unique face shape?

If you have a unique face shape, don't worry! The tips in this article are just guidelines. Ultimately, the best eyeglasses for you are the ones that make you feel confident and comfortable.

Can I wear contacts instead of glasses?

Yes, contacts are a great alternative to glasses. However, it's still important to choose frames that complement your face shape if you do wear glasses occasionally.

Do I need to spend a lot of money on glasses?

No, you don't need to spend a lot of money on glasses to find a pair that looks great. There are many affordable options available, especially online.

How often should I replace my glasses?

It's recommended to replace your glasses every 2 years, or sooner if your prescription changes or your glasses become damaged.


We hope this guide has helped you choose the perfect eyeglasses for your face shape. Remember, the most important thing is to choose a pair that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Happy shopping!
