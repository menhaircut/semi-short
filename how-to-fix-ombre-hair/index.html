How To Fix Ombre Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 21, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you recently gotten an ombre hair color and noticed that it’s not quite what you expected? Or maybe it’s been a while since you’ve had it done and it’s starting to look faded or brassy. Don’t worry, you’re not alone! Ombre hair can be tricky to maintain, but with the right techniques and products, you can easily fix any issues and get your hair looking beautiful again.

Description

Ombre hair is a coloring technique that involves gradually fading one color into another, typically from dark to light. It’s a popular trend that has been around for several years, and it’s not going anywhere anytime soon. However, maintaining ombre hair can be challenging, especially if you have naturally dark hair or if you’ve dyed it multiple times. Over time, the color can fade or become brassy, making it look less than perfect.

How to Fix Ombre Hair

If you’re experiencing issues with your ombre hair, don’t worry – there are several things you can do to fix it. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to fix ombre hair:

Step 1: Determine the Issue

The first step in fixing ombre hair is to determine what the issue is. Is the color fading? Is it too brassy? Is the transition from dark to light too harsh? Once you’ve identified the problem, you can move on to the next steps.

Step 2: Use a Color-Depositing Shampoo

If the issue is that your ombre hair is fading or becoming brassy, try using a color-depositing shampoo. These shampoos deposit small amounts of color onto your hair each time you wash it, helping to maintain the vibrancy of your ombre. Look for a shampoo that is specifically designed for your hair color (i.e., blonde, brunette, etc.) and use it once or twice a week.

Step 3: Use a Toning Treatment

If your ombre hair is too brassy, try using a toning treatment. These treatments help to neutralize unwanted yellow or orange tones in your hair, leaving it looking more natural and vibrant. Look for a toning treatment that is specifically designed for your hair color and use it once a week.

Step 4: Get a Gloss Treatment

If your ombre hair is looking dull or lackluster, consider getting a gloss treatment. These treatments add shine and vibrancy to your hair, making it look healthy and radiant. You can get a gloss treatment at a salon, or you can do it yourself at home using a glossing kit.

Step 5: Trim Your Hair

If the transition from dark to light in your ombre hair is too harsh, consider getting a trim. Trimming your hair can help to blend the colors together more seamlessly, making the transition look more natural. Plus, it’s a great way to get rid of any split ends or damaged hair.

Tips for Maintaining Ombre Hair

Once you’ve fixed your ombre hair, it’s important to maintain it properly to ensure that it stays looking beautiful. Here are some tips for maintaining ombre hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of its natural oils
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as they can damage your hair and cause it to fade
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends

FAQs

What if I’m not happy with my ombre hair color?

If you’re not happy with your ombre hair color, speak to your stylist about making some adjustments. They may be able to tweak the color or transition to better suit your preferences.

How often should I use a color-depositing shampoo?

You can use a color-depositing shampoo once or twice a week, depending on how often you wash your hair. Be careful not to overuse it, as it can lead to buildup and cause your hair to look dull.

Can I do a gloss treatment at home?

Yes, you can do a gloss treatment at home using a glossing kit. Just be sure to follow the instructions carefully and choose a kit that is specifically designed for your hair color.

Hair Care

Maintaining ombre hair requires some extra care, but it’s worth it to keep your hair looking beautiful. Be sure to use high-quality products, avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, and get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and looking great.
