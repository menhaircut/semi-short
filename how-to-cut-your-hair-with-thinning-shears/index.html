How To Cut Your Hair With Thinning Shears A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 22, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Thinning shears are a great tool to use when you want to remove bulk from your hair without sacrificing length. In this article, we will show you how to cut your hair with thinning shears, step-by-step.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How To
  • Step-by-Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth on one blade and a smooth edge on the other. They are used to remove bulk from the hair without creating blunt lines or layers. Thinning shears are great for creating texture and movement in the hair, especially for those with thick or curly hair. They are also useful for blending layers and reducing the weight of the hair.

How To

To cut your hair with thinning shears, you will need:

  • Thinning shears
  • Hair clips
  • Comb
  • Sharp scissors

Step-by-Step

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and will ensure that it is clean and free of any product buildup.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four equal parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Comb each section of hair to remove any tangles or knots. Make sure your hair is completely smooth before you start cutting.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Take a small section of hair from the bottom of one of the back sections. Hold it between your fingers and use the thinning shears to cut into the hair, starting at the mid-length and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to only cut about 1/4 inch of hair at a time.

Step 5: Continue Cutting

Repeat step 4 on the rest of the hair in that section, working from the bottom up. Once you have finished one section, move on to the next.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you have finished cutting all of the sections, use your sharp scissors to blend the layers together. This will ensure that there are no blunt lines or choppy layers.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Take a good look at your hair in the mirror and make any necessary adjustments. If you notice any unevenness or areas that need more thinning, go back and make the necessary cuts.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Once you are happy with your cut, style your hair as desired using your favorite products.

Tips

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in unevenness or choppy layers.
  • Make sure your thinning shears are sharp before you start cutting.
  • Use your sharp scissors to blend the layers together after you have finished cutting with the thinning shears.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you are unsure about cutting your own hair.

FAQ

Can I use thinning shears on any type of hair?

Yes, thinning shears can be used on any type of hair, but they are especially useful for those with thick or curly hair.

Will thinning shears make my hair look thinner?

Thinning shears will remove bulk from your hair, but they will not make your hair look thinner. They are designed to create texture and movement in the hair.

Do I need to have hairdressing experience to use thinning shears?

No, you do not need to have hairdressing experience to use thinning shears. However, it is important to start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections to avoid any mistakes.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair with thinning shears, it is important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking great. This includes:

  • Using a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Using a heat protectant spray if you do need to use heat styling tools
  • Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends
  • Using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and nourished

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can enjoy your new, freshly-cut look for weeks to come.
