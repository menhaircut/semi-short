Why Are My Edges So Thin Tips And Tricks To Fix It

Written by Bardi Jul 01, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're like many people, you've probably experienced thinning edges at some point in your life. Whether it's due to genetics, over-styling, or just plain old hair damage, thinning edges can be frustrating and even embarrassing. But don't worry, there are plenty of things you can do to fix it. In this article, we'll go over why your edges might be thinning and what you can do to help them grow back.

Description: What Are Thinning Edges?

Thinning edges, also known as "edges," refer to the hairline around the forehead and temples. When these areas of hair begin to thin or recede, it can create a noticeable gap in your hairline. This can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, over-styling, and hair damage.

How to Check for Thinning Edges

The easiest way to check for thinning edges is to pull your hair back into a tight ponytail or bun. Examine your hairline around your forehead and temples. If you notice any gaps or bald spots, you may be experiencing thinning edges.

Step by Step Guide to Fixing Thinning Edges

Step 1: Identify the Cause

Before you can fix your thinning edges, you need to identify the cause. Is it due to genetics, over-styling, or hair damage? Once you know the cause, you can take steps to address it.

Step 2: Reduce Stress on Your Edges

One of the most common causes of thinning edges is over-styling. Tight braids, weaves, and other styles can put too much stress on your hairline, leading to damage and thinning. To reduce stress on your edges, try to avoid tight hairstyles and use gentle hair ties or clips instead.

Step 3: Use Hair Growth Products

There are plenty of hair growth products on the market that can help stimulate hair growth and strengthen your edges. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, castor oil, and keratin.

Step 4: Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help increase blood flow to your hair follicles, which can promote hair growth. Use your fingertips to massage your scalp for a few minutes every day.

Tips for Preventing Thinning Edges

Prevention is always better than cure when it comes to thinning edges. Here are a few tips to help keep your edges healthy:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles
  • Use gentle hair ties and clips
  • Avoid over-styling your hair
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated

Frequently Asked Questions

What Causes Thinning Edges?

Thinning edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, over-styling, and hair damage.

Can Thinning Edges Be Fixed?

Yes, thinning edges can be fixed. By reducing stress on your edges, using hair growth products, and taking steps to promote healthy hair growth, you can help your edges grow back.

How Long Does It Take for Thinning Edges to Grow Back?

The amount of time it takes for thinning edges to grow back can vary depending on the cause and severity of the thinning. With consistent care and treatment, you can expect to see results within a few months.

Hair Care for Thinning Edges

If you're experiencing thinning edges, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent further damage. Be gentle when brushing or detangling your hair, and avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools. Stick to gentle, nourishing hair products that will help promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and tricks, you can help your thinning edges grow back and achieve the healthy, full head of hair you deserve.
