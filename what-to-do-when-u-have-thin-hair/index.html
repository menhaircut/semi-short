What To Do When You Have Thin Hair

Written by Petter Aug 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thin hair, you know how challenging it can be to achieve volume and body. But don't worry! There are several things you can do to add fullness and dimension to your thin hair. In this guide, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to style and care for thin hair to make it look thicker and fuller.

Table of Content

  • How to Style Thin Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Thin Hair Care
  • FAQs

How to Style Thin Hair

When it comes to styling thin hair, the key is to add volume and texture. Here are a few tips to help you achieve this:

Use the Right Products

Choose products that are specifically formulated for thin hair, such as volumizing shampoos, conditioners, and mousses. These products will help to plump up your strands and create volume.

Blow Dry Strategically

When blow-drying your hair, use a round brush to create lift at the roots. Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume and body. Use a cool shot of air to set your style in place.

Try a Sleek Ponytail

A sleek ponytail can make thin hair appear thicker. To achieve this look, smooth your hair back into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck. Use a hair tie to secure it in place and use a fine-tooth comb to smooth down any flyaways.

Add Some Waves

Adding waves can give the illusion of thicker hair. Use a curling iron to create loose waves or use a hair straightener to create beachy waves. Apply a texturizing spray to add body and hold.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you style your thin hair:

Step 1: Wash and Condition

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. Rinse thoroughly with cool water to help seal the cuticle and add shine.

Step 2: Apply a Volumizing Mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your damp hair, concentrating on the roots. Flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.

Step 3: Blow-Dry

Use a round brush to lift your hair at the roots while blow-drying. Blow-dry your hair upside down to add volume.

Step 4: Add Waves

Use a curling iron or hair straightener to add waves to your hair. Apply a texturizing spray to add body and hold.

Step 5: Finish with Hairspray

Finish your style with a light hairspray to keep it in place.

Tips for Thin Hair Care

Here are some tips to help you care for your thin hair:

Avoid Overwashing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and leave it looking limp and flat. Try to wash your hair every other day or every two days to keep it looking healthy and full.

Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

Avoid Heat Damage

Excessive heat styling can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and use a heat protectant spray when you do use them.

Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can help to promote healthy hair growth. Make sure to eat plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables to keep your hair looking its best.

FAQs

Can thin hair be made thicker?

While you can't change the thickness of your hair, there are several things you can do to make it appear thicker. Using volumizing products, blow-drying your hair upside down, and adding waves can all help to create the illusion of thicker hair.

What is the best haircut for thin hair?

A layered haircut can help to add volume and texture to thin hair. Ask your stylist for long layers that will frame your face and add movement to your hair.

What is the best shampoo for thin hair?

Look for shampoos that are specifically formulated for thin hair, such as volumizing shampoos. These shampoos contain ingredients that can help to plump up your strands and create volume.

With these tips and tricks, you can create a beautiful hairstyle that adds dimension and fullness to your thin hair. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
