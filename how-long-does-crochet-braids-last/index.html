How Long Does Crochet Braids Last Tips And Tricks To Make Them Last Longer

Written by Aprilia Aug 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're looking for a protective hairstyle that's easy to maintain and will last for weeks, then crochet braids might be for you. Crochet braids are a popular hair style that involves attaching pre-braided or pre-twisted hair extensions to your natural hair using a crochet hook. In this article, we'll answer the question of how long do crochet braids last and provide you with some tips and tricks to make them last even longer.

How Long Do Crochet Braids Last?

Crochet braids typically last between four to eight weeks, depending on how well you take care of them. The type of hair extensions you use and your hair's natural growth rate can also affect how long your crochet braids will last. Some people may be able to keep their crochet braids in for even longer than eight weeks, while others may need to take them out after just a few weeks.

It's important to note that while crochet braids can last for several weeks, you should not leave them in for too long. Leaving them in for too long can cause matting, tangling, and even damage to your natural hair. You should also avoid washing your hair too often, as this can cause the braids to become frizzy and lose their shape.

How to Install Crochet Braids

Installing crochet braids can be a bit tricky if you've never done it before, but with some practice, you can master the technique. Here's a basic overview of how to install crochet braids:

Step-by-Step Guide to Installing Crochet Braids

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair.
  2. Cornrow your hair in a pattern that will allow you to attach the hair extensions using the crochet hook.
  3. Thread the crochet hook through the center of the pre-braided or pre-twisted hair extension.
  4. Using the crochet hook, pull the hair extension through the cornrow, leaving a loop at the top.
  5. Take the ends of the hair extension and pull them through the loop, securing it in place.
  6. Continue this process until all of your hair is covered in crochet braids.
  7. Trim any excess hair or uneven ends.

Tips to Make Your Crochet Braids Last Longer

If you want to make your crochet braids last as long as possible, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often. Try to wash it no more than once a week.
  • Use a scarf or bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.
  • Avoid using too much product on your hair, as this can cause buildup and make the braids look dull and lifeless.
  • Be gentle when styling your hair, as rough handling can cause the braids to come loose or become frizzy.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized to prevent itching and flaking.

FAQs About Crochet Braids

Here are some common questions people have about crochet braids:

Do crochet braids damage your natural hair?

Crochet braids can be a great protective style if done correctly. However, if the braids are too tight or left in for too long, they can cause damage to your natural hair. It's important to take care of your hair and scalp while wearing crochet braids and to remove them after a reasonable amount of time.

Can you wash crochet braids?

Yes, you can wash crochet braids, but you should avoid washing them too often. Try to wash your hair no more than once a week, and be gentle when washing and styling your hair to prevent the braids from becoming frizzy or coming loose.

How do you remove crochet braids?

To remove crochet braids, simply cut the extensions at the base of the cornrow and unravel the braid. Be gentle when removing the braids to avoid damaging your natural hair.

Hair Care for Crochet Braids

To keep your crochet braids looking their best, here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your scalp, as this can cause buildup and make the braids look dull.
  • Keep your scalp moisturized with a light oil, such as jojoba or almond oil.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as this can cause damage to both the braids and your natural hair.

By following these tips and taking good care of your hair, you can make your crochet braids last as long as possible and keep them looking beautiful.
