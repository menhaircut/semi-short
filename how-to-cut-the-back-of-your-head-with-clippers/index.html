How To Cut The Back Of Your Head With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially when it comes to the back of your head. But with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. In this article, we'll walk you through how to cut the back of your head with clippers, including tips, FAQs, and hair care advice. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut the Back of Your Head with Clippers
  • Tips for a Successful Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Cut the Back of Your Head with Clippers

Before you start, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need a set of hair clippers, a mirror, and a comb. If you have longer hair, you may also want to use scissors to trim any longer sections before you start with the clippers.

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting the back of your head with clippers:

Step 1: Clean Your Clippers

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure your clippers are clean and oiled. This will help them run smoothly and prevent any pulling or tugging on your hair. You can use a brush or a toothbrush to remove any hair or debris from the blades, and then apply a few drops of clipper oil to keep them lubricated.

Step 2: Choose Your Clipper Guard

Next, choose the clipper guard you want to use. The size of the guard will determine how short your hair will be. Start with a larger guard if you're not sure, and then work your way down if you want a shorter cut.

Step 3: Start at the Nape of Your Neck

Begin by placing the clippers at the nape of your neck, with the guard against your skin. Slowly move the clippers up towards the crown of your head, using an upward motion. Make sure to keep the clippers level and don't apply too much pressure, as this can result in an uneven cut.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

Once you've cut the back of your head, move on to the sides. Use the same upward motion, starting at the bottom and moving towards the top. You may need to adjust the angle of the clippers slightly to get an even cut.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

Once you've cut the back and sides, you'll need to blend the hair together for a seamless look. To do this, remove the guard from your clippers and use the bare blades to trim the hair around your ears and the back of your neck. Use a comb to blend the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting your hair, take a look in the mirror to make sure everything looks even. If you notice any longer sections, use your clippers or scissors to trim them down.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you cut the back of your head with clippers:

  • Start with a larger guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut
  • Use a mirror to check your work as you go
  • Don't apply too much pressure with the clippers, as this can result in an uneven cut
  • Move slowly and make small adjustments as needed
  • Consider using scissors to trim longer sections before you start with the clippers

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers. It may take some practice, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done.

What size clipper guard should I use?

The size of the clipper guard you use will depend on how short you want your hair to be. Start with a larger guard if you're not sure, and then work your way down if you want a shorter cut.

How often should I cut my hair?

How often you cut your hair will depend on how quickly it grows and how short you want to keep it. On average, most people cut their hair every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care Tips

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth

Cutting the back of your head with clippers may seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Follow our step-by-step guide, use our tips and FAQs, and take care of your hair afterwards for a great-looking cut every time.
