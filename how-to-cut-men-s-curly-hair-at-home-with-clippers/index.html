How To Cut Men s Curly Hair At Home With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Cutting men's curly hair at home with clippers may seem daunting, but with the right technique and tools, it can be a breeze. Not only can you save time and money, but you can also achieve a personalized haircut that suits your style. In this guide, we will take you through the process of cutting men's curly hair at home with clippers, including tips and tricks to make the process easier.

How to:

Step 1: Gather supplies

Before you begin, gather all the necessary supplies. You will need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Clipper guards
  • Mirror
  • Towel or cape to catch hair clippings
  • Water spray bottle
  • Styling products (optional)

Step 2: Wash and dry hair

Before cutting your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and pat dry with a towel. Do not rub your hair vigorously, as this can cause frizz and damage.

Step 3: Choose the right guard size

Choose the right clipper guard size based on the length you want. For a shorter cut, use a smaller guard size, and for a longer cut, use a larger guard size. It's better to start with a longer guard size and work your way down, as you can always go shorter, but you can't go longer once you've cut your hair.

Step 4: Start cutting

Begin cutting your hair with the clipper guard attached. Start at the sides and work your way up to the top of your head. Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim the hair evenly. Move the clippers against the direction of hair growth for a smoother cut.

Step 5: Blend the hair

After trimming the sides and back, blend the hair by removing the guard and using the clippers without a guard to create a seamless transition between the shorter and longer hair. This will create a more natural look.

Step 6: Cut the top

Use the scissors to cut the hair on top of your head. Start by combing the hair forward and cutting it to the desired length. Then, comb the hair back and trim any excess hair. Use the scissors to create texture and layers, if desired.

Step 7: Check for symmetry

Use a mirror to check for symmetry and make any necessary adjustments. Make sure both sides of your head are even and the hair is cut to the desired length.

Step 8: Style the hair

Once you've finished cutting your hair, style it using your preferred products. Apply a small amount of product to your hair and work it through with your fingers. Use a blow dryer to create volume and shape.

Tips:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down
  • Use the comb to lift the hair and the clippers to trim it evenly
  • Move the clippers against the direction of hair growth for a smoother cut
  • Blend the hair to create a more natural look
  • Check for symmetry using a mirror
  • Style your hair using your preferred products

FAQ:

Q: Can I cut curly hair with clippers?

A: Yes, you can cut curly hair with clippers. Just make sure to choose the right guard size and use the comb to lift the hair and trim it evenly.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want it to be. On average, it's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be harder to cut evenly. If you do cut your hair wet, make sure to use a smaller guard size to account for shrinkage when the hair dries.

Hair Care:

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and appearance. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type, and avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers and straighteners too often. Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage, and use styling products in moderation to avoid product buildup.

Cutting men's curly hair at home with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right technique and tools, you can achieve a personalized haircut that suits your style. Remember to take your time, start with a longer guard size, and check for symmetry to ensure a great result.
