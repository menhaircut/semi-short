How To Cut A Short Layered Haircut Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Aug 11, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're looking to save some money and give yourself a fresh new look, cutting a short layered haircut yourself is a great option. But, it can be intimidating for those who have never tried it before. Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this blog post, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut a short layered haircut yourself. Let's get started!

How To:

Before we dive into the cutting process, let's start with some basics. Here are some things you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips to section your hair

Once you have these items, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's important to start with clean, dry hair. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, and make it easier to create the desired layers.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, separate your hair into four sections: top, left side, right side, and back. Use your clips to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut the hair at an angle, moving up and outwards. Repeat this technique for each section of hair in the back until you're satisfied with the length.

Step 4: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the same cutting technique as before, cutting at an angle and moving up and outwards. Repeat this for each section until you've achieved the desired length.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use the same cutting technique as before, cutting at an angle and moving up and outwards. Repeat this for each section until you've achieved the desired length.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the sections, it's time to blend the layers. Use your comb to comb through your hair, and use your scissors to trim any uneven ends.

Tips:

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you cut your hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair
  • Use a mirror to check your progress
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Take breaks if you need to

FAQ:

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting short layered haircuts:

Q: How often should I cut my short layered haircut?

A: It's recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be misleading and may result in uneven cutting.

Q: Can I use regular household scissors to cut my hair?

A: It's recommended to use sharp hair cutting scissors, as regular household scissors may result in uneven cutting and damage to your hair.

Hair Care:

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut

Cutting a short layered haircut yourself can be a fun and rewarding experience. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and follow these steps. Happy cutting!
