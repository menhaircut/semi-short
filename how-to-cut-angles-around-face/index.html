How to Cut Angles Around Face

Cutting angles around the face can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. Angled cuts are a great way to add dimension and interest to any hairstyle, and can be customized to suit any face shape. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors or shears
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps for a perfect angled cut:

  1. Begin by washing and drying your hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair so you can see the true length and texture of your hair.

  2. Section your hair into four equal parts - two at the front and two at the back. Use your comb to separate your hair and clip each section in place.

  3. Take the front section of your hair and comb it forward. Determine the length of the hair you want to cut, and cut a straight line across. This will be your guide for the rest of the cut.

  4. Starting at the back of the head, take a small section of hair and comb it straight up, perpendicular to the head. Use your guide to cut a straight line across the hair.

  5. Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting straight lines across, working your way towards the front of the head. Be sure to cut each section to the same length as your guide.

  6. Once you've reached the front of the head, it's time to cut the angles. Take a small section of hair from the front and comb it forward. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut diagonally from the guide to the bottom of the hair.

  7. Repeat this process on both sides of the head, taking small sections of hair and cutting diagonally from the guide to the bottom of the hair.

  8. Blend the angles into the rest of the hair by taking small sections of hair and cutting at a slight angle towards the face. This will create a seamless transition between the angled sections and the rest of the hair.

  9. Once you're happy with your cut, use your comb to check for any uneven sections or stray hairs. Trim as necessary.

  10. Style your hair as desired and enjoy your new angled look!

Tips for Success

Cutting angles around the face can be tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Start with a smaller angle and work your way up. It's easier to add more angle than to remove it.
  • Use a spray bottle to keep your hair damp while cutting. This will make it easier to control and cut evenly.
  • Be sure to cut in a well-lit area so you can see the true color and texture of your hair.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. A good cut takes time and patience.
  • Invest in a good pair of shears or scissors. This will make the cutting process much easier and more precise.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting angles around the face:

  • What face shapes are best suited for angled cuts?

    • Angled cuts can work on any face shape, but they are especially flattering on oval and heart-shaped faces.

  • Can I cut angles in curly hair?

    • Absolutely! Angled cuts can look great on curly hair, but it's important to work with a stylist who knows how to cut curly hair.

  • How often should I cut my hair?

    • It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain the health and shape of your hair.

Hair Care

Now that you have your perfect angled cut, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools too often, as this can damage your hair. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your cut and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as this can damage it and affect the color.

And there you have it - a complete guide on how to cut angles around the face. With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve the perfect angled cut every time. Happy cutting!
