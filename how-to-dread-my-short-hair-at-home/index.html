How To Dread My Short Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your short hair and want to try something new? Dreadlocks can be a great option for a unique and edgy hairstyle. But, you don't have to go to a salon to get them. In this article, we will show you how to dread your short hair at home with a step-by-step guide.

How to Dread My Short Hair at Home

Dreading your hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. Here are some things you will need:

  • A comb
  • A crochet hook
  • Hair clips
  • A spray bottle filled with water
  • African black soap or residue-free shampoo
  • A towel
  • A lot of patience

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to dread your short hair:

  1. Wash your hair with African black soap or residue-free shampoo. Avoid using conditioner, as it can make your hair slippery and difficult to dread.
  2. Towel-dry your hair and use a comb to section it into small, even sections. Use hair clips to secure each section.
  3. Take one section of hair and use your crochet hook to backcomb it. This means combing the hair towards the roots in the opposite direction of hair growth. Continue until the section of hair becomes tangled and matted.
  4. Use your fingers to roll the section of hair tightly into a dreadlock. Use the crochet hook to pull any loose hairs back into the dreadlock.
  5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for each section of hair.
  6. Once you have completed all the sections, use the spray bottle to mist your hair with water. This will help the hair lock and tighten the dreadlocks.
  7. Let your hair air dry. Avoid using a hair dryer, as it can cause frizz and damage to your hair.

Tips for Dreading Short Hair

Here are some tips to help you dread your short hair:

  • Start with clean, dry hair. Dreading dirty or oily hair can make the process more difficult.
  • Use a crochet hook to backcomb your hair. This will help create tight, even dreadlocks.
  • Be patient. Dreading short hair can take time, but the end result is worth it.
  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid product buildup in your dreadlocks.
  • Wear a headscarf or hat at night to prevent your dreadlocks from unraveling.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about dreading short hair:

Q: Can I still style my hair with dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can still style your hair with dreadlocks. You can braid them, tie them up, or even add hair accessories.

Q: How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

A: You should wash your dreadlocks every 1-2 weeks. Avoid washing them too often, as it can cause frizz and damage to your hair.

Q: Can I get rid of my dreadlocks?

A: Yes, you can get rid of your dreadlocks. You can cut them off or use a special dreadlock removal shampoo.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Here are some tips for taking care of your dreadlocks:

  • Wash them regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy products or chemicals on your dreadlocks.
  • Trim any loose or frizzy hairs to keep your dreadlocks looking neat.
  • Wear a headscarf or hat in extreme weather conditions to protect your dreadlocks.

Now that you know how to dread your short hair at home, go ahead and give it a try. With a little practice, you can achieve a unique and stylish look.
