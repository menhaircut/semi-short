How Do I Determine My Hair Type

Written by Christine Jun 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to determine your hair type? You are not alone. Knowing your hair type is important for choosing the right hair products and styling techniques. In this article, we will guide you on how to determine your hair type and provide some tips on how to take care of your hair.

Table of Contents

  • What is Hair Type?
  • How to Determine Hair Type
  • Hair Type Categories
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQs

What is Hair Type?

Hair type is determined by the shape of your hair follicle and the amount of sebum your scalp produces. There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Each hair type has different characteristics and requires different hair care routines.

How to Determine Hair Type

To determine your hair type, you can follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Avoid using any styling products or oils. Allow your hair to air dry without using any heat tools.

Step 2: Observe Your Hair

Observe your hair to see how it naturally falls. Look at the shape of your strands and the pattern of your hair. Pay attention to the texture, thickness, and elasticity of your hair.

Step 3: Take the Hair Strand Test

Take a single strand of hair and examine it closely. Stretch the strand and see how it behaves. Does it bounce back, or does it break easily? This will help you determine your hair's elasticity.

Hair Type Categories

Based on the shape of your hair follicle and the amount of sebum your scalp produces, your hair can be classified into one of the following categories:

Straight Hair (Type 1)

Straight hair is characterized by a straight and smooth texture. It reflects light well and is usually oily at the roots.

Wavy Hair (Type 2)

Wavy hair has a slight curl pattern and is prone to frizz. It can be further classified into 2A, 2B, and 2C, depending on the amount of curl.

Curly Hair (Type 3)

Curly hair has a definite curl pattern and is prone to dryness. It can be further classified into 3A, 3B, and 3C, depending on the tightness of the curl.

Coily Hair (Type 4)

Coily hair has a tight curl pattern and is prone to shrinkage. It can be further classified into 4A, 4B, and 4C, depending on the size of the curl.

Hair Care Tips

Once you have determined your hair type, you can follow these hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that is specifically formulated for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat tools on a daily basis.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can cause breakage.

FAQs

Q. How often should I wash my hair?

A. It depends on your hair type and how oily your scalp is. If you have oily hair, you may need to wash it every other day. If you have dry hair, you can wash it once or twice a week.

Q. Can I change my hair type?

A. No, you cannot change the natural shape of your hair follicle. However, you can use styling techniques and products to enhance your hair's natural texture.

Q. How do I prevent breakage?

A. You can prevent breakage by avoiding heat tools, using a wide-tooth comb, and deep conditioning your hair regularly.

In conclusion, determining your hair type is an important step in taking care of your hair. Follow the steps outlined in this article to determine your hair type and use the tips provided to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
