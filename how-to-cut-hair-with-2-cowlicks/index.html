How To Cut Hair With 2 Cowlicks A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to cut hair with 2 cowlicks? Don't worry, you're not alone. Cowlicks can be a challenge for even the most experienced hairdressers. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to cut hair with 2 cowlicks, along with some tips and tricks to make the process easier.

Description

Cowlicks are areas of hair that grow in a different direction than the rest of the hair. They can be frustrating to deal with, especially when it comes to cutting hair. Cowlicks can cause uneven or choppy haircuts if not handled correctly. If you have 2 cowlicks, it can be even more challenging to achieve a smooth and even haircut.

How to Cut Hair with 2 Cowlicks

Follow these steps to cut hair with 2 cowlicks:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut and may cause the cowlicks to appear differently than they will when the hair is dry.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Section the hair into manageable sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to cut the hair evenly and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Start at the Back

Begin cutting at the back of the head, where the cowlicks are usually the strongest. Use a comb to guide the hair and make sure you're cutting evenly.

Step 4: Work on One Side at a Time

Once you've finished the back, move on to one side of the head. Use your comb to guide the hair and cut it evenly. Make sure you're not cutting too much hair at once, as this can cause unevenness.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Once you've finished one side, move on to the other side and repeat the process. Make sure you're cutting the hair evenly and not cutting too much at once.

Step 6: Check for Unevenness

After you've finished cutting, check for any unevenness. If you notice any areas that need fixing, go back and fix them.

Tips

Here are some tips to make cutting hair with 2 cowlicks easier:

  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Use a comb to guide the hair and ensure evenness
  • Take breaks if you need to rest your hands

FAQ

What is a cowlick?

A cowlick is an area of hair that grows in a different direction than the rest of the hair. Cowlicks can be frustrating to deal with, especially when it comes to cutting hair.

How do I know if I have 2 cowlicks?

If you have 2 cowlicks, you'll notice that there are two areas of hair that grow in a different direction than the rest of the hair. These areas may be more difficult to cut and may require extra attention.

Can I cut my own hair with 2 cowlicks?

If you're comfortable cutting your own hair and have experience cutting hair, you can cut your own hair with 2 cowlicks. However, if you're not experienced with cutting hair, it's best to leave it to a professional.

Hair Care

After you've finished cutting your hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat tools too often. If you're unsure about how to style your hair, ask your hairdresser for advice.

Cutting hair with 2 cowlicks can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, it's possible to achieve a great haircut. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this article, and you'll be on your way to a smooth and even haircut.
