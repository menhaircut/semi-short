Do Dreads Take Long To Grow Tips And Faqs

Written by Daniel Sep 05, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're considering getting dreadlocks, one of the most common questions you may be asking is, "do dreads take long to grow?" The answer is yes, dreads can take a while to grow, but it ultimately depends on several factors. In this article, we'll explore some tips and FAQs about growing dreadlocks, so you can make an informed decision about whether or not to take the plunge.

Factors that Affect Dreadlock Growth

Before we dive into the tips and FAQs, let's first discuss the factors that can affect how quickly your dreads grow:

  • Hair type - Some hair types, such as coarser and curlier hair, tend to dread faster than others.
  • Hair length - The longer your hair is, the longer it will take for your dreads to fully form.
  • Hair thickness - Thicker hair can take longer to fully lock up than thinner hair.
  • Method of dread creation - Different methods of creating dreads, such as backcombing or free-forming, can affect how quickly your dreads form.
  • Hair care routine - Proper hair care is essential for healthy dread growth. Neglecting to care for your hair properly can slow down the locking process.

How to Grow Dreadlocks

Now that you understand the factors that can affect dreadlock growth, let's discuss some tips for growing healthy, strong dreads:

1. Start with clean, dry hair.

Before starting the dread creation process, make sure your hair is clean and free of any products. Dry your hair completely before beginning.

2. Choose a method of dread creation.

There are several methods of dread creation, including backcombing, twisting, and free-forming. Do some research and choose the method that best suits your hair type and desired outcome.

3. Be patient.

As we mentioned earlier, dreads can take a while to fully form. Be patient and resist the urge to constantly manipulate your hair or add products that can slow down the process.

4. Maintain healthy hair care habits.

Proper hair care is essential for healthy dreadlock growth. This includes regular washing and conditioning, using natural products, and avoiding heat styling and harsh chemicals.

FAQs about Dreadlock Growth

Here are some frequently asked questions about growing dreadlocks:

How long does it take for dreads to fully form?

It can take anywhere from 6 months to 2 years for dreads to fully form, depending on the factors we mentioned earlier.

Can you speed up the dread formation process?

While you can't necessarily speed up the process, there are things you can do to promote healthy dread growth, such as maintaining a healthy hair care routine and avoiding harsh chemicals and heat styling.

Do you need to cut your hair to get dreads?

No, you don't necessarily need to cut your hair to get dreads. However, if you have very long hair, you may need to trim it to make the locking process easier.

Can you undo dreads once they're formed?

Yes, you can undo dreads, but it can be a lengthy and sometimes damaging process. If you're not committed to having dreads long-term, it may be best to avoid creating them altogether.

Conclusion

So, do dreads take long to grow? Yes, they can take a while, but the end result can be well worth the wait. By following the tips and FAQs we discussed in this article, you can promote healthy dread growth and enjoy the journey of creating and maintaining your unique, beautiful dreads.
