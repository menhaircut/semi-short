What Hairstyle Fits My Face Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Sep 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? Choosing the right hairstyle can be overwhelming, especially when you're trying to find one that fits your face shape. In this article, we will guide you through the process of determining your face shape and suggest hairstyles that will help you enhance your features. Let's get started!

What is Face Shape?

Before we dive into hairstyles, it's essential to understand what face shape is. The shape of your face is determined by the bone structure of your skull, which includes your jawline, cheekbones, and forehead. There are six different face shapes, including round, oval, square, triangle, diamond, and heart-shaped.

How to Determine Your Face Shape?

There are several ways to determine your face shape. One of the easiest ways is to take a picture of yourself with your hair pulled back, and then trace the outline of your face on the photo. Alternatively, you can use a measuring tape to measure the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Once you have your measurements, compare them to each other to determine your face shape.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, you want to create the illusion of length and slimness. Hairstyles that add height to the crown and length to the sides, such as a pompadour, undercut, or side-swept fringe, work best for you.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you're in luck because you can pull off almost any hairstyle. Your face shape is well-balanced, and hairstyles that add volume to the top or sides, such as a quiff, messy fringe, or slicked-back undercut, work best for you.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, you want to soften your angular features. Hairstyles that add volume to the crown and length to the sides, such as a textured crop, messy fringe, or side-parted undercut, work best for you.

Triangle Face Shape

If you have a triangle face shape, you want to widen your forehead and narrow your jawline. Hairstyles that add volume to the top and sides, such as a messy textured fringe, slicked-back undercut, or side-parted medium length, work best for you.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, you want to balance your narrow forehead and jawline. Hairstyles that add volume to the top and sides and reduce volume at the cheekbones, such as a messy fringe, side-swept undercut, or side-parted medium length, work best for you.

Heart-Shaped Face Shape

If you have a heart-shaped face, you want to balance your forehead and narrow chin. Hairstyles that add volume to the crown and reduce volume at the temples and cheekbones, such as a textured fringe, slicked-back undercut, or side-parted medium length, work best for you.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hairstyle

Now that you know your face shape and which hairstyles work best for you, here are some tips to help you choose the right hairstyle:

  • Consider your hair type and texture when choosing a hairstyle.
  • Bring a picture or two of the hairstyle you want to show your stylist.
  • Choose a hairstyle that matches your lifestyle and personality.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles until you find the one that works best for you.

FAQ

What if I have a receding hairline?

If you have a receding hairline, you want to choose a hairstyle that creates the illusion of a fuller head of hair. Hairstyles that add volume to the top and sides, such as a messy textured fringe or a side-swept undercut, work best for you.

What if I have curly hair?

If you have curly hair, you want to choose a hairstyle that works with your natural curls. Hairstyles that add volume and shape to your curls, such as a curly fringe or a textured crop, work best for you.

Hair Care

Now that you have chosen the right hairstyle, it's essential to take care of your hair to maintain its shape and style. Here are some tips to help you maintain your hairstyle:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as blow dryers and straighteners, too often.
  • Use hair products, such as pomade, wax, or styling cream, to add volume and hold to your hairstyle.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and style of your haircut.

Congratulations! You now know which hairstyle fits your face shape and how to maintain it. Remember to choose a hairstyle that matches your personality and lifestyle and experiment with different styles until you find the one that works best for you. Happy styling!
