How To Cut Thick Hair With Thinning Scissors A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jul 04, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cutting thick hair can be a challenge, especially if you want to achieve a layered look without making the hair look too bulky. This is where thinning scissors come in handy. Thinning scissors are designed to remove bulk from the hair, creating a softer and more textured look. In this article, we will show you how to cut thick hair with thinning scissors.

Description

Thinning scissors are scissors with teeth that are used to remove bulk from the hair. They come in various sizes and tooth widths, with the larger tooth widths being more suitable for thicker hair. Thinning scissors are often used in conjunction with regular scissors to achieve a layered look.

How to Cut Thick Hair with Thinning Scissors

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools. You will need a pair of thinning scissors, regular scissors, a comb, and hair clips. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair. Make sure the hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Section the hair into manageable sections using the hair clips. This will make it easier to cut the hair evenly.

Step 3: Cut with Regular Scissors

Use the regular scissors to cut the hair to the desired length. Make sure the hair is cut evenly and straight.

Step 4: Use Thinning Scissors

Use the thinning scissors to remove bulk from the hair. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up, taking small sections at a time. Make sure you don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven look.

Step 5: Blend with Regular Scissors

Use the regular scissors to blend the hair that you have cut with the thinning scissors. This will help to create a natural and seamless look.

Step 6: Repeat

Repeat steps 3 to 5 for each section of the hair until you have achieved the desired look.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting thick hair with thinning scissors:

  • Use the right type of thinning scissors for your hair type
  • Take your time and don't rush the process
  • Use hair clips to section the hair and keep it organized
  • Make sure the hair is completely dry before you start cutting
  • Cut small sections at a time to avoid unevenness
  • Blend with regular scissors to create a natural look

FAQ

Can thinning scissors damage hair?

When used properly, thinning scissors should not damage hair. However, if you use them incorrectly or use the wrong type of scissors for your hair type, you can cause damage.

Can I use thinning scissors on curly hair?

Yes, you can use thinning scissors on curly hair. However, you need to be careful not to overdo it, as thinning scissors can make curly hair look frizzy if used too much.

How often should I use thinning scissors on my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. As a general rule, you should use thinning scissors every 4-6 weeks to maintain your haircut.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair with thinning scissors, it is important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and appearance. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair hydrated
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut

With these tips, you can cut your thick hair with thinning scissors and achieve a beautiful and natural look. Remember to take your time and be patient, as cutting hair can be a time-consuming process.
