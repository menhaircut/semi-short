Why Am I Always Shedding Hair

Written by Robby Nov 18, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you constantly finding loose strands of hair on your clothes, pillow, and in the shower drain? If you’re wondering why you’re always shedding hair, you’re not alone. Many people experience excessive hair shedding and it can be a cause for concern. In this article, we’ll explore the reasons why you may be shedding hair and provide tips on how to reduce it.

Description

Hair shedding is a natural process that occurs as part of the hair growth cycle. On average, we lose between 50-100 hairs a day. However, if you’re losing more than this, it could be a sign of a problem. Excessive hair shedding can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, hormonal changes, nutritional deficiencies, and certain medications.

How to Determine if You’re Shedding Hair Excessively

If you’re not sure whether you’re shedding hair excessively, there are a few ways to determine it. You can start by looking at your hairbrush or comb after you’ve used it. If there’s a lot of hair on it, this could indicate excessive shedding. You can also look at the hair in your shower drain after you’ve washed your hair. If there’s a large amount of hair, this could also be a sign of excessive shedding.

Step by Step Tips to Reduce Hair Shedding

Step 1: Eat a Balanced Diet

Nutritional deficiencies can contribute to hair shedding. Make sure you’re eating a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, fruits, and vegetables. Iron, biotin, and zinc are also important for healthy hair growth.

Step 2: Manage Stress

Stress can cause hormonal changes that can lead to hair shedding. Try to manage your stress levels through exercise, meditation, or yoga.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and lead to shedding. Try to avoid using them as much as possible and let your hair air dry instead.

Step 4: Use Gentle Hair Products

Harsh chemicals in hair products can damage your hair and lead to shedding. Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners.

Tips for Hair Care

Tip 1: Brush Your Hair Gently

Brushing your hair too harshly can lead to hair breakage and shedding. Use a wide-toothed comb or a brush with soft bristles to gently detangle your hair.

Tip 2: Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can pull on your hair and lead to shedding. Try to wear your hair in looser styles.

Tip 3: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and lead to shedding. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to shed hair every day?

A: Yes, it’s normal to shed between 50-100 hairs a day as part of the hair growth cycle.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can cause hormonal changes that can lead to hair shedding.

Q: Can nutritional deficiencies cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, nutritional deficiencies, such as iron or biotin deficiency, can contribute to hair shedding.

In Conclusion

Excessive hair shedding can be a cause for concern, but there are steps you can take to reduce it. By eating a balanced diet, managing stress, avoiding heat styling, and using gentle hair products, you can help keep your hair healthy and reduce shedding. Remember to also brush your hair gently, avoid tight hairstyles, and protect your hair from the sun. If you’re still experiencing excessive shedding after making these changes, it’s important to consult with a healthcare professional.
