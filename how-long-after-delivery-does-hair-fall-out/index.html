How Long After Delivery Does Hair Fall Out

After giving birth, many women experience hair loss, which can be alarming if you don't know what to expect. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about how long after delivery does hair fall out, why it happens, and what you can do about it.

Why Hair Falls Out After Delivery

Hair loss after delivery is a normal process that happens due to hormonal changes in the body. During pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen hormone cause hair to stay in the growing phase, resulting in thicker and fuller hair. However, after delivery, estrogen levels drop, leading to the hair entering the resting phase and eventually falling out.

Another factor that contributes to hair loss after delivery is physical stress. Giving birth can be a traumatic experience for the body, and the stress can push hair follicles into the resting phase, resulting in hair loss.

When Hair Loss Starts After Delivery

Hair loss after delivery usually starts around three months after giving birth, but it can vary from person to person. Some women may experience hair loss sooner, while others may not experience it until several months later.

How Long Hair Loss Lasts After Delivery

Hair loss after delivery is usually temporary and can last for up to six months. However, some women may experience hair loss for a longer period, up to a year. After the hair loss stops, hair growth usually returns to normal within a few months.

How to Prevent Hair Loss After Delivery

While it's not possible to prevent hair loss after delivery completely, there are some things you can do to minimize it:

  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet with plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair and cause breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  • Try to reduce stress levels through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques.

Hair Care Tips After Delivery

After delivery, it's essential to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth and minimize hair loss:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can strip the scalp of natural oils.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals like hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  • Avoid pulling your hair back tightly, as it can cause breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Does breastfeeding cause hair loss after delivery?

No, breastfeeding does not cause hair loss after delivery. It's the hormonal changes in the body that cause hair loss.

2. Will hair loss after delivery lead to baldness?

No, hair loss after delivery is a temporary condition and does not lead to baldness.

3. Can I use hair growth products after delivery?

It's best to avoid using hair growth products after delivery, as they can contain harsh chemicals that can further damage hair. Instead, focus on eating a healthy diet and taking care of your hair to promote healthy growth.

4. When should I see a doctor about my hair loss?

If you're experiencing excessive hair loss or hair loss that lasts longer than six months, you should see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Now that you know how long after delivery does hair fall out, you can take steps to minimize hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be gentle with your hair, eat a healthy diet, and take care of yourself to promote overall health and wellness.
