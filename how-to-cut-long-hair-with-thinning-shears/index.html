How To Cut Long Hair With Thinning Shears A Complete Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 24, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long hair looking flat and lifeless? Do you want to add some texture and volume to your locks? Thinning shears are a great tool to use for achieving just that. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting long hair with thinning shears, from start to finish. Whether you're a professional hairstylist or a beginner, this guide will help you achieve the perfect haircut.

Description

Thinning shears, also known as texturizing shears, are a type of scissors that have teeth on one or both blades. These teeth are used to cut hair in a way that removes bulk and adds texture. Thinning shears are great for creating layers, blending different lengths, and adding volume to hair. They are a popular tool among hairstylists and can be used on all hair types, including thick and curly hair.

How to Cut Long Hair with Thinning Shears

Cutting long hair with thinning shears requires a different technique than cutting with regular scissors. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a more accurate cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable sections using hair clips. Start with the bottom section of your hair and work your way up. This will help you see what you're doing and prevent you from cutting too much hair at once.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and cut into the hair, starting at the ends. Make sure you only cut a small amount of hair at a time. Take your time and work your way up the section of hair, thinning it out as you go.

Step 4: Blend the Layers

Repeat the previous step on each section of hair, blending the layers as you go. Make sure you're cutting at the same angle and taking the same amount of hair each time.

Step 5: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, check your work in the mirror. Make sure the layers are blended and even. If you notice any unevenness, go back and make adjustments as needed.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair with Thinning Shears

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle to achieve the best results.
  • Make sure the hair is dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with and can result in an uneven cut.
  • Use a comb to help guide the thinning shears and ensure a more precise cut.
  • Don't overdo it. Thinning shears can remove a lot of hair quickly, so be careful not to thin out your hair too much.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long hair with thinning shears:

Can you cut long hair with thinning shears?

Yes, you can cut long hair with thinning shears. In fact, they are a great tool for adding texture and volume to long hair.

How do you use thinning shears on long hair?

To use thinning shears on long hair, section your hair into manageable sections, hold the shears at a 45-degree angle, and cut into the hair, starting at the ends. Work your way up each section of hair, blending the layers as you go.

What's the difference between thinning shears and regular scissors?

Thinning shears have teeth on one or both blades, which are used to remove bulk and add texture to the hair. Regular scissors have two smooth blades and are used to cut hair in a straight line.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your hair with thinning shears, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends from forming.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet with plenty of vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and strength.

Conclusion

Cutting long hair with thinning shears can be a great way to add texture and volume to your locks. Follow the steps and tips outlined in this guide to achieve the perfect haircut. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Happy cutting!
