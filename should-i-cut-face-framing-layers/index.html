Should I Cut Face Framing Layers

Description

Face framing layers are a popular hairstyle that can enhance your features and add dimension to your hair. These layers are cut around the face to draw attention to your cheekbones, eyes, and jawline. However, not everyone is sure if they should cut face framing layers. It's a personal choice that depends on your hair type, face shape, and styling preferences.

If you're considering cutting face framing layers, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, you should have healthy hair that is free of split ends and damage. Second, you should have a good understanding of your face shape and how the layers will complement your features. Third, you should have a clear idea of the style you want and how much hair you're willing to cut.

How to Cut Face Framing Layers

If you've decided to cut face framing layers, you can either do it yourself or go to a professional stylist. Cutting layers can be tricky, so if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to leave it to a pro. However, if you're up for the challenge, here's how to do it:

Tools you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Spray bottle with water

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start with clean, dry hair that is free of tangles. You can blow dry your hair or let it air dry.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use the comb to section off the hair around your face that you want to cut. Clip the rest of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Determine the length

Decide how short you want your layers to be. It's best to start with longer layers and work your way up if you want them shorter.

Step 4: Cut the hair

Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut at an angle. Cut the hair in small sections, working your way around your face.

Step 5: Check the length

Once you've finished cutting, check the length of your layers to make sure they're even. Make any necessary adjustments.

Step 6: Style your hair

Style your hair as desired, using a curling iron or flat iron to add volume and shape.

Tips for Cutting Face Framing Layers

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly to avoid making mistakes.
  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once.
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you're unsure about how to cut your hair.

FAQs

What face shape is best for face framing layers?

Face framing layers can work for any face shape, but they're especially flattering for oval and heart-shaped faces.

How much hair should I cut for face framing layers?

The amount of hair you cut depends on how short you want your layers to be. It's best to start with longer layers and work your way up if you want them shorter.

Can I cut face framing layers on curly hair?

Yes, you can cut face framing layers on curly hair. However, it's important to cut the layers when your hair is dry so you can see how the curls will fall.

Hair Care After Cutting Face Framing Layers

After cutting face framing layers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Avoid using heated styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

In conclusion, cutting face framing layers is a personal choice that depends on your hair type, face shape, and styling preferences. If you decide to cut your hair, make sure you have healthy hair, a good understanding of your face shape, and a clear idea of the style you want. You can either cut your hair yourself or go to a professional stylist. After cutting your hair, take care of it with regular trims and quality hair care products.
