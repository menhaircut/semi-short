How To Cut Men s Hair On Sides A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Sep 14, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of paying for expensive haircuts at the barber shop? Learning how to cut your own hair can save you time and money in the long run. In this article, we'll be focusing on how to cut men's hair on the sides, one of the most important areas of the head.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Men's Hair on Sides
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

How to Cut Men's Hair on Sides

Cutting men's hair on the sides can be easy if you have the right tools and technique. Whether you want a fade, undercut, or classic side part, the process is generally the same.

Before you begin, make sure you have a good set of clippers, scissors, comb, and mirror. It's also helpful to have a spray bottle of water to dampen the hair and a cloth to wipe the clippers between cuts.

Step 1: Decide on the Length

The first step in cutting men's hair on the sides is to decide on the length you want. This will depend on your personal style and the type of haircut you are going for. Use the clippers with the desired guard length to trim the hair evenly all around the head.

Step 2: Blend the Hair

To create a smooth transition from the longer hair on top to the shorter hair on the sides, you'll need to blend the hair. Use a clipper with a shorter guard length to trim the hair around the ears and the back of the head. Then, use scissors to blend the hair between the longer and shorter sections, making sure to cut at an angle for a more natural look.

Step 3: Define the Hairline

To create a clean and sharp look, define the hairline along the neck and around the ears. Use the clippers with no guard to trim the hair along the hairline, making sure to follow the natural curve of the head. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the basic steps, let's dive deeper into the process of cutting men's hair on the sides.

Step 1: Wash and Dry the Hair

Start by washing the hair with shampoo and conditioner to remove any dirt and oils. Towel-dry the hair and comb it in the direction it naturally falls.

Step 2: Decide on the Haircut

Before you begin cutting, decide on the type of haircut you want. Look at pictures online or in magazines to get inspiration and ideas.

Step 3: Section the Hair

Use the comb to divide the hair into sections, starting at the crown and moving down to the nape of the neck.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

Use the clippers with the desired guard length to trim the hair evenly all around the head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, making sure to keep the clippers level with the head. Use the scissors to blend the hair between the longer and shorter sections, cutting at a 45-degree angle for a natural look.

Step 5: Define the Hairline

Use the clippers with no guard to trim the hair along the hairline, making sure to follow the natural curve of the head. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs.

Tips for a Perfect Cut

Cutting men's hair on the sides can be challenging, but with these tips, you can achieve a flawless look:

  • Use sharp scissors and clippers to avoid pulling or tugging on the hair.
  • Work slowly and carefully, making small cuts at a time.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of the head and the sides.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help if you're having trouble.
  • Practice makes perfect – don't be afraid to make mistakes and learn from them.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair on the sides?

Yes, with the right tools and technique, cutting men's hair on the sides can be easy and cost-effective.

What is the best length for men's hair on the sides?

The length you choose will depend on your personal style and the type of haircut you are going for. Generally, a length of 1/8 to 1/4 inch is common for men's hair on the sides.

How often should I cut my hair on the sides?

This will depend on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. Generally, cutting men's hair on the sides every 3-4 weeks is recommended.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting men's hair on the sides, it's important to take care of the hair to maintain its shape and style. Use a quality shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and healthy. Apply a styling product, such as wax or pomade, to create the desired look. Avoid using too much heat on the hair, as this can cause damage and dryness.

Cutting men's hair on the sides can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. Happy cutting!
