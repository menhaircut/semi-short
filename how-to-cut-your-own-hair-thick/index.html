How To Cut Your Own Thick Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have thick hair, you know how difficult it can be to maintain it. Going to the salon every few months can be time-consuming and expensive, so why not cut your own hair at home? With the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair can be easy and cost-effective. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own thick hair.

How to Cut Your Own Hair Thick: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step in cutting your own thick hair is to gather the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror. It is important to use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut and prevent split ends.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, it is important to wash and dry it. This will ensure that your hair is free from any tangles or knots, making it easier to cut. Once your hair is dry, comb it to remove any remaining tangles.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use hair clips to section your hair into manageable sections. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly. Start by sectioning your hair into four parts: two at the front and two at the back.

Step 4: Cut the Front Sections

Start by cutting the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the ends at an angle. Repeat this process for each section of hair, being sure to cut each section to the same length.

Step 5: Cut the Back Sections

Once you have finished cutting the front sections, move on to the back. This can be a little trickier, but it is still manageable. Take a small section of hair from the back and comb it straight. Hold the section between your fingers and use your scissors to cut the ends at an angle. Repeat this process for each section of hair, being sure to cut each section to the same length.

Step 6: Check for Evenness

After you have finished cutting all sections of your hair, it is important to check for evenness. Use your comb to check that each section is the same length. If you notice any unevenness, go back and trim the ends until each section is even.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you have finished cutting your hair, it is time to style it. Blow-dry your hair and use a straightener or curling iron to add some shape and texture. If you prefer a more natural look, let your hair air dry and use some styling products to add volume and texture.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own thick hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections
  • Cut your hair in a well-lit area to ensure that you can see what you are doing
  • Be patient and take your time â rushing can lead to mistakes and unevenness
  • Use hair clips to keep your hair in place and prevent it from getting tangled
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its health

FAQs

Can I cut my own thick hair?

Yes, you can cut your own thick hair. With the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair can be easy and cost-effective.

What tools do I need to cut my own thick hair?

You will need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a handheld mirror.

How often should I cut my own thick hair?

You should aim to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain its health.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

Cutting your own thick hair can be easy and cost-effective with the right tools and techniques. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can achieve a salon-quality cut at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and always use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut. With a little practice, you can become a pro at cutting your own hair!
