Does Baby Body Hair Fall Out

Written by Daniel Jul 29, 2023 Â· 3 min read

If you're a new parent, you may be wondering if it's normal for your baby to have hair all over their body. Some babies are born with a lot of hair, while others have very little. But what about that hair on their arms, legs, and even their backs? Does baby body hair fall out?

Description

Body hair on babies is actually quite common. This is because while in the womb, babies develop a fine hair called lanugo that covers their entire body. This hair helps to regulate their body temperature while they're still in the uterus.

After birth, some babies still have lanugo hair on their body, while others have lost it. This is completely normal and nothing to worry about. In fact, most babies lose their body hair within the first few weeks or months of life.

How to Tell if Baby Body Hair is Falling Out

It can be hard to tell if your baby's body hair is falling out, especially if they didn't have much to begin with. However, there are a few signs to look out for:

  • You may notice small patches of hair missing from your baby's body.
  • You may start to find small hairs on your baby's clothes or in their crib.
  • Your baby's hair may start to look patchy or uneven.

Step-by-Step Guide to Baby Body Hair Falling Out

While every baby is different, here is a general guide to how baby body hair falls out:

  1. Within the first few weeks of life, your baby's body hair may start to thin out.
  2. Over the next few weeks or months, you may notice small patches of hair missing from your baby's body.
  3. Eventually, your baby's body hair will completely fall out, leaving them with the hair they will have for the rest of their life.

Tips for Caring for Baby's Hair

While your baby's body hair falling out is completely normal, it's still important to take care of their hair as it grows in. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a gentle baby shampoo to wash your baby's hair, and only wash it a few times a week.
  • Don't use any hair products on your baby's hair, as their scalp is still very sensitive.
  • Brush your baby's hair gently with a soft brush or comb.

FAQs

When does baby body hair start to fall out?

Baby body hair can start to fall out within the first few weeks of life, but it can take a few months for it to completely disappear.

Is it normal for babies to have body hair?

Yes, it's completely normal for babies to have body hair, especially if they still have lanugo hair from being in the womb.

Will my baby's body hair grow back?

No, your baby's body hair will not grow back. Once it falls out, it's gone for good.

Hair Care for Babies

While baby body hair falling out is nothing to worry about, it's still important to take care of your baby's hair as it grows in. Use gentle products and be gentle when brushing or combing their hair. And remember, your baby's hair will continue to grow and change over time, so be prepared for a few surprises!
