Gray in the Bible

Gray is mentioned several times in the Bible, both in the Old and New Testaments. In the Old Testament, gray is often used to describe the hair of the elderly. For example, in Proverbs 16:31, it says, “Gray hair is a crown of glory; it is gained in a righteous life.”

In the New Testament, gray is not mentioned as frequently as in the Old Testament. However, it is used to describe the color of Jesus’ hair in Revelation 1:14, “The hairs of his head were white, like white wool, like snow.”

Symbolism of Gray

In the Bible, gray symbolizes wisdom, humility, and balance. Gray is often associated with the elderly who have gained wisdom through their experiences. It is also associated with humility as it is a color that is neither black nor white, but somewhere in between.

Gray is also a color of balance, representing the balance between good and evil, light and dark. It is a color that reminds us of the importance of finding balance in our lives.
