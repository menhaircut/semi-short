Are Eyebrows Removed Before Microblading

Written by Christine Jun 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Welcome to our blog post about one of the most common questions asked by people who are curious about microblading: are eyebrows removed before the treatment? Microblading is a semi-permanent cosmetic procedure that involves the insertion of pigment into the skin to create the appearance of fuller, more defined eyebrows. In this article, we will answer this question in detail and provide helpful tips and information about the microblading process.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Microblading is a cosmetic procedure that involves the use of a handheld tool to create tiny, hair-like strokes in the skin to mimic the appearance of natural eyebrow hairs. The pigment used in microblading is semi-permanent and fades over time, typically lasting between 1-3 years. The procedure is done by a trained and certified microblading artist who will work with you to create a customized brow shape and color that complements your facial features.

How to

The microblading process typically involves the following steps:

Consultation

During the consultation, you will discuss your desired brow shape and color with the microblading artist. They will also assess your skin type and determine if you are a good candidate for the procedure.

Numbing

A topical numbing cream will be applied to the brow area to minimize discomfort during the procedure.

Mapping

The microblading artist will use a pencil to draw the shape of your new brows, taking into consideration your natural brow shape and facial features. This step is crucial to ensure that you are happy with the final result.

Blading

The microblading artist will use a handheld tool to create tiny, hair-like strokes in the skin, depositing pigment into the skin to create the appearance of fuller brows. This step typically takes between 1-2 hours.

Aftercare

You will need to follow specific aftercare instructions to ensure that your new brows heal properly and last as long as possible. This typically involves avoiding water, sweat, and certain skincare products for several days after the procedure.

Step by Step

Here are the steps involved in the microblading process:

  1. Consultation with a microblading artist
  2. Topical numbing cream applied to the brow area
  3. Mapping of the brow shape and color
  4. Blading using a handheld tool to create hair-like strokes in the skin
  5. Aftercare instructions provided to ensure proper healing and longevity of the new brows

Tips

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind if you are considering microblading:

  • Research microblading artists in your area and choose one who is certified and has a portfolio of successful procedures.
  • Be prepared for some discomfort during the procedure, as it involves the use of a sharp blade in the skin.
  • Follow the aftercare instructions carefully to ensure that your new brows heal properly and last as long as possible.
  • Avoid sun exposure and certain skincare products for several days after the procedure.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about microblading:

Is microblading painful?

There may be some discomfort during the procedure, but a topical numbing cream is applied to minimize pain.

How long does microblading last?

Microblading typically lasts between 1-3 years, but this can vary depending on individual factors such as skin type and aftercare.

Are there any risks associated with microblading?

As with any cosmetic procedure, there are some risks involved, such as infection or allergic reaction. However, these risks can be minimized by choosing a certified and experienced microblading artist and following proper aftercare instructions.

Hair Care

After the microblading procedure, it is essential to take care of your new brows to ensure proper healing and longevity. Here are some hair care tips to follow:

  • Avoid water, sweat, and certain skincare products for several days after the procedure.
  • Avoid sun exposure and wear a hat or sunglasses to protect your new brows.
  • Avoid picking or scratching the treated area.
  • Apply a thin layer of ointment to the treated area as directed by your microblading artist.

Overall, microblading is a safe and effective way to achieve fuller, more defined brows. By following proper aftercare instructions and choosing a certified and experienced microblading artist, you can enjoy beautiful, long-lasting results.
