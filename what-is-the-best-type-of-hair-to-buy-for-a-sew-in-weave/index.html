What Is The Best Type Of Hair To Buy For A Sew In Weave

Are you considering getting a sew in weave but unsure about what type of hair to buy? With so many options on the market, it can be overwhelming to choose the best type of hair for your sew in weave. In this article, we will guide you through the different types of hair available and help you make an informed decision.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Choose the Best Type of Hair for a Sew In Weave
  • Step by Step Guide

Description

A sew in weave is a popular hair extension method that involves sewing wefts of hair onto braided natural hair. Sew in weaves can add length, volume, and versatility to your hair without the commitment of a permanent hair extension. However, to achieve a natural and long-lasting look, it is important to choose the right type of hair for your sew in weave.

How to Choose the Best Type of Hair for a Sew In Weave

When choosing the best type of hair for a sew in weave, it is important to consider the following factors:

  • Quality: Look for high-quality hair that is soft, silky, and tangle-free. Cheap hair may be tempting, but it can cause more harm than good in the long run.
  • Texture: Choose a texture that matches your natural hair or the look you are trying to achieve. Popular textures include straight, wavy, curly, and kinky.
  • Length: Decide on the length you want before purchasing hair. Longer hair requires more bundles and can be more expensive.
  • Color: Consider the color of your natural hair and choose a hair color that matches or complements it. You can also opt for a different color to create a bold and unique look.
  • Brand: Do your research and choose a reputable brand that offers high-quality hair.

Step by Step Guide

Follow these steps to choose the best type of hair for your sew in weave:

  1. Decide on the texture, length, and color you want.
  2. Research reputable hair brands and read reviews.
  3. Choose a hair type that matches your natural hair or the look you are trying to achieve.
  4. Consider the quality of the hair and avoid cheap options.
  5. Purchase the necessary amount of hair bundles for your desired look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Sew In Weave

Here are some tips to help you maintain your sew in weave:

  • Wash and condition your natural hair and the sew in weave regularly.
  • Avoid using heavy products that can weigh down the hair.
  • Use a silk or satin scarf or pillowcase to protect the hair while sleeping.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as it can damage the hair.
  • Get regular touch-ups to maintain the look of your sew in weave.

FAQs

Q: Can I use synthetic hair for a sew in weave?

A: While synthetic hair may be cheaper, it is not recommended for sew in weaves. Synthetic hair does not blend well with natural hair and can be easily damaged by heat styling.

Q: How many bundles of hair do I need for a sew in weave?

A: The number of bundles you need depends on the length and fullness you want to achieve. For a full head sew in weave, you will typically need 2-4 bundles of hair.

Hair Care Tips for Your Sew In Weave

Here are some tips to keep your sew in weave looking its best:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of its natural oils.
  • Detangle the hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Avoid using hot tools on the hair, as it can cause damage and shorten the lifespan of the weave.
  • Keep the scalp moisturized with a light oil or serum.
  • Do not leave the sew in weave in for longer than 8 weeks to prevent damage and breakage.

By following these tips and choosing the best type of hair for your sew in weave, you can achieve a natural and long-lasting look that will turn heads.
