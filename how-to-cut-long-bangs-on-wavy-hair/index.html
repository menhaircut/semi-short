How To Cut Long Bangs On Wavy Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 21, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of your long, wavy bangs getting in your face all the time? It might be time for a trim! But cutting your own hair can be intimidating, especially if you don't have experience or know what you're doing. Luckily, with the right tools and some basic knowledge, you can easily learn how to cut long bangs on wavy hair at home.

Description

Long bangs can be a great way to frame your face and add some personality to your hairstyle, especially if you have wavy hair. However, over time, your bangs can become too long and start to get in your eyes, making it hard to see and style your hair. Cutting your own bangs might seem scary, but with some practice, you can master the art of trimming your own hair and save yourself some time and money in the long run.

How to Cut Long Bangs on Wavy Hair

To cut long bangs on wavy hair, you'll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips
  • Mirror or two

It's also a good idea to cut your hair when it's dry, so you can see how it will look once it's styled. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Section off your bangs

Using your comb or brush, separate your bangs from the rest of your hair. Clip the rest of your hair back to keep it out of the way.

Step 2: Decide on the length

With your scissors, cut your bangs to the desired length. It's best to start with a longer length and gradually cut more hair, rather than cutting too much at once.

Step 3: Cut at an angle

Hold your scissors at an angle and cut your bangs in a downward motion. This will help create a natural, feathered look.

Step 4: Check for symmetry

Use a mirror or two to check that your bangs are even and symmetrical. You can also use a comb to make sure they're straight.

Step 5: Style your bangs

Use a brush or your fingers to style your bangs as desired. You can also use a curling iron or straightener to add some extra texture.

Tips for Cutting Long Bangs on Wavy Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you get the perfect cut:

  • Start with a longer length than you think you want, and gradually cut more hair as needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut your bangs too short, as they will likely shrink up when they dry.
  • Consider cutting your bangs when they're dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut accurately.

FAQs about Cutting Long Bangs on Wavy Hair

How often should I cut my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly your bangs start to get in your eyes. Generally, you should plan to trim your bangs every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking fresh.

Can I cut my own bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can! However, curly hair can be more difficult to cut accurately, so it's important to be patient and take your time.

Should I wet my hair before cutting my bangs?

It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut accurately. However, if you prefer to cut your hair when it's wet, make sure to cut it a little longer than you want it to be, as it will likely shrink up as it dries.

Hair Care after Cutting Long Bangs on Wavy Hair

After you've cut your bangs, you'll want to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, especially when it's wet.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair mask to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

With these tips and tricks, you should feel confident and ready to tackle your long, wavy bangs at home. Happy cutting!
