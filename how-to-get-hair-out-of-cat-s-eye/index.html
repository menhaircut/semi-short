How To Get Hair Out Of Cat s Eye A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a cat owner, you know how important it is to keep your feline friend happy and healthy. But sometimes, even with the best care, your cat may get hair in their eye. This can be uncomfortable for your cat and even lead to infection if left untreated. In this article, we will guide you through the steps on how to get hair out of your cat's eye safely and effectively.

How to Get Hair Out of Cat's Eye

Before we dive into the steps, it's important to note that if your cat has hair in their eye, you should act quickly to avoid any further discomfort or complications. Here are the steps you should follow:

Step 1: Calm Your Cat

The first step to getting hair out of your cat's eye is to ensure your cat is calm. Cats can become agitated and anxious when they feel something in their eye, so it's important to approach them calmly and speak soothingly to them.

Step 2: Examine Your Cat's Eye

Next, examine your cat's eye to determine the location and size of the hair. You can use a flashlight to help you see more clearly. If the hair is near the surface of the eye, you may be able to remove it yourself. However, if the hair is deeper in the eye or you're unsure about removing it, it's best to take your cat to the vet.

Step 3: Remove the Hair

If you feel confident in removing the hair yourself, use a clean, damp cloth or cotton swab to gently wipe the hair away. Be careful not to touch the eye directly or apply too much pressure.

Step 4: Rinse Your Cat's Eye

After removing the hair, rinse your cat's eye with saline solution or sterile water. This will help flush out any remaining debris and reduce the risk of infection.

Step 5: Monitor Your Cat

Finally, monitor your cat for any signs of discomfort or infection. If your cat continues to paw at their eye, seems lethargic, or has discharge from their eye, contact your veterinarian immediately.

Tips for Hair Care to Avoid Eye Issues

Prevention is always better than cure. Here are some tips to help you prevent hair from getting into your cat's eyes:

  • Regular grooming: Brush your cat's fur regularly to reduce loose hairs that can get into their eyes.
  • Trimming: Trim the hair around your cat's eyes to reduce the likelihood of it getting into their eyes.
  • Keep your cat indoors: Cats that spend time outdoors are more likely to get debris in their eyes, including hair.
  • Use a humidifier: Dry air can cause your cat's hair to become static and more likely to get into their eyes. Using a humidifier in your home can help reduce the likelihood of this happening.

FAQs

Can hair in a cat's eye cause infection?

Yes, hair in a cat's eye can cause infection if left untreated. The hair can scratch the surface of the eye, providing a breeding ground for bacteria to grow.

Can I use human eye drops on my cat?

No, you should never use human eye drops on your cat. Some of the chemicals in human eye drops can be harmful to cats. Always use eye drops that are specifically designed for cats.

When should I take my cat to the vet?

You should take your cat to the vet if you're unsure about removing the hair yourself, or if your cat shows any signs of discomfort or infection after the hair has been removed.

Conclusion

Getting hair out of your cat's eye may seem like a daunting task, but with the right approach, it can be done safely and effectively. Remember to stay calm, examine your cat's eye, and remove the hair gently. If you're unsure or your cat shows any signs of discomfort or infection, don't hesitate to contact your veterinarian. And most importantly, take steps to prevent hair from getting into your cat's eyes in the first place.
