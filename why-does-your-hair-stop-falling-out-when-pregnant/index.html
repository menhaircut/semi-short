Why Does Your Hair Stop Falling Out When Pregnant

Written by William Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of the lucky few who experience thick and luscious locks during pregnancy? One of the benefits of being pregnant is that many women find that their hair stops falling out. This can be a welcome change for those who struggle with hair loss. In this blog post, we will discuss why this happens and provide tips on how to care for your hair during pregnancy.

Why Does Hair Stop Falling Out During Pregnancy?

During pregnancy, the body goes through many changes, including hormonal changes that can affect your hair. Normally, hair goes through a cycle of growth, rest, and shedding. However, during pregnancy, the increased levels of estrogen in the body cause the hair to remain in the growth phase for longer periods, which means that there is less shedding. This can result in thicker and healthier-looking hair.

Additionally, the increase in blood volume during pregnancy means that more nutrients and oxygen are delivered to the hair follicles. This can result in stronger and healthier hair.

How Do Hormones Affect Hair Growth?

The hormones that are responsible for hair growth are estrogen, progesterone, and testosterone. During pregnancy, estrogen levels increase significantly, which can promote hair growth. Estrogen also prolongs the growth phase of the hair cycle and delays the shedding phase, which can lead to thicker hair.

However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop dramatically, which can cause hair to fall out. This is known as postpartum hair loss and is a common experience for many women. However, this is usually temporary, and hair will eventually grow back.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair During Pregnancy

While you may not experience as much hair loss during pregnancy, it is still important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and strength. Here are some tips:

  1. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals.
  2. Avoid using heat-styling tools and opt for air-drying instead.
  3. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals that promote hair growth, such as iron, biotin, and vitamin D.
  4. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause damage.
  5. Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to nourish and strengthen your hair.

FAQs

When does hair start falling out after pregnancy?

Hair loss after pregnancy usually starts around three months after giving birth and can last up to a year.

Can prenatal vitamins prevent hair loss during pregnancy?

Prenatal vitamins can help promote hair growth and maintain healthy hair during pregnancy. However, they may not necessarily prevent hair loss.

Is it safe to color your hair during pregnancy?

While there is no conclusive evidence that hair dye is harmful during pregnancy, many healthcare providers recommend avoiding it during the first trimester when the baby's organs are developing. It is always best to discuss any concerns with your healthcare provider.

Hair Care After Pregnancy

If you experience postpartum hair loss, don't worry. This is a common experience for many women, and your hair will eventually grow back. However, you can take steps to care for your hair during this time:

  1. Continue to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Avoid using heat-styling tools and tight hairstyles.
  3. Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  4. Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment to nourish and strengthen your hair.

In conclusion, the increased levels of estrogen during pregnancy can cause hair to remain in the growth phase for longer, resulting in less shedding and thicker hair. While you may not experience as much hair loss during pregnancy, it is still important to care for your hair to maintain its health and strength. After giving birth, it is common to experience postpartum hair loss, but this is usually temporary, and hair will eventually grow back.
