How To Cut Long Hair Male A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Oct 24, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're a guy with long hair, you know that it can be tough to maintain. Between regular trims and keeping your locks healthy, it can be a lot of work. But what if you could cut your own hair at home? It might sound daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to cut long hair male at home, and give you some tips for maintaining your new 'do.

Description

Long hair can be a lot of fun, but it can also be a lot of work. If you're tired of visiting the barber every few weeks for a trim, or if you're just looking to save some money, cutting your own hair at home might be the solution. While it can be intimidating at first, with a little practice and patience, you'll be a pro in no time.

How to Cut Long Hair Male

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

The first step to cutting long hair male is to gather all of the tools you'll need. This includes:

  • Scissors (preferably hair-cutting shears)
  • Clippers (optional)
  • A comb
  • Sectioning clips (optional)
  • A mirror

Make sure your tools are clean and sharp before you get started.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and dry. This will make it easier to work with and ensure a more even cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using sectioning clips or your fingers, section your hair into manageable pieces. Start at the bottom and work your way up, clipping each section out of the way as you go.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Using your scissors, start cutting the bottom section. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as this can lead to unevenness.

Step 5: Move Upward

Once you've finished the bottom section, release the next section and repeat the process. Continue moving upward until you've cut all of your hair.

Step 6: Trim the Sides and Back

If you want to trim the sides and back of your hair, use your clippers or scissors to do so. Remember to use a mirror to help you see the back of your head.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Once you've finished cutting, use a mirror to check for evenness. Make any necessary adjustments until your hair is the length and style you want.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair Male

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results when cutting long hair male:

  • Start with small sections and work your way up.
  • Use a comb to help guide your scissors.
  • Don't be afraid to make mistakes – hair grows back!
  • Take breaks if you need to – cutting your own hair can be tiring.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long hair male:

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes! Just be sure to cut your hair when it's dry, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

How often should I trim my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how long you want it to be. As a general rule, you should get a trim every 4-6 weeks.

Do I need to use clippers?

No, clippers are optional. If you prefer a more natural look, you can use scissors to trim your hair.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly, but not too often – once every 2-3 days is usually enough.
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Cutting long hair male might seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Follow these steps to cut your hair at home, and don't forget to take care of your locks afterwards. With a little practice and patience, you'll be a pro in no time.
