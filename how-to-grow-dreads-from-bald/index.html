How To Grow Dreads From Bald A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you're bald and want to grow dreads, you may be wondering where to start. Don't worry, it's possible to grow dreads from a bald head, and in this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to make it happen.

How to Grow Dreads from Bald

Before we get started, it's important to note that growing dreads from a bald head is a process that requires patience and commitment. It may take several months or even years to achieve the desired length and thickness of your dreads.

Step 1: Let Your Hair Grow

The first step in growing dreads from a bald head is to let your hair grow. This may take some time, but it's important to be patient and resist the urge to shave or cut your hair during this process. As your hair grows, you can begin to section it into small sections that will eventually become your dreads.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Once your hair has grown to a length of at least 2 inches, you can begin to section it into small sections. Use a comb to create even sections of hair, and secure each section with a rubber band or hair tie.

Step 3: Start the Dreadlocking Process

Now that your hair is sectioned, it's time to start the dreadlocking process. There are several methods you can use to dreadlock your hair, including the palm rolling method, the twist and rip method, and the backcombing method. Choose the method that works best for you and begin to work on each section of hair.

Step 4: Maintain Your Dreads

Once your dreads have begun to form, it's important to maintain them regularly. This includes washing them regularly with a residue-free shampoo, and re-twisting or palm-rolling them as needed to maintain their shape.

Tips for Growing Dreads from Bald

Here are some tips to help you grow dreads from a bald head:

  • Be patient and committed to the process
  • Start with small sections of hair
  • Choose a dreadlocking method that works best for you
  • Maintain your dreads regularly
  • Avoid using hair products that contain silicone or other chemicals that can cause buildup in your hair
  • Protect your hair from damage by wearing a hat or scarf when out in the sun or wind

FAQs About Growing Dreads from Bald

How long does it take to grow dreads from a bald head?

The time it takes to grow dreads from a bald head can vary depending on your hair type and how fast your hair grows. It may take several months or even years to achieve the desired length and thickness of your dreads.

Do I need to use products to grow dreads from a bald head?

While you don't necessarily need to use products to grow dreads from a bald head, using a residue-free shampoo and other dreadlock maintenance products can help to keep your dreads healthy and looking their best.

Can I still wear hats or other head coverings with dreads?

Yes, you can still wear hats or other head coverings with dreads. Just be sure to choose hats that fit loosely and won't cause your dreads to flatten or become misshapen.

Hair Care Tips for Dreads

Here are some additional hair care tips for maintaining healthy dreadlocks:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using hot water or heat styling tools on your dreads
  • Keep your scalp moisturized with natural oils like coconut or jojoba oil
  • Avoid using hair products that contain chemicals or alcohol
  • Protect your dreads from damage by wearing a hat or scarf when out in the sun or wind

With patience, commitment, and proper maintenance, you can grow dreads from a bald head and enjoy the unique, stylish look of this popular hairstyle.
